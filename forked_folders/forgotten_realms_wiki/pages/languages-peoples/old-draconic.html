Old Draconic

Language
nameOld Draconic
familyDraconic languages

Old Draconic, sometimes referred to as Auld Wyrm or Auld Wyrmish,

Derivations

  • The trade tongue of the sarrukh used a script that utilized a smattering of Thorass and quite significantly Old Draconic. This same script was later used to write the Nether Scrolls.
  • For much of Toril's history, mages wrote their spells in an everchanging "pidgin language" or "jargon" derived from Old Draconic, utilizing many of its articles and verbs. This script was augmented by various runes or sigils, which individually carried their own meanings and magical charges. Around 12% of the language in total consisted of words borrowed from Old Draconic.

History

Dating back to as far as the Dawn Age, for much of Toril's ancient history a variety of different cultures and creatures, including the giants, made some use of Old Draconic.

Notable Readers

  • Filfaeril Obarskyr could read Old High Wyrm.
