The Rengarth were a barbarian human people who lived in Netheril in the harsh regions below the Netherese cities north of the Narrow Sea and Zenith during the Shadowed Age.

Culture

The Rengarth subsisted primarily on tundra grasses, caribou, moose, mice, mammoth, and muskoxen. They used mammoths as both mounts and beasts of burden, as they had a particular skill at handling them and a peculiar affinity for them that the Netherese believed was like an animal friendship spell.

The Rengarth hated magic in all its forms, to a fanatical degree. An exception was that used by their shamans, which they saw as holy and sacred. Their hatred extended to the ownership and use of non-divine magic items, the definition of which the shamans, whose view on magic was narrow, decided for the people. Typically, this was just about anything the shaman could not make themselves.

A member of the Rengarth who reached the age adulthood and achieved warrior status undertook a rite of passage. They consumed a powder of secret origin and went on a three-day-long dream-walk. After that time, an animal spirit would appear and guide them out of this state. There was only a 75% chance of one appearing; failure would result in never acquiring an animal spirit guide. Those who succeeded and found such a guide gained a good sense of direction and the chance to gain one of the animal natural abilities for an hour each week.

Religion

The Rengarth considered the mountain range known as the Columns of the Sky to be a sacred place, and home to gods.

History

Circa , the Rengarth fighter Sunbright Steelshanks caught the attention of the Netherese wizards, Candlemas and Sysquemalyn.

The book Heart of the Mountain postulated that goliaths descended from the Rengarth people, when a group went into the Columns of the Sky following the desertification of the Netheril lands, in order to demand answers from their gods.

Another theory by Cormyrean sage Gaylyn Dimswart Wyvernspur in 1407 DR argued that the barbarians of the Ride were the result of Rengarth tribes mixing with a much older civilization of man that fell during the First Flowering. For supporting evidence, there was the oral tradition of the Varm, a tribe from that region, that their ancestors built the Citadel of the Raven.
