The Vistani () were a traveling people, who never stayed long in one place and were always on the move. The Vistani were renowned travelers of the Mists in the Shadowfell, which did not hinder them in the least. The Vistani originated from the Domains of Dread.

Description

Human Vistani had darker, dusky or olive complexions and usually sported jet-black or deep-brown hair and eyes (hazel and green eyes were also common), while their faces had an angular shape with aquiline noses. They wore colorful and cheery clothing and were big drinkers.

Non-human members bore little resemblance to their human counterparts, but shared the same spectrum of eye colors common to all Vistani, usually glossy black. Another commonality all Vistani shared was the violet scar from their respective Blooding rituals. Outlaws, Vistani banished from their tribes, had black scars instead. The Vistani can recognize the scar by intuition alone.

Traditional garb differed from wagon to wagon, tribe to tribe, nevertheless certain customs prevail and were universal. Each nomad put a lot of thought into preserving their beautiful and clean presentation. Clothing was of the practical amount and worn loose, just enough to feel comfortable, with more tightness on body features the Vistani wanted to emphasize. The bright color pallet rang through the patterns and stripes, incorporating the foreign cultures they interacted with. Accessories on the body included and were not limited to: veils, scarves, hats, sashes, bandanas, a plethora of jewelry, feathers, and fur. Males, by custom grew their facial hair if they could. Both sexes kept their hair long with little maintenance while keeping it attractive.

Traits & Abilities

Vistani were usually regarded with suspicion because of their "Evil Eye", the ability to curse anyone they looked at. While the Vistana would get a mental backlash after the curse ended, some powerful Vistani could inflict very terrible curses upon those who'd wronged them.

Society

Vistani traveled in tight-knit family groups or tribes using barrel-topped wagons Each family was part of one of three culturally distinct, extended tribal groups known as tasques. Those encountered by adventurers from Faerûn included the Boem, who were primarily entertainers, and the Kaldresh, who were dedicated to the arts of crafting and tinkering.

The caravans had viewpoints similar to druids, believing that in all aspects of life, one must find and maintain balance. Hence, most wagons emphasized blending with one's surroundings and were willing to commit amoral actions when survival demands it. One must note, that such acts are never cruel, too harmful or driven by hate, for such things were reserved for those who have truly wronged them, and were more akin to small sins done out whim or caprice. When a just cause was given their vengeance knew no bounds.

Some Vistani would offer shelter, food, and protection for travelers and strangers alike. While evil Vistani were likely help Darklords such as Strahd von Zarovich capture weary adventurers and take them into the Domains of Dread.

Culture & Specialties

Vistani were known for their musical talent as well as their skill in silversmithing, coppersmithing, cooking, weaving, entertaining, storytelling, toolmaking, and horse-trading.

Experienced travelers of the Shadowfell, the Vistani were the most likely to know the locations of shadow crossings, known as Bleak Paths in their tongue, in any given region and used them frequently to escape or surprise enemies, or to quicken the pace of their travel. Their knowledge of traversing the Mists was second to none, and were in fact the only ones who could do so freely. The ways by which they did it were secrets they treasured and guarded, threatening retaliation against those who want to be privy to them, even the likes of the Dark Lords were wise enough not to take such risky actions.

The Book of the Raven, found in Candlekeep, was a journal about Vistani day-to-day life as well as the author's travels with them.

Religion

Powerful entities that other mortals considered as gods were often mocked by the Vistani. This doesn't mean that they didn't practice spirituality, on the contrary, but only those who were granted divine power by the gods carried holly symbols on their person. Whenever a pious Vistani appeared in legends, his or her faith was a personal one or the individual was raised outside of Vistani culture. Divine souls—those most favored by the gods—rarely held holy symbols, and some Vistani have sworn to serve the gods as avengers.

That being said, most Vistani were either not religious or paid little mind to those practices. The closest thing that the Vistani had to a common pantheon that they all the revered and respected were the cosmic forces that governed the worlds: The Moon, Fate, The Mists, Death and the forces of nature.

History

Mythological Origins

Despite their wanderlust, each Vistani tribe told a tale of a lost homeland from which all Vistani originated from. No tale was more truthful from the other and encompassed the tribe's culture, values and viewpoints. Vengeful gods, persecutions from an evil outside force, prehistoric sins, and a spirit of defiance were prevailing themes.

When the doroq sets in after the end of the prastonata, the elders recount the genesis myths of their tribe, either in common tongue or in the patterna.

The Vanquished Conqueror

This was the origin story that was popular among the tribes of the Kaldresh tasque, each tribe having slightly different takes. In this legend the Vistani followed a conqueror king by the name of Vistan as a relief force, providing weapons, animals and other supplies for king's army. The king eventually failed in his endeavors, and the Vistani were punished for it. Far away from their original land and enemies blocking every root, the Vistani had no other choice but to settle for a nomadic life. Hungry and tired, the Vistani began their never-ending search for the "home forge", the lands of their ancestors.

Another variations of the legend came from the Zarovan tribe of the Manusa tasque. Instead of King Vistan, Strahd von Zarovich's ancestor was the one who lead the army, and in contrast with previously mentioned version actually succeeded in his campaign of world conquest. In this telling, the Vistani never bowed down before the king and were granted no land by him as a result. Trapped in a land that did not welcome them, they tearfully remember their homeland with no hope of return.

The Spiteful Gods

In legends of the Manusa tasque, gods and mortals lived in harmony, creating a universe where they could procreate, dance and play music. But mortals coveted the power of time which the gods did not want to share. The Dark Powers, evil reflections of the gods, sowed fear, ambition and hatred in the minds of mortals, and so mortals with their newly found allies waged a bloody and destructive war against the gods. Only Manusa defied both her mortal kin and the Dark Powers, choosing to fight on behalf of the divine. Wanting the destruction to end, the gods gave Manusa the secrets of divination. Her prophecies foretold the end of all things, which made the mortals feel shame and regret over their actions, swearing their fealty to the gods once more. The Dark Powers were driven back into the Mists, swearing revenge against Manusa. They did not need to wait for long. The gods with the help of their mortal worshipers, fearing what Manusa could do with the secrets of time, betrayed Manusa, throwing her into the Mists and the clutches of the Dark Powers. Through zealous courage and determination Manusa traveled defying death itself, and from her mouth another prophecy was foretold-the peace between gods and mortals will not last and their fates will be veiled in misery while Manusa laughs defiantly.

Modern History

According to some, the Vistani came from the same world as Strahd von Zarovich did before the mists claimed Barovia. While other scholars within the Domains of Dread asserted that the place of origin for the Vistani was unknown and that they did not appear until a century later, around the same era that Jander Sunstar appeared in Barovia. Any alleged evidence of Vistani presence in Barovia prior to then was thus a scholarly mistake, confusing the domain's non-Vistani gypsies for Vistani.

In the late 15 century DR, a group of Vistani came to the town of Daggerford on Toril and did not leave until a group of unfortunate adventurers accompanied them into Barovia.

Also during the 15 century DR, a group of adventurers known as the Heroes of Baldur's Gate were pursuing a pair of werewolves that had robbed a temple of Kelemvor they were protecting of relics when suddenly one of them activated. An amulet, originating from Barovia, created a thick fog that began to fill the air. The fog quickly filled the room and the heroes, the Kelemvorite Nerys Kathon, along with the two werewolves and their associate Sangin, were all transported to the Demiplane of Dread.

On their first day in Barovia, the heroes stumbled upon the Vistani caravan of Madame Eva. She told Minsc his fortune using the tarokka deck, foretelling of failure to rid that realm of its evil. This sent the barbarian into a rage, smashing Madam Eva's table before storming out of her tent, leaving his ally Shandie Freefoot to pay for the damages.
