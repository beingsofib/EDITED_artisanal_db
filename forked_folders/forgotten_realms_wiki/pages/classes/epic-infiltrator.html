An epic infiltrator was an individual whose skills in espionage, sabotage, disguise, and going undercover were supremely outstanding.

Activities

An epic infiltrator was most often found doing work for powerful merchant lords or governments. They were often engaged in long-term infiltration operations, but from time to time accepted more short-term, one-off missions.

Many of them worked freelance, taking contracts that had been arranged through a third party. And occasionally they could even be found as part of an adventuring company, using their skills in a manner akin to a scout.

While on a mission they typically had no less than three cover identities prepared to allay any suspicions towards them. Whilst some preferred larger repertoires of cover identities, other infiltrators chose to further perfect the ones they already had, making it harder for people to see through their disguise.

It took infiltrators roughly a week of rigorous practice to develop a new cover identity, perfecting a body language and subtle vocal intonations for the new identity.

Whilst all infiltrators were skilled in the art of going undercover, only some specialized in the field of concealment. Others chose to focus their talents in the areas of espionage, subterfuge, or interacting with others.

Culture

The Gleaners, a sorcerous society that operated across the multiverse, employed infiltrators from time to time.

Prerequisites

In order for one to qualify as an epic infiltrator they had to be incredibly skilled at bluffing and the art of disguise, at reading lips, be versed in multiple languages, and had to have successfully spent one month posing as someone else. Above all, their uncanny prowess in staying composed in heated situations and telling people what they want to hear were their most valuable assets on any mission.

People from any walk of life could become an epic infiltrator, though due to the high-requirements of the mantle it was typically taken up by bards and rogues. Beyond them, many a fighter, sorcerer, and wizard were more than capable in going undercover to step into the role of an epic infiltrator. By contrast, it was uncommon to find barbarians, druids, or paladins taking up this mantle.

Individuals who leaned towards any sort of chaotic nature were unsuitable for this station.

Abilities

All epic infiltrators exhibited an always active supernatural ability that was akin to the spell undetectable alignment–their complex web of multiple identities made it impossible for others to ascertain their alignment, confounding all manner of divination spells. However, they were still subject to spells that targeted certain alignments, such as holy smite or protection from evil.

As epic infiltrators grew more skilled in their role, they began to develop a supernatural ability akin to detect thoughts, allowing them to "hear" the surface thoughts of others one or more times per day. They could extend their auditory and visual senses in a manner akin to clairaudience/clairvoyance, perceiving even through barriers, though this ability was far more limited in range than the aforementioned spell and required the infiltrator to have previously visited a location.

Lastly, as they went on epic infiltrators learned how to rigorously silence their mind, making it immune to all manner of divinations and mind-affecting spells. This functioned in a manner similar to the spell mind blank.

Due to being masters of disguise, infiltrators were also skilled at changing into and out of armor, doing so in about half the time it would take others. And they were quite skilled at striking precisely at vital spots on a target.
