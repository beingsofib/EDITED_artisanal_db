A spiritualist was a type of psion who, rather than specializing in one of the disciplines, devoted themselves to an interest in death, undeath, and the afterlife. They honed their psionic powers towards such ends, learning how to sense and at times even control the spirits of the deceased. They were equivalent to the necromancers of arcane magic.

Activities

Spiritualists frequently attempted to contact spirits of the deceased, an occultic practice that they gained a reputation for. They also were often sought out by others for divination purposes.

Culture

Spiritualists were typified as rather enigmatic and mysterious individuals, in many cases even being of evil alignments. It was not uncommon for them to speak to others in the form of riddles, verse, or even roundabout phrases.

Unlike other psionicists, they were often found to be well versed in manners of ancient history, astrology, disguises, fortune telling, healing, and religion.

Spiritualist were found be active in Cormyr, Sembia, and Waterdeep. They tended to be viewed in the same suspicious light as necromancers or witches, even in such aforementioned areas of the Realms that were considered to be relatively civilized and enlightened. Many nervous and superstitious commoners would bolt their doors or bar their windows at a spiritualist's passing, or even go so far as to chase them down with torches. Those who weren't superstitious likewise looked down upon them, perceiving their occult divinatory practices as dangerous wastes of time.

Prerequisites

The one prerequisite to being able to go down this path as a psionicist was knowing the power spirit sense.

Abilities

Spiritualists had no particular discipline that they favored over others, though they typically drew from clairsentience, psychokinesis, psychometabolism, and telepathy. Because of their strong links to the dead they were quite proficient in seances and had a spell-like ability to speak with dead once or more per week.

Powers commonly learned by spiritualists included animate shadow, awe, cause decay, cell adjustment, death field, double pain, ectoplasmic form, false sensory input, incarnation awareness, life detection, life draining, project force, psychic clone, psychic drain, psychic messenger, retrospection, sensitivity to psychic impressions, shadow-form, stasis field, suspend animation, synaptic static, and wrench.

Possessions

The preferred manner of dress for a spiritualist was typically a long, flowing and concealing robe, rather than more modern styles. If a spiritualist had any armor, it would be found worn beneath their robes. They almost always carried around mystical equipment for seances and fortune telling, largely for effect. This included such things as candles, cards, spirit boards, and most often a mundane or magical crystal ball.
