Thrallherds were psionics users, most often psions (especially telepaths), who harnessed their mastery over the powers of the mind towards dominating the minds of beasts and other sentient creatures, forcing them to be their devout servants.

Culture

Thrallherds usually had a minor menagerie of enthralled creatures, who were referred to as either their thralls or believers. They preferred to delegate most tasks, including their own physical defense, to their thralls. Rather than directly involving themselves in combat, they would order thralls to fight on their behalf.

As this path of psionics involved subverting the free will of others, thrallherds were likely to tend towards the evil side of alignments. Even if not outright evil, a thrallherd's personality tended to be domineering, controlling, and in some cases even maniacal.

Abilities

Upon undertaking the path of a thrallherd, the psionicist's mind developed subtle psychic resonance, connecting them to nearby people and drawing them towards the thrallherd to serve them. The first servants of a thrallherd would arrive within 24 hours of their greater telepathic abilities awakening. If any servants died, the thrallherd's psychic call would draw in new replacements within 24 hours. All thralls were anxious and eager to fulfill the will of their thrallherd.

All thrallherds knew the power psionic charm and were able to use it more effectively than other psionics users. As they progressed in power, a thrallherd gained psionic dominate, which they likewise had greater mastery of. A thrallherd suffered less mental strain from augmenting the power to affect the likes of aberrations, animals, dragons, elementals, fey, giants, magical beasts, monstrous humanoids, and outsiders. Beyond these innate powers, thrallherds tended towards learning psionics that allowed them to manipulate others, such as causing enemies to fight amongst themselves.

Following the Spellplague, thrallherds obtained the power to use the raise dead ritual to bring their most central thrall back to life, costing them on average 500 or 5,000 gold pieces in ritual components.

Possessions

While traditionally psionics users such as thrallherds didn't need any physical tools or weaponry to practice their discipline, following the Spellplague they began using implements to enhance their abilities. Typically these took the form of orbs and staves, through which they would channel their psionic energy into powers.

Notable Thrallherds

  • Rhauron, the mysterious creator of the mind-controlling gems known as the Winking Eyes of Rhauron in the 1480s DR, was believed by some to be a thrallherd psion.
