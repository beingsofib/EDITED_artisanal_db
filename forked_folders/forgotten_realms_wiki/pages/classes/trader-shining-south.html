A trader, as understood in the Shining South, was both a crafty merchant and a thief. Few ever bested a trader in a bargain.

Activities

These traders were often more than mere storekeepers. While some were competent shop owners who never left their homes, most were world-traveling caravan masters or master traders who represented their chaka, the core social unit of the Shining Lands, in distant regions. Thus, Durpari merchants encountered abroad were most often these traders.

Culture

Durparian traders typically worked within the law, plying their trade while adventuring. More rarely, a trader might remain at home, never leaving it for the rest of their life. Traders found it difficult to resist a bargain. A successful trader preferred to purchase something at a low price instead of just stealing it, yet they were not above listening in or sneaking about to gather the information needed to strike the best possible deal.

While trader rogues were proficient with all manner of thief weapons, they preferred smaller arms such as daggers or throwing knives that could be concealed beneath a shop counter. The light crossbow was their favored weapon, valued for how easily it could be stored in the accessible spaces of a shop or caravan wagon. Traders rarely relied on big or flashy weapons.

Traders had little use for the skills often associated with thieves. Many traders did not even own a set of lockpicks or devices like climbing boots or grappling arrows. Traders usually carried something to sell or buy and often already started their adventuring with more gold than other adventurers.

Abilities

Traders had to have at least slightly above average intelligence. These merchants were always experts at appraising an item's worth, and knowing exactly how much profit could be made. In addition to appraising common items, many traders specialized themselves in appraising certain types of more unique items like jewels or spices.

A traders' life very often encouraged them to also be proficient in information gathering, observation, linguistics, forgery (used to recognize false documents and counterfeit currency), and alertness skills.

They were also proficient in all weapons that regular thieves were also proficient in.

History

This type of trader tradition was well established by the mid–14 century DR.

Notable Traders

  • Bilaska Semenir, a human master trader that encountered a group of elves whose spelljamming ship had crashed. She could not speak their language, nor could they speak hers, but within a week, she had traded them supplies, weapons, food, and whatever else they needed in return for gems that were completely unknown in the Realms.
  • Lata Hisiir, a half-elf trader. She was an expert in all forms of gems and jewels.
