An infiltrator was an elite scout within elvish society, a master of disguise and operating covertly in both urban and sylvan environments alike. They had a reputation for being able to acquire reliable intel quickly and quietly, even delighting in the unearthing of secrets.

Culture

A substantial number of infiltrators could be found across Faerûn, typically living lives as guides, mercenaries, or spies. In some rare cases, they were even professional thieves. But on Evermeet, infiltrators played an integral part in the island nation's defenses.

Infiltrators were not limited to any one subrace of elves, but were most commonly found amongst the likes of drow, high elves, and sylvan elves. The practice could be taken up by the likes of mages, warriors, and rogues.

Due to the extensive periods they spent in the role of others, infiltrators over time risked losing their sense of identity and becoming less sure of themselves than other elves. Their race's special meditative state, the reverie, proved inadequate in preventing this slippage. As a consequence of this bruise to their sense of identity, infiltrators lost some of racial resistance to charm-related and sleep spells.

Possessions

Infiltrators varied considerably in the types of equipment they carried, but they were always guaranteed to have a quality disguise kit on their person. With their mastery of disguise, an infiltrator could pass themselves off as a short human or tall dwarf. They also lacked any form of distinguishing mark in their appearance, thus if an elf wanted to find an infiltrator they had to rely upon word of mouth.

Notable Infiltrators

  • Rennyn Aelorothi, an elite spy of Queen Amlaruil Moonflower of Evermeet.
