Unicorn riders were an elite cavalry of Queen Amlaruil Moonflower and the defenders of Evermeet, notable for riding unicorns.

Activities

Unicorn riders served Queen Amlaruil as warriors in her army on Evermeent, like the elven knights, but some could also be found in Faerûn, where they operated as agents of Evermeet or simply as adventurers and mercenaries. Those on Evermeet could be mobilized for defense at a moment's notice.

Culture

For reasons unknown, only elven women could become unicorn riders. Each spring, elven shield-maidens ventured into the forests in hopes of attracting a unicorn, which might agree to serve as their mount for a year. On average, a hundred to several hundred unicorn riders resided on Evermeet at any given time.

An elf chosen by a unicorn was regarded with near-religious respect and awe by the other elves of Evermeet, Defending Evermeet was regarded as the sacred duty of these riders. These unicorns initially agreed to serve their riders for a year. However, some chose to remain with their riders indefinitely if they were treated with respect.

The unicorns of Evermeet were regarded as especially unique, for the elves believed them to be the direct descendants of Eachthighern, the god of unicorns and pegasi, and that he would return to defend them should they be endangered. Unicorns were common in the forests of Evermeet, and the elves held them to be sacred. To kill a unicorn was an unthinkable act the elves, and any visitor who harmed or slew one would hunted down and killed without mercy.

Abilities

Unicorn riders had at least above-average physical and mental traits.

They were adept at grooming horses and were proficient in using lances and all types of bows. They could also become proficient in any type of mounted weapon. They were proficient in training animals and riding land-based creatures, including unicorns. It was also good for unicorn riders to possess animal handling, orienteering, hunting, and survival skills. After becoming a unicorn rider, an individual had an easier time communicating with unicorns in general. Unicorn riders could also freely communicate with their unicorn.

History

A cavalry of 50–100 unicorn riders existed in ancient Cormanthyr as part of the Wing and Horn cavalry of the Akh'Velahr.

The traditions and practices of Evermeet unicorn riders were well established by the Year of the Shield, 1367 DR.

Notable Unicorn Riders

  • Ialantha Rumithamal, a cousin of Queen Amlaruil and formerly a unicorn rider.
  • Sister Iahalae, an elf woman from Aerilaya's tribe on Evermeet who became a unicorn rider.
