Harpers of legend were extraordinary Master Harpers who were graced with powerful divine blessings bestowed by the Seven Gods of the Dancing Place who in ages past had given rise to the Harpers.

Activities

Every such Harper bore a long record of bold deeds and daring exploits. Yet even in death their service often did not cease. Cylyria Dragonbreast and Dathlue Mistwinter, for example, remained vigilant beyond the grave, sometimes serving as mentors to Harpers of their own time and those in training.

Culture

Many Harpers served their organization for long years before achieving legendary status, yet at times the Seven themselves would intervene, bestowing their blessings upon individuals who had never belonged to the Harpers, should they prove a force for good worthy of such divine favor. Yet any such individual would have probably drawn the attention of the Harpers themselves, before the need for divine intervention. In their future, such Harpers would probably have their names spoken in the same breath as those of other great Harpers who have gone before them: Storm Silverhand, Cylyria Dragonbreast, or Arilyn Moonblade. Sometimes even after death, these Harpers participated in the life and culture of their organization, by giving advice or training new and old Harpers.

Abilities

Harpers of legend were always versed in at least two of the following four skills: diplomacy, insight into others, nature knowledge, or stealth.

These heroes were well-traveled individuals that learned more than most people do.

Mightier Harpers of legend bore a special blessing from the goddess Mielikki, allowing them to push their bodies harder than usually possible and thus perform astonishing feats unique to their non-Harper training, and more often.

Even mightier legendary Harpers received a blessing from Tymora, which allowed them to avoid powerful attacks, or stop negative conditions that were possible to avoid, even when they failed at avoiding them.

Once per day, when one of the most powerful Harpers of legend died, their body would disintegrate into silver dust and their spirit would walk free. When this happened, they retained their learned skills and attributes, became insubstantial, could pass through solid objects, were immune to poison, and were more resistant to damaging necrotic energy. After an extended rest, their bodies would restore themselves. If they died in this form, they were finally dead. However, thanks to the blessings of the Seven Gods of the Dancing Place, a Harper of legend was immortal in a sense, able to transcend this "permanent" death and endure as ghost Harpers, their vigilance undimmed.

History

In ages past, when the Harpers were founded, the Seven Gods of the Dancing Place collectively extended their blessings to mortals who vowed to fight against evil in the Year of the Dawn Rose, 720 DR. These blessings were the basis for the abilities of the adventurers called Harpers of legend.
