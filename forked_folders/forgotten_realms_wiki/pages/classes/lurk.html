Lurks were a martial form of psionics user, driven by a personal code of ethics that directed how they used their powers, who channeled their control of the Invisible Art towards augmenting their weaponstrikes and becoming masters of stealth.

Culture

Across the Realms, lurks were comparatively far fewer in number than the more common psionic pathways of the psion, psychic warrior, soulknife, and wilder. In some respects, the talent necessary to become one was comparable to that of a psychic warrior, but whereas warriors focused their efforts on training the body and augmenting martial prowess, lurks focused on their capacity for staying hidden and avoiding detection.

Every individual lurk followed some form of personal code. Some dedicated themselves to serving the good, some were focused only on their own self-interests, and some were intertwined with the dictates of a heavily religious lurk's deity.

Lurks never killed indiscriminately. Based on their personal code, they sought out to find and remove certain people from the world, such as instances of true evil or those who opposed the truths of their religion. Some simply worked on commission, using the advantages afforded to them by psionics to forge careers as deadly assassins or information brokers.

While lurks were equally as likely to come from any of the psionically-inclined sentient races of Toril, they were found to be most prevalent among dwarves and rare among monstrous humanoids.

Alignment

The alignment of a lurk could fall anywhere within the spectrum, though most tended towards a lawful outlook, and wherever they fell was reflected upon by their personal code.

Regions

The more criminally inclined of could commonly be found in congregating in major cities, particularly around any area that a significant thieves' guild, as they inevitably dominated and grew bored of the underground trade in smaller urban centers. In the late 14 century DR, a small thieves' guild of dwarven lurks called the Knights of the Wall operated in the Great Rift, doing seedy business beneath the notice of the Steel Shields of Eartheart.

Relationships

Lurks were amiable to working with all manner of spellcasters, warriors, or psionics users so long as they didn't interfere with their personal code. They particularly an appreciation towards healers and those who could serve as a front-line offender.

Abilities

In terms of psionic powers, lurks commonly chose those that helped them to evade harm or detection, such as chameleon power, defensive precognition, or evade burst. And their understanding of the Invisible Art uniquely granted lurks the ability to instill their weapon strikes with a number of temporary psionic augmentations. Over time, lurks honed their skills to the point they could use two or even three augmentations at a time.

These augmentations included some of the following:

  • Aligned Attack: Made weapons register as being instilled with a certain alignment in order to bypass alignment-based resistances. This would be the moral opposite of the lurk's own, either good or evil, though a neutral-aligned lurk could choose whichever of the two they desired.
  • Ghost Touch: Made a weapon's attacks incorporeal, as though they had the ghost touch weapon quality.
  • Greater Power Drain: An advanced form of the power drain augmentation.
  • Ignore Concealment: Enabled a weapon to hone in on invisible targets.
  • Mental Assault: Made weapons cause harm to a target's mind.
  • Planar Attack: Made weapons deal greater harm to either good or evil aligned creatures, the specific alignment being the lurk's choice.
  • Power Drain: Made weapons drain psychic energy from psionic users struck by it.
  • Sneak Attack Constructs: Made weapons deal greater harm to undead.
  • Sneak Attack Undead: Made weapons deal greater harm to constructs.
  • Stygian Weapon: Forming a momentary link between themselves and the Negative Energy plane, imbuing their weapons with negative energy, an effect similar to the stygian weapon power.
  • Synaptic Disconnect: Made weapons temporarily hinder a target's synapses, preventing them from casting spells or manifesting powers, as well as the use of spell-like and psionic-like abilities.
  • Unfocusing Strike: Made weapons break a manifester's psionic focus upon contact.

When maintaining a psionic focus, a lurk could channel their power of the Invisible Art to pinpoint the vital spots on a target that would allow the lurk to inflict greater harm. Some lurks neglected this in favor of other psionic abilities, The most skilled of lurks could occasionally evade being affected by enchantment spells.

When it came to combat, lurks preferred to operate in a manner similar to rogues – skulking about an area, waiting for the right opportunity to strike or turn to their advantage, and rarely being the ones to initiate a fight. They remained more interested in supporting teammates by harassing their enemies – and slipping around to sneak attack – than in standing up to an opponent directly, as reflected in their preference for light armors. As their skills in the Invisible Art grew over time, lurks would make distinct combinations of augmentations tailored towards their enemies.

Notable Lurks

  • Harhault Hammerheart, a gold dwarf lurk who led the Knights of the Wall thieves' guild.
  • Reliath Crescentia, a wood elf lurk and member of the Kaliesh'erai.
  • Zunkhasp Saltbaron, a duergar lurk who led the Gray Ghosts thieves' guild.
