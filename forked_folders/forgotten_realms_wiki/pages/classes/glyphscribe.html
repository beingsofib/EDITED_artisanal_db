Glyphscribes were specialty priests devoted to Deneir, the Lord of All Glyphs and Images.

Activities

Glyphscribes were focused with gathering any form of written knowledge so that it would not be lost. Whether they did this from the comfort of a library, or by exploring the vastness of Toril depended on the nature of the individual.

Abilities

Given their close association with Deneir, glyphscribes had a stronger chance of resisting or avoiding any traps or other negative effects triggered from reading or touching books, scrolls, or other magical inscriptions.

These individuals were incredibly skilled with languages, usually being fluent in well over a dozen different tongues.

Glyphscribes could identify magical runes including the type of person or creature who created the rune and the rune's general meaning.

Several magical abilities became available on a daily basis to glyphscribes as they gained power including glyph of warding, glyph of revealing, dispel magic, amanuensis, and symbol. They could use a speak with dead power up to four times per day.

Possessions

Glyphscribes wielded one-handed bludgeoning weapons in combat. They were permitted to wear armor as heavy as banded mail and they never carried shields.
