Alienists were spellcasters whose studies of the planes led them to the very boundaries of reality, to the plane known as the Far Realm, consorting with the unfathomable entities therein to ascend beyond their limitations and achieve new heights of power at the cost of their sanity.

Activities

While they could on rare occasions be found gathered in groups, such as to perform a special ritual, alienists were traditionally loners.

Culture

Alienists held a philosophical understanding of magic that was distinct from other schools of thought. They believed that magic was no more than an individual's mind applying their knowledge and sheer force of will to transcend the crude boundaries of dimension, distance, often times sanity, and eventually time itself. To an alienist every formula, enchantment, and spell in existence represented hidden truths in the structure of the multiverse.

While most were traditionally wizards, clerics, and sorcerers.

Much like warlocks, an alienist concerned themselves solely with the nebulous entities of the Far Realm, and elder evils such as Mak Thuum Ngatha, entities whose motivations were far less comprehensible to mortals.

Alienists generally fell into one of three archetypes: intellectuals, priests, and scholars. The first described alienists who were seekers of knowledge, the second those who revered their patrons, often in some form of cult or ancient religion, and the third the overly cautious researchers. While intellectuals were likely to plunge themselves into a maddening dimension or entity in their mad pursuit for greater knowledge, scholars sought to carefully avoid witnessing that which they believed mortals were never meant to see.

Within the Far Realm itself, this path of magic was frequently sought by the native kaorti.

Personality

An alienist's pursuit of the greater mysteries of the universe brought them plunging into abysses of madness that would fracture weaker minds, until inevitably their confidence twisted into a chilling mania.

Often their minds were assaulted with whispers from unknown entities far beyond.

According to some, because of the dark and dangerous magics they trafficked in, alienists were at risk of developing some form of madness, phobia, or affliction every time they learned or researched a spell, investigated formulae for potions or scrolls, or researched the ingredients or process of creating a magic item. These could range from melancholy and paranoia to more jarring conditions, like having their mind switched with that of an alien entity or perceiving sights, sounds, or sensations from the Far Realm. But most commonly, alienists developed a phobia towards a particular kind of creature, which extended to anything that resembled it or shared its attributes.

It was believed the mental instability of an alienist could be somewhat treated by means of a restoration or wish spell, psychic surgery, or an elixir of health. However, whosoever treated the conditions of an alienist had a small risk of contracting the condition themselves.

Abilities

An alienist often wielded specialized spells in service of, or in opposition to, the powerful aberrant entities of the Far Realm.

As a result of their connection to the Far Realm, any casting of one of the summon monster spells by an alienist would result in the conjuration of a pseudonatural equivalent—this meant they were largely incapable of conjuring other forms of outsiders. Even attempts to conjure celestial or fiendish creatures would instead result in the conjuration of a pseudonatural equivalent. However, they were capable of casting summon monster spells twice as often as other spellcasters. And over time even their familiar, if an alienist had one, would slowly take on pseudonatural aspects until they became fully functional.

As their studies progressed, alienists learned how to profit off of the enigmatic whispers of Far Realm entities, granting them access to metamagic abilities. Late into this path of magic, an alienist inevitably discovered an obscure means of achieving eternal youth, so absolute that not even magical means of aging could effect them. By this time, if an alienist were to die before they achieved true transcendence, their body would be whisked away by horrible entities, never to be seen again.

The apex of an alienist's development was that of transcendence, when their laborious studies and congress with Far Realm entities finally led them to becoming an alien creature.

An ascended alienist possessed some degree of resistance to acid and electricity, as well as suffered less harm from assaults of a magical nature. They were no longer affected by spells that specifically targeted humanoids and they were immediately recognizable as transcendent by other researchers of the Far Realm.

Notable Alienists

  • Marzut Rensha, the Fifth Rensha lord of Loudwater, was a cruel and mad alienist whose endeavors into the unknown eventually led to his death.
  • Zceryll, an alienist whose commune with the Far Realm eventually led her to become a vestige.
