Culture

Folk heroes varied greatly in the forms of professions they had prior to making the decision to become an adventurer, ranging from such things as farming, shepherding, woodcutting,

A folk hero could vary greatly in terms of their alignment, though owing to the expectations of such a role, heroes were usually virtuous individuals. Fighting against oppressive tyrants and monsters, freeing slaves, sheltering escaped slaves, and garnering wealth so that they could afford to help build much-needed structures in their home community. And because of their reputation, folk heroes were often looked upon as patrons of the commonfolk, someone who their community sought out in times of need.

Most folk heroes looked back upon their humble origins as a sort of blessing in disguise and cherished the communities they grew up in.

However, the benefits of being a folk hero were conditional. If a hero strayed from their down-to-earth ways or mistreated their former friends, they could find themselves shut out from their community. Furthermore, if a folk hero did manage to strike it rich whilst adventuring, they were likely to find themselves pestered from friends, neighbors, and any local beggars to share in the wealth. Some might resort to pressing charges against the hero for true or false misdeeds of the past, whilst others would make pleas of their blood relation in order to get some sort of gift or loan.

In several countries in Faerûn, with Tethyr being one such example, peasants had to swear an oath of loyalty to their liege. Because of this, a peasant hero could find themselves being rewarded by their liege for their bravery and then commanded to join the military or take on some quest or other responsibility. Refusal to obey their liege could incur an arrest or other troubles.

Possessions

In the early stages of their career as an adventurer, the appearance of a folk hero was not too dissimilar from that of the average peasant. Their clothing would be often dirty, yet durable, with gloves and shoes supplemented by cloth wrappings. And being hard workers they were often unhygienic and displayed callused hands. They usually were not adorned in armor unless they were out adventuring, and when they were it was typically either hide or leather armor.

In the early stages of their career, a hero's weaponry was limited to whatever was most available to the peasantry. This often meant long and short bows, clubs, daggers, footman's flails, footman's picks, hand axes, knives, machetes, military forks, quarterstaves, short swords, spears, slings, staff slings, sickles, and warhammers. A good deal of these were derived from tools that the peasant hero used in their daily life, such as mattocks for digging, threshing flails for beating the grain, or pitchforks for moving hay.

Other tools of the peasantry that could find use as weapons in the hands of a folk hero ranged the gamut of billhooks, various types of hoes, hunting knives, rakes, reavers and scythes, shears, shepherd's crooks, shovels, fisherman's tridents, and weeding claws.

Combat

Unlike more experienced warriors, a peasant hero often lacked the know-how for special combat maneuvers or martial arts.

History

Over the course of Faerûnian history, there were many wars and disasters that inspired commoners to rise above their station and become folk heroes. These included Cormyr's stand against the Tuigan Horde, Amn's war with the Sythillisian Empire, the nation of Thay's intrigues against Aglarond and the Dalelands, and the Tethyrian Interregnum and Reclamation Wars. Even the occasional attacks from humanoids or giants upon the people of Luiren could provide the spark necessary to become a folk hero.

In the late 15 century, the merchant Syndra Silvane became afflicted with a condition called the death curse. After clerics were unable to help her, she contacted the Harpers, who indicated to her that the cause was a necromantic artifact in Chult called the Soulmonger. After learning this news, Syndra gathered information about Chult and began hiring teams of adventurers to travel into its jungle depths. At least a few of these adventurers she'd end up recruiting would be sought due to the reputation they had amassed as folk heroes.

Notable Folk Heroes

  • The Blue Blade, a gallant brigand who became something of a folk hero in Cormyr for his robbing of rich travelers in King's Forest.
  • Bromdurr Tathen, a peasant hero who gained fame after helping defend his hometown of Memnon from pirates.
  • Danton, a tiefling druid from the Western Heartlands in the late 15 century DR.
  • Gellana Mirrorshade, an affluent cleric of Garl Glittergold who was renowned as a folk hero by gnomes across the Western Heartlands.
  • Hadarr Karayaka, a folk hero among the Calishite community of Daggerford.
  • Halsaime, an elder druid of Thorngap who was considered something of a folk hero among Cormyrians before he was driven insane by a strange staff in the late 15 century DR.
  • Herman Gresham, a blacksmith who became a peasant hero among the people of the small village of Gresham.
  • Ichi the Highwayman, a rogue who was renowned as a folk hero among the people of Miyama in the 12 century DR.
  • Minsc, a Rashemaar warrior that earned fame throughout the Sword Coast in the 14 and 15 centuries DR.
  • Peredus the Great, a folk hero in Daggerford who was renowned for defending caravans.
  • Ridal Sureblade, a peasant hero from the barony of Karondar who led his fellow commoners in overthrowing their tyrannical baron.
  • Wyll Ravengard, a warlock adventurer who went by the moniker "Blade of the Frontiers" on the Sword Coast in the late 15 century DR.
  • Zahara the Shark, a famous swashbuckler-thief folk hero and captain of a small privateer fleet on the Sword Coast called Zahara's Krakens.
