A crusader was a warrior in the service of a deity who acted according to a certain code.

Culture

Crusaders were rare warriors who were in the highest favor of their chosen deities. These warriors were true zealots, striking in the name of their chosen causes with fury. A crusader would never betray their chosen cause willingly, and was a devastating warrior on the battlefield. Each crusader was bound to an alignment, be it law, good, evil, or chaos.

Crusaders gained incredible abilities, rivaling those of mages, and were capable of shrugging off blows that would be fatal to other warriors. Good crusaders were often mistaken for paladins, the difference being revealed only when the crusader was powerless to heal a wounded man with her hands or summon a celestial mount. Evil crusaders were among the deadliest people and brought a blight of darkness wherever they went. Crusaders might arise in any part of Faerûn, from any race, but they were rare.

Abilities

These warriors gained extra accuracy and skill if they were harmed or injured in battle. A crusader also gained many powerful divine abilities and maneuvers, which they could not select when to use, but their god granted them in the course of combat. The maneuvers were rejuvenated by meditating for five minutes.
