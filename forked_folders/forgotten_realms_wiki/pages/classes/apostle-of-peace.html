An apostle of peace, also known simply as a pacifist priest, were prestigious divine spellcasters dedicated to peace and pacifism.

Culture

They were an unusual breed, forswearing violence of all kinds and material possessions in favor of using their life to promote peace and nonviolent resolutions to conflict.

They were often lone wanders and hermits in the wilderness, far apart from the violence-filled lives of adventurers. However, they could aid them by providing knowledge, healing, spells, and spiritual guidance.

Though apostles of peace came from all walks of life, clerics, bards, and druids most often took this class, but anyone could conceivably convert to pacifism and become apostles of peace. Former barbarians, fighters, and rogues were rare: they had understandable difficulty with the vows of nonviolence and peace required for the class, and attaining the necessary skills.

Pacifist priests could commonly be found in the Dalelands and among the elven clerics of Evermeet. There it was commonly taken up by clerics of Hanali Celanil, Labelas Enoreth, and Sehanine Moonbow.

Abilities

An apostle possessed powerful access to divine magic, gaining their own unique set of spells, but their spells were aimed at healing, ending hostilities, and helping allies. Like clerics devoted to good gods, apostles could spontaneously transform any prepared spell into a cure spell of the same strength.

Apostles could turn undead like clerics and paladins who worshiped good gods, or enhanced their turning power if they already were.

Fairly early on, apostles could pacify angry or hostile creatures with just a touch. Similar to a calm emotions spell, this effect suppressed any negative emotions. This ability also worked against mind-affecting spells that triggered fear, rage, or other violent emotions.

Much like turning undead, an apostle of peace could censure fiends. By channeling holy power from the celestial planes, the apostle could stun an evil outsider or, with sufficient power, banish it back to its home plane.

Sacred Vows

Regardless of their faith, all apostles of peace swore themselves to a set of sacred vows. with the exception of constructs and undead. They could not cause such harm through either spells or weapons, incapacitate or weaken foes so that someone else could slay them, nor could they target a foe with spells that had the immediate potential to cause great harm, suffering, or death. They also forswore the wearing of armor.

In return for their vows, apostles received an array of supernatural boons. They exuded a calming aura equivalent to the calm emotions spell, allies around them felt great remorse for slaying the helpless and defenseless, they became more difficult for other creatures to harm. In some cases weapons would even shatter upon their skin. They also became more skilled at casting pacifist spells. These usually fell under the schools of abjuration, enchantment, and illusion, though some divination and necromancy spells applied as well, like ray of exhaustion and waves of fatigue.

If an apostle ever wore armor or broke their vows of non-violence they would lose access to their spells and other special abilities. Whilst simply removing armor would grant them back their spells and abilities, the breaking of their vows of non-violence would require seeking penance and the atonement spell.

Possessions

While they could not don armor, apostles could wear protective magic items, such as bracers of armor, though any vows against owning valuables usually prevented this. In terms of weaponry, they usually had a dagger or knife on their person, though it was for the purposes of eating, and in some cases even bows and darts if they had happened to be skilled in their use prior to becoming a pacifist.
