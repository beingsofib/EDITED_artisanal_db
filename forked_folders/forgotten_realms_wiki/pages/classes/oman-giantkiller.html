Oman giantkillers were fighters and rangers from the Moonshae Isles who specialized in fighting giants. The faction arose during the 15 century DR following the fall of Iron Keep and Oman's Isle to fomorians.

Activities

As giant-killers, they were trained to go toe-to-toe with heavy-hitting enemies, and relished standing at the very front of the frontlines against such foes. This did not mean they were reckless, rather that they were willing to take calculated risks against large creatures. They kept their oversized enemies' attention confounded and distracted until the chance to strike a killing blow presented itself.

Culture

Most Oman giantkillers were young Moonshavian adventurers. They were trained in specialized tactics for slaying giants and giant-kin by a Northlander clan on the island of Norland. who had overrun the island's once proud Northlander kingdom in the . They mostly trained Northlanders who shared their dream of reclaiming the island, although they also shared their techniques with outsiders who promised to aid them in their goal. Even so, some of their students identified that their new skills were useful against many large monsters rather than just giants, and set out to pursue their own ends.

In true Northlander tradition, skill at sailing and knowledge about the sea were expected of an Oman giantkiller.

Prerequisites

In general, giantkillers were expected to be natives of the Moonshae Isles. Prospects were required to have at least some experience as a fighter and had to have mastered the hunter's quarry ability utilized by rangers.

Abilities

The tactics of the Oman giantkillers were useful for slaying any monster that towered over them. They were most dangerous in the thick of the action, and were tenacious and persistent in pushing through injuries and pursuing their quarry. More experienced giantkillers learned to refocus their battle fury to allow them to assault their foes with surprising vigor.

A favored maneuver was the "toss and close", in which the giantkiller hurled a weapon at their target, rapidly closed the distance, and retrieved their weapon for a follow-up melee strike. Another maneuver was to use two weapons to open a gaping wound in their enemy at a moment when any allies could strike at this same injury for a devastating coordinated attack.
