Tabit was a hearth-goddess worshiped by the Pazruki.

Legends

According to the legends of the Pazruki, in time immemorial, Tabit came down from the heavens to give her blessings to Pazruk, the youngest son of the Father of All Tribes. With this, she also gifted him a sacred golden torc. This legend was cited as the origin of the Pazruki's patriarchal practices, especially the passing down of inheritances from father to youngest son.
