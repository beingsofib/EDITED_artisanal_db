The Earthmother, often referred to as the Goddess, was a primal spirit venerated as the local deity of the Moonshae Isles by the druids of the Ffolk, who regarded her as the soul of the land and the guiding force in their lives.

The Earthmother was considered an aspect of Chauntea by followers of the Faerûnian pantheon, although she spurned clerics who beseeched her as a goddess of agriculture in favor of the druids who saw her as a goddess of nature.

Description

Within the Moonshae Isles, the Earthmother wielded the influence of a greater deity, but she was little known and had only minor influence outside of the Isles, where her power dwindled to that on par with a lesser deity. She treasured unspoiled wilderness more than cleared farmland, but was regarded as a generalized goddess of nature encompassing wilderness, agriculture, and balance between them.

Among those who understood the Earthmother to be an aspect of Chauntea, she was seen to be a partition of the goddess that had maintained a wild and neutral outlook from the ancient past. She was sometimes described as a more primitive facet of the agricultural goddess.

Children

The Earthmother directed several mortal servants, known as her "children". She granted them with powers far beyond the usual for their kindred. As they were mortal, her favor did not prevent them from aging and dying, however if that happened, a successor would carry on their spirit. Within the Moonshae Isles, each of her Children were regarded as a demipower in their own right, and were sometimes said to be aspects of the goddess herself.

  • Leviathan, the Old One: a huge black-skinned whale tasked with protecting the seas around the Isles.
  • Kamerynn, the Unicorn: a great white unicorn tasked with protecting the woodlands of the Isles.
  • The Pack: a gathering of all the wolves of the Isles tasked with inflicting the violence needed to maintain Balance.

Worshipers

The Earthmother was worshiped among the Ffolk and especially by her druids, who maintained sacred Great Groves centered around moonwells, pools of water tied to the Earthmother's essence through which she could observe and impact the world. Her druids ascribed to the belief that good and evil both could cause harm to the natural order, and so sought to keep both in Balance. They protected her sacred sites and facilitated both the births and deaths of all creatures in a manner intended to please their goddess.

Some small temples to the Earthmother existed, although they were often little more than caverns where seeds were stored.

The Earthmother was also venerated by many of the firbolgs of the Moonshaes as well as by the inhabitants of Sarifal.

History

The Earthmother was said to be as old as the Dawn War, and she had witnessed the separation of Toril and Abeir. In truth, the Leviathan arrived around , but fey had already been settling the isles since approximately . At this time, the isles were still forming and were wracked with volcanic upheavals, however things would calm down and the isles would assume their final shapes within about 500 years of the Leviathan's arrival. The fey believed that it was their gods who had brought this about.

The Earthmother's next child to arrive was Kamerynn, circa . Recognizing that both the Leviathan and Kamerynn represented benevolence and wisdom, the Earthmother acknowledged a need for Balance and so she summoned her final child, the Pack, representing natural savagery. Again, legendary histories would claim that all three children had settled the Isles before the coming of the first animals and even of the Llewyrr elves, however the Moonshaes were already home to the kingdom of the leShay before Kamerynn's arrival and to the elves by the time of the Pack's arrival. When the dwarves first arrived circa , the Earthmother did not notice them until they first left their underground homes, seeming to have simply sprouted into existence.

Around , the Beast known as Kazgaroth made its first appearance on the Moonshaes, and again, the Earthmother was unsure from where it had come. However, she knew that it threatened the Balance, and so she aided her children, the elves, and the dwarves in fighting it off alongside its evil minions.

The Ffolk would not arrive on the Moonshaes until the , but by the mid–5 century DR, they exclusively worshiped the Earthmother. and they were credited with developing the potent sanctify sacred site spell.

When Bhaal corrupted a moonwell in the , turning it into the Darkwell and beginning to corrupt the Isles, the Earthmother's strength began to fade, which prompted Chauntea to lend her aid. It would be two decades before the Earthmother's presence was felt again in the .

With the advent of the Spellplague in the , the Earthmother immediately intuited the coming conjunction of Toril and Abeir, and sought to warn her children.
