Genie worship

Genie worship was the practice of venerating or worshiping geniekind, usually supplanting real deities, whether out of adoration or fear.

Description

As planar beings of immense arrogance and possessing powers approaching that of the gods themselves, the greatest of genies satisfied their innate sense of supremacy by dominating lesser genies and mortals (any and all of them). This resulted in them obtaining palaces or realms worth of servants and slaves devoted to glorifying them, both in their native elemental planes and the Prime Material plane.

History

The genies Calim and Memnon built worshipful empires in the lands that would become Calimshan, the Calim Empire, and Memnonnar. With courts of lesser genies, they lorded over cities full of subservient humans and halflings that knew no god or power but their genie lord. This system lasted from about to , when the two genies and their courts were trapped in the Calimemnon Crystal and their empires fell apart.

Genie worship became fashionable and popular during the Erehnir and Vahlen dynasties of the later Calimshan Empire. From , they sponsored the widespread adoration and veneration of geniekind, as the horrors of Calim and Memnon were long faded and they thought of genies as a historical curiosity rather than an existential threat. The Erehnirs exclusively worshiped genies, while the Vahlens merged this with the worship of the various deities related to the sun and sky. These dynasties were great patrons of the arts, and genie-related imagery became common elements expressed throughout society, such as in clothing and architecture. They also constructed hundreds of shrines and temples devoted to the gods and genies they worshiped, though most genie "holy sites" were repurposed to proper deities once the fad had passed. The greatest of these temples and monuments were in the heart of the Calim Desert, as it was believed that they could better hear their worshipers from there.

Despite the eccentricities of the Erehnirs and Vahlens, for most of Calimshan's history the worship of genies was nigh-unthinkable, as the wars, slavery, and tyranny that Calim and Memnon inflicted upon the population created a powerful cultural aversion to the concept. Genies were regarded as a terrible danger to be avoided, contained, destroyed, or exploited.

In the 14 century DR, the Firestorm Cabal emerged, a secret society of genasi supremacists that sought to free and reinstate the former genie overlords of the realm. Their wishes came true in a fashion when, after 1385 DR, the Spellplague broke the Calimemnon Crystal and set the two genie lords loose with their servants. While the lands did come to be dominated by their rule and the genasi became a favored upper caste, the cost was the desertification and physical destruction of almost the entire region, the deaths of the majority of the native inhabitants, and the enslavement of the remainder. Though Calim and Memnon vanished once more in the , it took several more decades for their genie and genasi servants to be overthrown by their slaves.
