Erich Schmidt

Erich Schmidt was a member of the Role-Playing Group Association (RPGA) who authored a number of modules for the Living City organized play program involving planes, most notably the first three modules of the eight-part High-Level Planar Campaign series, and was part of its staff of organizers.

History

In 1998 Erich Schmidt was made a member of the Living City's board of organizers, named in charge of High Level Planar Campaign Development. This was later announced in the opening pages of Polyhedron #129. Schmidt remained on the board following Brian Burr's takeover and restructuring of the Living City program in 1999, becoming an organizer for planar plots in general within the campaign board's staff of plot organizers.

Works

  • Kuril's Tomb (June 12, 1998)
  • A Ticket to Midnight (May 28, 1999)
  • Three Coins in a Well (August 5, 1999)
  • Stone and Steel (November 1, 1999)
