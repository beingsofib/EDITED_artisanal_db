Officially Unofficial History of the Circus of the Last Days!, or Officially Unofficial Circus History for short, was a book dedicated to the history of the planar Circus of the Last Days from the Feywild, penned sometime before the late 15 century DR.

Content

The book collected the history of the circus and its last entry was the last time the Circus of the Last Days made a stop in Baldur's Gate before the . During that visit, the performances were headlined by someone called Dumpy Dumpling, who had been performing for a decade, combining firework farts and chicken-snapping, as well as his famous pie-in-the-ear trick. Despite the crowd cheering on, Dumpy lost his spark and passion for performances. One day, during a show, he called on a volunteer. A sly man with reddish-hair stepped onto the stage. The volunteer seemed to have charisma enough to inspire pure joy with his command of the stage. He caused riotous laughter with his one-liners and more with his witty comebacks. Not just the crowd was mesmerized by the redheaded volunteer, so was the book's author. When the show was over, the writer forgot that he was watching Dumpy Dumpling's show and not the red-headed man's.

Lucretious, the proprietress of the Circus of the Last Days quickly realized the volunteer's star quality and recruited him into her troupe. She offered him a job right there on stage to the crowd's thunderous applause. This was the origin of Dribbles the Clown, Norris Greenie.

History

In 1492 DR, a copy of Officially Unofficial History of the Circus of the Last Days! was sold by Nansi Gretta in her book store, The Bibliophile, in the Lower City of Baldur's Gate.
