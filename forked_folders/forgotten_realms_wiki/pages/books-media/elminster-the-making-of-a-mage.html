Elminster: The Making of a Mage is the first book in the open-ended Elminster series.

{{quote|In ancient days, sorcerers sought to learn the One True Spell that would give them power over all the world and understanding of all magic... The One True Spell was a woman, and her name was Mystra—and her kisses were wonderful.

– Priest Halivon Tharnstar, Tales Told to a Blind Wizard

It is the time before Myth Drannor, when the Heartlands are home to barbarians, and wicked dragons rule the skies. In these ancient days, Elminster is a shepherd boy, dreaming of adventure and heroics. When a dragon-riding magelord sweeps down upon him, though, the boy is thrust into a world of harsh realities, corrupt rulers, and evil sorcerers.

With patience and grit, Elminster sets about to change all that. The result of his labors is a world reborn and a mage made.

The origins of Elminster, mightiest mage in Faerûn, have long been lost in the wash of time. Only the old wizard himself remembers how he came into being, and he hasn't told. Until now...}}

Summary

Prelude

Lord Mourngrym is ushered by Lhaeo to Elminster Aumar's study in his tower, but doesn't find him there, or in his spell chamber, or even in his bedchamber, wondering where the wizard might've gotten himself to, and what his childhood may have been like.

Prologue

The sorceress Myrjala Talithyn, loved by the people but abhorred by the nobility, takes the form of a herald to access Morlin Castle, reminiscing about the great damage that came to Halangorn Forest recently. After the gate guards usher the false herald inside, tumult follows.

Part I: Brigand

While watching sheep, a young Elminster Aumar, from the village of Heldon, is accosted by a mage riding on a dragon, who demands to know about Prince Elthryn and enspells him. The boy reveals the appearance of Elthryn, but neglects to mention that he himself is Elthryn's son; the mage's spell makes him hurl himself down a cliff, but he reveals some innate magic, while out of sight of the wizard, in slowing his fall. The mage rides his dragon into town and slays almost the entire population, striking down Elthryn. By night, Elminster then comes into town and takes his father's shattered blade, but is later discovered by several bandits while resting at a nearby cave; after insulting their honor, he learns they are the Knights of Athalantar, now become outlaws who reject the rule of Belaur Aumar. Their leader, Helm Stoneblade, explains Elminster the history the royal Aumar family of Athalantar, warns him to never reveal his own name, and reports on the situation of the Magelords of Athalantar who rule in Belaur's name. Elminster means to avenge his father and mother, and Helm warns him it is a lifetime's work, then counsels him to go west to the Horn Hills, and that he may find succor among brigands if he tells them Helm sent him.

In the , by the Hammer of Winter, he has become part of a band of brigands led by Sargeth, and is taking part of an ambush on a squadron of armsmen of Athalantar, successfully slaying all twelve. The armsmen retreat to a hideout once dug out by dwarves they have come to call Lawless Castle. In reprisal, the Magelord Kadeln Olothstar, in consultation with the swordmaster at Sarn Torel, sends a force of twenty armsmen and twelve mages in a sledge. A patrol finds Lawless Castle, forcing the bandits to vacate; Elminster runs out, and battles more of the patrol's forces, being left for dead. He gets up later, slays the crossbowman pinning the bandits down at the entrance, and rides away on a horse.

While pondering his next move, he is accosted by a magelord's projected image who overhears him calling himself Prince Elminster and taunts him. As he gets underway to assault the armsmen's camp, he discovers a skirmish where Helm Stoneblade is fighting other armsmen, and uses captured crossbows to aid them. They meet up, and after explaining, Elminster recommends Helm run down the outriders to ensure the location of Lawless Castle stays unknown to the Magelords, rather than help him slay the magelord who has discovered his identity. Elminster manages to sneak up on camp later, and strikes down two magelords, but is almost overwhelmed by the warriors before Helm turns up again to bolster him. Agreeing the Magelords will not let such a string of defeats slide, Helm suggests Elminster try and spend the next season in Hastarl rather than out in the wild.

Elminster sneaks into the stable at Woodsedge Inn, where he sleeps, only to be attacked in his sleep by Magelord Caladar Thearyn, only to be saved by the innkeeper Broarn, who axes the magelord from behind, slaying him. Broarn, too, counsels him to go to Hastarl, where they won't be looking for him. Helm arrives, revealing he's taken a sleigh after twenty armsmen and some magelords came in it to attack Lawless Castle; Elminster once more states he'll spend the season in Hastarl, counseling Helm do the same.

Part II: Burglar

By early summer in the , Elminster has instead become a thief, and is aided by his fellow Farl in stealing from a rich merchant. Elminster successfully endeavors to steal a bejeweled pectoral from his sleeping wife, but later decides it is too risky to sell. At a later point, at the Kissing Wench tavern, Elminster tries to acquire the services of a coinlass Shandathe Llaerin, but is interrupted by the wealthy dandy Jansibal Otharr; the dandy almost orders his guards to sword Elminster, but is interrupted by another dandy, Thelorn Selemban, a rival. The two dandies almost sword each other, but a guard captain puts a stop to that. Elminster then decides to prank everyone, knocks out Jansibal while he's with Shandathe, convinces Thelorn that she's unsatisfied after he left her, knocks him out when he makes it to her booth, robs some of their valuables and leaves the two knocked out men, sneaking Shandathe into the bed of a local baker, Hannibur, to keep her out of the fallout. Hiding away as a precaution, Farl and Elminster weigh whether their next thefts should be from local sex workers, priests, or rich merchants; they agree on the merchants, and split to scout out two different merchants.

Farl passes himself off as a hired server at a rich merchant's house during a revel, while Elminster eavesdrops at a different banquet. At the banquet Elminster is eavesdropping, he discovers the magelord Neldryn Hawklyn, who Farl knows has unknowingly fathered him, who uses the Crystal Chain of Binding to imprison the Magister of Mystra, Dorgon Heamiiolothtar; however, when he tries to keep the Magister from speaking his mind, he challenges him, and the Magister accepts his challenge. Neldryn, many magelords, and a few guests are killed; he then looks upon Elminster, and they talk briefly. Dorgon realizes soon Elminster isn't one of Neldryn's men, and asks him if he'd like to become a wizard, which he denies. The host's armsmen then come upon them, and pursue Elminster, cutting their conversation short. Spell-slaying magelords leave a strong impression on Elminster; during their next theft, they are interrupted in their taking by members purporting to belong in a new gang that calls themselves the Moonclaws.

By late fall the Moonclaws get in the way of another theft, this one while Elminster and Farl are trying to steal silks in the port. The two recruit other unaffiliated thieves into a gang of their own, the Velvet Hands. Afterwards, Elminster and Farl, now part of the Velvet Hands, are cut off by the Moonclaws one last time, while thieving the bedroom of newlywed nobles Peeryst Trumpettower and Nanue Glarmeir; the struggle is cut off when Darrigo Trumpettower, the groom's uncle, comes into the room attracted by the noise and helps hurl away all the thieves. Elminster is disappointed and tired of stealing after realizing that the greatest prize in the room, a stag carved out of a single diamond, has been smashed to splinters in the struggle, and decides he ought move on, perhaps seek magic.

Part III: Priest

Elminster breaks into a local temple of Mystra and is soon caught by a Magelord whose wards protect it; the Magelord takes note of Elminster's magical potential, and offers him becoming an apprentice; Elminster refuses. However, his imminent execution is cut short by the appearance of the goddess herself, who propositions Elminster learn more of magic. He accepts, and immediately faints. He wakes up a woman, being informed by Mystra that he has become "Elmara" to learn about magic and that there is a teacher in this part of the forest. Raising a kindling flame to pray to her, she is caught by Baerithryn, an elven mage who prays to Mystra. He becomes her teacher, and they spend two seasons together before he decides she's ready, and sends her to the temple at Ladyhouse Falls, where Mystra, in the new guise of a crone, bids Elmara explore the Realms and learn from wizards without becoming or calling herself one.

She comes into conflict with a mage in Mlembryn, who refuses to believe that she has truly seen Mystra, and wants word of magic suppressed. He attacks her as she leaves the inn, and she strikes back at him; rather than kill him, she follows Mystra's commandments, and asks him what mage would best teach of magic. Even under a spell forcing the truth out of him, he tells her the most meaningful answer would be that of Ilhundyl, the mad mage of Calishar. She seeks out Ilhundyl at his Castle of Sorcery, who requests the surrender of Ondil's Book of Spells at Mystra's Dance. Elmara leaves to fetch it.

Part IV: Magus

By Marpenoth in the , Elmara has joined the Brave Blades adventuring band since before last year's winter; they explore the Floating Tower, where she slays a beholder, a mind flayer a red dragon, and a dread, recovering Ondil's Book of Spells before the way behind them is transformed into a path that can only lead further into the tomb. There, one by one, they're slain by Ondil's magic, until only Elmara and Tarthe Maermir are left to face Ondil; she defeats the lich, but then Undarl Dragonrider suddenly appears, slays Tarthe, and tells Elmara he knows she's Elminster, before hurling her explosively out of the window. Still under the effect of a flight spell and a protection against fire, she barely survives instead of falling to her death; Undarl loots Ondil's magic instead of pursuing her. She takes the book to Mystra's Dance, where it is taken to Ilhundyl by a lich. She later faints after a sign, and wakes up on the beach, with the word "Athalantar" scratched on the ground next to her.

She heads for Athalantar, but in Narthil, discovers the town's been taken by armsmen. Frightening them with a spell, she goes to a tavern, where she is accosted by some magelords, and slays one of them. By morning, prince Gartos of Athalantar has come, and she slays his horsemen, his accompanying magelord, but barely survives his enchanted sword; she only lives because an innkeeper slays Garthos, helpless after her attack. Mystra teaches Elmara the secret to converting her own latent magic energy into healing, and she rises, ready to flee into the hills. Unknown to her, Garthos' enchanted sword is flying after her.

As it turns into Flamerule of the , Elmara goes to Ambletrees Arms, where she learns Baerithryn has asked the innkeeper to watch for her. After leaving north for Far Torel where armsmen have been gathering for what is likely a foray against the elves, she is assaulted by the flying sword again, and destroys it with her own magic. At Far Torel, she employs a spell that rusts much of the armsmen's implements, which by including arms and armor, prevents the attack. On her way back to Athalantar, she hides at the granary of a farmer, who decides to aid her. On a later date, at a farm she comes across, armsmen are fighting Myrjala Talithyn, a sorceress; Elmara aids her, downing all the armsmen, and Darrigo slays a surviving armsman as he tries to kill the two of them while they retreat.

Elmara teleports Myrjala to a cave near Heldon, but discovers she won't get up even with the wounds closed. She heads over to a temple near Heldon, discovering a logging camp, and magelords on it; she kills one magelord to steal his healing potions. Myrjala, once recovered, agrees to apprentice Elmara. She has Elmara talk to a Netherese phantom, Ander; she then has her restore a stretch of land from the Haunted Vale to the Darthtil, a task that takes a year. Afterwards, by the , Myrjala admits she has little more to teach, and bids her go forth, grow in power alone. Elminster decides to slay Ilhundyl, and casts a spell from Ander, which turns her back into a man, explaining to Myrjala he is in truth Prince Elminster. He goes to the Castle of Sorcery alone on the , but fails to defeat Ilhundyl on his own; Myrjala rescues him and eliminates the Mad Mage at the last moment.

By Mirtul of the , Elminster is still slaying evil wizards outside Athalantar, girding himself for war. It isn't until Eleasias of the that Myrjala declares him ready.

Part V: King

By Eleasias of the Year of the Chosen, 240 DR, Elminster and Myrjala have been thwarting Athalantan envoys and decided it is the time to strike. They meet with their elven allies of the High Forest, who agree to help, but won't do so for a head-on attack. The elves also require proof of workability by slaying Taraj Hurlymm, a magelord; Elminster slays him, but to his aid comes Kadeln Olothstar, whom Myrjala slays. The elves agree it's not perfect proof, but accept it. Then Elminster meets with Helm Stoneblade, whose knights have since grown desperate, and so are willing to strike down the Magelords.

Elminster turns Magelord Seldinor Stormcloak's golem against him, and this warns magelord Ithboltar their attack has begun. He alerts the other Magelords. Then Elminster meets with Farl and Tassabra, who have since become rich merchants, but are still open to leading the Velvet Hands to attack the Magelords. His delay makes the Magelords dismiss Ithboltar's warning. Tassabra liaisons with Helm's knights, and the attack proper begins. Elminster and Myrjala, with the help of their allies, slay the Magelords one by one, save for Undarl Dragonrider; finally, the prince challenges king Belaur, and prevails by slipping the broken Lion Sword from under the king at the last moment, getting knocked out in the process. He is protected even then by Hannibur, who takes the dead king's sword to fend off the courtiers.

Elminster then crowns Helm Stoneblade as king, rather than rule by himself; Myrjala proves to be far older than they thought, and finally, the two mages ride off into the sunset. Just as they wonder where Undarl might be, the horse they rode on cries out he is Undarl, and launches a surprise attack, slaying Myrjala and harming Elminster badly with his spells as he morphs into a tentacled sphere. Elminster manages to stick the sphere with the Lion Sword, bypassing his protections with another lightning spell, and mourns Myrjala, crying he will forevermore forswear magic. She asks him to never say that again, and reveals she has been an avatar of the goddess Mystra all along.

Next morning, a cheese merchant and his wife witness something along the road to Hastarl: behind a hill, they see a sphere of silver light where the naked figures of what appear to be a man and a woman are enjoying themselves. The scare leaves them much work picking their cheeses back up after accidentally losing control of the cart.
