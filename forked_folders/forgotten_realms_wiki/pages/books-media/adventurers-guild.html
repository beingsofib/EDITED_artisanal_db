Adventurer's Guild

The Adventurer's Guild was an organized play program run by the Role-Playing Group Association (RPGA) for the 2 edition of Dungeons & Dragons between 1997 and 2000. Each module was intended to tie in with a larger adventure or sourcebook.

Product History

On October of 1997, the RPGA proposed partnering with TSR's creatives to launch a retail-level league play system, which would be out by January 1998. This system was to provide special tournaments not available at conventions to retail stores. Each season of play would last for six months, through which the adventures would be staged in 2-week intervals. Stores would be able to run two or four adventures per month, as desired, with players accumulating points by play; those points would then be redeemed in the form of special invites to events.

By December, the advertised seasons were finally settled on 3 months, while the adventures would be delivered with a fresh one every 2 weeks. Every season would cost $20, providing six novelty tournaments that featured new products related to up and coming game lines. Each adventure was to be sanctioned for a 2-week period, to encourage visiting the game store often. On April 1998, all players who played eight or more quests in the Adventurer's Guild were allowed into an event where a unique module, "Quest for Marvin" by Andrew Rothstein, would be played.

On January 1999, a number of the 1998 adventures were gathered in TSR Jam 1999. On December 1999, it was announced that the modules would no longer be charging an entrant fee to play, and would be delivered as PDFs for GMs to print instead of by mail. Every month, the RPGA would also send a new adventure that was keyed to an upcoming product; that adventure would contain characters and all needed to be ready to play.
