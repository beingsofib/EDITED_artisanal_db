Glacier's Edge is a novel by R.A. Salvatore, the second book in the The Way of the Drow series.

Summary

Prologue (circa 1490 DR)

Jarlaxle sits alone beneath the Qadeej Glacier, freezing in a chamber of ice. His companions—Zaknafein, Catti-brie, and Artemis Entreri—are entombed in magical stasis, defeated by a mighty slaad lord. As he endures isolation and bitter cold, Jarlaxle clings to memories of Callidae, the society that helped him reject the cruelty of his drow heritage. Far away, Drizzt finds peace at the Monastery of the Yellow Rose, treasuring time with his daughter. In Menzoberranzan, Yvonnel Baenre contemplates rising tensions and an approaching war. All wait in patient torment, each holding to fragile threads of hope.

Part 1

Menzoberranzan descends deeper into turmoil as House Baenre consolidates power and rival factions plot their rise. Dininae, once Dinin Do’Urden, partners with Voselly, a fierce former drider, to forge the Blaspheme into a united force. Together, they defend against ambushes and prove their worth on the battlefield, haunted by the scars of their past lives. Yvonnel Baenre, burdened with the memories of her ancient predecessors, grapples with longing for true connection and questions the legacy of Lolth. A fragile reconciliation with her mother, Minolin Fey, gives her purpose: to reshape Menzoberranzan’s future beyond fear and cruelty. But Matron Zhindia Melarn and Mez’Barris Armgo scheme in the shadows, their ambitions threatening to ignite full civil war. Baenre forces strike decisively, seizing Donigarten to isolate House Hunzrin and crush resistance. Despite early victories, Yvonnel’s miscalculation allows Zhindia to orchestrate a brutal raid on House Fey-Branche, upending the fragile balance of power. As alliances fracture and betrayals mount, Menzoberranzan spirals toward all-out conflict, with honor, loyalty, and joy hanging by a thread.

Part 2

Beyond the Spine of the World, Jarlaxle escapes his icy tomb, fighting through undead and frost giants in a desperate bid to free his frozen friends. Realizing he cannot succeed alone, he journeys to Callidae, seeking help from the secretive aevendrow. But their leaders, fearing exposure, erase his memories, leaving him lost and confused in the wilderness. Meanwhile, Drizzt trains at the Monastery of the Yellow Rose, struggling to reconcile the feral Hunter within with his disciplined monk self. A gnoll attack forces him into a brutal test, pushing him toward balance. Azzudonna, torn between grief, duty, and love for Zaknafein, remains silent about Callidae’s secrets, even as Bruenor and his allies search for answers. Tensions mount at the Ivy Mansion as Jarlaxle is found, his memories fragmented, deepening the group’s despair. With their friends’ fates unknown and time slipping away, the heroes must face hard choices between caution and the perilous hope of rescue.

Part 3

Menzoberranzan descends into full-scale war. Matron Mez’Barris Armgo and her handmaidens summon demons to stoke fear, while Quenthel Baenre struggles to hold her city together. Blaspheme warriors battle the abyssal tide, earning respect and dread in equal measure. Dininae’s true identity as Dinin Do’Urden is exposed by Aleandra’s desperate betrayal, giving Matron Zhindia a valuable prize for her schemes. The city fractures further, with houses turning on one another and ancient grudges erupting in bloodshed. Zhindia’s hunger for power and the chaos sown by Lolth’s followers leave Menzoberranzan on the brink of collapse, a city devouring itself as its people forget all but hatred, survival, and ambition.

Part 4

Jarlaxle regains his stolen memories through a harrowing mind-flayer ritual, learning his friends are likely still alive. He rallies Bruenor, Drizzt, Regis, Azzudonna, Gromph, Pikel, and others for a desperate rescue. Drizzt, guided by Grandmaster Kane and two copper dragons, masters the harmony between his Hunter instincts and monk training through grueling trials. In Callidae, Galathae leads strike teams against polar worms threatening the city’s survival, their victories hard-won and costly. The heroes launch their assault beneath the ice, battling frost giants, slaadi, undead, and Ygorl, the slaad god. Grandmaster Kane sacrifices himself, becoming light to weaken Ygorl, while Pikel summons Qadeej’s cante to banish the god. The cavern is sealed, the price of victory steep. Through loyalty, sacrifice, and courage, they prevail—but not without heartbreak.

Epilogue

The heroes retreat, rescuing their frozen friends and mourning Kane and seemingly Pikel. But Pikel returns, bearing a magical ice arm, guiding them home in a celestial chariot. Together, they journey to Callidae, marveling at its hidden harmony. Beneath the northern lights, they reflect on sacrifice and the promise of new beginnings, united in purpose and hope.
