The Imaskari Book of War was a book on military tactics, magic, and other matters by an unknown author purportedly originating in ancient Imaskar. It had two volumes.

Scholarship

The original text was lost well before the 14 century DR; instead, only translations into Common and Dwarven existed. One translation was made by Alaphondar, Sage Royal of Cormyr.

Availability

A copy was kept in Candlekeep around 1372 DR and another in Suzail, Cormyr.

History

Princess Tanalasta Obarskyr once read Alaphondar's translation. When Vangerdahast banished a demon in the , she was reminded of something she'd read in it: priests were better suited to dealing with demons than wizards, as they were less likely to let their pride get them killed.
