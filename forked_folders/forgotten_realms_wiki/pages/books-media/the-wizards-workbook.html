The Wizards' Workbook, also known simply as The Workbook, was a famous spellbook containing original spells devised by some of Faerûn's most notable wizards.

Description

There were no reliable accounts of The Workbook's appearance, although it was believed that it was a bound volume with no inscriptions on the cover.

Contents

Accounts of the book's contents varied, leading some to speculate that more than one so-called Wizards' Workbook had been created.

History

The Workbook (or maybe Workbooks) had been compiled in Myth Drannor sometime between the , and the . The spells were curated by a powerful archmage known as the Mentor Wintercloak, leader of the famed Seven Wizards who taught at the Incanistaeum school of magic. He personally tested the spells produced by the school's pupils, and included those that met with his approval into The Workbook. Those who received the honor of having a spell added to the book were said to include Archveult Tattercloak, Caligarde, Laeral Silverhand, Spendelarde, Tasirin, Tulrun, and potentially Khelben Arunsun or the Mentor himself.

The Workbook eventually found its way into the hands of adventurers who sold it to the sage Flamsterd of Waterdeep. The sage grew rich by copying and selling several of the book's spells, and used this money to buy his own island in the Moonshae Isles where he moved with his apprentices. While living there, he copied the entirety of the book into his personal diaries, which he then shared with his good friend Elminster.

In the , Flamstered became fed up with his squabbling apprentices and, seeking peace and privacy to continue his development of new spells, he transported his tower and the surrounding penninsula to the bottom of the sea in a great eruption that left many (including Elminster) believing he was dead. A common theory was that a former buyer of his spells had deduced that he held the full Wizards' Workbook, and had killed him for it. The Workbook was likewise believed to have been destroyed.

Flamsterd would not reemerge for several decades, and in the meantime, Elminster saw fit to share the secrets of a number of the spells that had been shared with him.
