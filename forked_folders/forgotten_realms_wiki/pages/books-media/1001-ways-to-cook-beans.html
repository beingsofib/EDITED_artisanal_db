1001 Ways to Cook Beans was a cookbook penned by the eccentric street vendor Hephtos, more commonly known as Friar Cookpot, in the late 14 century DR.

Description

1001 Ways to Cook Beans, along with Cobblers and Pies: Baked Beans Can Be Fun, were collections of colorful and overly-complicated recipes, most of which contained beans and grains. The books' author was commonly seen cooking and serving his recipes, as well as selling copies of his books, across the street from Ravens Bluff's Open Air Farmers Market, outside the city in Tent Town, or in the neighborhood of Seaglimpse just outside the Gates of the Watchful Raven.
