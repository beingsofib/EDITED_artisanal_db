Dungeons & Dragons: The Tyrant’s Eye is a pinball machine, based on the Forgotten Realms setting, that was designed & manufactured by to coincide with the 50 anniversary of Dungeons & Dragons. It was released in 2025.

Description

The Tyrant's Eye cabinet was produced in three variations: Pro, Premium, and Limited Edition. Each is visually distinguished by different decals that were hand-drawn by illustrator Vincent Proce. The Pro cabinet depicts the game's adventuring company facing off against Xanathar and his pet Sylgar, the Premium cabinet depicts the dragonborn Ardor riding a gold dragon against the red dragon Rath the Relentless, and the Limited Edition depicts Tiamat and Xanathar. The latter is also distinguished by having a reflective foil treatment on its decal.

All three cabinets depict the same playing field, an abstract map of Mid-West Faerûn, featuring a number of previously established locations like Arabel. It also features hazards in the form of a gelatinous cube, which traps balls through use of an electromagnet, and the dragon Rath the Relentless. Rath's head is a mechanical fixture with illuminated eyes. In the Premium & Limited Edition cabinets, Rath's head features multi-axis motion, and omnidirectional impact sensing technology, granting it a range of motion and the ability to react to the player's actions. In the Pro Edition cabinets, Rath's head features only vertical movement.

The cabinets also feature a wide cast of voice actors, with over 11,000 recorded lines of speech.

The most distinct of the three versions is the Limited Edition cabinet, of which only 740 will be produced. In addition to its distinct cabinet decal, the LE cabinet features a plaque denoting its numerical designation among the 740 produced, the Speaker Expression Lighting System, an upgraded sound system, a full-colored mirrored backglass that was inspired by dragons, a red finish to the body of the cabinet, an autograph on the bottom arch by the cabinet's designer, an anti-reflective quality to the playfield glass, and a shaker motor. Beyond the cabinet itself, purchasers were given a signed Certificate of Authenticity.

Summary

An adventuring company known as the Dragonshield Guild fights to thwart the goddess Tiamat's latest scheme for arising on the Prime Material, which has triggered "the War of Dragons", all the while trying to interfere in the machinations of notorious threats Balinor, Sammaster, and Xanathar.

Gameplay

Going beyond the typical pinball experience, The Tyrant's Eye features three campaigns, playable by up to four players. At the onset of the game, players can select from four distinct race/class combinations with predetermined character statistics. These statblocks include the attributes from Dungeons & Dragons, hit points, armor class, a luck percentile, and four item slots.

  • Anya the halfling rogue; receives extra luck & treasure
  • Ardor the dragonborn paladin; does extra damage to dragons
  • Helenir Weavhart the high elf wizard; shield stays active for longer
  • Frey Curlyfoot the dwarf cleric; does extra damage to undead

Three additional characters can be unlocked by beating each of the game's campaigns.

  • Goregath the goliath barbarian
  • Juniper Amari the wood elf bard
  • Rhyme the tiefling ranger

At the onset of each game, pressing the Right flipper selects the character, while pressing the Left flipper selects what location on the map to start at. Plunging the ball will start or resume the campaign with the currently selected character. When resuming a saved campaign, a location can't be selected, instead you automatically start in the last city visited in the previous game session.

Besides the two flippers, there is a central action button that can be pressed to temporarily raise a metal bar between them. Labeled as "shield", this serves to prevent the loss of balls.

Through either adventuring or visiting the game's store, Fizmo's Shoppe, and spending accumulated gold pieces players can obtain access to inventory items.

By hitting Dungeon targets on the playfield or paying 75 gp to Fizmo's Shoppe, players can open up the "Dungeon Crawl". In the Premium & Limited Edition versions, this takes the form of a grated trapdoor or scoop. By shooting a ball over the "Dungeon Crawl" space or into the trapdoor, players access dynamically generated dungeon levels. In the Premium & Limited Edition versions, this trapdoor holds the ball until a dungeon is finished. There are a total of five dungeon levels that change out on a weekly basis. These levels, as well as animated combat encounters and character stats, all play out on the cabinet's LCD screen. The difficulty of encounters scales with the player as they progress and level up.

Fighting enemies during an encounter involves shooting at certain red lights on the playfield. Whether or not an attack connects with an enemy is randomly determined by a dice roll. Initiating combat with Rath requires hitting the three bank targets under the red dragon that represent his treasure horde. This gives players the opportunity to amass gold pieces. In the Premium and Limited Edition models, Rath's head moves from left to right during combat and unleashes multiple balls from its mouth. All versions refer to this as the "Dragon Multiball".

Quests and game modes at each location are denoted by inserts, numbered 1 through 3, with a border colored either blue, orange, purple, or white. By traveling between cities and completing the color matched modes numbered 1 and 2, players unlock the level 3 mode of the same color in either Dragonspear Castle or Gauntlgrym.

Tiny's Dice Game is a timed, points-based mode in the game where the LCD screen displays the members of the Dragonshield Coster gathered in a tavern to play a game where three dice are shook around in a mug and covered before being revealed. To play one must shoot a ball through the spinner directly above the "Dungeon Crawl", situated under a sign labeled "Tiny's Tavern". Doing so will add a number of additional points to the player's score based on the value of their dice in the game mode. The value of a player's dice can be raised by shooting a ball over the "Dungeon Crawl" (or into the scoop) and the time of the mode can be extended by shooting balls over one of three adjacent arrow paths that are lit up purple.

Using integration with Stern's Insider Connected app platform–a system originally created for their Venom cabinet (2023)–copies of the The Tyrant's Eye are able to save and load a player's accumulated experience points, inventory items, and campaign progress across play sessions. For players wanting a more traditional pinball experience, each game can be started without saved progress.

History

The Tyrant's Eye was developed under the codename "ROPE". It was first teased by Stern on December 31, 2024 and received a full detailed announcement the following year on January 3. The Limited Edition soon sold out.
