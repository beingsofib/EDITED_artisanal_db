Den of Chaos is a short story by Amber Scott for Baldur's Gate: Siege of Dragonspear. The story was published in The Familiar 1.

Summary

Some time in the 1360s DR, Schael Corwin, a member of the Flaming Fist mercenary company, is on a temporary assignment in the Wood of Sharp Teeth with her new partner, a cheerful but clumsy half-elf wizard named Zeri. Their mission is to recover stolen alchemical mushroom samples, belonging to the noblewoman Lady Teritha.

Corwin's tracking leads them to a bandit camp. As they prepare to attack, Corwin recognizes the bandits as her former associates from before she joined the Flaming Fist, led by a man named Derrion.

Despite her history with them, Corwin attempts an arrest. The situation erupts into a fight. During the battle, one bandit, Liina, smashes a box containing the volatile alchemical ingredients to spite Corwin. The mingling gases transform Derrion into a horrific, melting slime that attacks everyone. Corwin, Zeri, and a terrified Liina are forced to work together to destroy the monster with sword and fire.

In the aftermath, a shaken Corwin accepts Liina's surrender. They salvage what remains of the cargo and begin the trek back to the Flaming Fist fortification with their prisoner. The story ends with a quiet moment underscoring the mission's cost and Corwin's growth into a more nuanced individual capable of mercy and understanding the complexities of the wilds.
