The Black Bouquet by Richard Lee Byers is the second novel of The Rogues series.

Summary

In the city of Oeble in the Border Kingdoms, Aeron ser Randal steals a strongbox from a caravan, although the heist goes awry when they encounter magical wards. His companions are killed by the Grey Blades, the law in the city, and Miri Buckman, a ranger from the Red Hart guild hired to deliver the box. Aeron returns to his father, Nicos sar Randal, a retired thief who urges Aeron to choose a different life, although Aeron insists he wants to stay a thief and needs the money to support Nicos’s injuries and infirmities. He was hired to steal the box by Kesk Turnskull, the tanarukk who leads the Red Axes, the largest gang in the city, but decides to renegotiate their deal due to the severity of the protection and the loss of his friends.

Seftris Uuthrakt, a monk of the Dark Moon, travels to Oeble to hunt the book, as it was stolen from a temple of Shar. Miri hunts for the book without success, although she attracts the attention of a club owner named Melder, who puts a bounty on her enslavement. Aeron confronts Kesk, realizes that Kesk planned to kill him upon delivery of the box, and flees. Sefris summons an arcanoloth, who uses divination to determine that she will find the item inside the box, which they refer to as The Black Bouquet, by finding Miri.

Kesk meets with a mysterious merchant wizard, who hired him to obtain the Bouquet, and explains the complications. Miri is attacked by a group of slavers but rescued by Sefris, who claims to be a servant of Ilmater and partners with her to hunt for the book. Aeron brings the box to Burgell, a gnome wizard, to dispel the wards on the box, but realizes that Burgell has sold him out to the Red Axes, and instead steals some magical tools and flees. Miri and Sefris are able to learn Aeron’s name.

Aeron uses the stolen tools to open the box, finding an old tome inside. However, he is illiterate, and unable to see why the book is important. Miri and Sefris find Aeron’s home and witness Nicos being captured by Kesk. Sefris convinces Miri not to interfere with the kidnapping and suggests they capture Nicos instead to trade for the book. When Miri is alarmed by the suggestion, Sefris tires of the facade and attacks her, sending her and the balcony falling off the edge of Aeron and Nicos’ apartment and leaving her for dead.

Sefris meets with Kesk and convinces him to turn over the book to her instead of his original client. Aeron brings the Black Bouquet  to a scribe, who tells him that it is a formulary for different types of perfumes. Aeron then returns home, discovering Nicos’ absence and Miri’s injured body. He brings her to a shrine of Ilmater for healing. Miri awakes and negotiates an uneasy alliance with Aeron, agreeing to help him save Nicos in return for the Black Bouquet. She also reveals that the Black Bouquet is the lost formulary of Courynn Dulsaer, a famed perfume maker whose remaining bottles are worth thousands of gold, and that someone who used the book to start manufacturing new bottles would be guaranteed incredible wealth. She explains that the book was uncovered by Lord Quwen of Ormath, who arranged to sell it to a rich merchant in Oeble, although she doesn’t reveal the merchant’s identity.

Aeron and Miri begin sabotaging the Red Axe’s operations. The merchant wizard who hired Kesk to steal the book visits him to join the search for the book, but Nicos reveals that Kesk sold him out for Sefris. The merchant forces Kesk to choose, and Kesk severs his alliance with Sefris, attacking her, although she escapes.

Aeron and Miri negotiate with a rival gang to continue the attacks against the Red Axes while letting the pair take the blame. Then, as most Red Axes are out on guard, they sneak inside the mansion and attempt to save Nicos. They are detected and attacked by the Red Axes, and Sefris arrives, joining the battle to try to capture Aeron. Ultimately, they are forced to flee without Nicos, and Sefris is driven away as well.

Kesk openly declares his intention to execute Nicos by midnight if the book is not delivered. Aeron seemingly betrays Miri, capturing her and delivering her to Melder. He then partners with Sefris, offering her the book if he helps save Nicos.

Aeron and Kesk meet and make the exchange. although both plan to kill the other afterwards. Sefris attacks as Aeron and Nicos flee, knocking out Kesk and forcing his ally, the merchant-wizard, to flee. Aeron lures Sefris away to an alley where Miri is lying in wait. Aeron’s seeming betrayal and enslavement of Miri was a ploy, in which Melder and Miri cooperated, to take Sefris by surprise, as neither Aeron nor Miri felt they could defeat her otherwise. Together, they are able to kill Sefris,

Aeron and Miri confront the wounded Kesk, but due to Miri’s influence, Aeron decides not to kill him. Miri delivers the book to Dorn Heldeion, the merchant who originally hired her and who hopes to legitimize Oeble. She then arrests the merchant-wizard who hired Kesk, Oriseus Forar. Aeron leaves Oeble with Nicos, revealing that he stole some pages from the center of the book before giving it to Miri, which they can use to support themselves.
