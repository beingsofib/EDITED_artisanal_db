One-Eyed Jax: A Forgotten Realms Adventure is a short story written by R.A. Salvatore and published by Audible Originals on June 3 2021.

Summary

Beniago Baenre, disguised as High Captain Kurth, reports to Jarlaxle at the One-Eyed Jax that another merchant ship has sunk near Luskan - this one from Waterdeep with a veteran crew and protected from most hostile magic. The survivors of the crew were found by Brevindon Margaster's people who said that nobody saw what caused the destruction save what could have been a silhouette of a ship in the sea mist, and that metal had rained from the sky to destroy their vessel.

Having already rejected wizards being responsible, Jarlaxle travels to Bleeding Vines via Gauntlgrym to meet Regis whom he asks to pose as a gambler in Luskan and spy for him, giving him back his old ruby pendant, which Regis believed had been lost forever. As he is making his way to the portal to Luskan, Regis is joined by Athrogate and Thibbledorf Pwent, who are to act as Regis' escort, but are bickering about the role of captain of Bruenor's guard, which was promised to Athrogate but given to Pwent after his recovery from vampirism. Regis enters the portal meeting Artemis Entreri on the other side. The dwarves had already gone on ahead to One-Eyed Jax, while Entreri took Regis to Ship Kurth to learn his role.

Regis would be 'Lord Regis' of Bleeding Vines, recently come into a lot of money purloined from Dagult Neverember's hoard of gold embezzled from Waterdeep during the Demon War, which while not totally true, was true enough to be widely believed. A series of high stakes games of poker are played and Regis has to work out what his opponent's skill levels are, discouraged from using his pendant by Jarlaxle. The tournament goes on for many hours until only Regis and one other opponent, a gnome woman known as 'Coins', is left and he has to use his pendant to persuade her to stay and exchange more of her money for chips. Eventually Regis is outplayed and Athrogate and Pwent stage a distraction, which is a little too real as their mutual frustrations boil over. However, it allows for Entreri to get a look at the gold that spills from the sack of a man identified as Coins' moneychanger when she went to get more chips. Entreri gets the man drunk and he admits that much of the gold that Coins has given him recently is from Calimport, damaged, and wet. The destroyed ship was known to be carrying Calimport gold, but this is not quite enough evidence for Jarlaxle. He is however interested in the ship Coins came in on, which flies the flag of Lantan, a nation Jarlaxle has recently visited, investigating a new type of boat that he'd heard the Gondians were developing there. Jarlaxle decides that he needs Regis to go on a dive into Luskan's bay to infiltrate the Lantanna ship anchored out there from beneath the sea's surface. Regis successfully infiltrates the ship and discovers an unusual machine belowdecks which he quickly discovers is a multi-barrelled cannon that he disables using a cannon ball and sovereign glue, he then goes into an adjoining room containing many treasures, including, to his surprise, Kozah's Needle. The crew become suspicious when they hear noises and he's confronted by several sailors and is almost shot by a smokepowder pistol. Regis escapes the ship and makes his way back to Ship Kurth, where the Deudermont's Revenge is quickly arranged to sail out after the Lantanna vessel. He informs Entreri that Kozah's Needle is onboard and the fact that Coins has a cannon is enough to convince Jarlaxle that her ship is responsible for the sunken merchant vessels. Captain Bonnie Charlee tries to outmaneuver the enemy for a broadside attack, but Regis tells them to sail straight at it since he'd disabled the cannon, and true enough, when the cannon is fired, it backfires, igniting their smokepowder reserves and destroying the ship with all hands.

The Deudermont's Revenge sails into Mintarn many days later, Artemis Entreri disembarks alone, seeking Dahlia Sin'felle's killer, a gnome priest named Ibanabard who has a tower on the island.

Jarlaxle studies the plans of the cannon in Illusk, brought to him along with Ibanabard's head by Entreri that morning, but throws them into the lit fireplace. However, in his desk drawer are the plans for the cannon's targeting mechanism as well as a submersible boat that Coins had used to retrieve the loot from the sunken wreckage of the ships she had destroyed. Once constructed, it's first task would be to loot Coins' ship for any other technological wonders that may have survived.
