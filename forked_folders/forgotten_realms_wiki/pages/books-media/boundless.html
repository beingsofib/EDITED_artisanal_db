Boundless is a novel by R.A. Salvatore, the second book of the Generations trilogy featuring Drizzt Do'Urden. The book was released on September 10th, 2019.

Summary

Prologue (1488 DR)

In the Year of Dwarvenkind Reborn, Regis races to Waterdeep through demon-infested forests, pursued by fiends and desperate to warn of a coming invasion. Entrusting his companions to defend the halfling town of Bleeding Vines, a valiant stand unfolds. Doregardo escapes to alert others while Showithal sacrifices himself. Lady Donnola and Clan Battlehammer lead an evacuation to Gauntlgrym, defended fiercely by Pikel and Ivan Bouldershoulder. Regis narrowly escapes a glabrezu with help from Yvonnel Baenre, while Drizzt Do’Urden rescues Doregardo. As the last tram flees, the darkness of war tightens its grip on the world above and below.

Part 1 (1118 DR)

In Menzoberranzan’s twisted beauty, Zaknafein and Jarlaxle rekindle an old friendship through rivalry, races, and revelry at the Oozing Myconid tavern. But beneath the merriment, House Fey-Branche plots Zaknafein’s death. Jarlaxle’s magical eye reveals Duvon Tr’arach in disguise, leading to a dramatic tavern duel. Zaknafein survives with style, bolstered by Jarlaxle’s quiet interference. The politics of the duel ripple outward—Arathis Hune rigs bets and seeks revenge, and Dab’nay, entangled in schemes and affections, manipulates from behind the curtain.

Zaknafein trains obsessively, unaware of deeper plots. The duel ends in victory, though foul play nearly changes its course. Duvon lives, spared at Dab’nay’s request, but betrayal festers. Zaknafein, suspecting treachery, clashes with Jarlaxle, whose priorities favor profit over principle. Zaknafein soon discovers Dab’nay's true motives when she paralyzes him—only to reveal a passionate entanglement instead. Their affair complicates the web of deception further.

As Dab’nay implicates Arathis in cheating, Zaknafein faces the growing realization that he's a pawn in a larger power play. Everyone—including Jarlaxle—plays their own game, but Zaknafein, master of the blade, begins to sharpen his focus beyond duels and into the heart of Menzoberranzan’s rotting core.

Part 2 (1488 DR)

Matron Zhindia Melarn leads a hellish alliance toward Gauntlgrym, bolstered by traitorous dwarves and abyssal retrievers. Inside the city, Bruenor Battlehammer readies defenses while Drizzt, Jarlaxle, and Zaknafein prepare to meet the horde. Explosives, magic, and steel clash in the tunnels as Gauntlgrym’s fate teeters on a blade’s edge.

Elsewhere, Waterdeep trembles. Regis, Dahlia, and Entreri battle bureaucracy and demons, while discovering the true threat: the Margasters, noble hosts to demonic horrors. Dahlia’s mission leads to a harrowing rooftop chase; Entreri is captured, cocooned, and tormented. Dahlia and Regis regroup to mount a rescue.

At sea, Wulfgar duels a cambion-possessed Brevindon Margaster, barely surviving as Luskan burns. In Gauntlgrym, battle intensifies with spider demons—massive retrievers designed to kill Drizzt and Zaknafein—forcing desperate decisions and divine prayers. The stakes escalate as Matron Zhindia’s plan reveals deeper backing, perhaps from Demogorgon himself. The scattered Companions of the Hall begin to converge on a single truth: the war isn't just for territory—it’s for the soul of Faerûn.

Part 3 (1118 DR)

As Zaknafein and Dab’nay share rare vulnerability, betrayal simmers. Dab’nay, cornered by Matron Soulez, lures Zaknafein into a trap, forcing a deadly duel with Arathis Hune. Yet Jarlaxle and Kimmuriel intervene, exposing the manipulation. Dab’nay begins to unravel, guilt and fear taking hold.

Dinin Do’Urden watches Zaknafein’s skill with awe and resentment, while Dab’nay faces new pressure to kill him. She hesitates at the brink—ambition at war with affection. In House Ben’Zarafez’s fall, Zaknafein is pushed to his limits, showing mercy even as Menzoberranzan revels in cruelty. His restraint hints at a changing soul, even as darkness surrounds him.

An ambush in a shadowed alley nearly ends his life. Betrayed again, this time by Arathis and House Armgo, Zaknafein fights side by side with his rival to survive. Uthegentel’s monstrous presence almost claims him, but a last-minute teleportation saves him. Dab’nay, again caught in conflicting loyalties, is spared. The cost: Arathis lies dying, and Zaknafein walks away from Bregan D’aerthe and Jarlaxle, choosing solitude over survival by treachery. Menzoberranzan loses one of its finest swords—and perhaps gains a man unwilling to bow to Lolth’s games.

Part 4 (1488 DR)

Chaos reigns. Wulfgar, poisoned and wounded, is rescued by Kimmuriel and Bonnie Charlee as Luskan burns. In the Hosttower, Gromph confronts Matron Zhindia, choosing reluctant neutrality under divine pressure. Gauntlgrym erupts—Drizzt narrowly evades a retriever while dwarves die in droves. Bruenor prays for divine salvation.

In Waterdeep, Sharon Margaster—an eerie child with divine or demonic power—emerges as a terrifying force. Her mother Alvilda, possessed, nearly murders her. Regis and Dahlia storm House Margaster, rescuing Entreri from a demonic cocoon with Sharon’s eerie help. The girl watches them flee, calmly calling her monstrous twin “my friend.”

Elsewhere, the tides shift. Quenthel Baenre rejects alliance with Zhindia and unites Menzoberranzan under her banner. A drow army marches at last. Wulfgar accepts a final mission—to kill Brevindon and stem the tide. In Gauntlgrym, the forge becomes a battlefield as Zaknafein and the vampire-dwarf Pwent topple a retriever into lava. Drizzt sacrifices himself at Thornhold, luring another retriever away.

The companions win—but only just. Gauntlgrym is scarred, alliances are fragile, and Menzoberranzan’s true gambit is still unfolding. The war isn’t over. It’s only just begun.

Epilogue

As silence settles over Gauntlgrym’s bloodied halls, hope flickers but does not flare. The drow army looms, allies teeter on the edge of betrayal, and Faerûn’s greatest heroes are broken, scattered, or forever changed. Drizzt’s fate remains uncertain, lost beyond the known roads. Sharon Margaster, the child of shadow and mystery, smiles in the dark. A fragile peace hangs like mist—beautiful, but bound to vanish at dawn. And deep below, Lolth watches, ever hungry.
