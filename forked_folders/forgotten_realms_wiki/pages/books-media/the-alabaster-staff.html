The Alabaster Staff by Edward Bolme is the first book in The Rogues quadrilogy, formerly known as "The Thieves series." It is set in the Old Empires from 1358 DR to 1373 DR.

Summary

During the Time of Troubles, Zimrilim, high priest of Gilgeam, witnesses his god’s death at the hands of Tiamat.

Fifteen years later, the city of Messsemprar is under siege by Mulhorandi invaders. A street performer named Kehrsyn is gifted a pear by a boy, but a guard confronts her and accuses her of stealing the fruit. She injures him and is about to escape when a sorceress named Ruzzara arrives and kills the guard, framing Kehrsyn for the murder. She reveals that the guard was a member of the Zhentarim and threatens to turn Kehrsyn in to the guards and the Zhents unless she steals the Staff of the Necromancer.

Reluctantly, Kehrsyn agrees. Ruzzara tells her to steal the staff from the merchant house Wing’s Reach and deliver it to Eileph, a Red Wizard of Thay. While scouting out the location, she encounters followers of Tiamat, and Tiglath, the high priestess, takes a liking to her.

Kehrsyn steals and delivers the staff successfully but encounters Demok, a soldier in the employ of Wing’s Reach. He brings her back, where she meets Massedar, the merchant who owns the house, and his advisor Ahegi. Struck by Massedar’s kind and gentle treatment, she decides to steal the staff back for him.

Kehrsyn finds the hideout of Ruzarra’s organization, who she discovers are the followers of Furifax, the notorious rebel. Shortly after she arrives, the followers of Tiamat attack, accusing the Furifax followers of violating some deal made for the staff. While Kehrsyn hides, Ruzarra and the other followers are killed. Tiglath arrives later and finds Kehrsyn, realizing that this attack was carried out by her followers without her knowledge.

Kehrsyn finds the staff broken and brings it to Eiliph for repair, but he reveals that the broken pieces are actually from a duplicate he created. She continues searching for the staff, and reflects on her past. Her mother, Sarae, was lusted after by Ekur, a powerful priest of Gilgeam who had her father killed before she was born.

Tiglath realizes that her followers have been acting without her knowledge or permission, and comes to realize that, though she began worshiping Tiamat for vengeance against Gilgeam, Tiamat is just a different tyrant. Kehrsyn arrives to look for the staff and encounters Tiglath, but Tiglath confirms the staff is not there, realizing that whoever organized the attack on Furifax’s followers must have passed the staff on to a different party.

Kehrsyn reports to Massedar, who reveals that the staff is actually the Alabaster Staff, a powerful artifact. Kehrsyn suspects Ahegi of being a double agent for the Zhentarim, so she leaves him a note asking for a meeting, then follows him. Her suspicions are confirmed when he meets with other Zhentarim, but she also realizes that he is actually Ekur. Ahegi immediately orders she be killed to prevent her from revealing his secret.

Kehrsyn escapes but is found by Demok. She tells Demok what she has learned, and he passes the information onto Massedar. He orders Demok to kill Ahegi/Ekur, which Demok does by revealing himself to be a Harper, then using the moment of surprise to run him through.

Massedar uses speak with dead spells to learn that the Zhentarim was behind everything, using the followers of Tiamat and Furifax to steal the staff, then turning them on each other. He explains that the Zhentarim plan to raise an army of undead and conquer Unther for Bane, and the ritual will be held at the Temple of Gilgeam. Massedar, Demok, and Kehrsyn plan to infiltrate the ritual and recover the staff.

Kehrsyn talks to Demok about her past in the wake of Ekur’s death. She explains that her father was killed by Ekur a year before she was born, but Demok points out that this is impossible, as pregnancy takes nine months. Kehrsyn realizes that Ekur was her true father and is distraught, but Demok reassures her that her connection to Ekur doesn’t define her. He tells her about the Harpers and invites her to join.

The trio infiltrates the Zhentarim ritual. Massedar claims the Alabaster Staff, but reveals himself to be Zimrilim, and uses the staff to reanimate the body of Gilgeam, intending to use the dead god’s power to ascend to godhood himself and rule Unther. Kehrsyn is able to steal the staff by swapping it out with the decoy, causing Gilgeam to escape Zimrilm's control and execute his treacherous high priest. She and Demok flee and ask Tiglath for help.

Kehrsyn, Tiglath, and the followers of Tiamat confront Gilgeam as he is regaining his power. After a battle, Kehrsyn manages to thrust the Alabaster Staff into him, causing both deity and artifact to vanish. In the aftermath, Tiglath leaves the church of Tiamat and Kehrsyn returns to performing on the street.
