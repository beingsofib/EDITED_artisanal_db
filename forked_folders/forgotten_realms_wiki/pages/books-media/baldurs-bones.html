Baldur's Bones

Baldur's Bones was a dice game popular in many taverns across the city of Baldur's Gate in the late 15 century DR, including the likes of the Elfsong Tavern and Low Lantern.

Rules

At the beginning of a game of Baldur's Bones, each player would put an agreed-upon ante into a pot. Each player needed three six-sided dice in order to play. After everyone paid their wagers, players would roll their starting dice and then play would proceed clockwise, with the host of the game intended to always go last.

On a given turn, a player could choose to roll or "stand", meaning they passed their turn over to the next player. If a player chose to "roll", they were given an additional die to roll. Once the roll was made, the player would once again be presented with the two options of "stand" or "roll" until they made the decision to end their turn.

The objective of the game was to have the highest numerical total on one's collective dice, with the highest numerical value allowed being 21. If a "roll" resulted in a collective value higher than 21, the player was considered to have "bust" and be out of the game. The player with the highest total at the end of play would win the ante pot.

Notable Baldur's Bones Players

  • Brinks
  • Bruno Indomicus
  • Tarina, a former pirate who retired to the city of Baldur's Gate, was an avid player of the game and frequent cheater, playing games at the Elfsong Tavern.
