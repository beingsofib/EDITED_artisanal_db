Development

Webtoon is a Korean-American webcomic platform focusing on the Korean mobile-friendly webcomics known as (utilizing very long and narrow images designed to be read as they are scrolled down a smartphone screen). Brie and the Borrowed Blade was to be written by Purpah (of Webtoon comic Suitor Armor) and illustrated by Ryan Maniulit (a contributor to something called Dream Daddy: A Dad Dating Comic Book). Maniulit had no prior experience of Drizzt nor D&D before being "scouted" for the project because he offered a "fresh perspective", but had become "definitely a fan" after working on it with R.A. Salvatore.{{cite web

The webcomic was announced on the "D&D Enters the World of WEBTOON – An Exclusive First Look!" panel at San Diego Comic-Con in 2023, where example art was presented and reported in press on July 23. It was said to be "coming soon" and to be released in 2024, in time for D&D's 50 anniversary. More details were promised for the near future.

Despite this, nothing further was reported, nothing can be found on the Webtoon site, and nothing was released even two years later. On September 13, 2024, artist Ryan Maniulit explained to a fan, "As of now it's uncertain. I had to step away for various reasons and I don't know what their plans are for the work I completed." He shared text-free sample strips.{{cite web

Story

According to Webtoon, "The series will see Brie (or Breezy) "borrow" her father's iconic sword, Twinkle, and embark on a journey to discover what it takes to be a hero."

Briennelle Zaharina, a.k.a. Brie-Zara, Brie, and Breezy is the daughter of drow ranger Drizzt Do'Urden and Catti-brie. New characters in the series are Quillian Harpell of the Harpell family; Merith Wickwyre, possibly a gnome artificer; and Gavric Belbarum, appearing to be an emerald dragon dragonborn with a prosthetic arm.
