Song of King Ethos was an exaggerated ballad about King Ethos and his enchanted ironwood mace, opened by the bard Duss Dorien sometime before the late 14 century DR.

Contents

The Song of King Ethos ballad sang about a good king who battled a powerful black dragon. In the fierce battle, the king lost his spear, lodged in the wyrm's thick hide. Desperate, Ethos called out to Torm for aid, and a dire mace materialized in his hands – the powerful enchanted ironwood mace. The king won by bringing the dire mace on the dragon's head with a satisfying crunch.
