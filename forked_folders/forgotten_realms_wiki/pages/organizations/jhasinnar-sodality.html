The Jhasinnar Sodality was a guild of female concubines known as jhasinnar in the city of Calimport, Calimshan, in the late 14 century DR.

Organization

Circa 1370 DR, it was led by Pasha Ralahn el Matyr (a man).

Base of Operations

The Jhasinnar Sodality had their headquarters in the Silkhanduq, a combined guildhall and khanduq in the city's Palace Ward.

They had complete control over a few festhalls in the city, the main one being The Sultry Siren in the Emerald Ward.

Activities

Under cover of its obvious pleasures, the guild engaged in criminal activity: Ralahn el Matyr used The Sultry Siren as a front for trading goods on the black market and fencing stolen jewelry. In addition, its secret passages into the Muzad undercity allowed pashas and sultans a discreet escape, so they owed Ralahn and the Sodality a favor for their lives or their reputations.

History

In the , the Trades Conflagration blazed across northern and central Calimport. The Sultry Siren was damaged, but calling in some favors, Ralahn had it repaired in less than a month.

Relationships

Muham yn Nasim el Pesarkhal, Sultan of the Crypt Ward, had a favorite jhasina from the Sodality. (In truth, she was Voraya, a lamia noble based in the Muzad and exerting hypnotic control over him on behalf of the Twisted Rune.)
