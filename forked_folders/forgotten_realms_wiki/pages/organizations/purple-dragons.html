The Purple Dragons were the standing army of the kingdom of Cormyr and the elite military force of the realm with a reputation to match, being renowned for their strength, and for their heroism and acts of valor in the field of combat. They were also occasionally called the Cormyrian Army and the Purple Dragoons.

Possessions

A typical Purple Dragon wore field plate armor and a steel helmet. Over this, they wore a tabard with the symbol of a purple dragon. Higher-ranking members were allowed to wear their family's crest on their uniform or shield as well. They could fight with any melee weapon but most often were armed with long swords.

Their uniform around 1335 DR include white tabards emblazoned with the Purple Dragon, the symbol of the realm.

Purple Dragon Knights and officers often wore enchanted rings that permitted War Wizards to track the location and scry them. Removing such a ring without good cause them to teleport in with battle-spells at the ready.

Membership

Enlistment

All loyal Cormyrean citizens were eligible to join the Purple Dragons by taking an oath. Although the oath was available to non-humans provided they were Cormyrean citizens, extra measures might be imposed on them to ensure their loyalty, owing to the fact that Cormyr was predominantly human. The common folk of Cormyr comprised the bulk of the Purple Dragons' ranks, and farmers would volunteer without delay during times of conflict or crisis. Meanwhile several noble houses had long traditions of service, including the Hawklins and Truesilvers. Whether common or noble, it was considered a great honor to serve in the Purple Dragons.

Nobles were actively expected to take the oath, and would generally be given positions of leadership. Non-nobles would form part of the militia with an initial service of two years. After the two-year service, the Purple Dragon chose to continue their role, or return to their former life.

If answered with affirmation, another interview followed, joined by a War Wizard (who might remain hidden). If the Purple Dragon had requested new responsibilities or locations, the interview came all the sooner. The War Wizard employed mind-reading magic (not as harsh as mind reaming) to confirm the applicant's acceptance of the upcoming assignment. As the command officer was not around for this interview, this presented an opportunity to ask the Purple Dragon about their contentment with the assignment, inquire about re-assignments (not guaranteed), or let them comment on their conditions.

In times of conflict, anyone who had left after the above-mentioned two years could be called back into service.

Training

The first six months consisted of an extensive training regime before starting active duty, such as patrolling Cormyr's borders or guarding its cities and towns.

Training could take one of three paths, including basic combat training, leadership training, or training in the protection of key personnel, often War Wizards.

Retirement

It was advisable to communicate one's intended departure to a War Wizard, often with the profession of divine dreams that preached pacifism. The War Wizard may not have believed such claims, but they would be unlikely to object or admonish such claims openly.

Barring an urgent need for their service (such as floods or dragon sightings), an amicable debriefing soon followed. The Purple Dragon informed their superiors of pending work or otherwise important matters, and was provided with sufficient food, equipment, and coinage. Ever since the Royal Magician Caladnei and Filfaeril Obarskyr, great care was placed into not straining relationships, as disgruntled Dragons could find themselves in bed with Sembia or disloyal nobles.

Purple Dragons that served for a cumulative 12 years or more could retire as "grayswords". They qualified for being healed, cured of diseases, and getting any missing limbs restored. A test for lycanthropy followed (with treatment, if needed). Grayswords could bear weapons without need of peace-bonds or charters. Grayswords were offered the choice between a pension of 6 gold lions a month, and a "glint" or "pepper" pension of 2 gold lions a month along with a land grant. Such lands were usually along the Sembian border and coast, or along the High Road facing the Tunlands. This served the crown well, for the civilization and protection of such areas was desired.

An early retirement as a graysword was rare, but was granted if the Purple Dragon had undergone severe trauma, or grew sick of their work after dispensation of noteworthy deeds.

If the Purple Dragon lacked discipline (but was loyal to Cormyr or a good combatant) or the Crown had too many Purple Dragons, an honorable discharge could follow. A lump sum payment of 10 gold lions was provided, no honor was considered lost, and the tenure served could be counted towards the aforementioned 12 years for an eventual full retirement.

If subjected to dishonorable discharge or an outright dismissal, a Purple Dragon faced exile or deprivation of any pension and respect. This was usually the case when the crown did not wish to punish them, or could not validate their wrongdoings.

Re-Enlistment

In principle, it was possible to "wear the Purple" again. Frequent enlistments and departures from the Purple Dragon ranks, however, were looked upon with suspicion.

Such aspirants had to approach a standing garrison larger than a mere watchpost or waypost. A War Wizard and an officer would then ask why the former Purple Dragon wished to serve again. Given sufficient cause for suspicion, the War Wizard employed mind reaming to ensure no blackmail, magical coercion, or subterfuge was at play. The applicant was asked where and how they wished to serve once more, but their assignment could very well be different.

Generally, they were assigned to one rank lower than they'd had when they left (unless they were a Blade, the lowest rank). After a period of good service (usually a "season"), they found themselves at their original rank and salary.

Numbers

The Purple Dragons numbered over 12,000 soldiers circa 1367 DR, stationed across all the realm's cities and fortifications.

Organization

Ranks

The Purple Dragons had specific titles for the members of their army, listed here from lowest to highest:

  • Blade: Equivalent to a private
  • Telsword, or simply "Sword": Equivalent to a corporal.
  • First Sword: Equivalent to a sergeant.
  • Swordcaptain: Equivalent to a lieutenant.
  • Lionar: Equivalent to a captain.
  • Ornrion: Equivalent to a major.
  • Constal: Equivalent to a colonel.
  • Oversword: Equivalent to a major general.
  • Battlemaster: Equivalent to a lieutenant general.
  • Lord High Marshal: Equivalent to a general.

A leading position was Warden of the Eastern Marches, based in Arabel.

Other titles and positions included:

  • Roadcaptain, scout

Knighthoods

The Bodyguard Royal were tasked with protecting the royalty of Cormyr and consisted of some of the most premier Purple Dragons.

The Knights Royal and Highknights would also often find themselves in their close proximity.

Classes

Members of the Purple Dragons were typically fighters, but paladins and rangers were also possible, and occasionally bards, clerics, and rogues. as well as warlords.

Purple Dragon knights were often top commanders and officers, though this status was not necessarily tied to rank. These mounted warriors were skilled in leadership and directing forces, able to rally troops or demoralize foes. Purple Dragon knights could cross-train as paladins of Helm or Lathander. They maintained a code of honor and chivalry and took oaths to fight villainy and uphold justice and serve the kingdom. Some Purple Dragon knights were permitted to quest beyond the borders as royal envoys or as knights errant, taking their fight to the enemy.

Relations

The Purple Dragons often worked in tandem with the War Wizards on the battlefield, at checkpoints, and in patrols. The War Wizards aided the Purple Dragons with the use of magic in combat and in the late 1400s DR they were familiar with fighting alongside swordmages.

A recurring enemy was the Zhentilar, the army of Zhentil Keep. They'd fought several times before 1369 DR. Similarly, the Purple Dragons regularly disrupted the efforts of the Zhentarim spy network to control smuggling and trade. Primarily, however, they operated indirectly, arranging for local threats to harry Purple Dragon patrols and thwart missions, with the aim of keeping them and King Azoun IV busy and distracted in the lowlands between the Storm Horns and the Sunset Mountains and unable to expand beyond or assist settlements in the Western Heartlands, as well as simply to spite them.

In the 1400s DR, however, the Purple Dragons stopped at nothing to track down and expose Shadovar spies in the capital.

History

Precursors

Cormyr was once part of the woodland empire of Cormanthyr, where elves and dragons fought for dominance. The most prominent of these was the black dragon Thauglor, who was so old that his scales had acquired a purple hue. After the Cormyreans later defeated Thauglor and took the land for themselves, the purple-tinged scales of Thauglor would inspire the name of the Purple Dragons.

In the 420s DR, the Purple Dragons were originally an adventuring company comprising several Cormyrean nobles: Elvarin Crownsilver, Glorin Truesilver, Jotor Turcassan, Omalra Dracohorn, and Dintheron Bleth. When the kingdom collapsed after the treacherous Melineth Turcassan sold the city of Suzail to the pirate lord Magrath the Minotaur in the , the Purple Dragons remained loyal to King Duar "Longyears" Obarskyr. They formed the core of a rebel army under the king's command, together with Amedahast and a dwindling number of rank-and-file soldiers following them. They strove for three long years to retake Cormyr from the pirate lord and the traitors that had allied themselves with him.

The increasingly ragtag band had great difficulty retaking the nation, even though they had the full support of Cormyr's commoners. Then, in the , another loyalist unit managed to take High Horn and the soldiers inside agreed to pledge themselves to the king if the Purple Dragons could win a single victory by themselves. Duar and Amedahast chose the remote Dheolur, the hold of one of the traitorous noble families. With the help of a farmer named Dhedluk (whom Dheolur was later named after), and Threena Cormaeril, the group stormed the main building. Magrath was being entertained there at the time, which allowed King Duar to kill the minotaur. This dealt a crippling blow to the traitors and earned the Dragons the allegiance of High Horn's troops, who helped to quickly bring the realm under Duar's rightful control.

Despite this, the royal army was named for the Purple Dragon banner flown by the realm and the crown.

Early History

When an immense horde of orcs and goblinoids emerged from the High Moor in the , King Proster dispatched a legion of Purple Dragon Knights to join a grand alliance to face them. But the great battle they fought ended in mass slaughter, creating the notorious Battle of Bones.

In the , Regent Salember refused to abdicate in favor of Prince Rhigaerd II, naming himself the Red Dragon King. In response, Rhigaerd mustered the Purple Dragons under his own banner to battle Salember's loyalist forces, dubbed the Red army. Salember would be ultimately slain by his own High Mage during parley.

Duke Bhereu Ammaeth commanded the army as Lord High Marshal by the early 1350s DR.

Similarly, in the , the so-called "Lost King" Gondegal assembled a mercenary army and launched raids across the kingdom, capturing much territory and reigning briefly as king from Arabel. Gondegal and his mercenaries proved capable and more agile than the Purple Dragons, able to move swiftly, react faster, and strike without warning. Meanwhile, the majority of Cormyr's army had been stationed in High Horn, ready for an external threat, not an internal one, and hence struggled to muster sufficient forces that could challenge Gondegal. The Purple Dragons also made fatal missteps; en route to a strategic location, the three-dozen-strong Starburst Swords unit elected to avoid the Stonebolt Trail, which was threatened by Gondegal's Twilight Brigade, whom they could've handily defeated. Instead, whether out of laziness or cowardliness, they took a safer path to get their target sooner. Unchallenged, the Twilight Brigade assaulted, pillaged, and razed a small village near the trail, Darkenshield. However, once the Purple Dragons regrouped and formed a cohesive unit, they would retaliate in significant force. Even common farmers quickly volunteered to join the Purple Dragons in the face of Gondegal's threat. But many of Gondegal's troops broke and retreated in the face of the advancing Purple Dragons, pillaging as they left. A significant number, having depleted local food supplies, simply surrendered and even proposed joining the Purple Dragons in exchange for food and shelter. The majority, however, elected to fight, but in all the allied advances, not one major battle was fought and not one more than an hour long or seeing more than a hundred slain. The remnants of Gondegal's army scattered. King Azoun IV led his Purple Dragons on the march to Arabel, expecting a massed battle or lengthy siege but finding the Lost King gone and they entered Arabel with no battle or bloodshed at all.

Modern History

Bhereu continued as Lord High Marshal through 1367 DR.

In the , Cormyr deployed an army of Purple Dragons to Tilver's Gap in order to protect Tilverton from oncoming threats in the region, including marauding orcs, the Zhentarim, and the conquests of Lashan Aumersair. In Kythorn of that year, an army of thousands of orcs was destroyed while it besieged Tilverton, and the city shortly after agreed to become a protectorate of the Dragon Throne. The Purple Dragons then used the Gap as a secure base to operate from, striking forth and fighting the Zhentarim in Daggerdale and adjoining lands. Surrounding powers like the Dalelands and Sembia actually desired a greater Purple Dragon presence in Tilver's Gap in the following year.

Since the Time of Troubles in 1358 DR, the Purple Dragons twice joined forces with the Red Ravens mercenaries to clear the Helmlands of raiders and marauders, but orcs, goblins, and gnolls would return each time, as they had by 1367 DR.

To confront the approaching Grand Army of the Tuigan in the , King Azoun VI forged an alliance of the realms of Faerûn. The Purple Dragons traveled and fought alongside the armed forces of many nations and even former foes. King Azoun and the Purple Dragons were memorialized in the highly embellished Lay of the Purple Dragons.

Lord High Marshal Bhereu was assassinated in the Abraxus affair in the .

Lord Dauneth Marliir, the former Warden of the North, was appointed Lord High Marshal of the Purple Dragons some time between 1379 DR and 1396 DR, thanks to his strategic talent and ability to listen, to both his monarch and to his sword captains.

Post-Spellplague

Following the Spellplague, Cormyr annexed the city of Proskur on the Dragon Coast in the , and later made Teziir a protectorate. Between them, Purple Dragon 'lords' came to dominate the western Dragon Coast.

Then, after High Dale leaned toward Cormyr, Purple Dragon Knights advanced into the dale in the , to guard it against threats from Shadovar-ruled Sembia. Similarly, Cormyr loaned a Purple Dragon continent to Urmlaspyr in Sembia to guard the city and keep it free.

In the , the Purple Dragons uncovered evidence that a majority of the people of Wheloon were secret worshipers of Shar. Thus, King Foril decreed that Wheloon be transformed into a prison city, sentencing all those inside to be prisoners for life, sealed in by physical and magical barriers. The decision was made following unprecedented activity by worshipers of Shar in the city, as well as evidence of their alliance with Netheril.

Purple Dragons and War Wizards in Marsember worked tirelessly to halt the illicit trade in the drug known as traveler's dust, but without success as of 1479 DR. One theory speculated that some amongst their ranks were corrupt and in league with the so-called Spice Lords.

Tactics

Under Lord High Marshal Bhereu in the 1350s and 1360s DR, the Purple Dragons focused on guerilla warfare tactics, employing speed, mobility, and swift strikes on vulnerable enemy concentrations and positions. As most of their enemies employed the same tactics, Bhereu advocated this to avoid ambushes and outflanking. Although his critics considered this dishonorable, Bhereu felt ranks of soldiers facing off on selected battlefields and following traditional tactics invited catastrophe. But once he'd made his reputation and demonstrated greater efficiency, faster successes, and lower casualties, such tactics were accepted.

On battlefields, the Purple Dragons were rarely pitted against fortifications. Thus, they had little use for siege machines, and often relied on War Wizards' spells.

Purple Dragon officers maintained an environment of excitement by sending their subordinates on surprise missions. This was done to stymie inactivity, agitation, or general dissatisfaction—all of which could lead to mischief or misplaced loyalties.

An old tradition within the Purple Dragons involved speedy creation of "strike cavalry" units, and filling its roster with nobles who sought laurels but lacked temperance and experience in equal measure. These "special forces" served as excellent diversions and cannon fodder.

Activities

The primary duty of the Purple Dragons was to defend Cormyr. Although simple, they had many and varied ways to achieve this. These involved guarding the borders from key fortifications like Castle Crag and High Horn and watching for threats from the Stonelands, Goblin Marches, and Hullack Forest, which they regularly patrolled.

They were also required to keep the peace within the realm. This usually entailed preventing raiders and bandits from outside crossing the borders and harassing travelling merchants, farmers, and others, so it was fulfilled by guarding the borders. As Cormyr could boast generally safe travel within the realm, the Purple Dragons were considered to be highly successful at this. Nevertheless, they could not be everywhere nor catch all threats, so a random bandit attack was not impossible.

Another aspect of ensuring peace was keeping a close eye on adventuring companies, who were armed, had their own quests, and were just as dangerous as bandit gangs. Purple Dragons dubbed such folk "lawless freeswords" and tried to ensure they didn't cause trouble.

The Purple Dragons also had the task of enforcing the laws of Cormyr. In particular, they would enforce the peace-bonding of weapons, by spotting folk whose weapons were untied, stopping them, and explaining the law or examining papers of chartered adventuring companies and authorized mercenaries. Purple Dragons had the power to imprison those who violated this law and confiscate their property.

Finally, the Purple Dragons were obliged to quash uprisings and insurrections. This was tested but once, during Gondegal's campaign, when the Purple Dragons were considered to suppressed the revolt with bloodshed, when they retook Arabel.

Symbol

Their symbol was of course the Purple Dragon, which they shared with the crown, It took the form of a purple dragon on a black field and was displayed on a banner carried by soldiers.

Bases of Operations

They had their headquarters in the Citadel of the Purple Dragons in the capital city, Suzail. This also served as garrison for the city. They trained and drilled ceaselessly in the courtyard.

In the city of Arabel in the late 1350s and 1360s DR, the garrison numbered 2,020 men, including palace guard, road patrols, and surrounding waypost personnel. They were commanded by Baron Thomdor Ammaeth, Warden of the Eastern Marches]]. Answering to the baron was the Purple Dragon Dutharr, who served as liaison officer to the city watch and effectively chief of police. A militia would be mustered in times of conflict. These Purple Dragons made regular patrols of the Stonelands.

Meanwhile, in the city of Marsember, there was a 3,000-strong garrison, who doubled as police and customs officers and were specialized for port operations and lighter armor. Warden of the Port Ayesunder Truesilver commanded them.

In the village of Eveningstar in the late 1350s DR, a contingent of nine Purple Dragons led the local militia.

Following its occupation, Tilverton's garrison had been strengthened to 850 by 1358 DR. They made constant patrols of the surrounding land and the Stonelands for orcs and other threats. But after the town's destruction in 1372 DR, only fifty Purple Dragons remained stationed in the area, to prevent visitors entering the shadowy ruins.

In Thunderstone, there was a garrison of 100 Purple Dragons under Faril Laheralson around 1367 DR, though they had a reputation for rowdiness and troublemaking. The town was an annual launching point for patrols of the Hullack Forest.

At Castle Crag, around 500 soldiers served under Bren Tallsword circa 1367 DR. They guarded the Gnoll Pass against raiders from the Goblin Marches.

At High Horn, there were some 400 soldiers posted around 1367 DR to defend against incursions through the Stormhorns.
