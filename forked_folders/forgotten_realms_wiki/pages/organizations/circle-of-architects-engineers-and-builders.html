The Circle of Architects, Engineers, and Builders was a guild in the city of Calimport, Calimshan, in the late 14 century DR. It comprised experts in architecture, construction, and engineering.

History

Over the course of a few centuries, the Circle of Architects, Engineers, and Builders undertook an extensive vertical construction project for the most affluent areas in the city, those that would be delineated in the , as the Grand, Jewel, and Palace Wards. They rose all three by roughly off the ground, creating a slightly vaulted, stonework secondary layer of solid street avenues and courts, accessible from the city's true ground level and vice versa by means of sets of stairs. The engineers responsible braced all of this with a series of stone and timber supports, arranging them in such a way as to ensure that the upper streets would stay elevated even if half of their timber supports were broken.

This construction project was done with the purpose of providing the upper classes with more uninterrupted movement than the sabban-wall byways afforded, as well as to keep their servants and slaves, who were subsequently restricted to the under streets of their wards, out of sight and away from "dirtying" up their own streets.
