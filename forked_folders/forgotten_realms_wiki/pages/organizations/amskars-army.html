Amskar's army was a force assembled by the Shadovar Archwizard Amskar in the late 15 century DR for the purpose of invading Cormyr. Created in parallel to the Shadovar invasion of Wheloon, it was intended to deliver a deathblow to the kingdom.

Organization

Amskar brought a force from Netheril with him, made up of priests of Shar, arcanists, shadar-kai, and shades. They served as officers and overseers of the wider force, making sure that the auxiliaries followed orders and were generally effective.

The vast bulk of the army was made up of local mercenaries and allies native to the Storm Horns region. This included orcs, gnolls, trolls, and harpies. Most notable of them were ten clans of hill giants, eight clans of stone giants and three clans of frost giants. They even attempted courting the white dracolich Arricaxerxes. Local humans were recruited to hunt and poach in the foothills, acting as a screening force and distraction from the real threat. Amskar was endlessly frustrated by the poor performance of the creatures he had tricked, threatened, and paid to serve him, and considered them completely disposable so long as his goals were met.

These auxiliaries took heavy losses as they performed labor and guarded the area, falling victim to wild beasts like owlbears, hungry dragons, being used to bait harpies, and being tricked by satyrs.

Base of Operations

The greatest concentration of forces were at the Netherese central camp, a fortified base in the snowy heights of the northwestern Storm Horns. Further into the mountain was Rungnir's glacier, in which an ancient Netherese mythallar was buried, and from which Amskar's enclave rose. The rest of the forces were scattered about in the peaks and foothills in temporary camps and fortifications.

History

Around the time of the Shadovar invasion of Wheloon overseen by Archwizard Tyvrus, Archwizard Amskar created his own operation hidden in the Storm Horns mountains. He set about making deals with local and regional groups, recruiting large numbers of orcs and gnolls as footsoldiers and even many giants. Most important of all were the frost giants represented by Rungnir, whose glacier home contained the mythallar the Netherese sought. This growing army was held together by a delicate web of threats and promises, and was constantly at risk of collapsing. Secrecy was of vital importance, and every effort was made to ensure that Cormyr wouldn't learn of the threat massing inside their borders. By coincidence this was greatly aided by the Sschindylryn invasion of Eveningstar, as the Purple Dragons and War Wizards were fully devoted to fighting the drow armies and couldn't be spared to investigate the Storm Horns. The ultimate goal of Amskar's army was for it to garrison Amskar's enclave and have it fly across Cormyr in a surprise attack upon Suzail. With the capital taken by surprise, Cormyr would surely fall or at least be vulnerable to further attacks, such as from Sembia and Wheloon.

This fell apart when Oriphaun Huntsilver arrived in the area, fresh from stealing the Nether Scroll in Wheloon Prison and evading Shadovar pursuit on the High Road. Acting as an agent of the Harpers, he pursued clues that led him into the foothills and then the peaks of the Storm Horns. Although he was eventually captured by Amskar and Shai at Yarrow Glade, the wider Harper network had been alerted, with Kara Patwin and Lorant Tass taking the lead on the investigation. Elsewhere, the hunters Olyce Tidewater and Thanbard Krys stumbled into an orcish patrol, barely surviving to report it in Eveningstar. Another major stumble for the Shadovar occurred almost simultaneously, when a group of Amskar's orcs attempted a raid upon Eveningstar but were defeated in detail. While the magistrates and many others were willing to dismiss it as a random tribal raid, the high-quality gear from Netheril they carried were a sign that greater forces were at play. This led Rina the Keen-Blade to send a raid upon the supply convoys in Tumblerock Pass and Orin Arroway to hire a strike upon the Netherese central camp high in the Storm Horns, inflicting great losses upon the no-longer-hidden army. Renton Finne followed this up by sending a group to find the frost giant Rungnir, who had grown suspicious and intolerant of the Netherese squatting upon his glacier. Though he was driving into hiding by his former allies, the Cormyreans managed to help him defeat Archwizard Tharno, causing his whole clan to join him in rebelling against the Netherese.

Despite the losses and setbacks, the activation of the mythallar succeeded and Amskar raised a crude Netherese enclave from the raw ice of the glacier. A final strike from the Harpers reached the glacier and rescued Oriphaun, only to find themselves aloft in the sky as the glacier flew south. They fought a desperate battle across the enclave and managed to critically damage its ability to fly while slaying Amskar, causing it to crash into a mountainside with the remaining army aboard it.
