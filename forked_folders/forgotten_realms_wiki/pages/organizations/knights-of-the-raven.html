The Knights of the Raven, also known as the Order of the Ravens was the most prestigious and elite knighthood in the city of Ravens Bluff,

Organization

The Knights of the Raven, like all knightly orders of Ravens Bluff, were overseen by a civic body called the Knights Council.

Unlike other knightly orders, a Ravenaar knight could not squire for the Raven Knights. Candidates for the high honor of joining this order were selected from among the city's other knighthoods.

Entry Requirements

The prerequisites for becoming a member of the Order of the Ravens included having two-calendar-years of service in another knighthood, having 15 different knightly honors, to have completed some form of a quest, and to have two letters of recommendation. These letters could be from either other knights, city officials, or important city leaders. Finally, the candidate needed to petition themselves to the city's Knights Council, at which point their entire history as a knight would be reviewed, i.e. their Knight's Diary and permanent record.

In the late 14 century DR, the prerequisites for entry application to the Knights of the Raven were modified. Instead of simply 15 knightly honors, an applicant had to have 20. Instead of two letters of recommendation, one was needed from each of Ravens Bluff's knighthoods. Additionally, the requirement of owning a deed to Titled Land was added, as well as a restriction towards rogues.

On very rare occasions an individual could be made an "acting" Raven Knight for the purposes of undertaking an important task, like representing Ravens Bluff at a treaty arrangement after the diplomat responsible for it fell to an assassin. This appointment could be made by the head of the Order of the Ravens, as well as senior lord or city official. If the acting knight performed their duties admirably, there was a small chance they could be awarded a permanent place in the order. However, these "back-door knights" as they were called by some were looked down upon and viewed with suspicion by those who earned their place in the Raven Knights in the traditional manner.

Once an individual was approved for admission to the order, the public ceremony of indoctrination would be held, wherein the new Raven Knight had to recite the order's oath as it was administered by Ravens Bluff's mayor.

Duties

Members of the Raven Knights carried the responsibility of serving as commanders and generals in the Ravens Bluff Army in times of war.

Sub-Orders

White Ravens: A small suborder of crusaders that was founded following the Time of Troubles, they were notably the originators of the blade magic discipline White Raven. Its members were pledged to the deities Lathander and Torm. Their mission statement was to provide damage control in times of war and catastrophes.

Activities

The day-to-day goings on of these knights and their long term missions were comparatively far more clandestine and kept secret than those of the city's initiate and secular knightly orders.

Tactics

Amongst the Vast, a region that was a bastion of the art of blade magic, its use was notably quite prevalent among the Raven Knights.

Knightly Honors

All knightly orders of the City of Ravens were open to the acquisition of "knightly honors", objects that officially recognized and respected their accomplishments as knights. The Knights of the Raven had a number of special honors that only members of their order could be awarded. These included the following:

The Marshal's Rod: An honor awarded to Ravens Knights who, whilst serving as an army commander, decisively defeats an enemy army on the battlefield. This honor was always found awarded at major city festivals.

The Purple Cloak: An honor awarded to Ravens Knights who complete a personal mission of heroism, such as vanquishing a dragon that was posing a threat to the City of Ravens. In order to be eligible for this honor there had to be real threat of dying or even died in the process of accomplishing their quest.

The Silver Scroll: An honor awarded to Raven Knights who, whilst acting on a mission as an ambassador, successfully navigated a negotiation, treaty, or trade agreement beneficial to Ravens Bluff.

The Silver Coronet: The highest honor for a Raven Knight. This was awarded to those who defeated a major threat to the city, such as the manipulative machinations and intrigues of the Red Wizards of Thay, the Zhentarim, or the leaders of Hillsfar and Mulmaster. Prerequisites for eligibility included having received the prior three honors and owning an estate measuring at least . After receiving the award a Raven Knight had to undergo a thorough investigation by the Ministry of Art to determine if they had any ongoing alliances or affiliations that could be harmful to the city, whether or not they were secretly a or other disguised creature with their own agenda, or under the effect of any magical influences (charms, curses, geas, etc). Once the investigation was cleared, the Raven Knight would be offered the title of baron and a voting seat on the Council of Lords.
