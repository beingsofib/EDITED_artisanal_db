The Viperhands was a cult of fanatics in service to Zaltec in Nexal, Maztica in the mid–14.

Markings

Their hands were stained blood red with a ritual dye.

During the initiation ceremony, the heart of a sacrificial victim was cut out and the priest took the blood of the heart on his palm and pressed it against the chest of the initiate. When the blood contacted the skin, it seared the skin with a red brand in the shape of the diamond-head of a viper. Initiates were required to stay silent, even though this was very painful. Those who failed to remain silent during the initiation were immediately sacrificed to Zaltec.

The Viperhand mark on the palm of Hoxitl, high priest of Zaltec, was also called 'the Viperhand' and it contained magical power capable of making a 'Sending' by summoning a magical creature that could travel long distances with incredible speed. . This 'Sign of the Viperhand' was the ultimate authority within the cult of Zaltec.

Beliefs

Those within the cult swore eternal allegiance to serve Zaltec.

The Ancient Ones

The Ancient Ones were drow living in Mount Zatal who disguised themselves as supernatural beings sent by Zaltec. They manipulated and effectively controlled the cult of Zaltec in Nexal, and by extension the cult of Zaltec throughout Maztica. The Viperhands were also effectively under their control.

History

Sometime before 1350 DR the Ancient Ones had a prophecy about the future of Maztica. They foresaw that the death of Nexal's ruler Naltecona could usher in far greater dominance of Maztica by the cult of Zaltec, and by extension far greater dominance of Maztica by themselves. However, several pieces had to be put into motion in order to make this prophecy a reality. The Viperhand cult was one of these pieces.

They prophesied that when Naltecona died, the Viperhand would fill the void of power and take dominance over Maztica in Zaltec's name.

The cult grew in size greatly when the Golden Legion arrived in Maztica and began their invasion. In the past this cult had been limited in size, but warriors, priests and common people joined into it. Its membership was mostly male, but some women also joined. The cult was filled with hatred, especially towards the foreigners who invaded Maztica and thus became a place for Mazticans who wanted to resist the invaders. Naltecona felt uneasy about this development although he still attended Viperhand initiation ceremonies out of respect. The Ancient Ones used the power of their Darkfyre to influence this and cause the cult to grow.

The Ancient Ones planned for the Viperhand to unite Mazticans under their control in resistance to the invaders after Naltecona died. When the Golden Legion arrived in Nexal and Naltecona effectively surrendered the city to them, the Viperhands wanted to fight, but they were restrained by Hoxitl and Naltecona. Several Viperhands tried unsuccessfully to kill Cordell leader of the Golden Legion, and Cordell then responded by taking Naltecona as a hostage of the Legion to prevent any further trouble.

On the Night of Wailing, Hoxitl, under the command of the Ancient Ones, sent the Viperhands to the plaza surrounding the Palace of Axalt, where the Golden Legion were camped and held Naltecona prisoner. At that time, the Viperhands had grown to 25,000 members. As they gathered, many thousands of Maztican warriors from Nexal came to join them. They clamoured in the plaza below and Cordell brought Naltecona to the roof of the palace to calm them down. One of the Ancient Ones (who had been disguised as a member of the Legion) then assassinated Naltecona within sight of the crowd, who mistakenly believed that the Legion had just executed their leader. They then surged forward to attack. The Legion and their Maztican allies were almost completely obliterated in the overwhelming assault, but a few successfully made a breakout and attempted to flee the city. These events were arranged according to the prophecy in order to bring the Viperhand to hold power over Maztica.

As they attacked the Legion and their Maztican allies, during the battle they took many captives and brought them to Hoxitl who sacrificed them to Zaltec. Among these was Bishou Domincus, the Legion's cleric of Helm. At the same moment that he was sacrificed, a Couatl and the leader of the Ancient Ones both fell into the magical darkfyre focus inside Mount Zatal. This focus had supposedly been the cause of the rpckfire cataclysm that destroyed the underdark beneath Maztica centuries earlier, and it was also a form of drow magic that had been created in the time when the Ancient Ones still served Lolth. A huge explosion then immediately erupted from Mount Zatal. Lolth who had been very jealous of how the Ancient Ones had abandoned her to serve Zaltec, was able to manifest her power through the darkfyre to corrupt the ashes from the eruption and when the ashes fell on those who had been marked by the Viperhand, they were horrifically transformed. The common warriors became orcs, the eagle knights and jaguar knights became ogres, and the Viperhand priests of Zaltec became trolls. Hoxitl was himself transformed into a giant humanoid monster by the ashes. This transformation was also done with the power of Zaltec, as the growing bloodlust of his followers in these new forms gave him greater power.

These creatures then marauded through Nexal, killing everyone, including many Nexalan civilians. Qotal used his power to turn the water around Nexal to ice so that the survivors could flee. A body of survivors, including Nexalans, other Mazticans, and survivors of the Legion then gathered together under the leadership of Erixitl of Palul, the chosen of Qotal, who led them south from the city into the House of Tezca. Zaltec had grown with so much power as a result of the massive killing and death on the Night of Wailing and what followed, that he was able to manifest his own presence physically in the material plane. He became a massive stone statue in Nexal that was able to walk and move. Hoxitl, still sensing the will of his master, led the Viperhand south to chase the fleeing refugees.

They fought and failed to overcome the refugees in the desert, but their trolls pursued Erixitl to Tewahca, the abandoned city of the gods, where she tried unsuccessfully to bring Qotal back to the world. Zaltec's avatar arrived and prevented the ceremony from succeeding. Hoxitl, then feeling the will of his master, then led the Viperhand first back to Nexal and then on an eastwards march towards Twin Visages where Erixitl did a second summoning of Qotal. Zaltec's avatar marched before the Viperhand creatures for most of the journey. At Twin Visages, the Viperhand again battled Amnian, Payit, desert dwarfs and halfling allies. Qotal was successfully summoned and he battled Zaltec after the armies had already fought each other. However, Coton, priest of Qotal, seeing the devastation of the battle between the two gods, betrayed Qotal and sacrificed himself to send both of them out of the material plane again. After the two gods disappeared, the Viperhand also retreated.

Notable Members

Hoxitl, high priest of Zaltec in Nexal

Shatil, cleric of Zaltec and brother of Erixitl, chosen of Qotal
