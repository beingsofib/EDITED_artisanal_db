The Gleaners were a planar sorcerous society dedicated to hunting down and retrieving magic items from all across the multiverse for the purposes of study and safekeeping.

Organization

The organization of the Gleaners differed little from what one might expect of an arcane college, save for minimal interest in teaching. They were governed by a senior council of wizards who held the title of regent. Over time the number of regents fluctuated, but by the 14 century DR it numbered seven. Council members served terms, eventually being put back to duties of full-time research and item acquisition. The council decided policy for the organization and were responsible for both the storage and protection of all items brought in. A subcommittee of specialists worked with them to maintain security.

Two regents were required to be present whenever items were to be stored or retrieved.

Outside of the council there was a great body of faculty wizards, trackers, and Gleaners who attended to day-to-day operations, research, and acquisitions. Everyone outside of the senior council were beholden to the orders of the regents. If the council ever asked a Gleaner to aid in a particular quest, that member was expected to follow their order. Beyond this, members had the freedom pursue whatever items and knowledge they so desired.

Activities

Members were expected to procure and contribute at least one new artifact to the archives of the Gleaners every ten years. Their missions of retrieval often proved to be rather long and perilous.

Other members served the Gleaners as capable guards for retrieved items or researchers that helped to demystify them, as well as devise new and clever methods of safeguarding them. Those who contributed to the organization through such work were exempt from the required procurement of an artifact. This requirement was also waived in the case of those who trained as agent retrievers. If a member met neither exemption status and couldn't procure an artifact, then they would be asked to make a donation of 200,000 gold pieces to the Gleaners if they wished to remain in good standing and continue using their facilities.

On rare occasions when the Gleaners were stolen from, exceptionally competent strike teams would be sent out to recover the relic and its thieves.

Many outsiders approached the organizations for either advice, knowledge, or a desire to research magic within their archives. At request, nonmembers could be granted access to one of the organization's artifacts for the purposes of study. This was always done under examine always under close observation and scrutiny. Even more rarely, the Gleaners would allow a limited use of items in service to correcting some imbalance in the fabric of the multiverse.

Tactics

There was little internal secrecy amongst the Gleaners, with members most other Gleaners, particularly the council members. Efforts towards secrecy were primarily aimed at safeguarding the protocols for accessing the Penultimate Vault.

Every six months the entirety of the Gleaners were called to return to the Temporal Archive for a meeting. Given the nature of their work, regular attendance was neither mandatory nor expected. During this gathering members would advise the council, discuss their progress on particular quests, and review the urgency acquiring specific items. This gathering allowed the senior council to adjust priorities and reallocate resources where they were most needed. Members often used this time to reveal knowledge of new items they might feel should be acquired or petition for assistance in a difficult quest.

Base of Operations

The headquarters of the Gleaners was a sprawling, arcane college called the Temporal Archive. It was located on an unknown Prime Material world, on a large tropical island that was well out of the way of civilization. In addition to workspaces for research, it provided members living quarters and food.

The college acted as the outer perimeter of the Penultimate Vault, where all of the Gleaners' prized and deadly artifacts were kept safe. It was secured by a combination of deadly traps, spells, guardian creatures, and spellcaster guards.

Possessions

Members of the Gleaners had ready access to a host of spell components at their headquarters at reasonable prices.

High-ranking members could requisition one stored artifact per year, checking it out for up to one month. Whilst this was primarily done for the purposes of study, a requisitioned artifact could be used however the Gleaner saw fit during this period. If the artifact was either damaged or destroyed, the member would have to obtain a new artifact or face disbarment from the Gleaners.

Relationships

The Gleaners had a mixed reputation amongst those who knew of them, with less favorable views being particularly harbored by those who the organization had taken items from. Being a powerful sorcerous society, however, even the most aggressive of people or organizations thought twice about actively opposing them.

In service to their goals of expanding their arcane collection, the Gleaners worked with a number of arcane schools across the multiverse, providing them materials and notes for teaching and research.

Members also commonly employed Surveyors, members of the Planar Cartographic Society, for assistance in finding obscure locales and surviving hostile regions. This included allowing deans to even examine artifacts whilst under supervision.

History

The Gleaners began as simply an arcane school, albeit one with a collection of artifacts of ancient power. Over time this collection grew so large and impressive that people grew increasingly interested in the study, safeguarding, and accumulation of yet more artifacts. Eventually, this shift in interests among students and faculty resulted in a reworking of the Temporal Archive's entire charter.

Across the history of the organization, only two members ever went rogue, and in both cases they had fled after stealing from the Penultimate Vault. Whilst one was captured and detained within the vault in a state of temporal stasis, the other managed to evade capture over the years.

Over the years, many members of the Gleaners sought to acquire for the organization the legendary Codex of the Infinite Planes.

Around the , both the Gleaners and Planar Cartographic Society laid claim to the Underdark maps, a series of magical maps scattered throughout Toril's Underdark that detailed its various tunnels, caves, and caverns. Both Gleaners and Surveyors had held possession of the maps at one time or another and each had stolen them from the other. By 1372 DR, both asserted that their respective organization were the original owners, not knowing that their true creator was actually a Chosen of Shar.

Members

The organization had a membership totaling over 500 individuals. The bulk of these members were powerful wizards, though all of the common classes could be found there. The organization also employed cosmic descryers and even epic infiltrators from time to time.
