The Planar Cartographic Society was a mercantile organization that specialized in dimensional exploration. Their ongoing mission statement was to survey and map locales all across the multiverse, making them a prime source for guides or maps to almost any place.

Organization

The Planar Cartographic Society is run like a company, with orders flowing from the top to the bottom. However, it had a loose hierarchy, as leaders were rotated out every ten years through internal elections. Members, also called Surveyors, were divided into the positions of president, directors, officers, and field agents. Their active president was the organization's founder, the mercane Khymez Ta'rol, who was incapable of being voted out. Despite his position, Khymez preferred to revel in acting as a field agent, delegating most business dealings to the officers below him. Chief among them was Director Hesmeth Schlade, a human wizard from the world of Oerth, who ran the Society's day-to-day operations.

High ranking officers within the Society were tasked with running the map archives. Other officers worked on putting together plans for expeditions, targeting stretches of uncharted regions they knew little about, and working with customers. They worked to determine the specific needs of customers and teams to fulfill the terms of their contracts.

The criteria looked for in potential Surveyors included an aptitude for direction, skill in making accurate maps, a penchant for surviving hostile environments, and a willingness to traverse deep into wild.

Usually, recruits were expected to show up at the Planar Cartographic Society's guildhall in Union with maps in hand, ready to get to work contributing to their body of knowledge. However, occasionally they approached individuals with offers of membership. In such cases these were people who were either well-traveled adventurers, had remarkable exploits whilst finding passage through an uncharted territory, or had a reputation in either map-making, tracking, or surviving the elements.

Activities

Originating as merely a scouting business, the Planar Cartographic Society offered sorts of scouting, guide work, and mapping services.

Nonmembers needed to pay appropriate fees in order to access their accumulated information, maps, or assistance of any sort. Surveyors giving these things away freely was grounds for immediate dismissal, especially the former two services, as whatever knowledge a field agent obtained during their travels was considered proprietary. Surveyors highly prided themselves on the accuracy and reliability of their maps, which in some cases show displayed astral conduits, useful for any psionic users wanting to manifest astral caravan.

Prospective clients of guide work would often hire a whole contingent of body-guards, rather than a single Surveyor.

Those unaffiliated with the Planar Cartographic Society could exchange unsolicited maps that, if found to be legitimate and of an area that hadn't already been thoroughly mapped out, could garner a standard finder's fee of 1,000 gp. On the other hand, if the Surveyors themselves solicited someone for a map of a region of interest, the seller could find them willing to pay upwards of 20,000 gp. Surveyors also had a penchant for paying adventurers fair prices for information gleaned on their travels, provided it was reliable and thorough.

Tactics

The Planar Cartographic Society operated openly, making no effort to conceal their bases, operations, or Surveyors.

Bards and rangers were quite common in the organization, as traversing the wilderness required field agents to have strength, magic, and healing on their side in addition to skills in tracking and a sense of direction. Those who particularly delighted in planar exploration trained as cosmic descryers.

A number of surveyors made use of transportation or travel-based magic in order to accelerate their journey away from headquarters to planar frontiers.

Base of Operations

The Society was based out of Union, an extraplanar metropolis in its own demiplane. There its headquarters was a large, prominent hall in the center of Union's Commerce Quarter. All commercial business was negotiated there, it was the archive of all their records, housed storage facilities for equipment and supplies, and it was where prospective clients would need to go if they wanted to contract the Society's services.

Possessions

There was a ready supply of cartographic equipment that Surveyors could temporarily requisition. These were usually of the mundane variety, such as chalk, compasses, high quality inks, parchment, measuring tape, surveying rods, and other such related equipment. Though they also supplied their employees with magical equipment for surviving hostile environments, such as potions, spell scrolls, or items like a Heward's handy haversack.

Surveyors also received salvage rights to any threats overcame whilst in the field and a stipend for each successful mapping mission they carried out.

Relationships

Members of the Gleaners, another planar organization based in Union, would commonly employ Surveyors for assistance in finding obscure locales and surviving hostile regions.

History

Around the , both the Planar Cartographic Society and the Gleaners laid claim to the Underdark maps, a series of magical maps scattered throughout Toril's Underdark that detailed its various tunnels, caves, and caverns. Both Gleaners and Surveyors had held possession of the maps at one time or another and each had stolen them from the other. By 1372 DR, both asserted that their respective organization were the original owners, not knowing that their true creator was actually a Chosen of Shar.

Members

The organization had a membership totaling over 850 individuals.
