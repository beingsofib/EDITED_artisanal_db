Cabal

Cabals were informal organizations all across the Realms. They usually formed in a very local area.

They consisted of informal alliances between persons, merchant families, or just peddlers, or any variation of such, who manipulated trade on a very small local scale in their favor. They engaged in nepotism (a cabal member's incompetent relative would be hired by another cabal member, where possible), trade redirection (if a buyer was looking for a trade good and a member of a cabal heard this, the buyer would be directed to a member of that cabal who dealt in that good), price-fixing, vacant land buying, and other underhanded, but mostly harmless actions. Elminster believed some of these cabals to even be benevolent.

Some cabals tended to be more aggressive, driving away any foreign merchant that dared to settle in their area of influence by hiring bandits to attack them or rob them and, in extreme cases, destroying that merchant's property or burning the foreigner's trade houses.
