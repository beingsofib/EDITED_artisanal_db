The Church of Eshowdow () was the primary religious organization dedicated to the worship and service of Eshowdow, the Shadow Giant. Known collectively as the Eshewtak, they hid in the darkest recesses of Chult and were largely composed of descendants from the lost Eshowe () tribe, whose hatred for the Tabaxi festered for nearly two millennia after a war between the two tribes led to the Eshowe's near-extinction.

Organization

The Church of Eshowdow respected a hierarchy based purely on individual power and skill, which oft led to conflict within their ranks. They seethed among themselves or broke into small, chafing cabals, and resolved their problems through ceremonial duels—or, most often, assassinations. Positions of importance and renown were held by the specialty priests of Eshowdow, the shadow stalkers, among both the faithful and their isolated tribesmen, but the Eshewtak did not order themselves according to their titles. In fact, these were individually chosen for each new priest. These titles were said to give some insight into the member's fate, being picked by the Shadow Giant himself and then passed onto them by the one initiating them into the Church.

Activities

The clergy of Eshowdow were involved in much more than their internal politics, however. Through methods both implicit and explicit, they held authority over not only the Eshowe's own governance, but also its cultural and spiritual systems; managing the creeping restoration of the tribe was one of their main interests. Beyond this, they were wholly dedicated to obtaining their vengeance and maintaining the covert nature of their existence. Both of these desires, however, frequently clashed.

As part of their atavistic conflict with the Tabaxi people, the Eshewtak were also known to prepare attacks and spy on their foes. If a member of Ubtao's faithful was captured, they were sometimes taken to Eshowdowan temples, where they were experimented on by the clergy; acolytes were instructed to maintain a hushed and monotonous chanting during this and any other nefarious research done by the Church. These investigations also led a large number of Eshewtak to the Demiplane of Shadow, where they looked for bygone magic to employ against the Tabaxi before returning to the Toril.

Rituals

Raiments

The Church also held ceremonial sessions wherein they worshiped the Ravager of the Tabaxi, prior to which its clergy was instructed to don specific vestments. These included necklaces shaped like local reptiles and made of jet or obsidian, as well as body paint, which served to represent the interchange of both darkness and light. This last was drawn in sequential lines of black and white.

Base of Operations

According to Tabaxi legend, the Valley of Lost Honor was the last bastion of the Eshowe who faced Ras Nsi's persecution in the last days of their civil war. It was a dark place—almost inaccessible—and often plagued by Batiri, dinosaurs, and their ilk, but it was nevertheless rumored to protect the treasures left by the entire tribe. Claims were equally made that the Valley was home to the shadowy giant who had both helped and betrayed the Eshowe in the last days of their civil war. In fact, the legends spoke the truth, as a vast majority of the Eshewtak's people and efforts were constrained to the Valley.

Temples

The Church's temples were dark edifices, built by the faithful out of walls of shadow and typically established in areas of the jungle floor that were already cast in persistent shade. From the exterior, they appeared like stepped pyramids. These low buildings were thickset, existing primarily on the Demiplane of Shadow to which many gates within its rooms and corridors led. Often, these same halls were infested with numerous beings in a variety of dark shapes.

Possessions

Magic

Eshowdow bestowed upon all of his faithful—except his crusaders—the power to command undead, though none were able to simply turn away such creatures.

Dogma

The lessons of the Eshewtak were directly passed onto them by their patron deity, who instilled within them a strong belief in the inherent difficulty of life. While a life spent heeding Eshowdow was still hard, their teachings said it was less so than the life of those who disobeyed.

By following Eshowdow, the faithful were capable of bringing together their body and spirit, which they believed came apart in the process during which a mortal entity was created. This union allowed them to achieve a superior state of being that cannot be attained without the tenets of the Shadow Giant.

Their doctrine only fomented their conflict with the Tabaxi, for it insisted that the Church's eventual goal was the eradication of their rival tribe from Chult, and that the Tabaxi were to always be distrusted. Only at the most opportune moment should the Eshewtak strike openly against Ubtao and the Tabaxi; otherwise, clandestine tactics were best. These included undermining the ingenuous, sabotaging, and even the murder of those Tabaxi with leadership or power. It was believed that, in this way, they might cause their ancient enemies to devolve into a frightened, suspicious, and infighting people, as well as demand little risk from the Eshowe.

Most simple of all their maxims was the importance of secrecy regarding the Shadow Giant's preeminent temple, the Great Shadow's Mansion. The Eshewtak were instructed never to divulge its location to people not of the faith, lest the traitor be killed.

Historical Revision

A large part of the Church of Eshowdow's beliefs regarded their own origins as part of the Eshowe tribe, which differed greatly from the truth due to Eshowdow's personal intervention.

Though the Eshewtak believed that Ubtao formed the world, they also thought that the process had split his spirit from his body, making him evil. Due to this shift, he attempted to forever torture and enslave the people he had created, with the worst among them—the terrible Tabaxi—as tyrannical supervisors. Against such a depraved tribe fought the peaceable Eshowe, until Ubtao turned his wrath upon them.

Religious Art

The Eshewtak's sacred imagery never depicted the Shadow Giant's true shape, and it was unknown whether such a thing existed, for it had never been seen. In its stead, Eshowdow's misshapen shadow was all they portrayed.

Classes

The Church of Eshowdow accepted a variety of different occupations among its clergy, though, notably, no member of the Eshewtak was a cleric. A majority among them, more than two-thirds, a little less than the remaining third worked as shadow stalkers, and the sliver that wasn't either were crusaders.

History

Civil War in Chult

According to Ras Nsi, the conflict between the Eshowe and the Tabaxi began in -438 DR, due to the then-numerous Eshowe's worship of Chult's spirits—even the sun and rain—and their derision of Ubtao. In the face of Mezro's military, however, the Eshowe and their spiritual magic proved insufficient; they were steadily forced deeper into the valleys of the dark jungle, living among their caves and the primordial creatures that laid within.

The Shadow Giant Awoken

It was in -137 DR, in one such valley, that the Eshowe first found a dark and massive being, which they named the Shadow Giant. They promised the souls of the enemies to this eldritch creature, for it hungered to feast on the Tabaxi's bravery, and bid it assist them on their last strike against the city of Mezro. Fighting alongside the giant, the Eshowe burned the fields and homes of the Tabaxi, with only a few of the city's central buildings—including the Temple of Ubtao—escaping such a fate. Even so, though they killed six of Mezro's barae in the process, both the Eshowe and their dark ally were routed.

Fall of the Eshowe

Rather than mark the end of the tribe's suffering, however, this failure only presaged greater pain.

By his hand, the Eshowe and their children were murdered, their homes buring, Finally, in -122 DR, the war between the tribes was ended.

Rise of the Eshewtak

The giant—renamed Eshowdow by the Tabaxi, to mean "Shadow of the Eshowe"—slunk back to the depths of Chult's jungle, still festering with hatred for Ubtao, and the centuries slowly passed. Even after he discerned, during his furtive attacks on the Tabaxi, that the Founder of Mezro was blind to the Shadow Giant's very existence, Eshowdow could not gather the courage to once more depart his sanctuary in the bowels of the jungle.

He was, therefore, lucky not to be alone. Despite Nsi's confidence that no Eshowe remained in Chult, some survivors had, in fact, made their home in the Valley of Lost Honor, their lives and those of their descendants spent under the thumb of Eshowdow. They were molded into a hateful cult over hundreds of years—one that no longer remembered the truth about the Betrayer of the Eshowe—for he had perverted their myths. So confident was their belief that their annihilation had come at the hands of Ubtao, not Eshowdow, that few would have listened to or accepted the true story.

The tribe degenerated, hatred for the Tabaxi and their patron god left as the only emotion that fueled them. Watched over by the one they saw as their savior, who would visit retribution upon their ancient rivals, the Eshowe spent decades in preparation prior to the mid-to-late 14 century DR. The nightmare of many Tabaxi, whose legends still spoke of the possible survival of the Eshowe within a secret enclave, was true: they were, indeed, readying to begin anew the tribes' millennium-old conflict.

Members

Though a small number among the tribe lived in the jungle beyond, membership of the Eshewtak was centered, in its near entirety, around the Valley of Lost Honor and the descendants of the Eshowe therein.
