The Ono Kumi were a notorious yakuza ninja clan operating in Kozakura circa .

Organization

The clan was headed by Ono Noburo, the oyabun. Among his lieutenants was Ono Arieki

Every member took the clan's name, Ono, as their own. This emphasized that the clan was their new family.

Tactics

Ono Kumi members and hirelings were all trained in Smiling Viper, an unarmed martial art designed to target vital areas.

Base of Operations

They had their headquarters in a city somewhere in northern Kozakura, though still far to the south of remote Maeshi Province. They controlled the northern cities of the islands.

Possessions

They would wield a variety of weapons, but often had three shuriken about their persons, and typically wore black fighting clothes and padded, leather, or chainmail armor underneath.

History

In , the Ono Kumi were discreetly contacted by a local lord on behalf of his brother-in-law, Lord Korimori, a daimyo of Maeshi Province far to the north. Korimori sought the Ono Kumi's services in seizing the Seven Swords, a set of relics being recovered by a band of adventurers for Korimori's rival daimyo, Michimori Uemon, and he proposed they intercept the adventurers whilst they stayed at Doi No Fugu—an inn under Imperial protection, and a permanent sentence of death for any who violated it.

That summer, Yoichi led six men of Ono Kumi to the Inn of the Globefish, posing as a traveling merchant and his retainers, guards, and servants; Yoichi wore a mask and claimed to have a cold to avoid being recognized by his family. They rented a room for the night and asked for a meal and a party with companions, during which they quickly surprised and subdued the Sumitomo family, imprisoning them in their own secret chamber, which gave Koichi away to his parents. They kept the women hostage there to ensure the men's cooperation and the children stayed confined in their rooms. Fourteen more ninja, including Chomei, lay in wait outside.

When the adventurers with the relics finally arrived, the Ono Kumi posed as the inn staff and welcomed them in, though male ninja made for poor geisha girls. They planned to distract them with a banquet at which they even performed "The Reward of Tomy", lull them with a biwa of calm and pearl of persuasion, and even have an allied shukenja named Toshiro mislead them by prophesying an ambush after leaving the inn, before ambushing them with thrown shuriken and poison-gas eggshell grenades and attacking. This would be complicated by the Sumitomo family's attempts to escape and alert the adventurers, the guard Haka Kinnori seizing the chance to strike back, and Yoichi's own crisis of conscience, which would be triggered if he was forced to fight his own family or saw them harmed or even killed, and he would potentially switch sides. The exact outcome of this terrible situation is unknown and the fate of the Sumitomos and others involved is unknown, but Michimori Uemon ultimately received the relics he'd employed the adventurers to recover.
