The Church of Istishia () was the primary religious body devoted to the worship and service of Istishia, the Water Lord. Although often dismissed by outsiders as an eccentric cult—largely due to their deity’s profound detachment—the Istishians understood that their lord’s will flowed according to principles far beyond mortal comprehension, even their own. So fluid and mercurial was Istishia’s nature that, over time, even his unpredictability became a kind of constancy to the Church.

Organization

The Church of Istishia was one of perpetual change, with distinct sects being subsumed into one or separating into many over decades. Each could differ in their approach to worshiping the Water Lord and how they interpreted their faith, but, despite the occasional dispute, these internal factions largely worked in unison with one another. Among them, only four had the highest importance, holding a majority of the clergy.

Tactics

Among the Water Lord's devotees, only his shamans could turn or command undead, though which ability they accessed depended on their alignment. The former was only bestowed upon those shamans who were good, whilst the rest instead used the latter.

As a whole, however, any member of the Church was urged to develop a strong deductive ability regarding upcoming weather conditions, or to be familiar with all of the skills of a sailor.

Possessions

Many offerings intended for the Water Lord were accepted by the Church of Istishia, who acted as intermediaries. These gifts included richly pigmented dyes and inks, papers or books bearing complex riddles or unsolvable mathematical problems, crystalline gemstones of colors anywhere between a deep sapphire or emerald to entirely transparent, and flowing textiles that moved like waves in their watery tones. These offerings were not received by clergy alone, however; Istishia was said to send a wide array of representatives to accept them, including even water elementals or fish.

Though Istishia's worshipers, in return, received his blessings, the Water Lord's gifts were bestowed without pattern. True to his inscrutable nature, those who received his attention–and those who were passed over—seemed chosen according to an unfathomable, fluid whim. Nevertheless, his favor often manifested as a sudden intuition regarding the discovery of a secret long-hidden beneath the waves, an aptness to move effortlessly through water, or even a most profound transformation—be it a moment of insight on a problem previously thought impossible or a physical shift in form.

The mystics among the Church of Istishia were also capable of making unique candles. These candles could be extinguished using a snuffing bell or one's fingers, like any other, but they were uniquely resistant to liquids, staying lit even when submerged underwater.

Dogma

Though the worshipers of Istishia depicted the Water Lord in many forms—ranging from an immense water elemental or water weird to a crashing ocean wave or raindrop—they believed that any significant body of water was a manifestation of his. This included the vast seas and oceans, but it also extended to smaller lakes, winding rivers or streams, the rain, and even modest puddles or fountains. In a similar fashion, the faithful saw it as an indication of Istishia's goodwill whenever one found water in an arid landscape or when establishing a well.

Orders

The ranks of the Istishian clergy included three orders. One of these, which was made up of mystics, was known as the Order of Cryptic Transformational Purity—although its members were informally and embarrassingly referred to as "the Water Lord's fishmen" or "fishtishians" due to the merman-like emblem of the group. The other two, known as the Cavaliers of the Ever-Changing Truth and the Cavaliers of the Seven Seas, were instead composed of crusaders. Differing only in that the former naturally breathed air, while the latter breathed water, both bid their crusaders to personally combat the church of Kossuth, to guard places of sacred importance, and to assist in escorting Istishian envoys.

Classes

Though the Istishians heavily favored clerics prior to the Time of Troubles, they were steadily turned into specialty priests by the Water Lord in the years after. Despite his unclear motivations, the clergy expounded the notion that it was done to exclude the other, more general duties of a traditional cleric, allowing the specialty priests to prioritize the elemental water above all else. To compensate, and handle the responsibilities previously seen to by clerics, the Church of Istishia formed an order of crusaders and a smaller order of mystics.

These crusaders, specialty priests, and mystics, however, were not the only members accepted into the clergy of the Water Lord. Istishia was also served by shamans, who typically worked within regions where a nomadic, primal way of life was customary.
