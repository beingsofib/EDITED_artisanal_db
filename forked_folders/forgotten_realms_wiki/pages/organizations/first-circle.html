First Circle

Organization
nameFirst Circle
typePantheon

The First Circle was a loose pantheon of the gods of nature worshiped by the druids as of the late 15th century DR. Their name was meant to convey that they were "first among the druids". Veneration of the First Circle was sometimes called the Old Ways.

Members

The circle was traditionally made up of Auril, Chauntea, Eldath, Malar, Mielikki, Silvanus, Talos, and Umberlee, and Grumbar as members. These gods collectively represented the many facets of nature—from its majesty to its violence and cruelty—and were honored for their parts in natural cycles of creation and destruction, growing and withering, or life and death. Each god was further regarded not simply as a divine being, but as an embodiment of the natural forces or phenomena which they represented. For example, Malar was synonymous with the hunger and instinct of predators, while Grumbar was synonymous with rolling hills and fertile soil.
