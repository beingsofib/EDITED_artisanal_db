Anatomical Academy

Organization
nameAnatomical Academy
typeSecret society

The Anatomical Academy was a secret society dedicated to aiding and advancing the field of anatomical study, spreading detailed knowledge of the body and providing its members cadavers for their studies, even in societies where their field was considered taboo or even outlawed.

Activities

The Anatomical Academy mission was centered around two things: providing members cadavers and facilitating the collection and dissemination of necromantic knowledge. this was a purely neutral organization, engaging with the necromantic from an analytical and scholarly perspective.

In whatever society they had roots in, the Anatomical Academy commanded an established network of contacts among local authorities. These were typically individuals that worked at cemeteries, morgues, prisons, and poorhouses. From these the Academy sourced some of their cadavers. They also frequently hired adventurers and rogues to establish connections with new sources of cadavers or to transport large shipments of bodies to subscribing members who lived out in secluded places. Cadavers were usually shipped across large distances by ship or wagon.

The Anatomical Academy generally didn't inquire as to the source of the bodies they paid for, with some members of a less-ethical bent merely caring that a specimen be "essentially intact." But other members, being more conscientious practitioners of anatomical study, were committed to only practicing upon corpses that had died a natural death.

Prior to being transported, cadavers would be magically preserved by a field agent through use of the spell embalm. Under dire circumstances, a field agent would resort to animating cadavers and disguising them. However, such a method could carry its own risk of attracting unwanted attention.

Base of Operations

The organization was not known to have a central base of operations. Rather, its members typically gathered at secluded or abandoned locations for any conventions or secret lectures, places such as dungeons, warehouses, or even university classrooms that were not seeing use.

Cities they were known to operate in included Neverwinter and Waterdeep.

Tactics

Being a secret society, this order strove to keep a low profile. They had no desire to risk facing condemnation or persecution from the more conservative and lawful good elements of Faerûn that frowned upon the mutilation of cadavers.

Members of the Anatomical Academy maintained steady contact with one another through discrete courier services, the use of certain spells, and enchanted devices.

Members

Whilst the bulk of the organization was composed of anatomists, the Anatomical Academy was not exclusive to necromancers. It was open to anyone that possessed knowledge of or an interest in anatomy. This included all other manner of wizards, priests, healers, and surgeons.

Notable Members

  • Doctor Ellandra Tolbert, a wealthy Neverwintan anatomist.
  • Matanzas, a Loviatan priest who had vivisection facilities in his estate that were sometimes used as a meeting place for the Academy.
