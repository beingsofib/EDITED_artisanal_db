The Three Coins Fellowship were a small adventuring company active in the Delimbiyr Vale in the late 14 century DR. They comprised Kars, a human ranger; Douver, half-elf bard; and Eman, a dwarf warrior.

History

Only newly formed in the summer of the , the Three Coins Fellowship visited the city of Loudwater in early Eleasis. They had heard the story of a ghost that wandered Tragedy's Grotto after each Shieldmeet and of how those who followed it were said to find great treasure, or else sorrow and doom. They, of course, wanted the treasure, They had little plan beyond that.

In the streets of Loudwater late one night, the trio encountered the ghost for themselves, finding it to be the spirit of a robed monk. It ignored them, and when Eman leapt to grab it, he fell through and into the muddy road. Thus, they followed it to Tragedy's Grotto and the hoped-for treasure. When another band of adventurers came across them, the trio had no desire to share and would refuse conversation and assistance with increasing vehemence, from Douver politely asking them to mind their own business to Kars spitting threats and foul language and warning shots from his bow.

One way or another, the Three Coins Fellowship and the other adventurers followed the ghost to Tragedy's Grotto.

If they survived the battle, Eman, Douver, and/or Kars joined the other adventurers in exploring a buried vault the ghost had entered, finding no treasure but the mysterious shard of the Fallen One. However, one of the three would become crazed, being possessed by a magical force of lawful and evil nature and attacked the others to seize the shard, whatever the cost. If subdued and questioned, he would be confused and have no memory of it. They might be persuaded to speak of the voice they'd heard, but if forcefully interrogated or magically compelled to explain why he'd attacked the others, he would be possessed again, screeching fiendishly the cryptic words "Because the key is mine!" He would then be choked to death from the unseen force; only a protection from evil or protection from law spell could prevent it. Should he be rescued, he would reveal a cruel and evil presence had been in his mind. The other adventurers took the shard and followed the trail of its mystery to the Phantoms' Cloister.
