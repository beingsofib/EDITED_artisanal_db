Amazon (culture)

Amazon () was a term used to describe a human woman or organized groups of human women (sometimes by members of the group themselves) who practiced various forms of warfare and lived independently of men or exercised significant authority over them.

Description

While some suggested that women in the Realms were treated equally and could excel in any activity available to men, they nevertheless experienced varying degrees of derogation, discrimination, and restrictions imposed by men on how they could live because of their sex. As such, some human women were motivated to create communities far removed from men, where men occupied lower positions in the social hierarchy, These individuals and groups were called amazons.

Culture

Amazons, whether as groups or individuals, often regarded men as lesser beings and treated them with spite or discrimination,

At least some common culture among amazons existed, as evidenced by the existence of traditional amazonian songs.

Amazons were sometimes referenced in other cultures. The Crinti, the half-drow and half-elf aristocratic ruling class of Dambrath, were called the shadow amazons. in addition to women warriors in general.

Notable Amazons

Organizations

  • The Jarnsaxa, a reclusive tribe, also known as "The Iron-Crushers of the World", that resided to the north of Yal Tengri, the Great Ice Sea of the Hordelands.
  • The She-Wolves, a mercenary company based on the island of Ixinos in the Vilhon Reach.

Individuals

  • Elissa Tarixon, the mysterious Queen who ruled the amazon isle of Ixinos.
  • Neysea Snowwolf, a barbarian amazon from the Jarnsaxa tribe.
  • Shinthala Deepcrest, a druidess who was called the Amazon Queen of Ilighôn.
  • Vala, a hero and an adventuring fighter and member of the Silver Blades.
