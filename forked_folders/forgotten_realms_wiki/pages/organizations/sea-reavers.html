The Sea Reavers were a long-running succession of pirate fleets operating out of the Nelanther Isles in the Sea of Swords of west Faerûn as of the late 14 century DR. Circa 1370 DR, they were a subsidiary of the Rundeen mercantile consortium.

History

Early Versions

Pirate fleets of the Nelanther Isles known as the Sea Reavers have existed since the early 1 century DR. The earliest to hold the name was reported to have participated in the attack on Ithalyr led by the second Black Alaric that led to the fall of House Ithal They would continue, in varying forms, down through the centuries.

Their longest-lasting incarnation to date was active in the 14 century DR. At one point, they were led by Captain Kesmer Red-Eyed, called the "Coast Wolf". The pirates believed Mintiper had killed the Coast Wolf and the rest by means of trickery and magical fire. (Mintiper himself escaped, telling acquaintances that an enchanted green gem gifted him by his mother had activated to save him, consuming the pirates and itself in order to teleport him away to the Purple Hills headlands.) After that, many thought the Sea Reavers had been obliterated for good.

Modern History

Yet they would soon find a new captain. Amidst the turbulent Tethyrian Interregnum after 1347 DR, a commander of Tethyr's royal navy named Toramir Kuurgan felt betrayed by the nobility for their selfishness and therefore betrayed his fallen kingdom. Circa 1350 DR, Toramir reassembled his flotilla with four ships and 200 crewmembers and officers loyal to him, and permitted safe passage back to the Dragon's Neck peninsula for any who refused and remained allegiant to the navy—only four departed. The new Sea Reavers joined with the survivors of the old, and operated as pirates for the next two decades. In time, their fleet grew to five vessels.

The Sea Reavers were amongst those Nelantheran pirate groups who held sway over the leaders of Tulmene, a small shipbuilding town on the Dragon's Neck. For some twenty years, they made the place a secret safe harbor for their kind. Two of the Sea Reavers' ships were damaged in the fighting, and when they were forced to enter the Race and its intense winds and currents, they were sunk.

Incensed by the loss of ships and crewmen and corrupted by two decades of crime, Toramir had no desire to rejoin in the reformation of Tethyr and instead swore vengeance against the new queen, even to see her dead. While some pirates like Captain Bahija the Fair shared this view, most others saw peace in Tethyr as good for business and therefore good for them. This put the Sea Reavers at odds with the other Nelantheran pirate factions. In any case, soon after the expulsion, the daughter of the newly appointed mayor of Tulmene, Jos Knightson, was kidnapped at sea. Those responsible in the Shadow Thieves forced Jos to begin reestablishing links with the pirates including the Sea Reavers.

Nevertheless, the Sea Reavers encountered difficulties supplying and maintaining their remaining ships and so in the late 1360s DR Toramir made a deal to work for the Rundeen mercantile consortium.

But with Tethyr's restoration in the , and a rebuilt strong and capable navy, pirates like the Sea Reavers found its shipping too hard a target and so turned to prey upon that of Amn. In late Eleint, the Sea Reavers attacked and looted four ships of a seven-strong fleet belonging to the Vemmil family of Amn, which were returning from a voyage to Zakhara. The other three made it to Murann. Some time the following year, under the auspices of the Rundeen, Toramir and Bahija made alliances with the ogre mage Sythillis of the Sythillisian Empire to blockade Amnian ports like Murann to prevent treasure ships fleeing, in exchange for a cut of that treasure, as well as stop the Amnians retaking the city. They hoped to acquire ships and a new safe port on the Sword Coast. By 1372 DR, the Rundeen and their pet Nelanther pirates were using Murann as a safe haven and defending it against naval counter-attack.

Organization

In the mid-to-late 14 century DR, the then-current incarnation of the Sea Reavers were commanded by Captain Toramir Kuurgan. A formerly lawful Tethyrian naval commander, he grew to hate his former kingdom and was driven to madness and paranoia.

Activities

Although once quite varied in their tactics and actions, they were far less so by 1370 DR under Toramir. By the terms of their agreement with the Rundeen, in each summer trade season they were required to dispatch at least one pirate ship to the Shining Sea to prey on non-Rundeen merchant shipping.

Base of Operations

They were primarily based on the island of Ioma in the Nelanthers. Whilst affiliated with the Rundeen, they could get their ships refitted in Rundeen-controlled ports in or near the Border Kingdoms.

Possessions

Circa 1370 DR, the Sea Reavers operated five ships.
