Dragon issue 360 was released in October of 2007 as a HTML edition. It contained the following articles related to the Forgotten Realms:

Demonomicon of Iggwilv: Graz'zt, the Dark Prince

By James Jacobs, illustrated by Jason A. Engle. p. 9–21

Background, stats, goals, cults, minions, and realm of Graz'zt, demon prince and son of the obyrith known as the Pale Night. Includes the Thrall to Graz'zt prestige class, the Thrall to Demon and Vile Spell feats, and the incorporeal demon caligrosto (including the lourmara demon traits).

The Ecology of the Death Knight

By Matthew Sernett, illustrated by Marc Sasso and Steve Argyle. p. 34–38

A scholarly treatise on the Death Knight.

Infernal Aristocracy: The Dukes of Hell

By Robert J. Schwalb, illustrated by Jim Pavelec. p. 40–48

Explanation for why the dukes are more powerful than the archdevils. Followed by stats, strategies, tactics, servants, enemies, and goals of Moloch the Outcast, Titivilus, Bael, Balan, and Bathym.

Design & Development: D&D Cosmology

By Richard Baker. p. 68–69

The new or reconfigured planes known as the Feywild, Shadowfell, Elemental Chaos (aka the Tempest or Maelstrom), and the Astral Sea.

Design & Development: Encounter Design in 4th Edition D&D

By Mike Mearls. p. 71

Monsters now have default stats at various power levels, the entire encounter is designed around multiple monsters instead of starting with one, monster roles are explicitly defined, and the rise of the hazard and the environment becoming an integral part of an encounter.

Design & Development: The D&D Pantheon

By James Wyatt. p. 73

The default gods include a mix from Greyhawk and the Forgotten Realms, and racial gods are no longer as tightly tied to their race.

  • Bane
  • Bahamut
  • Corellon

Design & Development: Zombies!

By Chris Sims. p. 74

Shambling, mindless corpses are getting all gussied up for 4e.
