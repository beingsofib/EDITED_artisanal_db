Giant nautiluses were an extra-large variety of their diminutive cousins native to the Elemental Plane of Water.

Description

Giant nautiluses looked much like the ordinary nautiluses that were found in the oceans. Adult giant nautiluses had spirally coiled shells of in diameter, and twenty strong tentacles encircled the mouths of the giant nautiluses that they used for every kind of manipulation necessary.

Personality

Often called "druids of the deep" for their absolute neutrality and great insistence on maintaining the balance of the underwater world, they were known to be used by tritons, merfolk, and locathah as impartial judges to mediate disputes among themselves and the different races. They considered sunken ships to be terrible eyesores and would actively help in removing them, even going so far as to protect those who were removing the ships from the great predators that lived in the depths. While they were totally uninterested in treasures of any kind, that were not stupid nor naive, and understood the concepts of avarice and greed. Thus they would demand that sunken vessels carrying mundane cargo be removed before they allowed those storing treasure to be taken away, and that the entire vessel be removed, not just the cargo.

Giant nautiluses seldom attacked humans or any other intelligent ocean-faring or ocean-dwelling race unless in self-defense—even the sahuagin and ixitxachitl were safe as long as they refrained from attacking. For all of their peaceful ways, however, giant nautiluses were quick to stop any who overfished or polluted the areas where they held sway. If their pleas and attempted negotiations went nowhere, they would intimidate the offenders. If even that went on deaf ears, they would resort to force, dragging down small ships and rocking the larger vessels until damaged.

Combat

In combat, giant nautiluses would attempt to constrict their foes with some of their tentacles, and bite their victims if they could drag them into their mouths. They additionally used their spells to either pacify or slay their foes.

Abilities

They were very long-lived, with a natural lifespan of between 3,000 and 4,000 years. They had a form of telepathy that allowed them to communicate with any intelligent creature, regardless of languages spoken and understood.

Giant nautiluses had a constant know alignment spell-effect and could cast charm person or mammal, conjure fish, control temperature, 10' radius, detect evil and good, detect magic, fish summoning I or summon monster I, fish summoning II or summon monster II,, fish summoning III or summon monster III, locate fish or plants, lower water, and wall of coral at will. They were also able to cast conjure water elemental thrice a day.

Society

Natives to the Plane of Water, they wandered the depths of the deepest oceans on the Prime Material plane. They were also encountered in Thalasia, the great sea that occupied a layer of Elysium; in Lunia, on the first layer of the Mount Celestia; and in Aquallor, in the depths of Poseidon's realm Caletto.

Solitary wanderers of the deepest depths, giant nautiluses maintained neither lairs nor permanent abodes, using magic to return to the Plane of Water when they wished to mate and reproduce. As carnivores, they preyed on huge crustaceans such as giant crabs and giant lobsters. They often saw krakens as foes, owing to the latter's tendency to have grandiose schemes of underwater empire, which ultimately always came at the detriment of the environment.

Usage

The shells of giant nautiluses were great prizes, able to be used as roomy, near-indestructible submarines for exploring the depths of the oceans.
