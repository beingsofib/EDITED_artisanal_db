Apricots were a variety of orange-red fruit in the Realms that grew on trees of the same name.

Availability

Places in the Realms where apricots were known to be cultivated or grew wild included:

  • Dried apricots could be ordered in many parts of Faerûn through Aurora's Whole Realms Catalogue, which sold them under its "Aurora's Larder" section for 15 gp per .
  • In Anauroch, several oases in the Sword region had apricot trees around them that had been carefully selected and were tended to by the indigenous Bedine. Where possible, the Bedine propped these trees up to maximize the shade they provided and to ensure that any bird nests were out of reach of a few of the predators that roamed the Sword. For similar reasons, the Bedine also wrapped thorny vines around the trunks of trees like the apricot, but kept back just far enough to ensure they weren't choking the tree.
  • Also in the Anauroch, the ruined city of Orofin had both acacias and wild apricot trees along the four canals that radiated outwards from its central fortress, the remnants of ancient parkland.
  • On the Sword Coast north, just south of Neverwinter, the foodstand Gelgul's Feast of Plenty sold dried apricots.
  • In south Faerûn, apricots were grown around the Lake of Steam.
  • In southwest Faerûn, apricots were grown in the nation of Tashalar.
  • In the city of Yartar, Dannath's Pickles, Nuts, & Foods sold dried apricots that had been imported from Tashalar and the Lake of Steam region.
  • In Interior Zakhara, close to the banks of Al-Naqus River and east of the city of Fahhas, were grasslands dotted with orchards of apricots and other fruits.

Cultivars

Fent-Moyer Apricot: One of several magical fruits that were cultivated by the gardener-mage Fent-Moyer Thrist Hauser in his manor in the Vast in the late 14 century DR. When consumed, these apricots produced an effect equivalent to a potion of rainbow hues. If a second apricot was eaten, it would increase the duration of the effect. Eating a third apricot, or any of Fent-Moyer's other fruit, would cause an individual to polymorph into some manner of animal. Additionally, eating one of Fent-Moyer's other fruits after an apricot could result in any number of magical side-effects.

Uses

  • Like many fruits apricots were enjoyed in a variety of forms, including dried, candied, liqueur, syrup mash,
  • Dried apricots were popular as a handfood in the Shining South, particularly in the lands of Estagund, Luiren, Tashalar, and Var.
  • For some recipes, like the Cormyrian delicacy roast Gammon pie, apricots could be used as a substitute for flamefruit.
  • In the Dalelands, trout from the River Ashaba were filleted and served with black-truffle oil and warm apricots.

Notable Users

  • The Bedine considered apricots served with camel milk to be a delicacy.

Businesses

  • Under its "Aurora's Larder" section, Aurora's Whole Realms Catalogue sold candied apricots for 5 gp and an apricot & almond jam for 20 gp.
  • The Battlescarred Bard, an inn along the Trade Way in Amn, offered patrons dried apricots around winter.
  • The Inn of the Dripping Dagger, an inn and tavern in Waterdeep, offered its patrons apricot liqueurs.
  • Whenever apricots were in season around Ravens Bluff, Umbrel's Hot Tarts would offer sweet apricot tarts.
  • Wizard's Hand, an inn in the village of Maskyr's Eye, offered apricot jams at a price of 5 sp.

Individuals

  • Whenever his apricots were in danger of spoiling, Alukk Dannath would convert them into some other form, such as adding them to a syrup mash or a wine.

Trivia

  • Apricots were among the fruit that the enchanted bowl of mixed fruit could potentially produce once per morning. Whenever it did produce apricots, a bundle of 36 would appear in the bowl.
