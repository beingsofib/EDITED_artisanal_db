Forest trappers, also called miners, were a sub-species of trappers who had adapted to life in the woodlands of Faerûn.

Description

They quite resembled tailless manta rays, around in diameter, with mottled or greenish brown skin adapted to better blend in with the woodlands they inhabited. Their frontal and dorsal ridges were hard bone. They had between 6 and 25 venomous spinal barbs running down their backs, which they used to hunt. At the base of each spine was a toxin sac where they stored their paralyzing venom.

Behavior

Forest trappers would travel quite slowly when on the move, either rippling like snakes on the surface or burrowing through soil, sand, and gravel. They tried to stay below the surface whenever possible for protection against enemies.

Combat

When hunting for prey, the forest trappers would burrow down a few inches into the ground, raise their spinal barbs up through the surface and letting the prey walk over them, hopefully hitting one or more spinal barbs and being injected with the forest trapper's venom. The venom would paralyze the prey. Only once all surface vibrations had seized would the forest trappers reveal themselves, dig out and envelop the paralyzed prey to feast.

Ecology

Forest trappers primarily preyed on small woodland animals, but were quite open to dining on more substantial meals, like that which traveled on roads.

They would lay their eggs in clutches several feet below the surface, and the young forest trappers would spent the first year of their life hunting and feeding on various burrowing creatures that they hunted without ever surfacing.

Habitation

They were most often encountered in the great Cormanthor forest,

but were found throughout the Eastern Heartlands in forested places.

Usage

The venom-filled spines of the forest trappers were sometimes used as ammunition in blowpipes, or as hand-weapons by small creatures who would wrap the grip as to not poison themselves.
