Venomous snakes, often wrongly called poisonous snakes, were snakes no more than long with venom glands and hollow fangs who bit their prey, injecting various venoms into the preys bodies.

Behavior

All types of venomous snakes were ambush predators and would conceal themselves in foliage or underneath rocks, waiting for prey to amble near whereupon they would strike out and bite, injecting their venom into the target.

Combat

Venomous snakes could pose a danger in two different ways; The first was by having a strong venom that would induce sickness or even death in normal sized or larger targets. The other by slithering in swarms of up to 6 members and overwhelming their prey by the combined wight of their venomous bites. Of course the greatest danger was when they combined both and a swarm of snakes with deadly venom would work together to bring down even hardy foes.

Species

Adder: A venomous snake found in the Duskwood.

Asp : A common species of venomous snake.

Boomslang : A small and docile snake with a very dangerous bite.

Cobra : A venomous snake with an expandable hood near the neck.

Fer-de-lance : A formidable, aggressive type of pit viper.

Nightviper : A venomous snake of the Nelanther Isles. А poison called Nightviper venom was extracted from its fangs.

Rattlesnake : A viper name for a keratin segment at the end of its tail that produced a loud rattling or buzzing noise.

Tundra widowmaker : A venomous snake.

Viper : A common type of venomous snake that came in various sizes and colorings.

Water moccasin : A common species of venomous snake.

History

Various venomous snakes were all over the place, from the Calim Desert in Calimshan, in the midwood of the Cormanthor forest, to the Umber Marshes near Thay.

Notable Venomous Snakes

  • Ssura, an awakened snake who worshipped the god Merrshaulk and inhabited the Shrine of Fangs during the late 15 century DR.
  • Y'zleth, another awakened snake and companion to Ssura who likewise worshipped Merrshaulk.
