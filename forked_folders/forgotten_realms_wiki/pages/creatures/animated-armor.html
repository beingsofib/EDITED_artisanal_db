An animated armor, or just a suit of armor for brevity,

Behavior

Animated armor could remain motionless for unlimited period of time, being virtually indistinguishable from ordinary sets of armor on display. They were capable of speaking in hollow whispers, reciting warning or stating their purpose, yet unable to negotiate with intruders.

Abilities

As constructs, animated suits of armor were unaffected by all spells that affected one's mind, such as charm, sleep, etc, as well as necrotic magic and energies left them unscathed. They also immune to all poisons, and could not be blinded, deafend, frightened, paralyzed, or petrified. They could never get tired.

Combat

Possessing only the simplest spark of intelligence, the armors could not use any complex strategies, instead, they directly attacked with brute strength, using their weapons, or unarmed slams.

Ecology

Functioning through magic that animated them, these armors could not function within dead-magic zone or antimagic fields. There was a chance that if faced with the dispel magic spell, they were turned into mundane, non-animated armor sets.

History

In the late 14 century DR, numerous animated armors were task with defending Lyran's Hold, the stronghold of the undead villain Lyran in the Forest of Wyrms.

In the late 15 century DT, ancient decaying animated sets of armor were guarding the Jade Temple of Dagon in the Moonsea North. Animated armors were also used at the infamous Hillsfar Arena as one of many challenges for the gladiators to overcome. Another use for the armors in the late 15 century DR was stationing them, alongside flying swords and guardian portraits within nobles' tombs of Waterdeep's City of the Dead.

During Elturel's Descent into Avernus of the , several suits of animated armor escaped from the High House of Wonders in Baldur's Gate, briefly becoming a threat in the Upper City. During the Absolute crisis of the same year, animated armors were discovered within the ancient Grymforge in the Underdark, still defending it from the cultists and adventurers.

Notable Armors

  • Frostsilver Knights, suits of Frostsilver armor that were both forged and animated within the Frostsilver Mines. Each piece of armor was said to have received a blessing from the god Dumathoin that protected the secrets of their creation.
  • Hollow armor, a variant of animated armor created through shadow possession.
  • Klank, one of the bouncers for the Elfsong Tavern, later returned to its creator – Lorroakan.
  • Strahd's animated armor, a more powerful variety found exclusively under the control of Strahd von Zarovich of Ravenloft.
