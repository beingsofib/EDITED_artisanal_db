Sharks were aggressive carnivorous fish. They could be found hunting alone or in groups and were known for attacking anyone approaching them without provocation.

Behavior

All shark species were utterly fierce, aggressive, and fearless. They were relentlessly hungry.

Combat

Sharks were tactical hunters, using their keen senses to circle their prey just outside of their field of view and charging to attack at the most opportune moment. Their powerful jaws and sharp teeth were effective against many different targets, sometimes even ship hulls.

History

These creatures had been swimming in the oceans for thousands of years with little change in their appearance or methods.

Species

Sharks were categorized into medium, large, and huge species.

Medium shark

Reef sharks: the species of shark that usually inhabited shallow waters and coral reefs. They hunted in packs.

Goblin sharks: the species of ugly deepwater sharks, vicious predators that could be found in the deepest parts of the seas.

Mako sharks: the most agile and fierce of their kind. They hunted in packs akin to their reef cousins.

Hammerhead sharks: the species of shark notorious for their agility and their distinct hammer-like head shape.

Large Sharks

Large sharks included hunter sharks that preyed on deep waters, and fleshtearer sharks, which were found in various environments, including underground rivers and lakes.

Huge Sharks

Dire Shark: The second largest species of the shark in the Realms, they behaved in a similar manner to great whites. They were prone to attacking anything that they perceived to be edible, no matter how large.

Great White: Also known as "huge sharks" or "giant sharks," they were the third largest type of shark in the Realms. This species could be found in deep oceans and attacked anything that approached them, including whales and ships.

Megalodon: An ancient and monstrous species of shark, sometimes known as "giant sharks". They were one of the largest species of shark in the Realms.

Ecology

Habitats

The smaller types of sharks were very common in the waters surrounding Chult. The Bay of Chult and Refuge Bay contained large groups of reef sharks. Hunter sharks and reef sharks hunted off the coast of Ishau, as well as Jahaka Anchorage. In the Shining Sea, they lurked in the sunken ruins of Untisczer.

Mako, goblin, and hammerhead sharks lived and hunted in the close vicinity of the Temple-Under-The-Sea, the submerged temple to Umberlee in the Fiddler's Green underwater region of the Sea of Swords.

Outside of the Prime Material plane, sharks were extremely prevalent in Draga Salt-Biter's domain of Saragoss in the Domains of Dread.

Relationships

Sharks were revered by sahuagin and could be trained by them. Their kinship to sahuagin was so strong that even wild sharks considered them allies and did not attack them. Sahuagin could also telepathically issue commands to nearby sharks.

Kalbari al-Durrat al-Amwaj ibn Jari had a wide variety of sharks as servants in her Citadel of Ten Thousand Pearls.

Summoning

A fiendish medium sized shark could be summoned with the spell summon monster II while in a saltwater environment, while three medium sharks would answer the call if summon monster III were cast. summon monster IV would call forth a large fiendish shark, and three of the creatures would come were the spell summon monster v cast. With summon monster IX a fiendish Dire shark would be summoned.
Ordinary non-fiendish sharks could also be summoned by the casting of summon monster IV, summon monster V, and summon monster VI for increasingly hardier specimens. By casting summon monster VIII a giant shark could be called upon to serve.
Those well-versed in sea magic could with summon monster IV call up to three of the beasts, with summon monster V two of the creatures, and with summon monster VI up to 3 of the hardiest of ordinary sharks.

Usages

Aurora's Whole Realms Catalogue sold shark hides for 15 gp a piece.
