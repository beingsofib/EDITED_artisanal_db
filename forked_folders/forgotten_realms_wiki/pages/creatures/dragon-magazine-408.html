Dragon magazine 408 was released in PDF format only in February 2012. It contained the following articles related to the Forgotten Realms:

History Check: Corellon and Gruumsh

By Jeff LaSala, illustrated by Wayne Reynolds. p. 5

This article gives a summary of the animosity between the core versions of Corellon and Gruumsh.

Character Themes: Reborn from Chaos

By Matt Goetz. Illustrations by Chris Seaman and Scott Murphy. p. 12

This article describes several themes available for characters whose origins begin in the Elemental Chaos.

Spells

  • Bound by brass
  • Use cinderspeech
  • Me and this army
  • Blazing reprisal
  • Seed of chaos
  • Foment chaos
  • Command chaos
  • Warp the world
  • Storm the deck
  • Storm legs
  • Blitz rush
  • Blood and thunder

Elemental Patrons and Palaces

By Claudio Pozas, illustrated by Noah Bradley. p. 19

This article gives players several tips on how to role-play a character who worships one of the powerful immortal creatures of the Elemental Chaos. It describes four such beings: Kristobal, Zephyria, Fumeran, and Lureq.

Bazaar of the Bizarre: Treasures of the Elemental Chaos

By Michael H. Haneline, illustrated by Patrick McEvoy. p. 28–31

This article describes several items which originated in the Elemental Chaos.

Items

  • Chaos storm dagger
  • Babau gauntlets
  • Hailstorm ki focus
  • Rift dancer boots
  • Elemental ward cloak
  • Ring of the djinn slayer
  • Wand of fiery hordes

Making Race Count, Part 3

By Matt Sernett and Robert J. Schwalb, illustrated by Raven Mimura, William O'Connor, and Chris Seaman. p. 32–36

This article gives several new spells available to dragonborn, half-orc, and tiefling characters.

Spells

  • Baleful glance
  • Dragon's dive
  • Arkhosian resilience
  • Triumphant roar
  • Untamed aggression
  • Revitalizing charge
  • Blood-fueled fervor
  • Killer's mind
  • Hellish gaze
  • Grim isolation
  • Hellfire shroud
  • Eternal returns

Eye on the Realms: The Enchanted Painting of Manyshields Hall

By Ed Greenwood, illustrated by Chris Seaman. p. 44–47

This article describes the plight of Malthus Traganther and a powerful deepspawn who has magically been transformed into a painting.
