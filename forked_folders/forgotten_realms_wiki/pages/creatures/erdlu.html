Erdlus were a hardy species of flightless, reptilian-esque birds found only on the desert world of Athas and were among its most common domesticated animals.

Description

The average adult erdlu stood tall and weighed around . Despite being birds, erdlus lacked feathers, instead exhibiting scales on their large, round bodies. The non-functional wings that folded at the sides of their bodies exhibited harder, armored plate-like scales. Erdlu scales ranged from a flaky or pale gray to deep red in coloration, though the ones on their long, snake-like necks, were yellow.

Erdlus had small, round heads with large wedge-shaped beaks that were quite sharp, able to pierce creatures like a spearhead. Their legs were lanky, yet powerful, ending in four-toed feet that were tipped with razor-sharp claws. They could sprint at great speeds over distances of several hundred yards, but generally no more than or .

Behavior

Erdlus exhibited an animal level of intellect and a temperament considered ideal for a herd animal. When threatened they preferred to flee, rather than fight, relying upon their great speed and dispersion tactics to outmaneuver predators.

Combat

When fleeing seemed impossible an erdlu flock would fight together as a group, while a cornered erdlu would fight to making an opening for them to escape. They always struck at opponents with their sharp beaks first, followed by raking them with their claws.

History

In The Year of Despair, Malatran calendar, late 14 century DR, a group of heroes of the Malatran Plateau were sent through an elemental gate to the far-away world of Athas. Created temporarily by Roshink, the elemental noble explained that he and his fellow nobles fled from this world long ago and it is what could befall the Malatran Plateau should the heroes fail to vanquish the dreaded Tamara. In order to return home, he instructed them to head for the mountains until they came across a great forest, where they would find an "oak tree that is too big for a saru to put his arms around". When they found such an oak, he would be able to bring them back to the Plateau through the Dreamlands.

Immediately upon arrival, the group was captured by Athasian elf slavers. A young Athasian thri-kreen named Ka'cha was among creatures captured by the elves, and he lamented being separated from his clutch.

After two days of travel across the hot sands, guided by Ka'cha, the group stumbled across his tribe, the Windrunners, and their leader, Ki'rik'ku. Ka'cha convinced them that the strange adventurers were friends, not food. Happy with one of their number returned, chief Ki'rik'ku gifted the aliens four large erdlu eggs and two apples of healing. The thri-kreen then explained to the Malatran heroes how to reach their destination—the Ringing Forest—the way back to Toril.

Ecology

Erdlus instinctively congregated and traveled in large flocks for protection.

Their eggs had a wrinkled, leathery appearance and red yolks.

Diet

These creatures had a Omnivorous diet, allowing them to survive in most habitats on Athas. In terms of plant life, their hardy beaks allowed erdlus to eat many tough vegetation and to work through thorns to forage at roots. They also fed upon various reptiles, snakes, and insects.

Relationships

A variety of different cultures across Athas raised erdlus, with most nomadic herdsmen relying upon them for their livelihoods.

Outside of being herded, wild erdlus were hunted by Athasian thri-kreen.

Usages

Erdlus were raised for a variety of purposes, chief among them their edible eggs. as they were packed with a variety of nutrients and essential vitamins. If cooked, the egg whites would form a spongy cake that had a rich taste, reminiscent of sharp cheese. But if consumed raw, an act which was customary among nomadic herdsmen tribes on the move, an erdlu egg could act as a substitute for of water. Such substitution was only ever successful a period of one week. This raw taste was said to be both gamey and zesty.

Erdlus were also prized for their meat, though nomadic herdsmen only resorted to this when an erdlu was too old or infirm to travel. Beyond food, parts of erdlu bodies were harvested for a variety of purposes. Their claws could be made into daggers or other tools, while their beaks could be fashioned into proper spearheads. The tendons of their long necks could be fashioned into bowstrings. And the scales on their wings could be fashioned into either light armor or shields.
