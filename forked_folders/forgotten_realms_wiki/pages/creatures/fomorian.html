Fomorians were a race of giant-kin supernaturally altered by an ancient curse, considered to be the most hideous and wicked of all the giant races.

Description

Beguiling creatures, ancient fomorians were the fairest of all giants. Identified by their pointed ears, angled faces, and apparent agelessness, they resembled gigantic, purple-skinned elves.

Accursed fomorians were as misshapen and strange as they were varied in appearance. Often disproportionate and exaggerated, some had thick and stocky builds, while others were contortionists with long and sinewy limbs that possessed enough gripping strength to crawl along walls and ceilings. They had hunchbacks, crooked appendages, conic heads, flapping ears, twisted toes, oversized noses, or tiny mouths. Scrambled facial features, such as eyes on the wrong side of the head, were also common. Many fomorians had one eye that far outsized the other. This "evil eye" emitted light and differed in iris color as well as pupil shape, often resembling a cat's eye.

Their deformed bodies were covered in large warts, wandering pustules, and weeping lesions. Internally, their veins and organs bulged and lurched. Their skin tone commonly matched that of hill giants, but also ranged from purplish-gray to pale white. Hairlessness was relatively common among fomorians of both sexes, though some were known to have scattered patches of wriggling hair that was as tough as wire. There was no single odor associated with them – some left no scent, while others smelled strongly due to rainbow-hued fluids produced by overactive sweat glands.

They largely wore makeshift scraps of cloth, They tended to work any bits of metal or pieces of armor they could find and scavenge into their clothes for added protection.

Personality

Fomorians were known to be very chaotic evil-aligned creatures, greedy and disloyal by nature.

Abilities

Due to sometimes having enlarged ears and/or noses,

Fomorians were capable of passing their race's curse on to other creatures, which was a remnant of their race's lost spellcasting abilities. This was done by staring at creatures within of them with their "evil eye." Those afflicted would temporarily have their bodies twisted and deformed, subjected to the malice and pain they live with, and in some cases even immobilize them.

A number of fomorians sought to regain the arcane glory their people once had. Some went about this by making warlock pacts with members of the Gloaming Court or eldritch entities buried deep underground. Such fomorians gained a mastery over shadows.

Combat

Due to their deformities, fomorians were incapable of throwing boulders the way other giants did.

A typical strategy for them was to sneak up behind their opponents and hit them as hard as possible.

History

Although the progenitor of this race was said to be one of four sons produced by Ulutiu and the giant goddess Othea, the fomorians descended from Karontor, son of Annam All-Father. Their place in the ordning once fell between hill giants and stone giants.

Fomorians were an advanced and learned race in ancient times, though a vile lust for power darkened their selfish hearts. They were an arrogant, entitled, and prideful people whose merit-based, hierarchical society valued knowledge and arcane power. while others settled in the Feywild and allied with the elves. However, the most ambitious and inquisitive of these early fomorians left their homes for the Inner Planes to pursue their goals unopposed.

Spurred on by Karontor to advance their rank in the ordning, they invaded the Feywild to subjugate its denizens and claim their magic. In response, the archfey banded together to defeat the fomorians, who then fled from the light of the surface world, down into the Feydark and Underdark.

While the strange ambient magic of the Underdark was thought to have twisted the fomorians, a generational curse placed by the victorious archfey reshaped their bodies to reflect the evil within their hearts, robbing them of their spellcasting abilities. However, fomorians who were away in the Inner Planes were unaffected by the curse.

Circa , after forces on the Moonshae Isles united against the unleashed Kazgaroth, Malar brought fomorians over from the Feywild to be its allies. The conflict dragged on for decades until the minions of Kazgaroth were driven to isolated corners of the Moonshaes. The fomorians conquered the firbolgs, forcing them into slavery.

Circa , most of the fey, including the fomorians, abandoned their domains in the Moonshaes and retreated back into the Feywild.

In 1369 DR, fomorians were among the alliance of giant-kin that sieged the Wynn Castle to retrieve Queen Brianna and her newborn child.

Following the Spellplague, a group of fomorians loyal to Queen Connomae and their servitors entered parts of the Moonshae Isles from the Feywild, taking up residence in Dernall Forest and conquering Oman's Isle in 1479 DR.

Society

Fomorian society was based around viciousness, with the cruelest and strongest among them typically taking the role of leader. They were also highly patriarchal, typically treating their women and children no better than slaves. Acts of violence were common among them.

In the Feydark, fomorian rulers were typically known to enforce their rule through fear and oppression. They were notorious for being incredibly paranoid, fearful of insurrection, and having extensive spy networks that they relied on to oversee their domains. This paranoia wasn't unwarranted through, for fomorians often plotted against one another and schemed to usurp the power of those who ruled them.

Diet

Fomorians could live on little to no food for many weeks. Some typical foods eaten by those underground included fungi, lichen, various types of plants, bats, fish, and mice. They especially favored the taste of large mammals.

Homelands

Fomorians were primarily known to inhabit the Underdark They could be found throughout the Underdark, but were most prevalent in the Middledark. Proper cities or holdings were unheard of to most creatures in the Underdark, though all-fomorian settlements certainly existed.

Their lairs within the Underdark typically were abundant in fish, water, and mushroom forests. They marked their territories in a variety of macabre manners, such as painting their cavern walls with blood, stitching together limbs and body parts to make mockeries of the creatures killed, or simply by using the corpses of their enemies. Beyond these underground realms, solitary fomorians were occasionally found in mountain caves and abandoned mines.

In northwest Faerûn, a clan of fomorians lived in the southern arm of the Spine of the World, near the Dungeon of the Ruins. In Anauroch, they could occasionally be found in the fertile areas of the Plain of Standing Stones, where they competed with local hill giants for resources and would even resort to eating the flesh of other giants.

In the Underdark they were known to inhabit Tethyamar and the kingdom of Xothaerin, the latter of which was home to scattered tribes. Under the island of Gwynneth in the Moonshae Isles, a coalition of fomorian tribes ruled by nine powerful families quarreled with the fey of the surface.

In the Feydark, fomorians lived in incredibly large caves full of glowing crystals and bioluminescent fungi. These domains had no central court organizing or ruling over them all.

Besides the Feywild, fomorians could be found in the plane of Acheron and reclusive bands of them could be found in hidden caverns in the Elemental Plane of Earth.

Languages

Fomorians were typically known to speak Giant and Undercommon.

Religion

Some claimed that fomorians held esteem for no deities,

Most fomorians believed that the prophesied king of giants would be born as a fomorian and would not realize his destiny at first, but when he did, he would be transformed into a giant larger than any titan, and subsequently transform the entire fomorian race also.

Relationships

Within the Ordning, fomorians like other giant-kin were considered "maug". Among giantkind, the only races that were known to tolerate them were frost giants, hill giants, and ogres. A large number of the fomorians that inhabited the Ice Spires region were known to perform manual labor for them, jobs that were often dangerous or distasteful, in exchange for food and lodging of poor quality.

They were known to forge alliances with other creatures when it suited them

Among the denizens of the Feywild, fomorians were almost never members of the Seelie and Unseelie courts. Throughout their existence, eladrin were one of the races most hated by fomorians due to their world views being such polar opposites.

Fomorians were often known to engage in slavery. myconids, and spriggans.

Other creatures that they were often known to keep within their lairs or settlements included boggles, derros, giant beetles, carrion crawlers, gorgons, ropers, and troglodytes. Out of all the dragon types in the Realms, they were most likely to ally themselves with deep dragons.
