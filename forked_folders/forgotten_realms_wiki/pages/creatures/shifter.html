Shifters, also sometimes called the weretouched, were a race of humanoids descended from interbreeding between lycanthropes and humans. Fierce and wild-spirited, shifters were feared by many as much as their shapeshifting brethren.

Description

Shifters resembled humans, their close brethren, but with distinctively bestial features such as sharp teeth, claws, pointed ears, or fur.

However, to a human's eye, the resemblance was less obvious than the differences. Unlike humans, shifters had flat, animal-like noses, larger than usual eyes that allowed them to see better in low light, heavy eyebrows, pointed ears, skin that was often of a brownish shade, and much more hair, also commonly brown, than was typical for the common human.

Lifespan

Shifters lived about as long humans did on average.

Personality

Shifters were commonly self-reliant and free-spirited.

Culture

Shifters were often feared wherever they went, much like their lycanthrope ancestors. This was due to a common misconception that all lycanthropes were invariably and irreversibly evil, including shifters. Though false, this conception shaped the lives of shifters, who rarely achieved a prominent status in civilized society. Instead, shifters preferred either a nomadic lifestyle or living close to the edges of society, supporting it as fishers, trackers, hunters, guides, or scouts. Others took up the call of an adventurer's life while yet others gave in to the expectations of society, letting their primal bloodlust fuel them in a life of crime.

Religion

Shifters favored gods of nature, the moon, and primal strength. Malar worship emphasized embracing their inner savagery and was often passed down through shifter families dating back to their lycanthrope ancestors. Those who turned away from Malarite faith generally gravitated toward either Selûne or Silvanus depending on whether they viewed their bestial heritage as more of a burden or a blessing respectively.

Homelands

Just like lycanthropes, shifters could be found scattered throughout Faerûn in the late 15 century DR, but a few gathered in larger communities. The Werewoods near Baldur's Gate and the Glimmerwood in Luruar were two such locations where lycanthropes were found in larger numbers and hence, as a result, so were shifters. However, most shifters, even in these regions, stayed away from the dark influence of their ancestors. Other large shifter communities were found in the Great Dale and the Forest of Lethyr, as well as Dambrath, where the local culture was xenophobic and had a hatred for drow.

Shifters could also notably be found in the Dalelands, Durpar, Luskan, the Moonshae Isles, Rashemen, Sembia, and Tethyr.

Types of Shifters

Different shifters manifested different features when assuming their more bestial shapes. While some types of shifter were more commonly associated with descent from a specific type of lycanthrope, their ancestors did not dictate their abilities. Shifters could potentially manifest the features of multiple types as they increased their shapeshifting powers.

Longtooth shifters

Longtooth shifters were wolf-like, athletic, durable, and wise to the world around them. They were generally known to be descendants of werewolves.

Razorclaw shifters

Razorclaw shifters were cat-like, agile, subtle, and highly perceptive, which aided them in their hunts. They generally known to be descendants of weretigers.

Cliffwalk shifters

Cliffwalk shifters became agile and gained a much-improved climbing ability.

Beasthide shifters

Beasthide shifters were tough and resilient, making them hard to bring down. While they shifted, they became tougher still. They tended to be descendants of werebears or wereboars.

Swiftstride shifters

Also known as longstride shifters, they became fleeter of foot and were able to increase their speed for a time while shifting. They tended to be descendants of wererats or weretigers.

Wildhunt shifters

Wildhunt shifters were dog-like, resilient, and had the extraordinary ability to smell as well as a predatory animal, allowing them to track by scent and making them exceptional trackers. They tended to be descendants of werewolves.

Dreamsight shifters

Dreamsight shifters were the rarest of all. While shifting, they became more perceptive and gained the ability to speak with animals in a supernatural manner, increasing their ability to manage animals.

Gorebrute shifters

Gorebrute shifters gained a pair of horns that could be used in a natural charge attack, as well as enhanced strength.

On the Moonshae Isles, some shifters were descendants of the monster Kazgoroth, and were said to have the "blood of the Darkwalker".
