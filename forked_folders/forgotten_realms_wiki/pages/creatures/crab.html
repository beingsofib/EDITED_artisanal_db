Crab was a collective name given to a number of species of crustaceans found in the Realms and often used in culinary pursuits. There existed mundane and giant crabs, as well as saltwater and freshwater varieties.

Description

Crabs were foul-tempered crustaceans encased in shells with its colors differing depending on the species. They had powerful pincer claws that, depending on the crab's side, could open an adventurer's armor like a tin can. They were also known to be able to become one's familiar or an animal companion, while giant crabs could be dominated through magic and turned into fine mounts.

  • Bigclaw, a species of giant blue-shelled crab, larger than river snipper crab, that inhabited the Soshenstar River in Chult.
  • Blue king crab, one of the biggest and most expensive species of crab from the Frozenfar.
  • Devil crab, an edible species of crab eaten in the Vast.
  • Dikanie, a species of orange crab from Kara-Tur.
  • Emperor crab, a lesser but dangerous species of king crab from the Sea of Moving Ice with succulent meat.
  • Finialian's crab, a species of brightly-colored crab found in Scardale.
  • Flameshell crab, a species of red-shelled crab.
  • Giant crabs were a common long danger found in the Underdark and an edible prey to stronger creatures, svirfneblins, and adventurers. They were also commonly found in the Sea of Fallen Stars. There existed giant crab ghosts in the eastern lands of Kara-Tur.
  • Hermit crab, a species of crap that used discarded shells, found in the Sea of Swords and Waterdeep.
  • Hulking crab, a species of crab bigger than the giant crab.
  • Purple marsh crab, a species of freshwater river crab from Chult.
  • River snipper, a species of giant brown-shelled crabs that inhabited the Soshenstar River in Chult.
  • Sand crab, a species of crab pests.
  • Siege crab, a titanic crab and a living siege machine created in the time of the Tenth Serôs War.
  • Soft-shelled crab, various species of edible crabs.
  • White helmet, an anemic-colored cave-dwelling crab from the Sea of Moving Ice.
  • Wooly crab, a species of the Sea of Moving Ice crab with wool-like spines on their shells.
  • Yeti crab, a species of large deep-sea saltwater crab from the Sea of Moving Ice found near seafloor thermal vents.

Ecology

Crabs were commonly cooked across the Realms. In Calimshan, crabs were served boiled and stuffed.

In Cormyr, soft-shelled crabs were breaded and fried, served dusted in sugar.

Along the Dragon Coast, clams and crabs were abundant in Westgate, where the city's poor descended on the beaches to dig for their next meals.

In the Dalelands, freshwater crabs were caught for food in Archendale, along the shores of the River Arkhen.

On the Sword Coast, crab meat was used to make crab cakes, like specialty dish of the Elfsong Tavern in Baldur's Gate.

In the Frozenfar, numerous crab species inhabited the Sea of Moving Ice, but smaller crab was fished out of Maer Dualdon and used as a food source by the locals. The meat was nutritious but quite bland in flavor. The region's crabs consumed serpent stars and fishers sometimes used them as crab fishing bait.

Sharkey's Bar & Grill of Ravens Bluff in The Vast sold live-caught crabs, and served cooked giant crab's legs or whole giant crabs. Crabs were also the specialty of the Fresh Fish shop and The Lambent Eye, where crabs were fried in lemon beer. And Fish Tails served fishcakes made with various sea-critters, including crab. And in the nearby Mossbridges, the Golden Goblet Inn had stuffed devil crab on the menu.

Notable Crabs

  • Karkinos, a massive and powerful legendary bigclaw crab who terrorized Chult in the late 15 century DR.
  • Suratuk's giant red crab, a creation of an eccentric Calishite artist who permanently dyed and pained companion and mount animals in the late 15 century DR.
  • Suratuk's giant sunset crab, another victim of Suratuk's paintbrush.
