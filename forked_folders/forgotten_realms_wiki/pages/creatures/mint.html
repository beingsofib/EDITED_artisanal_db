Mint was a plant commonly used as a form of seasoning in the Realms, primarily classified as a type of herb. It was noted for its aromatic qualities, sweet flavor,

Availability

  • Aurora's Whole Realms Catalogue sold mint under its "Aurora's Larder" section at a cost of 3 copper pieces per ,
  • In the Dalelands, mint could be found in Archendale, Daggerdale, Deepingdale, and Mistledale.
  • Traveling rations in the land of Calimshan often included mint leaves for making tea.
  • The Inn of the Weary Pilgrim had a garden of mint.
  • In the Sword Coast North, mint sprigs and leaves were sold in the markets of Waterdeep.
  • In south Faerûn, the port city of Khaerbaal had a public garden where herbs like mint grew.
  • In the Vilhon Reach, mint could be purchased in the city of Hlondeth.
  • Mint could be found growing wild in the forest of Cormanthor, in High Forest, and across the Sword Coast North.
  • In Interior Zakhara, mint was cultivated in the herb garden of the fortress Sarahin. They was also available in parts of north Zakhara, being enjoyed by Caliph Harin al-Hayif of Liham.

Varieties

  • Catmint, a member of the mint family that grew wild in Cormanthor.
  • Pale mint, a member of the mint family that was endemic to the scorched sands of the Calim Desert.
  • Pennyroyal
  • Peppermint
  • Spearmint
  • Ticklemint, a member of the mint family endemic to the wilds of Icewind Dale.
  • Wintermint

Usages

Food & Drink

  • Greentart, a gnomish ale served at Le PoTeete Café in Ravens Bluff.
  • Mint ale, a Sembian gnomish ale.
  • Mint was sometimes candied, much in the same way as one candied fruits.
  • Mint jelly.
  • Mint leaves were commonly brewed in water to make mint tea in both Faerûn
  • Mint water, water that's been laced with mint to give it a refreshing taste. Whenever water was flavored with mint it was referred to as having been "minted".
  • Mint could be used in the making of a variety of wines, including mint wine, the fern-and-mint wine produced by the druids of Kirinwood, and a type of mushroom wine produced by Klarinthra Larntrout.
  • Moonslake was made by boiling water with crushed wild mint, then straining that water to remove any leaf residue, before using that water to cut apple cider.
  • In some places, kaeth was mixed with mint leaves sprinkled over its surface and prepared by stewing the beans in a pot over a fire or a hearth.
  • Runsun, a variety of common trail food—a pie made with meat cooked in spinach-and-mint or spinach-and-hot-pepper simmer sauce.
  • One of the listed libations in Volo's Guide to Spirits and Spectres called for a garnish of mint.

Magic

  • Mint could be used in the brewing of a variety of different potions, including potions of accuracy, defense, and force.
  • Duthlah'hass oil, a type of osssra, was made in part with mint.

Medicinal

  • In the Moonsea region, healers from Phlan used juniper and mint as components in their healing ointments. These salves possessed strong but pleasant scent of both. Others from that region used a combination of mint and raspberries to create a medicinal herbal tea.
  • Tea could also be made with mint for the purposes of soothing an upset stomach or inducing drowsiness.
  • The variant of speak with dead known by Tessaril Winter used sprigs of mint as a material component.

Miscellaneous

  • The aromatic quality of mint lent itself to use in tubs of water to make herbal baths. In the port city of Uthmere, this was especially the case when washing hair.
  • Some mrathaera was flavored or scented like mint, from its use in the herbal mixtures boiled to make it. This variety was mostly used among lovers desiring to please their partners.
  • Could be used to make a green dye.
  • Could be combined with other herbs as part of incense.

Notable Users

Businesses

  • The Hungry Man, a restaurant in Arabel, typically served mint alongside a platter of boiled carrots and other greens.'
  • Moon Mountain Brewery
  • The Moonwyvern Inn in Luruar treated the buckets of water that it kept on standby, in case of fires, with mint so as to keep away stagnant odors.
  • The Old Wheel, a club in Crimmor, offered a dressing of vegetable juice with mint for its greensplatters.
  • One of the peculiar cloaks served at the Owlroost Head Inn in Purkul was a dish of squid-stuffed yams, whose squids were boiled in lime juice laced with lemon slices and mint leaf garnish.
  • The Ship's Prow, an inn in Waterdeep, made its own mint water by lacing water brought over from wells near Amphail with crushed mint.
  • Stargath House in Purskul was known for it's "Welcome Soup", a creamy stew of smoked bacon, black beans, and chives garnished with mint.

Creatures

  • Asperii prized mint leaves.
  • Feystags had a fondness for wild mint.

Individuals

  • Dawan Pax, a legendary druid active in the North during the 15 century DR, made an invigorating tonic from mint, peaches, and ginger
  • The goddess Lurue was widely believed to have a special love for mint.

History

In the , the Downunda Patisserie in Ravens Bluff offered mint & green apple cookies as one of its Summer Specials. The bakery charged 5 cp for a singular cookie and 3 sp for a dozen.

Trivia

  • Some of the more expensive healing draughts were said to have a taste profile of clover, honey, mint, and zzar.
  • The scent of this herb was one of many that saurials expressed through their glands to communicate emotions. The scent of mint expressed an emotion of remorse.
