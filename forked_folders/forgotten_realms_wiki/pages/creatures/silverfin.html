Silverfin was a term for varieties of fish found in the waters of Faerûn.

Availability

Silverfin were common in the rivers and oceans of Faerûn.

Central Faerûn

Large schools of whitefish were encountered in Sea of Fallen Stars, and the waters of the Dragonmere and the Neck that lay between Cormyr, Sembia, and the Dragon Coast.{{cite web

Silverfin were also found in the Wyvernwater in Cormyr, and were a common catch of the 'mist fishers' of Immersea. In Immersea's fishpacking plants, silverfin were preserved in salt or oil and stored in kegs for transport and sale both within and beyond Cormyr. and in the mansions of Arabel.

Silverfin were also caught in the Moonsea. In cities like Hulburg on the north shore, Moonsea silverfins were cured in smoking-houses and there sold to traders. Moonsea silverfins would then be traded by fishmonger's wagons inland to places like Voonlar.

Sea of Swords and the Sword Coast

Silverfin was common in the Sea of Swords. On the island of Mintarn in the Sea of Swords, tiny whitefish were farmed in breeding pools and fed with kitchen scraps.

They also caught by the fishermen of Kalathtyr, Amn, which lay at the mouth of the River Specie where it flowed into the Sea of Swords.

Silverfin, both saltwater and freshwater, were the most common seafood on offer in Waterdeep's fish markets, all year around.{{cite web

Western Heartlands

The River Chionthar was another source of silverfin, and they were fished around Berdusk and Scornubel.

The North

In the Silver Marches, silverfin were almost the species found in the River Rauvin, River Surbrin, and lesser rivers of the region. The majority of human travelers here could tell a silverfin from a bluefin or other fish, and how to net or cook one simply.

  • A traveler could cook a silverfin or other fish simply by putting it in a skillet over a fire.
  • The namesake silverfin species was considered best cooked by pan-frying in wine, brandy, or ale, but was typically rolled in mud with butterweed or wild onion or garlic, and baked in a fire. They were also preserved in oil.
  • Roast silverfin was a common feature of the eveningfeast menu in respectable inns and restaurants in coastal towns and cities.
  • Bowls of pickled silverfin and other small fish were often on the eveningfeast menu in high-class eateries,
  • Spiced silverfin was among the spreads served on biscuits at the Elfstone Tavern in Waterdeep.
  • Silverfin daggers—slivers of fish on lemon or lime slices—were served as canapés at The Gentle Mermaid in Waterdeep.
  • Smoked silverfin was served in a buffet at morningfeast at the Palace of Waterdeep for Piergeiron Paladinson and guests.
  • In Waterdeep, businesses sold 'coldsear silverfin', which was grilled silverfin meat frozen in ice and delivered to customers for defrosting and consumption.
  • Stuffed silverfin was served at Firefall Keep, seat of the Summerstar family of Cormyr, in 1365 DR.
  • Crusted silverfin cheese was a smallbite served at functions in Cormyr.
  • Smoked silverfin on skewers, locally caught, were commonly served at the Axefall club in Scornubel.
  • Plate of Silver was a dish of grilled silverfin drizzled with raspberry juice and vinegar served at Stargath House in Purskul, Amn.
  • Lightly smoked silverfin in a cream broth with fried yellow peppers, mushrooms, and asparagus was served at Blackthorn's Brew in Velen, Tethyr.
  • Barrels of silverfin in sauce were kept as provision in Shadowdark House in Daerlun.
  • Butter-fried Moonsea silverfin was a seasonal treat in Voonlar.

Arcane

An eye from a silverfin fish caught with a silver fishhook was once used as a random ingredient included in the tincture used in the shell creation stage of item enchantment in order to introduced deliberate randomness to a magic item.

Species

Some particular types of fish were considered silverfin:

Silverfin: Known simply as "silverfin", this namesake species had silvery scales on their sides and silvery fins with black roots that effectively highlighted their appearance, and grew to the size of trout. They were abundant, lived in freshwater, saltwater, and brackish water, and they consumed insects, worms, small eels, and frogs. Hence, they were easy to catch by even amateur fishers, even with nets or baskets. They had a taste like bass and kept well in oil.

Blindfin: Blindfin were a voracious subterranean fish found in pools in Downshadow in Undermountain. These were mutated from silverfin by the Spellplague.

Luslar: A blind species of fish very similar to Sword Coast silverfin that swam in Lake Thalmiir in the Underdark. These were fished by the Sschindylryn drow and preyed on by predatory eels.

Trout: Some kinds of trout were considered silverfin, at least in dwarven cuisine.
