Flame flowers were a rare plant native to the Elemental Plane of Fire.

Description

These flowers were composed entirely of fire, and appeared and glowed much like individual flames.

Ecology

Flame flowers propagated their seeds by exploding in a burst of fire. Touching a flame flower with anything other than fire could trigger this blast, which could be dangerous for creatures within a .

They grew best in cooled lava.

History

A garden known as the Hall of Flame Flowers was carefully cultivated in the catacombs beneath the Flaming Brazier—the grand temple to Kossuth within the city of Bezantur, Thay—as of the mid-to-late 14 century DR. Located in a large chamber beneath the temple's Inner Sanctum, the meandering paths and thousands of flame flowers offered a serene place for meditation.

Flame flower motifs were sometimes used to decorate wizard robes. Chevesh of Airspur wore such decoration as of the late 15 century DR.
