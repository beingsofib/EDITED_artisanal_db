Sphinxes were a race of intelligent, immortal magical beasts. It was said that they once held a vast kingdom given to them by the gods in an effort to teach mortals the way of truth. The sphinxes had their own methods, however, by teaching mortals to think for themselves with riddles and parables. Four common subraces of sphinx included the androsphinx, criosphinx, gynosphinx, and hieracosphinx.

Description

All sphinxes had leonine bodies with great feathery wings.

Ecology

Sphinxes tended to be very solitary creatures and were territorial. Many made their lairs within the ruins of ancient civilizations or in isolated caves. Alignment among sphinx subraces differed vastly; androsphinxes were known for their good nature, while hieracosphinxes were usually evil.

Reproduction

A gynosphinx could mate with any of the three male subraces of true sphinx. The subrace of the father determined the subrace of any male offspring.

Language

Sphinxes spoke their own language as well as Common and Draconic.

Types

Androsphinx

Androsphinxes were a male subrace of sphinx, with the body of male lions, masculine humanoid faces, and massive wings. They were physically powerful creatures with inherent magical powers and a terrifying roar. While they tended to be short-tempered and terse, androsphinxes possessed good-natured ideals and always attempted to do the right thing.

Gynosphinx

A gynosphinx was a female sphinx, with a winged female lion body and feminine humanoid facial features. While not as physically powerful as an androsphinx, the gynosphinx was still a capable combatant and possessed a number of magical powers. The gynosphinx was the sole female sphinx subrace.

Gynosphinxes preferred to pursue intellectual ideals. They loved to collect knowledge and ancient lore, and delight in solving riddles, puzzles and mental challenges.

Criosphinx

Criosphinxes were a male subrace of ram-headed sphinxes with low intelligence. They generally had the sole aim of finding a gynosphinx mate or collecting treasure.

Hieracosphinx

Another male subrace of sphinx, hieracosphinxes were evil, savage, and only animal-intelligent. They had heads similar to those of hawks.

Other Types

Other types of sphinxes purportedly existed on other worlds besides Faerûn; among these were the jackal-headed and predatory canisphinx, the wolf-headed, forest-dwelling luposphinx, and the reptile-faced, conversational saurosphinx. However, none of these were known to exist in the Realms.

The dracosphinx, an evil, red dragon-headed creature that lived on bleak cliff sites in the Old Empires, was not actually a sphinx at all and was likely confused with one for its similar leonine body features. Indeed, dracosphinxes came in two sexes and were infertile with true sphinxes. In wildspace, astrosphinxes were said to exist. These, too, were not true sphinxes.

Notable Sphinxes
