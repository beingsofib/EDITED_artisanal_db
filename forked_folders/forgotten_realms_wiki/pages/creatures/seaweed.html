Seaweed referred to a diverse array of marine algae found across the many seas of Toril, other worlds, and even other planes of existence.

Description

Most seaweed could be found growing in far spanning beds known as "forests", in seas as far down as , at depth zones where sunlight could be readily received. Some seaweed could also be found at the first few hundred feet beyond that, though it was limited to only the most hardiest of breeds.

On average seaweed grew to heights of . Their height and clustered density could cause minimal vision obstruction for creatures underwater. The average coloration of seaweed was varied brown and green hues, though they could also come in countless brilliant colors, such as purple.

Some, though not all, species of seaweed were edible and being able to identify which were which was considered an essential part of survival skills for any sailor. Other species could be quite poisonous if consumed.

Seaweed often found its way entangled in fishing nets, left to be discarded in harbors where it could be hazardous to ships Some cities might have a group of people tasked with removing it, such as the Guild of Watermen who maintained Deepwater Harbor.

Species

Arame: A species of seaweed that could be found in the waters of the Dragon Reach.

Armistice Seaweeds: The planet Armistice in Winterspace was home to several species of seaweed that were highly toxic to non-native lifeforms.

Dleem: An olive-green, stringy seaweed in the Sea of Swords that grew in starfish-shaped clumps. They partly clung to rocks and partly floated "adrift" in passing water. When roughly boiled a long period, the seaweed lost its tough, chewy consistency and would begin to take on the flavor of whatever it was boiled with.

Dulce: An edible species of seaweed found in the Sea of Swords.

Eel Grass: An edible species of seaweed found in the Sea of Swords.

Golden Angel Seaweed: A species of seaweed that nereids were often mistaken for whilst submerged in water.

Hijiki: A species of seaweed that could be found in the waters of the Dragon Reach.

Kelp: A common edible subtype of seaweed for which there were a great variety of in the Realms, including magical species.

Kelpie: A malevolently intelligent and carnivorous breed of seaweed that could shapeshift its form into a beautiful woman.

Kombu: A species of seaweed that could be found in the waters of the Dragon Reach.

Nori: An edible species of seaweed that could be found in the Celestial Sea.

Salt Ivy: A species of seaweed unique to the Moonsea. Growing in clumps of green strands that measured in length, it was considered a navigational hazard to ships and often fouled fishing nets.

Sargasso Seaweed: Seaweed that drifted free-floatingly on the surface of the ocean, though this was mainly a concern for ships propelled by oars.

Strangle weed: A carnivorous breed of kelp with an animal-level of intellect that was found in relatively warm sea water. It was indistinguishable from normal seaweed.

Seaweed of Water Breathing: Magical seaweed that granted the ability to temporarily breath underwater to those who ate it.

Related

Kelp angler: A sentient, carnivorous breed of plant creatures that looked like jellyfish-shaped clusters of kelp.

Sluk: A species of seaweed endemic to Wildspace. Rather than photosynthesis, they thrived off of magic. And much like sargasso seaweed was to terrestrial ships, it posed a hazard to the spelljammers that sailed through Wildspace.

Habitats

  • In interior Faerûn, seaweed could be found across the temperate and tropical depths of the Sea of Fallen Stars, including in the areas known as the Dragon Reach and the Dragonmere.
  • In north Faerûn, seaweed could be found in the Moonsea region's lake of the same name.
  • In northwest Faerûn, seaweed could be found in Icewind Dale's Lac Dinneshere.
  • In south Faerûn, seaweed could be found in great abundance in the Lake of Steam, wherein it grew in larger sizes and caused those creatures that fed upon it to grow larger in turn.
  • In southwest Faerûn, seaweed could be found in the Shining Sea.
  • In west Faerun, seaweed could be found in the Sea of Swords and the wider Trackless Sea that separated the continent from Maztica. And in the Sunset Mountains, Wolover's Keep had a lake surrounding it with a long stretch of floating seaweed, used to conceal the presence of strangle weed.
  • In Thingulph and the waterways of the Thindol Basin.
  • Beyond Faerûn, seaweed could be found in the seas of Kara-Tur and Zakhara, being most often encountered in coastal waters.
  • On the Malatran Plateau in Kara-Tur, seaweed could be found in the Sleepy Lake.
  • Elsewhere in Realmspace, the planet Karpri had a massive bed of floating seaweed and kelp in its equatorial region, so thick that it could support weights exceeding a few thousand pounds.

Beyond the Prime Material

  • On Arborea, seaweed could be found in the oceanic layer Ossa.
  • In the Domains of Dread, seaweed could be found within maritime domains. Most notably, Draga Salt-Biter's domain of Sargoss, whose waters were crowded by a gargantuan mass of sargasso seaweed, and was home to a number of kelpies.
  • On Mount Celestia, seaweed could be found in the oceanic layer Lunia.
  • Among the Inner Planes, seaweed could be found in the Elemental Plane of Water, where it sometimes took the form of large floating spheres that grew equally in all directions.
  • In the Outlands, seaweed could be found in Tir fo Thuinn, the divine realm of the Celtic god Manannan Mac Lir.
  • On the plane of Elysium it could be found in the layer of Thalasia, where Surminare's divine realm of Selkies' Grotto was hidden beneath vast mazes of seaweed and kelp, and in the River Oceanus, where it flowed out of Thalasia and into the layer of Belierin.

Availability

Places in the Realms where one could purchase seaweed included:

  • Aurora's Whole Realms Catalogue sold dry seaweed under its "Wizards' Laboratory" section for 2 silver pieces per .
  • In the markets of Immersea, taken from the Mistfisher Bay of the Dragonmere.
  • In Caer-Dineval, where residents harvested edible seaweeds from Lac Dinneshere.
  • In Daggerford, one could find seaweed for sale at the Flying Fish fish market.
  • In Kara-Tur, on the island nations of Kozakura
  • On the island of Nimbral.
  • On the island of Ruathym, where residents kept steady supplies of edible seaweed.
  • In Ravens Bluff, though only members of the Fishmongers Association were allowed to publicly sell or barter seaweed as it was classified as a type of seafood.
  • In Waterdeep, Felhaur's Fine Fish sold jars of pickled seaweed.

Uses

Camouflage

  • Some creatures had natural body colorations that lent themselves well to blending in amongst seaweed, such as giant pikes, the spines of pungi rays, and weed eels. The latter, when gathered in large groups, could even be mistaken for tangled masses of seaweed.
  • Through use of their color shifting abilities, giant octopi often disguised their tentacles as seaweed.
  • Some ambush predators, such as giant pikes, and will o'seas would hide within beds of seaweed waiting for their prey.
  • The most preferred area of combat amongst aquatic elves was forests of seaweed, for their typically greenish-silver skin and often stringy hair, which ranged in complementary shades of green and blue, helped them to blend in and hide amongst the weeds just as well as surface elves hid amongst the trees. They also had a propensity for hiding all manner of spring-loaded booby-traps and magical entrapments within beds of seaweed.
  • Some aquatic creatures made their homes near thick seaweed beds due to the concealment they afforded, such as locathah, hai nu, and reavers.

Clothing

  • Seaweed was commonly woven into clothing by nixies, some aquatic elves,
  • In the case of nixies, they typically wove colorful seaweed into tight-fitting wraps for women and loincloths for men.
  • Ningyo used lengths of woven seaweed in the bracelets and necklaces they fashioned.
  • In the undersea realms of Serôs, all aquatic races commonly wore accessories fashioned from woven kelp fronds, including jewelry, belts, harnesses, and bandoliers.
  • Some Umberlants wore clothing woven out of seaweed, carried accessories wrapped in it, or simply had decorative seaweed designs on their clothes.

Food

  • Seaweed was widely consumed by many animals and humanoids as either a primary or supplementary part of their diets. These included giant octopi, giant otters, island giants, lung wangs, ningyo, selkies, and zoveri.
  • Across the Sword Coast the species dleem, due to its ability to take on the flavor of whatever it was boiled with, was often used to bulk up soups, stews, and other dishes. In Waterdeep in particular, it was used as part of Tharval-and-dleem.
  • Kelp in particular was a staple in the diets of many of the aquatic races of Serôs, going as far back as the early days of the Aryselmalyr Empire, where the aquatic elven rulers taught many how to cultivate and grow it, even in places that it was previously never found naturally. It was actively farmed by merfolk, shalarin, and the Noble Malenti of Kymal. Beyond Serôs, seaweed was also actively farmed in the seas surrounding Zakhara by the pahari.
  • The Blushing Mermaid in Baldur's Gate served a rather revolting small-fish stew consisting of rotting baitfish boiled with sea salt and seaweed.
  • Braethyn, a thick broth in Northkeep that used sea ivy.
  • Seaweed was used by the alesmith Bowser to create the exotic brew Seaweed Ale.
  • Among the islands of the Crowded Sea, those who worshiped the savage god Pag restricted themselves to a diet of fish and seaweed.
  • In parts of Kara-Tur, seaweed was collected by fisherfolk to be consumed raw or dried for use in many ways. Kozakurans used sheets of dried nori, for example, by crumbling them into their rice or using them as a wrapper into which they stuffed rice. and Flirin's Sea Morsels in Ravens Bluff.
  • The Moonstone Mask offered patrons fried dulce seaweed.
  • Nimbrians harvested various types of seaweed for use in teas and wines.
  • Seaweed salad, a type of salad that heavily featured seaweed.
  • Seaweed could be used to produce sauces. In the village of Uthmere, residents produced strongly flavored sauces from concoctions of herbs, spices, Great Dale wildflowers, and diced and cooked seaweed. And in Ravens Bluff, Flirin of Flirin's Sea Morsels made seaweed sauces using secret recipes that she herself had devised.
  • In the Sembian village of Tulbegh, farmers were renowned for the baby food that they produced by mixing osquip milk from a mulch of seaweed harvested from the Sea of Fallen Stars.

Housing

  • The aquatic elves who lived near Evermeet constructed some of their buildings out of seaweed.
  • Nixies, beyond just weaving seaweed into clothing, typically used the material to construct their homes.
  • Ningyo sometimes constructed temporary shelters by lining underwater ledges with seaweed.
  • When not residing in natural caverns, the nomadic locathah of Serôs commonly wove tents out of hydrozoan branches and kelp fronds.
  • The shalarins of Serôs coated kelp fronds in a pearl-like substance, hardening it into a tough material, which they would then apply as siding to their architecture in a style reminescent of what Surface Worlders called "wattle and daub".
  • Beds of floating seaweed sometimes served as a lair for aquatic creatures, such as sea hags. Likewise, the balls of seaweed on the Plane of Water were sometimes used by native outsiders, such as tritons, as lairs.

Magic

  • Seaweed was so commonly of use to wizards that Aurora's Whole Realms Catalogue sold dry seaweed under its "Wizards' Laboratory".
  • Certain seaweeds were quite valuable to alchemists.
  • Seaweed could be affected by most spells that manipulated vegetation, such as charm plants, entangle and recover trail.
  • Arame, hijiki, and kombu seaweed could all be used as material components in certain spells. Likewise, salt ivy could be used as a component in certain water-based spells.
  • Clutter path could substitute seaweed in place of the weeds required to cast it.
  • Sargasso, a spell that could conjure a thick bed of sargasso seaweed, required a small amount of seaweed as a material component.
  • Samprey's sensible sea sphere required a necklace fashioned from kelp as a spell component.
  • Sarphoon's seaspell could transform seaweed and seawater into exact duplicates of nearby living fish.
  • Under candle magic, seaweed could serve as one of the three ingredients needed to produce a candle with the effect of divination.
  • A variation of Keoghtom's ointment could be created using seaweed.
  • Sea hags could create simulacrums of other humanoids using a variety of flotsam, such as seaweed, slime, and half-eaten fish.
  • Serôsian wizards used mixtures of magical kelp to create the inks they wrote spell scrolls with.
  • Aquatic elf wizards frequently used papyrus-like sheets of thick, fibrous seaweed for their spell scrolls. These were alchemically treated so as to prevent rotting and water damage.
  • To get around the hurdles of imbibing in a fluid volume, both Serôsian wizards and aquatic elf wizards in other seas produced unguents, such as thick gelatinous pastes of kelp, to mimic potions. Additionally, the aquatic elves of Faerûn bred magical seaweed that could mimic the effects of potions when ingested.
  • The magical tome Lions of Tomorrow had parchment consisting of seaweed soaked in a solution of iodine in order to ward off Geomantic divinations.

Medicine

  • Some seaweed could be used to produce healing salves.
  • Some seawoods could be used to produce cures against poisons.
  • Kelp in particular could be used treat thyroid glands and goiter, as well as applied internally or externally to relieve rheumatism.

Miscellaneous

  • The aquatic elves of Aumrauth constructed what they called "seawater sleeves" out of weaving whale rib-cages with seaweed, using them to transport "deep-caught" fish from the waters of the Shining Sea up to the surface for trade.
  • Bales of kelp were commonly used as a trading good in the barter economies of Serôs.
  • Imroad Gaskulyn of Saerloon used various seaweeds alongside gravel, wines, and vinegars to concoct pastes that imparted a patina, tarnish, or wear to the small metal items he produced forgeries of.
  • Merfolk sometimes used kelp and other seaweed in the construction of furniture, such as using it for padding in chairs and reclining sofas or filling clam-shaped "beds" filled with pallets of the stuff.
  • Both crabfolk and yurians were known to weave stuff out of seaweed.
  • Some kuo-toa fashioned fossilised shells into makeshift shields by wrapping a sticky, rope-like seaweed around them.
  • Some merfolk wove tapestries out of seaweed.
  • In the Ruined Kingdoms region of Zakhara, prior to the 14 century DR, it was not an uncommon practice to use seaweed to make parchment.
  • Certain seaweeds were quite valued by scent-makers,
  • Some sahuagins enchanted seaweed so that it would entangle and trap those who wandered close to their lairs.
  • Seaweed could be knotted together into nets, ropes, and rope ladders.
  • Whenever it was time for Umberlants to remove offerings from the stone altars found in shrines of Umberlee, they would sluice buckets full of sea water and seaweed to signify that the Sea Queen had taken what was rightfully hers.

Trivia

  • Duthlah'hass, a type of osssra, had a smell reminescent of burning seaweed.
  • The Maztican goddess Watil's portfolio of plant-life included seaweed.
  • Marels, merrow, saltwater trolls, sartani, sea hags, sea sprites, In some cases, even the hair of a sea elf could be mistaken for seaweed.
  • Sea druids, the specialty priests of Deep Sashelas, were skilled in identifying aquatic plants like seaweed and in effortlessly swimming through beds of them without leaving any sort of trail.
  • The venom of a lock lurker had a smell reminiscent of seaweed.
