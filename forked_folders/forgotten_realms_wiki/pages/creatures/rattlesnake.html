A rattlesnake was a common variety of viper and poisonous snake, named for a keratin segment at the end of its tail, that produced a loud rattling or buzzing noise when vibrated, to ward off predators.

Description

A rattlesnake could grow to be in length.

Behavior

The disposition of these snakes were once equated by the wizard Elminster with that of a cockatrice. When surprised, they were known to raise their heads and coil them slightly into an 's' shape to prepare for striking.

Abilities

The venom of a rattlesnake was less dangerous than that of some other snakes. It affected the nervous system, causing a paralysis in the breathing muscles that could lead to death by asphyxiation. and the bent priests of the Azuposi were notably immune to their venom.

Ecology

Habitats

Rattlesnakes were typically found in dry and rocky terrains. in parts of the Serpent Hills, and in Emory Wood.

Beyond Faerûn, rattlesnakes could be found in the bleak deserts of Maztica, such as the Pasocada Basin, as well as possibly in Zakhara.

Usages

These creatures were hunted by some, both for consumption and for the use of their snakeskin goods such as boots. The pelt of a rattlesnake was often worth around 1 to 4 gold pieces. In Maztica, their rattles were harvested for use as a material component in the spell snake charm.

Relationships

Rattlesnakes were symbols of the deities Sseth and Zaltec, who was sometimes described as having two fangs like those of a rattlesnake. And these creatures were among the commonly chosen totem animals for a totemic druid.

Trivia

  • The god Sseth infrequently manifested himself as the sound of a rattlesnake's rattle to warn his priests of danger, particularly imminent ambushes. Additionally, the third highest rank within his hierarchal church had the title "Rattler".
  • Rattlesnakes were among the creatures that a psionicist could emulate with the power animal affinity.
  • Some serpentfolk were found to develop rattles on their tails similar to those of rattlesnakes.
