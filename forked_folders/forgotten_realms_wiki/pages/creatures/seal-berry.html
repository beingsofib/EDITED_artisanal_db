Seal berries was a type of berry used as a preservative.

Description

Seal berries were pea-sized berries that had a color and texture resembling seal skin.

Uses

Although they were inedible, the berries served as valuable preservatives. When a handful of berries was scattered over of meat, the meat was preserved indefinitely, even throughout the warmest months of spring and summer.
