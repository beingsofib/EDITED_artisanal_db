A snail was a variety of simple s that could be found on land, in the sea, and in bodies of freshwater. Those that inhabited bodies of saltwater were typically distinguished as sea snails.

Description

Like many mollusks, snails carried around a hard protective shell that often had a whorled pattern. These shells had an opening which was fitted around the rear of a snail. Whenever they felt threatened, a snail would retract its body into its shell.

Snails typically left trails of slime wherever they moved, which was at a notably slow speed. The underside of a snail had an adhesive quality to it, which allowed them to climb up near-vertical surfaces.

Behavior

Snails were generally docile creatures.

Varieties

Desert snail: A variety of land snail endemic to the sands of the Anauroch.

Giant snail: Monstrously sized snails.

Steaming Isle snails: On the Steaming Isles, a place in the Crowded Sea where all animals were gifted with great intellect and the power of speech, snails were the great theoretical scholars and pedants when it came to the Law of the Loregiver. They loved to engage in debates in their slow, deliberate way. And any questions asked of them tended to receive a long, theological answer.

Usage

Snails were used as a food item in many parts of the Realms. The manner in which snails were most commonly eaten in the Realms was hot and buttered. or as part of an eveningfeast meal at higher-class inns, taverns, and other eateries. Some variations on buttered snails included having them roasted on skewers and then dipped into butter, having the snails fried prior to being buttered, and some served them in spiced wine.

Some of the many establishments in the Realms known to serve hot & buttered snails included the Maid of the Moors in Mornbryn's Shield, the Fair Maid in Ripplestones, and The Raven's Glory in Ravens Bluff.

In Menzoberranzan, it was not uncommon to find drow dining upon spiced, steamed snails. And besides humans and demihumans, a number of lizardfolk were also notably fond of snails.

Their use as a food item was of such commonality in Daunting, a city in the Deep Kingdom, that its dwarven residents had snail farmers.

Dishes

  • Emerald eyes, a spicy Calishite mixture which had snails diced up.
  • Glemmeraeve soup, a Suzailan soup made with mustard, daeradorn wine, and simmered snails in gravy.
  • Platter of delights, a platter of small edibles, such as clams and snails. It was commonly served as part of Vaedrence, the second course in a drow banquet.
  • Pride of Velen, a specialty at Blackthorn's Brew.
  • Rif's Truffle Chowder, a specialty at the Underdark tavern Rif's Place, featured several meaty snails.
  • Skullcap treat, an ancient and quite legendary form of the customary Turmishan guest-dish, consisted of a upturned human skull filled with snails.
  • Snail-and-eel soup, dish made by the lizardfolk of the Vast Swamp.
  • Some regional variations of talyth used snails.
  • The Seven-Stringed Harp in Secomber offered patrons salted biscuits spread with snails.
  • The cooks at the Singing Sprite sometimes added snails to the warm cream sauce they drenched their spit roasted fowls in.

Magic

  • The spell surface sojourn required a snail shell as a material component.

Other

  • A number of freshwater and saltwater snails were harvested to make dyes.
  • Though not a common option, some kept snails as pets.
  • The elastic, though very tough "crust", of hardened snail slime left behind by some subterranean species could be used in a manner similar to white-wash.
  • Jalasss, a type of osssra, was made in part from a distillate of crushed snails.
  • Jesseret, a peppery tasting poison whose recipe was a closely guarded secret, required a particular sort of snail.
  • The perfume Bluestars was produced in part from the powdered shells of a particular species of snail, whereas the perfume Sunrise was produced by boiling snails, likely those of the maritime variety, alongside mussels and oysters in alcohol.
  • It was claimed by the coin lad Sorn Orlith that some snails could act as aphrodisiacs.

Ecology

Most land-dwelling snails were hermaphroditic, manufacturing both eggs and sperm cells.

Most land dwelling species also entered a state of dormancy, similar to hibernation, during winter months due to the scarcity of vegetation.

Diet

Snaills commonly fed upon vegetation. Some subterranean snail species fed upon fungi. Those that fed upon the fungi that grew in areas moderately-strong to very strong levels of faerzress had trace amounts of that magical radiation imparted into their flesh.

Some species of snail were, alarmingly, flesh eaters.

Habitats

These creatures were known to be found in Cormyr, parts of Northwest Faerûn, such as the High Forest, in the marshy waters of the High Moor, in the Serpent Hills, as well as in Undermountain.

In Kara-Tur, they were known to inhabit the Shao Mountains, where the locals referred to them in their language as 'siput'.

In Zakhara's Crowded Sea, land snails could be found on the Steaming Isles

Relationships

Snails were preyed upon by a number of wild creatures, including frogs and toads, saltors, and sea gulls.

History

Sometime in the 14 century DR, at The Salty Dog in Ravens Bluff, the wizard Mastraero of Calaunt lost in a game of chess to an unwashed sailor and was so embarrassed by this defeat that he turned all of the eye witnesses into snails. His opponent then revealed himself to be Ambassador Carrague, who proceeded to use his own magic to reverse the polymorphing.

Around that same century, the wizard Elminster visited Ed of the Greenwood on Earth with a Turmishan skullcap treat and sat down for a chat about that Vilhonna republic. The two sat together by Ed's fireplace, roasting the snails on bamboo skewers and dipping them in garlic butter. Ed would later find himself feeling sick from the evening of dining on snails, though Elminster was none the worst for ware.

On Eleasis 4, , Elminster had the mage Calabrista and several of her students over for tea, the mage wanting him to by test her students' aptitude for the Art. At this soiree he pranked her by pretending to be eating sandwiches made of snails and tree slugs, garnished with pepper and lemon-squeezings. He claiming they were all the rage among the Waterdhavian nobility and that it was considered good fortune to come across one with a still live snail or slug in it. Using illusion magic, he even made it appear that a slug was trying to undulate out of one of the sandwiches. After he had his fun messing with them, Elminster eventually revealed that the sandwiches actually consisted of merely mustard, cheese, and cucumbers.

Trivia

  • The DeSheers family of Ravens Bluff had a white snail shell as their coat of arms, crossed over with a red feather.
  • Dwarven priests that used the rune of power spell's Thundaril effect typically chose to have it polymorph trespassers into snails, slugs, and toads. Runehammers, which carried this type of spell, commonly did the same.
  • Some described the taste of piercers as resembling that of snails.
  • Harlent Gauntyl, a Waterdhavian noble in the 15 century DR, had a great fondness for snails. He loved eating them, racing them, and keeping them as pets. Some of the snails he owned were of the flesh eating variety and he made use of that whenever he needed to torture someone or, more rarely, dispose of a body.
  • Laeral Silverhand collected small carvings of snails, toads, and frogs, including some of which were carved from gemstones in Myth Drannor.
  • The Tempurans of the House of War in Ravens Bluff sometimes used spells in combat to polymorph their foes into snails. They would then swiftly snatch up the snail and label upon its shell with chalk the polymorphed victim's identity.
  • The legendary ranger Yornar was said to have devoted much of his time to casting minor spells that would provide shelters and food for bears and snails alike within the woodlands of northwest Faerûn.
