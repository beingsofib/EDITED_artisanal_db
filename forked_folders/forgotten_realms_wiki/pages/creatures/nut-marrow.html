Nut-marrow was an edible marrow-like plant.

Description

Nut-marrows were akin to other marrows like the courgette and zucchini, but on the inside, they were beige. The taste of their insides was like roast almonds.

Usage

These plants were grown by many as a food source. They rotted far more slowly than zucchini, and so were easily stored as winter food. They could be fried in thin slices, or in many other ways, such as making lubbath with diced and cooked nut-marrows.

History
