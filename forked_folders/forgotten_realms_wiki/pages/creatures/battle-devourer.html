Battle devourers were former intellect devourers that had been modified for more military purposes. They were considered virtually unkillable.

Description

While similar in shape to their psionic cousins, battle devourers possessed green brains and bodies covered in jagged, stone-like armor plating. Their claws were sharper and more blade-like than those of normal intellect devourers.

Behavior

These aberrations were mostly aggressive on sight towards other creatures. They were always chaotic evil.

Abilities

These former intellect devourers no longer retained their psionic abilities. Battle devourers were completely immune to acid, cold, fire, and sonic damage, and they possessed minor resistance to electricity. They could withstand punishment even from the most powerful foes, unless struck by a weapon with a +3 enchantment or higher.

Battle devourers were both dexterous and resilient, noted for their heightened alertness, darkvision, and keen senses of sight and hearing. They could use their fear gaze up to six times per day. It worked similar to the fear spell. This ability affected all creatures within an area that did not resist its effect, thus becoming frightened.

In melee, their claw attacks not only dealt significant damage but could also slow enemies upon a successful hit.

Battle devourers were also able to spy on others and relay messages.

Usage

These aberrations were used as spies and messengers in the Underdark.

History

Sometime before Year of Wild Magic, 1372 DR, these creatures were bred for use as spies and messengers in the Underdark.

Around the 1370s DR, one could occasionally encounter these beings on the Sword Coast North, for example in the service of certain members of the Arcane Brotherhood within their Host Tower.

Ecology

Battle devourers could either be found alone or in small to medium groups. They were almost always aggressive on sight.
