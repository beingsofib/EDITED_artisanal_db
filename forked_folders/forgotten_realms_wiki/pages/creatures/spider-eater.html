Spider eaters were large hornet-like creatures with huge wings akin to those on bats.

Description

These large flying beasts weighed around and had a wingspan of . They resembled a mix of giant hornets, giant bats, and giant spiders with long dexterous and venomous stingers.

Behavior

Spider eaters were so-named due to their tendency to lay their eggs in the bodies of paralyzed arachnids, and their near-uncanny ability to shrug off webs.

Combat

When engaging in combat, they would use their stingers to deliver a dose of paralyzing venom, then retreat to a safe distance and wait for the venom to take effect. They hated having to give up their prey, and ranged attacks with arrow, bolt or spell would quickly gain a reaction from the spider eaters in the form of a total assault.

Ecology

When laying eggs, the female spider eater would always choose a creature that was large or larger in size, paralyze it with her stinger, then insert the eggs into the body of the victim. The eggs would over the course of six weeks hatch and then devour their host, emerging into the world ready to take flight.

Usage

Training a spider eater to bear a rider usually took six weeks of hard work, but resulted in a large, dangerous, and agile mount. Fighting from atop a spider eater however was harder than just riding it and letting it do all the fighting, something it was quite capable of doing. They were able to carry up to as a light load, as a medium load, and up to as a heavy load.

Due to their usefulness as mounts and beasts of burden both, their eggs would often go for as much as 2,000 gp on the open market, while young went up to 3,000 gp apiece. Another 3,000 gp were usually required to then hire a professional to actually train the spider eater, unless one wanted to make the attempt oneself.
