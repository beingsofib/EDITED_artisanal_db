Brownfin was a species of freshwater fish found in streams and rivers of Cormyr, the King's Forest, and in the cold waters of Dathyl.

Ecology

Brownfins were carnivorous creatures like the majority of fish. Brownfins were bigger than minnows, which they consumed, while river eels were bigger than brownfins and consumed them in turn.

History

In 1348 DR, delicious roasted brownfin fish were a part of Florin Falconhand's and Narantha Crownsilver's morningfeast along with roasted rabbit and fresh cavanter buds.
