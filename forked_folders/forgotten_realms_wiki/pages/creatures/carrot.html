Carrot

Plant
nameCarrot
typeRoot vegetable
value1 gp per

Carrots were a type of hardy, root vegetable found in the Realms and often used in stews and salads.

Description

These vegetables grew to a much larger size on Toril compared to those on Earth.

Availability

Places in the Realms where carrots were known to be cultivated included:

  • In Interior Faerûn's Heartlands, carrots were cultivated in Cormyr Eveningstar was one such Cormyrian village that farmed the root vegetable. and the town of Ghars.
  • In interior Faerûn's Vilhon Reach, one could find carrots being cultivated in Turmish.
  • The Oak-Father's Boon inn in Mosstone of Tethyr served a spicy carrot and ginger soup as one of its specialities.
  • The North, where carrots were cultivated in the walled abbey of Goldenfields;
  • North Faerûn, where farmers in the Battledale and Mistledale regions cultivated carrots; In the Moonsea region, farmers in Elmwood cultivated carrots.
  • South Faerûn, where they were cultivated in the Arnaden region.
  • The Western Heartlands, such as in the gnome village of Anga Vled.
  • The Vast, at the village and vale of Maskyr's Eye; carrots and other root vegetables grew well in the vale.

Beyond Faerûn

  • Carrots were grown throughout much of Kara-Tur.

Usages

  • A whole dried carrot was required to cast the detect lycanthrope spell. Just a bit of a dried carrot acted as a material component for the spells darkvision, deeper darkvision, and mass darkvision.

Notable Users

  • Carrots, grilled prawns, bass, and onions on skewers were a commoners feast in Sembia and its city of Selgaunt.
  • Targos's Trip and Shuffle tavern served a stew of meat, potatos and carrots in the late 15 century DR.

History

In the , three Chosen of Silvanus got together on Sapra with the leaders of the Turmishan druidic order known as the Emerald Enclave and conducted a magical ritual that ended a famine that had been plaguing that nation. This caused all manner of food to instantaneously grow throughout the land, including carrots.
