Pleistocene animals, also called prehistoric animals,) were a group of large mammalian species that evolved in ancient times.

Notable Species

  • Axebeak: a flightless, carnivorous bird;
  • Baluchitherium: a gigantic, hornless ancestor of rhinoceros;
  • Cave bear
  • Dire wolf
  • Hyaenodon
  • Megaloceros, also called Irish deer;
  • Mammoth
  • Mastodon
  • Megatherium: a massive, herbivore ground sloth;
  • Neanderthal: also known as cavemen.
  • Oliphant: a sub-species of mastodon.
  • Phororhacos: a nonflying, predatory bird with powerful beak and a deep, braying voice;
  • Saber-toothed tiger
  • Wooly rhinoceros: similar to modern rhinoceros, yet covered with shaggy coat, living in subarctic regions;
  • Titanothere: also called brontotherium, a massive, rhino-like herbivore with a yoke-shaped horn on the nose.
