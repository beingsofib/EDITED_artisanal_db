The leShay were immortal, albino, elf-like fey creatures of extraordinary magical power. and as such were considered the rulers of the plane of Faerie since ancient times. They were sometimes referred to as sylvans or simply as the fey.

Description

LeShay were legendarily beautiful. They were tall and thin and looked like albino elves, with white skin and glossy dead-white hair. However, instead of the red eyes common to true albinos, leShay had deep black eyes; even the whites of their eyes were black. Their ears were pointed, but more closely resembled the ears of a half-elf.

Personality

A leShay typically was haughty and very concerned with proper behavior, manners, and mores. They cared strongly about matters of respect.

Abilities

LeShay were immortal; they could not die of old age or illness but only be killed by someone.

LeShay had a vast array of magical powers inherent to themselves. Most notable among such powers were their ability to charm other creatures by simply looking at them and the strange power of forming weapons out of nothing by power of thought alone. These "leShay weapons" were formed from the essence of each individual leShay as if they were extensions of his or her own body. LeShay could also change form, teleport, turn invisible, and breathe underwater with their magics, among other things.

The minds of leShay were particularly geared towards the learning of language; that is, all leShay were essentially . They could understand nearly any language within only moments of hearing it spoken.

Combat

Despite their great power—on par with the oldest of dragons—leShay tended to avoid initiating combat. They were superb fighters, but they preferred to act on the defensive.

Society

LeShay were solitary and could survive in any climate and terrain. They were said to worship the faerie gods.

Many leShay were highly accomplished and dangerous spellcasters.

They spent most of their time in their immortal lives attempting to occupy their attention and amuse themselves.

Despite their eternal lifespans, leShay females did not tend to bear more than one or two children owing to low fertility and yearslong, dangerous pregnancies. Generational differences within a family meant little to the endless leShay, whose familial bonds grew strained and strange over the eons: knowing that ultimately their fellow leShay were the only ones who could always be there for them, they came to both hate and cling to each other. Inheritance and succession favored the youngest rather than the eldest child.

Uses

The amber-colored blood of a leShay was said to be so rare and potent that it made for a powerful component in dark summoning rituals.

History

The leShay were said to be the progenitors of all fey, If true, this meant that they were responsible for the creation of beings like the korreds, sprites, and pixies, and that they ruled Faerie as of . and not competing with the empires of the sarrukh, batrachi, or aearee. They had largely retreated back to Faerie as of , and those who remained on the Material Plane became scattered, with some scholars believing that this was a result of a plague brought about by dragons or demons. It was said that the descendants of those who stayed behind became further types of fey.

Circa , they opened crossroads between Faerie to Faerûn to allow the ancient sylvan elves to immigrate there.

In the millennia after the First Sundering, the High Lady Ordalf led the leShay in founding the kingdom of Sarifal circa on the island that would one day be known as Gwynneth of the Moonshae Isles. Some believed that it had been the leShay's influence through the preceeding 500 years that ended the volcanic activity of the islands and caused them to take on their final shapes. From their shining capital city of Karador, they ruled over the many fey inhabitants of the island and subsequently welcomed high elven refugees circa , who would become the Llewyrr.

Around , the leShay were said to have allied with the Imaskari,

The leShay would subsequently retreat from Toril circa , traveling to the Feywild and causing Karador to sink to the bottom of the Myrloch lake. They returned in dramatic fashion in the , causing Karador to reemerge and beginning the process of driving all humans from the island of Gwynneth as they reestablished their realm of Sarifal.

As of , only a handful of leShay yet lived on Toril, all of whom were immensely powerful spellcasters residing in Karador in Sarifal on Gwynneth island. Most of the leShay were reclusive, rarely seen even by most of the city's populace, and served as advisors to the less-reclusive High Lady Ordalf and her son Prince Araithe, who governed the city.

As of the late 15 century DR, some elven crusaders who revered Lashrael opposed the influence of the leShay of Sarifal.

LeShays of Sarifal

While there were many families of leShay in the Feywild, as of the late 15 century DR, the ruling leShay of Sarifal all hailed from the same family, They were cruelly protective of the purity of their bloodline, which led to a great deal of inbreeding, ultimately resulting in several stillborn or monstrous births.
