Shators () also known as shaggy demodands or shaggy leths were the highest-ranking of the demodands. They were the most powerful of the gehreleths, spreaders of nihilistic futility seemingly covered in loosely hanging skins.

Description

Shators were the heaviest and widest of the demodands, standing between tall and weighing somewhere between . They were squat, obese humanoids with large heads, huge bat-like wings, and a pale ooze dripping from their disgusting forms. Their massive maws resembled those of a bulldog or a fanged frog, and drooled a similar slime to that exuded by their bodies from the corners. Their common name was actually a misnomer rooted in the fact that the hairless shaggy demodands were draped in overlapping hides. In addition, the hulking shators were also covered in fungal growths.

Personality

Shators were the self-appointed wardens of the Red Prison, and kept meticulous records of their subordinates and claimed prisoners. They were fearsome entities with great intellects that projected an almost palpable air of self-confidence and possessed shocking levels of cruelty. Like the farastu they were slow to react and secretly desired escape attempts by Carcerian petitioners, but they wanted potential escapees to succeed so that they could send bounty hunters to bring them back.

Corruption and Summoning

While capable of sending themselves and others to various planes, summoning was actually considered fortuitous to the shators, as it gave them an opportunity to engage in their favorite activity: writing. Thus, on the rare occasions they found themselves on the Material Plane, they took the opportunity to spread their poisoned words and tempt souls toward Carceri's waiting chains.

To accomplish these aims, shators would often work through mortal leaders, poets and philosophers, using them as unwitting mouthpieces for their ideas. They induced visions of poetic despair, and granted their targets texts about cosmicism, futility, and the bleak senselessness of the multiverse. They preferred their victims to be compelling enough to present their miserable ideas favorably and made sure that their summoner would not try to banish them, leaving only on their own terms and slaying their unsuspecting servitors when they did.

The other works of shators included guides on summoning various fiends aside from demodands, normally named beings and particularly yugoloths, that had drawn the ire of the shator.

Bizarrely, shators on other planes treated the three-headed chimerae like most humans would treat kittens, reserving their ordinary sadism and occasionally showing concern for them.

Abilities

Shators were the strongest of the demodands but at the same time the slowest and clumsiest. Their slime could serve as an nerve toxin that induced numbness and paralysis for anywhere between a few seconds to a minute or so. The ooze could be transferred through the shator's claws and fangs, or be spat up in globules or exhaled as a spray against distant enemies.

Nonmagical weapons were useless against the shators and, because of their many folds of skin, even the impact of enchanted weapons was absorbed. Large, edged weapons such as broadswords and longswords in particular were less useful against shaggy demodands. Heat and cold energy was less effective, if not completely useless against them and they were completely immune to mind-affecting magic. Shators possessed incredible olfactory and auditory senses, as well as the natural ability to see the invisible.

In addition to the spell-like abilities present in all gehreleths and those unique to them such as cloudkill, stinking cloud, and mass charm, shators were adept sorcerers. They often learnt the spells bull's strength, confusion, dancing lights, daze, detect thoughts, enlarge person, flare, ghost sound, mage armor, mage hand, magic missile, obscuring mist, open/close, ray of frost, read magic, Tasha's hideous laughter, and true strike. They also possessed the innate ability to plane shift, although they were fated to return to Carceri and in some cases could only shift back there.

Shators also had access to powerful rituals. Their minute-long liquefaction ritual could turn all nearby willing farastus or kelubars of their choice into living liquids, either until the shator freed them or they were placed in a flask and the flask was opened. Once per day they could also use the imprisonment ritual spell, chaining them to the ground and totally preventing attempts to move them.

Combat

Shators preferred to fight from a distance using their magical abilities and paralyzing spit rather than engage enemies in melee, although their vast intellects allowed them to choose the best tactic for a given situation. They often surrounded themselves with farastu and kelubar servants and kept bottles of their lesser demodand kin in their lairs to open or break when threatened, allowing them to refrain from combat if they so chose. A shator would not hesitate to retreat if it felt its opposition was too powerful.

While they could always use their natural armaments when forced into close combat, shators possessed the dexterity and power needed to wield oversized weapons and used enchanted weapons nearly half of the time. They were known to wield guisarme and tridents, and to use their superior reach to trip opponents from a distance and attack them while they were down, a technique their excellent reflexes allowed them to utilize.

Society

Shators ruthlessly dominated demodand society and their inferior kin. As the nobility and commanders of their kind, shators were responsible for making the important decisions for their race. Much of their time was spent scheming against the other factions of Carceri and deciding whether or not to send gehreleth bounty hunters after escaped petitioners. Although normally found with their lackeys nearby, their capricious nature led them to organize their troops only when pressed, and such was their incredible suspicion of each other that they only workied together against a dangerous, common enemy.

In addition to their native tongue, shators spoke Abyssal, Common, and Infernal.

Relationships

Shators, along with kelubars, and farastu served the drow deity - Vhaeraun.

Rumors and Legends

Apomps was thought to have given the shators a compulsion to write and influence mortal poets. The intention was to focus hatred onto the yugoloths and draw attention to their sinister plots.

Notable Shators

  • Xideous was a shator that had decided to compile the true and public names of as many yugoloths as possible into his own rendition of the Book of Keeping. He worked from an asylum in Sigil through various contacts and planned to obtain all four of the original copies.
