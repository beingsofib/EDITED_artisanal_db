Dragon issue 221, Uncovering the Arcane Arts, was released in September of 1995. It contained the following articles related to the Forgotten Realms:

In Dreams

By Dave Gross. p. 10

The lost art of Oneiromancy.

The Little Wish

By Jon Winter. p. 20

Making the most of the cantrip spell. The article also provided rules for a cantrip nonweapon proficiency, which allowed for a proficiency check to cast cantrip-like effects.

Eight Ways to Tame the Dark Overlord

By Peter C. Zelinski. p. 36

Creating an Enemy for your PCs.

Role-playing Reviews

By Rick Swan. p. 42–46

Ricks wades in to do battle with a horde of new combat rulebooks.

  • Elminster's Ecologies Appendix I
  • Player's Option: Combat & Tactics
  • The Seven Sisters

Announcing the 1994 Origins Awards Winners

By Uncredited. p. 62

  • Best New Play-By-Mail Game: Forgotten Realms by Reality Simulations, Inc., Designer: Paul Brown.

The Dragon's Bestiary: Lords of Chaos

By Edward Bonny, illustrated by Terry Dykstra. p. 72-78

The Slaad Lords.

  • Chourst, Lord of Randomness
  • Rennbuu, Lord of Colors
  • Ssendem, Lord of Insanity
  • Ygorl, Lord of Entropy

Bazaar of the Bizarre: Familiar Items

By James R. Collier, illustrated by Bob Klasnich. p. 83–85

Magic items for your wizard's best friend.

The Game Wizards

By Steven Schend, illustrated by Stephen Daniele. p. 112-113

The Blood Wars checklist for the Powers & Proxies expansion set, plus variant rules for the planar card game.

TSR Previews

By Uncredited. p. 120

New products for September and October.

  • Elminster's Ecologies Appendix II sourcebook.
  • Giantcraft sourcebook.
  • The Return of Randal Morn module.
  • The Titan of Twilight novel.
