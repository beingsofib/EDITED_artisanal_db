Nightshades, also known as wood woses, were elemental spirits of poisonous plants, such as foxglove.

Description

Nightshades appeared as stocky, dark-brown-skinned fey dwarves, with thick beards and thatch-like hair full of plants. Their garb consisted of woven fiber kilts and vests.

Personality

Nightshades were described as cold and uncaring.

Abilities

These creatures were impervious to harm or injury from all manner of mundane and enchanted wooden weaponry, such as bo sticks, clubs, and quarterstaves. However, as plant spirits they were particularly vulnerable to flames.

Nightshades had the spell-like ability to cast pass without trace and speak with plants at will, as well as entangle and plant door once per day. Once per month, when gathered together in a group of seven or more, nightshades could recite a summoning spell to summon and command a shambling mound for a full night. As a material component, their unique spell required the nightshades to first drink blood.

Combat

Nightshades were poisonous to the touch, creating a painful stinging sensation on contact. Using their bronze or copper spears and short swords covered in their sap, nightshades tried to kill their opponents by poison, as victims slain in this manner would sprout as a new nightshade when next Selûne was full. Some nightshades were druidic mages, and large groups could summon and command shambling mounds once monthly.

Society

Instead of currency and gems, nightshades valued potions and poisons, the latter of which they traded with quicklings in exchange for weaponry.

Nightshades were the enemies of treants, druids, and rangers. Among their allies were the korred, needlefolk, and evil myconids.

Notable Nightshades

Ruling over the nightshades through terror and blackmail was high queen Ainecotte, the eldest and smartest of their kin.
