Magma claws were one of the two more bestial species of magma beast.

Description

These creatures bore a resemblance to scorpions, but had only four legs and a stubby tail.

Abilities

Like all magma beasts, claws were immune to the effects of petrification, had some resistance to fire, and were particularly vulnerable to cold-based spells and abilities.

They could uniquely disgorge lava out from their claws.

Combat

Magma claws preferred to ambush their prey, hiding in plain sight by posing as mere hunks of volcanic rock. When they felt their opportunity had come a magma claw would rush towards their prey and immobilize it by pouring out sticky lava around its legs. They would then attempt to bludgeon their prey to death with their fiery claws.

History

Following the Spellplague, many magma claws made their way through elemental vortices and planar rifts into volcanic regions of Toril's surface and the Underdark. In 1479 DR, magma claws could be found inhabiting Mount Hotenow.

Also late into the 15 century DR, a group of adventurers on the continent of Laerakond prevented the Cult of Reborn Flame from conducting a ritual to awaken the sleeping Dawn Titan named Achazar that would have sacrificed the residents of the port-city Tarmalune. In the wake of the failed ritual, magma claws and other elementals crossed over from the Plane of Fire and sought to destroy the adventurers as they tried to flee to Tarmalune.

Ecology

Diet

Magma claws could subsist on merely clay, ores, and even minor elementals. Though they preferred to hunt and feast upon organic creatures, savoring the taste of burned flesh. Whenever they killed a humanoid they would devour them whole, including their equipment.

Habitats

These creatures were native to the Elemental Chaos, and on the Elemental Plane of Fire.

Languages

Like all magma beasts, claws were capable of speaking the Primordial language.

Relationships

Magma claws were occasionally bullied by magma brutes and magma hurlers into fighting alongside them. They were also often lured into serving fire archons, fire giants, salamanders, and even the occasional eye of flame.
