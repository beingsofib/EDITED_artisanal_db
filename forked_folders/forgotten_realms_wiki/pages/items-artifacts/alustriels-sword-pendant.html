Powers

Upon Other Metal Objects

Sword pendants had a number of different magical abilities, all of which required that the pendant be worn against skin, rather than simply held.

Transformative Effects

Into Weaponry

Secondly, an Alustriel's sword pendant could be used to turn a weapon that had both a metal blade and no enchantments into the form of whatever other weapon was imagined. This ability, however, came with limitations. The resulting shape, like its old form, needed a metallic blade that was not infused with magic, nor could it be more than twice, or less than half, as long or voluminous in comparison to the original. Beyond these functional restrictions, there were also aesthetic ones, as the exact appearance or engravings of preexisting weapons could not be replicated.

Through this ability, a typical spear could be converted into a lance. It was similarly possible that a knife could become a shortsword or a longsword become a greatsword. This effect lasted an entire day, during which the weapon could no longer be transformed by use of the pendant and after which the weapon reverted to its original state. After an approximate minute's rest in its first form, it was once again able to be affected by the pendant's power, suffering no adverse effects whether it be repeatedly turned into the same shape or into many different ones.

Into Carry-Globes

Thirdly, the last of a sword pendant's external abilities was another transmutative power, one that could affect any sort of object—not just weapons—as long as they were metallic and smaller in mass than a complete plate mail suit. By activating this particular effect, the pendant's wearer could transform the targeted object into a globe. This particular globe was a hollow one, with a distinct lid that sealed itself through the use of magnetism, preventing any gases or liquids from escaping the globe's inner space. There were limits to the size of the object created by the pendant, as with its other power, but these were no longer affected by the volume of the original item. Rather, the globe's size depended on the wearer's own physiology. Specifically, the pendant's upper limit was the size of the wielder's head, whilst its lower limit was that of their smallest fingernail.

Typical uses of this power included the transport of precious liquids, like water from a magical pool or dragon blood. Sealing the carry-globe with a flaming object within caused said object to immediately extinguish, and any destructive liquid inside it left the globe unscathed. This was also a longer transformation, lasting two days or until willed to revert. The latter option simply transformed the globe back, unaffected, but if the time limit was passed without the regressive command of the sword pendant's wearer, the globe disintegrated into nothingness, leaving any contents to spill.

Upon the Wearer

An Ironguard

Alustriel's sword pendants had only one sustained effect that did not first require their wearer's conscious command. Non-magical armor, too, simply passed through them, unwearable.

This ability also functioned differently if the metal had some kind of enchantment upon it. In that case, the offending object was stopped by a kind of field around the wearer's body, rather than going through them, which allowed for the use of the pendant alongside magically imbued metal armors. Weapons were also turned away, though they were capable of damaging the sword pendant's wielder according to the power of their enchantment.

A Swordarm

As its name implied, an Alustriel's sword pendant also allowed its wearer to transform their forearm into the edge of a longsword that was sharp and thin. It was capable of piercing armor and parrying someone else's blade without suffering damage, despite not being metallic in itself, as well cutting through hair, parchment, and even rope, as though the swordarm's edge was recently sharpened. It even behaved as if it had been masterfully tempered. Although, in this form, the swordarm lacked digits—and so was unable to carry or even grip anything—it could nonetheless be used to support the user's weight and, ultimately, it was even capable of hurting larger targets more deeply than smaller ones.

This particular pendant ability, however, required that the sword pendant's wearer wait four days between uses and, even then, would only last around forty minutes before returning to normal. If willed away, or if the pendant was separated from its wielder, then the swordarm faded even earlier.

Notable Owners
