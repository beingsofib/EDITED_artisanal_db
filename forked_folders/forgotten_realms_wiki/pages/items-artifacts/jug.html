A jug was a kind of liquid storage container, resembling an enormous bottle or flask.

Description

A typical jug held of liquid,

Pitchers were usually open-topped and designed specifically for pouring, with a molded spout and sturdy handle. Decanters resembled large, decorative bottles, intended for pouring fine drinks such as wine or sparkling water. They would have a handle to ensure that body heat from holding it did not warm the beverage within.

Availability

Aurora's Whole Realms Catalogue offered wooden jugs for sale, valued at 4 cp each, as well as pitchers at 6 sp (porcelain), 4 sp (stoneware), and 3 sp (wood).

Notable Jugs & Similar

  • Alchemy jug, a useful item that produced a variety of substances on command.
  • Decanter of endless lamp oil, which produced lamp oil.
  • Decanter of endless water, which produced fresh or salt water.

Trivia

  • In the Roll of Years, the year 933 DR bore the name "Year of the Five Jugs".
