The Sphix was a ship belonging to Captain Lukas that sank in Kork Bay in the Strait of the Leviathan in the late 1470s DR.

Description

The ship was fast and sturdy, made with broad beams and with masts crowded with sails. Below deck was cramped, with some one-person cabins.

The ship carried a skiff to be used as a lifeboat or for reaching land while the ship was anchored. This skiff contained several hidden compartments large enough to hide weapons.

Inhabitants

The crew of the Sphinx on its last voyage comprised:

  • Captain Lukas, a human ranger from Loudwater who was a skilled fighter and tracker.
  • Gaspar-shen, a watersoul genasi swordsman from Calimshan with a keen interest in pastry.
  • Kip, a young cat-shifter with unique empathic abilities.
  • Marikke, a priestess of Chauntea in her role as the Earthmother.
  • The Savage, a golden elf swordmage dressed in black to hide his true nature as a daemonfey.
  • Suka, a gnome with a foul mouth who specialized in concealed weapons.

History

The Sphinx was built in Baldur's Gate by Lukas himself, and when it arrived in the Moonshae Isles, it was one of the fastest ships yet seen there, leading to copies. For years, Lukas sailed the ship across the Trackless Sea, and everything he owned came to be stored on it.

After Lukas met Gaspar-shen in Alaron, the pair ran Amnian cargo between Snowdown and Callidyrr. while Suka simply did not like her.

Soon after, about seven months after Lukas paid off the last of his debts incurred from building the ship, the crew incurred more debts to House Kendrick, the crown of Callidyrr, possibly relating to the Savage's stint in jail. They were offered a chance to waive this debt in exchange for taking Lord Aldon Kendrick, a cousin to the king, on a potentially dangerous mission to the ruins of Caer Corwell on Gwynneth to meet with the Winterglen Claw, a group of human rebels opposed to the fey of Sarifal who ruled the island. This would turn out to be a trap for Lord Aldon laid by a rival family member, and he wound up dead by the hand of the High Lady Ordalf, the queen of Sarifal. She, in turn, offered to hire the Sphinx for a mission to kill her half-sister, Amaranth, on Moray Island. In addition to offering a large sum of Amnian gold, Ordalf imprisoned Suka as an assurance of loyalty. However, she herself would immediately betray them upon realizing that Marikke and Kip together satisfied a prophecy held by Argon Bael and the Black Blood tribe of Moray, leading her to signal them as the Sphinx sailed along the coast.

Despite knowing terrible stories of fire nagas in the waters around Moray, Lukas gambled on a nighttime approach at the narrowest point between Moray and Gwynneth at Kork Head. Sure enough, the ship was attacked by the nagas, set aflame, and sunk to the bottom of Kork Bay a mere four days after setting out from Caer Corwell. Even so, the entire crew survived: Kip, Marikke, and the Savage reached the shore in the skiff while Gaspar-shen saved Lukas from drowning and swam ashore with him. The three aboard the skiff were immediately attacked and captured by Argon Bael and his lycanthrope followers, while Lukas and Gaspar-shen pursued them after retrieving their equipment stashed in the skiff.

The timbers of the Sphinx came to litter the beaches of Kork Head.
