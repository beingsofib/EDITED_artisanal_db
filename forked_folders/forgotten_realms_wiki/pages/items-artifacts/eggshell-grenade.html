Eggshell grenades were a variety of diversionary or incapacitating thrown weapons unique to the lands of Kara-Tur Despite their name, these objects did not exhibit the explosive force typical of grenades, instead "bursting" with their contents.

Description

These devices were so named for traditionally being made of carefully blown-out eggshells, though it was not uncommon for one to find grenades made from the hollowed out shells of nuts, paper packets, or other materials that were small and easy to break. The interior of these objects would be filled with different types of powder in order to create a distraction or blind an opponent. Because of the materials they were made from, eggshell grenades were quite fragile.

Types

Dust: Eggshell grenades filled with dust, designed to thrown at a singular foe's face in order to temporarily blind them.

Flashpowder: Requiring a source of fire to activate, these eggshell grenades exploded in brilliant, flares of light that blinded all those looking in its direction.

Grease: In the continent of Faerûn, some cat burglars utilized eggshell grenades filled with slippery oil to hinder pursuers.

Pepper: Eggshell grenades filled with pepper were designed to hinder a singular foe, inducing choking and sneezing in the target as it got into their eyes and nose.

Poison smoke: Much like those filled with flashpowder, these eggshell grenades required a fire source in order to activate. Once activated it released a foul cloud of smoke with an acidic stench. This lightly harmed those within its reaches like poison, blinding and incapacitating them.

Smoke: Eggshell grenades designed to produce a cloud of smoke to obscure the thrower so that they may make an escape. If thrown into a source of fire, they would create an even denser area of smoke, comparable to a fog.

Notable Users

  • Around the , a tengu in Kozakura used flashpowder grenades in conjunction with stilts and an oni costume to terrorize the people of Myudo village with his band of raiders, masquerading under the guise of "Dai-Oni".
  • Hoseki, a ninja and sohei who worked for the Dai-Oni.
  • Motoharu Kaijitsu, a ninja and yakuza adventurer from Kozakura.
  • Naga, a yakuza in the town of Aru in Wa, was skilled in the use of eggshell grenades and often carried a pair.
  • Nezumi and Rokan, a pair of ninjas on the Kozakuran island of Hinomoto.
  • Saisho, a ninja and wu jen adventurer from Kozakura's Miyama Province.
  • Sujin Makoto, the supreme leader of the Five Deadly Shadows in 1479 DR.

Trivia

Some were known to conceal eggshell grenades within shikomi-zue, another common weapon among ninjas.
