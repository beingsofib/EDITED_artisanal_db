namePicatrix
typeBook

A picatrix was a kind of book that contained recipes and instructions used by skilled craftsmen in the creation of artisanal goods. What separated them from mundane instructional books was the inclusion of magical sigils that were used for improving on mundane procedures, as well as the creation of magical items.

Description

Picatrices were sturdy and heavy tomes, usually with solid covers of woods like oak, ash, yew, maple, elm, spruce, ebony, and walnut. Most copies were carefully prepared by masters to not include powerful sigils, lest an inexperienced artificer have a dangerous accident with magic beyond their ability.
