Plate mail (sometimes known as half plate, but not to be confused with the half-armor style of that name) was a form of heavy armor used in Faerûn and beyond. It was a type of plate armor widely used by warriors, and valued for its protection and versatility.

Description

Though subject to countless variations and styles, plate mail broadly consisted of partial chain mail combined with solid shaped steel plates that covered vital areas. This included a cuirass, armored gauntlets, greaves, tassets, elbow and knee guards, and epaulettes, held together by buckles and straps (a helmet was also well-advised). This loose-fitting, modular design meant that the whole ensemble was easier to fit to new wearers, especially compared to suits of field plate and full plate which were custom-made and fitted. and dragon scales.

Barding for animals and mounts could be made with a style similar to the mixed plate and chain worn by people, and this too gave good protection at a reasonable cost.

Thanks to the addition of plate pieces, this form of armor was more protective than splint mail, banded mail, chain mail, and the rare bronze plate mail, and withstood heavy use and abuse better too. It was especially effective against slashing and chopping strikes, and had no particular weaknesses or vulnerabilities. As it used less advanced techniques to make than elaborate full plate or field plate, it was much cheaper and more accessible for warriors, though still an expensive investment. Because of its balance of qualities, this form of plate was among the most popular and standardized forms of armor used by professional soldiers, knights, mercenaries, and sensible adventurers.

Notable sets of Plate Mail

  • Ankheg plate mail, crafted by Taerom Fuiruim from ankheg chitin.
  • Doomplate, a powerful but ill-fated suit of plate.
  • The Practical Defense, an extraordinary suit used by Bolhur the Clanless.

Notable Users

  • The Hellriders of Elturel wore suits of white plate mail.
  • In Northmen society, plate mail was often worn by kings or an army's captain.
  • The Purple Dragons of Cormyr favored plate mail. When adventuring abroad, they typically wore suits of field plate.
  • In the nation of Sespech, plate mail was among the armor types generally favored by warriors.
  • In the nation of Unther, warriors within the army wore bronze plate mail.

Religions

  • Battleforges, a variety of specialty priest of the Church of Tempus, wore sturdy suits of plate mail.
  • Bulwarks of the Church of Helm generally wore suits of plate mail.
  • Plate mail was one of two types of armor that chaos knights of the Church of Talos favored. However, the plate mail of chaos knights was notably black in coloration and decorated with barbs.
  • Plate mail was one of two types of armor that gauntlets of the Church of Iyachtu Xvim used. Their plate mail was notably black in coloration.
  • Plate mail was among the armors that the glorybloods of the Church of Tempus most often wore.
  • Hands of Tyr (of the Church of Tyr) wore white suits of plate mail.
  • Paragons of the Church of Torm wore glistening white suits of plate mail.
  • Questers of the Church of Helm always wore suits of full plate mail.
  • Scales of Tyr, of the Church of Tyr, often wore suits of plate mail underneath their white robes of office.
  • Plate mail was among the types of armor that swords of Cyric, a variety of specialty priest of the Church of Cyric, most often wore.
