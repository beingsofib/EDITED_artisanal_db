A whetstone was a stone tool used to sharpen and polish blades and other utensils, similar to a grindstone or leather strop. A waterstone was a variety that was used when soaked in water.

Description

Common whetstone blocks were mainly carved from siltstone, slate, mudstone, and sandstone. Waterstones could be made of chert.

Availability

Whetstones were sold and used in many places over Faerûn and Zakhara. Aurora's Whole Realms Catalogue sold flat whetstones for 2 cp each.

Whetstones were used by many races across the Realms, from dwarves to giants.

Uses

Whetstones were used to sharpen and polish metal items, such as blades and edged weapons, jewelry, gems, and mechanical components.

As well as being used as tools, whetstones were used as metamagic spell components. Whetstones constructed from tombstones were required for extending disrupting weapon and those from pyrite were required for extending keen edge.

Notable Whetstones

  • Augmenting whetstones, magical whetstones that gave items touched to them a temporary damage enchantment.
  • The whetstone of sharpening was a magic whetstone that was claimed from the depths of Undermountain. The unique item could sharpen blades to a point at which they had a greater chance to hit, and even deal greater damage.
  • Whetstones of keen edge, magical whetstones carried by duergar, urdunnir, and shield dwarves.
  • Prismal's whetstone, a magical stone that removed rust and dullness from blades. It temporarily made the blade more likely to hit an opponent and deal more damage.

Notable Owners

  • Artus Cimber, the guardian of the Ring of Winter, carried a sharpening stone for his trusted enchanted dagger, the Bookmark.
  • Twoedge, a businessman that lived in Skullport. He made a living from sharpening blades and offered his services to others. He also owned the magical whetstone of sharpening, which he would use on other's swords and daggers for the price of 50 gp per use.
