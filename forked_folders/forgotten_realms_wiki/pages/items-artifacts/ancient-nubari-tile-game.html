Ancient Nubari tile game

A tile-based game that was played by the Ancient Nubari of the Malatran Plateau, usually for the purpose of teaching their children.

Description

This game came in a box, measuring wide and long. They were made of a hard form of ceramic These boxes had a lid that was hinged from the inside, which was opened by simultaneously pressing down upon the top left corners and bottom right corners of their long side.

Within each box were twenty-four colored tiles. Each of these tiles were marked on both sides with symbols that came in pairs. The goal of the game was to turn the tiles over so that each pair showed.
