Bell of warning

nameBell of warning

A bell of warning was a type of small magical bell from the land of Kara-Tur that could detect the presence of anyone approaching it covertly.

Description

These bells measured in length and in diameter.

Powers

Activating a bell of warning required hanging it, such as upon a branch or rafter, and then speaking its command word is spoken. Each activation used up a magical charge, with each bell having upwards of 30 charges, and lasted for around 3 to 18 hours.

While activate a bell of warning would ring whenever any creature came within from either the Astral plane or Ethereal plane. It likewise would sound from any creatures that were invisible or attempting to move stealthily or silently. This ringing would then persist until a bell of warning either deactivated or the detected presence moved outside of its effective radius.

Notable Owners

  • Ayana No-Shui, a powerful wu-jen.
  • The Horseshoe Temple in the Quoya Desert had a bell of warning amongst its collection of magical instruments. When the temple was later overran by oni in 1358 DR, it was amongst the items that survived.
  • Lui-hsia Chih, a leader of the Many-Hued Peacock Society.

History

Around to early , a group of bakemono took up residence in the long abandoned Lihat Observatory, believing it held unusual weaponry inside. As part of establishing defenses within the observatory, the bakemono placed bell of warning in the anteroom to alert them of any intruders.
