A wand of fireballs was a rare magical item, which was capable of shooting fireballs.

Description

There wands were made out of ironwood and topped with a small flaming globe.

These wands could cast fireballs, with up to seven charges, before potentially crumbling to ash. The user could also use the wand to release more powerful balls of fire, but this would result in more charges being used up.

At dawn, two to seven charges (the maximum amount) would be replenished.

History

Wands of fireballs have been used for many centuries. Because of how common was study of arcane flames, it was has to pinpoint the exact origin of these enchanted items. Many sages believed that the modern refined wand of fire design came from the worshipers of Kossuth in the land of Thay.

The bridge of Womford was incinerated by such a wand.

Notable Wands of Fireballs

  • Wand of fire: magic wands of similar power. This variation allowed the user to cast the fireball spell at a target up to away. The wand allowed the caster to use two charges at a time for increased explosive power, and was only usable by wizards.
