The danter (sometimes spelled dantar, or lovingly – "little pearl") was a gold coin minted in Amn and used as standard currency there. Its value was equivalent to one gold piece.

Description

Gold danters were pressed with the image of a circle of six tiny stars on one side. Gold coins were the default currency in Amn, and all prices were generally listed in Danters.

Value

A single gold danter was worth 200 fandars, 20 tarans, or 2 centaurs, while 1 roldon was worth 5 danters. The two most common trade bar denominations pressed in Amn were for 500 or 1000 danters. However, there were trade bards worth as much as 3.5 million danters.

When dealing in currencies from other nations, Zazesspur's gulder was less valued than Amnian danter. 100 danter could buy you 107 gulders in the late 14 century DR. When dealing in Ithmong's currency, 113 moleans bought 100 danters. 138 Myratma's myrats were worth 100 danters, and 175 Saradush's zoths were worth 100 danters.

Laws

Counterfeiting Amnian coins was illegal in Tethyr to no surprise, punishable by a strict prison sentence and public shaming after the confiscation of assets of deemed equal value to the damages done to the country. The oldest counterfeiting practice in Tethyr consisted of melting copper coins down and pressing them into danter shapes. A gilding layer finished the illusion of an authentic danter.

History

As of the late 15 century DR, Amnian gold coins had become common in circulation throughout the Moonshae Isles. The locals referred to these coins as thalers.
