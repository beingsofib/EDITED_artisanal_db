Soup was a wide variety of liquid cooked and cold dishes eaten across Toril, usually made with a liquid base, which could be broth, stock, water, gravy, or milk, with added vegetables, meats, and various seasonings and herbs. or ale. Charitable soup kitchens operated in big cities, such as Arrabar and Baldur's Gate, offering free bowls of soup to the poor and the needy.

Preparation

Perhaps the most common soup was a perpetually bubbling cauldron found in inns being cooked non-stop through the winters. They were made with roasted fowl and similar scraps added to boiling soups alongside vegetables. Fresh scraps, vegetables, and water were regularly added in as the soup boiled away for months at a time.

Varieties

Meat

  • Beef broth, a soup base that was often served on its own or with additional ingredients, such as a beef broth soup served with an egg and vegetables as a morningfeast at the Pampered Traveler of Waterdeep.
  • Boar soup, a simple game soup made with wild boar.
  • Carrion crawler soup, a drow dish from the Underdark.
  • Dragonblood soup was a dish made of a dragon's blood and various vegetables, enchanted via foodomancy.
  • Dragon soup, a long-simmered soup made with dragon flesh, onions, tomatoes, and milk, served at the Old Monster Shop in Waterdeep.
  • Frog soup was a chunky soup made by Dekanter goblins of the Greypeak Mountains.
  • Leek-and-hock soup was a delightful pork soup from the Boar's Head Inn in Ravens Bluff.
  • Leek-and-leftovers soup was a general name given to a simmered concoction of leeks with various vegetables, meats, and cheese chunks from the famed Tabra's eatery in Bargewright Inn.
  • Oxtail soup, a hot appetizer found in the Barony of Bloodstone in the Cold Lands as well as in Sembia.
  • Pea soup, a staple of the Dalelands and among travelers, available in Baldur's gate. It was a thick concoction of peas or split pea, smoked ham, and pepper.
  • Peppered lizard soup, a dish that dated back to Castle Felmorel in the mid–8 century DR.
  • Pork and cabbage soup was a famous dish made by Sot, the cook of the Laughing Goblin Inn in Phlan.
  • Rabbit soup, a simple game soup made with wild rabbit.
  • Rabbit, thyme and fennel soup, a soup found in the Moonsea region and the lands near Phlan.
  • Sausage and morkoth soup, a variety of everything-in perpetual soup that was cooked throughout the day with almost anything left in the kitchen tossed into it.
  • Smoked venison soup was a common dish made by the gnomes of Samek, on the Great Glacier. The soup was fatty and rich. It had a strong salty and smoky flavor of venison.
  • Snake soup, a specialty delicacy dish from the Amnian city, Crimmor.
  • Stagshead soup, a soup of mysterious ingredients served at Donder's Dancing Masques of Tharkar.
  • Venison soup, a simple game soup made with wild deer.
  • Welcome Soup was a robust soup from the Stargath House of Purskul made with cream, bacon, and black beans.

Poultry

  • Chicken with rice soup, a poultry specialty priced as six silver per bowl from The Painted Boat restaurant in Ravens Bluff.
  • Chicken stock was a broth served by itself or used as a soup base throughout Faerûn. Monksblade hamlet's morning soup was chicken broth-based and flavored with mushrooms.
  • Duck soup, a common variety of soups found in the Realms, including being a table staple from The Silver Taproom in Essembra, the Dalelands.
  • Turkey soup, an old recipe that dated back to the years of Athalantar.
  • Turkey and wildfowl soup was one of the staples made with game birds at the roadside Calling Horns inn.
  • Sage-and-pheasant soup was a soup served hot in Cormyr's capital, Suzail, and its Royal Castle.

Vegetable

  • Acorn soup a special treat from the Hog Hill inn, not far from Ravens Bluff.
  • Amnian Dragoon Soup was made from thinly sliced vegetables, eggs, lemon, and beef broth.
  • Asparagus-and-artichoke bean broth was a well-spiced dish from Kirinwood in Cormyr.
  • Barley soup was a simple meal from the Langdarma valley in the Utter East and Innarlith.
  • Blackbark soup, a dish made using a collection of forest barks, resulting in a soup as thick and meaty as beef stew.
  • Blalatha soup, a soup of various diced mushrooms.
  • Butter-tomato soup, a sweet soup made at the Sheaf of Wheat eatery in the Cormyrean village of Gaj.
  • Cabbage soup was yet another cheap vegetable soup found served in many places, including High Horn in Cormyr.
  • Carrot and ginger soup, a spiced soup from the Oak-Father's Boon of Mosstone.
  • Cheese-and-potato soup, a specialty of the Elfsong Tavern in Baldur's Gate.
  • Darblalatha soup, a soup of various diced and fried mushrooms and leeks.
  • Fieldgreens soup was a slow-simmered and fragrant soup found on the Sword Coast.
  • Fiddlehead soup, a dish prepared using fiddleheads of a fern and topped with a knob of butter, served in Baldur's Gate, the village of Dead Tree Hollow in the Vast, the Stag Steads of Cormyr, and the Moonwyvern Inn near Silverymoon.
  • Five-bean soup was a recipe from the land of Red Wizards – Thay.
  • Grass broth, a salty boiled concoction of various local greens, served on its own or with dumplings.
  • Leek soup, a dish made with leeks.
  • Mushroom soup, a common variety of soup. A highly spiced version was made by the drow of the Underdark.
  • Nettle soup, a type of soup cooked using the Malatran jungle nettle plant from the Malatran Plateau of Kara-Tur.
  • Onion soups were quite common. In the Dalelands, bowls of hot onion soup were served as morningfeast. A variety of this soup made in Vahtov, a great Zakharan city, smelled like a camel's breath.
  • Potato chowder, thick potato soup, flavored with leeks, garlics, onions, and milk.
  • Potato soup a common variety found in the Hordelands. Similar soup, naturally thickened with potato's starch, was a dish made by Sembian halflings A cold variant of the same dish was known in the village of Kirinwood in Cormyr.
  • Potato leek soup, another staple of Faerûn, served at the Flying Stag and the Whistling Wizard of Voonlar, Phontyr's Unicorn, in Elturel, at the Lonesome Tankard of Eveningstar in Cormyr, and favored by the dwarves, including those of Mithral Hall.
  • Pumpkin soup, an elvish dish made with spices and pumpkin, dating back to the ancient Myth Drannor and found across the Sword Coast.
  • Ratatouille was a type of northern soup made from sautéed tomatoes, onion, zucchini, and eggplant.
  • Vegetable broth, a soup of long-simmered vegetables, topped with a slab of tofu.
  • Vegetable soup made with various fresh vegetables was a simple and cheap warming meal in Innarlith, on the eastern tip of the Lake of Steam, in Impiltur, and in Ravens Bluff.
  • Vegetable and root sauce soup was a hearty winter dish of the Bargewright Inn.

Seafood

  • Blentra's oysters and wild rice in mushroom soup, a complex and delicious soup of cream, rice, oysters, and mushrooms from The Platter of Plenty in Marsember.
  • Brine shrimp soup, an offering from Sharkey's Bar & Grill's exotic menu, priced at five silvers per portion.
  • Clam chowder was a thick white soup made with clams.
  • Clam soup from Kozakura was cooked with mushrooms.
  • Cormyrian seafood soup, a variety of soup that lacked spiciness but was cooked with clams, mussels, and shrimps, spring onions, and lemon juice.
  • Fish-head soup, perhaps one of the cheapest seafood soups made with leftover heads of butchered fish.
  • Fish soups were thickened and served in tureens in Durpar.
  • Fish and vegetable cream soup of Yeshpek was a variety of goat's milk-base soup.
  • Fried eel soup was one of the staples in The Sleeping Cat of Wheloon.
  • Mussel-and-basil soup was a highlight of the Moonstone Mask's soup menu.
  • Octopus broth was more of a traditional offering of the Moonstone Mask's soup menu.
  • Oyster soup was made with various species of oysters, such as river-oysters that were poached in the soup, served in Crimmor.
  • Seafood chowder, a thick soup made with various types of seafood from The Shark Fin eatery of Ravens Bluff.
  • Shark fin soup was a specialty of The Shark Fin eatery of Ravens Bluff.
  • Spicy shrimp soup was a dish from Baldur's Gate's harborside taverns and inns.
  • Squash soup, a traditional summer festival vegetable soup made with chicken stock.
  • Stone soup, a dish from Faerûnean folklore, prepared using vegetables and sizzling hot stones.
  • Turtleback soup, a green and briny soup garnished with cucumber, from the city of Velen.
  • Turtle soup, a famous soup served at the Maided of the Moor in Mornbryn's Shield; in Neverwinter, in both the Fallen Tower and Moonstone Mask, and the Singing Sword tavern in Waterdeep.
  • Vegetables and fish soup was consumed by the Ffolk of the Moonshae Isles. It was a hearty broth, served hot that possessed muscle revitalizing properties.

Other

  • Cashew soup was a thick soup made with cashew nuts from the City of Splendors, Waterdeep.
  • Cheeeese soup was a special healing soup made using Luiren spring cheese by the hin healers.
  • Cheshleek soup, a Turmishite specialty of Khaligeira Moonthumb.
  • Dippin' soup, a struggle meal made from stale bread, tomatoes, and onions.
  • Glemmeraeve soup, a Suzailan soup made with mustard, daeradorn wine, and simmered snails in gravy.
  • Haendur soup, a glowworm soup flavored with sharp-flavored leaves.
  • Monstrous spider web soup a dish made out of boiled spiderwebs of Vaerluth in the Rauvin Mountains, presumably, only edible by goblins.
  • Mucjara soup, a traditional citrus soup of the Maquar people of Estagund.
  • Noodle soup was a staple across the nation of Shou Lung, cooked with vegetables and noodles. The best was made by Jui-jung Nu Nam in the mid–14 century DR.
  • Plankton soup, an offering from Sharkey's Bar & Grill's exotic menu, priced at three silvers per portion.
  • Zurek was a northern soup made from fermented sour flour and eggs.

History

During his many visits to the world of Earth and Ed Greenwood's home, Elminster and The Wizards Three partook in that world's versions of leek and chicken soup, cream of mushroom soup with turkey, and cream soup.
