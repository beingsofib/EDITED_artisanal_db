Cauldrons, boilers, and kettles were common cooking vessels in the Realms, similar to pots.

Description

These vessels were used to boil water and cook foods over a fire such as soups and stews. Kettles were smaller and often used for family meals, while cauldrons were much larger and able to cook for large groups. They could be cast or forged from iron, steel, or copper, and the most massive cauldrons could weigh as much as 40 tons. Spoons and ladles were vital for serving food from these vessels.

Cauldrons and kettles could also be used to pour substances such as boiling oil down castle walls onto invaders.

Availability

Aurora's Emporium sold cauldrons with tripods, available as for 10 gp, for 22 gp, and for 32 gp.

Notable Cauldrons

Magical cauldrons tended to have functions related to preparing potions, poisons, and extraordinary amounts of food.

  • The Boiler, a massive cauldron used for siege warfare.
  • Cauldron of doom, a necromantic artifact.
  • Cauldron of gateways, a rare cauldron that could open portals to places on the same plane of existence.
  • Cauldron of planar travel, an even rarer cauldron that could open portals to anywhere in the multiverse.
  • Everbountiful soup kettle, a magical kettle that made soup.
  • Harvest cauldron, a cooking pot worn as a helmet by Pikel Bouldershoulder.

Trivia

  • In the Roll of Years, the year 1378 DR bore the name "Year of the Cauldron".
