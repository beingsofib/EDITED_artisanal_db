Zagyg's spell component case was an enchanted spell component pouch created by the Oerthian wizard Zagyg Yragerne.

Description

These belt pouches had no distinguishing features, appearing like ordinary spell component pouches.

Powers

This item was usable only by mages and radiated a faint aura. The possessor of this item had only to think of a material component they needed for a particular spell and it would manifest within Zagyg's spell component case, however, if the component was not used shortly afterwards it would disappear. In the case of spells that did not destroy material components in their casting, the component would still disappear immediately after the casting.

Copies of Zagyg's spell component case varied in how many times they could be used per day, ranging from twice to seven times.

History

In the , a group of adventurers traveled into the depths of the Bloodstone Mines, to the caverns of Deepearth. Within one of its caverns they discovered the lair of a pair of intellect devourers, as well as the belongings of their previous victims, which included a Zagyg's spell component case.

In , Michael Melandor was kidnapped and his kidnappers sent an extensive ransom to his father Sirrus Melandor that included magic items such as Zagyg's spell component case.

Notable Owners

  • Emelyn the Gray, a powerful archmage in the Bloodstone Lands of northeast Faerûn, possessed one in 1359 DR.
  • Krinklespine, a traveling merchant mage in the Vast in the 14 century DR.
