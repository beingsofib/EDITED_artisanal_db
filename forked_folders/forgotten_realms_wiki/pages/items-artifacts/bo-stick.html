A bo stick was a type of wooden staff commonly used in the land of Kara-Tur.

Description

These hard wood staves typically measured around long, They greatly resembled quarterstaves in terms of length and balance, but lacked the iron-shod bands one would find on some.

Weaponology

The skills necessary to wield a bo stick were comparable to that of a quarterstaff, though the style in which they were fought with was distinctly different. some martial artists made swift blows and twists with their bo sticks to break an opponent's sword.

Due to their length, bo sticks often struck slower than the comparatively shorter jo sticks.

Notable Owners

Classes

The bo stick was particularly favored as a weapon among monks, ninjas, shukenjas, and wu-jen.

Individuals

  • Deng Tse Chin, a shugenja adventurer.
  • Diogi, a human wu-jen.
  • Ishi Barasume, a Kozakuran fighter.
  • Kane, a monk of the Monastery of the Yellow Rose.
  • Loy Ho-dun, a kensai adventurer.
  • Tsao Ho, a sohei adventurer

Races & Sentient Creatures

  • Among the oni-related races, bo sticks were a common weapon among the wang-liang.

History

  • In the mid-14 century DR, in the continent of Faerûn, it was not uncommon for one to find bo sticks being wielded in the city of Waterdeep. Around the same time, bo sticks were being sold in Tilverton by the Weaponers of Cormyr.
