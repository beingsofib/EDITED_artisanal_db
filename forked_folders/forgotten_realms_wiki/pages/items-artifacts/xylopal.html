Xylopal was a form of petrified wood with a more lustrous beauty.

Description

Xylopal was similar to tempskya (petrified wood) except the wood fiber was replaced by opal instead of . Sometimes called opalized wood or lithoxyle, it was highly prized for its beauty (it made good bookends). Especially detailed pieces could be used as inlays, statuettes, or for jewelry. A typical price was 22sp per pound (49 sp/kg).

Powers

This hardstone was able to substitute for the material components of the reverse gravity, specifically the iron filings and the lodestone. It required a hand-sized amount, either in a single stone or a bag of fragments.
