Portrait of Gortash was a unique painting of the one-time Grand Duke Lord Enver Gortash, created in the late 15 century DR by an unknown painter.

Description

The portrait was an image of Lord Enver Gortash in his younger years. He appeared to stand tall in his fine robes, holding a cane, looking devilishly dashing. The painting's frame was lovingly polished.

History

The Portrait of Gortash was "proudly" displayed by Enver Gortash's parents, Sally and Dravo Flymm, being hung over the counter in their humble Lower City shop, Flymm's Cobblers, in the .
