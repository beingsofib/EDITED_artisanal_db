Brass was an alloy of copper and zinc, widely used for decorative and practical purposes.

Properties

Brass was commonly made from a mixture of 80% copper and 20% zinc, making 'red brass', or 70% copper and 30% zinc, making 'yellow brass'. Brass had a melting point between and .

Availability

  • Aurora's Emporium had a large catalogue of brass items from all throughout Faerun. as well as brass cylinders and dust intended for use in religious rituals and spellcasting.
  • Hillmarch in Cormyr was a center of brasscasting industry, as well as the mining of the copper and zinc used in it. It produced and exported fine brasswork used across the realm.
  • Hornshield keep in the Storm Horns contained a small but proficient brasscasting, glassblowing, and ironworking industry.
  • Ember Me More, a specialty store in Ravens Bluff, sold a wide variety of fireplace-related products made from brass.
  • Lamps by Watt, another specialty store in Ravens Bluff, specialized in constructing light fixtures and various other items from brass.
  • Brass was one of the major exports for the Lake of Steam region.

Usages

Currency

  • The Waterdhavian taol was an unusual square brass coin, worth 2 gp in Waterdeep, but nothing elsewhere.
  • Gond bells were small brass bells typically worth 10 gp each, though valued at 20 gp by temples of Gond.

Mundane Items

  • Common, everyday objects were often made of brass, such as keyrings, pins, lamps, chandeliers, thuribles, ornaments, tools, animal cages, censers, bells, door knockers, candlesticks. water faucets, and barrel spigots.
  • Diningware was often made from brass as a cheaper alternative to pewter and silver, including mugs, steins, tankards, knives, forks, and spoons.
  • Some Calishite lamps and kaleidoscopes were known to be constructed from brass.
  • Brass was one of a variety of metals that hook-hands and peg-legs were typically constructed from.
  • The mechanisms for timepieces and music boxes were often housed in brass casings.
  • Brass was one of three metals that the spinning tops of Amn were known to be constructed with.
  • Brass was one of three metals that hurricane lamps were known to be constructed with. The craftfolk of Dapplegate were particularly known for crafting brass stormlanterns.
  • Inexpensive jewellery and ornamentation could be made from brass, such as decorative pins, hairpins, rings, and chokers.
  • Brass was commonly used as a fitting or decoration in other projects, such as bookcases, mapcases, buckles for shoes and clothing, and books.
  • A wide variety of musical horns were typically constructed of brass, including fanfare horns, glaurs, hunting horns, longhorns, and sackbutts. Other musical brass instruments included wargongs, tockens, and chime lamps.
  • The Crown of Cormyr dispensed Court tokens to grant the right to bear arms. The token consisted of a numbered brass plaque engraved with the Purple Dragon symbol.
  • A special hull sheathing that protected ship timber from shipworms and seaweed growth used brass and other metals.
  • Although mundane weapons and armor were rarely made of brass, it was a common decorative element, and some items could be plated or sheathed in brass.
  • Oscray forged chainmail, as well as many of their weapons, from brass.
  • Cultists who worshiped Baphomet were known to decorate their altars with brass weapons.
  • The holy symbol of Gond was a toothed cog made of brass, bronze, iron, or bone.

Construction

  • In Memnonnar, the domes and minarets that topped the buildings were inlaid with brass.
  • The Twin Towers of the Eternal Eclipse had several alarm bells throughout the complex that were made of brass.
  • The bricks of Gilgeam's Ziggurat of Eternal Victory were decorated with blue enamel, brass, gold, silver, and other precious metals.
  • The Great Brass Gate was the portal though which the noble efreeti Memnon entered the world of Toril to found Memnonnar.
  • The Grand Cemetary of Elturel was surrounded by a spiked brass fence and gate.

Other Planes

  • Boats made of brass were used by efreet to traverse the Sea of Fire.
  • The City of Brass made heavy use of this metal. One notable building in the city to utilize this material was the Charcoal Palace.
  • Azer were often known to wear apron-like garments made from beaten brass.
  • Celestial archons, with the exception of lantern archons, wore armor made of brass to indicate that they had reached the third rank within the archon hierarchy.
  • Orthons were known to carry crossbows made of brass.
  • The Bleeding Citadel had large double doors of brass.
  • Ember guards were made of brass and stone.

Magic Items

  • Spell focuses could be made of brass.
  • The spellbooks Arcanabula of Jume and Detho's Libram were both bound in brass rings.
  • Braziers of commanding fire elemental were constructed of brass.
  • Efreeti bottles were usually constructed of brass.
  • Eversmoking bottles were usually constructed of brass.
  • The Farseer of Illusk was partially made of brass.
  • Harvyn's Ring
  • Helms of underwater action were forged from brass.
  • Some Horns of Valhalla were constructed of brass.
  • Lightbringer was an enchanted mace made entirely of brass.
  • Rings of dragons were brass rings highly prized by members of the Cult of the Dragon.
  • Rings of flying were made with brass lining the edges.
  • Peacemaker's rods had sculpted brass ends.
  • Purple Dragon rings were always crafted from brass.
  • The serpent rings worn by worshipers of serpent and reptilian deities were fashioned from brass.
  • Staffs of entrapment were traditionally made by the Netherese with brass.
  • The wooden Quarterstaff of Alcedor had brass covers on each end.
  • Countess Zaranda Star's talking head statue was made of brass.
  • Clockroaches were usually made of brass or bronze.

Spell Components

  • Early versions of Bigby's clenched fist required a pair of brass knuckles for casting the spell.
  • Call of despair required a small brass gong for casting the spell.
  • Darsson's music box required a metal cube for casting the spell and brass was one of the two metals that could be used.
  • Detect scrying required a miniature brass for casting the spell.
  • Dreamspeak required a bell made of either brass or silver for casting the spell.
  • Find familiar required a brass brazier for casting the spell.
  • Horizikaul's versatile vibration required a hollow brass cone as a focus.
  • Hornung's surge selector required a brass spinner for casting the spell.
  • Brass tuning forks were required when using a plane shift spell to enter the Para-Elemental Plane of Magma.
  • Undetectable lies required a handful of brass dust for casting the spell.
  • A brass bell was one of two options for material component when casting the spell Watchware.

History

  • The Lodestar Mountains of ancient Netheril supplied the realm with great amounts of various ores, including native brass. The mines were abandoned as of and infested with monsters.
  • The city of Ulshantir in Asram was famous for its copperworking and brasscasting.
  • A brass coffin was used for the burial of Theldrin Rallyhorn.
