Awls were a kind of small, handheld tool used for various trades.

Description

Though there were different designs, the typical awl had a or longer needle-like metal tip with a wooden handle,

Usage

Awls were widely used for working with leather and fabrics, allowing the artisan to punch holes through material and make clothing, armor, shoes, and other products. Other awls were used by woodworkers to scratch lines to mark the wood, or to tap shallow holes in preparation for nails. A similar but larger device called an auger was used to prepare holes for larger spikes.
