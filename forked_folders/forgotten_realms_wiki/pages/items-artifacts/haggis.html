Haggis was an ancient halfling dish, found in the city of Baldur's Gate, the Western Heartlands, and west Faerûn in the late 15 century DR.

Description

Haggis was a boiled sausage-like dish. The casing was a sheep's stomach, filled with sheep's minced organ meats: liver, heart, and lungs, as well as the animal's regular meat. Additionally, the mince was stretched with oatmeal and flavored with onions and spices.

Haggis was believed to be as old as halflings themselves. "Neeps" and "tatties" were traditional side dishes served alongside haggis by the hin.

Availability

Apart from the Western Heartlands, haggis was served at The Happy Stein tavern in the city of Ravens Bluff in the Vast.
