The Sword of Myrloch Vale was a legendary artifact that was said to have been created by the fey of the Myrloch lake in the Moonshae Isles, long before the 13 century DR.

Description

The Sword of Myrloch Vale was a beautifully crafted light longsword. It had a unique and unmistakable design, making it appear to be unearthly and very old.

Powers

It was a longsword that was swung with a greater speed than most similar weapons. In addition to its feather-light design, the blade possessed a potent +3 enchantment that made the weapon more deadly and that guided the wielder's hand towards the target. As such, The Sword of Myrloch Vale could damage creatures that were otherwise invulnerable to non-magical weapons. The blade also imbued the wielder's mind with unprecedented creativity, sensitivity, as well as knowledge of lore. When The Sword's true power was unlocked, it greatly increased the creativity and lore knowledge of the wielder even further than before, and boosted their charisma, making it a perfect weapon for a bard or a minstrel. Lastly, the magics that permeated The Sword of Myrloch Vale made the weapon impossible to wield by creatures of evil and malicious nature.

History

According to the legend of the Sword, it was forged with the help of fae creatures of the Moonshae Isles. It was believed that the creation took place in the depths of the cold waters of Myrloch, the lake at the center of the Isle of Gwynneth.

The only documented wielder of the blade was a half-elven bard named Daen Winterbrush. Some said that the bard found the blade by accident, but once it touched his hand, Daen was blessed with extraordinary creativity and sensitivity to the world and everything else that surrounded him. Daen's travels with The Sword of Myrloch Vale in his possession lasted for forty years, and the bard's artistic output made Daen known as the creator of some of the most unique and haunting melodies in all of Faerûn.

It was said that Daen Winterbrush's travels ended in a fit of powerful melancholy when he became disillusioned after witnessing misery and oppression. The fae smiths who created The Sword of Myrloch Vale welcomed the bard into their home in Myrloch. The Sword then remained lost until the late 13 century DR.

In the , The Sword of Myrloch Vale was found in the stock of Conlan's Smithy in the town of Kuldahar, nestled in the Spine of the World mountains. The powerful blade's true magic was still dormant and still needed to be discovered. It was believed that a group of adventurers sought out The Ballad of Daen Winterbrush, an unfinished work of the long-dead bard. They sang the ballad over the Sword, possibly with the help of Telanis of the Severed Hand and conversed with Daen Winterbrush's ghost, which was tortured by his unfinished masterpiece. Once the adventurers finished the bard's work, he was allowed to rest once more, and the true power of The Sword of Myrloch Vale was unlocked.

At some point before his death in the , the white dragon Ingeloakastimizilian, also known as Icingdeath, the mate of Ixbillailzakzillimiliax, added The Sword of Myrloch Vale to his hoard, where it remained until at least the , when the Hero of Neverwinter and their companions may have faced Icingdeath in his ghost dragon form and looted the remnants of the hoard.
