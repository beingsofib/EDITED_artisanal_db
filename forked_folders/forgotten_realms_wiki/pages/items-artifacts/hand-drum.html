A hand drum, sometimes called hand-drum, was a percussion musical instrument.

Description

Hand drums consisted of double-headed drums with a shoulder strap along their sides. They were used by bards either as rhythmic instruments to accompany ensembles or by themselves as alarms to alert of danger or to mark narrative campfire tales.

Availability

  • Aurora's Whole Realms Catalogue sold hand drums made out of solid ash, cherry, and ironwood for 20 gold pieces a piece.
