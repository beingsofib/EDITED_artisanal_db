A hand of the mage was an enchanted amulet that, aptly named in both appearance and use, allowed its wearer to call upon a mage hand spell at their leisure.

Description

It was nothing less than a mummified elven hand, typically attached as a pendant to a gold chain and worn around the neck as though it was any other amulet. Altogether, it weighed about .

Powers

An experienced-enough artisan of magical items needed only be able to cast the mage hand spell themselves to imbue it into their hand of the mage, which could then be activated by the wielder's will. They could do so as often as they wished, being restricted only by the spell's own limitations.

Notable Owners

By Faerûn's late 14 century DR, a hand of the mage was a typical piece of equipment used by experienced Red Wizards of Thay, as well as by Chondathans as a whole. So pervasive was the use of such hands among the latter that, in any sufficiently populous city of the Vilhon Reach, Sembia, the Dragon Coast, and Cormyr, the item was normally discounted a tenth of its typical cost whenever sold.

Specific individuals who owned a hand of the mage included:

  • Dark Stag, an ex-adventuress and the local Herald of Silverymoon.
  • Helcav, a drow wizard faithful to Laveth, whose mummified hand of the mage was specifically that of a drow.
  • The green dragon Claugiyliamatar, as well as the dracolich Daurgothoth, both of whom kept a hand among their hoards.

Hands of the mage could also be found on the distant world of Oerth, and even on that of Krynn, typically in the varied pouches of the kender.
