Bronzewood tlahuitolli and their more powerful variant, exalted bronzewood tlahuitolli, were a variety of artifact created to be used by ranger adventurers in the Chultan city of Port Nyanzaru in the late 15 century DR.

Description

These bronzewood tlahuitolli were testaments of their owners' strength and the influence of their guilds. These magic items could be created by the most competent weaponscrafters of Chult with the use of obsidian, living bronzewood, ornamentation of jungle feathers, Chultan silk, and lakh varnish.

Powers

Bronzewood tlahuitolli could be enchanted to house various magics customized for each owner, in addition to boosting the owner's accuracy, battle prowess, and their ability to land well-aimed, devastating, and lucky strikes. When a feathered ilhuilli was wielded with a bronzewood tlahuitolli, both items boosted each other's damage output, improved healing magics, and absorbed a small portion of incoming damage.

History

Bronzewood tlahuitolli were created in the late 15 century DR, and their design suggested the items' origins to be tied to the ancient Lost City of Omu.
