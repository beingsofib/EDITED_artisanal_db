Longspears were a variety of spears noted for having a greater length and weight than the average spear.

Description

The length of the shaft for a longspear varied, though was generally in the range of , and weighed . another member of the spear family of pole arms that had considerably longer shafts.

Weaponology

Due to their considerable size and length, longspears were ineffective throwing weapons and required both hands to wield properly.

Notable Longspears

  • Death spears, enchanted longspears that were infused with negative energy and used by elite drow warriors.
  • Rod of surprises, an enchanted +1 rod that could transform in a variety of weapons, including the longspear.
  • Spear of Morgur, an enchanted longspear originating from the ruins of Illusk.

Notable Users

  • Demeter, the Olympian goddess of agriculture, wielded an enchanted longspear.
  • Pil'it'ith, a sarrukh cleric, a wielded a +4 longspear with the weapon qualities brilliant, energy, and unholy.
  • Pujik, an ormyrr chieftain, carried a +3 longspear.

Races & Sentient Creatures

  • For aquatic ogres the longspear was often their weapon of choice.
  • Coal golems were typically armed by their creators with oversized metal longspears that glowed red from the heat of their bodies.
  • Among cyclopses, those in the Shining South region often wielded longspears.
  • Firenewts were sometimes known to fight with longspears.
  • For fossergrim a longspear was typically the weapon of choice for close-quarters combat.
  • Lamia nobles sometimes fought with enchanted longspears.
  • Among ogres a longspear was common weapon.
  • Orcs were often known to wield longspears.

Religions

  • Longspears were the favored weapon of a variety of deities, including Sebek and Sseth.
