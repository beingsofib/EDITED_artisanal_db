Lucerne hammers were a type of polearm in the Realms that had an infamous reputation.

Description

At a glance, this weapon bore a resemblance to a bec de corbin or military picks. Lucerne hammers had long poles, reaching as long as in length. These were mounted with a small, clawed three or four-pronged hammer head and sporting an awl spike that was longer than that of a bec de corbin.

It was not uncommon for the whole weapon to be forged of steel, often gilded with precious metals or decorated with carvings.

Weaponology

The lucerne hammer was rather heavy and slow to move. but were also sometimes used to dismount enemies. The awl spike, rather than being used to pierce armor, was meant to keep enemies at bay.

Availability

Places in the Realms where one could purchase lucerne hammers included:

  • The Weaponers of Cormyr.

Notable Lucerne Hammers

  • The Hammer of Lucerne, an enchanted lucerne hammer that was commissioned by Lodur the Brave under the mistaken assumption that it was actually a hammer.

Notable Owners

  • Aruma Blane, a monk who later came into possession of The Hammer of Lucerne.
  • The Ravenian merchant Bendekar had a lucerne hammer in his home's expansive weapons collection.

Trivia

The entrance to Shraevyn's Tomb featured a statue of an armored warrior holding a massive lucerne hammer.
