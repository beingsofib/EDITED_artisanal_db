Tonthur-nut sauce was a condiment made out of tonthur nuts harvested from the mountainous areas in Amn, Tethyr, near Starmantle, Turmish, and the Vilhon Reach, often used to give flavor to bland foods like potatoes or cover up the cook's mistakes.

Description

To make the sauce, tonthur nuts were crushed and then boiled with a number of spices. The resulting sauce was a thick brown condiment with mouth-sizzling spicy heat. The sauce also inherited the heavy body from the natural fats that tonthur was fulled with. The sauce also had a nutty, peppercorny, and mushroomy flavor.

The sauce was packed in small clay jars. Their tops were sealed and, once the sauce was used up, the jars were used to store oils and other similar substances.

History

By the 15 century DR, tonthur-nut sauce was sold by merchant caravans and drovers all along the Sword Coast, the Tashalar, and anywhere else the Calishite merchants traveled.
