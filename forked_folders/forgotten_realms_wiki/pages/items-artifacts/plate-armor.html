Plate armor (also known variously as plate mail, field plate, full plate, or simply plate) was a type of armor used on Toril. It made up a wide variety of different heavy armor designs that covered the entire body.

Description

Plate armor consisted of shaped, solid plates that covered various parts of body. It was supplemented by chain armor that covered any gaps, and beneath the metal was a thick layer of padding. The whole suit was held together with numerous straps and harnesses to distribute the weight.

Many of the pieces that made up a set of plate had their own distinct names, such as the couter (elbow guard), pauldron (shoulder guard), rerebrace (upper-arm guard), vambrace (forearm guard), and gorget (throat-plate).

Plate was often the heaviest type of armor available, sacrificing mobility and agility for superior protection.

Variations

Plate armor was among the most expensive and heavy armor a warrior could buy. The different types each had their own advantages and drawbacks to their designs.

  • Bronze plate mail: A variety of partial plate making use of bronze, either due to limited resources or for ceremonial purposes.
  • Plate mail: Also known as "half plate", plate mail was a basic but effective version of armor that protected part of the body with plates, with chain mail covering the rest. Dwarven plate mail was a perfected form of this armor, used almost exclusively by dwarves.
  • Field plate: A general form of full-coverage plate armor which had to be fitted to the wearer.
  • Full plate: Also known as a "coat-of-plate", this represented the peak of plate armor technology, with expertly fitted interlocking plates that provided the best protection.
  • Heavy plate: A heavily-reinforced version of full plate worn by extremely strong humanoids.

Notable plate mail

  • Storm armor of the earth's children - a rare full set of plate mail that protected its wearer against the elements of cold, electricity and fire.

Notable Users

Groups

  • The Hellriders of Elturel wore suits of white plate mail.
  • In Northmen society, plate mail was often worn by kings or an army's captain.
  • The Purple Dragons' warriors tended to favor plate mail. When adventuring abroad, they typically wore suits of field plate mail.

Regions & Nations

  • In the nation of Sespech, plate mail was among the armor types generally favored by warriors.
  • In the nation of Unther, warriors within the army were made to wear bronze plate mail.

Religions

  • Battleforges, a variety of specialty priest of the Church of Tempus, wore sturdy suits of plate mail.
  • Bulwarks, a variety of specialty priest of the Church of Helm, generally wore suits of plate mail.
  • Plate mail was one of two types of armor that chaos knights, a variety of specialty priest of the Church of Talos, generally favored. However, the plate mail of chaos knights was notably black in coloration and decorated with barbs.
  • Plate mail was one of two types of armor that gauntlets, a variety of specialty priest of the Church of Iyachtu Xvim, generally wore. Their plate mail was notably black in coloration.
  • Plate mail was among the types of armor that glorybloods, a variety of specialty priest of the Church of Tempus, most often wore.
  • Hands of Tyr, a variety of specialty priest of the Church of Tyr, wore white suits of plate mail.
  • Paragons, a variety of specialty priest of the Church of Torm, wore glistening white suits of plate mail.
  • Questers, another variety of specialty priest of the Church of Helm, always wore suits of full plate mail.
  • Scales of Tyr, another variety of specialty priest of the Church of Tyr, often wore suits of plate mail underneath their white robes of office.
  • Plate mail was among the types of armor that swords of Cyric, a variety of specialty priest of the Church of Cyric, most often wore.
