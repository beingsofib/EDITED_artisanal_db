The Chain of Scales was the artifact jewelry piece that once belonged to Fenthaza, the high priestess of Dendar, who laired in the Fane of the Night Serpent of the ruined city of Omu in Chult, created sometime in or before the late 15 century DR.

Description

The Chain of Scales was a collection of six interlocking heavy platinum rings on a chain. This jewelry was of the finest quality, but too small to be worn as a necklace. Each of the large rings had a large yuan-ti scale attached. Each scale was unique and magically fortified.

Powers

Even in its broken state, the Chain of Scales felt alive with magical power, humming with the energies of its former mistress, who likely had kept the artifact close to her for the longest time.

As the Chain of Scales was restored from a single ring to all six and then further empowered via magic, the artifact's powers were restored and, eventually, boosted. At full strength, the Chain of Scales increased the wearer's awareness of their surroundings and alertness in battle. Additionally, it boosted their physical strength, defense, ability to dodge, stamina recovery, and empowered their well-aimed or lucky attacks.

History

In the late 15 century DR, as the death curse ravaged Faerûn, adventurers from the north attempted to end the magical malaice. The quest brought them face-to-face with Ras Nsi and his yuan-ti cultists. One of the snakemen slain in the struggle was Fenthaza, a high priestess of Dendar. As her corpse was deliquescing, a collection of broken rings of the Chain of Scales was recovered and brought to Port Nyanzaru to be gradually restored to full power.
