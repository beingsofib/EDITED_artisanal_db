The prison of Zagyg was a minor artifact created by the Oerthian wizard Zagyg Yragerne that could imprison almost any creature. It was widely believed that there were only five copies of the artifact in existence.

Description

All copies of the prison of Zagyg were nearly identical, having the appearance of a small, well-made brass birdcage, studded with gems and sporting a padlock that had the appearance of diamond. They weighed no more than .

Powers

These artifacts were only usable by wizards and other arcane spellcasters.

Both the interior and exterior of a prison of Zagyg were constantly under the effect of permanent anti-magic The latter effect made these cages and their prisoners impossible to locate by magical means, including by divinations of all sorts. If detect magic was ever cast upon a cage, the spell had only a 50% chance of detecting its magic, which was a strong abjuration dweomer. Otherwise, detect magic would either not discern what class of magic it was or give a false reading.

Additionally, any creature imprisoned within the confines of one of these cages was magically sustained, no longer requiring food or water.

Prisons of Zagyg were nearly impervious to damage, harm, or otherwise being affected by all manner of physical force or magic. However, each copy of the prison of Zagyg had its own weakness that could be exploited to cause its destruction. These methods were unknown, but guaranteed to be dangerous. If somehow one of these cages were destroyed, the creature imprisoned within them would be returned to their normal size. Alternatively, the spells alter reality or wish could be used to open a prison of Zagyg.

Operating a prison of Zagyg required attunement and knowledge of its four command words that activates it, though with tremendous expertise in the arcane a spellcaster could bypass the need for them. A prison of Zagyg would function only for those attuned to it, even if the appropriate command words were spoken. They could only be attuned to one creature at a time and upon changing ownership the previous owner was immediately made aware.

Each cage had own particular set of command words, separated into: activation, trigger or target, command spell or imprison, and freedom.

Action: This command word had two effects. The first was to attune itself to whoever spoke it. The second effect spoking it had was activating its padlock, sealing the door to the cage shut as if wizard lock had been cast upon it.

Target: By speaking this command word the possessor targeted a creature for imprisonment within the prison of Zagyg. According to some, this could only be done on creatures that were within of the cage, while others claimed it had no limiting range.

Imprison: Otherwise known as command spell, for some sources told of it being a distinct spell or incantation that had to be spoken, this command word teleported the target creature to the interior of the prison of Zagyg and shrunk their body to in size. Those who claimed it to be an incantation said that the possessor had to include either the personal name of the creature being targeted or a detailed recitation of their history.

Freedom: By speaking this command word the possessor caused the imprisoned creature to be teleported out of the prison of Zagyg, instantly restoring their former size and abilities. The possessor had control over where the the prisoner would be teleported to within of the cage.

Creation

The methods and materials needed to create a prison of Zagyg were unknown, lost with their creator,

History

In the Oerthian year of 318 CY, Zagyg led the Company of Seven on an expedition to find the lost ruins of Veralos. Within its depths the wizard discovered a prototype magical cage that would serve as the basis for his prison of Zagyg.

Notable Owners

  • Emelyn the Gray, a powerful archmage in the Bloodstone Lands of northeast Faerûn, possessed a prison of Zagyg in 1359 DR.
  • Iggwilv, a powerful archmage from Oerth who had studied under Zagyg, stole one of his prisons and stored it along with other treasures inside the Lost Caverns of Tsojcanth. She had its command words written down in the Demonomicon of Iggwilv. These and other powerful artifacts fell out of her possession during her captivity in Azzagrat.
  • The government of Ravens Bluff held a prison of Zagyg within their vaults circa 1353 DR. This copy was stolen by the wizard Panese the Excitable, who hid it beneath of water in the bottom of a well within his basement, with a serpentine owl stored inside it. His theft landed the wizard a ten year prison sentence. He was offered the chance to commute his sentence to five years if he revealed where he hid the prison of Zagyg, but the wizard decided he would remain tight-lipped until he had served five years of his sentence.
  • A will-o'-wisp in the caverns of Deepearth was in possession of one of these in 1358 DR.
