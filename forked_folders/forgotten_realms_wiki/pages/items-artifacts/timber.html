Timber or lumber referred to the raw materials of wood or whole trees harvested and traded across Faerûn and beyond. "Timber" generally referred to the rough or unprocessed wood while "lumber" generally referred to smooth-sawn or milled timber. They were regarded as valuable natural resources.

Usages

Timber and lumber were predominantly used as construction materials and as inputs to manufacture various trade goods, furniture, and weapons. They were also used in shipbuilding

Availability

Major sources of timber included the forests of Cormanthor and the Arch Wood in the Dalelands, the Forest of Amtar in Dambrath, the King's Forest in Cormyr, the Moonshae Isles, the Moonsea, The North, Rashemen, Turmish, and the Yuirwood of Aglarond. Major exporters of timber included Aglarond, Chondath (especially the timber industry of Hlath near the Nunwood), Chult (where large or exotic jungle timber, such as that of rubber trees, was a much-sought resource), Cormyr, the Dalelands (especially Archendale, Deepingdale, and Harrowdale), the Moonsea (especially in the south around Hillsfar), the Moonshae Isles (who notably sold massive pine timbers favored by shipbuilders of the Sword Coast), the settlements of the Savage Frontier (notably Beorunna's Well, Everlund, and Silverymoon), the Sword Coast North, Thay (notably timber of the blackwood tree), Thesk, and Turmish.

Timber often was transported from the remote areas in which it was harvested via cart or via rafts and barges down large rivers, such as the Neverwinter River in the Sword Coast North, the River Rauvin in the Silver Marches, Major importers of timber included Calimport, Melvaunt, Mintarn, and Waterdeep. The civilizations of the Underdark were also major buyers of timber, although they also were known to cultivate timber-like fungi known as funguswood and zurkhwood.

Notable sources of timber in Kara-Tur included Chu' Yuan and Kozakura (especially Hinomoto, Kanshu, and the Waturi Forest). Bamboo was a major source or substitute for traditional timber in some parts of Kara-Tur.

In Zakhara, timber was a building material that only the wealthy could generally afford.

Outside of major timber exporting and importing, many settlements harvested timber from nearby forests for their local needs. For example, Bowshot supplied lumber to Daggerford, the Cloakwood supplied timber to Baldur's Gate, Lonelywood's timber industry supplied the needs of Icewind Dale, and the Riftwood was used for timber by the dwarves of the Deep Realm. In places where natural timber was not available, some settlements repurposed timber from other structures, such as ships or nearby ruins. The spell Leomund's tiny hut could conjure structures made of timber.

History

Settlers seeking new sources of timber were a historic driver of the formation of new human settlements across Faerûn. For example, the Emerald Enclave was known to keep an eye on lumber industries in the Vilhon Reach Clashes with druids over timber-cutting was known to sometimes lead to persecution of the worshipers of nature deities like Silvanus. Timber-cutters themselves were known to make offerings to Mielikki for protection or to erect her shrines to mark the places beyond which cutting trees might anger her.

After the fall of ancient Netheril, its successor kingdom of Hlondath became known for its exports of timber from the Border Forest.

Sembia had once been known for its high-quality iliyr-wood timber, which was ideal for shipbuilding, however fears of clearcutting led to violence with the local elves in the , who drove them back from the woods. Likewise, the forests of the Great Dale were long used as sources of lumber, but the woods had become dangerous and were largely left alone as of the mid–14 century DR. Meanwhile, careful management of the timberland in Deepingdale had given the region an almost garden-like aesthetic.

In the longstanding conflicts between Thay and Rashemen, the latter's greater natural supply of timber was an important strategic factor in laying claim to Lake Mulsantir, for they could build more ships more quickly than the Thayans.

Beginning in the 1360s DR, the church of Mielikki had begun a project of buying large tracts of land in order to farm timber, which they then sold in order to dissuade cutting of nearby natural forests. They referred to these as "forest shields", and used violence to deter cutting in the forests which these shields were intended to protect. The first such shields were planted in Sembia, followed by Amn and Tethyr.

By the mid-to-late–14 century DR, the Silver Marches' economy depended heavily on the export of timber. Some cities, like Everlund and Sundabar, had mostly clear-cut their available woodlands, but the region remained densely forested. Timber-cutting was a dangerous business, sometimes requiring adventurers to protect the workers from monstrous fauna. Despite the large size of the High Forest, it remained largely untouched because it was simply too risky to cut wood within its borders without the consent of local treants.

As of the late 15 century DR, Amnian merchant interests had nearly clear-cut the island of Snowdown in the Moonshae Isles, sending the lumber back to Amn. Meanwhile, the city of Neverwinter clear-cut the nearby Upland Rise to rebuild its docks after having lost access to the timber from Thundertree following the Ruining.
