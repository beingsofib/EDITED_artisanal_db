A quaggi was a structure found in Iulutiun villages.

Description

Traditionally, Iulutiuns gathered for communal feasts and celebrations in outdoor settings. However, during inclement weather, they resorted to using a quaggi, which was a feast house specifically designed for such occasions. The quaggi was a spacious dome, towering between in height and boasting a diameter of at least . At the center of the dome, there was a prominent quidlirin, a communal hearth.

Inside the quaggi, two ledges constructed from snow served as seating arrangements. The first ledge, positioned along the interior wall, stood approximately tall, while a second ledge sat just below it, measuring around in height. Typically, a village was equipped with one quaggi for approximately every hundred adults, ensuring ample space for gatherings and festivities.
