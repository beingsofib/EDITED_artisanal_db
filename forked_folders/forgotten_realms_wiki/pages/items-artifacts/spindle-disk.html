A spindle-disk or spindle-disks were a weapon and an amusing curio used by Cadderly Bonaduce.

Description

It was composed of two razor-sharp disks as large as a human man's fist and constructed of hard rock crystal. These were connected via a central axle that acted as a spindle. This was attached to a string some with a loop tied into the end, so as to be placed over the finger, allowing it to return to the hand when thrown. Cadderly improved on the original design by using a metal bar at the axle, with a hole through it that the string could be tied onto.

Usage

In combat, a wielder flicked the spindle-disks outward toward the opponent, making the disks spin forward to deal damage. Spindle-disks must be manually rewound if they hit their target, but if they missed they rolled back to the wielder's palm, automatically rewinding for the next attack. An attack from spindle-disks could be used to incapacitate or disarm a foe.

History

They were based on ancient halfling weapons from the tribes of southern Luiren.

By the mid–14 century DR, the weapon was mostly forgotten about apart from being described in books. From these books, Cadderly built his own spindle-diskes and improved the design, using it as a weapon in his adventures.

Notable Users

  • Cadderly Bonaduce, a noted adventurer of the mid-to-late 14 century DR, a Chosen cleric of Deneir formerly of the Edificant Library in Erlkazar, and the founder of the great library-cathedral of Spirit Soaring.
