Bronzewood quauhololli and their more powerful variant, exalted bronzewood quauhololli, were a variety of artifact maca created to be used by paladin adventurers in the Chultan city of Port Nyanzaru in the late 15 century DR.

Description

These bronzewood quauhololli were testaments of their owners' strength and the influence of their guilds. These magic items could be created by the most competent weapon-crafters of Chult with the use of obsidian and bronzewood.

Powers

Bronzewood quauhololli could be enchanted to house various magics customized for each owner, in addition to boosting the owner's battle prowess, their skills to deflect enemy strikes, and strengthening their especially lucky and well-aimed attacks. When a bronzewood cuauhchimalli was wielded with a bronzewood quauhololli, both items boosted each other's damage output, the wielder's healing capabilities, and absorbed some of the incoming damage, as well as granted the same boons to the wielder's allies.

History

Bronzewood quauhololli were created in the late 15 century DR, and their design suggested the items' origins to be tied to the ancient Lost City of Omu.
