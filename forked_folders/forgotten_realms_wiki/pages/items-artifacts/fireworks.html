Fireworks

Fireworks were a type of low explosive in the Realms designed for the purpose of entertainment and aesthetic displays. Those who made or handled fireworks were sometimes known as pyrobolists.

Description

Fireworks produced a sort of popping sound when they went off, usually in a rapid succession that could be heard from great distances, and dazzling displays of colors and lights in the sky. Through the use of dyes, different effects could be achieved. Some fireworks required a mortar in order to be shot into the air, while others flew by their own propulsion.

Smokepowder was used in the production of fireworks. More specifically, small, magically limited kernels known as fireseeds. These were what imparted fireworks their distinctive "popping" noise.

The smell of fireworks was compared by some to that of brimstone.

Availability

Across the Realms, fireworks were produced in Baldur's Gate, Lantan, Shou Lung, The fireworks made in Shou Lung were exported all across Kara-tur and western Faerûn's Sword Coast, though Shou fireworks were an uncommon sight in Waterdeep. Outside of Kara-Tur, fireworks were considered by some to be a rather Gnomish field of crafts, with some becoming quite devoted to their making. Most fireworks in Waterdeep were produced by rock gnomes.

In the city of Baldur's Gate, Felogyr's Fireworks had a monopoly on fireworks production, as its owners were the only Baldurians with a license to produce smokepowder, a state of affairs that went as far back as the shop's founding. In the late 1360s DR, the prices for fireworks ranged from 25 to 75 gold pieces.

Additionally, a number of Gondar temples produced and sold fireworks. Beyond manufacturers, fireworks were found to varying degrees in Calimshan, Delzimmer, Halruaa, the Blade Kingdoms, and Westgate, as well as in the City of Brass in the Plane of Fire,

Varieties

  • Firecrackers, a type of small ground firework that was developed in Shou Lung.
  • Oddfire, a brand of fireworks that used a unique mixture of powders to create particoloured explosions. Named for the company that produced them, Oddfire, they could be found for sale at Felogyr's Fireworks in the late 15 century DR.
  • Twinkledust, a type of firework similar to firecrackers, but produced in the Blade Kingdoms.
  • Whizbangs, a type of firework sold at Felogyr's Fireworks in the late 15 century DR.

Safety

Though fireworks were designed for recreational purposes, like any explosive they could become dangerous if misused or handled improperly, both to the person operating them and bystanders, ranging from burns to serious wounds. For safety it was often recommended that one wear gloves while handling them, to ignite them at arm's length, and to be mindful of wind direction.

Animals, such as dogs, could be frightened by the loud noise they produced.

Usage

Fireworks were often used at festivals and celebrations.

  • In Thentia, fireworks featured in many periodic, popular festivals.
  • They were used as part of some Feast of the Moon celebrations in Baldur's Gate and Delzimmer.
  • In Neverwinter, celebrations of the Shou festival Feast of Lanterns featured fireworks displays, which were organized by Far Eastern pyrobolists.
  • In west Zakhara, fireworks were fired off in the city of Gana as part of the Festival of the Pearl.
  • Small fireworks were used by House Baenre during celebrations.
  • The Church of Berronar Truesilver used sparkler fireworks on their holy days, Midwinter day and Midsummer night, to mark the ending of each unison prayer.
  • In Waterdeep, the Church of Mystra celebrated the holiday Gods' Day with a huge festival that culminated in a magical fireworks display that went on well into the night.
  • A fireworks display was a common feature of the festival of Lliira's Night in the Sword Coast in the late 15 century DR, having been first popularized in Baldurian celebrations of the festival by Felogyr's Fireworks.

History

By 1357 DR, fireworks had been a common fixture in the nation of Shou Lung for several hundred years.

In the , the arcanaloth Yrkhetep impersonated Khan Kertep and led his barbarian army against the city of Hlath in Chondath. During the siege of Hlath, in the heat of battle against the city's gathered defenders, he set off fireworks to confuse and frighten the Faerûnians, counting on them having no experience with them.

In the , Iakhovas attacked Baldur's Gate with his army of sahuagin and other sea creatures as part of the Twelfth Serôs War. Adventurers Khlinat Ironeater and Jherek Wolf's-get managed to kill many sahuagin and repel the attack by detonating a wagon full of kegs of smokepowder and fireworks that they had gotten from Felogyr's Fireworks.

Celebratory fireworks became a common occurrence in the skies of Halruaa following the nation's victory against the Crinti

Sometime in the , a disguised Imbralym Skoond visited Felogyr's Fireworks and bought everything it had in stock, as well as all of its raw materials, under the alias "Horus Kope", on behalf of Grand Duke Torlin Silvershield. Initially he sought to break Felogyr's monopoly on fireworks, having Skoond line up suppliers so he could arrange to produce more smokepowder, though his plans were derailed by Bhaal's growing influence on his mind. By 1492 DR, Felogyr's continued to have a monopoly over fireworks in the city.

Following the end of the High Sun Games, the downfall of Forge Fitzwilliam and Sofina, and the liberation of Neverwinter and the Neverwinter Wood in the late 1490s DR, the people of the area celebrated with a display of fireworks in the air between the city and the forest. These were watched by the adventurers responsible—Edgin Darvis, Holga Kilgore, Simon Aumar, Doric, and Kira Darvis—from a wood elf tree village in the forest.
