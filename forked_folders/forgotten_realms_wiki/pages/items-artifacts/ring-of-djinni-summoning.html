A ring of djinni summoning, also sometimes called ring of djinni calling, or simply djinn ring, was a variant of a ring of genie summoning from Zakhara, more common in Faerûn, that could summon a particular djinni from the Elemental Plane of Air.

Powers

When activated via rubbing, The ring would lose its magic if the djinni associated with it died.

Similar items were known to exist, like rings of efreeti calling, that were identical to the rings of djinni summoning, but summoned forth a different variety of a genie.

When observed through magic-detecting, rings of djinni summoning radiated powerful aura of conjuration.

Creation

Creating a ring of djinni summoning required a heart of a great wyrm brass dragon, valued at 25,000 gold pieces. The crafter should also be proficient at ring-making and creation of enchanted items, and being able to cast the gate spell.

History

By the mid-14 century DR, it was illegal to own rings of djinni summoning in Calimport and was considered to be a capital crime.

  • Aziz min Hajama, the Captain of the Gate of Revelations in Huzuz, in the 14 century DR.
  • Iltharagh, a topaz dracolich, who laired north of Luskan, owned a rings of djinni summoning in the late 14 century DR.
  • Iryklathagra, a great blue dragon, also known as Sharpfangs, had a ring of efreeti calling stashed away in her hoard in the Small Teeth mountains in the late 14 century DR.
  • Jonathon Morningmist, a vampire in the mid-14 century DR.

  • Tertius Wands, whose servant through a ring was the djinni Ampratines in the late 14 century DR.

  • Tiralia, an adventurer and a wizard, part of the Iron Maidens in the 14 century DR.
  • Tyranthraxus, kept a djinn ring in his lair within the ruins of Myth Drannor in the mid 14 century DR.
