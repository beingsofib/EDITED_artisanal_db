Road to Tarath Marad

The road to Tarath Marad was a series of underground passages within Eberron's underworld of Khyber, which led to Tarath Marad and the Spinner's Prison.

Description

The road was a twisting and circuitous path through many caverns and tunnels, starting from near the Harbor of Stormreach in Xen'drik and then passing to the depths of Khyber. The passageways were lined with many dragonshards that glowed in the darkness, and the deeper reaches were lined with enormous and thick spider webs. Many areas were partially or wholly blocked off by flows of molten magma, chasms, and cave-ins. Further obstacles were posed by active magical barriers created by the servants of the Silver Flame, which could only be broken with great difficulty by the forces of evil.

The tunnels were inhabited by ferocious and deadly fiends, including hell hounds, hezrou, and fire reavers. The fire reavers were especially fond of the magma pools.

History

The Spinner's Prison was created during the Age of Demons, when the Silver Flame trapped the Spinner of Shadows in the depths of the earth and bound her with the Silver Flame Guardian. Outside the prison on the road, a servant of the Silver Flame was teleported in from the surface and maintained the various magical wards that kept the prison together and prevented outside interference. Brother Aurelius was the latest attendant, and lived in a homey nook near the prison.

Around the time of the Darkening and the Sschindylryn invasion of Eveningstar in the mid-1480s DR, the Lords of Dust in Stormreach performed a great ritual that blasted open a hole in the surface of the Harbor and opened up the formerly hidden road to Tarath Marad, seeking to find and free the Spinner of Shadows below. The Church of the Silver Flame, the Twelve, and the Gatekeepers all arrived to stop the Lords of Dust, but were slowed by the many fanatical cultists and the demons they brought to bear. While the cultists harvested the local dragonshards for the Spinner, they were also predated upon by the local fiends, such as the Fire Reaver Lord, who found them quite tasty.
