The Imperial City was the core city inside Shoonach, capital of the Shoon Imperium.

Geography

The Imperial City was in the center of Shoonach, surrounding the Imperial Mount. It was just north of Agis and south of Iltakar, and surrounded by the Fedhiyajar farmlands. To the east was the town of Debukkher, filled with the temples and tombs used by the imperial residents. Attached to the Imperial City to the west was the town of Mamlukkar, devoted entirely to the slave population who served and maintained the capital.

Description

The city was filled with palaces, temples, luxury apartments, and offices for clerks, all dedicated to the running of the empire. It was a well-planned city with an extensive undercity level, sewer system, and a grid of streets and walkways, intended to be the perfect urban environment for the rulers of the empire. Even after thousands of years, ancient dwarven water pumps from High Shanatar pumped fresh water to the surface which was then used in fountains. However, the water in the Imperial City was tainted by the intentional placement of a black dragon's carcass in a wellspring beneath the city, making the waters mildly poisonous.

History

The Imperial City was constructed from when the efforts to build Shoonach began.

In 450 DR Prince Strohm I slew Emperor Amahl Shoon VII and destroyed the city in the Great Fires of Shoonach, killing the entire known Shoon Dynasty and most of the generals and officials responsible for administrating the empire. The razed city was abandoned, with a wall built around most of it in order to contain the strange horrors that emerged afterwards.

Inhabitants

By the mid-14 century DR, a force of drow explorers and treasure-hunters had infiltrated the sewers and tunnels below the Imperial City as they looted the place, only to find themselves trapped by the containment measures of the place. Controlling about 30% of the underground ruins, they were contested by leucrottas, doppelgangers, and other monsters, and roving bands of goblins, hobgoblins, lamias (including nobles), and gnolls. Above all, the ruins were dominated by the seemingly endless undead that emerged from the Imperial City, largely originating with Shoon's old Necromancers' Corps.
