Daerlun was a large city in Sembia.

Government

As of , the city was run by an elderly merchant named Halath Tymmyr, although he was a puppet for the evil commander of the guard, Allathast, who was a member of the Cult of the Dragon.

Circa , Daerlun came under the control of the Netherese Empire along with the rest of Sembia, The city declared itself a neutral independent city-state in the , rather than be absorbed by Cormyr or stay under the direct influence of Sembia.

Defenses

Daerlun was the most heavily fortified city in Sembia, and boasted the tallest curtain wall on all of Toril. This wall was made of magically hard obsidian and was erected by the Netherese Empire in the . It was garrisoned by a massive army of conscripts from across Sembia, who drilled on massive parade grounds near the city's markets.

Patrols out of Daerlun—mounted on flying griffons or veserabs—ranged as far as the Vast Swamp, the Thunder Peaks, and the southern Dales.

Trade

The city had a bustling marketplace and a wealthy trade quarter.

As of the mid-14 century DR, Daerlunian linen sold for around 8 sp per square yard, while Daerlunian wool sold for between 9 sp and 2 gp per square yard depending on the weight of the fabric. The city imported such commodities as parchment, wine, and wool from Eveningstar in Cormyr.

History

The evil semi-religious organization, the Cult of the Dragon, had a presence in Daerlun as of the mid-to-late 14 century DR, but preferred a secretive approach, using the city as a place to store armaments and to treat wounded members. The commander of the city guard, Allathast, used the elderly merchant Halath Tymmyr as a puppet to rule the city.

In the , the city prepared defenses against a possible Shadovar and Sembian invasion. Rumors abounded in the city about gods, war, and natural disasters.

Notable Locations

  • Alask's Arrow: Decent inn and tavern.
  • Bergun Hall: Courthouse and records library.
  • The Black Banner Inn: High-class accommodations with gambling and a well-stocked wine cellar on the Street of the Dragon.
  • The House of Firehair: Large walled temple complex in the center of town.
  • The Mighty Mace: Another fine inn and tavern that faced the Westmarket.
  • Oakwaters: The city's large public bathing pool.
  • The Old Plough: Not-so-fine inn and tavern.
  • Place of Banners: Large, open area in the center of the city that contained Bergun Hall.
  • The Sea Arch: The southern gate into the city; opened onto Barrel Lane.
  • Shadowdark House: A magic shop on Saurovin Street.
  • The Sunset Gate: The western gate into the city.
  • The Westmarket: Large, open marketplace on the west side.

Notable Inhabitants

  • Aerbront: A kind wizard, sharper than he pretended to be.
  • Alask "Eagle-eyes": Owner/operator of Alask's Arrow inn and tavern.
  • Dhelarr "the Night Blade": Paymaster of the Firehands Group trading coster.
  • Elmareen Whistestone: Magical researcher, tutor, and reputed dragonslayer.
  • Halath Tymmyr: Old merchant. High Bergun in name only.
  • Harlyn Grimmerhand: Retired warrior, tutor, horse breeder, investor.
  • Minthalar "the Many-eyed"; Well-informed merchant that could get most anything for a price.
  • Othnyl Risingstone: Premier stone cutter, sculptor, and part-time weapons smith.
  • Sandar "Silkenvoice" Kathklan: Potion- and poison-maker, necromancer, dangerous.
  • Thauna Maskalar: High Priestess of the House of Firehair.
  • Thelbaerone Shadowdark: Wizard, sage, and proprietress of Shadowdark House.
