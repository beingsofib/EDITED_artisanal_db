Lake Zaltec

Lake Zaltec was one of the four lakes surrounding Nexal in Maztica. It was dedicated to the Maztican god Zaltec.

Description

Lake Zaltec was the largest of the four lakes surrounding Nexal, which was interpreted by Poshtli, prince of Nexal, to be because Zaltec was the god of war and war was the highest purpose, with no nation being better at war than Nexal. It lay on the east side of the city. The causeway of the Kultaka road on its northwest side separated it from Lake Azul and the causeway of the road to Palul on its south side separated it from Lake Tezca.

History

Erixitl, chosen of Qotal, and her husband Halloran went across this lake by boat to re-enter the city of Nexal after it had been surrendered to the Golden Legion in 1361 DR. During the Night of Wailing, when Nexal was destroyed, a small surviving remnant of the legion attempted an escape on a causeway but masses of Maztican warriors in boats on Lake Zaltec pulled out the boards that connected the causeway in order to prevent their escape. Bishou Domincus, the Legion's cleric of Helm, was grabbed by a hook onto one of these boats and later sacrificed to Zaltec.

Following the destruction of Nexal, Zaltec himself took physical form in the city and walked across the lake to leave the city.
