Callidyrr, sometimes called the Realm of the High King, was the most powerful Ffolk kingdom in the Moonshae Isles. It occupied the south of the island of Alaron.

Geography

Callidyrr encompassed much of the Fairheight Range on its northern border with Gnarhelm, and included all lands south of those mountains. The kingdom was broken into four distinct counties: Fairheight in the north, Stagford in the west, Talffolk in the east, and Stirgewick in the south. Aside from the Fairheight Range, the Dernall Forest was the dominant feature in northern Callidyrr, through which flowed the Swanmay River. The plains of Bregonshire covered much of southern Callidyrr. Much of the land was gentle, rolling hills and open country, which was crossed by many roads (some well-paved in white granite and others badly in need of repairs).

Government

Callidyrr was ruled from the Palace of the High King in Caer Callidyrr by the High Kings and Queens of the Ffolk.

During the early-to-mid 14 century DR, the council of sorcerers wielded a great deal of direct power over many parts of Callidyrr despite the fact that they ostensibly existed only to advise the High Kings and Queens.

Defenses

The High Kings and Queens had a standing army consisting of their own elite bodyguards (consisting of 180 skilled fighters) and an additional 1,800 troops, which included 300 elite infantry, 400 longbow-wielding archers, 300 cavalry, and 25 chariots each manned by a crew of 2 charioteers. This force mustered alongside Callidyrr's knights of the realm, and could be supplemented by militias from the major cantrevs, each of which could field 200-800 troops (about 1-in-4 of whom were trained with the longbow and another 1-in-6 trained in mounted combat) as well as a few more chariots. In general, the cantrevs closer to the dangers of the Fairheight range (including frequent attacks by orcs and goblins) maintained the larger militas.

Thanks to the presence of the council of sorcerers, Callidyrr long boasted by far the greatest number and potency of battle-ready wizards in the Moonshaes.

During the Darkwalker War, the realm assembled a fearsome mercenary army known as the Scarlet Guard, comprising three brigades of ruthless, battle-hardened soldiers and a fourth brigade of armored ogres. It was claimed to be the most feared and dangerous army in the Moonshae Isles despite numbering no more than 2,400 strong.

During the 15 century DR, an elite force known as the Callidyrr dragoons rose to prominence: these particularly loyal knights and heroes had the power to speak with the authority of the High Kings and Queens, and often embarked on clandestine missions for the crown.

Trade

Callidyrr's primary exports were weapons, armor, and other fine steel products, which were said to be of better quality than anything produced along the Sword Coast. It mainly traded north to Waterdeep and south to Calimport, where Moonshae timber and furs were also in demand. The land was also rich in mineral resources, including gold, silver, and other precious metals. and foreign merchants brought in spices, oil, and cloth from the north and silk, spice, horses, and parchment from the south.

As of the mid–14 century DR, Callidyrr also traded metal goods with the other Ffolk kingdoms of Corwell and Moray, and received ale, livestock, coal, iron, and labor in return. They additionally sold timber to nearby Mintarn, where it was used for shipbuilding.

The fertile lands made Callidyrr was a major agricultural breadbasket, and fishing was a major industry in the Strait of Alaron to the west and in Whitefish Bay to the east. The only mining in the realm was in the south of the Fairheight Range on the northern edge of the kingdom. These metal mining operations were the sole reason for the existence of the cantrevs in this dangerous region.

Culture

Callidyrr was said to be the center of Ffolk artisanship.

History

The realm was founded in the , by Cymrych Hugh upon his ascension as the first High King of the Ffolk.

By the mid–14 century DR, the kingdom had increasingly come under the sway of the council of sorcerers.

Following the founding of the United Moonshae Isles in the , the High Kings and Queens of Callidyrr became High Kings and Queens of the whole of the Moonshaes. Following the death of High Queen Alicia Kendrick in the , separatists and insurgents severely weakened the kingdom, and Callidyrr struggled to recover for over seven decades.

By the late 15 century DR, the kingdom was harried by mercenaries from Amn, lycanthropes, and unseelie fey from Dernall Forest, forcing many to flee their homes and seek refuge in Caer Callidyrr. The appearance of the fey in Dernall was seen by the kingdom as a violation of the armistice agreed upon between Callidyrr and the fey of Sarifal in prior decades.

Inhabitants

Owing to it being more well-connected to the Faerûnian mainland than any of the other Moonshae realms, Callidyrr had long been known be have the most diverse population on the Isles. Especially in the ports, visitors from Calimshan, Tethyr, or Mintarn were common sights. In outlying towns, residents were largely Ffolk and halflings.

Notable Inhabitants

  • The Kendrick family, who ruled from the mid–14 century DR onwards. As of the late 15 century DR, the High King was Derid Kendrick.
  • Antola Pilark, the Great Druid as of the late 15 century DR.
  • Huedyn, a druid who betrayed the realm in the late 15 century DR.
