Adder River

The Adder River ran south to north from the Adder Peaks to empty into the Bay of Chessenta through the Adder Swamp.

Geography

The Adder River was described as being a wide, slow flowing river with muddy water. Lush vegetation surrounded the river for its entire length, hiding plenty of dangerous snakes.

Geographical features

Mountains

  • Adder Peaks

Swamps

  • Adder Swamp

Inhabitants

A community of werecrocodiles was also known to inhabit its waters near the Adder Swamp.
