Danozi River

The Danozi River, which was also called the Danozi Wash, was a prominent river found within the Pasocada Basin of northern Maztica.

Geography

The Danozi originated from an unnamed cliff northeast of Michaca at the Dunobo Springs. It flowed southwest through the Sun Canyon past the City of Gold, then turned to flow southeast to eventually merge with the Pasocada River near Yapoza. This main section of the river was approximately long. The Danozi River was joined by a seasonal tributary originating in the east in the lands of the mountain lion tribe. This seasonal tributary joined the main river at Michaca.

Notable Locations

Michaca, the renowned "City of Gold", was built along the Danozi River. The settlements of Ukolo and Shokogya were found along the banks of the seasonal tributary.
