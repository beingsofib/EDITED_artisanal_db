The Old Wheel was a popular loud and frisky club where customers came to eat, drink, and be merry. It was located in Crimmor, the City of Caravans of Amn in the late 14 century DR.

Location

The Wheel stood on The Drae, the moot of The High Ride that ran from east to west, and the Dausann – south to north. The Drae was thought to be the destination for the city's best revelry, food, and feast.

Services

The provender served at the Wheel was a collection of greensplatters – the eatery's specialty. These salads were made with diced apples on the bed of long-beans, radishes, served in a hollowed-out crust of blackrind cheese. Other greensplatters included leafy greens, groundspears, stewed prawns, with a dressing of vegetable juice with mint, soft-boiled quail eggs, atop parsley and fried ground-moss.

The main course dishes on the menu included famous roasts of rothé awash with puddles upon puddles of wine gravies. For those preferring fish, the Wheel offered smoked Athkatlan harpfish encrusted in peppercorns and sweet treebark syrup. And lastly, venison, roasted with honey and ginger.

The menu's last kissess were berries in sugar and sweet liqour-poached pears.
