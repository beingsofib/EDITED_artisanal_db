The Faiths Ward was the foremost religious district of Calimport and one of the city's eight Outer Wards.

Description

One of the notable aspects of Faiths Ward was its buildings and architecture. More specifically, that most structures in the ward were still standing after more than a thousand years, despite living through the same sundry turmoil as the rest of the turbulent city—blazing fires, plagues, street battles between guilds, and street fights between criminals.

Many of the inns and taverns of the ward cater to the worshipers of specific faiths. This was often denoted by marking the requisite god's symbol upon the sign above a business's entrance.

Government

In the 14th century DR, the ruler of the Faiths Ward was Sultan Uarel yn Petal el Waukeen. As his name implied, the man was a member of the Church of Waukeen, as well as Pasha of the Guild of Money Lenders, Coinsmiths, and Pawn Brokers. His duties as Sultan rarely ever interfered with his ecclesiastical duties.

Sabbans

The Faiths Ward was divided into three sabbans and twelve drudachs.

Bakkal Sabban

Formerly known as "Joadhruz Sabban" and alternatively known by the moniker "High-Priests' Sabban", it was named for the bakkals, the ancient priest-rulers of Coramshan and later Calimshan during its First Age. Marked by a mixture of ancient and newer temples, it was most notable for the multitude of statues that dotted its streets and rooftops.

The sabban mark of Bakkal was that of a general face, topped with the sort of crown that the ancient bakkals wore, that of a coiled serpent. The sabban was divided into four drudachs—Ayal Drudach, Daid Drudach, Kariq Drudach, and the Masal Drudach.

Notable Locations

The Basilica of Night: A magnificent temple dedicated to Shar. Once the seat of her power in Calimport, it was destroyed in -373 DR by the Sharrans themselves after it was taken over by Talontars.

The First Trader's Drink: A small tavern that was a popular meeting place among the Waukeenar clergy.
Location: Kariq Drudach

The Fountain of the Qysars: An open-air, octagonal palisade and fountain, covered by a glassteel dome. It was constructed in 194 DR, during the time of the Shoon Imperium. By 1370 DR, it was one of the few remaining intact structures from that regime. Fresh water could be drawn from the well, though attendant guards ensured that no one withdrew more than a cupful. A local tradition encouraged people to take vows and make alliances at the fountain, with both parties submerging their hands beneath its waters. According to legend, vows made in this fashion were bound by a curse created by the Shoon, one which would strike dead any who betrayed the vow made.
Location: Kariq Drudach

The Imperious Flame: A temple dedicated to Kossuth.
Location: Ayal Drudach

The Measure House: A luxurious temple dedicated to Anachtyr.
Location: Masal Drudach

Minarets Guardant: A temple dedicated to Helm.
Location: Daid Drudach

Erehnir Sabban

Also known as "The Statue Sabban", due to the many carved or molded statues along its inner-facing brick walls. Most of these statues were of bakkals, posed in stoic positions, though a few were armed with weapons against opponents. By 1370 DR, much of the finer details on these statues had been worn away by the passage of time, though their rough outlines could all be made out.

Based on its name and wall of statuary, many assumed the Erehnir Sabban was constructed during the Erehnir Dynasty. In actuality, the wall was erected in honor of the Erehnirs following The Deepblaze of 911 DR, when it was the only sabban left untouched by the flames.

The Erehnir Sabban uniquely had no sabban mark, for it was felt by government officials that its inner-facing walls were so distinctive that it was impossible to confuse with any other.

Evyrtaan Sabban

Otherwise known by the monikers "Dark Gods Sabban" and "Fallen Grace Sabban", for it had long been host to the greatest concentration of temples to evil deities in Calimport, the sabban's official name was likewise chosen for its negative connotations. acceded independence to the land of Tethyr.

Evyrtaan Sabban had a reputation as the most dangerous of the Faiths Ward's three sabbans and was considered by many to be unholy ground. However, despite this reputation, the sabban was host to a great number of businesses and drew in large swathes of coinage.

The sabban mark of Evyrtaan depicted the head of a hawk with a coin in its mouth. It was often abstracted in crude mosaics as something resembling concentric C's around a small gold dot.

Notable Locations

Aurensahldah: A luxurious two-building temple complex, dedicated to the goddesses Lliira and Waukeen. The larger and older of the two temples by more than 300 years was the one dedicated to Waukeen. The smaller temple was dedicated to Lliira, built sometime after the announcement of by a Lliiran prophet that Waukeen was dead
Location: Noal Drudach

The House Khateera: Once a temple complex dedicated to Bane, following that god's destruction during the Time of Troubles it was bought out as a private residence by the merchant Maryn el Khateera. Unbenknownst to all, Maryn used his residence as a private shrine to Bane's son, Iyachtu Xvim.
Location: Ibran Drudach

The Towers of Fury: A large temple complex of four buildings, each dedicated to one of the Gods of Fury—Auril, Malar, Talos, and Umberlee. After centuries of temples to the Gods of Fury being destroyed by either divine or mortal acts, Syl-Pasha Saban el Djenispool had this complex constructed in 1356 DR.
Location: Samilar Drudach

History

Prior to Syl-Pasha Ralan el Pesarkhal's establishment of wards as the city's largest sectors in the , Faiths Ward was but part of what was known as the Temple and Learning Quarter.

In the summer of 320 DR, the Evyrtaan and Cajaan Sabbans become affected by the Infernal Death. The combusting bodies of the deceased cause a blaze that became known as the Plague Fires. Both sabbans ended up being temporarily gutted and abandoned.

In the , the Church of Lathander lead a zealous campaign against the churches of evil gods in the Bakkal Sabban. The arson they would commit would become known as the Zealot Fires. Over half of Bakkal Sabban would be engulfed in flames, with temples dedicated to Ibrandul, Shar, and Sharess being destroyed in the process.

Following the destruction of Bane in the , pandemonium swept amongst the orthodox Banites, the Cyricists, and the Maskarrans. The havoc in their wake would lead to the deaths of more than two hundred people and the destruction of several buildings in the Evyrtaan Sabban.
