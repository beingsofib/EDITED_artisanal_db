Serôs (SAY-ros) was the name of the undersea realms of the Sea of Fallen Stars,

Etymology

The word Serôs in the merfolk language translated into the embracing life.

Geography

Surface-worlders organized the undersea lands of Serôs into six major depth zones. Each of these were distinct from one another in terms of the flora & fauna that lived there, the ambient temperature, the pressure of the ocean depths, and the amount of sunlight that penetrated through the water. Beyond the depths of , surface dwellers were unable to see without some external assistance or light source, nor could their bodies survive the increasing pressure of the depths beyond without outside assistance, like pressure resistance.

Shallows (Surface–): The uppermost depth zone. As the depth most exposed to Toril's sun, its waters had the highest abundance of life, accounting for more than half of all coral and plants in Serôs. The vibrant array of colors from all of this flora was unimaginable by most surface-dwellers and the dwellers of the deeper depths. However, it was not uncommon for the sunlight to be obscured by passing ships or local flora and fauna, such as sargasso weeds, schools of fish, and clouds of plankton. Other fauna found in this layer included many air-breathing mammals, such as dolphins, otters, seals, and smaller varieties of whale. Additionally, one could find the Inner Sea's 1% of brackish mixtures of fresh and saltwater at this depth, near the coasts and at inlets where inland rivers drained into the sea.

Gloom (151 300 ft): The second depth zone, so named for being dimly lit by surface-dweller standards. At this level shadows were nonexistant due to light dispersion through the waters above. In terms of flora, it was at this depth where most of the great forests of hydrozoan and kelp were to be found. And most egg-bearing sea creatures made their hatcheries here. Being the boundary layer between the sunlight Shallows and the darker depths, this layer was also the sight of most of the Serôs Wars and other conflicts between the undersea races. Lastly, this layer was notably where most shipwrecks came to settle beneath the waves.

Twilight (301 600 ft): At the third depth zone, though some light filtered down from above, the waters became pitch black to most surface folk and took on a chilly temperature. The darkness and cloudiness of this layer was due in part to "marine snow" – the detritus and debris that floated down from creatures and consumed plants of the upper zones. At this depth the amount of began to drop off and the roots of hardier animals, such as corals and hydrozoans, began to emerge. The Twilight depth was also subject to undersea currents, generated by a combination of wind on the surface and fluctuating temperature shifts of the depth zones between it.

Upper Depths (601 1200 ft): At the fourth depth zone the waters of Serôs became as dark as a moonless night. Only the hardiest of species of coral and sponges survived at this level, as well as lucent coral that could be found near civilized settlements, providing a bioluminescent glow to the darkness. In terms of fauna, one could find deep-water fish, aquatic scavenger species, and wild herds of hippocampi and giant sea horses.

Lower Depths (1201 2400 ft): The seafloor of Serôs. Much like the world above, this depth zone was full of plains, hills, seamounts, dormant and active volcanoes, and oceanic ridges riddled with canyons and valleys. These deepsea canyons and plains were predominantly taken up by cephalopods, like octopi and squids, jellyfishes, huge sea hydras, and several giant versions of other undersea life, such as giant squids or giant eels that could grow as large as long. Additionally, many creatures at this depth and beyond displayed bioluminescence. It was not uncommon at this depth to find sealife around the hydrothermal vents of the seafloor, which cycled water near volcanic magma. They superheated the water around them, causing intense currents in their vicinity, as well as inundate the water with minerals. For some, prolonged exposure to these mineral-rich waters could cause sickness.

Underdeep: ( and beyond): The lowest of the depths zones, its frigid waters were relegated to three deepsea trenches that laid at the seafloor, dropping off into unfathomable depths. These were the Bymmal Trench, the Pythan Trench, and the Trench of Lopok. The generic term used for this zone was coined by Netherese explorers in reference to the Underdark. Information on this depth zone was scarce, relegated to the realm of legends and rumors, as few races other than tritons and great whales could survive at this depth. Those who did inhabit the underdeep were massive monstrous creatures, and aquatic dragons, like dragon turtles.

East of the Hmur Plateau, resided a portal called the Wildtide Gate beginning in .

Flora & Fauna

While coral on most worlds were typically limited in their capacity for growth to a fragile ecosystem of warm, sunlit waters, the over 500 breeds of coral indigenous to Serôs were far more hardy and even capable of thriving in cold-water. They could be found all across the depths, but were most prevalent in the Alamber Sea. They grew for hundreds of miles and beyond, creating moderately sized reefs as the coral died or moved on.

Other largely immobile, hardy animal life-forms, that could be found in many varieties across Serôs included hydrozoans The former were often mistaken for plant life, referred to by some as "aquatic ferns" or as the "trees" of Serôs. There were many varieties of them that grew across the continental margins, forming so called "forests", such as Duna's Forest in eastern Serôs.

The Inner Sea was home to a variety of smaller sea life that fed many of the creatures of Serôs, such as plankton, krill, various types of fish, scallops, urchins, sea snakes, eels, and bottom-feeding crustaceans in the shallows zone. The latter three were considered by some aquatic sentients to be delicacies.

Many larger creatures in the depths were utilized as beasts of burden or draft animals, most often docile pilot whales, narwhals, and sea turtles. Among the sahuagin, sharks were used for this purpose. And while giant crustaceans were considered by some to be food, they were more often than not viewed as being more valuable as draft animals. When it came to mounts, hippocampi and giant sea horses were the standard choices.

Besides pilot whales, over nine distinct breeds of these massive mammals plied the waters of Serôs. They could be broadly divided into two diet-based categories, the baleen and toothed. The baleen included the pilot whales, humpback whales, night whales, sapphire whales, and snow whales. Rather peaceful and more intelligent than their toothed kin, these whales strained seawater through their mouths to feed upon the miscroscopic plankton and krill. The more aggressive toothed whales subsisted upon the other large species of the deep, such as krakens and giant squids. They included beaked whales, narwhals, killer whales, the truly massive sperm whales, and potentially one of the legendary leviathans.

The depths were also home to a number of pearl-bearing creatures, such as giant clams & oysters, standard-sized clams & oysters, and cultivated in farming community "pearl beds", though some also found use for giant clams shells in the building of housing.

Inhabitants

The undersea lands of Serôs were host to a wide range of aquatic races, divided amongst its sixth depth zones.

Shallows: This uppermost zone was the primary home of by far the most sentient races, with most spending 75% of their days at this depth. This included the likes of aquatic elves, crabmen, ixitxachitl, locathah, seawolves, selkies, sirines, One could also occasionally find sea hags, though they lived solitary lives, lairing within the caves of cliffs and slopes that stood above or near settlements. Tako later found a home amongst the Shallows, having traveled from Kara-Tur through a portal the Shou Lung empire had created to enable trade with the nations of the Inner Sea - up until something had disrupted the gate and left the tako stranded.

Gloom: This depth was home to merfolk civilizations, the tribal societies of the koalinth and merrow, and the occasional sea hag.

Twilight: Most surface-folk believed that no sentient races lived at this depth, but in reality the twilight zone was home to scrags, predominantly in the eastern and south-western reaches of Serôs. Following the Twelfth Serôs War, one could also encounter sahuagin.

Upper Depths: This depth was home to the civilizations of the morkoths and shalarins, who shared its territory in detente. The latter were the larger of the two, with the shalarin having managed to grow their population by means of agricultural techniques that allowed for tremendous kelp cultivation within certain regions.

Lower Depths: Tritons were the only civilized life that inhabited the fifth lowest depth zone, though other sentient creatures at this depth included eyes of the deep and kuo-toans.

Other races to be found in Serôs included anguiliians, storm giants in the Mountains of Volar, kelpies in the coastal shallows, reef giants, and a small population of vodyanoi, who tunneled through Hmur Plateau. many aquatic denizens of the Outer Planes found their way to the undersea realms on more than one occasion, as did water weirds and other creatures of elemental water.

In a move that set the Serôsians apart from surface-worlders, dolphins and whales were viewed as intelligent equals, even capable of serving within the clergies of undersea faiths. To kill and eat whales was considered by most to be a horrific offense. Only a select few evil races, such as the ixitxachitl and sahuagin, would savagely attack whale herds. And unlike the horses of the surface, the rather intelligent hippocampi would often expect to be treated as allies and friends, rather than mindless steeds.

Biological Differences

Much of the Serôsian populace had varying degrees of optical light-sensitivity, which influenced what times of day they could comfortably make their way up to the surface without being blinded by the sun. Among the undersea races, only the ixitxachitl, merfolk, and sea elves could easily bear bright sunlight. The next degree down were those who could only handle sunlight visible from dusk to godswake – koalinth, merrow, shalarin, and tritons. The third degree of sensitivity were those who could only handle nighttime, though even then the light of a full moon was uncomfortable to their eyes. This group included the morkoths, sahuagins, and scrags.

Another dividing point physiologically between the undersea races was the capacity to breath brackish waters. The vast majority of aquatic sentients could only breath salt water, which made up about 99% of the total volume of the Inner Sea. Exposure would cause them to feel unsteady and light-headed or sluggishness. Continued exposure would lead to extreme nausea and for weaker creature's potentially death. The only races that could experience water of differing salinity without suffering any ill effects were the aquatic elves, koalinth, and scrags.

Notable Races

Aquatic elves: Of all the aquatic races of Serôs, it was the sea elves who achieved a level of impact upon the depths that was unsurpassed by others. creating several kingdoms that united together into the Aryselmalyr Empire in . Through their empire they dominated all aspects of life, history, culture, and growth for all of the races of Serôs for over 10,763 years, leaving marks upon the other aquatic races that continued to last well into the Present Age. Among its long lasting contributions to Serôsian society was teaching others how to domesticate coral, kelp, and pearls.

Ixitxachitl: An evil, arrogant, and paranoid race of creatures that resembled manta rays, albeit with toothy maws. For much of their history the ixitxachitl were at conflict with the benevolent races of Serôs, Prior to the Fourth Epoch, they were demon worshipers with societies lead by militant rulers. This all changed with the advent of the Prophet Ehjax, who in founded the theocratic Xedran Reefs, what would become Serôs's longest-lived 'chitl civilization. They embraced this god of revenge, hopeful to regain lost power, and would begin to pattern their daily lives upon the teachings of the Ilxendren faith. Vampiric ixitxachitl made up the upper echelons of this society.

Koalinth: In Serôs's past they were once a more advanced race, akin to the locathah in the 14 century DR. But these aquatic goblinoids were forced into an interminable barbaric way of life by Aryselmalyr, who visited genocide upon them. They lived in tribal societies in caverns, cliffs, seamounts far away from the civilized aquatic races.

Kopru: a degenerated race of monstrous humanoids that were expelled from their homes in Chult. So horrid were they, that even the sahuagin rallied alongside the other races of Serôs to eradicate them.

Kuo-toa: A debased race of amphibious fish-men whose history was shrouded in mystery. In the early centuries of Aryselmalyr they were common in the Inner Sea, counted among its big powers. But then in the Second Serôs War their kingdom was destroyed by the elven empire, who visited genocide upon them and made them virtually extinct in Serôs. The survivors fled into the deepest caverns and trenches of the Altumbel peninsula, escaping into the Underdark. Over the next fifteen millenia, well into the 14 century DR, sightings of kuo-toa in Serôs were rare and isolated. Traveling through the Tunnels of Iratis, they would arise on nights off the coast of Sembia to raid for food, valuables, and slaves. They were commonly sighted near the Cave of Lost Hope and within the Xedran Reefs. Early into the Era of Upheaval, they gained a renewed presence in Serôs, seizing power over the central kingdom of the morkoths.

Locathah: One of the most persecuted races in the history of Serôs, the locathah at one point or another had been slaves to nearly every other aquatic race, as well as some permanent residents within the Hmur Plateau and atop its slopes. And while they were not vengeful towards all of their oppressors, they held enmity towards the sahuagin for exterminating the locathah of the Alamber Sea and almost always attacked morkoth on sight.

Malenti: Mutant offshoot of sahuagin that resembled sea elves, but could breed with the mainline species to create more of their kind. In Serôs, they were far more numerous than in the Outer Sea, and were divided into two groups. The first and more predominant of the two were the "true" malenti, those who were loyal to the sahuagin and used by them as spies. The second were the "Noble Malenti", those who fled from the sahuagin to form two small colonies of their own, rejecting the savagery of their progenitors' society and faith. One clan was situated above the Bymmal Trench, whilst the other was located in the Bay of Chessenta. Both types of malenti were subject to the same blind hatred as the sea devils and, though fearful of persecution, the noble malenti were hopeful that they could form alliances and live in peace with the other races of Serôs.

Merfolk: The one race to achieve comparable impact on Serôs to the elves. after the fall of Aryselmalyr the merfolk ruled over much of Serôs through their own empire, Hmurrath, for over 619 years. Though in time it too would fall apart for similar reasons. They ruled in much the same fashion, After the fall of their empire, the oracle Selana led them not only back to their old social order, but to embrace a new mindset. From then on they diverged culturally from the merfolk of the Outer Sea, ceasing to be patriarchal, cautious, and isolationist.

Merrow: The marine ogres of Serôs faced similar oppression to the koalinths across history, not only from the elves of Aryselmalyr, but the merfolk of Hmurrath as well. Denied the chance to stand as equals among the civilized races of Serôs, about 80% of them fled to the Alamber Sea in . To the surprise of all, the merrow put up an enduring opposition against the reigning sahuagin, acting as a buffer against their attempts to expand past the Mountains of Volar. Living in clans and tribes, they could otherwise be found in coastal caves across the eastern, northwestern, and southern parts of Serôs.

Morkoth: A cruel and evil race of aquatic predators with strange chimeric morphologies, whom differed physiologically and sociologically from their counterparts in the Outer Sea. Whereas the latter were reclusive cave-dwellers, the morkoths of Serôs commonly congregated into theocracies Morkoths were widely despised by locathah, merfolk, and shalarin. During the Twelfth Serôs War, whilst the major country of morkoths in the deep allied with Iakhovas, a hidden city-state rallied alongside the other races of Serôs.

Sahuagin: A ruthless, violent, and warmongering people that garnered the enmity of all other Serôsians, who the sahuagin viewed as little more than meat. Many of the Serôs Wars, as far back as the very first, were waged against them. would sneak past the Sharkbane Wall every summer, but they were largely confined to the Alamber Sea for most of history, raiding the lands of both Serôs and the surface-world. Though they ultimately lost the war, the sahuagin had still won the freedom to spread across Serôs and aspire to establish new territories and kingships. And without the confines of the Alamber to limit their growth, many Serôsians feared that a population boom was all but inevitable.

Shalarin: A humanoid race whose physiology was quite alien to that of other Serôsians. They were foreign to the Inner Sea, yet largely unknown outside of it, having migrated from the Sea of Corynactis, a body of water across the planet in the Western Ocean. They emigrated from there in large droves on five occasions, each separated by 720 years, through the undersea Wildtide Gate.

Tritons: A mysterious race of warriors devoted to protecting Serôs and its people from great evils. They arrived to the depths from another plane of existence, the Plane of Water, sometime long before the kingdom of Hmurrath. But the tritons kept themselves hidden from the other races of Serôs until the Seventh Serôs War, when they intervened to put an end to the threat posed by the morkoths. Afterwards they would go on teach the other races some of their magic or fighting skills.

Xedran reefs: A theocratic ixitxachitl nation, composed of Six Holy Cities dedicated to the reverence of their patron god Ilxendren. Out of all the realms of Serôs, this shallow-water nation had one of the longest stretches of territory, rivaled only by Kurrimal.

Society

Amongst the more malevolent aquatic races of Serôs, raiding other communities was a common activity. This was known to be conducted by koalinth, kuo-toans, morkoth, sahuagin, seawolves, and scrags. The purposes behind raiding were varied, such as a desire for food, valuables, slaves, or as simple acts of war. In the case of morkoth and sahuagin, there were certain songs each cycle that marked the most common months for them to be engaging in raiding, these being Eldasong and Sekolhunt respectively.

Calenders

Over the course of Serôsian history, nearly every sentient race devised its own methods of measuring the passage of time. These methods were usually lax when it came to the specific passage of bells and such, instead focused on broad, activity-specific terms such as feeding, hunting, resting, etc. They also generally made no use of timekeeping devices like clocks, for it was seen as common knowledge that all life in Serôs attended to similar expected norms and patterned times for their activities.

In terms of calendars, most were solely of an oral nature, with only three prominent ones taking any written form. These were the Aryselmalyn calendar, the Calendar of Hmur, and the Timesong calendar. The first was established by the aquatic elves of Aryselmalyr, inscribed upon coral and cryscoral spindles. It fell out of use when the empire fell. The second was established by the merfolk of Hmurrath and continued to see use after that empire's fall, well into the 14 century DR. It was used by most locathah, as well as some merfolk, though the former kept it strictly oral. by the Oghmanyte Arkallus Vordahl, who drew correlatives from various different surface-world and Serôsian calendars. The priest purposely designed it with the intent of it becoming a unifying calendar system for the various undersea races, one that they could all follow.

The fourth prominent calendar of Serôs, one that had also been taken into account by Arkallus when designing the Timesong calendar, was Whaletime. A purely oral tradition, it was a method of measuring the passage of time that was wholly unique to whales.

Culture

The undersea realms lacked taverns and inns as the surface-world understood them. While one could find a room to eat and rent for the night, there was nowhere to purchase a beverage, as the concept of "drinking" was foreign to most Serôsians. In terms of architectural style, it varied quite considerably from race to race. Most cities had their buildings laid out close to one another, so as to conserve warmth and provide another layer of protection in case of an attack or inclement weather.

Serôsians generally eschewed armor, as the crucial elements of combat beneath the waves were maneuverability, mobility, and speed. The only forms of armor to be found in the undersea lands were pearl and silverweave. Both of these were the work of shalarin and triton craftsmen, though the latter was only worn during times of war.

Clothing too was generally absent among Serôsians, save for diaphanous silken weaves among the courts of the shalarin and sea elves, where they served as indicators of rank or positions. They were also once common among the nobility of Aryselmalyr, possibly a remnant of when the elves lived on the surface. The Serôsian indifference towards clothing was due to the fact that few fabrics existed that could provide any sense of protection or warmth within the water, the two primary purposes of clothing in the surface-world.

The most one could find adorning the body of a Serôsian were accessories such as jewelry, belts, harnesses, and bandoliers. These item-holding garments were typically made from woven kelp fronds that had been treated for durability or the treated hide of an eel or sea snake. anklets, occasionally even amulets, and other forms of accessory that would produce as little drag as possible whilst swimming. Most rings were typically carved from blaze or lucent coral, almost never metal, and inset with a pearl.

In terms of weaponry, Serôsians relied upon those that were best suited for aquatic combat. This often limited their selection to weapons that used a thrusting motion, such as daggers and knives, cutlasses, gaffs, harpoons, military forks, javelins, short swords, and most predominant of all tridents. Nearly all of the aquatic race had some slight variation on this weapon. Other races, namely locathah and sahuagin, also made offensive use of nets. But by far the most sought-after weapon among Serôsians was the tapal, an ancient weapon unique to the tritons.

When it came to materials, most weaponry in Serôs was forged of either bone, coral, stone, or specially treated materials, such as fire-hardened cryscoral. For example, the shalarin crafted light crossbows from coral and kelp, which unlike the wooden crossbows of the surface did not deteriorate underwater. Metal was usually not an ideal material, for it was subject to rusting and , but this could be overcome by enchantments. For spellcasters, shells typically served the role as spell receptacles that wands and staves did on the surface-world. Tritons preferred the use of shells from clams, nautiluses, and sea turtles. Shalarins introduced to Serôs the use of hydrozoan branches coated in pearls. But the most rarest spell receptacle of all, capable of storing great magic, was that of a narwhal's horn.

Just as aquatic wizards had to adapt to a scarcity of wood, Serôsian mages found ways to adapt potions and oils to life in a fluid medium. They developed viscous substances, such as a thick gelatinous paste of kelp, that could then be either ingested or applied to the skin. these magical substances were carried around in either clam shells or flexible tubes made from eelskins. Spell scrolls took the form of chemically treated sharkskin, cut into sheets, and written with inks derived from mixtures of magical kelp and coral. Once activated, this special ink would cause the scroll to be consumed, leaving nothing but a desiccated sharkskin.

The sharkskin method was used by some to create spellbooks very akin to those on the surface, though the shalarin magically treated their pages with a coating of pearl in order to enhance their durability. Other undersea races took more unique approaches, carving them into stone. Tritons carved their spells into isolated cavern walls in an esoteric script, whilst morkoths carved spells into stone spindles that they could read tactilely with their tentacles.

Languages

Whereas the lands and races of Faerûn were unified by the trade language of Commonspeech, the people of Serôs had no true unifying language, but three cross-cultural tongues allowed most Serôsians to communicate with one another. The Common Tongue was one of these three, with about 12% of aquatic people being versed in it by 1371 DR. In cosmopolitan areas, where increasing interaction was being had with surface-world adventurers and merchants, many more were learning how to speak it. In and around the city of Myth Nantar for example, about 25% of people knew Common. Most learned this tongue from aquatic elves.

Among the aquatic races of the Shallows, a pidgin language known as Serusan was used to bridge communication, Serusan was an amalgamation of speech patterns from a multitude of racial languages. Surface dwellers could find teachers of Serusan in Myth Nantar.

The third cross-cultural language was that of Currentspeak. It was the most incomprehensible to surface-worlders, as it relied upon the fluctuation of water across gills, flukes, and other membranes. It was commonly used by the aquatic races to speak with other creatures that couldn’t communicate using the aforementioned languages, such as whales.

Religion

The people of Serôs had a more limited range of deities that they worshiped, in comparison to the over a hundred gods revered across the surface races of Faerûn, though not all of these undersea gods were known to chroniclers and surface-worlders. How some of the known deities of the surface-world came to be revered in Serôs had no singular origin point. Some became known through contract or trade with the surface, others through the rescued faithful lost at sea spreading word of their faith, and still others were speculated to be so pervasive that knowledge of them precluded any sort of surface contact, like Mystra.

Among the Faerûnian pantheon, the gods that primarily saw reverence included Eldath, Milil, Mystra, Oghma, the most prominent in Serôs were Deep Sashelas, Eadro, Ishtishia, Persana, and Trishina. Lastly, among the known monstrous deities of the Realms, one could find reverence of Blibdoolpoolp by kuo-toan raiders from the Underdark and in the past by some morkoths, Ilxendren by the ixitxachitl, and Sekolah by the sahuagin.

Of all these gods, none had more temples throughout Serôs than Deep Sashelas, whom the sea elves believed created the undersea realms. By contrast, the ancient holy sites that once existed for Blibdoolpoolp were systematically eradicated from the depths, on a level not experienced by other deities, following the fall of the morkoth Theocracy.

Out of the surface powers of the Faerûnian pantheon, the one with the oldest presence was Mystra, whose worship beneath the waves stretched back to the time of Netheril. She most acted as a sort of bridge between surface-worlders and Serôsians. And Umberlee, while considered the ruler of the waves on the surface-world, was considered to only have dominion in the shallows. Those beneath the shallow zone viewed Eldath as the true goddess of the sea, working in concert with Deep Sashelas and Trishina, as well as Ishtishia to a loose extent.

One of the most mysterious groups of worship in the deep were Those Who Sleep Below, malevolent entities that dwelled in the sunless reaches of the Trench of Lopok. They were worshiped by the likes of kuo-toa, morkoths, and even krakens. Unbenknownst to the mortals, these entities were obyrith lords, with Dagon as the eldest among them. Dagon also has his own cult lurking amongst the shalarin communities of the deep, known as the Cult of Corynactis.

Relations

Generally speaking, the people of Serôs and the surface-world seldom interacted with one another. much of the surface nations around the Inner Sea became more acutely aware of their undersea neighbors. However, they surface-worlders remained woefully ignorant in many ways. Some, especially those in Sembia, feared that its aquatic races were new menaces to fear. Others felt it inconvenient having to acknowledge the sovereign territories thay lay beneath the waves their trade routes plied. And very few regarded the undersea kingdoms of Serôs as rightful equals to those of the surface.

Law & Order

Over a span of eight thousand years, from keeping dangerous artifacts like the Emerald Eye of Kyron in custody, and protecting bystanders and innocents in the midst of wars.

In their last century of public activity, the Dukars established the Laws of Battle alongside a committee of merfolk and Shalarin in , following the end of the ghastly Ninth Serôs War. due to some internal strife.

This pact, followed by nearly all of the civilized races of Serôs, was a shared code of conduct for waging war against one another. Serôsians almost never willingly forsook these conventions, save for the likes of the savage koalinth, merrow, scrags, and sea hags. Even the sahuagin honored the Laws of Battle, for they were based heavily upon their culture, though this detail was long forgotten by many in Serôs by the 14 century DR. Due to these conventions wars in Serôs tended to last no less than three months, composed of of intermittent battles with numerous lull. This pattern of fighting was to be adhered to regardless of a conflict's severity.

The Twelfth Serôs War would see the establishment of an alliance between the benevolent races of Serôs, including the likes of sea elves, shalarin, merfolk, morkoth, and the long thought lost Dukars. Named the Nantarn Alliance, for its formation and headquartering in the city of Myth Nantar, it continued to persist beyond the war, albeit in a tenous state. Members were hopeful of establishing defensive pacts in light of the looming sahuagin threat, of learning more from the surface-worlders, and interested in profiting from trade with them. Councilors rabidly discussed the need to put their ideals and hopes to writing, tying the alliance to constitutions, lest in time it all be muddled or forgotten. There were even talks to turn Myth Nantar into the center of a new union of sorts, much like Silverymoon was to the Silver Marches.

The returned Dukars situated themselves as the sword & shield of the Nantarn Alliance, to one day become the peace officers of not just Myth Nantar, but the allied waters the council represented. However, being mere shadows of their former glory, the Dukars chose to work in secrecy as they rebuilt their strength, accepting new members into their ranks from various races, even immigrants from the surface.

Economy

Whereas most of the surface-world used coins as currency, the people of Serôs lacked a standardized currency or monetary system. In most places trade was conducted through barter, with common barter goods including deepwater lucent coral, fish varieties, weapons, works of art, and bales of kelp.

Among the races of the shallows zone, pearls were commonly used as a form of currency, with the value applied to an individual pearl being denoted by its color. The proliferation of pearls as currency was promoted by merfolk and shalarin, who with their knowledge of how to culture pearls hoped to ease trade between Serosians and surface-worlders. However, by the late 14 century DR, this practice still had yet to be adopted by the likes of ixitxachitls, morkoths, and tritons. To them they held no greater value than as ornamentation.

Over time, some began to recognize that people on the surface valued their pearls at a greater rate than the Serôsians themselves attributed to them. These differences were referred to by some as the "wet" and "dry values.

As far back as the Arresylmar Empire, one could find the people of Serôs engaging in trade with the surface world, when the sea elves had open relations with the elves of Cormanthyr. However, a new wave of interest in commerce with the surface was ushered in following the Twelfth Serôs War. In the month of Huntsong, , limited trade began between the sea elf kingdoms and the port cities of Selgaunt and Suzail. This was followed in same year, in the month Eleasis, with Suzail's parent kingdom of Cormyr opening up direct trade with the undersea realms of Eeadral, Myth Nantar, and Naramyr. The Cormyrians traded sculptures and pickled fruits, whilst the Serôsians traded gold bars, raw coral, and various items long lost to the sea floor.

Some on the surface who had long made their riches on harvesting things from the deep, such as sponge-divers, feared competition with the Serôsians. Some sponge-divers resorted to spreading tall tales about the aquatic sentients in order to sully their reputation, telling of attacks on divers and child abductions.

History

The historians of Serôs divided the long history of the undersea realms into five distinct epochs. The First Epoch, also known as the Time of Beginnings, stretched far back into ages shrouded in fable and myth. None could agree upon a starting date for the epoch, though all historians seemed to judge it as having ended in , with the migrations of the sea elves into Serôs and their rise to power.

The first of the elven migrations occured around -16900 DR, during the War of Three Leaves. Many fled from the kingdoms of Eiellur and Syorpiir, settling below in the Selmal Basin.

The Second Epoch, also known as the Time of Elves, with the building of Aryselmalyr. This epoch of Serôs was also marked by several significant events, including the creation of the Dukars, the first extended intrusion by surface-dwellers into the undersea realms, the first wave of immigration from the shalarin, the development of Serusan, and the first six Serôs Wars. The first of which was instigated by Aryselmalyr's building of the Sharksbane Wall and culminated in the elven empire herding the sahuagin behind it.

In , part of what became known as the Golden Age of Netheril, Coronal Essyl Merynth, more than half the mages of Serôs, and a full contingent of Dukars bandied together to decisively drive out the Netherese. This later became known as the Battle of the Three Seaflames. Through their combined magics the domed cities were not only exploded, but their explosive force and resulting shock waves were held back, leaving them no place to go but back towards the coastline. The undersea lands of Serôs were left unmarred, whilst the coast gave way to Saerloon Bay and Selgaunt Bay.

In , the Wildtide Gate opened for the first time, ushering in the First Passing of the shalarin into Serôs. Unfortunately, the newcomers were quickly attacked by the mad kings Essyl and Kyron with the Emerald Eye,

On the surface in the , the green elves of Nikerymath harnessed the power of elven High Magic to call forth a cataclysmic tidal wave from the Jhaamdath Bay, instantly destroying all of the Twelve Cities of the Sword that sought to annihilate them. Unbeknownst to them, at the mouth of the inlet was Coryselmal, the capital of Aryselmalyr, and their wave would cause it too to be destroyed. Several small undersea villages and farms would also be destroyed, the victims of debris picked up and dragged back by the crashing wave. Over 40,000 sea elves perished, along with several other aquatic sentients. For the next forty years the people of Serôs would be at each others' throats, vying for territory long denied to them and to eradicate the elves, if possible. This conflict later became known as the Sixth Serôs War.

With the end of this war came the end of the Second Epoch, This was followed by the Third Epoch, the Time of Merfolk, which was marked by the rise of the merfolk empire of Hmurrath.

The Fourth Epoch, also known as the Time of Tempering, was marked by all the oracles and priests of every aquatic race in Serôs receiving a vision that three great challenges were to come and "temper the mettle" of the Serôsians, forever changing the currents of life in the undersea realms. while the people of Serôs waited in dread anticipation of the "Forgers of Destiny", as the merfolk referred to them, the various undersea realms stagnated in a state of detente. For much of the period there was lack of any serious complications with trade, few looming threats to unify the people, and what threats did exist seemed under control by the "more civilized races".

The Fifth Epoch, the epoch of the Present Age, was ushered in 1371 DR. By that time, many Serôsians fell to a similar consensus on what exactly the prophesied "Forgers of Destiny" were, though some continued to debate the topic. The first event was believed to be the emergence of the tritons from the hidden depths when the deities Anhur, Sekolah, and Umberlee appeared in the Inner Sea in their mortal avatar forms. And the third was Iakhovas, the great instigator and warmonger of the Twelfth Serôs War, and caused the shattering of the long-standing Sharksbane Wall.

When the Spellplague struck Toril in 1385 DR, great rifts opened up in the Underdark beneath the Inner Sea. This caused some of its water to drain down into the Glimmersea, leaving behind a large pit called the Underchasm. By the , the waters of Serôs had stabilized, with the average drop in water level measured to be by nearly . This left the upper of the Sharksbane Wall to tower over the surface waters. In the years between the Spellplague and the Second Sundering, control of the depths was largely split between the sahuagin in the east and the sea elves of Myth Nantar in the west.
