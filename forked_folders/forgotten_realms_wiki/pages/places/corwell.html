Corwell was a Ffolk kingdom on the isle of Gwynneth within the Moonshae Isles. While not the most glamorous of the Ffolk kingdoms, it was the oldest and was sometimes called the Heartland of the Ffolk.

Geography

Corwell was located in southern Gwynneth. Its northern borders were marked by the Highlands and Cambro Mountains, both of which formed a rocky boundary between the northern half of the island. Much of Corwell's south was covered by the Llyrath Forest. The eastern arm of Corwell included the land between the Cambro Mountains and the Strait of Alaron from Kingsbay up to the settlement of Thorndyke, while in the west it included the whole of the southwestern peninsula as well as the northern coast of Corwell Firth up into the Meirig Peatlands and the Shrouded Lands. Some maps further indicated Corwell's borders extending as far north as Aspenheight. The long Corwell Road spanned much of the realm from east to west.

Government

The kingdom was ruled from the town of Caer Corwell by House Kendrick until its fall at the end of 14 century DR. In practice, the king wielded little direct control over the kingdom, instead deferring to the lords of the major settlements (known as cantrevs) to self-govern. As Corwell was only sparsely populated, the cantrev lords' domains were located too far apart to warrant any major squabbling.

Trade

Corwell's location and rugged coastline meant that it received few visitors, and relatively few merchant vessels from outside the Moonshaes would call at its ports each year. The primary ports were at Caer Corwell on the west coast and Kingsbay on the east.

Corwell boasted major agricultural centers along the southern coast of Corwell Firth in the west and along the eastern coast. Mining was a major production in the Highlands and especially in the Cambro Mountains. Major fishing grounds were present at the mouth of Corwell Firth and in the Kingsbay and Strait of Alaron.

Culture

Because Corwell received few visitors prior to the mid—14 century DR, it was known as a land where Ffolk culture was much more pure than in places like Callidyrr.

Defenses

Corwell was known for its elite swordsmen. Each cantrev could muster a militia of 40-100 fighters, about 1-in-5 of whom were trained as archers with the longbow while the remainder were largely equipped with swords. Military leaders, including the militia sergeants and the cantrev lords, were all skilled warriors in their own right.

The most notable threats were outlaws out of the Llyrath Forest and firbolgs from the Highlands.

Religion

Corwell, and Gwynneth more broadly, lay at the heart of the Ffolk's worship of the Earthmother. This meant that the Ffolk rarely traveled into the sacred Myrloch Vale just north of the kingdom, and did not aspire to harvest any of its resources. As of the mid–14 century DR, this also meant that the gods of the Faerûnian pantheon had failed to gain any foothold in Corwell.

History

Early History

Corwell was the first and oldest kingdom of the Ffolk within the Moonshaes Isles. It was founded in the , by the first Ffolk king, Deric, shortly after the initial settlement of the island by Talfiric refugees from Faerûn at the site which would come to be known as Caer Corwell. After fighting to unite the various cantrevs of these early Ffolk, Deric managed a meager peace with nearby Synnoria but ultimately died without an heir in the , sparking a Ffolk civil war. This ended when Callidyrr Hugh united the kingdom once again as its new king in the , and founded the Hugh Dynasty of Corwell. His son, Cymrych Hugh, would become a legendary Ffolk hero after slaying Kazgaroth, and later became the first High King of the Moonshaes. He moved his capital to Caer Callidyrr, and left Corwell in the hands of a regent, Christopher Riker. Some attributed the true founding of modern Corwell directly to Cymrych Hugh.

Christopher Riker would pass the regency of Corwell to his son, Cedric, after which rule would pass to a branch of the Cymrych family. While Cymrych rulers reigned in both Corwell and Callidyrr, the families soon diverged, with the former being the ancestors of the Kendrick family and the latter being the ancestors of the Carrathal family. In the , the famously cruel Prince Ketheryll Cymrych, heir to the throne of the High King, accused Corwell's King Durnhal of conspiring against his father, and put both him and his queen to death in the Castle of Skulls.

Modern History

Corwell would continue to battle the Northlanders. During the reign of King Bryon Kendrick in the early-to-mid 14 century DR, the warrior-king would help to repel a Northmen invasion of neighboring Moray. A two-decade period of peace then followed the birth of King Bryon's son, Tristan Kendrick, in the .

The kingdom would be thrown into chaos during the events of the Darkwalker War in the , when Kazgoroth returned and led an assault on the capital known as the Battle of Caer Corwell. However, Kazgoroth was defeated by the king's son, Tristan Kendrick, who acquired the Sword of Cymrych Hugh and would go on to become the first true High King of the Moonshaes in centuries, founding the Kendrick Dynasty. The kingdom weathered yet another invasion the following year when forces of the god Bhaal launched the Second Battle of Caer Corwell, which was again thwarted by King Tristan.

After peace was forged between the Ffolk and the Northlanders by Tristan and his daughter, High Queen Alicia Kendrick, the greatest threat to Corwell in the mid-to-late 14 century DR were Nelanther pirates who made occasional landings along the coast.

With the return of Karador and the rise of the fey realm of Sarifal in the , House Kendrick was unable to stem the tide of the encroaching fey into Corwell. After the United Moonshae Isles came into conflict with Amn over the island of Snowdown in the , Amnian mercenaries subsequently put Corwell to the torch, creating the opportunity for High Lady Ordalf of Sarifal to declare that all humans were to be expelled from Gwynneth in the . The fey subsequently laid siege to Caer Corwell and enslaved the humans who were not killed or who did not escape the island.

Over the following decades, the enslaved humans of Corwell were bred for docility by the ruling leShay of Sarifal, and any technological or cultural advancement among the humans was ground to a halt. Eventually, a human resistance managed to gain a foothold, notably a group known as the Winterglen Claw However, Corwell itself lay as little more than a string of ruins being rapidly reclaimed by the wild nature of Sarifal.

Inhabitants

The realm had long been home to Ffolk humans and their halfling neighbors, as well as outlaws and pirates who made their bases along the kingdom's southern coast. By the late 15 century DR, these groups were largely absent, and the land once known as Corwell was home to fey and to wild moorhounds and owlbears. Notably, quicklings had come to make their homes in the former halfling burrows.
