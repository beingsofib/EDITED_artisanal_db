Emerald Ward was one of the Outer Wards of the city of Calimport, the capital of Calimshan. A newer, tidier, and grander ward compared to others, following devastating fires, it became the abode of rising merchants.

Location

Lying on the northern side of the city, Emerald Ward was sandwiched between the Trades Ward on the west and the Khanduq Ward on the east, with the Grand Ward to the south.

Description

As its name implied, Emerald Ward was one of the first sights travelers had of the riches and luxuriousness of Calimport as they journeyed down the Trade Way from the north. Standing in stark contrast to the dirt and desperation of parts of the neighboring Trade Ward and other areas of Calimport, Emerald Ward of around 1370 DR was cleaner, more recently built, and more ostentatious, thanks to the rebuilding effort after the fires devastated this area of the city only a few years before. The grand buildings here sported a dazzling profusion of bright-hued paints and tiles. The old bazaars also expanded into the cleared areas, becoming more spacious marketplaces like those seen in northern cities.

As such, it was the residence of many of the up-and-coming merchants of Calimport's Outer Wards and an Emerald Ward address marked this to the rest of the city. Such folk were 'new money' and the uppermost of the lesser classes—if not the peak, then near to it. They competed in social matters and in building taller villas and businesses.

History

The area was formerly a part of Calimport's Market Quarter, before Syl-Pasha Ralan el Pesarkhal established the system of wards in the .

However, in the , the Trades Conflagration blazed across northern and central Calimport. The cause was unknown but rumored about: some attributed the fires to smokepowder shipments being transported through the overcrowded bazaars of Marekh Sabban, and others blamed Mystrans and Cyricists for fighting in the streets. In any case, it was concentrated in that sabban and it wiped out the old bazaars, row houses, and khanduqs. By the time the fires were out, it had burned 60% of the Emerald Ward and razed the entire Marekh Sabban, as well as countless areas of the Muzad undercity. The temples of Cyric, Mystra, and Sune in Marekh were destroyed and many who lived there had perished.

Nevertheless, work was underway on rebuilding much of Marekh Sabban by early 1370 DR. Taking advantage, landowners bought up large stretches of land in Marekh and neighboring parts once belonging to deceased residents.

Government

Circa 1370 DR, Emerald Ward was controlled by Sultan Nasim the Younger, the favorite nephew of Ralan el Pesarkhal, the ruler of Calimshan. He was loyal and considered equal in talent to the sultan of the neighboring Trades Ward, his cousin Faruk, but to prefer business to crime, though he did have a gambling habit. He did not exert much control on use of the land.

A rival to Nasim was Husuf el Halid, the spendthrift son of a wealthy moneylender and the local nirdey, or chief collector, of the Rundeen that dominated local merchants.

Sabbans

Emerald Ward was divided into three sabbans and ten drudachs.

Axash Sabban

An ever-bustling space, Axash Sabban had the largest marketplace for clothing, from the most mundane to the most extravagant, so it was often called the Cloth Sabban or Sash Sabban. However, because of such competition, the tailors and sellers often take their business and best items into the Grand Ward or Palace Ward, the better to sell straight to the wealthy pashas and sultans.

The sabban mark for this sabban was three sashes, represented by three wavy parallel lines.

Marekh Sabban

Also called the Black Sabban and later the Great Bazaar Sabban, Marekh Sabban was largely destroyed in the Trades Conflagration. Once it held countless bazaars, khanduqs, and hundreds of row houses, as well as temples to Mystra, Sune, and Cyric—all were destroyed. Instead, it was actively being rebuilt as of 1370 DR. The six minarets surrounding the Great Bazaar were among the first to be erected, though it was yet to gain any new temples.

The sabban's whole western half came to be covered by open market, called the Great Bazaar. It permitted temporary stalls for periods of 12 hours in either day or night, enabling 24-hour business. The majority of goods traded here were daily foods, common spices, and other essentials. The market was controlled by Pasha Sufontis. The rest of the sabban was owned by a dozen major families and more than a hundred individual merchants and business owners.

The sabban mark for this sabban was a seven-pointed star within a circle of seven coins, with a coin at each point. It contained four drudachs: Azhaya, Maz'ahl, Yeaz, and Yeroh.

Otahl Sabban

Also known as Tumahl's Sabban, this sabban was most famous as the birthplace and residence of the pirate Tumahl el Otahl, the Rogue Son of El Otahl, a legendary adventurer of the Eighth Age of Calimshan. A large white marble statue of him stood in the center of the biggest bazaar in the sabban, pointing a scimitar to the southwest, either toward the Pasha's Sabban or the Armada Ward docks as a challenge to the two figures with which Tumahl had most contended.

The sabban mark for this sabban was a slender dagger with no guard and a hole in the base—this, too, was the signature of Tumahl el Otahl. It contained four drudachs: Farix, Khumil, and Mabul.
