Memnon was a key port of trade and garrison city for the kingdom of Calimshan. capital of the efreet kingdom of Memnonnar. it became the capital of a reborn Memnonnar, ruled by genies and genasi.

Geography

Memnon was situated on the northern border of the Calim Desert, at its very edges, It stood at the mouth of the River Agis, and along the Trade Way. It was also connected to Calimport by way of the Coast Trail, which skirted along the Calim Desert's edge.

Description

Allegedly smaller in size than the capital city Calimport, it was no less an impressively large metropolis, rivaling some of the great cities of the Sword Coast. Its streets were vast and labyrinthine. Much of the city's architecture was made up of stone, and mudbrick. dating back to the Era of Skyfire, were composed of a smooth dark rock. This rock had the unique properties of being totally immune to wear and heat, withstanding even the likes of fireballs and fiery dragon breath. No matter the local conditions, the shale-like stone was always cool to the touch and shined. But when removed from the walls proper, the stone miraculously became mere coal.

The most striking feature of Memnon's architecture was its scarlet brickwork—the local rich red clay and mud brightened into this shade as they were baked—which could be denoted from great distances. Some of the upperclasses of Memnon viewed this scarlet shade as the "commoners' red" and paid for stone and tiles, imported from elsewhere in Calimshan and the far off Sword Coast, to construct homes and businesses with.

Many of the general housing structures were squat, clay buildings of a rectangular shape, measuring a dozen feet across. They faced out towards the Trackless Sea, allowing the sea breeze to waft onto them, and were equipped with awnings. Even poorer housing were shacks made from driftwood. The city sprawl spread out into the docks and lining the docks were a number of windowless warehouses.

On a hill overlooking the southwestern-most point of Memnon's harbor, behind a rocky outcropping, a patch of dirt was used as burial ground amongst the destitute. The city proper had its own cemetery, full of commoner markhouts and filigreed khamarkhas.

In the years spent under genasi rule following the Spellplague, the city came to be transformed into an even more magnificent metropolis, full of glittering palaces and verdant villas.

Beneath the depths of Memnon, one could find ancient and oft sealed tunnels connecting to Old Shanatar.

Organization

While ignorant foreigners visiting Memnon sometimes felt that the city seemed as though it was laid out without any kind of civic planning, This Calishite form of urban structuring, established all the way back to when it was the rule of genies, The city had a total of seven sabbans and twenty-six drudachs.

Like other port cities in Calimshan, Memnon had piers and wharves that were specially designated for use by the navy. Known as the Naval Sabban, it also enclosed the shipwrights and naval barracks.

Government

The rulers of Memnon were referred to as "sultans", During the period of genasi rule, the title changed to that of Vizier. The later return of human rule to the city came with a resurgence of the "sultan" title.

The official ruler of Memnon in 1370 DR was Sultan Arin yn Kahar el Sujil, through political and trade alliances. However, the real power was held by his vizar, Anders el Gnurlbrach. Despite being a half-orc, since at least 1357 DR, Anders was very popular both with the general populace of Memnon and with the syl-pasha of Calimshan. This was in part because he established an elected city council that he voluntarily shared power with. He encouraged the other pashas and vizars beneath him to leave Arin in place, for purposes of maintaining a good appearance. The two of them were spied upon on behalf of the syl-pasha by Arin's cousins, the lesser vizars Asraf and Kadar, a fact that Anders knew all too well.

Almost all magic and wizardry in the city were controlled and regulated by the Guild Arcane. Memnon long had a reputation for being friendly towards wizards, but fawned over magic far less than other Calishite cities.

Law & Order

In 1370 DR, around 750 amlakkar were stationed in the city, Memnon's amlakkar were considered to be some of the most heavily armed in all of Calimshan, though their armaments and armor paled in comparison to those of the soldiers garrisoned within the Defender's Drudach.

The city's harbor was overseen by the Memnon Harbor Patrol, who plied around the busy waters in longboats with a beacon lantern at their bow a discerning flag at their stern. They searched all incoming vessels for contraband. Those found to be carrying contraband were to be denied access to the port, though in practice patrolmen tried to wring out bribes to be allowed access into the port. They also oversaw the detention of pirates, offering a reward for any captured and brought into port.

Crime

In 1370 DR, the vast majority of criminal enterprises in Memnon were controlled by El Pesarkhal, one of the nation's four major crime families. They were led by Nasim yn Feroz, the self-proclaimed "Dark Caleph" and brother to Syl-Pasha Ralan. Serving as the lieutenant of Memnon was Basad yn Malik. Nasim was a loyal subject to the syl-pasha, the underworld agencies being inter-meshed with the government system of Calimshan.

El Pesarkhal primarily presided over blackmarketeering, thuggery, enforcement, and spying. They also had direct control over the other crime families, such as the piracy and smuggling focused El Vyndahla of Almraiven, who had major links to enterprises in Memnon. The other prominent crime family in Memnon were El Khamir. They oversaw the bulk of the piracy and smuggling that went on in Memnon, as well as the slave trade, burglars, thieves, and assassins. Additionally, the greater assassination trade across Calimshan was centered in Memnon.

According to some, Memnon also boasted one of the best organized beggar's guild in Faerûn, with very strict rates. They'd charge for any service, even helping someone up if they'd fallen, and just to spit-polish a sword was 10 copper pieces. Foreigners were often targeted by the beggars, as they typically brought wealth to Memnon. By the late 15 century DR, this beggar's guild was still around.

Other criminal organizations with a presence in Memnon included the Rundeen, the Many Long Arms, the Twisted Rune, and the Shadow Thieves, who controlled a school for assassins within the city known as the School of Stealth. The Twisted Rune were represented by the dracolich Sapphiraktar, who in the 14 century DR was the puppet master of over a third of important underworld and government figures in the city, all being merely illusory disguises or projections. One of these disguises, a corrupt baelnorn who others believed was restricted to the subterranean depths of Memnon, had a monopoly over the corpse trade and trade of necromantic supplies throughout Calimshan.

The Janessar, an outlaw anti-slavery organization, also had scattered hideouts in Memnon.

During the period of genasi rule, the Twisted Rune were able to forge alliances with genasi leaders in Memnon. This period also saw the active presence of remnants of the Janessar, helping Memnonite slaves to escape to the human-controlled city of Almraiven.

Trade

Memnon was a key trade city for Calimshan. It was that kingdom's northernmost port, the first port for goods coming down from the north and the last Calishite port for goods heading northward. On some days over a hundred ships, flying the flags of a hundred different ports of a dozen different kingdoms, could be found anchored outside a full port.

Nearly all caravaneers on the Trade Way stopped at Memnon for at least a day, regardless of whether they were traveling north or south. The city thus served the function of a massive caravanserai, offering various caravan services. These included offering inns, stables, wagon building and repairs, supplies and equipment, or entertainment to those who'd just survived the trek across the desert or to those about to make it.

Altogether the festhalls, inns, and taverns of Memnon numbered around 600 or so. These establishment all typically set their prices according to supply and demand, leaving them to be higher than the going rates elsewhere in Calimshan, even for the crudest of accommodations. The best a traveler on a tight budget could hope for was a hayloft bed and a daily handmeal.

Additionally, Memnon was considered a fishing town, All manner of other goods could be found in its markets, including exotic pipeweeds, perfume, slaves, and oil lamps.

During the Tethyrian Interregnum, the city played a key role in the Calishite slave trade, acting as a staging point for slaves that had been captured from the lawless land of Tethyr. But after Zaranda Star was crowned Queen of Tethyr in 1369 DR and restored law to the land, the city became less appealing Calishite slavers, with talks of relocating to either Athkatla or Murann. with those that could be spared from the war effort being forced to mine a special dark rock in the nearby Marching Mountains, the same type of dark rock that the walls around the city were made of by during the First Era of Skyfire. The lives of these slave miners were usually brutal and short, further complicated by raids from orcs and ogres that called the Marching Mountains home.

Once every year, the city sent a heavily armed contingent of guards and stonecutters across the River Agis into the ruins of Memnonnar. There they scavenged stone for use in building repairs as well as decorative usages. By 1370 DR, this tradition had been going on for centuries.

By the late 15 century DR, some of the Chultan merchant princes of Port Nyanzaru had established outposts in Memnon.

Defenses

The city had a strong military focus. It served as the northernmost naval base for the Qysanallojal, Calimshan's navy, as well as their second primary port. Their warships and soldiers were considered essential to combating northern aggression and any monsters and other horrors that could emerge from the ruins of Memnonnar across the River Agis. Many wizards also served within the navy, acted as navigational aids, or provided other unconventional defenses against the pirates of the Trackless Sea.

In 1370 DR, over 2000 were garrisoned in Memnon within the Defender's Drudach. All were outfitted with bristling armor and gleaming weaponry.

The ships docked in Memnon were the smallest and fastest in the Qysanallojal, designed for scouting and anti-piracy purposes. In 1357 DR, the navy was growing so quickly that fishermen were being forced to find new docks. On average there were no fewer than 30 to 35 ships moored in Memnon at any given time. In addition to their assigned crews, these ships were tended to on and off board in 1370 DR by roughly 700 people.

Following the Spellplague, the city's heavy militaristic focus was continued by its new leaders, who fed part of the enslaved populace into their ongoing war with Calimport. Their constant fighting across the Calim Desert became a source of infamy among the rest of Faerûn. Even many of the Memnonite citizenry disapproved of the city's warmongering.

Culture

Events & Festivals

In the wake of the loss of their genie leaders in 1450 DR, starting at an oasis somewhere between Calimport and Memnon. To the champion of this race would be afforded a large gem referred to as the "Heart of the Desert". This race went on to become a triennial event among the three cities, each time with the ultimate prize being the largest gem found in the prior three years.

Religion

All of the major churches of the Faerûnian pantheon were represented among the clergical population of Memnon, with nearly all having some sort of temple. Some of the many deities worshiped in Memnon included Ibrandul, Selune, Shaundakul, Silvanus, Umberlee, and Valkur.

Very few of the temples in Memnon were major complexes, like those found in Calimport and Keltar. And despite being a port city, Memnon did not boast any more temples to gods of water than inland cities. One of the most prominent religious structures in the city was the Theater of All Faiths. Once a theater and stadium under the days of genie ruler, it became a venue for religious ceremonies by any and all faiths.

Inhabitants

The bulk of Memnon's populace was made up of humans. Beyond them there were prominent numbers of half-orcs, halflings, and elves living in the city.

The central and eastern quarters of the city were occupied by caravan travelers and the local nobility, with the city's sailors rarely venturing far beyond the Naval Sabban and the drudachs that surrounded it.

In the 14 century DR, homeless was an ongoing issue in the city, with many in Memnon's poorer southwestern district having little choice but to sleep on the sandy streets. Many of the poorest of the destitute suffered maladies of malnourishment and poor hygiene.

Following the Spellplague, efreet and firesoul genasi made up a prominent portion of the population and became the upper class of society, enslaving the rest of the population. Like their counterparts in Calimport, the genasi were notorious for their treatment of humans as slaves. Among the genasi, the firesouls pledged to the efreet were believed to be superior to all other elemental souls, but the earthsouled were considered the lowest ranking amongst all of the aspects of the genasi.

There were also large numbers of migrant tieflings in the city, many of whom were put to work in Memnon's military, earning positions of power and respect among the fire genasi elite.

History

Memnonnar

In , the Great Pasha Memnon and his efreet Army of Fire arrived in southern Faerûn through a portal close to the River Agis, he founded the river-spanning city of Memnonnar and it would become the capital to a kingdom by the same name. His armies forced the local dwarves of Deep Shanatar to build this city and clear down the forests for where it would stand.

Circa , a centuries long conflict between Memnon and the djinni Calim, known as the First Era of Skyfire, came to a close. they used their elven high magic to create a powerful spell that would be unleashed at the Battle of Teshyllal Fields by the High Mage Pharos.

With the loss of its leader, it wasn't long before the remaining inhabitants of Memnonnar fell prey to the elves of Darthiir Wood. Over the next 30 years, all traces of the city of Memnonnar were ruins. What survived of it on the southern side of the River Agis would one day go on to become the site of the city of Memnon.

Calishite Empire

In the , needing to transport troops to their frontier wars against the elves of Tethir, the the Calimshan Empire used slave labor to build a road between the cities of Memnon and Zazesspur. Almost immediately, this military road became dominated by merchant traffic.

In the , following the Throne Wars of Calimshan, the Syl-Pasha Tasyn el Tarshaj yi Manshaka and his family simultaneously conquered the cities of Memnon, Almraiven, Calimport, and Manshaka, unifying the Pasha's Lands.

In the , Memnon is hit by the Shaking Plague.

In the , Akkabar Shoon rose to power as the Syl-Vizar and ruler of Memnon. Over the next seven months, through careful political maneuvering, Akkabar would go on to become the Syl-Pasha of all of Calimshan, founding the Shoon Imperium.

Shoon Imperium

Over the course of the Shoon Imperium, the Guilds Arcane were established by the ruling Qysars. The last of these to be established would be in Memnon.

In the , with Umberlant worship waning among Calishites after more than a century of peaceful waters, five massive waves struck at different points across the Shoon Imperium. The cities of Almraiven, Manshaka, Teshburl, Memnon, and Calimport. About a third to two-thirds of each settlement was severely damaged by flooding. Through this terror Umberlee inspired renewed reverence among the Calishites. Since that day, Memnon was never without at least two structures dedicated to Umberlee, for fear that lack of reverence would bring the "Vengeance Waves" to bear upon them once more.

In 75 DR, cities across the Shoon Imperium are hit by Fog Fever. Memnon, Calimport, and Myratma are hit hardest by the plague.

In 138 DR, Memnon is among a number of cities in the Shoon Imperium to suffer great fires within its poorer, wooden sections. While likely the result of fireworks the Qysar had demanded for his birthday, Qsar Amahl Shoon IV blamed the incidinces on the Church of Ilmater.

In 319 DR, with rates of crime and violence increasing across the Imperium, Qysar Shoon VII decided to take drastic measures. He ordered oil to be poured into the sewers of major imperial cities, causing the Great Fires. While originally planned to be controlled blazes, the fires raged almost unchecked. With a death toll reaching over three million people, the cities of Memnon, Ankhapur, Calimport, Innarlith, and Ithmong were near completely devastated.

In 320 DR, Memnon is among the cities of Calimshan to get hit by the Infernal Death.

Calimshan

In 1334 DR, during the tumultous Ninth Age of Calimshan, rumors circulated in Memnon that the upstart kingdom of Mulsparkh was eyeing the city as a new base of operations. Before long troops from Memnon were sent out to aid the army of Tethyr in the Battle of Nightflames, bringing an end to Mulsparkh.

In the late 1340s DR, Conner and Vaji ran confidence tricks in all the major cities of Calimshan, including Memnon, before they went on to Manshaka.

In the , Vajra, Conner, and Salabak sailed back into Memnon on the trail of the mage Chancil Crystalheart. However, his thugs and apprentices waylaid them with a wand of telekinesis, hurling them high into the air. Fortunately their fall was broken by an orange-seller's stall, and they were surrounded by eager-to-help beggars, which Salabak drove off with the illusion of a squealer. They followed Chancil's apprentice to his home, where they attempted to rescue the kidnapped cat child.

In the , in the month of Hammer, a great spell battle broke out in the streets of Memnon between the archmages Galthaunt and Yuthrygg and their many magical creations, after the latter had declared himself "Overking of Tethyr". Spanning most of a day, Memnonites fled the city in terror.

Under the rule of Syl-Pasha Ralan el Pesarkhal, both the Guilds Arcane of Calimport and Memnon were relied upon in ongoing efforts to restore safety to the Trade Road.

Post-Spellplague

After the Spellplague of the , the High Magic that bound Calim and Memnon in the Calimemnon Crystal shattered, unleashing them upon the world. Freed, the two genies resumed their millennia-old hostilities, overthrowing the Calimshan government and ushering in the Second Era of Skyfire or "Memnon's Blessed Return" as some referred to it.

In the wake of the return of Calim and Memnon, many of the residents of Calimshan, presumed to be human, manifested their genasi heritage. These descendants of the elemental powers pledged themselves to either elemental fire or air. Those who became firesoul genasi took over the city of Memnon, joining its namesake in his war against the djinn and windsoul genasi who ceased control of Calimport. With this, the old realm of Calimshan effectively ceased to exist, with the firesoul genasi followers of Memnon proclaiming the whole region to be Memnonnar reborn.

The Second Era of Skyfire lasted until 1450 DR, when both Memnon and Calim were banished to the Elemental Chaos by an unknown event. They left in their wake a devastated Calimshan, torn apart by the remaining genasi warlords. This period also saw an The influx of exotic and valuable goods to Memnon, making the city a tempting target for overly ambitious thieves and adventurers to plunder.

By 1489 DR, the human slaves of Calimshan had overthrown their genasi masters with the aid of a Chosen of Ilmater. Though the slaves initially used nonviolent methods in resisting the genasi, their efforts escalated into full rebellion after the Chosen disappeared. These rebellions deposed the genie lords of Calimport and Memnon, sending them either to the Elemental Planes or the deep deserts. Free of their genie masters, the people of Calimshan needed to find new ways to live together and tolerate those genasi who remained. Many Calishites prayed for the Chosen to come back and complete his work.
