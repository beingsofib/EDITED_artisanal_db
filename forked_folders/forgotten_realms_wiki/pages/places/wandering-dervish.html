The Wandering Dervish was a festhall in the Central Port of Skullport in the late 14 century DR.

Structure

The Wandering Dervish was a one-story building in fair condition.

Interior

The interior presented a Zakharan theme, though this was limited to faded desert-themed wallpaper and attendants who affected an exotic atmosphere. The serving maids spoke in a lilting foreign tongue, and either did not understand Common and Undercommon or choose not to, instead dancing for patrons in exchange for coins.

Inhabitants

In 1370 DR, the owner of The Wandering Dervish was Nicamar Turtlebuck. He served as Master of the House, but the business was reputed to be owned by an individual from outside Skullport.
