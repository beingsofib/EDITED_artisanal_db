The Hook Ward was one of the four Port Wards of Calimport, making up the eastern third of the city's public docks.

Description

Whilst the upper classes of Calimshan rarely made much in the way distinguishing between the Port Wards, the Hook Ward stood out among its peers as being a balanced blend between the commercial and the militant. Its streets were far cleaner and more organized than those of the Dock Ward. It was also the site of the actual shipyards that serviced the city's port and many of the city's seafarers' guilds had their guildhalls based here.

One of the other aspects of Hook Ward that made it stand out amongst the other Port Wards was the predominance of temples and shrines to maritime deities, both major and minor. Very few of these religious sites stood out as particularly luxuriant compared to the temples of the Upper Muzad. Many temples of other wards commonly sent proselytes and new acolytes down into the Hook Ward's markets and dock areas to perform streetside sermons, hoping to draw the attention of new arrivals to their own god and house of worship, but more often than not this would culminate in proselytes of opposing churches duking it out on the streets.

Government

In the 14 century DR, the second son of Syl-Pasha Ralan el Pesarkhal, Tahyr yn Ralan el Pesarkhal, was the Sultan of Hook Ward. He typically spent about two months of the year out at sea, during which time his brother-in-law and Syl-Vizar of the Hook Ward, Khomaya, would take over as acting sabbalad.

As for the Syl-Pasha, he notably visited the Hook Ward and its Eraré Sabban more than any other sabban outside of the Palace

and Grand Wards. He could invariably be found presenting himself every time a new dignitary or emissary of noble birth showed up to one of the embassies in the Eraré Sabban.

In a way the Hook Ward held undue control over the flow of entry into the city of Calimport, for no more than five people could exit from a docked ship until its docking fees were cleared with the harbormasters.

Defenses

The ward had a more subdued military presence compared to the Armada Ward.

Of its three sabbans, Eraré was afforded additional security in the 14 century DR, due to being a core component of Syl-Pasha Ralan's long-term plans to grow the nation's foreign trade. The Ajvent Sabban and Osiir Sabban were afforded two amlakkhans and a sadidah. By contrast, the Eraré Sabban was afforded two sadidah, three amlakkhans, two minarets dedicated to its defenses, and an additional garrison of troops that were assigned to its embassies.

Visting foreign officials usually brought their own guards and servants, though the local Mercenaries' Guild insisted that to ensure safety an embassy should have no less than 25 guards on duty. Because of this, the guild would provide their own warriors to fill out the ranks.

Trade

Much like in other parts of the port city, one could find all manner of things for sale within the bazaars of the Hook Ward.

Sabbans

The Hook Ward was divided into three sabbans with nine drudachs.

Ajvent Sabban

Also known as the "Shipwrights' Sabban" or the "Sails Sabban", this sabban comprised the entirety of Calimport's shipyards. It was adjacent to the Caravans Ward's Drakhon Sabban and the Maker's Ward's Golnar Sabban. It was under the shared control of Sultan Tahyr, the Sabbalad Saibh el Daafib, and the pasha of shipwrights. The underlings of the sultan handled the day-to-day operations of the sabban, making sure the ruler's ships had highest priority, including in terms of maintenance or repairs. Not even visiting ships from Calimshan's allies would receive precedence over the Sultan's vessels.

The sabban mark of Ajvent depicted a leaping dolphin above a curling wave.

Eraré Sabban

Also known as the "Wyvern Sabban" or more commonly the "Wraith Sabban", Eraré was the central sabban of the Hook Ward. It served as the receiving for dignitaries, a space for many of the embassies of foreign powers, and was frequently an unofficial meeting place among adventurers. It was also notably the most safest place in all of the Port Wards, due to its high security. Its latter moniker was derived from a horrid creature known as the "Eraré Wraith".

The sabban mark of Eraré was originally a warhammer, backed by a silhouette of a wyvern in flight. Over time the warhammer disappeared from the sigil, though it could still be found on some of the ward's walls.

Badjit Drudach

Located directly on the waterfront, the Badjit Drudach was Eraré's drudach of entertainment, being home to the majority of the sabban's festhalls and taverns.

Taorahl Drudach

Also known as the "Twelvetors Drudach", the Taorahl Drudach was dedicated to providing the service of lodgings. It was the easternmost drudach, nestled against the shipyard walls of Hook Ward and the Wizard Ward. It was home to the residences of the sabban's leaders, most of the lodgings that were available to the sabban's visitors, and in 1370 DR was selected by the Syl-Pasha as the site for foreign embassies meant to further gain foreign alliances in commerce and politics. The "Twelvetors" nickname was derived from the presence of twelve minarets in the Taorahl skyline. Three of these belonged to amlakkhans, six were attached to opulent villas, and three belonged to temples.

The embassies of the Taorahl Drudach provided free living space for visiting dignitaries and officials of foreign powers, They were all beautiful houses and villas,

Early on, none of the embassies were used as permanent residences for ambassadors and their support staff, though there were hopes that such a thing could be established by 1371 DR as Calimshan developed more binding relations with foreign powers. In particular, sights were set on this occuring with the Cormyreans and Thayvians, whose trade delegates were visiting Calimport with greater frequency each month in 1370 DR.

When the embassies were first established, they were met with a mixed reception from the Taorahl Drudach's many pashas. Whilst some railed about it giving non-Calishites an undeserved social status above their own and desires to claim their housing, others viewed this as the opening of new business opportunities. Local pashas and businessmen would spend great sums in hopes of endearing themselves. And local businesses would supply most of the food and drink available at embassy meetings, having them brought over from nearby inns and taverns.

For those who resided within embassies, it was socially expected to sponsor a gala at the residence a night or two before one left Calimport. This gave both the visitors and local a chance to settle any final business then, as well as a final chance for the pashas and other social climbers in Calimport to either endear themselves or slander their political rivals.

Tynar's Drudach

Also known by many as the "Three Temples Drudach", Tynar's Drudach was situated as the commercial trade center of the Eraré Sabban. It covered the western end of the sabban, abutting the Osiir Sabban. The streets leading through it allowed for goods to be conveyed from the docks, into the main trading areas of Tynar, and into the Grand Ward. And with wall-top byways, the druzirs and pashas could avoiding having to sully their feet upon its cobble. Besides official goods, bribery of the local amlakkhan allowed for contraband to travel through here and up to the Upper Muzad.

Tynar's Drudach was by far the busiest and most crowded of the three drudachs, not only because of its bazaars, but due its major temples and numerous private and public shrines.

In the 14 century DR, with the Sultan Tahyr as an absentee sabbalad the drudach was unofficially under the control of the Nine Pashas Trading Coster, who exerted their influence over many of the bazaar businesses surrounding their headquarters. At that time Tynar's Drudach had an ineffectual druzir, who was nothing more than a puppet of the Nine Pashas consortium, and the coster regularly bribed the sabban's vizar to look the other way.

Osiir Sabban

Also regularly known as the "Harbor Sabban", for it was the sabban where all incoming captains had to register their ships and pay docking fees. It handled all management of the docks and harbor. And it was widely considered among sailors to be the entrance to Calimport, even when docking in one of the other Port Wards.

The sabban mark of Osiir was comprised of a silhouette of a single-masted ship with, full sails visible from the side. It was divided into three drudachs—Biguh Drudach, Piqaz Drudach, and Enqir Drudach.

History

Prior to Syl-Pasha Ralan el Pesarkhal's establishment of wards as the city's largest sectors in the , Hook Ward was but part of what was known as the Dock Quarter.

Around the 12 century DR, during the Eighth Age of Calimshan, the fourth son of the last Pasha of the Eraré Dynasty was entombed alive within an unmarked tomb in the Muzad by an evil necromancer.

When a Rage of Dragons occurred in the , the continual bluish-white beacon of light emitted by the Lighthouse of the Moon by focusing the moon's rays attracted undue attention to Hook Ward one night, likely from the blue dragon Sapphiraktar.

When the Time of Troubles struck in , unleashing magical chaos across the Realms, the magic that had sealed the spirit of the Eraré prince failed and he escaped from his unmarked tomb as a wraith. Dubbed the "Eraré Wraith", this wrathful undead creature would prowl the shadowy streets of Calimport for years, until allegedly being destroyed by Javir yn Zamar el Hosaan in the , though some would continue to attribute the occasional mysterious death to the wraith.
