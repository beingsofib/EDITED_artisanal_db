The Brothers Humuz was a stone-carving business in the city of Calimport, Calimshan, in the late 14 century DR, owned by Nur & Harun el Humuz.

Location

A quality four-story building, it was located Tar'ea Drudach, Sojrab Sabban, in the city's Maker's Ward.

Activities

Circa 1370 DR, Brothers Nur and Harun specialized in carving murals into stone and mud-brick walls on the insides of fine homes, often enhancing the building's value or social status. The brothers worked well together without issue, but when a client desired to paint over the finished mural, Nur got upset at his artistry being soiled and stormed off. Harun, more focused on the trade, was left to finish the work.

In addition, the Brothers Humuz were spies for the Rundeen mercantile consortium. After working for long periods in wealthy clients' homes, they reported what they saw and heard to their Rundeen masters every sixmonth.
