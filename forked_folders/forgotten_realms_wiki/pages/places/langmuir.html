Langmuir () was a small port village located on the isle of Moray within the Moonshae Isles.

Geography

The village was located on the eastern shore of Last Head at the very south of Moray. It was the nearest settlement to an important lighthouse located on the southern tip of the island, This location meant that cloudy weather, fog, and light rain were common.

Government

Langmuir was ruled by a king and a council composed of the village's princes, merchants, mine owners, and minor nobles. The people generally trusted their rulers to make wise decisions. The council was known to engage in animated debates, sometimes around the fireplace in the common room of the village inn.

Defenses

The village was heavily fortified as of the late 15 century DR to defend against the Black Blood tribe. It had sturdy walls, but not enough soldiers to defend against a major, coordinated assault. Strangers arriving at the gates were not allowed inside until they proved that they were not lycanthropes by cutting themselves with a steel dagger. Lycanthropes were not allowed inside without support from a trusted member of the community, and likewise they refused entry to other shapechangers, any creature known to ally with the Black Blood (including goblins, orcs, half-orcs, and shifters), and any individuals who appeared to be part beast (such as gnolls or minotaurs).

Trade

Ore mined from the nearby Orcskull Mountains was brought into Langmuir where the valuable raw metals were extracted and then shipped to places like Alaron and Norland. The extraction and refinement of this metal ore was highly lucrative, making it worth the risks of mining and traveling in the dangerous mountains.

Langmuir was isolated and relied on trade. As of the late 15 century DR, the area around Langmuir was too dangerous to support largescale farming, and thus the people relied on food imported by ship to survive.

History

During the 15 century DR, lycanthropes of the Black Blood tribe frequently attacked Langmuir.

In the , a Black Blood werewolf known as Bloodeye began escalating attacks on the area and on mining caravans. A couple months later, a war hero from Alaron named Daffyd Mac Lyrr arrived in town to help coordinate efforts to drive him out, but less than two tendays later, Bloodeye captured and snuffed out the lighthouse near the village. The council sent a half-dozen of the best members of their militia to retake the lighthouse, but none returned. This led to a split in the council over whether to send their remaining militia on a second mission or to keep them in town in case of an attack.

Over the next three days, a longship called The Lightning wrecked just north of the village without the lighthouse to guide it and panic was spreading within Langmuir. Tensions rose when it was confirmed that a war band of the Black Blood tribe was amassing nearby, prompting the council to decide that the militia had to defend the village and adventurers needed to be found to strike at the Black Blood, for whom they turned to the crew of The Lightning led by Ulrik Ragnarsson. No agreement could be reached about whether to reclaim the lighthouse or attack the war band, and the decision was deferred to the adventurers. and were readying handcarts filled with such explosives to breach Langmuir's walls.

Inhabitants

By the late 15 century DR, most inhabitants were young people from abroad who came to work in Langmuir's lucrative but dangerous mining industry, whether as miners, ore refiners, caravanners, or guards. and because of the constant threat posed by these monsters, the townsfolk were highly suspicious of any "monstrous" individuals. Even if such individuals gained access to the village, they were mistrusted, feared, and were liable to be thrown in jail for the smallest infraction.

Notable Inhabitants

As of the late 15 century DR, notable inhabitants included:

  • King Donald Mac Innis, who deferred rulership to his council.
  • Jinkerton, a logistics clerk who secretly worked with the Black Blood tribe.
  • Keeron Mac Innis, the son of King Donald Mac Innis.
  • Maldwyn, head of a shipping consortium.
