Strait of the Leviathan

The Strait of the Leviathan was a channel of the Trackless Sea that separated the islands of Moray and Gwynneth in the Moonshae Isles.

Geography

The strait joined the Sea of Moonshae with the Trackless Sea, cutting into the heart of the Moonshae Isles from the southwest. The Strait of the Leviathan was approximately long.

The narrowest point of the Strait was marked by Kork Head on Moray—a hill beneath which was Kork Bay—and a headland across the Strait on Gwynneth defined by a line of rock spires that looked liked a spine.

History

During the Darkwalker War of the , the Leviathan intercepted a fleet of Northmen longships sailing south through the Strait under the command of Kazgoroth (in disguise as King Thelgaar Ironhand). The ensuing battle saw much of the fleet damaged or sunk, but ended in the death of that incarnation of the Leviathan.

By the late 1470s DR, the Strait was well-known to be treacherous for human sailors. Despite the risks, a ship called the Sphinx attempted to approach Kork Head through the Strait and was sunk.

Inhabitants

The narrows at Kork Bay were patrolled by fire nagas as of the late 15 century DR, although many sailors dismissed their existence as mere rumor.
