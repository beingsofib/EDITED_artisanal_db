White Plume Mountain

White Plume Mountain was a volcanic mountain on the world of Oerth that had been hollowed out into a dungeon lair by a powerful archmage named Keraptis. and supernatural awe among Oerthians, attracting sightseers and powerful adventurers alike from miles around. Around the late 15 century DR, having recovered from his latest defeat, Keraptis planeshifted the entirety of the mountain to the Prime Material world of Toril, transposing it into the Crags of northwest Faerûn.

Geography

On its home world of Oerth, White Plume Mountain was situated in the northeastern part of an area known as the Shield Lands, close to the Bandit Kingdoms tangled thickets, marshy valleys, and the legendary Fields of Pesh.

Description

White Plume Mountain was a volcanic hill, almost perfectly conical in shape, that had formed overtime from a gradual leakage of lava. And the hollowed out dungeon segments of the mountain were originally a system of ancient lava tubes. The mountain measured in diameter at its base and stood tall.

From the peak of mountain spewed a tremendous, continuous geyser of water that shot up and spread across the sky, eventually raining down upon the eastern slopes, giving off a sort of "feathered" appearance that was the mountain's namesake. This water collected downslope into depressions, coalescing into a stream. There were also smaller steam vents along the mountain.

Besides the vent at its peak, the only other known entrance to the mountain was a fabled cave on its south slope, known as the "Wizard's Mouth". It was so named because currents of wind seemed to systematically cycle in and out like someone breathing on a cold day, blow into its dark depths and coming out not long after as a large cloud of steam. This cycle was also accompanied by a whistling sound, made faint by the roar of the mountain's geyser.

History

Circa -800 CY (-20 DR), Keraptis came to White Plume Mountain with an army of loyal gnomish warriors and slew its guardian, a hierophant by the name of Aegwareth, taking the mountain for himself.

Sometime between 1479 DR and 1492 DR, a bald wizard clad in blue robes and a strange accent, possibly that of another world, entered the Yawning Portal and spoke of the fabled White Plume Mountain. There he a told a tale of three powerful weapons that had been spirited away from a city into its depths and how a group of adventurers dared to recover them. In time, this planar story would be one of many spread across the Sword Coast. The weapons in question were the legendary warhammer Whelm, trident Wave, and sword Blackrazor.

According to some sources, following the recovery of this Blackrazor that had been taken by Keraptis, the blade had somehow made its way to the city of Baldur's Gate on the Prime Material world of Toril. But another instance of Blackrazor notably found its way into Bhaal's Throne of Blood, where it was later taken by Abdel Adrian of Toril.

Elsewhere in the multiverse, on the world of Eberron a group of druids called the Gatekeepers set up a temporary gate to White Plume Mountain, wanting to retrieve a page of the Codex of the Infinite Planes that Keraptis had in his possession.

Over the centuries Keraptis devoted himself to researching various means of achieving eternal life, albeit methods that would not require magical maintenance or healing. One such grand experiment for immortality involved creating his own Demiplane of Shadows. As he grew older, the mage's memories increasingly flashed back to him. Once fully an adult, he sought to enact a new plan for immortality and opened a gate to the world of Toril, taking him to a continent he would mispronounce as "Fair-rune", banking on the absence of his legend to allow him a chance to begin anew.

Before long Keraptis caught wind of the gloomblades, and blessed by the goddess Shar. He began to plot a new ritual for immortality. He would undertake a new ritual, Through this ritual he would bind himself to the Shadowfell and create a permanent shadow passage. Keraptis theorized that in doing so, he would remain alive so long as the plane existed. He would have eternal life without the drawbacks of undeath. And unlike his previous plan, Keraptis would have the freedom to roam Toril as he pleased.

Over the next tenday, his allies would gather potential sacrifices, kidnapping travelers and villagers. Towards the end of that tenday, under his pseudonym Kelvan, Keraptis would approach a group of adventurers in the Muddy Malard Tavern in Neverwinter. Introducing himself as having traveled to the area to study the mountain itself, he would explain that he found its entrance was warded and was in need of someone to obtain a piece of elemental darkness for him to perform a bypassing ritual. Luckily he had made the acquaintance of Hildehrune, an Illuskan scholar well-versed in ancient Netheril and Selunite priest, who knew of one of those gloomblades lying within a Netherese vault called the Umbral Repository.

Once they plundered the depths of the Umbral Repository, the adventurers used a teleportation circle to take themselves straight to White Plume Mountain. After exchanging the gloomblade with the rewards he promised, Kelvan urged the adventurers to step back in case things went awry as Hildehrune enacted a spell to open the entrance. Once the adventurers were far enough away, Kelvan enacted a forcecage to prevent being followed and ushered a charmed Hildehrune into the depths of the mountain, proclaiming his plans to sacrifice her. By that time his allies had already gathered the other sacrifices, leaving Hildehrune to be the one-hundredth.

The adventurers quickly set out to notify the various factions they worked with, namely the Order of the Gauntlet, and hastily formulated a plan of action against Keraptis. Under the command of the dwarf Dram, teams were formed. Some would attempt a frontal assault, distracting the wizard's minions, whilst others would enter covertly through tunnels found by scouts and try to rescue his prisoners. Each team was given sending stones so they could communicate freely.

Navigating a trap-filled maze, designed to break down the mental defenses of all who wandered it, the adventurers eventually came across a trio of imprisoned bards. The triton Talcorus recounted being kidnapped by a wizard; the dwarf Amberlee recounted losing in a game of chance in Waterdeep to an efreeti; the dragonborn Krija told them that he was once part of a proud mercenary company, but had made a bargain with one Sir Bluto Sans Pite that would spare his life at the cost of his comrades and his own freedom. Elsewhere, another group found a set of prison cells and rescued three malnourished wereboar brothers.

By the time all of the adventurers fought their way to where work for the ritual was being done and commoners were being held captive, it was already underway. And with a temporary shadow passage open, a shadow dragon had crossed over. As they struck at Keraptis, a contingency of greater invisibility activated and he fled with a misty step to a hidden simulacrum, ordering it to fight. Sources differed on whether the wizard managed to sacrifice Hildehrune or fled from the scene through teleportation.

Sometime after this, White Plume Mountain found its way back to the Prime Material world of its origins.
