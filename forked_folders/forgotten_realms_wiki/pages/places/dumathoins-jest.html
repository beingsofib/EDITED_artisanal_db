Dumathoin's Jest was a shop in the Upper Trade Lanes of Skullport in the late 14 century DR.

Structure

Dumathoin's Jest was a one-story building in good condition.

Services

Dumathoin's Jest was a well-regarded gem-cutting and appraisal establishment. The shop was known for meticulous craftsmanship and discretion, qualities that attracted both legitimate collectors and those who preferred their transactions remained unrecorded. The shop's services do not come cheaply: a flat fee of 20% of a gem’s assessed value was the charge for services.

Inhabitants

Despite its name, Dumathoin's Jest had little direct connection to the dwarven deity Dumathoin. When asked about the shop's enigmatic name, owner Nora Ketterling offered no explanation to inquirers, responding only with a knowing smile.
