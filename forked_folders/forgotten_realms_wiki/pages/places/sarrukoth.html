Sar'Rukoth, known to outsiders as the Ruins of the Serpent Kings, was the capital of Okoth.

Description

Appearing outwardly as a sprawling and half-buried ruin of low walls and crumbling monuments, a small central island was secretly inhabited and served as the seat of power for Okoth. Underground, the city was connected by narrow crawl spaces and corridors that were dark and humid, and further down were catacombs, grottos, and underground pools of salt water.

Beyond this central portion that comprised the inhabited capital, the broader Ruins of the Serpent Kings sprawled across a wide area and were rumored to conceal vast wealth.

Geography

It sat on a low hill on the edge of the Lake of Salt. During the wetter time of the year, the water level rose, flooding the catacombs and turned the hill into an island.

Government

The city was led by a council of eighteen sarrukh led by a khuzdar, or "imperator". Each councilor held a unique title describing their expertise and was the leader of their respective clans. During the mid-to-late 14 century DR, they were led by a priest named Pil'it'ith, who was lord of Clan Sar'Ruuss. As of , the khuzdar was Ziss'thiss, whose influence was spread across Faerûn.

Defenses

The secrecy of the city was maintained by a magical field that filled intruders with dread, causing all but the bravest interlopers to leave in fear.

History

Okoth was the first of the ancient sarrukh realms (founded circa ), and Sar'Rukoth had been its ancient capital. When the realm went into decline, the sarrukh of Okoth retreated into Sar'Rukoth.

Following the Time of Troubles (), the inhabitants turned away from their patron god, Sseth, as he had grown quiet. While serpentkind across Faerûn sought to reawaken their god, the inhabitants of Sar'Rukoth instead venerated the god Set under the guidance of Pil'it'ith, who in truth had struck a deal with Set to bind Sseth in eternal slumber. This soon invited reprisals by the khaastas, and then during the events of the Spellplague ( - ), all of Okoth came under attack by serpentfolk seeking to stamp out its heresy. Pil'it'ith and his followers were slain, and not long after, Sseth reawakened and Set began to fade.

As of , the sarrukh of Sar'Rukoth continued to bide their time and build up their strength away from the prying eyes of the outside world.

Notable Locations

  • The crumbling ruins included a series of new, unlit domes of the late 15 century DR. Each dome contained one of the eighteen sarrukh councilors and all of that sarrukh's servants, protectors, advisers, and entertainers, most of them being yuan-ti. They were connected by a system of tunnels that passed through various salt lakes, caves, and sarrukh tombs.
  • A pyramid occupied by wereserpent and human cultists was notable for its dark twin in the Shadowfell.
  • The Vaults of Record were a long-since plundered area of the Ruins of the Serpent Kings near the village of Buldamar.

Inhabitants

Sar'Rukoth had long been home to the sterile sarrukh and their yuan-ti servants. Following the city's alliance to the church of Set, wereserpents came into existence and settled in the city, and a contingent of werecrocodiles immigrated to the city on the orders of Set's ally, Sebek.
