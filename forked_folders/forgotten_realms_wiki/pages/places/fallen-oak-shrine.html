Fallen Oak Shrine was a woodland site found in the Lost Souls' Swamp in the King's Forest of Cormyr.

Location

The shrine was in the midst of the cursed Lost Souls' Swamp, an at-best dismal patch of waterlogged woodlands, pools, and rock formations located on the northern edge of the King's Forest.

Description

The shrine, such as it was, was a set of partially submerged ancient megalithic ruins and shallow caves scattered through the swamp, and earned its name from the hollow trunk of an immense fallen oak tree that served as a bridge and tunnel from the rest of the swamp. The path to the fallen oak was marked by glowing posts set up by an enterprising wizard in order to make the journey safer.

History

Following the Shadovar invasion of Wheloon in the mid-1480s DR, Oriphaun Huntsilver scattered pieces of the Abyssal Planus Mechanus Nether Scroll along the High Road of Cormyr, hoping to confuse the Shadovar pursuit and give the Harpers time to catch up. He left one such piece in the care of the esteemed hermit Seamus, who kept it well hidden in the ruins—only for the artifact's emanations to instead attract the medusa Sshaastess. She chased him off and took the Scroll fragment, intent on studying and obtaining the powers within.

Not too long later, her new lair was well-decorated with the petrified forms of people and woodland creatures, and defended by her subservient earth elementals, crazed wolves, and maddened dryads. Her reign of localized terror ended when Harper reinforcements arrived and found Seamus, fought through her minions, and slew her. The Nether Scroll piece was found in the shallow cave where she stored her treasures (alongside the ironwood khopesh) and removed to a safer location before something worse showed up.
