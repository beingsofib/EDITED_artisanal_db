Alaron, also known as Westshae was the most populous island in the Moonshae Isles.

Geography

The island was roughly divided in half by the Fairheight Mountains, which trapped rains and clouds on the northern side of the island as they blew in from the Trackless Sea, and shielded the southern side from the ocean harsh storms. The southern part of Alaron was further moderated by the currents of the Sea of Swords, and was a fertile land of grassy low hills and dales ideal for farming. The northern half was rocky and steep, save for some grassy hills in the central region, with its weather being mostly dictated by the stormy seas. The largest city on the island was Caer Callidyrr, located on the northern edge of Dernall Forest.

Government

Callidyrr

The southern half of the island traditionally comprised the Ffolk Kingdom of Callidyrr, ruled from the capital city of Caer Callidyrr. With the rise of High King Tristan Kendrick in the mid–14 century DR, Caer Callidyrr became the capital of all Ffolk realms in the Moonshaes, and with the rise of High Queen Alicia Kendrick, it became the capital of the United Moonshae Isles in the . Alaron was thus considered the most powerful island prior to the Spellplague. However, with the decline of the unified kingdom, the Kendricks came to be kings and queens of only Alaron as of the late 15 century DR. The High King at this time was Derid Kendrick. The castle from which the Kendrick family ruled, the Palace of the High King, was built by the very first High King of Moonshae, Cymrych Hugh.

Gnarhelm

The northern half of the island comprised the Northlander kingdom of Gnarhelm, traditionally ruled from the city of Gnarhelm. As of the late 15 century DR, even as their kin in Norland had declared independence from the High King, Gnarhelm remained under Kendrick rule, its culture having become a blend of Northlander and Ffolk and its inhabitants coming to see themselves as Moonshavians rather than as Northlanders. The combined might of Callidyrr and Gnarhelm made Alaron effectively the most powerful kingdom in the Moonshae Isles.

History

The island was once populated only by the Llewyrr. When humans first migrated to the Moonshaes, the Llewyrr were pushed into the wilder parts of the Isles.

As the island became beset by threats in the late 15 century DR, notably unseelie fey, lycanthropes, and Amnian mercenaries, many of Alaron's inhabitants were forced to flee their villages and travel to the relative safety of Caer Callidyrr.

Inhabitants

The majority of the Moonshaes' human population lived on Alaron. The southern half of the island was traditionally home to the Ffolk while the northern half of the island was home of the Northlander kingdom of Gnarhelm, and the two realms had little in common. As these two cultures merged in the 15 century DR following the unification of the Moonshae realms, they came to share many traditions as well as a strong respect for both the land and the sea.

As of the late 15 century DR, Dernall Forest teemed with goblins, werewolves, fomorians, and other evil fey.

The Fairheight Mountains were home to the dwarven clans Rustfire and Rookoath, who were at war with the dark fey and a shadow dragon during the late 15 century DR.

As of the mid-to-late 14 century DR, the bronze dragon Nymmurh laired in the most inaccessible peaks of the island within a maze of caverns known as "the Pit". The white dragon Arveiaturace was known to sometimes stopover in northern Alaron while hunting sailors.

Unicorns lived in the isolated wilderness of the island.

Notable Locations

  • Caer Callidyrr, the capital and most populous city.
  • Gnarhelm City, the largest city in the northern realm of Gnarhelm.
  • Kythyss, a small city of fishermen and woodcutters that was a hotbed of intrigue as of 1479 DR. When the High King was unable to protect the residents, they hired mercenaries in league with Amn, meaning the Amnians had effective control over the city.
