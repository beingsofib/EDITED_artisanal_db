The Last Head was a cape found on the island of Moray within the Moonshae Isles.

Geography

The cape was located at the southern tip of Moray, jutting south from the Orcskull Mountains for into the Trackless Sea where the Strait of the Leviathan began. Even so, it was often overcast and rainy.

The peninsula's coast was marked by dreary beaches and the inland was covered in foreboding woodland. The area was dominated by a wide hill at its center with a small clearing at its top. The Ossian Reefs curved around the waters south of Last Head,

Flora & Fauna

The wilderness was home to dangerous wildlife, including animals and creatures which were drawn to the scent of blood.

History

Inhabitants of Last Head long had to contend with the threat of orcs from the nearby mountains, and more than one cantrev had been wiped out by their raids.

In the , a warband of the Black Blood tribe led by Bloodeye seized the lighthouse and then moved to attack Langmuir. Three days later, without the lighthouse as a beacon, a longship called The Lightning wrecked upon the eastern shore of Last Head, causing its cargo (mostly consisting of dangerous alchemical supplies) to become scattered upon the beach.

Notable Locations

During the height of the Kingdom of Moray in the mid–14 century DR, the gentler weather of the region made Last Head home to a few small Ffolk settlements, mostly on the eastern coast where they lived on the bounty of the sea. These were connected to the rest of the kingdom via a handful of passes through the Orcskulls.

As of the late 15 century DR, notable locations included:

  • Langmuir, a port village on the eastern coast that served as a hub for mining in the southern Orcskulls.
  • A lighthouse located on the eastern side of the cape's tip, which served to help ships avoid the perilous rocks around Moray.
