The Fool's Altar was a small shrine dedicated to Finder Wyvernspur, the Nameless Bard, located in the Lower City of Baldur's Gate in the 15 century DR.

Location

The Fool's Altar stood just south of Ascloakan's Fine Inn, along Hammaskarl's Ride, a street that began at the city's easternmost gate—the Basilisk Gate—and curved south then ran north

History

The shrine was tended by a finhead saurial cleric named Aszloakh the Hooded in the late 15 century DR, who was known for his patience, calm, and cynical good humor.
