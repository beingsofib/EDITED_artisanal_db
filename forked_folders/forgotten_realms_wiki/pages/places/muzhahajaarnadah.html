Muzhahajaarnadah

Muzhahajaarnadah (loosely translates from Alzhedo as "the city of shadow"), it functionally operated as its own part of the city.

Structure

The depths and labyrinths of the Muzhahajaarnadah were nearly immeasurable, ranging in different places from depths of to nearly beneath the surface. It was composed of all manner of caverns, rebuild atop the old whenever Calimport suffered great destructive events, rather than clearing away the rubble.

The oldest parts of the Muzad, its very foundations, were made up of the crumbling sewers and basements of ancient Coramshan.

Upper Muzad

The uppermost layer of the Muzhahajaarnadah was composed of the original streets of Calimport's three richest and central wards – the Grand, Jewel, and Palace wards – which had been paved over with the intention of providing a larger space, out of sight, for the slaves and servants of the city's upper classes. It was accessible through gates that opened into the Dock Ward and other outer wards. While some light came in through these gates and streamed down through the stairwells up to the elevated streets, illumination was otherwise provided by torches.

The "Upper Muzad", as it came to be known by many, largely operated as an ordinary part of Calimport.

Sewers

Part of the undercity was composed of the two separate systems which were in part the repurposed halls of an ancient svirfneblin city. The older, defunct system was known as the "Lost Sewers". Its abandoned tunnels, connected to many ancient buried cellars, were often partially collapsed, destroyed, or blocked in places. Over time, the leaders of the wererats that came to inhabit the Lost Sewers would set about creating connections between the Lost Sewers and the city's functioning sewer system.

The passages of the newer, functioning sewer system were denoted as being either one of two types based on their size, primary and secondary. Primary tunnels were the more easily navigable of the city, measuring across and had wide walkways on each side. Additionally, stepping stones over the sewage could be found every . Secondary tunnels were about half as wide, with pathways only on one side. The sewers also had many other pipes, ranging in diameter from , though these were in no way mapped.

Much of the city's waste entered the sewers through grates and eventually emptied out into the Shining Sea, east of the harbor proper. capped by covers known as "manholes".

Surface Shafts

  • Buildings classified as type B buildings commonly had two or more cellars that connected to Calimport's sewers or at times even further down into the Muzad.
  • Open shafts and stairs leading down into the depths of the Muzad could be found at certain points near the western edges of the Upper Muzad, as well as around the dense stone pillars beneath the Pasha's Palace.
  • The Irataq Sabban in the Crypt Ward had a number of empty tombs that were secretly surface accesses to the undercity.
  • Katars High: A tavern in the Suq Drudach whose cellars led into the Muzad. The owner granted many a slave the opportunity to escape to the hills west of Calimport by traversing the treacherous undercity.
  • The Pasha's Sabban, which had secret passages within its walls leading to Qysaghanni Fortress, potentially had secret passageways down into the Muzad.
  • The Sahalarka, guildhall of the Fellowship Nykkar, had a secret tunnel to the Muzad in its cellar that led to a secret Kelemvorite shrine.
  • The Sultry Siren: An opulent festhall in the Gagi Drudach that had secret links to the Muzad. On many ocassions these tunnels saved the lives of pashas and sultans, allowing them escape from traps by rival houses and pashas.
  • Temples of Shar: Scattered across the city were five "Temples of Shar", in actuality mere guarded shrines that granted access to Calimport Muzad and the true Temple of Old Night. These were found in the following sectors of Calimport: the Caravans Ward's Pahlemm Sabban, the Crypt Ward's Kirruk Sabban, the Hook Ward's Osiir Sabban, the Khanduq Ward's Sholeh Sabban, and the Quill Ward's Jhaapir Sabban.
  • The Vihaddah Semohl: A festhall in the Taorahl Drudach, had a cellar that linked up to a shrine of Iyachtu Xvim in the undercity.

Dungeon Ecology

The Muzad was inhabited by all manner of creatures and monsters, ranging from sentient clouds of gas to rats and other feral vermin that roamed the middle and lower levels of the undercity.

Groups of Interest

Church of Shar: The largest power in the Muzhahajaarnadah. It absolutely dominated a third of all territory in the eastern portion of the undercity, the lowest third of Dark Calimport, including the majority of its extant buildings and streets. Their dominance was maintained by their veritable army of clergymen, who regularly patrolled the undercity's dark corridors. The church not only directly controlled some of Calimport's pashas and vizars, but manipulated numerous illicit businesses that operated in the undercity. However, contrary to their own boastful claims, they did not have any control over the undercity's Dark Bazaars.

Guild of Plumbers and Sewer-Workers: A Calimport guild dedicated to the maintenance and upkeep of the municipal sewers.

Nadhari: An anti-slavery movement that operated to some degree in the Upper Muzad.

Night Parade: The Muzhahajaarnadah was once home to one of the largest colonies of this monstrous group, numbering around 6,000 individuals by 1359 DR. That year their population was drastically decimated by the Harpers in event later known as the Nightcleansing. Myrmeen Lhal led the Harpers in exposing the Night Parade infestation in Calimport and sent the Calishites into a frenzy. Only 75 members of the colony would survive the incident.

Tuamir's guild: A thieves' guild based in the Muzad in the 11 century DR. Under the Djenispool Dynasty, much of the guild's power base was sundered after its hidden bases were destroyed by the pashas collapsing parts of the overhead Kaval Sabban down into the Muzad.

Twisted Rune: This cabal had some presence in the Muzad due to their agent Voraya, a lamia noble.

Wererats' Guild: A guild composed primarily of wererats, alongside other types of lycanthropes. It largely dominated the Lost Sewers of the undercity and vied to usurp the thieves' guilds that were controlled from the surface.

Trade

Much of the illicit activity that went on throughout Calimport was in one way or another linked to the Muzhahajaarnadah.

In the mid–14 century DR, a large portion of the illicit trade in the Muzad was dominated by Sultan Faruk el Pesarkhal, the twelfth son of Syl-Pasha Ralan el Pesarkhal. And Pasha Daud el Vehmet was involved in the black market trade of potions and spell components.

One of the most coveted hubs for trading was the Dark Bazaars, wandering arcane bazaars often found on the upper levels of the Muzad. The only way to access them was to be invited by their mysterious caretakers, the Gray Callers. These gray-robed figures would show up seemingly at random, across both Calimport and Calimport Below, to invite individuals to the bazaars. This occurred no less than once a month, but no more than once a tenday. If someone accepted their beckoning, the Gray Caller would soon lead them down a path that opened into the Dark Bazaar. Those who attempted to teleport to a Dark Bazaar would find themselves amidst nothing but mists and shadows, filled with whispering far off voices.

Within the Dark Bazaars, assassins and thieves mingled amongst all manner of creatures. The few written accounts of the bazaars recount such varied visitors as archmages, dragons, kobolds, nishruu, nyth, and even tanar'ri lords. Attendees traded in foul magic, forbidden lore, illicit secrets, lost knowledge, and even magic items such as divination spell scrolls, bottles of dream vapor, and rare elven kiira. One of the most common methods of trade in lore and secrets in the bazaars was that of tales.

The one cardinal rule for trade in the Dark Bazaars was "Ask or speak wisely, but only spare a secret or knowledge equal to that which you seek". Attendees could only ever trade one secret for another, and once their secret was given, regardless of whether or not it was understood by the recipient, they would suddenly disappear, dissipating into gray mist and returning back to where they had first met the Gray Caller who had beckoned them into the bazaar, as if it all had never happened.

Due to the sensitive nature of secret trading, scrying was expressly prohibited outside of a relevant trading situation. If it or violence was brought to bear within the Dark Bazaars, the offending party would be similarly disappeared.

Inhabitants

The Muzhahajaarnadah was inhabited by a wide array of people, from simple outcasts to priests of god darks, and albino humans that had adapted to life below ground. The demonym for these various inhabitants of the Muzad was "Muzaddi".

The wererat population of the Muzad numbered no less than 500 in total by 1370 DR. They largely inhabited the Lost Sewers, divided into seven separate consortiums.

One group of note in the mid–to–late 14 century DR was a tribe of kobolds, led by a Chieftain Skax. They had been moved to Calimshan from the Clawrift in the Northdark in 1364 DR by the Bregan D'aerthe

Notable Locations

Crypt of Felin: The lost crypt of Felin yn Maram yn Ali el Samesaj, who was buried here in secrecy beneath the Sholeh Sabban in -496 DR. Many believed it held great riches and even greater magic and that it was somehow linked to a minaret called The Protector's Tower.

Samesaj Gate: A fabled enchanted archway that plummeted down into the Muzad in -649 DR, beneath what later became known as Vahlen Sabban, completely intact.

Temple of Old Night: A gargantuan temple on the lowest level of the Muzad. Once dedicated to Tyche, This included the dungeons and dark areas below Caravans Ward, Hammer Ward, Maker's Ward, Quill Ward, and Wizard Ward, as well as some of the sections of Calimport Muzad that were beneath Hook Ward and Khanduq Ward. Reputation

Muzhahajaarnadah was quite well known to the people of Calimport, its reputation comparable to that of Waterdhavians and Undermountain. Likewise, the existence of the Temple of Old Night was common knowledge, though not the true extent of its reach. However, few knew of the Muzad from actual experience. The various rumors and surrounding it were more often than not the products of fear and nightmares than truth. The multitude of mysteries surrounding the undercity's dark, hidden depths was comparable not only to Undermountain, but to ruined wonders of Myth Drannor and Shoonach. Likewise, the existence of the Temple of Old Night was common knowledge, though not the true extent of its reach.

History

In the , the red dragon Ylveraasahlisar attacks Calimport out of a desire to avenge one of her offspring, destroying two-thirds of the city in the process. The Rose Dragon wrested control of the city from the Raor bakkal and demanded immediate reconstruction efforts. Not wanting to displease their new draconic ruler, the Calishite builders chose to smooth out the rubble and build

atop the old structures, consequently creating much of the Muzhahajaarnadah.

In , the Calimshan Empire became embroiled in the first of a series of conflicts with the drow of Guallidurth, the drow had taken over one quarter of Calimport. In a last ditch effort to save the Empire from the conquering drow, a combined force of priests and wizards destroyed large swathes of the city, burying the drows' temples and partially subterranean villas. Some of their buildings ended up relatively intact amidst their burial. The wizards responsible would then help to rebuild Calimport, making certain to seal off any connections between the city and Underdark through means of stone and new buildings. However, within a decade the drow of Old Shanatar managed to reopen those old passages and reestablish their hold on the Muzad.

In the , the Sharrans establish themselves in the Temple of Old Night after they destroyed their surface temple, the Basilica of Night, in a conflict with Talonites.

Sometime during the Eye Tyrant Wars, one of the early attempts by the beholders to invade Calimport was done through the Muzad.

In the , with the death of Caleph Hazamir al Aktorral the Shoon Imperium neglects Calimport, leaving numerous buildings in the city to deteriorate. Calimport begins to be referred to as the "Old City", as many flock to the Muzhahajaarnadah, expanding both its size and population. During this time the undercity also saw a rise in its monstrous population, as the Old City had fewer garrisons.

In the , the ruler of the Shoon Imperium, Shoon VII, took drastic measures in ridding Ankhapur, Calimport, Innarlith, and Ithmong of crime. He ordered oil to be poured into their sewers, causing the Great Fires that destroyed the four cities nearly completely, with the death toll reaching over three million people.

Around the Seventh Age of Calimshan, a new sewer system was constructed beneath Calimport.

In the , the self-proclaimed Syl-Pasha Bollus el Kahdan found his efforts to rebuild Calimport repeatedly interfered with by powers of the undercity. He would retaliate by flooding the ancient sewers and buried avenues of Calimport Below with oil and igniting it, an event later known as the Deepblaze. However, when the fumes and heat died down, the undercity was more secure than ever before and better able to access the city above.

In the , baseless rumors spread around the Lands of Intrigue that some 80,000 drow were lurking in the Forest of Mir. This stirred up panic in both Calimshan and Tethyr. In Calimport, this lead to people venturing into the Muzad with the aiming of murdering any drow they came across.

In 1357 DR, the Companions of the Hall traveled all the way from the Sword Coast to Calimport in effort to rescue their friend Regis from Pasha Pook and his guild of thieves. In the month of Eleasias they would reach the city and travel into the sector of Market Quarter that would later become Khanduq Ward. There they rested at the Spitting Camel Inn, before making their way into the sewers of Calimport, only to find themselves facing off against its wererat denizens.

In the , attempts by powers within the Calishite government to get involved with underworld politics leads to the Darkstalker Wars. Amidst the chaos of this underworld conflict, the magic unleashed by Jarlaxle and others caused a few buildings in the Sholeh Sabban to crumbled down into the Muzad.

In the , Artemis Entreri, with the help of Jarlaxle and the Bregan D'aerthe, assaulted the palace of House Basadoni from below. They entered through the sewers with a force of forty drow and hundreds of kobolds that the Bregan D'aerthe had brought with them from Clawrift.

In the , the Trades Conflagration engulfed half of the Grand Ward, destroying the Marekh Sabban and consequently numerous areas of the Upper Muzad that were beneath it. The fires also struck the Cajaan Sabban, where the four marble khanduqs of the Cajaan Market burned to the ground and the vast sums of gold stored within them melted, pouring down into the Muzad to be collected by the undercity's denizens of dubious repute. This gold amounted to 10,000 gp and 2,500 trade bars.
