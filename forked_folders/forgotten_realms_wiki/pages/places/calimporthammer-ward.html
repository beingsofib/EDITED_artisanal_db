Hammer Ward was a ward of Calimport dedicated to all things metallurgy—armorsmiths, blacksmiths, and weaponsmiths—and was one of the city's eight Outer Wards. Whilst these three types of smiths could be found to varying degrees throughout the ward, each were typically sequestered to a dedicated sabban within its walls.

Location

This ward was located in the northeastern corner of Calimport. Hammer Ward was bordered to the west by Khanduq Ward's Kaval and Sholeh Sabbans. To the south it was bordered by the Adlel Sabban of Crypt Ward and the Nasim Sabban of Quill Ward.

Description

Throughout the ward one could hear the clammor of hammers striking anvils at all hours of the day, as well as often into the night.

History

Prior to Syl-Pasha Ralan el Pesarkhal's establishment of wards as the city's largest sectors in the , Hammer Ward was but part of what was known as the Craft Quarter.

In the , Syl-Pasha Pesarkhal broke ground on a long-term construction project, a defensive barrier he dubbed the Pesarkhal Wall, that would eventually encompass all of the Central Wards. In preparation for its eventual construction along Quill Ward, the Guild of Skilled Smiths and Metalworkers decided to demarcate its future placement by erecting a fence of tall, barbed metal spears along the sabban wall that separated These spears did nothing to obstruct passage along the sabban byways, but did make it very difficult to vault across into Nasim Sabban.

Government

In the 14th century DR, the ruler of Hammer Ward was Sultan Harel yn Rafam el Qardeen. Formerly a humble blacksmith, the man was bestowed this position by the Syl-Pasha after he inadvertently saved El Pesarkhal's life during the Darkstalker Wars.

Sabbans

The Hammer Ward was divided into three sabbans and twelve drudachs.

Amyran Sabban

Also known as the "Hammer Sabban", this sector of Hammer Ward was taken up by over fifty coarse smithies and workshops, producing common metalwork such as horseshoes and wheelrims. Here hardworking, commoner blacksmiths operated, who would say go went against the grain of the common Calishite work ethic.

Competition was fierce in this sabban, though there was always enough work to go around. There was also a strong sense of community amongst its inhabitants, and sharing the goal of keeping their sabban free of the city's rampant corruption. This in turn made it a very safe place to live amongst Calimport's many sabbans.

The sabban mark of Amyran was a smith's claw hammer.

Sjûl Sabban

Also known as the "Anvil Sabban", for it was name was the Alzhedo word for anvil, this sector of Hammer Ward was dedicated to weaponsmiths. Under government ruling, weapon-makers needed to live closer to the heart of the city, so as to ensure that their wares could easily be put to use in defending the richer wards of Calimport.

Additionally, the True and Skilled Guild of Weapon-Makers ensured that all who operated here had to meet minimum standards of quality and were only permitted to sell two types of weapons in their shop, including such things as scimitars and spears. The latter restriction was put in place so as to ensure that no one weaponsmith could totally dominate Calimport's markets.

The sabban mark of Sjûl depicted an anvil in a circular field. It was divided into four drudachs—Nakhla Drudach, Roba Drudach, Sareef Drudach, and the Qima Drudach.

Tyrbos Sabban

Also known as the "Ringing Step Sabban", was the primary home of skilled armorers and shield-makers, who much like the weapon-makers were required to live closer to the heart of the city. This sector of Hammer Ward was also home to blacksmiths of less larger metal wares. The bulk of their trade was in architectural pieces, such as the metal shingling that graced domes and minarets, door hinges, portcullises, and the grills and screens used in windows.

Due to the space needed for such smiths and the size of some of their products, Tyrbos Sabban boasted buildings that were larger than the norm, though it had fewer buildings overall. The majority of these structures were warehouses and three-story tall buildings. The latter of which were altered in their interior, making them merely two floor buildings, creating space on their ground floors for larger foundries and forges.

The name of the sabban was derived from the two tyrant Tyrbos pashas of the Second Age of Calimshan and its sabban mark was their sigil. This consisted of a chevron-shaped shield, placed off-center, and overlapping a dark circle. It was divided into four drudachs—Adifa Drudach, Harar Drudach, Maadan Drudach and the Zhakush Drudach.
