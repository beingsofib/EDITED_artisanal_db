Khanduq Ward was a mixed-focused sector of Calimport and one of the city's eight Outer Wards.

Description

Khanduq Ward got its name from the abundance of khanduqs and marketplaces scattered throughout it.

It once had a connection to Quill Ward through the Hapij Sabban, however, at somepoint it was sealed shut.

Government

In the 14th century DR, the Sultan of Khanduq Ward was Daud yn Daud el Vehmet. He was a rather young ruler, barely past 20 years of age in 1370 DR, and was also the pasha of the Spicemonger Fellowship.

Trade

By the 14th century DR, much of the business and trade that Khanduq Ward had once been privy to had shifted westward into the adjacent Emerald Ward.

Sabbans

Khanduq Ward was divided into three sabbans and ten drudachs. These sabbans were unofficially "segregated" into sectors that focused on business, entertainment, and residence respectively.

Kaval Sabban

Also known as "Tuamir's Sabban" or "the Sabbazaar", was the trade focused sector of Khanduq Ward. Residential buildings were absent in this sabban, for khanduqs occupied more than half of the available land, with the rest taken up by the khanduqs' street bazaars. At sunset all within Kaval Sabban would be cleared out and its gates would be locked shut. During the night a contingent of twelve amlakkars were stationed along its walls at night, whilst another contingent of twelve would patrol the interior for rogues, eventually swapping posts with the wall-guards.

Its colloquial nickname was in reference to Tuamir, an infamous rogue back in the 11 century DR who ran a far reaching thieves' guild from down in Calimport Muzad.

The sabban mark of Kaval was the old symbol of Tuamir, an eye an bisected by a vertical, downward facing arrow.

Notable Locations

Khanduq of the Coinmother: A marketplace and the city's foremost temple of Waukeen, Its high priest in the 14 century DR was Pecal el Saiduwm, the Druzir of Syral Drudach.
Location: Syral Drudach

Taanith's Khanduq: A khanduq and guildhall that was shared by the Esteemed Guild of Builders and the Circle of Architects, Engineers, and Planners.
Location: Rhamal Drudach

The Treant's Khanduq: The guildhall of the Fine Wood Carvers Order. It stood out from many other buildings in the sector due to being made entirely out of wood.
Location: Aruv Drudach

Naiid Sabban

Also known as "Pomartal's Sabban", was the entertainment focused sector of Khanduq Ward. It was replete with clusters of festhalls, inns, taverns, and gambling establishments.

Its colloquial nickname was in reference to Pomartal, a balak who was sworn enemies with Tuamir back when it was a more lawful sabban. Ever since his death to assassins one night in the , Pomartal's ghost walked the byways of Naiid Sabban's walls, punishing any criminals he caught in the act.

The mark of this sabban was the ancient signet of Pomartal, scales of justice balanced upon the upper tip of a lightning bolt. In mockery of the lawfulness that the ward once stood for, members of the entertainment industries and their clients marred and graffiti this mark in many places. The one exception to this was an area known as Pomartal's Walk.

Naaid Sabban was divided into three drudachs—Balamhir Drudach, Tanradh Drudach, and Yuam Drudach.

Notable Locations

Miatarra: The guildhall of the Spicemonger Fellowship.
Location: Tanradh Drudach

The Minarets Muzhadahr: A luxurious temple that was once dedicated to Bane. Following the Time of Troubles, it became a house of Cyric worship.

Sholeh Sabban

Also known as "Felin's Sabban", was the residential sector of Khanduq Ward. It was a horribly overcrowded place, Its name was likely derived from the Sholeh Dynasty.

What would become known as Sholeh Sabban was originally built in the Forth Age of Calimshan, early into the Vihad Dynasty. During that time, spanning the course of five Vihad pashas, the area was protected by a powerful mage known as Felin. Under his vigilant watch, harm never befell the area's structures nor its people. After his death and covert burial in Calimport Muzad in -496 DR, people would continue to say they felt his protective, ghostly presence. And indeed, ever since that time, Sholeh Sabban was never once devastated by fires.

The area would later gain a reputation as the "Thieves' Quarter" of Calimport, beginning around the 9 century DR, and this would be the state of things for over five centuries. The underworld presence in Sholeh disintegrated as the Djenispool Dynasty fell and the underworld was fractured by the chaos of the Darkstalker Wars. Ever since that conflict, the ward had been free of thieves' guilds.

The sabban mark of Sholeh was modeled after the mage sigil of the ancient wizard Felin, being composed of an hourglass that was centered over a quintet of five-pointed stars.

Notable Locations

Pook's Palace: The home of the infamous Pasha Pook, a crimelord who used to have wide-reaching influence over the assassins' and thieves' guilds of Calimport, as well as the guildhall of the Pook guild. After Pook's criminal enterprise fell apart in 1357 DR, Sabbalad Nur yn Aban el Esarn took up residence in the structure and allowed it to fall into a state of partial disrepair.
Location: Sarkh Drudach

The Protector's Tower: An ancient wizard tower that had once belonged to the renowned Felin. It was magically protected structure that managed to keep out all trespassers over the centuries, leaving it a mystery as to what treasures it held inside.
Location: Denos Drudach

Rogues Circle: An open cul-de-sac, at the end of which lied Pook's Palace.

The Spitting Camel: A low-class tavern
Location: Eachid Drudach

Temple of Old Night: A nondescript shrine to the goddess Shar. It was one of five such buildings across Calimport that provided entrance to the Muzad and the true Temple of Old Night.

History

The area was formerly part of what was known as the Market Quarter, before Syl-Pasha Ralan el Pesarkhal established the system of wards in the ,

Sometime after the Djenispool regime of Calimshan was established in 1187 DR, the pashas eradicated the subterranean power base of Tuamir's guild by collapsing parts of Kaval Sabban atop it. They would go on to rebuild that portion of the sabban atop those ruins.

In 1357 DR, the Companions of the Hall traveled all the way from the Sword Coast to Calimport in effort to rescue their friend Regis from Pasha Pook and his guild of thieves. In the month of Eleasias they would reach the city and travel into the sector of Market Quarter that would later become Khanduq Ward. There they rested until night at the Spitting Camel Inn, making note of how the cul-de-sac it was on, Rogues Circle, became even more packed with activity. At night they went out and the barbarian Wulfgar pried open one of the many iron grates that lined the street, making their way down into the sewers of Calimport, in hopes of sneaking into Pook's Palace. Later on, after they had brought an end to Pasha Pook, Drizzt Do'Urden would look out upon the nightly streets of the area and remark to himself how things seemed suspiciously quiter, a sense of growing anticipation at the conflict to come over the power vacuum he left.

During the Darkstalker Wars of 1359 DR, the streets of Sholeh Sabban were faced with the destruction of a few buildings and over 2,000 deaths.

In the , Calimport was wracked by the Trades Conflagration. Whilst an outstanding portion of damages would be suffered by Emerald Ward, much of the western quarter of Naiid Sabban would also be hit by the blaze. However, unlike Emerald Ward, the shells of much of Naaid's buildings and older khanduqs would be left intact. Over the next year, the sabban would be met with a flurry of bidding wars over real estate, as many flocked to gain ownership of one of the partially built homes and businesses that abutted Emerald Ward.
