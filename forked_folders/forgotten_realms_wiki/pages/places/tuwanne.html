Tuwanne was a Nubari village located close to the edge of the Huroola tribe's territory, bordering the slopes of the Fire Mountain.

History

In the late 14 century DR, Tuwanne was closely allied with its neighbor – the village of Valapana, only six hours trek through the jungles to the very edge of the Fire Mountain. Tuwanne aided Valapana with its patrols and stopping garuda attacks on the villagers. In the Year of Sorrow and Hope, Tuwanne's neighbor became a hunting target for a swam of little thunder garuda that developed a taste for Nubari flesh.

Notable Inhabitants

  • Alabbe, a female Huroola warrior who patrolled Tuwanne and Valapana territories in the late 14 century DR.
