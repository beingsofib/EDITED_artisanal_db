Myrloch

The Myrloch, also known as Lake Myr, It was the namesake of the Myrloch Vale and the subject of many Ffolk songs.

Description

The lake was deep, cold, clear, and blue.

Geography

The Myrloch dominated the south of Myrloch Vale in central Gwynneth. The lake was easily the largest freshwater body of water found within the Moonshae Isles, covering approximately . The Myrloch filled the basin between the Cambro Mountains in the east with the Highlands to the south and west.

History

In ancient times, the Myrloch was the heart of the fey realm of Synnoria until it retreated into the Feywild circa . The capital city of Karador was located on the lake, and it sank beneath the waves when the kingdom vanished. Only legends remained of this ancient fey kingdom as of the 14 century DR, and no clues of the city could be gleaned from the lake's surface.

At some point following their arrival in the Moonshaes in the 3 century DR, However, long after their departure, a handful of their crannógs continued to be maintained by nereids in Eldath's service.

The city of Karador reemerged from the lake in the , heralding the return of Sarifal.

Notable Locations

  • Karador, which sat at the center of the lake.
  • Sacred Grove of the Earthmother, which lay on the eastern shoreline.
