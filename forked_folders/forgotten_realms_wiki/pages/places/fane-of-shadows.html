The Fane of Shadows was a temple dedicated to various gods of shadows that traveled between worlds, planes, and times via the Shadow Weave. It was said to have names in many different languages; it was also known as the Temple of Night and the Umbral Shrine.

Location

Although the Fane of Shadow between different worlds, planes, and times, it was anchored to several locations, one of them being the Shadow Keep in the Shadowfell, so it returned there now and then, appearing from out of the darkness and becoming part of the keep once more.

On Toril, it appeared within the Lightless Lake, also called the Moonmere, a place in the Gulthmere Forest not far from Starmantle on the Dragon Coast. However, it only materialized on certain dates

Upon its arrival on Toril, the Fane appeared within the depths of the Lightless Lake in an ocher light that spread diffusely through the water.

Structure

The entirety of the temple was situated on an invisible platform encapsulated within a dome of air. The architecture included black-veined marble columns, graceful arches, and thick pillars.

Defenses

The Fane of Shadows was guarded by a host of shadows inside the courtyard area. They were only hostile to those not subservient to shadow, that is, those devoted to a god or goddess of shadow or being adept in the Shadow Weave.

Interior

Courtyard

The courtyard contained an array of hundreds of sculptures, carved of basalt, ebony, marble, or obsidian, all of them depicting the gods of shadow of each world the Fave visited. Many held weapons or treasures. Statues of Toril's deities of night and shadow, Shar and Mask, were among the collection.

The Grand Hall

From the courtyard, doors opened onto a long, wide hallway, called the Grand Hall, which was filled with shadows and bathed in a low, green light.

Inner Sanctum

The hallway ended at a pair of double doors, which led into the inner sanctum. The inner sanctum contained a domed ceiling of black encrusted in gems, representing the night sky of the world in which it then existed. The inner sanctum itself had a polished slate floor inlaid with an amethyst border, and in its center lay a black sphere bordered in purple: Shar's holy symbol.

A horseshoe-shaped altar built from basalt stood in the center of the symbol, under an area of the ceiling of the blackest black, depicting an empty area of the night sky known as Shar's moon. It was underneath this symbol where a host received the transforming energy of the greatest gift offered by the Shadow Weave: transformation into a shade. The floor within this altar was some kind of black crystalline substance.

Black curtains lined the walls of the inner sanctum, as well as a mosaic of the Weave Tap.

Activities

The temple was believed to be a gift from the gods of shadows to their faithful and servants, to serve them as a mobile sanctuary, armory, and stronghold and to turn the mysterious ends of those gods.

History

Although used by many gods, the Fane of Shadows was in truth created by the goddess Shar. In ancient times, she transformed a part of the Shadow Keep into the Fane and set it on its journey.

According to legend, Mask tricked the other gods of darkness so that he could appoint a guardian to the Fane, but none knew this being's name.

In the , the Dark Diviners of Windsong Tower ventured into the Fane of Shadows and recovered the Book of the Black, a tome of dark prophecies. After Shar cursed them for uncovering a weakness that lay in her future, one of the Dark Diviners, Raanaghaun Cormrael, returned to the Fane of Shadows in search of an explanation. He never returned.

In the , the Fane of Shadows was due to reappear in the Lightless Lake on the night of the new moon, Flamerule 16, at midnight. Following behind on a mission to stop them were Erevis Cale, Drasek Riven, Magadon Kest, and Jak Fleet.

Rumors & Legends

The Fane of Shadows was believed to hold great treasures and magical artifacts stolen by Mask from across the multiverse, as well as all kinds of traps and guardians to thwart those who steal it themselves.
