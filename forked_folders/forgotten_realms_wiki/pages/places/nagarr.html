Nagarr, sometimes marked as Kagarra on maps, also named Yacla around the 9th century DR, In 1370 DR, it was a protectorate of Ankhapur.

Geography

The village sat on the southwest bank of the Nagawater upstream from where the Nagaflow began. It was on the fringes of the land between the Nagawater and the Winterwood called the Serpents' Holding. The Ankh Road connected Nagarr with Ankhapur to the southwest, paved since 1370 DR.

Government

The mayor in 1370 DR was Torgis Hatchetson. In reality, the true influences were the merchants along Nagarr's docks and Janol I.

History

Nagarr was a simple farming-fishing village, but by 1370 DR with the rise of Janol I of Ankhapur, major reconstruction and increase in population as a trade transit point gradually expanded it to accommodate trade.

The most common stories were told in the inns and taverns of Nagarr all involved the monstrous and mysterious naga. The locals had no idea that separate species of good, evil and neutral naga all lived in the area (which Orswin failed to do with his attempts) and the consensus was the only good naga was a dead naga.

Armed forces

As of 1370 DR, 375 members of Ankhpur's Watch were permanently loaned to the Mayor of Nagarr.

Organizations

The Red Masks was a fledgling organization headed by Rathan Silverknife who lost in a turf war in Innarlith and ran to hide in Nagarr in 1370 DR.

Notable Inhabitants

  • Alamandra Whitehair, a cleric of Mystra overseeing the House of Mysteries in 1370 DR, who advocated the appreciation of the wonders and beauty of magic and not just its convenience.
  • Cadrin Velaclaok, a cleric of Ilmater running the small Triadic temple Palace of Justice, Duty, and Sufferance in 1370 DR.
  • Cathel deFaux, an elderly cleric of Lathander presiding over the newly founded Shrine to Dawn in 1370 DR, who arrived from outside Nagarr.
  • Jordan Ariadne, a Half-sea elf of the passing sea elf Ariadne. He was a water elementalist disciple of a local non-evil naga. He lived on an anchored sailing vessel with an enclosed cabin and came out to help the farmers with irrigation.
  • Orswin, a sage from the North who advocated the the search and study of all varieties of serpents, was drawn to Nagarr because of the naga.
  • Rathan Silverknife, the founder of the new local Red Masks by 1370 DR.
