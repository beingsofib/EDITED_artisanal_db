The Stonehill Inn was a quaint inn in the village of Phandalin.

Location

The modest inn was located close to the center of town.

Structure

Newly built in the 15 century DR, the inn was a large, two-story building made of unrefined rocks and crudely cut wood.

The upper floor with its guest rooms could be access through two staircases: the one in the courtyard and the one in the common room. A staircase from the common room provided access to the ale cellar. The Stonehill Inn additionally had a larder. The inn's amenities extended to a courtyard containing a privy and stables.

Atmosphere

The villagers of Phandalin, from miners to farmers, enjoyed relaxing in the common room, which became a good place to hear local gossip and stories. The illumination was provided by oil lanterns, which were mounted on the walls or placed upon tables.

Services

The inn provided six rentable rooms on the upper floor, plus alcoholic beverages and food. A bed was five silver pieces a night, and prices were fairly standard for food, ale, and cider, with meals coming to about 1 silver piece.

Staff

The Stonehill Inn was owned by Toblen Stonehill, who operated it alongside his wife Trilena Stonehill and with help from his son Pip Stonehill.

A barmaid called Elsa served drinks at the bar.

Paulina Previne could occasionally be found working as a stable hand at the inn.

History

The Stonehill Inn was constructed upon the ruins of a barrow established by Phandalin's original settlers centuries earlier. Located five feet beneath the floor of the inn's ale cellar lay a stone-lined tomb, the final resting place of a long-deceased human chieftain.

The Stonehills arrived in Phandalin from Triboar early in the construction of the town, and founded the inn around 1490 DR. During the Redbrands' reign of terror throughout Phandalin in 1491 DR, the bandits did not frequent the inn, preferring to drink at the Sleeping Giant a few buildings to the east.

Business continued more-or-less as usual during the rampage of the white dragon Cryovain across the countryside. The inn played host to important visitors during this time, such as Don-Jon Raskin, the man who was in charge of reopening the Mountain's Toe gold mines. The inn's common room also became a favorite haunt for Argus Skeel, a vampire spawn in service to the necromancer Ularan Mortus.

In the early 1490s DR, the Stonehill Inn was assaulted by a swarm of cranium rats. The creatures were pushed by unknown forces to acquire the brightblade interred within the tomb of the human chieftain buried beneath the inn's foundation. The vermin cracked and crunched their way through the inn's furniture and gnawed two horses alive in the courtyard stables. The swarm was stopped by a group of adventurers which included Crantor Thranax, Reizagar Stormhelm, Eirina Illoris, and Merovich Farfoot. The same year, the inn served as the venue for a feast celebrating the return of Mordred Wester, a brother of the local townmaster, Harbin Wester.

By 1496 DR, the inn remained prosperous and largely unchanged. However, at some point the Stonehills had borrowed money from the town banker and former townmaster, Harbin Wester, and he was attempting to leverage the small amount they still owed to take away their business.
