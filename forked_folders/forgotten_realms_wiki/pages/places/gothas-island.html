Gotha's Island was the name given to a small island within the Moonshae Isles on the edge of the Trackless Sea as of the mid-to-late 14 century DR.

Geography

Gotha's Island was one of the northernmost of the Moonshae Isles (discounting the northern parts of the Korinn Archipelago), located north of the Norheim Isles, Grayrock, and Dragonshome.

The island was largely barren and little more than a pile of rocks that sat not far above the waterline. Toward the center was a low hill containing a cave which opened into a natural cavern that connected to a vast network of sea caves. Parts of the island were pastureland suitable for animals to graze.

History

In the , the dracolich Gotha arrived on the island, and swiftly annihilated its inhabitants and destroyed all of its boats in service to the will of his master, Talos. He was soon joined by the sahuagin King Sythissal of Kressilacc.

Inhabitants

Prior to the arrival of Gotha, the island was inhabited by isolated Northlanders who fished and kept sheep. The largest settlement comprised only about a dozen huts. After Gotha's slaughter, he left most of the structures intact (save for those in which families had attempted to hide, which he had incinerated) and threw the corpses of all the humans and livestock into the sea.

Notable Locations

  • Gotha's lair was located in the cave atop the central hill, where the dracolich had excavated to connect it to the sea caves below.
