Nakalpgotak Sea

The Nakalpgotak Sea was a large sea in the Alpuk region of the Great Glacier.

Geography

The Nakalpgotak Sea was a massive inland sea bordered to the north by the mountains of Nakvaligach and the east by the Keryjek Ridge. A network of shallow streams linked the Nakalpgotak Sea with the Lugalpgotak Sea. With a shape similar to a bowl, the Nakalpgotak Sea was long with steep shores, some with a sheer 45° angle. During the warmer months of the year, meltwater from the mountains rushed into the sea, causing the water level to rise significantly.

Flora & Fauna

Due to the low population of humans, the lake was home to a thriving population of polar bears and winter wolves. These predators fed on the large populations of fish, birds, and seals that lived within the sea.

History

In the summer of , an unseasonal heat wave caused a massive flood, inundating dozens of Iulutiun villages that were located near the eastern and northern shores. As a result, thereafter few Iulutiuns would settle near the sea.
