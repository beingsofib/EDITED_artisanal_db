Cantrev Dynnegall was a small village located on the isle of Moray within the Moonshae Isles.

Geography

The village was located in north central Moray along the northeast shore of Lac Dynnegall, The road between these two settlements was never more than a dirt path through the Shannyth River Valley that could take three days to traverse. As of the late 15 century DR, the roads were poorly maintained.

Trade

The village's location on the swampy Lac Dynnegall and near the Breasal Marsh provided it with an excellent source of peat, valued for heating, and of kryne, a nutritious plant. As of the late 15 century DR, soldiers stationed in the port of Caer Moray by High King Derid Kendrick of Callidyrr had the responsibility of making sure trade flowed to and from Dynnegall.

History

Dynnagall was named for a king of the island.

As of the early 14 century DR, Dynnegall was an impoverished and squalid collection of hovels. However, it would grow into a flourishing town as of the collapse of the Kingdom of Moray. By the late 15 century DR, the village had a bloody history, having been completely wiped out several times by monsters. Attacks from the Black Blood Tribe occurred several times per year. Soldiers stationed here often came away with many stories of their heroics (assuming they survived), and were said to say that it was the most dangerous post in the whole of the Moonshaes.

As of the , adventurers were needed to help secure Dynnegall's future, both by securing the road and by helping to construct walls and barracks within the town. The High King's troops continued to struggle to reinforce Dynnagall as late as the .

Inhabitants

The original Ffolk of Dynnegall were proud, highly self-sufficient, and not at all concerned with how their lifestyles might be perceived by outsiders. By the late 15 century DR, the village was begrudgingly populated only by nervous settlers seeking peat and kryne in service to High King Derid.
