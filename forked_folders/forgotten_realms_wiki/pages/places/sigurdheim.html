Sigurdheim was a Northlander village on the island of Norland.

Geography

Sigurdheim was situated in the southeastern area of Norland, in an area surrounded by tall pine forests. The village rested on a small lake, located up a narrow river flowing down from the South Jotunhammer Mountains.

Description

There were twenty longhouses within this village.

Inhabitants

The people that inhabited Sigurdheim were very warlike and suspicious of strangers, keeping the Northlander culture more intact than in other areas of the Moonshae Isles. Besides Northlanders, there were a few Ffolk that lived within the village. However, outside of hunting, the two ethnic groups lived apart from each other and the Ffolk liked raiding less than their Northlander neighbors.

Hunting was a major facet of the village's culture.
