Tent City, also known as Tent Town, was a sprawling tent community outside of the North Gate of Ravens Bluff in the 14 century DR. It was considered by some to be an unofficial district of the city.

Description

The community's name derived from it being largely made up of canvas or brightly-dyed silk tents. It was also host to corrals, stalls, and a few permanent structures. Many of the tents had wooden plank walkways between them.

The Cross Road, the road connecting Tantras and Mossbridges to Ravens Bluff's North Gate, cut right through the center of Tent City. A smaller, less impressive gate was situated on the road to mark the outer boundary of Tent City.

Trade

Tent City was host to a variety of different merchants, vendors, and ware hawkers.

History

In the spring of , the notorious con artist illusionist Mortimer Mittlemer was running a scam outside Tent City's gate with his wife Marilyn and associate Elmerth Willowit, impersonating a City Guardsmen and shaking down newcomers for 10 gold pieces in return for "permits" to enter Ravens Bluff, as well as smaller fees for keeping their weapons or horses.

When The Three Women War ravaged the Vast countryside, those who had the money to do so moved into Ravens Bluff, hoping to wait out the conflict. Eventually bands of thieves moved into Tent City and began using it as a staging ground for their raids on Ravens Bluff.

The many vendors that once did business in Tent City were sent overflowing into both the Market District and the Torchtown neighborhood of the Uptown District, seizing vacant rooms in many of the city's abandoned buildings. The residents with money filled Ravens Bluff's one-story cottages, causing such overcrowding that they were eventually torn down in favor of constructing taller structures. Others found homes in the rundown buildings along the docks and the banks of Fire River. The more destitute of the displaced had little choice but to sleep in the city's alleys or upon its rooftops.

Many felt that Tent City would never return, with the Three Womens War having demonstrated the superior safety of life behind Ravens Bluff's walls. Residents slowly trickled back in following its reconstruction,

Notable Locations

  • The A.V.O.W. shelter, a permanent structure and animal shelter that was established after the Three Women War with the hope of finding homes for all the animals that lost their owners during the conflict.
  • The Dancing Bear inn.
  • Marigold's Menagerie, a permanent structure animal shelter and animal training center. It managed to remain standing following the forced dismantling of Tent City, due in part to it being co-opted by a band of thieves.

Inhabitants

Tent City was a tight-knit community whose population fluctuated with the seasons, usually peaking during the summer months. with many beggars working the crowds that passed through.

Residents were suspicious of non-residents, though not hostile or rude. most of the time unwilling to offer up information freely.

Notable Inhabitants

  • Amy-Sue, a destitute child.
  • Madame Zorbano, a fortune teller, lived there in a tent of fine purple silk that was festooned with silvery moons and stars and topped with a yellow pennant.
  • Mather Sagra, a man that was skilled in providing people disguises.
  • Tent town was among the places that the roving street vendor Friar Cookpot frequented.
