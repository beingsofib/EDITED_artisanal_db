The Hanged Hobgoblin House or the 'Obgoblin was the only tavern in the hamlet of White Ford in Archendale in the mid-to-late 14 century DR.

Description

The Hanged Hobgoblin House was more of a large, run-down barn in its final stages of disrepair.

Structure

The tavern's ground floor had a taproom.

Interior

Several impressive antler arrangements adorned the taproom on the tavern's ground floor, surrounding the mounted head of a hobgoblin with wide-open eyes. The head was known by the name "Urbert"

Atmosphere

The Hanged Hobgoblin House served as a decent but unremarkable lodging option for White Ford offering all the conveniences typical of a village tavern, but its survival in a bigger community could have been questionable, possibly leading to closure.

Staff

At the Hanged Hobgoblin House, patrons could rely on friendly and eager-to-help staff members.
