Anauroch ( ), also known as the Great Sand Sea or the Great Desert, was a desert in north Faerûn. The Anauroch formed due to phaerimm magic in the former region of Netheril, which was significantly different than the desert after. The name "Anauroch" once referred only to High Ice glacial region in the north, before it was applied to the whole desert.

Geography and Climate

Anauroch was bordered on the west by the Silver Marches, the Savage Frontier, and Evereska; on the south by Cormyr and south-east by the Dalelands; and on the east by the Moonsea and the Ride.

All of Anauroch was a desert and suffered harsh winters, but the regions of the desert had distinct climates. During the height of Shadovar rule, around , the temperatures were moderated and it rained in the region, Anauroch was commonly divided into three general regions: the northern High Ice, the middle Plain of Standing Stones, and the Sword in the south.

The High Ice was a frozen land of rocks and glacier. The Frozen Sea was often considered part of the High Ice, but its landscape was a maze of frozen sand dunes that melted during summer in the southern regions

In the Plain of Standing Stones winds abraded jagged rocks that stood amidst a dry sea of gravel.

The Sword was a hot sandy desert.

Geographical Features

Bodies of Water

  • River of Gems: A long clearwater river that stretched from the southern edge of the High Ice to the large sinkhole known as the Throat.
  • Sixstreams River: A river that originated from streams flowing west out of the Desertsmouth Mountains and flowed past the D'tarig village of Tel Badir.

Hills & Mountains

  • Azirrhat: Called home by a large population of asabi, these rocky spires were avoided by the Bedine.
  • El Rahalat: This lone peak housed both an oasis and its namesake place spirit, Rahalat.
  • Hills of Scent: These sandy hills were bare of vegetation save gnarled frankincense trees.
  • Scimitar Spires: This range of mountains earned its name from the blade-like appearance of the mountains.
  • The Swordpoint: Though a useful landmark, this soaring peak was a known gathering place for manticores.
  • Wall of Fallen Djinn: This range of mountains and foothills within the center of the Sword separated a number of regional landmarks.

Landmarks

  • At'ar's Looking Glass: Distinctive but dangerous, this 'sea' of finely polished stones was located northeast of the Scimitar Spires.
  • Bowl of Loneliness: This immense dune field was situated between the oases of El Rahalat and El Ma'ra.
  • Fallen Giant Rift: This miles long and several-hundred-foot-deep rift was home to highly territorial hill giants that hunted any who entered their rift by hurling weapons at them.
  • Helm Rift: One of the many rifts within the Plain of Standing Stones.>
  • Quarter of Emptiness: This stretch of landscape was known to be particularly harsh, being little more than an expanse of open bedrock and sandy dunes, with no plant or animal life to be found.
  • Saiyaddar: This region of lush grasslands was the shared "hunting-lands" of the Bedine.
  • Shoal of Thirst: This large basin was a seemingly endless salt flat, and a place avoided when possible by the local Bedine people.
  • Smokeholes: These large round holes in the High Ice were created by lava-heated air deep below ground, and were home to a number of heat-loving monsters.
  • The Throat: This large crater or sinkhole was the terminus of the River of Gems and was rumored to contain heaps of gemstones at its bottom.

Oases

  • Bhaerlith: This Zhentarim-created oasis was rich in date palms and fruit trees, and could be found north of the Hills of Scent
  • Bitter Well: This oasis could be found southwest of El Ma'ra and the Bowl of Loneliness.
  • Hlaunga: Located south of the Scimitar Spires, this oasis was created by the Zhentarim.
  • Lion's Eye Oasis: Considered the most verdant oasis in the Sword, this oasis was home to a large community of lamia.
  • Ma'atar: Located west of the Wall of Fallen Djinn, this created oasis was bountiful in nut-producing trees.
  • Oasis of Vipers: Largely unvisited by wandering Bedine due to an infestation of snakes, this grouping of three pools was home to countless desert fauna.
  • Olomaa: Created by the Zhentarim, this oasis was protected by a fiercely loyal beholder.
  • Sister of Rains: This canyon-oasis was the refuge of outcast Bedine.
  • Vuerthyl: Located in the northwest part of the Sword, this man-made oasis was discovered to contain a wealth of emeralds and an imprisoned mind flayer wizard.
  • Well of the Chasm: Hidden within the Scimitar Spires, this oasis was commonly used by the Raz'hadi tribe.
  • Well of the Cloven Rock: This large oasis was located within the Hills of Scent and, when not used by the Bedine, was occupied by lions.
  • Yliyl: A relatively minor oasis, the primary thing it was known for was legends of buried treasure.

Valleys

  • Alagh's Pass: This mountain pass allowed travel through the Wall of Fallen Djinn, between the Saiyaddar and the Hills of Scent.
  • Raudilauth: This long-dried river valley passed through the Desertsmouth Mountains and connected the Shadow Gap to the lands of the D'tarig.

Life and Society

For a millennium after the collapse of Netheril, and the creation of the Anauroch desert due to phaerimm magic, the nomadic Bedine were the primary humanoid inhabitants of the desert. Then, in the mid-1300s DR, the Zhentarim invaded the desert with plans of creating a permanent trade route. Some decades later in 1372 DR, the Shadovar returned from the Plane of Shadow with the goal of restoring the land of Netheril, and stationed their flying city called Shade near the Scimitar Spires.

Bedine Society

The Bedine were a nomadic human people who lived in the wastes and traveled from oasis to oasis, rarely lingering staying anywhere for long. They made a living by guiding caravans through the hidden passes and by raiding. They comprised dozens of small tribes, each led by a sheik. Their wealth was counted not by gold, but by the size and health of their herds.

Zhentarim

The Zhentarim, always eager to make a profit, fought long and hard to secure routes for their caravans through the desert. They found the northern routes over the High Ice threatened by more monsters than they could fight, while in the south humanoid raiders and interference from the Dalelands and Cormyr hindered their efforts. Thus, the Zhentarim went across the sandy southern land of the Sword, working for years to establish a caravan route via caravanserais at various oases. The Zhentarim garrisons and their own raiders fought constantly against the Bedine to keep hold of their claimed territory, but despite years of bitter fighting, the Zhentarim held little more land in Anauroch than that on which they stood.

Shadovar

Initially upon their return, the Shadovar ignored the human inhabitants of Anauroch, and melted the High Ice. By , rain fell, the temperature was moderated, and the Anauroch was an arid dust bowl spotted with youthful forests, rivers, and grasslands. The Shadovar declared the phaerimm extinct on Toril.

During the interim, the Shadovar fought the Shadowbane War with the Zhentarim, and the Zhents lost control of the Black Road. The Shadovar also exterminated, subjugated or drove out a number of other races.

Their relationship with the Bedine was complex. Many former Bedine considered themselves citizens of Netheril by 1479 DR, and were residents of the rebuilt cities of Landeth, Orofin, Rasilith, and Oreme

Other Bedine fought adamantly against them, allying with foreign powers and Zhentarim to resist Shadovar rule. A full rebellion began two years before Shade crashed into Myth Drannor.

After the fall, Shadovar survivors clashed with Bedine around the Memory Spire, awakening a hive of dormant phaerimm.

Phaerimm

Below the dunes of Anauroch lurked what remained of the evil phaerimm, a race of spellcasters with near-unrivaled mastery of the Art, long imprisoned in a magical shell called the Sharn Wall under the desert. The phaerimm were forgotten by all but the wisest of sages until the barrier holding them was broken. After fighting the combined armies of most of Evereska, Evermeet, the Heartlands, the Chosen of Mystra, and the Shadovar—all at once—the remaining phaerimm were scattered beneath the surface.

Notable Locations

  • The Black Road was a Zhentarim-built trade route across the northern portion of the Sword that included the artificial oasis Vuerthyl.
  • Lundeth, an ancient village near the southwestern edge of the Anauroch that was rebuilt by the Shadovar.
  • Negarath, the ruins of the flying city that created a cavern system beneath a canyon in the Plain of Standing Stones.
  • The Obsidian Chapel, a temple to Bane that was plundered by Bedine raiders sometime well before 1281 DR.
  • Oreme, an ancient sarrukh city near the frozen sea that the Shadovar rebuilt.
  • Orofin, a fortified ancient city southwest of the Scimitar Spires was rebuilt by the Shadovar.
  • Rasilith, an ancient city at the edge of the Quarter of Emptiness that the Shadovar rebuilt.
  • Ularith, a hidden sand-swept Netherese death-sect temple complex defended by a powerful lich-lord. The complex held a massive portal network that connected the temple and its many hidden crypts all across Faerûn, some being as far as Mulhorand or even beyond, reaching the Utter East.
  • Hlaungadath: this former flying city of Netheril crashed about east of the ruined city Ascore on the northwestern border near the High Ice, and believed to contain a powerful evil. Old but largely intact, the ruins were inhabited by a clan of lamias that stalked nearby oases.
  • Thultanthar: returned from the Plane of Shadow in , the City of Shadows was a floating mountaintop, sliced off and inverted, with a city built upon it. It was stationed northeast of the Shadow Sea until when it was moved to Myth Drannor and crashed into the elven city.

History

The sarrukh empire of Isstosseffifil rose to prominence around . Throughout the Days of Thunder, they ruled much of the land that would one day become Anauroch. Eventually, their empire fell at the hands of the phaerimm and they were forced by the Netherese to scatter.

In the , the phaerimm started casting spells in the land of Netheril. These would eventually cause the creation of Anauroch.

After the fall of Netheril in the , survivors from the three enclaves that were saved by Mystra formed the Lost Kingdoms in the southeast, but the sands and conflicts overtook them within a few centuries.

The Netheril region was renamed Anauroch in the .

In the , the Anauroch desert finally stopped expanding outward as the sharns had defeated the phaerimms. But in the , the sands of Anauroch began to spread out southward once more.

The arrival of Thultanthar in the , brought about significant change in the political and geographic landscape of the Anauroch. In that year, three Princes of Shade performed a ritual that created a huge storm above the Shoal of Thirst, and transformed it into the Shadow Sea. For around a year, they used shadow blankets to melt the glacier of the High Ice, before that plan was disrupted.

In the , the Spellplague caused the merging of Abeir and Toril, creating changes in the landscape of Faerûn, including large areas that collapsed into the Underdark.

Anauroch was still an arid dust bowl but spotted with youthful forests by 1479 DR. The melted glacier created rivers and grasslands. Rain fell and the temperature was moderated. Some areas had grown lush and verdant.

In the , Bedine rebels began a concentrated effort to overthrow their Netherese overlords following Netheril's war in Cormyr.

Thulanthar's cataclysmic destruction in Myth Drannor in the , signaled the end of the Second Netherese Empire and, by 1488 DR the empire was reduced to a shadow of its former size. By this point, Netherese forces and Bedine tribes were in full conflict, warring over key points in the desert. One such battle near the Memory Spire awoke a hive of phaerimm sleeping within, causing the aberrations to begin leeching life from the land around them.

Some time after 1485 DR, Anauroch was once more a desert.

Rumors & Legends

The ruins of the very first cloud palace, belonging to the father of the cloud giants, Nicias, were thought to be located somewhere in the great desert.

There was a Faerûnian folktale that stated the Anauroch desert was formed by a single golem. The construct was tasked by its master to shovel sand into the region, but was subsequently forgotten about. After continuing with its task for so long, it was said to have formed the desert.

Inhabitants

Prior to 1372 DR, when the Netherese enclave of Thultanthar (also known as the City of Shade) re-emerged from the Plane of Shadow and began to reestablish Netheril in Anauroch, the only permanent human residents were the nomadic Bedine tribes, and a small Zhentarim garrison, tasked with patrolling and defending a line of oases along the Black Road. The Shadovar brought krinth with them from the Plane of Shadow, and some Shadovar were born as shadar-kai.

A diminutive people called the D'tarig lived along the eastern borders of the desert and interacted somewhat frequently with peoples from the surrounding lands.

The asabi, or laerti, a reptilian race who lived with the related stingtails in the mountains, in ruins, and in caverns under Anauroch, coming out at night to hunt. Groups of them were subjugated by various powers including the sarrukh liches of Oreme, the phaerimm of the Buried Realms,

Blue dragons lived and hunted in Anauroch, Young blue dragons had agreements with tomb guardians of Evereska on the edge of the desert to despose of robbers' corpses. A society of blue dragons was associated with the Cult of the Dragon prior to the return of the Shadovar, who subjugated their dracolich leader and deposed the entire cell of cultists.

A few barbarian human and hobgoblin tribes, along with small numbers of ogres, hill giants, and verbeeg lived in the valleys of the Plain of Standing Stones.

Death giants inhabited a number of Netherese ruins.

In the Sword, goblin tribes were active in the southern region, where the Goblin Marches bled into the Anauroch and the western region around the Black Road.

Gnolls were said to make up 5% of the non-Shadovar population in 1372 DR.

Notable Inhabitants

  • Telamont Tanthul, ruler of the Shade Enclave, a powerful shadow archmage.
  • The Princes of Shade, the sons of Telamont Tanthul.
  • Ruha, a Bedine Harper. She attempted to capture the Seraph of Lies Malik el Sami yn Nasser to bring him before a High Harper court.
