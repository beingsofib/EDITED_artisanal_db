The Caravans Ward was the ward of Calimport where the majority of caravan business and related enterprises were conducted. Due to its location in relation to other wards, it was considered to be technically both one of Calimport's four Port Wards and one of its eight Outer Wards.

Description

The Caravans Ward was situated on the eastern outskirts of Calimport, one of many areas that had once been enclosed by the Elder Wall in ancient times, abutting the shore east of Hook Ward. Those coming in from trade roads had to traverse roundabout trails encircling the city until they could finally reach the staging grounds of the Caravans Ward.

It was a very grimy place, full of dust, dirt, and the constant foot traffic of beasts of burden. By the 14 century DR, the walls that partitioned the ward into sabbans were all in a state of dilapidation and lacked upper walkways, Because of this ill maintenance, the sabban walls were considered unfit byways by the upper classes. However, this was somewhat to the ward's advantage, as it made it one of the few areas in Calimport with large courtyards and wide avenues, perfect for assemblies of caravans.

In place of these walls, drudach boundaries were marked by small plinths, spaced apart by . Sabban markers were often placed at ground level, almost like stepping stones, spaced apart .

Government

In the 14th century DR, the Sultan of Caravans Ward was Kemar yn Kadadf el Dasanm. He was also the Pasha of the Guild of Excellent Drovers, Shepherds, and Animal Handlers and a senior member of the Mighty Guild of Bouncers, Laborers, and Sellswords. Working his way through the ranks of both guilds, as an oxen driver and caravan guard respectively, he managed to prove himself as a capable individual.

Recognizing himself as hardly amongst the smartest of sultans, Kemar entrusted most of his business affairs, as well as some negotiations, to the Syl-Vizar Conol yn Agar el Dasanm.

Defenses

Though the area was well guarded by the amlakkar, murders were not uncommon in the Caravans Ward. Particularly, murder of caravan drivers who failed to act according to the societal norms understood by the guilds and traders. The understood norms of the system were that one should not flaunt their wealth after a caravan shipment, nor should they refrain from promptly using those earnings to load up wagons with new goods for trade at the next city. These groups would seldom mourn those who died because of not knowing how to act in the system.

Furthermore, the amlakkar afforded to the Caravans Ward were not enough to ensure the safety of its cattle yards. Because of this, the Guild of Excellent Drovers frequently hired mercenaries to protect their penned cattle against wolves and other predators, especially those who were drawn towards the Pahlemm Sabban from the odors of its slaughterhouses.

Trade

Though the Caravans Ward wasn't conveniently situated near Calimport's main markets, as was the case for the staging grounds of so many other northern cities, trade good always managed to make their way to where they were needed through a system of intermediaries. Firstly, caravan drovers would sell to their guilds in the Thytos Sabban. These guilds would then sell it to an appropriate sales guild, such as the Grocers' and Farmers' Guild. Finally, these sales guilds would present the products within the markets of Calimport. And though this would result in markups, Calishites were accepting of the method, for it had long been the way in which business was conducted in Calimport.

Additionally, though the bulk of imports came in through the city's ports, a good deal of that shipped cargo was received by the Caravans Ward for shipment else. From the towns further inland to the north, to estates, and various other settlements, such as slave plantations and mining towns.

Sabbans

The Caravans Ward was divided into three sabbans and nine drudachs.

Drakhon Sabban

Also known as the "Stallions Sabban", was the definitive stables section of the ward. It was tucked against the walls of Hook Ward's shipyards, as well as the Maker's Ward's Golnar Sabban. Its official name was derived from the Drakhon Dynasty of Calimshan.

Beyond stables, the Drakhon Sabban housed tanneries that crafted and sold tack, such as bridles and saddles, as well as many bazaars that sold and purchased horses and other exotic mounts, like displacer beasts and pegasi. The presence of these tanneries, coupled with the profusion of stables, made Drakhon a rather odorous sabban.

Among the more underprivileged caravan guards and attendants, the Drakhon Stable also served as an important rest stop. One where could rest at an inn, stock up on supplies, and find customers to buy the last of their caravan's goods. In some cases, they could even find themselves selling a full wagonload.

The sabban mark of Drakhon depicted a horse in silhouette. It was divided into three drudachs—Edaad Drudach, Hosaan Drudach, and the Meaza Drudach.

Pahlemm Sabban

Also known as the "Oxen Sabban", was the section of Caravans Ward tied to the cattle industries of Calimshan's northern shanates. This included cattle yards towards its southern end, manure pits, some slaughter houses, and additional tanneries. The slaughter houses all sat on the outer reaches of the sabban, up against the walls of the Crypt Ward, so as to minimize disturbances to the public.

The sabban mark of Pahlemm depicted the head of a steer with large horns. It was divided into three drudachs—Gebna Drudach, Laban Drudach, and the Zebda Drudach.

Thytos Sabban

Nestled against the cliffs, the Thytos Sabban or "Court Sabban" as it was otherwise known, was the primary receiving area for caravans both entering and leaving Calimport. It was here where caravan drovers could sell their goods for resale by other parties.

The sabban mark of Thytos depicted a black wagon wheel. It was divided into three drudachs—Ba'ara Drudach, Krayim Drudach, and the Vifar Drudach.

Inhabitants

A majority of Calimport's carters and drovers made their homes in the Caravans Ward, who exuded relatively relaxed moods and social attitudes, save for in times of unrest or when issues arose with the caravans. Aside from some pashas working in the ward as they had arisen from lowly ranks, few would ever dream of visiting this ward. Because of the aversion to this ward by the upper classes of Calimport, those on the run from the upper echelons of society often fled to and hid within the walls of the Caravans Ward.

It was not particularly safe to go wandering aimlessly through the ward, as it was said that those who got lost there could often end up finding themselves enslaved.

History

Prior to Syl-Pasha Ralan el Pesarkhal's establishment of wards as the city's largest sectors in the , Caravans Ward was but part of what was known as the Craft Quarter.
