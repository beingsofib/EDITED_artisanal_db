South Jotunhammer Mountains

The South Jotunhammer Mountains, sometimes considered simply the Jotunhammer Range, was a mountain range located on the island of Norland within the Moonshae Isles. They were a southern continuation of the Jotunhammer Mountains, and were known as the Eilyr Dyrr among the Llewyrr and as part of the Westlook Peaks to the Ffolk.

Geography

The mountains covered the entire expanse of southern Norland where it jutted in between the Sea of Moonshae and Trackless Sea, measuring approximately in length from end to end.

The most notable mineral deposits of value on Norland were located in these mountains. Ancient gem mines could be found high in the ice fields atop the peaks.

History

Many mining operations in the mountains were abandoned during the many conflicts between the Ffolk and the Northmen. Only after the signing of the Treaty of Oman in the , did prospectors from both cultures begin seeking the ancient mines. After a decade, they needed adventurers to battle the fell monsters encountered within.

As of the , a volcano in the mountains was threatening to erupt within the mountains.

Inhabitants

Blue and white dragons could be spotted above the peaks, and were believed to breed and lair in the area.

The Jotunhammer Mountains had long been home to giants, including frost giants who lived in seclusion in the high glaciers. As of the late 15 century DR, a large number of giants, most notably firbolgs lead by a firbolg crone named Lallargh, claimed the South Jotunhammer Mountains as their own colony of Grondsgrunn. Their presence made prospecting and mining too dangerous for the people of Norland, and many attempts by the Northlanders to oust the giants had failed over the years. The giants were supported by a clan of duergar from the Underdark.

The threat of a volcanic eruption in the late 15 century DR prompted the giants and their allies to push north into the rest of Norland.
