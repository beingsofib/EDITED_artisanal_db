Red Griffon Room

Building
nameRed Griffon Room
typeRoom

The Red Griffon Room was a chamber in the High Palace of Silverymoon during the 14 century DR that contained a dead magic zone.

Interior

The room was stately, its walls lined with duskwood panels. There was a polished table for meetings, and many chairs; the chairposts were decorated with scarlet-colored carved griffons.

The room was very stuffy, with no way in or out beyond the door. A copper heat-turned fan hung from a curved rod, over a candle-lamp, acting as the only ventilation available.

Defenses

The room was built around a dead magic zone, and thus magic did not really work in there, with very few exceptions known to include some abilities of the Chosen of Mystra. To verify the zone wasn't tampered with, there was a coin hidden in one hollow carving that held a burning spell. If the spell was ever able to resume, the coin would burn away.

History

The room was created after a wizardly duel following a MageFair led to the inception of a dead-magic zone inside one of the palace's rooms. After praying to Mystra, Alustriel decided to preserve the zone rather than remedy it. The room was rebuilt and divided in two, with the dead-magic zone becoming the Red Griffon Room.

The trader Garthin Muirtree requested the Red Griffon Room in the for a meeting with Auvrarn Labraster, whom he heard employed a "sneaking magic". But he was mysteriously murdered in that same room; he was recorded to have also met on the day before his death with Dauphran Alskyte, Draevin Flarwood, Janthasarde Ilbright, and Oscalar Maerbree.
