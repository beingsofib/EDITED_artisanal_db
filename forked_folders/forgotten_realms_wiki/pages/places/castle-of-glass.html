The castle of glass was the name by which tales among the Ffolk of the Moonshae Isles identified a legendary and hidden location atop Highpeak in the Orcskull Mountains of Moray. Although largely a topic of tall tales, the castle did indeed exist.

Location

The castle's exact location was unknown. According to the tales, the glass structure was said to be all but impossible to see by mortals. It lay high in the Orcskulls surrounded by impassable chasms filled with jagged rocks at the bottom. The castle proper was said to be surrounded by magical trees, the fruit of which could ward off disease and even death.

Structure

The castle could only be reached by a long, slender, and equally invisible drawbridge. Both the castle and the bridge said to be beautiful, delicate, and made all of glass.

Rumors & Legends

According to an old Moonshae tale, a woman known as the maiden of Highpeak and her sons dwelt in this grand glass castle atop the mountain. The sons grew tired of living in the castle, and so they constructed a glass bridge to a nearby mountain, and set out to explore. However, as they left, the castle disappeared. All but the oldest son would go on to die in battle, at which point he returned home and managed to rediscover the bridge during a bitter winter. As he crossed it, he slipped on the ice and fell to his death. The woman was grief-stricken, and her cries could henceforth be heard echoing out of the mountains during hot summers or freezing winters. The tale concluded with the thought that, one day, a descendant of the maiden would rediscover the castle and relieve the mother's suffering.
