Crown (constellation)

A constellation appearing in the North consisted of six stars and resembled a crown.

Observation

The constellation could be observed in the North in autumn and winter, looking to the south. It rose early in the night and set late during late autumn, reaching its zenith at around midnight.

Significance

The drow ranger Valas Hune used the constellation as a reference point with which to tell the time of night while leading a group of his people across the surface.
