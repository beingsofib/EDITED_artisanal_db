The Palace of the High King, also known as Caer Callidyrr, was a wondrous structure located in the city of Caer Callidyrr on Alaron within the Moonshae Isles. It was the tallest man-made structure in the Moonshaes.

Structure

The splendorous castle was spread over three hilltops and was bigger in size than some towns. Its tall walls of alabaster and white granite were polished to shine and glimmer in the sunlight. The walls were topped with crenellated battlements and several drawbridges allowed access into the castle past guarded high gatehouses. Its spires, decorated with streaming pennants, were seen from across the entirety of the city, projecting elongated needle-like shadows down on the town and often stood above the clouds, making them easily visible for ships approaching Whitefish Bay. The stonework was so precise that the towers were able to rise to much narrower spires than seemed possible for such stones.

History

The palace had been built in ancient times by Cymrych Hugh, the first High King of the Moonshaes, and served as the seat of power for the Cymrych Dynasty of High Kings as well as their lesser successors, the Carrathal Dynasty of Callidyrr. The castle had never fallen to any invader.

By the mid–14 century DR, the castle provided a constant reminder of the peak to which Ffolk civilization had once ascended. By this time, the white stone walls of the palace were becoming damaged and stained with soot, with some towers even being deemed too dangerous to enter.

The palace was set ablaze in the , when the red dragon Hoondarrh attacked the city.

As of the , the High King was Derid Kendrick, who remained on the throne through at least the .

The castle was one of the rare places in the Moonshaes in which arcane spellcasters were often encountered. A cabal of powerful sorcerers and wizards known as the Council of Sorcerers, who were ostensibly advisors to the High Kings and Queens, operated from the castle. Members of the council maintained apprentices and other students, potentially numbering at least 50 spellcasters in total.
