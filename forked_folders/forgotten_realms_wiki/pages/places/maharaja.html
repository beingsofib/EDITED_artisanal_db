Rakshasa Titles

Within raksasha society, Maharajah was a title roughly analogous to what some on the Prime Material would call a "duke". Maharajahs were the unquestioned leaders of several small interrelated clans, or sometimes singular massive clans, composed of hundreds of members. Some were known to serve under even more dangerous entities and would leave a rajah to rule in their absence.

Befitting beings of deception, early reports on rakshasa society contradicted later descriptions, although both were based on subservience and subjugation and it was possible some combination of the two was present. Earlier claims were that rakshasas belonged to a unshakable caste system and that individuals were incapable of rising through the ranks.

On the plane of Acheron, one Maharajah ruled over all raksashas who dwelt there. The raksashas of Acheron would often kidnap petitioners and planewalkers to serve as slaves in their palaces, hoping to impress the Maharajah. And a mysterious town in the divine realm of Merratet was rumored to be ruled over by a Maharajah.

Trivia

  • The Great Padisha of the Marid was sometimes referred to as the "Maharajah of the Oceans".
  • At some point the criminal organization known as the Night Masks divided their members up into three cadres, each entitled after a particular monarchical title. These were the Deys of Night, the Sultans of Sunset, and the Midnight Maharajas.
