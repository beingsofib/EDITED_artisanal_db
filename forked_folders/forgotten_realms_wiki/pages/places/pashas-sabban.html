Pasha's Sabban

namePasha's Sabban

regionCalimport

The Pasha's Sabban was a unique sector within the city of Calimport where the syl-pasha, ruler of Calimshan, lived and worked. Though nestled within the heart of the city's Palace Ward, it was considered its own entity it was especially important for the Pasha's Sabban, given the city's long history of coups and sieges upon the syl-pasha's domain.

Description

The Pasha's Sabban was by far the smallest sabban in all of Calimport. The area was even smaller than some of the city's drudachs. It had once been made up of two distinct drudachs, one where the syl-pasha lived and one where he worked, but over time most of those delineating walls fell or were replaced by other structures.

As far as the vast majority of Calimpanni knew, there was only one entrance into this area - the Caleph's Gate.

The whole sabban was covered by a magical ward, which was instated back during the Sixth Age of Calimshan.

Being that it was part of the Palace Ward, the Pasha's Sabban too was supported by a slightly vaulted, stonework upper street. Braced by both stone and timber supports, it was comprised of solid street avenues and courts. The engineers responsible constructed it in such a manner as to ensure that it would stay elevated even if half of these timber supports were broken. Directly below the palace, there laid an impenetrable mass of stone pilings.

The lower level was often lumped together with those of the Grand and Jewel Wards as the "Upper Muzad", being considered the uppermost layer of the Muzhahajaarnadah. In some areas there were even said to be hidden entrances to the Muzad, including hidden amongst the stone pilings beneath the royal palace.

Defense

The Pasha's Sabban was the most highly defensible position in all of Calimport. 100 members of el Qysaghanni, who directly guarded the syl-pasha and his family, were garrisoned within its walls. amlakkar who were stationed on the walls, and el Farisan, who guarded the grounds and their buildings.

If needed, the Pasha's Sabban could also receive the aid of the larger contingent of 500 el Qysaghanni troops These secret tunnels connected to practically every building in the Pasha's Sabban. This was necessary so as to ensure that the syl-pasha could be whisked away on a ship in Armada Ward or that sabban troops could be bolstered in the case of an attempted coup.

Whilst in direct service of the syl-pasha, guards from all of these groups were supplied magical weaponry from the royal armory. Generally this amounted to +1 scimitars and +2 spears for the rank-and-file warriors, whilst officers were granted weapons with item enchantments of +2 or greater. Additionally, some officers of el Qysaghanni were outfitted with wands. The armory in which these weapons were stored was no larger than the standard armories of amlakkhans.

By far the greatest defense the Pasha's Sabban had was its magical ward. This prevented entrance into the area through means of inter-dimensional travel and teleportation, though accounted for some exceptions in regards to both, including portals. It also prevented people from entering through means of flight or immaterial phasing, acting as an invisible barrier equivalent to a wall of force. Additionally, those attempting to fly over its walls would be met by a hail of arrows from the stationed guards.

Notable Locations

In 1370 DR, there were a total of 20 buildings that occupied the Pasha's Sabban, the area being comprised of the syl-pasha's -high walled villa estate and support buildings for his slaves, guards, food storage, and more. The vast majority of the buildings in this area were of the highest quality and rose no less than three to seven stories high. Entrance to the villa was guarded by the Pasha's Gate. Within the walls of the Pasha's Villa stood the following structures:

Pasha's Palace: The royal home of the syl-pasha. It consisted of a central building, connected to four smaller wings that spread out in a cross shape and were topped with domes of solid gold.

Djenassadh House: A building that contained the living quarters of the syl-pasha's family, his wives, and his personal servants.

The Jhasinnadah: As the name implied, was the dedicated living quarters of the syl-pasha's jhasinnadah, The jhasinni and jhasinnar lived in separate wings and were kept under close watch.

Minarets of the Bakkals: A religious site, consisting of a trio of towers that were used by the bakkals, the ancient priest-kings of Calimshan, to call out prayers over Calimport and wider nation.

The second major area of the sabban was the Houses of Ardor. Similarly structured as a villa, it contained four luxurious palaces that on the surface the syl-pasha entertained guests. Beneath the surface were three-levels of cellars, containing numerous prison cells, some of the highly secretive great treasures of Calimport, and portals to important sites across Calimshan.

Around the sabban's southern wall stood its miscellaneous support buildings. A well for man and beast, a garrison, an amlakkhan, three granaries, three warehouses, and four large stables for labor animals and horses.

History

The area was formerly part of the Temple and Learning Quarter, before Syl-Pasha Ralan el Pesarkhal introduced the system of wards in the , superseding the long-standing system of sabbans and drudachs.

Circa -1150 DR, roughly six years after Calimport suffered a blaze known as the Villaflames, the ruling Drakhon Dynasty under the reign of Kamal el Drakhon usurped control over some of the cleared out areas that had been affected, restructuring them and expanding the boundaries of the Pasha's Sabban.

In the the Evyrtaan Dynasty would have the long standing Pasha's Palace constructed through slave labor.

In the , a group of conspirators set off magical explosions with the intent of destroying the Pasha's Sabban and the adjoining slave drudachs. The conspirator would ultimately succeed in cutting short the reign of Syl-Pasha Fahd el Daosiin, as well as killing his sons. The resulting blaze would later be dubbed the Flag Fires.

Over the course of a few centuries prior to 1365 DR, the areas that would be delineated as the Grand, Jewel, and Palace wards underwent vertical construction by the Circle of Architects, Engineers, and Builders. This was done with the intention of providing both the upper classes greater areas of mobility, as well as to keep the latter out of sight.

In the , Ralan el Pesarkhal broke ground on a long-term construction project, a defensive barrier he dubbed the Pesarkhal Wall. By the next year it encompassed the Armada, Palace, and Shackles Wards. Ralan planned for it to eventually enclose the entirety of the Central Wards.
