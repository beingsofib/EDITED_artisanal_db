Burnleir's Barrels was a cooper business in the Dock Ward of Waterdeep in the 14 century DR.

Location

This business was located in a building that fronted the west side of the Street of Six Casks, four doors south from where it met Presper Street.

Services

Being a cooper business, Burnleir's specialized in the production of barrels. By the mid-to-late 14 century DR, the building focused on producing small handkegs and ornamental-finish "show" barrels. Another building owned by the business, located somewhere outside of Waterdeep, was centered around the "heavy" work of mass producing and storing larger barrels.

History

Burnleir's Barrels was one of the six competing cooper businesses that fronted the Street of Six Casks, earning its moniker. As the business was passed down through generations, it would come to be one of the two surviving businesses on that street, the other being Lithandy Fine Casks.
