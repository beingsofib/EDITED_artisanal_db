Hide n' Hair was a tannery in the Upper Trade Lanes of Skullport in the late 14 century DR.

Structure

Hide n' Hair was a one-story building in fair condition.

Interior

The interior of the shop consisted of crude wooden shelves stacked floor to ceiling with skeletal remains of varying sizes, ages, and species.

Services

Hide n' Hair processed and worked hides of all species, with no apparent restriction based on race or origin. The walls were adorned with a diverse and sometimes disturbing array of finished pelts, contributing to the establishment's eerie ambiance. Pricing was determined by size, quality, and the time required for preparation, with medium-sized animal pelts starting at 1 gp.

Inhabitants

Hide n' Hair's shopkeeper seemed to change with each visit. In 1370 DR, the shop was owned by Norm Flendur. He ran the business as though nothing was unusual. When questioned about his predecessors, he offered only vague recollections, as did all who had managed the shop before him.
