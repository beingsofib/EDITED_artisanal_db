Starnar's Cozy Cove Rest was a ramshackle tavern and inn, a notable step down from Tulmene's Tower, located in the Tethyrean town Tulmene on Cape Velen in the late 14 century DR.

Interior

Starnar's establishment and most all what was inside was shabby and patched or mended. Vermin was a frequent sight in the inn, but it was recommended to the guests to avoid pointing rats and mice to the staff, unless the guest wanted a dubious entertainment of watching young unwashed women throwing daggers at the scurrying rodents.

Services

Starnar's Cozy Cove Rest offered lodgings to those who could not afford to pay for beds in Tulmene's Tower. The rooms came with fresh linens and pillows, but, unless requested, the said rooms were sparsely furnished if at all. As for the dining experience, the Rest had little to offer, apart from black bread to be dipped in large bubbling cauldrons of heavily-spiced thick hog stew. By some accounts and evident by the beaks found in the crocks, the stew was a collection of "every dead bird they could gather." Despite that, both the stew and the bread on offer were quite tasty. Lastly, when the catch of the day came into Tulmene's harbors, the inn served fresh pan-fried fish that ranged from a single copper to a single silver piece. The hose beer on tap was back in color, thick, and packed a wallop with a hint of saltiness. Strong beer was a welcome sight among all the watered-down drinks served in many dockside watering holes.

Inhabitants

In the 14 century DR, the inn was run by a crippled retired sailor named Starnar, who was forced into the profession by the need to support his nine or ten daughters (Volothamp Geddarm, when describing the establishment, lost track of the exact number of daughters). Starnar's man-craized daughters were the inn's staff and attracted a large number of sailors and other, more shady individuals, like pirates. Many of these guests heard of the daughters' needs for husbands and tried to woo the young women during their stay.
