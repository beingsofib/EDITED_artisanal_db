The Shannyth River Valley, also known as the Shannyth Valley, was an idyllic and fertile river valley through which the Shannyth River flowed on the island of Moray in the Moonshae Isles.

Geography

The valley lay in northern Moray where the river approached the Sea of Moonshae. It was a low and verdant valley through which travelers followed a rutted dirt path that barely qualified as a road, but was well-worn enough for carts to travel at speed.

Flora & Fauna

The valley was filled with lush grasses and colorful wildflowers of blues, reds and yellows. It was home to bees, butterflies, leprechauns, sprites, and small mammals.

Inhabitants

During the height of the Kingdom of Moray, the Valley hosted the densest concentration of Ffolk settlers scattered along its length from Dynnegall in the south to Moray-town in the north. These Ffolk spoke of the fertile valley in reverent, almost mystical terms.
