The Highlands

The Highlands were a mountain range located on the island of Gwynneth within the Moonshae Isles.

Geography

The Highlands were a majestic and prominent feature in central Gwynneth, towering over high and stretching for over and forming most of the southern border of Myrloch Vale above the Myrloch lake, seperating the Vale from Corwell to the south and from the Shrouded Lands to the east. On their western end, the Highlands were separated from Aspenheight by the Aspenheight Gap, and they ran into the Cambro Mountains in the east at Synnoria.

Geographic Features

The mountains were jagged and rocky, comprising rows of craggy peaks that served as a massive windbreak for Myrloch Vale.

Flora & Fauna

Deciduous forests extended up the slopes in a few places, as well as forests of aspen and pine pocked by occasional meadows in the uplands.

Trolls were common in the mountains, although their numbers had been significantly culled by banshrae of Sarifal as of the late 15 century DR.

Inhabitants

The Highlands were home to firbolgs, who would occasionally raid the cantrevs of Corwell to the south to steal cattle and sheep. As of the late 15 century DR, the western slopes were the domain of a particularly large tribe of firbolgs led by Garbh Lludd.

Notable Locations

  • Bukhara Spires, a permanent, two-way portal.
  • Crane Point, the site of a temple to the moon during the reign of the Kendricks and of a hunting lodge during the reign of the leShay.
  • Innes and Ulls, a pair of small lakes.
  • Ulls Peak, a sheer mountain.
