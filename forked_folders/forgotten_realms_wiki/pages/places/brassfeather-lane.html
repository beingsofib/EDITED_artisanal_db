Brassfeather Lane

Brassfeather Lane was a street of hard-packed dirt and gravel in the North Ward of Waterdeep.

Geography

In the mid-to-late 14 century DR, this street ran from northeast to southwest, coming off of Trollkill Street and terminating at Immar Street. Along the way it acted as the western terminus of Anteos Lane.

In later years, the area that Brassfeather terminated at would be designated as part of Thunderstaff Way.

History

Brassfeather Lane was named, sometime prior to the 14 century DR, after a famous smith who had lived along it.

Notable Locations

  • Brossfeather Towers, the family estate of the noble house Brossfeather, which had been constructed along Brassfeather Lane due to its similarity to the noble family's name.
  • The home and workshop of Huldeth Brassfeather, the street's namesake.
