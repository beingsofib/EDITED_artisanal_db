Trades Ward was the oldest mercantile sector of Calimport and was one of the city's eight Outer Wards.

Location

Located in the northwestern corner of Calimport, Trades Ward was sandwiched between Emerald Ward to the east and Faiths Ward to the west. To the south it was bordered by the Grand and Jewel Wards.

Description

The Trade Way directly entered Calimport through this ward, with its west side dominated by Bajhit Sabban.

Once guarded by the Elder Walls, Trades Ward's only remnant of this defensive barrier was the Malikhan Gates, whose entrances had long been blocked off and left to serve as a mere sightseeing piece for travelers. Trades Ward was also notable amongst the wards for having the highest concentration of taverns outside of the Port Wards.

Government

In the 14th century DR, the twelfth son of Syl-Pasha Ralan el Pesarkhal, Faruk yn Ralan el Pesarkhal, was the Sultan of Trades Ward, astonishingly rising to the position at the mere age of eighteen.

Trade

By the , Trades Ward continued to thrive as the main area of mercantile business with the northern shanates (outlying estate farms).

Sabbans

Trades Ward was divided into three sabbans and eleven drudachs.

Bajhit Sabban

Also known as "The Tent Sabban", this outskirts sector of Trades Ward was made up of a disorganized bazaar and tents, centered around the ancient Malikhan Gates, which far outnumbered the few brick buildings in the sabban. Home to a sizable impoverished section of the Calimpanni populace, those traveling by Bajhit Sabban were given the impression that Calimport was a chaotic city. And any who traveled at ground level, more away from the Grand Gate, were oft swarmed by pickpockets. Yet a semblance of peace was managed in the sabban, thanks to a combination of its walls and amlakkhans.

Its sabbalad in the 14 century DR was Pasha Amahd el Sufontis.

The sabban mark of Bajhit consisted of four slashes, making a sort of crude tent shape.

Larau Sabban

Also known as the "Arena Sabban" or "Games Sabban", for nearly all aspects of business and life in this northeastern sabban were tied in one way or another to the Djen Arenas, the two foremost arenas of Calimport. These structures were so imposing, they could be seen from sabban walkways up to three sabbans away. Larau Sabban was named for the Larau, a family of qysars during the reign of the Shoon Imperium who were big proponents of the Djen Arenas.

It was one of the two sabbans that primarily attracted visitors to Trades Ward. No matter the time of day, it was by far the busiest of the three. And the cacophony of cheering crowds was audible all across its expanse.

Fitting its central focus, the sabban mark of Larau was an elongated oval, reminiscent of the shape of the arenas. At the center of this oval was set a trident.

Sahar Sabban

This sector of Trades Ward was centered on the brewing, distilling, and vinting of beverages. Most services in the sabban were under the control of the Vintners', Distillers', and Brewers' Guild. And it was one of two sabbans that primarily attracted visitors to Trades Ward.

Sahar Sabban was colloquially referred to as the "Golden Sands Sabban", whose guildmaster Tasyn el Effyd was also the the ruling sabbalad in the 14 century DR. He maintained the inequity of this situation, being more than happy to improve his profits by forcing people out of Sahar Sabban, or into an overcrowded drudach.

In opposition to the Golden Sands' near monopoly on alcoholic beverages, black marketeers were turned to by the public to meet an ever growing demand for other ales and wines.

The name of the sabban was derived from House Sahar and its sabban mark was once their sigil. This was later replaced with a depiction of a goblet, with three red drops spilling over its rim. It was seperated into three drudachs. The Coniq Drudach and Jhafer Drudachs, which colloquially had collective name of "Golden Sands Drudachs", and the Zeqra Drudach.

Sunned Barley Villa: The guildhall of the Vintners', Distillers', and Brewers' Guild. Its main building fronted the wall of Sahar Sabban.

History

Prior to Syl-Pasha Ralan el Pesarkhal's establishment of wards as the city's largest sectors in the , Trades Ward was but part of what were known as the Market Quarter and Temple and Learning Quarter.

Sometime after 100 DR, during the reign of the Shoon Imperium, the Trade Way from Athkatla to Calimport was constructed, cementing Trades Ward's future importance to the city.

In the summer of , much of Larau Sabban is set ablaze during the Caravan Fires incident, as fighting breaks out amongst trade factions in eastern Calimport amidst droughts.

Early in the month of Tarsakh, in the Sabbalad Amahd el Sufontis passes away. Various family members and heirs begin fighting over his estate, including his real estate and trade holdings. People fear that street warfare may be soon to follow in Sahar Sabban.
