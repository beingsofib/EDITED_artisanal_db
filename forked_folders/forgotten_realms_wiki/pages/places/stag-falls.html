Stag Falls was a small village nestled in the mountains near Ravens Bluff during the mid-to-late 14 century DR.

Geography

The area was nestled between mountains.

Government

Stag Falls was a barony, and its baron recognized the mayor of Ravens Bluff as their liege.

Trade

Of note, the village exported furs, and star rose quartz from nearby streams. Its taxes were paid in furs, wool, and fur/wool products.

Defenses

There was a castle.

History

Centuries before the mid-to-late 14 century DR, the castle in the future site of Stag Falls was attacked and destroyed by hosts opposing the vampire wizard Romanisk Vlaensk. Unable to pursue him effectively into the foundations and tunnels underneath, the hosts settled instead for collapsing the castle to prevent him from escaping. The vampire decided to bide his time underground, researching sorcery.

The village and castle were resettled and rebuilt by Karl von Stag Falls, the grand-grandfather of Vilhelm von Stag Falls. In the mid-to-late 14 century DR, baron Vilhelm ran the barony as a protectorate of Ravens Bluff, recognizing the Mayor as his liege. The village came to prosper from trade with the Bluff.

Nearly a decade into Baron Vilhelm's reign, he became curious about the history of the area, and had dwarves dig into the foundations of his castle; over the course of a year, they finally found the chambers beneath. Unfortunately, their digging released the vampire Romanisk Vlaensk, who slew them all and turned the Baron into an undead thrall. The Baron in turn was charged with terrorizing the village, in preparation for the vampire's further schemes.

The vampire's actions prevented the village's taxes from being paid, which in turn prompted investigation from Ravens Bluff; Field General Marcus Rubicon sent a patrol, which never reported nor returned. About a month after the vampire's release, the man sent a small detail of adventurers instead.

Along the way, those adventurers learned the trail was muddy, though passable, and spoke to other travelers, learning no one had come from Stag Falls; on the muddy trail, they found the patrol, torn apart by wolves. They were then attacked by extremely rare werebats and a pack of wolves; they found the farms near Stag Falls uninhabited, yet sowed, and the blades of all farm tools taken at the Latimer farm, and the Haldine farm thoroughly ransacked. The shrine of Chauntea was abandoned, with the altar service missing, and the Church of Torm dilapidated and desecrated. A maiden had committed suicide in one of the homes, and a boy had been killed for freeing all the dogs from their kennels. The first live person they encountered was a young man, Tommy Fletcher, a shepherd; he was heavily injured, and only survived thanks to their aid, taking them to the nearby caves, where the people had fled the Baron, led by the priestess of Chauntea, a woman named Sidone. Rather than return to Ravens Bluff for aid, the adventurers decided to spend the night in the caves and search further in the morning.

On the morning, they helped deliver a child; Sidone explained she was late, because she'd been searching for two youths, Kitarno and Lyra; she wished the adventurers luck, and offered them a ring of spell storing. On their path to the castle, the adventurers came across them shortly after, discovering the boy believed she'd been bit by a vampire, and in the process of torching her body. They were able to prevent this and heal the two of them; finally at the castle, they discovered the Baron had been turned into an undead, and that he had taken mental control of almost the entire castle staff. Managing to defeat him in battle, the adventurers pursued his master, the vampire Romanisk Vlaensk, into the underground.
