Aqueduct of Axalt

Building
nameAqueduct of Axalt
typeAqueduct
locationValley of Nexal, Maztica

The Aqueduct of Axalt was a wonder of engineering and a famous landmark that rose above the Valley of Nexal in Maztica.

Location

The Aqueduct began at the freshwater pool formed by the lush Cicada Spring that ran off Mount Popol's southwestern slope. The waster was carried by the Aqueduct of Axalt for over eight miles, to the north, over the great Lake of Tezca and ended in a reservoir at the Sacred Plaza of Nexal.

Structure

The Aqueduct of Axalt was a wide structure with two troughs, allowing the water supply never to stop even when the aqueduct was cleaned or repaired.

History

The aqueduct that supplied Nexal with freshwater was built during the twenty-two-year reign of the Revered Counsellor Axalt, the successor of Chalco. It was considered to be the most impressive accomplishment of the Revered Counsellor of Nexal and greatly benefited the city, bringing fresh, clean, and good-tasting spring water made available to all. Despite the fact that the four great lakes that surrounded Nexal city had drinkable water, the Aqueduct of Axalt provided water of better quality.
