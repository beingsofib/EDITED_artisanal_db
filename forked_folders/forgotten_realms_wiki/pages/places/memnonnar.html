Memnonnar was the remains of the city center of Memnon from the time when it was the capital city of the kingdom of Memnonnar under the Great Pasha Memnon.

Location

The ruins of Memnonnar were half of what was once a great city. They lay on the northern side of the River Agis, across from the Calishite city of Memnon.

Description

When it was the capital city, Memnonnar appeared much like the City of Brass, with buildings made from red brick and minarets and domes inlaid with brass. Scattered throughout it were paintings and etchings of genies, statuary of efreet, building spanning mosaics of oathbinder genies, and gargoyle watersprouts that resembled marids.

Millennia after the destruction of the city, the red bricks of the ruins still were hot to the touch. This was because the Great Brass Gate, the original portal through which Memnon came to Toril, still functioned deep below the surface as late as 1374 DR.

The subterranean ruins contained luxurious palace chambers buried under smoking debris, which were filled with magic items, rubies, and gold. Near the seeping Great Brass Gate, the tunnels were so hot that they glowed, and the air was full of unbreathable smoke.

Inhabitants

In its heyday, the bustling city was ruled by full of the ruling efreet, as well as fire genasi, and human citizens. Below the surface, slaves mined for precious gems and metal.

The old mining tunnels below the ruins were inhabited by fire elementals, mephits, salamanders, and other creatures of fire. The Great Brass Gate itself was defended by a powerful and loyal genie named Ufbrann.
