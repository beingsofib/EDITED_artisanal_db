Caer Corwell (castle)

Caer Corwell was a castle that had long been the seat of the Kendrick kings of Corwell on the island of Gwynneth in the Moonshae Isles. It overlooked the small city of Corwell, sometimes also known as "Caer Corwell" in honor of the castle. The castle was in ruins as of the late 15 century DR.

Location

The keep was located on a rocky cliff several hundred feet high that overlooked the common fields of Corwell and the waters of the harbor. This position made the keep visible from a great distance, and in particular, it could be seen from nearly a day's travel away along the Corwell Road.

Structure

The castle was surrounded by a high wooden which ended in a stone gatehouse. The front gates of the castle were made of strong wood and an iron was lowered from the gatehouse, which had four stout watchtowers. Rising above the wide courtyard, the central keep was protected by three towers. Prior to the fall of the kingdom of Corwell, the banner of the Kendrick family (a silver bear on a black field) hung in splendor from the tallest point of the castle.

As of the late 15 century DR, the castle was a crumbling shell of its former self,

Interior

Within the keep, Caer Corwell had a huge hall with two great tables and a large fireplace in the corner. Adjoining to this hall were the barracks, stables, and other outbuildings, all of which sat safely within the outer palisade.

Defenses

While the wooden wall was a point of weakness, the position of the castle atop the steep, rocky hill balanced this by making it extremely dangerous for attackers to even approach the wall. The only way to reach the gate was to climb the winding road up the hill, which was completely exposed to defensive arrows and burning oil from the castle.

The garrison was led by an exceptionally skilled fighter. Below him were 10 sergeants, each of whom commanded about twenty soldiers.

History

The sturdy castle had a long history as a proven redoubt in times of conflict, and was famed at the time of the Darkwalker War for having never fallen to an invader.

Corwell fell in the early 15 century DR when the fey of Sarifal expelled all humans from the island of Gwynneth. The Kendricks who still resided at the castle were killed.

As of the late 15 century DR, the ruins of the castle had come to be occupied by a mad gnome known as Phillwyp the Wary, alongside his boggart minions, who aspired to rule a kingdom of unseelie fey. During this time, High King Derid Kendrick of Callidyrr aspired to reclaim his family's ancestral seat.
