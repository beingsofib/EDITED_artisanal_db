Melvaunt (), also known as the City of Swords, was a frontier city that sat on the northern coast of the Moonsea, in the Moonsea North region of north Faerûn. It was a city of smithing and mercantile services, as well as factories serving as the port of entry to many travelers coming to the region.

Description

Melvaunt was a cold and severe city, beneath the oppressive clouds of heavy smoke that constantly billowed from its many manufactories.

The city was was home to a select few noble families, each of whom exerted their control over the city's ruling council,

Geography

Melvaunt was situated on the norther shores of Moonsea lake, in the appropriately named Moonsea North region of the Realms. It was located at the point where the Tormel River opened up into the the sea's northern shores, east of the Bay of Phlan and south of the Moonwatch Hills.

A number of roads and trails connected Melvaunt to other nearby settlements. The Phlan Path extended west from the city to Phlan, a long trail wound north through the lands of Thar towards Glister, and the Sword Trail went east along the coast towards Thentia.

Government

A Council of Lords ruled over the city of Melvaunt, though they were more often concerned with affairs that could line their coffers. All members were required to be merchants of some kind and vacant seats could be purchased for at least 100,000 gp while a new one could be created starting at a sum of 2,000,000 gp. As of the late 1360s DR, the Nanther, Leiyraghon, and Bruil noble families held all 21 of the council's seats.

Four positions in the council had more influence than others. The Lord Chancellor was Chairman of the Council, Treasurer and also Last Speaker. The First Envoy was the Council's First Speaker and Diplomat. The Lord of the Keys was the General of Melvaunt's army while the Lord of the Waves was the Admiral of it's navy and Chief Inspector of the city's docks. Below these four lords, all of the others were theoretically equal in influence. The lords met once a month to discuss issues but these meetings could last up to five days depending on the importance of the topics being discussed and the power plays made by council members.

The council did not tax its citizens like most governments, instead, the treasury was filled by taxes on every single transaction made; taxes on the mooring of ships in the city's harbor; and taxes on every conveyance entering through the city's gates as well as state seizures of property. The lack of state taxation ensured the Council's popularity with the citizenry, despite near-constant infighting and jockeying for better position.

Law & Order

Crime ran rampant through Melvaunt, as the city's nobles were far more concerned with their financial interests than the Melvauntian citizens. As of the late 14 century, the city guard was not given nearly enough resources to successfully maintain order and penalized criminals, unless of course they targeted a member of the nobility.

Trade

Melvaunt was famous for two business, industry and mercantilism. The city's blacksmiths and craftspeople were among the most skilled in the Realms. They were provided with a wealth of raw materials from the Melvaunt's mining interests, located in the White Peaks mountain range north of the Ride, and in the eastern slopes of the Dragonspines.

Guilds held much of the power within the city, primarily through legitimate trade but also as a front for criminal organizations. This caused many foreign traders to become apprehensive about underpricing the local merchants. Important guilds owned by the three noble families included the Metalworkers Guild, the Brotherhood of Carpenters and Shipwrights, the Jewelers Guild, and the Council of Silversmiths.

Ships from Melvaunt constantly traded goods to Mulmaster and Hillsfar, where they could be more efficiently distributed around the region. Legitimate trade with Zhentil Keep was dangerous and rare, That being said, the city did employ inspectors to work its docks. Due to the need of such measures, these inspectors were equipped with magical items that spewed fire for use if trouble arose on visiting ships.

As well as the city's primary exports of worked metal goods, Melvaunt was also a popular stop for those interested in the purchase of slaves,. The slave trade was tolerated by the citizenry due to fears of the involvement of powerful wizards in the business. This fear was somewhat valid, as Zhentarim agents were surreptitiously replacing Melvaunt's own slavers, at least as of the late 1360s DR.

Defenses

Melvaunt had a curtain wall surrounding it and it was patrolled by contingents of the army night and day.

The Melvauntian army doubled as the city guard and was kept at a steady number of five thousand trained men, They were easily identified by their purple cloaks and matching armbands, and the silver sword-and-anchor badges they wore. while Abarel Stendale served as the general of the army, before being demoted the captain of the City Guard.

Meldonder Nurian was admiral in charge of Melvaunt's navy - While it was small, consisting of between twelve and twenty ships of varying sizes, it was continuously being upgraded and operated with great efficacy. The ships were given powerful names, such as the warship known as The Thunderer or the two cruisers, Dawn Warrior and Dusk Smasher. Old ships were sold when they became too outdated.

Adventurers were welcome to hire on as mercenaries for the ruling nobles and as long-range troubleshooters when threats arose from the Moonsea, Zhentil Keep, or Thar. The Council had also proven amenable to hiring entire mercenary armies from Hillsfar if they felt suitably threatened.

History

Melvaunt's recorded history was primarily one of conflict with creatures from Thar and forces out of Zhentil Keep. In the , Melvaunt was drawn into a battle between it's ally Phlan and Zhentil Keep, who had invaded part of Phlan. Melvaunt's navy drove off the Zhentilar invaders but the allies were steadily beaten by the Zhentilar in the resulting four-year-long war. Just as defeat seemed inevitable, the creation of Shadowdale to the south distracted the Zhentilar enough that they abandoned the war and bullied both Phlan and Melvaunt into signing the Treaty of the Ride in the , and formed the Triple Alliance.

Around the , orcs out of Thar stormed Melvaunt's outposts among other settlements surrounding their territory. Zhentil Keep (who had arranged the orc attacks in the first place) pushed the tuskers back in an effort to regain support from their neighbors so they could be better persuaded to help fortify the Citadel of the Raven. Melvaunt was one of eight Moonsea powers who fell for this ploy, sending warriors and construction crews to secure and strengthen the massive fortress in the .

Melvaunt refused to send aid when Plan was attacked by the ogres of Thar inn the , and its allied city to be overrun. Melvaunt itself was then attacked by the same horde, but fared better, managing to survive the assault despite Zhentarim agents assisting the ogres. Three years later, Melvaunt was involved in the Moonsea War against Mulmaster, helping to defeat Mulmaster and free the River Lis up so they could again trade with the realms surrounding the Sea of Fallen Stars.

Melvaunt narrowly avoided a direct attack from the Zhentarim in the , thanks to forewarning from Phlanite scouts and the refusal of the Zhentarim's orc mercenaries to redirect their attack via Thar. Eight years later, adventuring diviners gave advance warning of the Zhentarim betrayal at the Citadel of the Raven, allowing Melvaunt to save their troops from walking into a trap. Lord Chancellor Rather declared an official end to the Triple Alliance forced upon the city by the Treaty of the Ride and banned the Zhentilar from Melvaunt. A reprisal was organized by the Zhentilar as Melvaunt's army marched home, but hired mercenaries from Hillsfar surrounded the Zhents before they could reach Melvaunt's walls.

In the , Melvaunt discovered that one of its own, Lyran Nanther, was a lieutenant of the Zhentarim agent Jyordhan and had launched a failed invasion of Shadowdale. After this defeat, they had the Nanther family banished from Melvaunt.

Around the same time, Melvaunt began building a large military fleet, more than sixty ships strong, all of which appear to have been destroyed two months later when a dragon split off from the Dragon Run and temporarily conquered the city, ruining a significant portion of it. Rather than rebuilding the city when the dragon left, the Council of Lords began rebuilding the fleet instead.

The following year, the fleet was involved in a disastrous three-way naval battle with Zhentil Keep and Mulmaster. Immediately afterward, it became apparent that Lord Orm had absconded with the city's treasury to an estate outside of Zhentil Keep, where he was considered the ruler of Melvaunt in absentia by the Zhents.

The spring of the saw unusual cold weather compared to the warmth experienced by neighboring cities. Firewood came to be in great demand and reinforced the public's disapproval of the worship of Auril.

As of the , Melvaunt had formed an alliance with nearby Thentia, for mutual defense against Zhentil Keep and Hillsfar. When the experienced and influential Lord Envoy Dundeld Nanther died in early 1373 DR, a power vacuum formed in Melvaunt that threatened to spark another civil war between the noble families to fill Dundeld's empty seat.

After the Shadowbane War in the , between the Zhentarim and Netheril, Melvaunt struck a fragile pact with Myth Drannor to curb Netheril's power on the Moonsea.

Notable Locations

Landmarks

  • Asberyth, a civic building and the meeting chamber for the Council of Lords.
  • Finger of the Gods, Melvaunt's lighthouse.
  • Four Quarters Warehouse, a spacious warehouse in the center of the city's smith district.
  • Center Market, a bustling open-air marketplace in the middle of the city.
  • The Livestock Pen, the city's slave market and holding area.
  • Docks, a sprawling network of piers and docks that house the city's lighthouse.
  • Shipyards, the home and construction site of all Melvauntian vessels.

Homes

  • Bruilhaven, the Bruil family estate.
  • Leiyraghon Manor, the extravagant estate of the Leiyraghon.
  • Nanther Keep, a minor castle that served as the home of the Nanthers.
  • Theodorus' House, the subterranean home of the reclusive enchanter Theodorus.

Inns and taverns

  • Breakwater, a respectable inn that attracted many adventurers.
  • Deil's Fire Tavern, a tavern that was owned by the semi-retired pirate Corwyn Jaffe, that served his smuggling enterprise quite well.
  • Floating Fighter, a successful establishment that was favored by the city's nobility.
  • Umberlee's Bosom, a rowdy dockside tavern.

Shops

  • Blackalbuck's Swap Shop, a well-supplied store run by agent of the Harpers.
  • Ganza's Arms and Armor, a large smithy run by Jemel Gamza.
  • Hermuk's Allgoods, the famous shop run by an honest honest man that happened to dabble in black market goods.
  • Markov Steelworks,a smithy run by Corkin Markov.

Shrines and Temples

  • Laughing Halls, the temple of Lliira also known as the Hall of Laughter.
  • Purple Portals, a popular temple to Gond led by Hlessen Muragh.
  • Resting Place of the Whip, a massive temple dedicated to Loviatar.
  • Temple of Waukeen, the holy house of the Golden Lady that exploded in 1345 DR.
  • Shrine to Sharess
  • Shrine to Tempus
  • Shrine to Tymora

Inhabitants

The nearly 40,000 residents of Melvaunt were a hostile and callous population compared to those from some other cities. As a testament to its commercial success, nearly one quarter of Melvauntians were earned their living as miners, seamen, or merchants of some kind or another.

Notable Inhabitants

  • Company of the Werebat, an adventuring group that suffered great loss in Halvan's Keep.
  • Forjia Chase, a Zhent refugee that found a second chance at success working for Melvauntian slavers.
  • Killian Kree, the scion of the Nanther family that left the life of nobility to join the Loviataran faith.
  • Orm, the exiled lord of Melvaunt that bankrupted the city engaging in a catastrophic naval war with Mulmaster.
