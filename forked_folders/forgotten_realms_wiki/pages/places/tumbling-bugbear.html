The Tumbling Bugbear was a tavern in the Central Port of Skullport in the late 14 century DR.

Structure

The Tumbling Bugbear was a one-story building in fair condition.

Services

Modest in size and atmosphere, it functioned as a local gathering place and was considered the nearest equivalent to a neighborhood tavern in the area. Prices were inexpensive, and the quality of fare was average. The tavern was best known for its soft mushroom bread, baked fresh daily. The bread was popular with the locals and was always served fresh. Any remaining loaves from the previous day were sold to Hugo Littlestaff of The Narwhal.

Inhabitants

In 1370 DR, the owner of the Tumbling Bugbear was Orthon Calliar and his wife Thoma Calliar.
