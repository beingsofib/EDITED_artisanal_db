The Track was a gambling den and a tavern located on the Market Triangle in the city of Westgate in the late 14 century DR.

Interior

The entrance to The Track faced the Market Triangle. Once the visitors walked down the stairs, the entryway opened into a sizable gambling den located in a cellar. The taproom was spread around an oblong dog-racing track. Numerous tables were scattered around the hall. Beyond the gambling hall laid the establishment's kitchen, storage area, kennels, and the proprietor's offices.

Services

The Track's guests were offered betting on the dog racing, food, and a number of drinks, such as Maelstrom's Punch, Sembian Swill, Souleman's Sublime, and Brother Tobias' 1368 vintage wine.

History

In the , Thurlow Fezziwink and his cousin, Tudor Fezziwink, devised a plan to cheat at The Track's races. Thurlow concocted something he called a "simple mixture of spices" that could be smuggled past the Track's bouncers. The gnome spotted a group of well-armed adventurers on the street and chased them down to hire them for the simple task of bringing the vial of spices to Tudor, who was ready to pull the scam off from the Track's taproom to fix a race between Big Bruiser and Daleland Downs. It was unknown if the scan worked, but if it were successful, it would surely have attracted the attention of the proprietor and his shady business partners.

Inhabitants

In the late 14 century DR, The Track was fully staffed with bartenders, servers, several strict guards, one foul-tempered elvish chef, as well as a kennel master. The den's proprietor was Mordecai, who cracked a whip on visitors trying to fix the races. Under his management, all potions and magical items that could affect the race were confiscated upon entrance and only returned upon exiting The Track. Mordecai was also corrupt and squeezed coin from the gamblers and was allied with the notorious Night Masks and was protected by the Night Mask named Haman.

One of the notable guards who worked at The Track was the human named Brom.
