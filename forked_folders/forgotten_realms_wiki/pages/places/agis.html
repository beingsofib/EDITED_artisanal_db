Agis was an ancient town of the Calimshan Empire and later the Port Town of Shoonach. It shared the name with Fort Agis and the River Agis upon which they sat.

Geography

Agis was the southernmost urban area of Shoonach, and sat upon a small peninsula on the River Agis below the Palace Mount. In the west it connected to the wide-spreading Fedhiyajar farmlands, and was close to the Mamlukkar and Imperial City. A long bridge crossed the water from Agis, reaching the eastern end of the Imperial City.

Description

The town was predominantly made up of docks and warehouses, the rotting remains of which endured to later ages. A wall stretched across the neck of the peninsula, separating it from the Fedhiyajar. Like other parts of Shoonach, ancient water pumps from the time of Shanatar remained intact and functional, providing clean water to the area's fountains.

History

Agis was present as a river port in , when the construction efforts of Shoonach began upon the nearby hill, creating the Imperial City and Palace Mount. Agis became the dedicated port for the entire urban complex that sprung forth, carrying traffic to and from the River Agis.

In 450 DR Prince Strohm I slew Emperor Amahl Shoon VII and destroyed the city in the Great Fires of Shoonach. The port remained a desolate ruin for the centuries that followed, with the undead being the only inhabitants there and the river largely empty of boat traffic.

Inhabitants

Agis was most noted for the large amounts of spectral undead that continued to haunt the town a millenia after it was destroyed. By the mid-14 century DR, the main living inhabitants of Shoonach were goblins, hobgoblins, and lizardfolk. Roaming bands of gnolls claimed the grasslands of Agis as their home, becoming the dominant group immediately around it.
