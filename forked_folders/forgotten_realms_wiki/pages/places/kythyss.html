Kythyss was a small coastal city located in the kingdom of Callidyrr on the island of Alaron within the Moonshae Isles.

Geography

Kythyss was the southernmost settlement found on Alaron. It was located at the tail end of the High King's Road where the Sea of Swords and the South Great Head met. Kythyss was found in the county of Stirgewick.

Government

Kythyss historically was ruled by the High King or Queen from Caer Callidyrr. In the mid–14 century DR, the local ruler was Lord Kythyss.

As of the late 15 century DR, the city had come under the thumb of ruthless mercenaries led by Ghelvan "the Buzzard", who were in turn loyal to Amn. Ghelvan's lieutenants dictated the city's policies while Ghelvan himself kept the people fearful and oppressed with violence and bloodshed.

Relationships

The town contested its northern neighbor, Llewellyn, for rights to fish in the fertile waters of the Llewellyn Harbor that lay between them.

History

In the late 15 century DR, the the people of Kythyss came under threat after their High King Derid Kendrick's forces became too distracted with crises at the Dernall Forest to provide adequate protection for the city and its property. The natives of Kythyss decided to hire mercenaries to protect them, but those mercenaries were bribed by the Amnian occupiers of nearby Snowdown to serve the needs of Viceroy Erliza Daressin of Westphal. As of the , the city had been infiltrated by these Amnian mercenaries, aided by traitors. Soon, Kythyss was being ruled by these mercenaries, and any citizens who did not profess loyalty to Amn kept quiet or else risked the deaths of themselves and their families.

As of the , Kythyss continued to rely on mercenaries and adventurers to protect its trade caravans running north to Callidyrr.

Inhabitants

Most of the city's people were fishers or woodcutters.

During the mid–14 century DR, a band of doppelgangers were known to live in and north of the town, and were known to impersonate travelers in hopes of ambushing unsuspecting victims on the roads and in the inns.
