Ithal Crag

The Ithal Crag was a large hill in the Royal Quarter of Darromar where the queen's royal palace of Faerntarn was built.

Geography

The Ithal Crag was a rocky headland overlooking the River Ith to the south.

Sites

The lower portion of the hill was surrounded by the Garden of Rhinda.

On the top of the hill rested the two main buildings of the palace complex, the main keep and the garrison building, along with other buildings, all surrounded by a granite wall. One of the city's four Watchtowers sat on the hill as well.

History

In , sixteen-year-old Darrom Ithal defeated the Calishites at this location, bringing freedom to Ithmong—and ultimately Tethyr—again. The hill was named after him.

The first royal palace was built on the site in 843 DR, after the coronation of Queen Alisande, and the palace of Faerntarn made use of the same structure.
