Tyr's Eye

Tyr's Eye, sometimes colloquially referred to as the Upper Square, was a large and busy walled strake, or plaza, located between the Blacklake District and Castle Never.

Description

Tyr's Eye was a roughly shaped, round, and slightly elongated plaza. It was paved with stone and housed a collection of civic buildings, businesses, and other high-profile locations that benefited from the proximity to Castle Never. It also housed an open-air market of many stalls and tents. The merchants dealt in a number of goods, from school supplies, paper, parchment, quills, fruits, potions, and even expensive magical items, all of these goods were available in a mundane form and rarer exotic variety.

History

The Blacklake District survived the war with Luskan of the almost unscathed and Tyr's Eye, which was technically a part of Blacklake, remained unaffected. Soon after, Shadow War of the ravaged Neverwinter's economy. Soon after the war, a great fire blazed across the plaza, torching the mages' school that was a prominent part of Tyr's Eye, and several other buildings. Eyewitnesses claimed that a red-headed sorceress was seen stoking the flames. To strengthen the Neverwinter economy, Lord Alagondar extended a personal invitation to the Church of Waukeen to establish a temple in the City of Skilled Hands and aid in its financial recovery. The Temple of Waukeen was built at the site of Neverwintran mages' academy. Along with the temple, a new Thayan enclave was established out of financial desperation. During that time in 1374 DR, access to most of the city was restricted for non-citizens. The Blacklake district was the only area open to guests, but only via a pass purchase.

Notable Locations

  • Mages' academy, a school of magic that taught wizards and sorcerers until its destruction in the late 15 century DR. The school stood on the northeastern side of Tyr's Eye.
  • Neverwinter Archives, was a big civic building at the center of Tyr's Eye.
  • Thayan enclave, the city's Thayan embassy and a trading post for Thayan goods in the late 15 century DR. The enclave was built on the northern side of Tyr's Eye.
  • Temple of Waukeen, a relatively new temple that was erected after the mages' academy's destruction.
  • The Overlook, a defensive structure perched atop Tyr's Eye's western wall.
  • The Wailing Wench Tavern, a tavern that was built in place of buildings devastated by a fire in the late 14 century DR. It stood on the northeastern side of Tyr's Eye.
  • Trading Post, a mercantile building where business was done among merchant houses and trade costers. It was built on the eastern side of the plaza on the site of the burned-down buildings in the late 14 century DR.

Notable Inhabitants

  • Alanna, a cleric from the Temple of Waukeen in the late 14 century DR.
  • Dayne Lynneth, a merchant of enchanted goods who ran his stall on the plaza and offered special stock to the Merchant's Friends – allies of the Waukeenar faith in the 14 century DR.
  • Eramus, a Manycoins priest from the Temple of Waukeen in the late 14 century DR.
  • Jeanelle, an upper crust Neverwintran lady whose reputation suffered from a tryst with Finch the bard in 1374 DR.
  • Jenks, the assistant kennel-keeper who worked for the mages' academy in the 14 century DR.
  • Kiggo, an imp who was purchased by the mages' academy to be experimented on in the 14 century DR.
  • Sir Nevalle of the Neverwinter Nine, who oversaw the Blacklake during the Forktongue Cultists crisis of 1374 DR.
  • Phineas the Sensate, a planar traveled from the city of Sigil, who sought to experience Toril and Neverwinter in the 14 century DR.

Notable Organizations

  • Circle of Friends, a trading cartel of stellar reputation active in Neverwinter in the late 14 century DR.
  • Fated Winds, a trading company of unsavory reputation, rumored to have had dealings with the Zhentarim in the late 14 century DR.
  • Forgotten Lords, a merchant coster of questionable ethics headquartered in Waterdeep in the late 14 century DR.
  • Sa'Sani's Mercantile House of Samarach, a busy and profitable trading company that established its foothold in Neverwinter in the late 14 century DR.
