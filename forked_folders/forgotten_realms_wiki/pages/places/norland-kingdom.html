Norland, sometimes called Rogarsheim, was a Northlander kingdom that dominated most of the island of the same name in the Moonshae Isles. It was one of the most powerful and populous realms of the Northlanders as of the late 15 century DR.

Government

The King of Norland was known as the "king of the Norls", a title held by Grunnarch the Red in the mid–14 century DR and by Rault the Wise in the late 15 century DR. The King of the Norls ruled the realm from Stormbanner Hold in the city of Rogarsheim, where they presided over about a dozen lesser kings scattered along the eastern coast of the island.

During the era of the United Moonshae Isles, Norland pledged fealty to the High Kings and Queens of House Kendrick beginning in the mid-to-late 14 century DR. In recognition of their new status as vassals, the local kings of Norland came to be known by the title of "jarl". Following Jarl Rault the Wise's decision to secede from the Kendricks' rule in the late 15 century DR, he once again adopted the title of "king".

Defenses

The various kings of Norland could collectively field a fleet of about 45 longships, each manned by 40 burly fighters, and this fleet could very quickly muster and transport itself across the sea. Most kings boasted fleets of between two and five ships, one of which was crewed by elite warriors, while largest and most elite of these fleets were the six ships based in Rogarsheim.

Norland boasted the best cavalry among the Northlanders of Moonshae. The warriors of Rogarsheim were known as the Stormbanner Wolves.

The people of Norland worked together to keep their homes safe from the monsters of the island.

Trade

The realm engaged in trade with the other Northlander kingdoms of Gnarhelm, Norheim, and Oman, as well as further ports north such as in Ruathym and Waterdeep. Rogarsheim served as the realm's primary port on the maritime trade routes between these locations, where visiting galleons from the Sword Coast or Calimshan could drop anchor in the cove.

Prior to the Treaty of Oman, the Northlanders' primary export was the spoils of their raids against the Ffolk, including slaves and weapons. In the century following the treaty, many Norls turned to farming or fishing.

Norland's main imports were amenities that they did not produce themselves, such as gold, exotic liquor, oil, and textiles.

Culture

The Norls regarded the sea as their highway to the world and prized their longships highly. They would "anchor" their longships by pulling them up onto Norland's gravel beaches, a fact which allowed them to settle any location on the coast where they could find shelter and resources, whether in a cove or on the edges of forests, despite a lack of natural harbors. These vessels were primarily reserved for raiding or trading voyages, while more mundane activities (like fishing) were performed in smaller boats. Traditionally, women were not permitted to engaging in either raiding or fishing, although these norms were challenged by the rise of the Storm Maiden in the late 15 century DR.

Because they valued the sea so highly, the Norls were largely disinterested in the interior of their island, and did not bother to make roads nor maps of it. They travelled between their coastal settlements by sea. Even so, they were highly invested in keeping these woods free of the likes of orcs and trolls, and such monsters' numbers had been vastly diminished by Northlander warriors as of the 14 century DR. The Norls also had great animosity towards giants such as firbolgs, however they held great respect for the frost giants of the Jotunhammer Mountains, whom they saw as being culturally similar to themselves. They were known to leave offerings of food and drink for these giants.

Norls enjoyed feasting. Their diet was heavy on cod, herring, and salmon. They also raised chickens and pigs.

Relationships

The Northlander raiders of Norland took great pleasure in running down and sinking Amnian merchant ships, whom they saw as colonizers following their annexation of Snowdown in the 15 century DR.

Tensions existed between the Northlanders and the Ffolk who had settled among them by the late 15 century DR, especially as Norland became a rival to the Ffolk High King on Alaron. However, the two groups worked together to repel invasion attempts by the giants of Grondsgrunn.

History

The island on which Norland would be founded had originally been settled by Ffolk and halflings prior to the start of Northlander raids in the mid–3 century DR. Norland was the first Northlander realm on record in the Moonshaes, with it being ruled over by a King Stromm as of the 7 century DR, who warred with the Ffolk High King Embro II. An effort was made in the , to thaw relations between the Northlanders and Ffolk via a political marriage between Prince Scothgar of Rogarsheim and Princess Bridget of Callidyrr, however the bride-to-be vanished on her voyage to Norland, leading both sides to blame the other. In the ensuing fighting, King Stromm would slay High King Embro II, and war between the two peoples would continue. The Ffolk refused to even conceded the Norls' claim to the island until the collapse of the Cymrych Dynasty of High Kings in the .

In the , King Grunnarch the Red allied with King Thelgaar Ironhand of Oman and King Raag Hammerstaad of Norheim to attack the Ffolk kingdom of Corwell, thus joining the Darkwalker War on the losing side. Of these three kings, only Grunnarch would survive, and would go on to be a party to the signing of the Treaty of Oman in the , which largely ended hostilities between the Northlander realms and the Ffolk lands led by High King Tristan Kendrick.

In the , Norland was one of the Northlander realms that agreed to put aside ancient grudges and swear fealty to High Queen Alicia Kendrick, thus joining the United Moonshae Isles. Over the following century, Norland would see an increase in Ffolk settlers as well as an intermingling of Northlander and Ffolk cultures, and many Norls who would have traditionally lived as raiders instead pursued lives as farmers or fishers. When the Storm Maiden finally began her wars shortly after the , she and her fleet would ultimately be defeated, but the fighting ended with the death of all of Rault's male heirs as well as a great diminishment of Norland's influence in the Moonshae Isles.

Notable Locations

The capital and largest settlement of Norland was Rogarsheim.

Other major settlements included:

  • Arfinnsheim
  • Follebu
  • Gudbrandstad
  • Sigurdheim
  • Sordal
  • Sommersdal

Inhabitants

The population was a mix of Northlanders and Ffolk, with some fey and dwarves residing at Rogarsheim. The Northlanders were famously warlike.

At least one small community worshiped celestials and acknowledged Zariel as their patron goddess, seemingly unaware of her fall from grace.

Following the conquest of Oman's Isle by fomorians, a clan of Norls calling themselves Oman giantkillers began to develop and share tactics for killing giants and their ilk. They hoped to recruit allies to help them retake Oman's Isle, and were willing to train adventurers and outsiders in exchange for a promise of assistance.
