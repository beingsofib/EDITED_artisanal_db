Grand Ward was an upper-class district of Calimport, considered the second richest of all the wards, and was one of the city's seven Central Wards.

Location

As its classification implied, Grand Ward was a centralized part of Calimport. To the north it was bordered by the Axash Sabban of Emerald Ward, the Naid and Sholeh Sabbans of Khanduq Ward, and the Sahar Sabban of Trades Wards. It was sandwiched between Quill Ward to the east and Jewel Ward to the west. And to the south it bordered the Ylar Sabban of Dock Ward.

History

The area was formerly part of what was known as the Market Quarter, before Syl-Pasha Ralan el Pesarkhal established the system of wards in the . superseding the long-standing system of sabbans and drudachs. Leading up to this point, over the course of a few centuries, the areas that would be delineated as the Grand, Jewel, and Palace wards underwent vertical construction by the Circle of Architects, Engineers, and Builders. This was done with the intention of providing both the upper classes and their servants greater areas of mobility, as well as to keep the latter out of sight.

In the , the Trades Conflagration blazed across northern and central Calimport. The cause was unknown but rumored about: some attributed the fires to smokepowder shipments being transported through the overcrowded bazaars of Jewel Ward's Marekh Sabban, and others blamed Mystrans and Cyricists for fighting in the streets. In any case, though the fires were largely concentrated in that sabban, they would spread over to Grand Ward.

Much of the real estate in the ward was cleared and would only be rebuilt by 1370 DR. In the wake of this rebuilding, Grand Ward's Cajaan Sabban had become the largest market area in Calimport.

Government

In the late 14th century DR, the Sultan of Grand Ward was Perat el Zasta. He was among the many "new money" Calimpanni at that time, having moved over from the Wizard Ward.

The Sabbalad of Makista Sabban, Fahd yn Ralan el Pesarkhal, was notably the holder of the office of Sultan of Armada Ward.

Inhabitants

The residents of Grand Ward were affluent individuals, possessing such wealth that they could live idle lives. An address in the ward was considered a sure sign that one had reached the upper class in Calimport. However, they were looked upon with disdain by the residents of Palace Ward as being "new money" upstarts.

The lower level of the ward, out of sight of the upper classes, was reserved for their servants and slaves. Life here largely operated as any other part of Calimport, but due to its obscured nature many sultans, pashas, and the like conducted clandestine activities there with the city's criminal powers.

Sabbans

Grand Ward was divided into four sabbans and thirteen drudachs. However, much like its neighbors, the distinctions between these sabbans and drudachs was blurred due to its layered infrastructure.

The slightly vaulted, stonework secondary layer of Grand Ward was the "ground level" to its rich denizens. Braced by both stone and timber supports, it was comprised of solid street avenues and courts, granting the upper classes more uninterrupted movement than the sabban-wall byways afforded. The engineers responsible constructed it in such a manner as to ensure that it would stay elevated even if half of these timber supports were broken. One could access the city's true ground level and vice-versa by traversing sets of stairs.

The edges of the ward's byways, served as the "curbs" of this layer. As the boundary defining sabban marks were not present on the second layer, those that walked its elevated streets used the glazed coloration of these curbs to determine which sabban and drudach they were standing in. Each were assigned a distinct coloration, with the latter being denoted as a stripe at the base of the sabban color.

The lower level of Grand Ward was often lumped together with those of the Jewel and Palace wards as the "Upper Muzad", being considered the uppermost layer of the Muzhahajaarnadah. In some areas there were even said to be hidden entrances to the Muzad. With the sky blocked out by the elevated streets, save for stairwells and the gates to the open-air Outer or Port Wards, torches had to be relied upon for illumination.

Cajaan Sabban

Also known as the "Market Sabban", was the central mercantile sector of the ward. Its most defining feature was the Cajaan Market, which had significantly expanded in scope following the Trades Conflagration.

The sabban mark of Cajaan was composed of a fiery sun, pierced from behind by three arrows that were all pointed southwest. The color displayed on the edges of its walls was green, whilst the colors of its drudachs were crimson red, midnight blue, and wheat yellow. The three drudachs that made up the sabban were Dabun Drudach, Mit'adh Drudach, and Semalej Drudach.

The four marble khanduqs: A group of khanduqs composed of marble that were destroyed in the Trades Conflagration. They had stored vast sums of gold, which were melted by the heat of the conflagration, pouring down into the Muzad to be collected by the undercity's denizens of dubious repute. This gold amounted to 10,000 gp and 2,500 trade bars.

Casajr Sabban

Also known as the "Stars Sabban", was largely dominated by the entertainment business, as well as some more scholarly trades.

Nearly a third of the sabban was set aflame during the Trades Conflagration due to its warehouses, which held large stocks of ink, parchment, and vellum used by the Dutiful Circle of Binders and Printers and the Masterful Order of Clerks, Scribes, and Scriveners. The libraries of the sabban were luckily spared any damage.

The sabban mark of Casajr depicted a book, with a five-pointed star on its outer cover. The prominent point of this star was always facing up. The color displayed on the edges of its walls was slate gray, whilst the colors of its drudachs were ivory, sky blue, and tan. The four drudachs that made up the sabban were Baela Drudach, Farag Drudach, Vihat Drudach, and Zhamur Drudach.

Makista Sabban

Also known as the "Golden Maidens Sabban", was a relatively ordinary sabban. It had the jumble of row houses and shops that one would expect of any Faerûnian city, constructed of either glazed mud-brick or carved stone, though they dealt only in expensive luxury wares. It was a particularly ancient part of Grand Ward, with some saying that its winding streets had been put in place farther back than the measurable lifetime of a dragon.

The carts and tents that made up the sector's bazaars, whilst impermanent in most other parts of Calimport, were permanent fixtures of Makista. And the sub-streets of the sabban winded precariously around khanduqs and other buildings. Due to the layout of these streets making it difficult to engage in a pursuit, both layers of Makista Sabban were prized by thieves and pickpockets alike.

The nickname and mark of the sabban were both derived from a particularly renowned festhall that had been around since the 9 century DR. It was composed of four outlines of female figures, arranged in a cross-pattern, with their feet pointing together and merging. The color displayed on the edges of its walls was yellow, whilst the colors of its drudachs were ash gray, black, and sea green.

Vahlen Sabban

Also known as the "Meal Sabban", for the sector prided itself on its many inns and restaurants, all of which catered to the rich upper classes of Calimport society. Its banquet halls were far different from the usual Calishite dining experience, featuring extensive arrays of extravagant foods and cooking styles. This sabban was so noted for its dining experience that eating there, referred to as "Dining Vahlen", was enough to raise one's status, though only for as long as the stories of one's experience continued to garner interest among their compatriots.

Much like Makista Sabban, the buildings in this sector were quite ancient, though they were adorned in all manner of lavish decorum that so greatly enticed Calimpanni social climbers.

The sabban mark of Vahlen consisted of a small dagger, plunged into a wedge of cheese. The color displayed on the edges of its walls was scarlet red, whilst the colors of its drudachs were black, white, and yellow. The three drudachs that made up the sabban were Gimela Drudach, It'aem Drudach, and Ysaav Drudach.
