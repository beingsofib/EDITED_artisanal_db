Caer Allisynn was a grand castle located beneath the seas surrounding the Moonshae Isles that would occasionally rise to the surface. It served as the tomb for Allisynn, the virtuous first queen of the legendary hero Cymrych Hugh.

Location

The sunken castle was capable of changing location within the Sea of Moonshae and the straits surrounding the Moonshae Isles, and it could even "sail" across the surface of the water when it rose from the depths. It was originally built upon a small island within the Strait of Alaron between the isles of Alaron, Gwynneth, and Snowdown, and it was in this area that it most commonly would make appearances above the water. As many as four times in a given year, the structure would rise from the sea for several hours on sunny and pleasant summer days. Its emergence was marked by roiling seas and thunderous noise.

Structure

The castle was a magnificent and breathtaking structure with high, crenelated walls of polished and finely cut rose quartz. The whole castle was remarkably narrow given its looming height, with slender and delicate towers topped with conical roofs rising even higher than the walls. A small number of high windows marked the walls, and the highest tower sported a silk flag emblazoned with a black chariot with silver wheels. The castle glittered with salt water as it rose above the waves, giving the appearance of being encrusted with diamonds. Puddles and strands of seaweed often littered the castle during these periods on the surface.

The wall surrounding the castle formed a roughly rectangular complex. The only entrance was a single gate closed with both a drawbridge and a portcullis,

Interior

The inside of the keep was as magnificent as the exterior. This included a massive great hall lined with granite columns and tall colored windows, a mezzanine level lined with treasure rooms, at least one cozy guestroom, and Allisynn's tomb: a marble room with a domed ceiling inlaid with gold. Below the castle were tunnels and shafts of rough-hewn stone that aided with drainage.

The treasures stored within the keep and library included a great many scrolls and artifacts, Magic prevented evil creatures from stealing the treasures.

Defenses

Although designed as a beautiful structure, Caer Allisynn's battlements had also been built with strength in mind. The quartz walls were too smooth to climb, and the threat that the whole island could suddenly sink made invading the castle a risky gambit while it was above the waves. A trapdoor was installed into the ground outside the main entrance into the keep, sending unwary visitors plummeting into a flooded pit if they attempted to force the doors.

By the 14 century DR, the castle became the domain of scrags and a waypoint for sahuagin patrols, with both serving as de facto guardians both above and below the waves.

History

Allisynn was the first wife and queen of King Cymrych Hugh of Corwell, who originally commissioned the castle as a wedding present for her. The island was to return to the surface periodically so that Allisynn, even in death, might enjoy the sunshine.

By the early-to-mid 14 century DR, many tales and rumors were shared by the sailors and bards of the Moonshaes about Caer Allisynn, including that the long-dead queen could grant wishes to the pure of heart. While some fishers claimed to see the castle from time to time, this was often dismissed as bluster or drunken fantasy. When Elminster crossed the Strait of Alaron during his tour of the Moonshaes in the , he witnessed the castle rising from the waves, but did not attempt to visit it. The captain of the ship on which he sailed, Durkin, told him the tales of the place.

In Eleasias of the , Tristan Kendrick and his companions Daryth and Lord Pontswain happened upon Caer Allisynn after their ship was sabotaged. Tristan and Daryth fell into the pit trap while attempting to enter the keep, but the great age of the structure meant that they were able to force their way out through a weakened drainage system. Tristan would end up meeting with Allisynn, who cryptically foretold that he would become the next High King.

In Marpenoth of the same year, The castle took them northwest to Norland and then south to Corwell Town. Caer Allisynn then ran aground in Corwell Firth about from Caer Corwell, and was able to be explored by anyone who came across it. Much of the library was emptied and transported to the royal library in Caer Callidyrr.

Caer Allisynn remained anchored to the shore of Corwell for two decades, serving as a symbol of Tristan Kendrick's reign. In this time, it came to be appreciated as one of the most architecturally important buildings in all of Faerûn, and sketches of it sold for 1 gold piece across the Realms. However, in the , the tall castle mysteriously vanished back into the sea, and its sudden disappearance was widely seen as a bad omen.

Inhabitants

Deep in the heart of the castle's tomb was a glowing glass coffin lined with purple curtains and sitting atop a dais. This coffin contained the body of Queen Allisynn, who would rise from her rest to greet petitioners and perhaps prophesy about their futures. Any who reached her coffin without defiling or attempting to steal from the castle was offered a wish.
