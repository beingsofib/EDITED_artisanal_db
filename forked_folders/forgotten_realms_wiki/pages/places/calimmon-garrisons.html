Calimmon Garrisons

The Calimmon Garrisons were a series of major forts along the border of County Calimmon, the county of Tethyr's Duchy Ankaram that took up much of the border with Calimshan.

Description

The Garrisons were each an unimaginative but solidly built fortress situated atop a small hill and ringed with trenches ten to fifteen feet (three to five meters) deep. Their walls were between ten and fifteen feet thick and stood twenty feet (six meters) high. The garrisons themselves had between 10–15 officers and 200–300 soldiers under a fort commander, and each fort had different tactics and strategies according to its situation.

Geography

The forts were arrayed along the northern side of the River Agis between the Trackless Sea coast and the ruins of Shoonach. From west to east, the forts were Fort Agis near to Memnonnar and Daraqadim, Fort Tufenk in a southern bend of the river, Iltakar Garrison further up the river, and Fort Calim on the edge of County Monteshi, nearer to Shoonach and Fort Teqlar.

History

The Garrisons were over six centuries old by the time of the ascension of Zaranda Star following the Tethyrian Interregnum in the mid-14 century DR. Old Calishite claims on southwestern Tethyr—particularly Shoonach and Monrativi Teshy Mir in Duchy Ankaram—led to the ongoing threat of hostilities and land grabs, making the Calimmon Garrisons an important part of the border defenses managed by the Countess Kyrin Hawkwinter.
