The Djen Arenas were a pair of arenas—the Arena Sabam and the Arena Efreetum—located in the city of Calimport in Calimshan. They were dedicated to entertaining the rulers of the city and the common folk alike and were among the most important structures in the City of Slaves.

Location

Both grandiose four-story buildings, they lay in the Larau Sabban of the Trades Ward: the Sabam stood in the Mirab Drudach, while the Efreetum stood to the east in the Shomuz Drudach.

Formerly, they were part of the Games Sabban,

Structure

The arenas were huge: four stories in height and visible across almost the whole Trades Ward, being seen from atop the sabban walks up to three sabbans distant.

Beneath both of the Djen Arenas, was a tunnel formed from an old sewer line that linked the staging areas to the Arms House, a barracks and temple to Tempus. Favored gladiators who were unlikely to try to escape were permitted to live and train there; they used the tunnel to reach the arena to prevent escape attempts regardless.

Interior

Around 1479 DR, illumination was provided by massive floating lamps.

History

The newly built Djen Arenas received 1,480 enslaved warriors captured when Calimshan's forces reconquered Calimaronn and Artrimmar in the . The majority of these gladiators died fighting one another or against monsters for the entertainment of the people.

Under the Shoon Imperium, the eastern Djen Arena was dubbed the Efreetum by Qysar Shoon II (74–107 DR) for its flame-shaped entry pillars.

True to its name, though, the Arena Efreetum was burned in the Arena Fires of the . A wizard attempted to escape by causing fires in both the arena and audience, which spread to the surrounding slave and gladiator barracks and beyond into the Games Sabban. The resulting inferno killed 5,600 slaves and gladiators, whose deaths went unrecorded, another 3% of the population, and destroyed 1% of Calimport.

In the , riots broke out at one of the Djen Arenas, leading to an escape of slaves.

After the Second Era of Skyfire in the 15 century DR, the windsoul genasi and pasha Marod el Arhapan rose to power off the gladiatorial games. He had his flying palace fly constantly between the two Djen Arenas and owned stables of slave gladiators. After Cephas tampered with the magic, Marod's own flying manor ultimately crashed into the Arena Efreetum, obliterating one whole side of it. Although Marod hoped to escape and restart gladiatorial combats in the Sabam, Cephas fittingly slew his evil father on the arena sands.
