Dougan's Hole was the smallest of the Ten Towns in Icewind Dale. It comprised a meager grouping of buildings arranged around a pair of piers, one long and one short. It was known to be an unwelcoming and insular place.

Geography

The town was located on the western shore of Redwaters, about southwest of Good Mead. The only road out of town was liable to become blocked by snow drifts in the winter.

The northern edge of town was marked with a path of gravel. The few streets of Dougan's Hole were all lined with gravel to prevent them from turning to mud.

Government

The town speaker lived in a house in the middle of town connected to the fishhouse by a boardwalk.

During the 1360s DR, the speaker was Freya Grynstead, the widow of the previous speaker who had died while fishing out on the lake.

Throughout the 1480s DR, the speaker was the outspoken and gruff trapper Edgra Durmoot. She took little to no interest in the politics of Icewind Dale beyond Dougan's Hole.

Defenses

The small town could muster a militia of only about 13 warriors.

Trade

The inhabitants fished for knucklehead trout on Redwaters, and were known to resort to cutting holes in the ice if the lake froze over. While they once participated in the knucklehead scrimshaw trade, the town lacked the resources to sustain this industry as of the late 1480s DR. Instead, they traded the raw knucklehead ivory and some salted fish to the larger Ten Towns in exchange for grain to help them survive the winters.

Relationships

Dougan's Hole and nearby Good Mead were perennial rivals over the meager fishing grounds of Redwaters. They had a long history of feuding, sometimes resulting in the violence that gave Redwaters its name. The two towns were at peace by the late 15 century DR, although some animosity remained.

The folk of Dougan's Hole were known to take offense with anyone who lumped them together with the folk of Good Mead, which other Ten Towners were known to do.

History

The hamlet of Dougan's Hole was founded by Dougan Dubrace when he found a great spot to fish on the shore of Redwaters lake.

By the late 15 century DR, the population of Dougan's Hole was in decline owing to inbreeding.

During the Everlasting Rime of the late 1480s DR, the people of Dougan's Hole made sacrifices of their sources of warmth in hopes of appeasing Auril to end the long winter. By 1489 DR, their shore had frozen over for hundreds of yards out into the lake, rendering both of the town's piers useless and badly damaging the smaller one. Around this same time, a pair of winter wolves named Koran and Kanan began extorting and kidnapping townsfolk.

Notable Locations

Perhaps the only noteworthy landmark of Dougan's Hole was the megalith structure known as the Twenty Stones of Thruun. These twenty granite slabs were arranged in an equilateral triangle that overlooked the town.

Inhabitants

The folk of Dougan's Hole valued their independence. They had no love for visitors and kept to themselves, although they were not above asking for help from adventurers if needed. Their silence and seeming indifference to the cold winds blowing off the lake made them seem eerie to visitors. The town's insularity eventually led to inbreeding, and by the 15 century DR, many inhabitants had visible deformities attributed to their parentage, notably small misshapen ears and unusually sharp teeth.
