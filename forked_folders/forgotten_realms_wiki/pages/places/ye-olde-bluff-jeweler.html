Ye Olde Bluff Jeweler was a jewelry shop in the city of Ravens Bluff during the mid-1300s DR, run by the extremely skilled Shorlock Revahl, who performed his craft with absolute enthusiasm and alacrity.

Location

This store was located along MacIntyre Path, in the Swordspoint neighborhood of Ravens Bluff.

Structure

This two-story building was a striking sight, being composed of polished white marble and having a roof of obsidian tiles.

Interior

The interior was just as impressive as the exterior, with a stunning showroom that featured Revahl's fine pieces, as if they were works of art. The second story served as home to the old jeweler, featuring his personal collection of sculptures and paintings. Among these were an original from the renowned Ambrose Pistlewaite.

Services

All sorts of gem-ornamented accessories were sold at this store. From rings, brooches and tiaras, predominantly crafted from gold, to bracers or amulets that would later be enchanted by arcane wizards.

History

In the late 14 century DR, Ye Olde Bluff Jeweler was robbed by a young and daring thief called Lotus. She noticed that the shop's clerk – the dimwitted man named Vurtin, left the goods unattended as he counted the day's profits. Lotus grabbed a jumble of diamonds, pearls, and bracelets and fled into the night without shedding a drop of blood.
