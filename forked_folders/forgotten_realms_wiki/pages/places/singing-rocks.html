The Singing Rocks was a tiny archipelago in the Sea of Swords or Trackless Sea off the coast of the Lands of Intrigue in west Faerûn.

Geography

The rocks rose from the sea due west of the midway point between Memnon in Calimshan and Myratma in Tethyr, so it, or rather its northernmost end, marked the sea border between the two realms. Moreover, captains commonly used the Singing Rocks as a vital aid to navigation in order to avoid going too far off course to the west, away from the safety of the coast. However, this was less of a concern by 1370 DR.

The majority of storms on the Sea of Swords were expended well before they reached the Singing Rocks, and the most fierce broke up on the rocks themselves. Thus the port cities of Calimshan did not enjoy strong sea breezes as found in the north, while the coastal waters were calm.

One of the outer isles was called Tonter.

The remote, lonely, and tiny hermitage island of Falconsrise was even further west of the Singing Rocks, and rarely even marked on maps.

Description

The rocks were named for a strange effect reported by sailors whose ships had ventured close by some time before 1357 DR, claiming to have heard singing from hundreds of feminine voices. They described it as beautiful, elusive, and haunting. However, it apparently had no dangerous charming effects, as was the case in harpy or siren song. Moreover, landing parties found that the singing stopped around from the shores and that, when they landed, there was no living creature, magical effect, or any other source for the sound. Although detect magic sensed the presence of magic, other forms of magical detection failed to give any more detailed information, as if the divination was blocked by something.

History

The Bloody Buccaneers often hunted in the vicinity of the Singing Rocks circa 1370 DR with their two cutters, Umberlee's Wake and the Water Weird.

Around that time, a kraken (or krakens) known as the Curse of Irphong commanding a force of merrow, sahuagin, and scrag led its forces into the seas amidst the Singing Rocks and Nelanther Isles. Beneath the waves, they demolished settlements or conscripted aquatic peoples into their swelling army.

Inhabitants

The ancient white dragon Arveiaturace had, on occasion, roosted on the isle of Tonter whilst on her far-reaching hunts.

But an even more dangerous occupant of the Singing Rocks was Shond Tharovin, the wizard and vizar of Schamedar cursed into the form of an enormous gibbering mouther, last seen drifting out to sea in 1367 DR. Evidently, he washed up on the Singing Rocks and made them his lair. Shond could cover more than of one of the isles or, by stretching his amorphous form along the seabed to up to , could extend across nearly all the northern isles. The chemicals exuded by his aberrant body also repelled most predators. As such, it's possible he had some contact with the krakens, though he would seek another powerful magical relic, Kayas the Krakenscourge, to manipulate the Curse of Irphong and other krakens.
