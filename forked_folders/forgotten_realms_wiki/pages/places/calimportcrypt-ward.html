Crypt Ward was the funeral district of Calimport and one of the city's eight Outer Wards. Popular colloquial names for the district among the locals included the "Dead Ward" or "Skulls Ward", as it had long been the site for tombs and mass burials, going as far back as the Sixth Age of Calimshan.

Description

Situated at the easternmost section of Calimport, this district was dominated by cemeteries, khamarkhas, markhouts, and temples. It had a great deal of shadowy alleyways and ordinarily empty avenues, perfect for the deplorable elements of Calimport to engage in illict activities and plot intrigues.

Government

In the 14th century DR, the eldest nephew of Syl-Pasha Ralan el Pesarkhal through his brother Nasim, Muham yn Nasim el Pesarkhal, was the Sultan of the Crypt Ward. Considered a petty underachiever, the Syl-Pasha backhandedly bestowed this position upon Muham in order to keep him away from the affairs of court and its power players, referring to it as "a fitting reward for one of your ambitions".

Sabbans

Crypt Ward was divided into three sabbans and ten drudachs. Each of these sabbans were named for one of the noble houses of the pashas, whilst their colloquial names referred to the form of burial traditionally found in each sabban and its drudachs.

Adlel Sabban

Also known as the "Khamarkha Sabban" or the "Pasha's Rest Sabban", due to this northern sector of the Crypt Ward being dominated by the opulent mausoleums of the upper classes,

Its sabban mark was the sigil of House Adlel, a scimitar on the (left-hand side) atop a cluster of three palm trees. It was divided into four drudachs—Nagaeh Drudach, Ovohl Drudach, Shamisad Drudach, and the Tunis Drudach.

Notable Locations

The Anhakhamarkha: A massive marble khamarkha, topped with minarets, that was built in dedication to Syl-Pasha Abon III el Larau and his wife, Anha.
Location: Shamisad Drudach

The Khamarnari: A khamarka built for the Grand Vizar Samesaj. It was composed of solidified flames, which shifted in hue with the time of day.
Location: Tunis Drudach

Nualorminoalh: A temple dedicated to Oghma.
Location: Ovohl Drudach

The Sahalarka: The guildhall of the Fellowship Nykkar, Calimport's undertaker guild. This tower topped building also held a hidden shrine to whoever was the most prominent diety of death. By 1370 DR, the shrine was dedicated to Kelemvor.
Location: Nagaeh Drudach

Irataq Sabban

Also known as the "Manycats Sabban" or the "Markhout Sabban", due to its abundance of individually, walled markhout tombs. This section of Crypt Ward was dedicated to the interment of merchants, pashas, and the middle class of Calimport society. Most of its markhouts were only a single story stall, the skies pierced by only few temple spires and some of the greater markhouts. Within these greater markhouts were kept the cremated remains of thousands, reposited within brass lamps packed in the floor and walls of the crypt.

Additionally, there were a number of markhouts in the sabban that were vacant. Said markhouts were clandestine connections between the surface and Calimport Muzad.

The sabban mark of Irataq depicted a cat's head.

Notable Locations

Chathaodah: The guildhall of the Skilled Circle of Butchers and Dressers.
Location: Nasharot Drudach

The Dawn Belltowers: An upscale temple dedicated to Lathander.
Location: Orbafura Drudach

Spectralhouse: A tower and library full of on the undead. It belonged to one of the few Cloak Societies to persist following the fall of the Graycloak Society. This minaret was also the tallest buildings in Crypt Ward, looming above the the markhouts of the Irataq Sabban.
Location: Sabayn Drudach

Kirruk Sabban

Also known as the "Mutar Sabban" or more disparagingly the "Forgotten Sabban", this section of the Crypt Ward was devoted to mass burials and the commoners of Calimport. Like the other sabbans, it too had a number of funerary monuments, though very few buildings, and fountains. Obelisks and small plinths marked its mass graves.

Where Kirruk Sabban stood out from the other two sabbans was in its lack of traditional drudach walls. In their place stood metal spear fences, which offered an unrestricted view into the sabban. Despite its "Forgotten" moniker and commoner ground, it was among the most popular public parks in Calimport around 1370 DR.

Unusually, the Kirruk Sabban lacked a sabban mark, for officials felt that its surroundings were so distinct from any other that there was no need for one.

History

Prior to Syl-Pasha Ralan el Pesarkhal's establishment of wards as the city's largest sectors in the , Crypt Ward was but part of what was known as the Craft Quarter.

Around the 11 century DR, an old Calimpanni practice of mass interring the middle class in brass lamps. By 1370 DR, five public markhouts had been filled with lamps of ashen remains.

In the late 14 century DR, Crypt Ward was growing a not too baseless reputation as a "Thieves' Quarter", Not only did criminal factions and other rogues use the markhout facades in Irataq Sabban to travel between Calimport and Calimport Muzad, but Voraya of the Twisted Rune held a mesmeric influence over the young Sultan Muham, one which she used to her cabal's benefit.

In the month of Kythorn, in the , numerous crypts and khamarkhas were found either desecrated or destroyed over the course of a tenday. Coffins, along with the bodies that had been interred within them, were found missing. And in Kirruk Sabban, more than half of the mass graves showed signs of the ground being disturbed and sunken in, a potential sign that their corpses too had been stolen. One of the few pieces of evidence left behind by the culprits were mysterious claw marks in the drudach walls of Crypt Ward, as well as some parts of the walls being partially melted. The known necromancers in Calimport managed to produce alibis, proving they could not have been the ones responsible for the grave robbing. El Amlakkar, as well as bounty hunters hired by families of some of the affected deceased, would go on to scour the Port Wards for any signs of new arrivals with links to necromantic activities.
