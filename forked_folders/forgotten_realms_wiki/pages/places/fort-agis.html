Fort Agis, also known as "the Memnonnar Citadel", was one of the border keeps of Tethyr, collectively known as the Calimmon Garrisons. It shared the name of the port of Agis in Shoonach and the River Agis that they both overlooked.

Location

This fort was located two miles north of Calimshan's Memnon across the River Agis in County Calimmon, on the northern edge of the ancient ruined city of Memnonnar.

Structure

Like most of the Calimmon Garrisons, this non-elaborate fortress was built from huge stones. Its walls were ten-to-fifteen-feet thick (3.0–4.6 meters) and 20 feet high (6.1 meters) with a deep, empty moat around them.

Defenses

Due to its proximity to one of Calimshan's major cities and one of its most dangerous ruins, Fort Agis was defended with a greater complement of troops than its sister garrisons. Around 300 Tethyrian soldiers were stationed there, overseen by 15 officers and a fort commander.

History

As of 1370 DR, the fort was approximately 600 years old. As the closest fort to Calimshan and the most vulnerable to dangers (including from Memnonnar), it was the most militant and watchful of the forts, with the largest garrison.
