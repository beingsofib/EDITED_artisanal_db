The City Cemetery, also known as the City Graveyard or Central Cemetery, was the central public graveyard of the city of Ravens Bluff in the 14 century DR. While by that time there were many graveyards in and around the city, both public and private, this cemetery was the foremost resting place for Ravens Bluff's most important residents.

Location

The cemetery was located some distance east of Ravens Bluff, atop a bluff overlooking the Fire River, across from Castle Iron Guard. The distance of the graveyard from the city proper was a deliberate choice made to minimize the threat of undead intrusions in the Ravenian streets.

Description

A fence measuring in height surrounded the cemetery. where the night-shift caretaker would reside.

Law

The cemetery was originally overseen by the city's Lord Regent, Exhumation of bodies from the cemetery required permits from the office of the Regent. Any unlawful digging in the cemetery would be judged as grave robbing and punishable by 10 years imprisonment in the Golden Ball.

Notable Locations

Carrague's Mausoleum: The personal mausoleum of Ambassador Carrague. It was a rather plain-looking mausoleum, though parts of it were adorned with gold and silver. Back when it was originally constructed it was quite the elegant structure.

DeFrague plot: Located near the center of the cemetery, was a plot owned by the DeFrague family. It had several tombstones, dating back as far as 1300 DR.

DeVillars Mausoleum: The personal mausoleum of the DeVillars noble family. It was a large and ancient mausoleum, elegantly carved with coins of various varieties and origins. Atop its entryway was carved the words "The Family DeVillars" in Old Common. It measured around x and stood an approximate tall. At one point it acted as the home of the vampire Ramsey DeVillars.

Guarded mausoleum: A mausoleum of unknown ownership. It was owned by some member of the Ravenian nobility in the late 14 century DR, possibly a member of the Knights of the Hawk, who frequently hired individuals to guard it throughout the day. The interior of the tomb was empty, save for a large resting stone and six empty stone coffers along the sides. Its door was trapped with a heavy crossbow bolt.

Helthgather Mausoleum: A very large and extremely well-decorated mausoleum belonging to the Helthgather family. It had all manner of symbols and wards etched into its stonework. The sealed door was tied to an alarm and had four wards that fired electricity and fire, drained life, and turned flesh to stone respectively.

True Temple of the Dead: The city's dedicated temple to Kelemvor.

Van Gryphon Family Crypt: The personal crypt of the Van Gryphons family.

History

Late in the , a night after the funeral service for Deputy Mayor Belanor Fenmarel, a group of adventurers defeated the Cyricist lich Tindel Miller and his undead minions, who had been eliminating Kelemvorites in an effort to improve his standing with Cyric. A minor death then appeared, judging some of them worthy of one day being specialty priests of Kelemvor, and then motioned towards a dark mausoleum. The adventurers watched as the structure warped, growing in both size and height, until finalizing into what would be the True Temple of the Dead. The temple's doors then swung open and a priest who had spoken earlier at Belanor's funeral, Damien Rethart, stepped out. He proclaimed himself to be "a priest of the people and Kelemvor's representative to all Ravenians", then implored those who could see the good in death to consider this new temple as "the rightful home for all souls." Following this decree, two minor deaths joined Damien, one emerging from the temple walls and the other erupting straight out of the ground. Damien then announced that these two minor deaths would be permanent guardians of the temple's interior.

Following the establishment of this temple, Damien approached the city's Clerical Circle and declared that his order would from then on be seeing to the care of the city's dead. The assembly were uncertain what to make of Damien's words, whether to consider them a mere request or an authoritative ultimatum.

In the , a group of adventurers visited Damien and were informed that the necromancer Szass Tam was posing a threat to the city. When the adventurers tried to leave the cemetery, they found that dread warriors had cut down the far side of the bridge and were in the midst of cutting down the remaining pieces of their side.
