The Fedhiyajar, the Granary Towns was an expansive area of farms, villages, and other agricultural infrastructure inside Shoonach, the capital complex of the Shoon Imperium.

Geography

The Fedhiyajar took up the bulk of Shoonach's area within the massive external walls, surrounding the Imperial City, Iltakar, Agis, Mamlukkar, and Debukher.

Description

The Fedhiyajar comprised dozens of villages scattered around Shoonach, attending to the farmlands that fed the city. Following the destruction of Shoonach, it turned to wilderness dotted with ruins, with both wild descendents of crops and monstrous plants growing there.

History

The specific form of the Fedhiyajar was developed from , when Shoonach's construction began. It was devoted to feeding the hundreds of thousands that lived there.

In 450 DR Prince Strohm I slew Emperor Amahl Shoon VII and destroyed the city in the Great Fires of Shoonach. Like the rest of Shoonach it was abandoned, with the farmlands reverting to wilderness.

Inhabitants

By the mid-14 century DR, the Fedhiyajar was home to wild crops such as wheat, and more aggressive plants like tendriculoses.
