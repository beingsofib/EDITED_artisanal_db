Layer 99

Layer 99 was the unnamed 99 layer of the Abyss.

Description

It consisted of seven sub-realms, each with completely different characteristics. Each sub-realm was adjacent to two, three. or four other realms. However since physical laws were different here, realms weren't always mutually adjacent, and instead obeyed their own capricious nature.

Visitors to Layer 99 could get visual clues about which realms were adjacent by observing the horizon: about one-third of it displayed the color associated with a neighboring realm. Traveling to another sub-realm could be accomplished by using special inter-dimensional devices or simply by walking, and sometimes even staring or concentrating long enough, toward the chosen horizon color.

Gray Realm

This realm resembled a desert of black sand and undulating hills, stretching endlessly in every direction. The landscape was utterly barren, and the sky above was choked with dense gray clouds. Roughly one-third of the horizon glowed with a yellow tint, another shimmered with a swirling mass of colors, and the final third was lit by silent lightning bolts tearing through the blackened sky. Blinding dust storms swept across the terrain with alarming frequency.

The only known inhabitants of this realm were the dretchs.

Lightning Realm

True to its name, lightning crackled across the blackened sky, and rain fell frequently upon the realm. The stark white terrain was punctuated by towering stone pillars, each rising miles into the air. These slender columns, no more than thick, clustered in small groups every or so. This realm often served as a battleground for the warring legions of Orcus and Demogorgon.

One-third of the horizon swirled with shifting colors, another was veiled in dense fog, and the final third glowed with a soft silver light—a clear indication of proximity to the Silver Realm.

Swirling Realm

This realm was defined by a sky of swirling colors and a churning ocean below—a grotesque parody of natural beauty. From a glistening hole suspended in mid-air, more than a mile (1.6 kilometers) above the surface, a roaring multicolored waterfall poured down, stretching at least wide. It originated from a conduit leading to the Abyssal layer ruled by the loathsome demon lord Juiblex.

The waterfall and the ocean were composed not of water, but of oozes and jellies—dripping rot, fungoid secretions, and other vile matter seeping from Juiblex's domain. Despite the reversed gravity field of the realm, the ocean remained as buoyant as any ordinary sea, its surface littered with massive floating chunks of fungus. The only known inhabitants of this realm were vrocks, circling above or lurking within the gelatinous tides.

Half of the horizon crackled with streaks of lightning, while the other half shimmered with a soft silver glow—a clear sign of adjacency to the Silver Realm.

Yellow Realm

This sub-realm was a lush tropical forest, reminiscent of prehistoric jungles. Yet its scale defied comprehension: blades of grass towered high, and palm leaves stretched as large as houses. The bright yellow sky was barely visible through the canopy, as the trees themselves soared more than a mile into the air.

One-third of the horizon shimmered with swirling colors, another crackled with flashes of lightning, and the final third was cloaked in a dull gray haze.

Known inhabitants of this realm included vrocks, achaierai, and thunder beasts.

Fog Realm

A thick gray fog prevailed in this realm, heavy with the stench of decay and rot, so dense that visibility was reduced to just . The ground beneath was slick and adhesive, reminiscent of walking on jellyfish. Bubbling and popping sounds constantly echoed around any interloper, while in the distance, muffled cries—eerily like those of infants—carried through the gloom. From far above drifted the screeching and cackling of bird-like creatures.

This realm served as an incubation zone for demon spawn. Shallow, swampy pits stretched for miles across its surface, each one brimming with writhing demon embryos. Vrocks frequently flew overhead, occasionally swooping down to snatch embryonic morsels from the muck.

The demon embryos were in various stages of development—from bloated embryonic sacs containing half-formed entities, to fully grown juvenile spawn already capable of movement and behaving aggressively.

A fourth of the horizon was saturated with swirling colors, another portion shimmered with stars against a backdrop of black sky, a third was tinged with a pale yellow hue, and the last radiated a soft silver glow.

Silver Realm

The landscape of this sub-realm consisted of flat, smooth sheets of silver ice, stretching for miles in every direction. It was blindingly bright and bitterly cold, especially for human-like creatures. The terrain was utterly featureless, devoid of sound or movement. No fires could be kindled here, and heat- or fire-based spells failed to function entirely.

The physical laws of the realm absorbed sound waves instantly, rendering speech useless. Those who entered had to rely on alternative means of communication.

Roughly one-third of the horizon was tinged with yellow light, while another third was cloaked in darkness and streaked with jagged lightning.

Star Realm

A desert of black sand stretched across this sub-realm, pocked with vast, shallow craters—each easily a hundred yards (90 meters) wide. Rivers of molten lava snaked between the pits, while steam and acrid smoke billowed from fissures in the scorched earth. Thousands of tormented souls writhed within the craters. Some staggered blindly between them, only to tumble into the lava or collapse into the pits. Emaciated and encrusted with filth, their faces were contorted in perpetual agony. Moans and screams echoed without end.

A number of lava mephits flitted above the craters, their glowing red bodies dripping molten rock. They reveled in the suffering, gleefully tormenting their victims. Overhead, several chasmes patrolled the skies, swooping down to attack any creature that aroused their suspicion.

The black sky shimmered with bright, pulsating stars—some of which vanished abruptly, only to reappear elsewhere. At intervals, gaping voids tore open in mid-air, disgorging fresh victims who plummeted, shrieking, into the craters below. Moments later, the rifts sealed and disappeared.

The chasmes here were demon slaves of Kali, tasked with retrieving fresh sacrifices for the dreaded Layer 500(her domain). The holes in the sky served as conduits, opening and closing to allow the demons passage. They also admitted cultists from other planes, who dropped screaming into the infernal landscape. The blinking stars were distant conduits, while a glowing red aperture marked a permanent gateway to Layer 500.

Should any interlopers enter this realm, they might remain unnoticed for a time—lava mephits and chasmes were vastly outnumbered, with hundreds of suffering souls for every demon. Though the victims could not speak, they would grope and clutch at outsiders, desperate for contact. While they posed no physical threat, their grasping limbs could hinder movement and delay escape.

This forsaken sub-realm of Layer 99 was the only known passage into the terrifying dominion of Kali.
