Maker's Ward was a ward of Calimport dominated by skilled tradesmen and trade guilds. It was also one of the city's seven Central Wards.

Location

This area was located in the eastern center of the city, sandwiched between Wizard Ward to the west, a bit of Quill Ward's Jhaapir Sabban to the northwest, and Crypt Ward to the east. To the south it was bordered by Caravans Ward and the shipyards of the Hook Ward.

Government

In the 14th century DR, the Sultan of Maker's Ward was Zelmazzar el Fiqr, a man who by 1370 DR was counted among the oldest government officials across all of Calimshan, due in part to his unwavering loyalty to the Caleph's Throne, rather than any one man holding the office of Syl-Pasha.

Trade

A wide variety of businesses operated in Maker's Ward, many of which benefited from the ward's close proximity to Caravans Ward. Unlike the other major trades district, Hammer Ward, the people here were lacked any sort of altruistic communal sense of unity. Rather than working together to make the ward a better place for all, the tradesmen were fiercely competitive. Vendettas and street brawls were the norm for this area, sometimes blowing up into full scale guild wars, as guilds or families endeavored to dominate costers, particular business deals, or even specific trades.

Inhabitants

One of the remarkable aspects of Maker's Ward in the 14 century DR was its near complete lack of slaves, with all residents being free people, at least in the strictest sense of the term. Slaves were considered to be a luxury by more than half of those who inhabited the ward.

Sabbans

Maker's Ward was divided into three sabbans with nine drudachs.

Golnar Sabban

Also known as "The Builder's Sabban", for this quite dirty and noisome sabban was the home of builder tradesmen, such as carpenters and masons and the like. It was located in the southeastern section of the ward, abutting the Caravans, Crypt, and Hook Wards. Together these three wards kept the tradesmen in steady business, with fresh building materials seemingly in near-constant demand in Calimport.

The sabban mark of Golnar consisted of two tools, a hammer and a chisel, crossed over one another.

Jaleh Sabban

Also known as "Saamark Sabban", was a sector of the ward predominated by sellers of rare goods, luxuries, trinkets, and amenities for assuaging inconveniences in one's life. The sundry rare goods on offer were fit for satisfying any taste one might have, whether it be antiquities or sweets. Additionally, this area was home to tradesmen of softer goods, including clothing, rugs, and tapestries.

The Jaleh Sabban's name, as well as its nickname, were derived from Saamark II el Jaleh, an overly indulgent pasha who was remembered for spending his wealth lavishly with little care for what the goods he bought actually did for him.

The sabban mark of Jaleh depicted a lion's head. The area was divided into three drudachs—Bakkrad Drudach, Lanish Drudach, and Loqha Drudach.

Sojrab Sabban

Also known as "Heart's Joy Sabban", was a sector of the ward predominated by artists. Fiercely as competive as the rest of the tradesmen of Maker's Ward, the courtyards of this sabban acted as the canvasses for impromptu sculpting competitions against one another, often times creating works solely for the purpose of outdoing their rivals. Its northeastern corner, Qanôn Drudach, was perceptively quieter than the rest due to its proximity to the Crypt and Quill Wards. Because of this, the neighborhood was a common meeting space among temperamental artists, bards, and creators of all kinds, either looking to reunite with old friends or to forge new relationships with kindred souls.

Sojrab Sabban was named in remembrance of the Sojrab Dynasty, whose pashas were largely remembered for being avid patrons of the arts and culture. The sabban's colloquial name was derived from it streets commonly being wandered through by lovers, poets, and romantics.

The sabban mark of Sojrab consisted of a harp, displayed against a triangular field, with both tapering to points at the bottom of the mark. The area was divided into three drudachs—Dowr Drudach, Qanôn Drudach, and Tar'ea Drudach.
