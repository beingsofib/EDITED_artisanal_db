Synnoria, also known as Llewyr, was an ancient kingdom that was home to a race of elves known as the Llewyrr. It lay hidden in a high valley within the mountains bordering the Myrloch Vale on Gwynneth, one of the Moonshae Isles.

Geography

The valley sat on the southeastern border of the Myrloch Vale where the Highlands and the Cambro Mountains met. It was nearly impossible to locate by outsiders: not only was it surrounded by the rugged and nearly impassable mountains, but it was covered in a powerful mass illusion spell so that, if viewed from above, it simply looked like a continuation of the surrounding mountains.—added to the treacherousness of attempting to travel into the valley.

At the heart of Synnoria was a circular lake containing a rounded hill on which sat the city of Chrysalis.

Flora & Fauna

The woodlands of Synnoria were home to aspen and rhododendron trees. Flitting through the trees were small mirror-moths as well as fireflies the size of fists.

Government

The Most Serene Matriarch, Ate`Niah, oversaw the Elders of the Llewyrr and presided over the elves of Synnoria from the palace of Argen-Tellirynd for centuries. As of the late 15 century DR, they had pledged fealty to the ruling leShay of Sarifal.

Defenses

Synnoria was well-hidden, and any who entered without proper permission or escort were swiftly detained by the elven marchlords. and the Knights of Synnoria as of the late 15 century DR.

Relationships

The Llewyrr of Synnoria regarded the humans of the Moonshae Isles with derision and hostility for centuries. While their attitude toward the Ffolk was measured, they regarded the Northmen as outright enemies. Unauthorized entry of a human—or most other non-elves—into Synnoria was seen by some Llewyrr as a defilement of their land.

History

Synnoria was founded by elves from the Llewyrrwood fleeing the Vyshaan occupation of their homeland in . They were welcomed in the Moonshae Island by the fey of ancient Sarifal, and they named their new realm after the queen who had led them over the sea. When Sarifal retreated into the Feywild circa , the Llewyrr remained in their hidden valley.

After the first human settlers arrived in Gwynneth in the 2 century DR, they soon came into open conflict with Synnoria. The two groups agreed to a tenuous peace in the , after a decade of fighting.

Synnoria was attacked by the Ityak-Ortheel in the when Malar and Talos sent it through a portal but the elf-eater was defeated and banished back to the Abyss after it had destroyed Argen-Tellirynd, the Palace of Ages.

Following the return of Sarifal in the , Synnoria would once again pledge allegiance to the leShay.

Inhabitants

The city of Chrysalis was the only real settlement within the valley, and it lay mostly abandoned by the 14 and 15 centuries DR. The Llewyrr population steadily decreased due to a low birth rate and deaths by accident or in battle, Legends among the Llewyrr foretold a resurgence would one day bring them back to dominance of the Moonshaes, but they always doubted this legend until it proved true when the entire island of Gwynneth was taken over by the noble eladrin of Sarifal in the early 15 century DR, leading to humans being banned from its shores.

Notable Inhabitants

  • Askepel, a lord and a leader among the Knights of Synnoria who plotted against the leShay.
  • Ate`Niah, queen of Synnoria.
  • Brigit Cu'Lyrran, a member of the Sisters of Synnoria.
  • Erashanoor, guardian of the Synnorian Gate—a portal to Evermeet—and a member of the Elders of the Llewyrr.
  • Karrin and Kerrin, twin adventurers and former members of the White Sails.
  • Mindarion, a lord and notable dissident who plotted against the leShay.
  • Oemeril Talos–claere, a guardian and protector of Synnoria's borders.

Notable Locations

  • The Grove of Meditation: a circle of pine trees that was said to increase the ability of elven magic users to learn powerful spells.
  • Harrowfast, an abandoned dwarfhold that sat atop the mountains overlooking the Synnorian vale from the north. It could be reached from the valley via a steep path.
  • Mirror Lake: a deep blue enchanted lake that had the ability to answer questions posed at dawn.
  • Singing waterfalls: created by the run-off from glaciers high in the mountains, it was said that the water sang a song of heart-wrenching beauty that could enrapture humans such that they would sit and listen until they died of starvation.
