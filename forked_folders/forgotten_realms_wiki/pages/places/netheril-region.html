The land of Netheril was the land largely occupied by the nation of Netheril during the beginning of the Age of Humanity. This article covers the region from the birth of Netheril to the fall in when Karsus's Folly effectively ended the nation. For information outside this time period see the Lost Kingdoms and Anauroch articles.

Society and Inhabitants

The High Netherese floated around in relative isolation, but the low Netherese shared the realm with a number of other inhabitants and creatures.

While the low netherese primarily settled around the Narrow Sea and the Eastern region of Anauroch along the Netheril River, and the Banks of Hidden Lake, the tribes of Rengarth patrolled the tundra to the north, and the plains south of the Bay of Ascore in the West. The Rengarth built a series of permanent trading settlements with the Netherese, and had a schism when a portion of the tribe, the Angardt began to learn magic from their neighbors.

This conflict resulted in the Angardt being chased from the north, and they migrated to the Flats in the south, where they built Kismet as a permanent trading location.

Other humans fell to Netherese conquest, or plagues. Some rebelled against Netheril, and worked with the phaerimm to bring about Netheril's fall, such as the raider Olostin, and the Kozah worshiping inhabitants of Monikar.

In the early days of Netheril, much expense was paid to establish trade routes to the Delzoun dwarves of Ascore, and elves taught the fledgling humans magic.

Attempts were made to trade with goblins around the Bay of Ascore, which resulted in goblins claiming the town of Cantus when their goods were rejected by dwarves and other Netherese.

The Amanir Cloud giants lived in the northern peaks of the Channel Mountains in relative isolation, and were sid to have rescued some Netherese during the fall.

Orcs were a threat throughout most of the region throughout the time of Netheril.

Lizardmen occupied the Marsh of Simplicity and Marsh of Deception .

Eventually Phaerimm from the Buried Realms turned the region to desert with their magic, creating the Anauroch, and most of the survivors fled to the surrounding regions of Faerûn.

Climate

Netheril was a verdant land, until the coming of the phaerimm. In , the phaerimm began to cast spells that caused the lush land to change into desert. In 10 DR, the region became known as Anauroch to reflect the desert that it had become.

Geography

During the time of Netheril the following places existed in the region that is now the Anauroch, for more recent geography, see that entry.

Forests and Plains

  • Cold Forest North of Ascore, this forest formed in rich soils exposed by a receding glacier
  • Eastern Forest The massive forest on the eastern edge of Netheril, which corresponded to Arcorar during the height of Netheril, but fragmented into the Border Forest and Elven Wood by the time of the Lost Kingdoms.
  • Far Horns Forest The massive forest to the west of Netheril was full of orcs and fey creatures.
  • The Flats These relatively peaceful plains to the Southwest became the homeland of the Angardt after they fled from the Rengarth.
  • Hidden Forest North of Hidden Lake between the Lodestar and Shinantra Mountains, its orc tribes were a nuisance to nearby settlements.
  • High Ice The endless glacier north of Netheril continually encroached on the Narrow Sea until it eventually overwhelmed it after the fall.
  • The Hollow The original valley home place of humans on Toril was buried in sand by the fall of Netheril.
  • Myconid Forest Sustainably logged by the Netherese of Conch until it was overtaken by sands, the Myconid inhabitants it was named after were neutral toward their human neighbors.
  • Rengarth Ancestral Lands Between the Far Horns to the west, Bay of Ascore to the North, and Narrow Sea to the east, was an expansive relatively peaceful plain claimed by the Rengarth.
  • Rengarth Tundra The harsh Tundra north of the Narrow Sea was home of the most hardy Rengarth, and was eventually overwhelmed by the High Ice.
  • Two Rivers Reservation An ancient Rengarth burial ground between two rivers north of the Narrow Sea.

Mountains & Hills

  • Abbey Mountains Located to the east of Netheril, they were originally called the Dragonsheart Mountains.
  • Barren Mountains Located west of Netheril they were mostly barren of vegetation
  • Channel Mountains A long mountain range that ran North to South in northern Netheril, which created the Channel Locks where it crossed the Narrow Sea, and was a significant barrier to travel. The Amanir Cloud giants lived in the northern peaks.
  • Columns of the Sky some of the tallest mountains in Netheril were centrally located, and the City of Conch sat in their eastern foothills, to the chagrin of the Rengarth Barbarians, who consider them sacred.
  • Dementia Range Far to the northwest of Netheril, they sported a few gold and platinum mines.
  • The Glorifier An impressive log cabin monastery to Jannath on the slopes made this a mecca for the god's followers.
  • God's Legion Revered by the Angardt and Rengarth who believed the range uprooted to crush their enemies and saved their people in ancient times.
  • The Hazardous Climb A massive solo volcanic mountain that was impossible to climb.
  • Lodestar Mountains East of Hidden Lake The southern peak was Hatchet Mountain, and northern peak Stade Mountain
  • Moander's Footstep In southeastern Netheril, the range was known as a place of death, and housed an important temple to Moander.
  • Patrician Peak In the plains of Eastern Netheril, it was a difficult climb.
  • Purple Mountains On the western edge of Netheril, they were extremely dangerous and went largely unexplored outside of the Mines of Dekanter.
  • Rampant Peaks Used as material for floating cities, this mountain range decreased in size over the time of Netheril, and was named for orcs that worshiped Gruumsh One-Eye amongst the peaks. Thunder Peak was named for another conflict with the orcs.
  • Shinantra Mountains West of Hidden Lake, they were named for a group of diminutive humanoids said to live in the peaks.
  • Spiregardt A massive glacial peak in the Easern Rengarth Tundra just south of the High Ice.
  • Sunrest Mountain A very tall peak west of the Glorifier that was a holy place to followers of Amaunator
  • Widowmaker Mountain A very tall solitary mountain east of the Glorifier.

Bodies of Water

Narrow Sea and Tributaries

The Narrow Sea was the largest and most important body of water in the region, providing the majority of food for Netheril, and was heated by Mythallars until they failed during the fall, causing it to be slowly swallowed by the High Ice.

  • It was used as a trade route to the dwarves in Bay of Ascore to the northwest.
  • Its other two bays, Annagoth Bay, full of narwhals and salmon east of the Ascore Bay, and Paw Bay to the northeast led to the Rengarth Tundra in the North.
  • The Narrow Sea had a number of tributaries. The most important to Netheril, and largest, was the Netheril River near Seventon in the southeast.
  • To the east of the Netheril River, near the border of Netheril, The Alley was dangerous for hidden orc attacks, and its tributary Escalade Rill for its raging rapids.
  • North of the Narrow Sea, the extremely wide Lough River.

Bay of Ascore and Tributaries

The Bay of Ascore, known for the stone ships the dwarves of Ascore patrolled the cold deep waters with, was the Northwestern arm of the Narrow Sea, and dried up after the the fall of Netheril. Its two major tributaries were the Waterbourne River north of Ascore from Delzoun lands in the west, and a more extensive northern system.

From the High Ice the often frozen over Weimar River flowed into volcano heated Oxbow Lake, to the Macht River*, to steamy Portolake down the Meiter River before spilling into the bay.

'*There are two Macht rivers labeled on the map, the other was to the south near Macht Lake, and mentioned in that concordance. Finick Flow, frozen for 9 months of the year, but otherwise unmentioned, may have been this stretch of river.

Paw Bay and Tributaries

Paw Bay in the north eastern Narrow Sea was named for the Paw Islands and known for its sea life, including seals and walruses. It was fed by two major tributary systems.

  • Up the Peril Current * Mere River fed Nanza lake from the east and was fed by Terrace Spate which had tributaries Portent River and the apotherycal Last Ditch River.

The High Tributary fed Nanza Lake from the northwest with its tributary The Backwash

  • The frigid waters of Glacier Lake and its tributaries, which were collectively named Syke, spilled into the Dienere River and Fria River, both of which flowed into Paw Bay from the east.

Netheril River and Tributaries

The Netheril River ran north to the Narrow Sea by Seventon, and provided a relatively safe route for transport of goods and people all the way south to Dagger's Point along the Eastern Forest, causing many towns to be built around it, and making it the most important river in Netheril. It was fed by a number of tributaries, including Gers River and Gustaf River, which wound south of Patrician Peak, both named for villages of Seventon. Gillan River had a Temple to Selune built at the headwaters after the Abbey of the Moon burned down. Nauseef Flow began in the Firehorn Peaks.

In a relatively uninhabited length the river swelled into Macht Lake which was full of merrow, and also fed by Zeig River as well as its tributaries, the Macht River and Fenwick River which served as a irrigation source for farmers.

Past Macht Lake, Janick River flowed from the Shadowtop Alp west to Netheril River

Watercourse and Tributaries

At the delta of the Watercourse, where it met the Narrow Sea, the Marsh of Simplicity was a dangerous swampy area occupied by lizardmen until the Anauroch overran it. was itself fed by the Hadely River that flowed from the Columns of the Sky. In the plains further south, the Tempera Channel was a system of canals designed to water thousands of acres of crops.

Heip Lake and Tributaries

In the central plains of western Netheril, Heip Lake was an important source of bass and trout, which were exported across Netheril and Delzoun.. It was fed by the whitewaters of the Wont Surge and extremely long Zweihaus River to the south, as well as the Conch River, considered sacred by the Rengarth, and its tributary Pommel Estuary from the Columns of the sky in the north.

Zweihaus River was named after two extremely long houses that were once said to float on the river , and aside from the Westview River that supported farms in the western plains of Netheril , the Zweihaus had a number of tributaries in the far southwest. The southernmost Moray River was known for its clear waters and eels Just north of the Moray, Redguard Lake was believed to be haunted by undead after a slaughter of Angardt by kobolds long ago, and was fed by the Sunderiver, a favorite fishing spot of the Angardt.

Basin Lake and Southeastern Rivers

Basin Lake was a deep lake created by an earthquake in southeastern Netheril, stocked with fish by followers of Jannath, and fed by Jackal Crick, as well as the Hale River which flowed through Opus from the God's Legion Mountains to the East. A low area to the southwest of the basin created the Marsh of Amnesty.

  • Wolfsberg River connected the basin to the Netheril River to the north, and other rivers to the south, including the Hungry River which had poor fishing, the Esslin River which had excellent fishing.
  • Moran River flowed from the God's Legion Mountains and collected in a pool in the Eastern Flats.
  • The Pox The Apothec nation that Netheril traded with was wiped out by a plague that originated in this river that flowed from Moander's Footstep into the eastern flats.

Hidden Lake and Tributaries

Hidden Lake was nestled between the Shinantra Mountains to the west, Lodestar Mountains to the east, and Hidden Forest to the north in the middle of the plains of Netheril. A number of settlements on the banks sustainably harvested lumber to avoid conflict with Slyphs of Hidden Forest, and steered clear of the trolls in caves on the southeastern banks. It was fed by a number of tributaries.

  • From the Shinantra Mountains, Cede Run was considered holy by pilgrims of Jannath who journeyed up it to visit an oracle at the heart of the river, Cede Pool.
  • Dahla River, a gentle stream with very clean water also flowed from the Shinantra mountains.
  • From the Lodestar Mountains, the torrents of Kraal Brook, the winding Southfill River near Canlespiere, and the Tarn River near Algrid all flowed into Hidden Lake.

Uncategorized

  • Hairald Cascade and the waterfalls of Canton River flowed from the Rampant peaks into Snarefell Lake
  • Fed by Filibeg River to the north Mill Lake was known as a good fishing spot before two failed irrigation projects created the Marsh of Deception around it, and the Lark-Rindle swamp to the southeast.
  • Flux River Far southwest, in the rocky hills of the Flats, it spilled into a chasm and had a large waterfall that supported a local mill.
  • Karsus Overwatch, or the Winding Water, in the Farhorn Forest was within view of the Karsus Enclave.
  • The Moss In the Far Horn Forest West of the Purple Mountains, a swampy valley with quicksands fed from pipes below.

Islands

  • The Channel Lock The portion of the Channel Mountains half sunken as they crossed the Narrow Sea from north to south, which resulted in a chain of islands and poorly navigable straights that split the Narrow Sea.
  • The Paws, or Fivetoes, were five uninhabited islands in Paw Bay important to the Rengarth for a seal hunt each fall.

Built Locations

Netheril - Narrow Sea and North

  • Arctic Rim Established to keep an eye on the Rengrath Tundra, this small watchpost was the northenmost settlement of Netheril and fell to ruin far before Netheril did.
  • Harborage On an island in the Channel Lock Mountains, Harborage was important for trade as it provided portage across a stretch of shallow Narrow Sea, until was destroyed by a volcano during Karsus's Folly.
  • Northreach, or Runlatha. One of the oldest Netherese cities, it was established at the entrance to the Bay of Ascore as part of a trade route with the dwarves of Delzoun and slowly faded into obscurity with the raising of the enclaves, until it was abandoned after the fall.
  • Scourge Built on the southern bank of the Narrow Sea just east of the Channel mountains, a salty wind caused metal to rapidly rust. It was plagued by earthquakes and destroyed by phaerimm shortly after the fall.
  • Sepulcher (town) A logging community on the northeastern banks of the Bay of Ascore near the Cold Forest that was threatened by orcs from the Dementia Mountains for hundreds of years prior to the fall, and abandoned decades after due to encroachment of the desert.
  • Seventon The collective name for seven villages (Gers, Gillan, Gustaf, Janick, Moran-Fenwick, and Nauseef) that started Netheril on the southeastern banks of the Narrow Sea, and were destroyed by orcs two years after the fall.
  • Zenith An early northern town on a peninsula in southwestern part of the Narrow Sea, it struggled and was forgotten after Northreach was established, but became a popular destination with Angardt and Rengarth tribes due to its weather attenuating mythallar.

Netheril - East

  • Abbey of the Moon The abbey to Selûne that gave the Abbey Mountains their name was destroyed long before the fall of Netheril.
  • Algrid A town on the southern edge of the Hidden Lake that was destroyed by phaerimm.
  • Canlespiere A former ogre city on the banks of the Southfill River taken at large costs to the Netherese, its declining population survived the fall of Netheril but succumbed to the Anauroch in the years following.
  • Conch A frontier town in the foothills of the Columns of the Sky.
  • Coniferia A prosperous town on the edge of the Eastern Forest that was wiped out by trolls after the fall.
  • Dagger's Point Founded on the shores of the Wolfsberg River as a refugee for those against the lofting of floating cities, it later became a mining community that survived the fall, potentially through agreement with the phaerimm, until the sands overtook it many years later.
  • Frothwater A farming city on the banks of the Hungry River that survived long after the fall, until the desert claimed the land.
  • Hoyden a small town founded by elves in the Hidden Forest, it became known for silver weapons and was abandoned due to orc attacks prior to the fall of Netheril.
  • Specie The breadbasket of Netheril on the Macht River, near the Marsh of Deception, relied on crop enhancing weather magic and only survived a few decades after the fall, as its field turned to sand.
  • Trinity Founded by followers of Amaunator on the southeastern shore of Hidden Lake, it served as a stop for Jannath pilgrims to the Cede River, and was abandoned in mere hours during the fall.
  • Xanth A city of xenophobic humans that repelled a huge force of orcs and celebrated when the enclave Spiel fell, was abandoned some decades later.
  • Yeoman's Loft The primary site of Spelljamming in Netheril, located north of the God's Legion Mountains near the Eastern Forest, the dock was destroyed in Karsus's Folly

Netheril - West

  • Bandor Village or Bazor was a mining community in Northwestern Netheril that succumbed to a plague long before the fall.
  • Dekanter The mines of Dekanter in the eastern Purple Mountains were an important source of ore until they were depleted, and then became an important place of magical research.
  • Fluvion A mining camp where the Wont Surge met the Zweihaus River, an area where chardalyn gems were found.
  • Grog A rather inconsequential farming town built where the Westview River fed into the Zweihaus River, it was named for its disgusting beer and abandoned prior to the fall.
  • Imbrue A centrally located farming city on the banks of Heip Lake that became home to lively gambling halls after building the House of Tyche as a temple to the god.
  • Remembrance A religious center for Jannath monks and priests on the banks of the Wont Surge near Sunrest Mountain, that pilgrims visited on the way to a temple on the Glorifier. It was slowly abandoned to sand around the time of the fall.
  • Spellgard On a ridge near the Far Horns Forest, east of the Purple Mountains, this Castle's ruler Lady Saharel became a lich and outlived Netheril, and eventually became a ghost within the ruins.
  • Unity Established a base to fight orc tribes by elves, dwarves, and humans in western Netheril, Unity had a large temple to Amaunautor, who lost favor after the fall, and it was abandoned to the sands.
  • Westwendt Village A mecha of trade in western Netheril with productive mines nearby, it saw representatives from the elven nations of Eaerlann and Illefarn as well as the dwarves of Delzoun, and was destroyed by fiends in one night prior to the fall.
  • Wreathe A hunting camp at the edge of the Far Horns Forest, at the base of the purple mountains that specialized in rare and dangerous game that was destroyed by dragons in an act of revenge prior to the fall.

Netheril - South

  • Blister built into the mountains south of Moander's Footstep as a fort to protect against aggression from the south and Eastern Forest, it was raised and buried by the Anauroch after Netheril's fall.
  • Holloway A base camp for archeological expeditions into the Hollow that survived for decades after the fall.
  • Rasilith The former capital of Thaeravel was an important southern garrison after Netheril seized it, until it was one of the first cities to be abandoned due to phaerimm attacks prior to the fall.
  • Thiefsward A city made to raise thieves, it was abandoned when the mythallar became inert after Karsus's Folly.

Neighbors

  • Ascore This dwarven city of the Delzoun nation was a major trading hub with Netheril on the western edge of the Bay of Ascore in the Narrow Sea.
  • Cantus A short lived goblin town south of Ascore that was sacked multiple times before being abandoned.
  • Coldfoot A Rengarth city established on the Northern coast of the Narrow Sea as a permanent trade settlement with the Netherese.
  • Frosty Paw The primary Rengarth port in the Rengarth Ancestral Lands on the banks of the western Narrow Sea was abandoned as the High Ice advanced.
  • Kismet Built by the nomadic Angardt tribes as a permanent trade location, and a holy site in southern Netheril.
  • Monikar Built in the Eastern Forest by former netherese against the use of magic, the citizens worshiped Kozah and helped aid in the fall of Netheril, then killed those that sought refugee in its walls, until it was destroyed by Hlondathan forces during the era of the Lost Kingdoms.
  • Oreme In the plains west of Widowmaker Peak, the buried ruins of this ancient Isstosseffifil city were ruled by Sarrukh liches including Terraseer, who disguised as the arcanist Arthindol introduced the Netherese to the Nether Scrolls.
  • Vandal Station Named for the Rengarth tribe that established it on the Southern Banks of the Bay of Ascore, it served as a base to patrol the Rengarth Ancestral Lands, but began to decline with the plague from Bandor Village and was completely abandoned after a mysterious cold killed everyone one winter well before the fall of Netheril.
