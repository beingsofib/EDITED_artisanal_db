The Shrouded Lands was a remote stretch of land located on the island of Gwynneth within the Moonshae Isles. It was named for the tendency of fog rolling off the Trackless Sea to settle over the region as it became trapped against The Highlands.

Geography

The Shrouded Lands were a westward hollow on the slopes of the Highlands wedged between those mountains and the Meirig Peatlands along the western coast of Gwynneth. It was a region of rolling hills and uncultivated plains and pasturelands that stretched between the Toadstool Thicket in the north to Corwell Firth in the southeast.

Flora & Fauna

The region was was fertile, verdant, and grassy, making it ideal for grazing animals and livestock like rothé. It was thus also ideal hunting grounds for wild drakes and giants. The soil became increasingly sodden and acidic as it sloped down into the Meirig Peatlands.

Inhabitants

During the 14 century DR, the Shrouded Lands were considered a remote borderland north of Corwell. It took about six days to travel through the region from Caer Corwell in the south into Myrloch Vale via the Aspenheight Gap in the north.

As of the late 15 century DR, ettins and firbolgs dwelt in the Shrouded Lands, and competed with each other over hunting grounds.
