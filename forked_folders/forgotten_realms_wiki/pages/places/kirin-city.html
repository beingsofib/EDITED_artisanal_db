Kirin, formerly known as Hai Sheng, was a city in Hungtse Province in Shou Lung. It was the third capital of the empire of Shou Lung.

Geography

Found in the far northeast corner of Hungtse, near the border with Ti Erte Province, the city sat at the mouth of the Ch'ing Jung River where it flowed into the Celestial Sea. Trails from here led west by the Yang Road to Linshung, north to Tsingtao, and south by the Southern Waypath to Karatin.

History

Following the Sharing of Meals during the Ho Dynasty, the Imperial Capital was relocated from Hsi-Feng to the city of Hai Sheng in .

However, the Ivory Plague struck Hai Sheng in . Many citizens died in the following years, Finally, in , the city saw a visitation by an enormous ki-rin that saved the people from their inevitable death and ordered the surviving members of the Imperial family to leave. Afterward, the city was renamed Kirin in its honor and the Imperial Capital was relocated to Kuo Te' Lung in Wang Kuo Province.

Culture

Following the ki-rin's saving of the city, the people there believed the city was still divinely protected by the Celestial Emperor.

Notable Locations

Kirin was home to the White Crane Temple, home of the Order of the White Crane.

Notable Inhabitants
