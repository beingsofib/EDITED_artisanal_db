Corwell Road

The Corwell Road was the only inland trade route that passed through the heart of Corwell on the isle of Gwynneth in the Moonshaes.

Geography

It stretched from Kingsbay in the east to Caer Corwell in the west, before continuing as a lesser road onto Pontswain further west. For much of the Road's span, the foreboding Llyrath Forest was visible to the south.

Description

The Road was a well-engineered thoroughfare paved with smooth, white stones. It ran remarkably straight for long stretches, rising sharply at others, These cantrevs, sometimes consisting of little more than an inn, lined the road and were spaced to ensure that travelers had somewhere to stay each night of their journey. Lodgings were comfortable and reasonably priced.

By the late 15 century DR, long after the fall of Corwell, the road fell into ruin. It was largely reclaimed by nature, although it could still be discerned in some places.
