Norland was a large island in the Moonshae Isles. It was inhabited primarily by Northlanders.

Geography

Norland was the westernmost of the Northlander-ruled Moonshae Isles, and was located in the northwest of the archipelago, north of Moray and southeast of the Norheim Isles. and the glacier-capped North Jotunhammer Mountains that traversed the island from north to south. Sheltered to the east of these mountains were the broad lowlands of Norland, characterized by the massive Frostfenn, This eastern coast was dotted with sheltered coves, gravel beaches, and shallow waters. The seas along the northeastern coast were teeming with fish, notably cod, herring, and salmon.

Virtually no roads or trails existed through the forested interior of the island, meaning overland treks were arduous.

Government

Norland

The human Kingdom of Norland dominated most of the island, ruled from the city of Rogarsheim. a title held by Grunnarch the Red in the mid–14 century DR and by Rault the Wise in the late 15 century DR.

Grondsgrunn

The southern tip of the island spanning the South Jotunhammer Mountains was colonized by the giant realm of Grondsgrunn, inhabited mostly by firbolgs. By the late 15 century DR, the largest band of firbolgs was led by Lallargh, who aspired to dislodge the humans of Norland.

History

Circa , the first firbolgs arrived on Norland, led by Grond Peaksmasher.

After the Ffolk first established themselves in the Moonshaes in the 2 century DR, a few made their way to Norland and established small kingdoms there, alongside a number of halflings. Northlanders would begin raiding the isolated Ffolk kingdoms of the region in the 3 century DR, and would soon desire to conquer and settle their lands.

By the 14 century DR, there were no more functioning moonwells on Norland as they had all long ago fallen into disuse.

With the rise of House Kendrick in the mid–14th century DR, the High Kings and Queens of Caer Callidyrr exerted nominal control over the island.

As of the , the Northlanders of the isle rallied under the leadership of the warrior maiden known as the Storm Maiden. During the following decade, she led her followers on a mad conquest of the Sea of Swords, which ended in many of their deaths—including the heirs to the Norl throne—and the Storm Maiden's own disappearance at sea.

At some point in the 1480s DR, the red dragon Hoondarrh attacked the island, annihilating at least one coastal settlement.

Notable Locations

The largest settlement on the isle was Rogarsheim.

Inhabitants

The population was a mix of Northlanders, Ffolk, and dwarves.

Although the forested interior of the island was largely uncharted and difficult to navigate, journeys through these woods were surprisingly safe except for the occasional brown bear, cave bear, or wolf attack. This was because there were very few monsters thanks to centuries of Northlander warriors culling the local populations of orcs, trolls, and the like. The remaining humanoid monsters had largely been driven into the mountains, where they lived near the island's giants, notably frost giants and firbolgs.

The mountains were home to white dragons in the far north and far south, as well as blue dragons in the south.
