Ravens Bluff waterfall

The Ravens Bluff waterfall was a geological feature among the Vastian city's hills that was considered unique for it being upwelling – its waters emerged from a fissure in the ground, coming off of an underground stream. The exact origins of the fall's water was unknown to most Ravenians.

Description

These roaring falls fell off a cliff at a height of and seemingly vanishing into the ground.

Roughly above the base of the falls, situated behind the waterfall itself, was a ledge running along the cliff face that protruded out . Leading from the middle of this ledge was an opening in the cliff face, a crevice measuring high and wide. The narrow, mist choked passage, opened into a cave that housed a one-way portal to the Abyss. On all but the night of a new moon, only tanar'ri could enter this portal.

The rocky surface at its bottom, seen by very few Ravenians, was exceedingly wet, slippery and covered in both algae and a constant thick mist. This mist billowed upwards, thinning out at above the ground, The bottom of the falls were aptly named Bottomless Lake, but beneath it lay a portal to the Elemental Plane of Water.

History

At some point in the 14 century DR, a bard attempted to explore the cave behind the falls and therein met his demise. The bard left behind a mist tent, hovering invisibly above the cave's entrance, and it would remain there for several years. Several years later, a nabassu moved into the cave Feeling its time on Prime Material was finished and anxious to return home to the Abyss,

That day a group of wandering adventurers witnessed Sir FM Lillywhite, being accosted by the faux brigands. The disguised nabassu than fled from the scene holding a reproduction of the teapot. When questioned as to what had occurred, FM Lillywhite explained to the adventurers that the teapot contained his soul and implored them to retrieve it. Rather than engaging them in combat, the adventurers explained why they wanted the teapot. Kantor then agreed to go with the adventurers to confront FM Lillywhite and his heretical claims of the Holy Container housing his soul, bringing along with him the relic after having cast weighty chest upon it.

This confrontation went down at the Trende Cafe, where a feigning illness Lillywhite tried to offer all manner of valuables in exchange for the teapot – initially 60 platinum pieces and six air spores, then eventually throwing in an everbountiful soup kettle and jar of preserving out of desperation. Meanwhile, Kantor was fuming and outraged, cursing Lillywhite and trying to provoke him into a fight. The Field Surgeon's volatile behavior in such a public venue resulted in the adventurers having to restrain him. Regardless of whichever way it went down, sources agree that Krag then lead the party to the Ravens Bluff waterfall.

Over the deafening roar of the falls, Krag shouted, "Trust in Tempus, just leap over the edge here, and he will guide you to your destiny!", having foreknowledge of a hidden ledge. Down they went, at last confronting the horrid nabassu and its ghast victims.

Around the , during the Three Women War, the magics of the Ministry of Art somehow interfered with the portal at the base of the waterfall.
