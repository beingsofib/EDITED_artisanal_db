The Calishar Emirates was a vast expanse of land that made up the northern territories of the Calimshan Empire.

Geography

The Emirates encompassed the various frontier regions of the Empire as it expanded north. Areas close to the homeland were ruled directly from Calimport, including Coramshan, the Purple Marches, Mir, Tethyr, and Iltkazar, while lands further afield were governed locally by the Pasha's emirs. These colonial regions included Amin, Meiritin, Minsorran, Valashar, and the Ralamnish Ridings, reaching as far north as the Greenfields.

History

The Emirates came about from the wide-ranging conquests and settlement that the Calimshan Empire undertook in the Third Age ( to ).

The vast region was ostensibly under Calimshan's control, but faced repeated internal uprisings and resistance from the many enemies that Calimshan had made over the centuries. Tethyrian human tribes and rebellious slaves, dwarves of Shanatar, elves of Tethir, and drow raiders all picked away at the stability and control that Calimshan sought over its northern frontier.

In the , the Empire Plague struck the cities of Calimshan and caused a collapse of central authority. Although some generals and nobles maintained power, the Empire lost control of the Calishar Emirates and their colonies on the Lake of Steam. The native Tethyrians took to open and unified rebellion and managed to capture Zazesspur in , and the people and their lands were formally given independence in . After another attempt at reconquest between and , Tethyr was solidified as an independent and unified monarchy outside of Calishite control.

Tethyr effectively lost its independence after most of the royal family and many nobles were killed by rebels and pirates in the , leading to the installation of Amahl Shoon I by Calimshan and the reclamation of much of the empire's lands via the Shoon Imperium.

Renewed colonization and settlement in the north led to founding of Athkatla in 100 DR, Crimmor in 163 DR, and Murann in 212 DR. These became the core of the Emirate of Amin, which eventually became the kingdom of Amn.

The Mad Mage Ilhundyl set about terrorizing the region in 231 DR, conquering the largest settlement in Meiritin and declaring himself the ruler of all the Emirates. His cruelty went largely unchecked due to internal unrest in the Imperium that delayed a response, and only ended in 238 DR when he was slain by the aspiring wizard Elminster and his mistress Myrjala.

The vassal kingdom of Valashar was founded in the Ralamnish Ridings in the by Ashar Tornamn of Tethyr, who led a campaign of overly-ambitious expansionism that briefly extended the Emirates as far as the distant High Moor. This resulted in provoking a disastrous war with Cormyr in 376 DR that led to his own execution, and the abandonment of Valashar until the later settlement of Cortryn.

Around 650 DR, the city of Myth Drannor had a collection of rare and exotic plants in the Glim-Gardens, including flame lilies taken from the Calishar.
