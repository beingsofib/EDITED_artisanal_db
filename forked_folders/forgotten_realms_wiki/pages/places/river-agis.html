River Agis

The River Agis flowed from the east out of the Marching Mountains and into the Trackless Sea near the city of Memnon.

It served as the border between Calimshan and Tethyr ever since the Eye Tyrant Wars.

Description

The river was wide and slow-moving and very suitable for cargo-carrying barges and lumber.

History

The river became the border between Tethyr and Calimshan in the , when the syl-pasha of Calimport, Kamus el Tarshaj, ceded the lands of Monrativi Teshy Mir over to Tethyr—supposedly as a reward for Tethyr's assistance in the Eye Tyrant Wars more than a decade earlier.
