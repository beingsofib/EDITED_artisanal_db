Armada Ward was the westernmost ward of the four Port Wards of the city of Calimport. Its enclosed docks comprised the naval portion of the wards, housing the armories, garrisons, and ships of el Nallojal, the navy of Calimshan.

Government

In the 14 century DR, the eldest son of Syl-Pasha Ralan el Pesarkhal, Fahd yn Ralan el Pesarkhal, was the Sultan of the Armada Ward. In addition to other duties, he was also the Syl-Sultan of el Nallojal itself.

Sabbans

The Armada Ward was divided into two sabbans and six drudachs.

Djenispool Sabban

Though official documentation labeled it the Djenispool Sabban, most sailors and officers took care to refer to it only as the "Wharf Sabban" or more simply "The Wharf", due to the name being one of the few remnants of the prior ruling dynasty of Calimshan.

The sabban dominated 65% of the Armada Ward's total area, being composed of the docks proper and a few buildings, namely the ones that collected dock fees, the equipment warehouses of the League of Harbormasters, and a few minor businesses. Its docks could harbor two-thirds of the entire Nallojal fleet at any given time.

The sabban mark of Djenispool Sabban was a genie's lamp, prominently marked with a black six-pointed star, and pouring water out of its spout. It was divided into three drudachs—Fashol Drudach, Nahla Drudach, and the Nowm Drudach.

Tavihr Sabban

Also known as the "Hammock Sabban", this sabban comprised the armory and barracks of the naval armada. Its official name was derived from the Tavihr Dynasty, one of the more militaristic of the ancient bakkal dynasties of Calimshan. Its alternate title was derived from a unique facet of el Nallojal's garrisons—the bunks for seamen consisted of hammocks, two stories above the ground from ceiling beams and pillars. And in place of floors or stairs, the only way to get around were riggings and climbing ropes.

Though it comprised only 35% of the ward's total area, it housed 90% of its buildings and businesses. Of these buildings, more than half were owned by the city and dedicated to the service of managing and coordinating el Nallojal. Many of the businesses in this sabban were operated by former Nallojal seamen. From them one could find almost any form of maritime service or support trade.

The sabban mark of Tavihr depicted a black anchor. It was divided into three drudachs—Hikal Drudach, Khadra Drudach, and the Seahrm Drudach.

Inhabitants

Whilst civilians were more than allowed to enter the Armada Ward, those looking to cause trouble were more than likely to get press-ganged into active service and shuffled off onto one of el Nallojal's ships for a tour of duty lasting three months. The only visitors that could avoid such a fate were those who had already voluntarily signed up for the navy and those that had connections to a pasha or someone of an even higher social ranking.

History

Around the year , the Drakhon Dynasty expanded and restructured the Armada Ward and other waterfront areas of Calimport that had been destroyed six years prior by the Villaflames conflagration. This would lead to the construction of the defenses and massive stone docks that would grace the ward for centuries to come. It was also around this period that Syl-Pasha Kamal el Drakhon commissioned the formation of El Qysanallojal.

Over the course of 1350 DR to 1370 DR, the Syl-Pashas of the El Djenispool and El Pesarkhal dynasties expanded upon the docks of Armada Ward, making it reach its two-thirds capacity. In particular, Syl-Pasha Rashid Djenispool commissioned much of the work done on the ward. He went on to become the namesake of the Djenispool Sabban and the only reason it would retain the Djenispool name in official documentation.
