Mystra's Temple in Hastarl was a place of worship in the 3 century DR.

Structure

The two front corners of the roof were adorned by bat-winged effigies that clutched columns on the front of the temple. There were faintly glowing letters over the double door at the entrance.

Interior

By , the temple was largely dilapidated, but there were stone columns inside in the entry hall; the space was large enough that echoes were expected. The great circular chamber had a very high roof, a bare circular stone altar at its center, and three rows of balconies on the walls.

Atmosphere

It was possible to make light appear by calling for it.

Activities

Presumably, the temple had been used for worship at some point.

Defenses

During the reign of the Magelords of Athalantar, the temple doors had been locked; there were other spells inside preventing any echoes, and warning one of the magelords if anyone got in.

History

After the Magelords of Athalantar took control of that kingdom, the Temple of Mystra was closed to the public, and wards were placed by Magelord Ildru, warning him of any intruders.

During late fall or early winter of , Ildru captured Eladar the Dark, a thief who was secretly Prince Elminster Aumar after the latter broke into the temple, but before he could execute him, he was stopped by direct intervention from Mystra.
