Gwynneth was an island in the Moonshae Isles traditionally settled by the Ffolk, and the largest island in the archipelago. As of the late 15 century DR, Gwynneth was called Sarifal by its fey inhabitants, who controlled most of the island by that time.

Description

Gwynneth was an island of lush nature Following the rise of Sarifal, it had become a breathtakingly beautiful, wild, and magical place by the late 15 century DR.

Among the druids of the Moonshaes, it was said that Gwynneth was the most sacred of the isles to the Earthmother.

Geography

Gwynneth lay south of the Sea of Moonshae, with Oman's Isle and Alaron to its north, Snowdown to its east, and Moray and Flamsterd to its west.

The forest of Winterglen dominated the northeast of the isle while the forest of Llyrath covered much of the southern coast. and open, level plains in the east.

The island was bounded to the east by the Strait of Alaron and to the north by the Strait of Oman, with these waters meeting at the northernmost tip of the island known as the Gray Headlands. Much of the western coast along the Strait of the Leviathan was dominated by tall granite cliffs or poisonous bogs with few beaches or inlets, meaning the only safe harbor on this side of the island was at the terminus of Corwell Firth where Caer Corwell sat. For much of the year, fog rolling off the Trackless Sea would settle on the west coast as it butted against the Highlands, leading to this region being dubbed the Shrouded Lands. Off the southeastern coast was the underwater forest of Tír faoi Thoinn.

Flora & Fauna

Unicorns could be spotted in the wild places of the island. After the Ffolk were driven from the island in the 15 century DR, the pastoral southern lands where they had once settled became overrun with wild moorhounds and violent owlbears.

Government

Corwell

The south of Gwynneth had long been the domain of the Ffolk Kingdom of Corwell, ruled by the kings and queens of House Kendrick from Caer Corwell. This realm began to shrink as fey overran the island beginning in the late 14 century DR before finally collapsing in the early 15 century DR.

Oman

The northern coast of Gwynneth had fallen under the control of the Northlander Kingdom of Oman as of the mid–14 century DR. When Oman's Isle was overrun by giants in the mid-to-late 14 century DR, the Kingdom collapsed. The Northlander settlers on Gwynneth would eventually be expelled by the fey at the same time as the Ffolk of Corwell in the early 15 century DR.

Sarifal

The fey who claimed Gwynneth as their own in the 15 century DR comprised the Kingdom of Sarifal. It was ruled by High Lady Ordalf and her son, Prince Araithe, and generally did not allow humans to set foot on the island. Although they claimed the whole of the island, their rule was resisted by giants in the Highlands, fomorians in the Underdark, and human holdouts at Borth and Kingsbay in the east.

Synnoria

Hidden in a secret valley was the ancient Kingdom of Synnoria, home of the Llewyrr high elves of the Moonshaes. Long ruled by the Most Serene Matriarch Ate`Niah, this realm swore allegiance to Sarifal as of the late 15 century DR.

History

In ancient times, the leShay had founded their capital city of Karador on Gwynneth before retreating into the Feywild many centuries before the arrival of the first humans. During their reign, the first of the Llewyrr elves and dwarves would settle the island.

Gwynneth was the site of the first human settlements on the Moonshaes when the ancestors of the Ffolk arrived in the . It was named for Gwynneth "the First Mother", who had led them to the island and founded the settlement that would become Caer Corwell. After conflicts with the Llewyrr and amongst each other, the Ffolk would become the dominant inhabitants of the island until the arrival of the first Northmen raiders in the 3 century DR.

By the mid–14 century DR, the entire northern coast of Gwynneth as far south as the mountains of Highhome was under the control of the Northman kingdom of Oman, including some Ffolk settlements. The Northlander towns were frequently menaced by firbolgs from the Cambro Mountains, and were no friends to the Ffolk druids who dwelt in Winterglen.

In the , Ityak-Ortheel "the Elf Eater" attacked Synnoria and rampaged across part of Gwynneth.

By the mid-to-late 14 century DR, the Ffolk and the Northlanders had come to live together in peace, and the greatest threat to Gwynneth were Nelanther pirates along the southern coasts.

In the , the fey city of Karador rose once more from the waters of the Myrloch on the heart of Gwynneth, and the noble leShay High Lady Ordalf proclaimed the return of the realm of Sarifal with herself as queen of the whole island. The human kingdom of Corwell subsequently fell into ruins. Humans would be all but banned from the island during the 15 century DR.

Following the Spellplague, magic infused some of the woodlands that had once been felled by the Ffolk, causing them to grow back as fierce places that the fey dared not to tread.

In the late 15 century DR, the island came under attack by the red dragon Karrnsyrrl and its allies. Much of the south of the island was scorched until the dragon was driven off by a ki-rin named Daiwon. Taking advantage of this chaos, the fomorians of Oman's Isle attempted to invade from the north while lycanthropes began attacking the south. These threats were ultimately thwarted by foreign adventurers in accordance with something known as the Prophecy of Ash and Flame.

Inhabitants

For most of its history, Northlanders lived on the northern part of the island and the Ffolk lived in the southern lowlands of the island. The Llewyrr elves lived in Synnoria and firbolgs lived in Myrloch Vale.

In the Underdark beneath the island lived drow and fomorians. The drow lived beneath Winterglen in the northeast, and practiced an unusual version of the Lolthite religion in which they honored their goddess as a multifaceted version of her ancient identity, Araushnee. The fomorians were divided into nine clans, and over generations had overseen the construction of a long highway underground that followed the Cambro Mountains.

Notable Locations

  • Caer Corwell, the former seat of House Kendrick, the Kingdom of Corwell, its capital city, and the castle at castle at Caer Corwell lay in ruin by the late 15 century DR.
  • Highhome, the primary realm of the dwarves of the Moonshae Isles as of the 14 century DR.
  • Myrloch Vale, a valley that occupied the central part of the island. It was known to be home to the elves and dwarves who predated the humans of the Moonshaes.
  • Karador, a magical city in the middle of Lake Myrloch that was home to 3,000 fey. The city contained a mythal that was capable of transporting the city to the Feywild.
  • Sacred Grove of the Earthmother, a druid grove on the eastern shore of Lake Myrloch which contained a moonwell.
  • Winterglen, a dense forest in the northern part of Gwynneth. The interior of the forest was corrupted by the power of the Shadowfell, although the outer edges were relatively normal.
  • Citadel Umbra, a castle in the middle of Winterglen that sat at an intersection of Faerûn, the Feywild and the Shadowfell. The castle was home to many dark fey and was ruled by Urphania, a hag who was banished from the Feywild by High Lady Ordalf.
