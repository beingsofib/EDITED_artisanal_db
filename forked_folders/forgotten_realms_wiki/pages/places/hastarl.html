Hastarl was the capital city of Athalantar in the 2 to 4 centuries DR. Secomber was later built upon its ruins.

Description

Like most cities, Hastarl was crowded and dirty, as the city was dense rather than sprawling.

The northern side of the city held a number of rich houses, with spired towers.

There was an old burial ground across the city from the Kissing Wench festhall; it was walled, had trees planted, and had already grown dilapidated by .

Side streets to the right of one of the ports held residences without walls, where well-off but not wealthy people lived

Geography

Geographical Features

Hastarl was built on the crossing of the Delimbiyr River and the Unicorn Run.

Demographics

The population was about 17,000 people in the early-to-mid 3 century DR, reaching 26,000 at its peak.

Government

Hastarl was the capital of Athalantar.

Trade

During the reign of Belaur Aumar, the Magelords of Athalantar did not allow guilds in the city.

Sometimes, traders of Calishite silks whose stocks hadn't run dry closer to the sea made the trip to Hastarl to trade.

Defenses

Hastarl was a walled city.

History

Hastarl was destroyed in , by an orcish horde.

The ruins were reclaimed by the founding of Secomber in the .

Notable Locations

  • The Kissing Wench, a festhall
  • Ansildabar's Last Rest
  • The mage Orthalar owned a house in the city.
  • The baker Hannibur had a bakery. There were silver-bit baths nearby.
  • The trader Havilyn had a home, with sewers fed by and discharging into the Unicorn Run, close to the docks; the location was in the same zone as the upscale houses.
  • The trader Fentarn had a home, too.
  • The trader Nalith owned at least one warehouse.
  • There were shrines to Sune, Helm, Lathander, Tyche, and Tyr. There was also a temple of Mystra, in disuse during the reign of King Belaur.
  • Upriver past the wall (almost certainly the north wall, upstream along the Unicorn Run) there was a refuse pit around which traders often camped.
  • West of the city, outside the wall, there was a nameless shrine of Mystra on a grave-knoll, surrounded by a low wall.
