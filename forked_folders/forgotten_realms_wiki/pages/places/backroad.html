Backroad

A backroad, also known as a hidden path, was a mystical pathway of geomagical energy that connected two distant locations within the Prime Material plane. An uncountable number of backroads crisscrossed Faerûn, and entering a backroad allowed one to instantly traverse the full length of its path.

Description

Backroads did not correspond to any physical or observable roads, and could not be seen, felt, or tracked by nonmagical means. Only fey truly understood the backroads, allowing them to enter and navigate them more freely than nonfey. While travel along the backroads bore some resemblance to extraplanar travel, the backroads were actually a natural feature of the Prime Material plane.

Nonfey could only enter the backroads by a fey crossroads, and could only follow a single backroad to its predetermined destination (meaning they served nonfey more as portals than as roadways). Nonfey best understood the backroads as folds or pinches within the Prime Material plane that brought two distant points into close proximity.

Finding the entrance to a backroad could only be reliable done with a spell such as detect crossroads. A backroad could be created with the spell create crossroads and backroad, which was believed to be known only to some fey and druids.

History

Fey were the longtime guardians of the backroads, although they believed that they belonged to no one.

When the drow of Maerimydra established their Land Under Shadow in (in the area that would come to be known as Shadowdale), they were vexed by the high concentration of fey crossings and backroads in the area. They thus released a terrible monster called the Susurrus into the backroads, forcing the local fey to abandon them.

Because so few nonfey knew of the backroads or how they worked, many considered them to be merely legends. Faerûnian druids referred to the backroads as the "Hidden Paths", and had made efforts to map them. As of the mid-to-late 14 century DR, they had only discovered a relatively small number of them, and fey refused to share the secrets of the backroads with them or anyone else as they feared the possibility of the pathways becoming corrupted. A common use of the backroads among these druids was for communication as sound could freely travel the full length of a backroad between two fey crossroads.

The Selûnite Swords of the Lady were believed to have access to a number of backroads, which allowed them to move swiftly across the land to strike at their Sharran enemies.
