The Serpent's Demise was a boisterous inn and tavern in the city of Taruin, Samarach.

Services

The Serpent's Demise offered drinks and food to its guests as well as lodgings. The establishment was among very few places open to non-citizens of Samarach. The tavern's taproom was constructed in a traditional way for drinking establishments in Samarach. The taproom was built in the shape of a cross. Tables, booths, and places to mingle were placed at each end of the structure. At the center stood the bar from where the innkeeper did business. The Serpent's Demise employed musicians and dancers who entertained its guests.

History

In the , the Serpent's Demise was frequented by a wine connoisseur named Ottelmar who sought out adventurers brave enough to acquire him rare deepwine from a mind flayer black market.
