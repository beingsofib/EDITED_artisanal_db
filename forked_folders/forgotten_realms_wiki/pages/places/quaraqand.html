Quaraqand was a city in the Plain of Horses of northwestern Kara-Tur. It was the home of the Igidujin tribe.

Geography

The city stood deep in the southern Chigidi Mountains, leaving it quite isolated.

Government

The city was ruled by the cruel and despotic Qaland family.

Law & Order

Under the vicious law of the Qalands, thieves and protestors were executed, no trial necessary. However, the most common crime in Quaraqand was apparently treason, and resulted in the alleged traitor, their family, and even their livestock all being slain.

Furthermore, Qaland T'akk forced those who served him to kill enemies according to weekly quotas. Failure to kill enough was of course punished by execution, by T'akk himself.

Activities

From Quaraqand, the Igidujin often raided the surrounding villages, nomad camps, and caravans, especially those of the Fankiang tribe, whom T'akk hated. Ostensibly, most of these raids were to steal Steppe horses but in truth it was largely to sate their bloodlust. They massacred their victims, leaving no man, woman, or child alive.

Defenses

The Igidujin favored clubs, both the war club known as chokhor modo and the throwing club called a sidam. Their commanders also employed hawks trained to attack targets.
