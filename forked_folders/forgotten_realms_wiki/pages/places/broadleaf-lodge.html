Broadleaf Lodge was a bardic college on the island of Snowdown in the Moonshae Isles founded during the mid-to-late 14 century DR by the bard Donalbain.

Location

The Lodge was located a half-day's ride west of Brannoch. From the muddy road between Brannoch and Harloch, a cart track headed into the nearby foothills to the building. It sat about halfway up a steep hill surrounded by a dense stand of evergreens.

Structure

The lodge was a two-story wooden building attached to a large, three-story circular stone tower with a diameter of . The building had three chimneys (two in the main building and one in the tower) which connected to several fireplaces such that almost every room contained a fireplace. Even so, the lodge was chill during winter months. Shutters on the windows afforded some additional protection from inclement weather.

Interior

Ground Floor

The front door entered into the great hall, a drafty room with a large flagstone fireplace, a huge dire bear pelt rug, and several pieces of old but comfortable furniture.

A set of stairs on the far wall from the front door ascended to the second level of the tower while a hallway led to the rest of the ground floor. The doors on the left-side of this hall led to the working and living areas of the servants who prepared meals for the bards, cleaned the lodge, and maintained the livestock. This area included the kitchen and the pantry, from where stairs led down to a root cellar that contained a freshwater well. On the opposite side of the hall were Donalbain's private quarters and a lecture hall, which was laid out like a classroom but was more often used to hold debates and discussions, to hear a visiting bard speak, or to demonstrate novel uses of magic. This room had a large canvas map of the Moonshae Isles.

Second Floor

The stairs from the great hall arrived in a library on the second story of the tower. This was known as the lore hall, and was full of floor-to-ceiling bookshelves secured to the walls and to each other with iron brackets. The library's collection was impressive even by the standards of most sages, and contained over a thousand volumes covering topics including alchemy, crafting, geography, heraldry, history, language, magic, music, poetry, and warfare. Every book, map scroll, and spellbook had the spell magic mouth cast upon it, which would make a racket if anyone tried to remove it from the lore hall.

Down a hallway from the lore hall were five rooms set aside as guest quarters. These were simple accommodations with no locks, and each room contained two beds, two chests, a washbasin, and a table with chairs. At the end of this hall was the solar, which had large, southward-facing windows that kept the room well-lit and afforded a grand view of the countryside. This room was often used for private activities such as reading or writer rather than for meetings.

Third Floor

The tower stairs continued up from the lore hall to the top level of the tower, which contained a high-ceilinged music hall. Unlike other rooms in the tower, the stones were covered with wood panels and then the whole of the walls and ceiling were coated in white plaster, giving the room exquisite acoustic qualities and making it an ideal venue for musical performances. Hooks along the northern side of the tower were hung with musical instruments that guests were welcomed to play.

Activities

While the lodge was a place in which bards could learn and teach, these were not necessarily the primary goals of Broadleaf Lodge. Resident and visiting bards alike met in the lodge to share knowledge, help each other to solve mysteries, conduct research, or practice their music. In the music hall, bards were welcomed to perform their music, including singing and dancing, or to recite poetry and perform plays. Newly created or rediscovered works were especially encouraged, and Donalbain frequently opened these performances to the public.

The Lodge had only a limited ability to host visitors, and any who found the guest quarters insufficient or too crowded were encouraged to find lodging in nearby Brannoch or with neighboring farmers.

On occasion, Donalbain hosted briefings among Ffolk bards about serious threats to the Moonshaes, which might conclude with the decision to send out spies to counter these threats.

Defenses

Hidden beneath the moth-eaten dire bearskin rug in the great hall was a secret trapdoor that led to a chamber large enough to hide eight people. Donalbain kept this panic room stocked with enough food and water to last the occupants for a tenday, and additionally used it to conceal a number of magic items for use in emergencies, namely an enchanted longsword, four potions of healing, and a scroll of glyph of warding.

History

The Lodge itself was constructed by Sir Miles Broadleaf, a knight from Silverymoon who retired to Snowdown to pursue his passion for hunting. When he died in the , the Lodge fell into disrepair. It was then acquired by Donalbain in the , who fixed the structure and transformed it into a bustling bardic college. By the , it was rare not to find bards congregating in the great hall at all hours of the day or perusing the famous library, which attracted bards and their companions from far and wide seeking to conduct research. Whenever the music hall hosted a famous performer, people from as far as Caer Callidyrr were known to journey to Broadleaf Lodge to see the performances.

Inhabitants

In addition to Donalbain himself, the lodge employed several servants. As of 1372 DR, these included three women named Branwyn, Dara, and Shannon, all of whom hailed from Brannoch and were under Donalbain's protection.
