Mhandyvver's Poultry was one of two poultry stores in the town of Red Larch.

Geography

Mhandyvver's Poultry was located along the Long Road, next to Luruth's Tannery and across from Vallivoe's Sundries.

Interior

The interior of this store resembled a barn, with numerous exposed beams and posts. Pens for livestock filled most of the building's odd corners and halls, leaving only a narrow aisle down to the back where the staff and owner lived. These living quarters were separated from the livestock pens by a workroom that had cutting benches and a central hearth.

Services

Mhandyvver's Poultry produced fowl, fresh eggs, and chicks for raising. These fowl included turkeys, chickens, and ducks whose wings had been clipped. They sold this poultry either roasted or preserved in oil, while eggs were either sold fresh or pickled. They were also known to sell pickled chicken livers.

Inhabitants

In the late-14 century DR the store was owned and operated by Oskler Mhandyvver. He frequently hired local boys to protect his livestock from foxes, hawks, owls, weasels, and other predatory pests.

In the late-15 century DR, the store was operated by a descendant of his named Minthra Mhandyvver and her three children.
