Astral dominion

Astral dominions, sometimes simply called dominions, were distinct planes connected by the Astral Sea that were inhabited and maintained often, but not always, by one or more deities. In many ways an astral dominion was a plane in and of itself, and sometimes contained its own demiplanes. Nonetheless, each dominion was a part of the greater Astral Sea as described by the World Axis cosmology model.

Description

Each astral dominion was unique and shaped by the will and nature of its creator and ruler. Some dominions were planet-sized, while others were far smaller—no bigger than small cities or even singular structures—although in general dominions were depicted as wondrous isles floating in a vast and infinite sea.

Often, astral dominions were viewed as places of divine reward or punishment, where most gods, evil or good, rewarded their faithful in the afterlife.

History

Prior to the Spellplague, the astral dominions were connected in a tree-like structure, within the astral plane, as described by the World Tree cosmology model.

The collapse of the Weave resulted in a loss of cohesion and the reshaping of the astral plane into the amorphous Astral Sea. The dominions went flying freely through the Astral Sea, undergoing their own, sometimes catastrophic, transformations. Some dominions were merged into old ones (as the Golden Hills did with Arvandor), some split apart (like Celestia was from the House of the Triad), and some were destroyed entirely, like Dweomerheart.

The connection map of the planes changed as well. Many astral dominions had connections with new planes, either other dominions or entirely separate planes such as the Prime or even some elemental realms.

Known Astral Dominions

World Axis Cosmology

As of 1479 DR, the eighteen known astral dominions of the World Axis Cosmology model were Arvandor, Banehold, Celestia, Cynosure, the Deep Wilds, the Demonweb Pits, the Dismal Caverns, Dwarfhome, Eternal Sun, the Fugue Plane, the Gates of the Moon, the Green Fields, the House of Knowledge, the Nine Hells, Nishrek, the Supreme Throne, the Towers of Night, and Warrior's Rest, as well as others that had been lost.
