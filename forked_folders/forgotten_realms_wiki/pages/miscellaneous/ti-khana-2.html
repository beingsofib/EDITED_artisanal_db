A ti-khana was a magical mutation of deinonychus, carnivorous species of dinosaur.

Description

The ti-khana was a unique creation born from yuan-ti experiments and hybridization, representing a modified version of the deinonychus dinosaur. These beings had a similar appearance to their forebears: reptilian creatures that move bipedally. Their physiques were somewhat more elongated compared to a deinonychus, stretching to approximately in length. They had a height of about and a weight nearing . Their skin was protected by robust, vividly colored scales, and they featured a pair of prominent fangs that protruded amidst their array of sharp teeth.

Combat

A ti-khana attacked its prey with a leap similar to that of a deinonychus, using its rear claws to deliver a powerful rake attack. It employed its forearms for grasping and its formidable jaws for biting, enhancing the already formidable attack strategy of a deinonychus with the addition of a potent toxic venom.

Abilites

A ti-khana was able to use detect poison at will. The ti-khana possessed the ability to transform into a viper, ranging in size from small to large. This transformation capability was akin to the effects of a shapechange spell. In this transformed state, the ti-khana could utilize either its own poison or that of the viper form it has assumed, opting for whichever was more potent.

The ti-khana had the ability to generate a compulsion effect that targeted a single creature within a range of . This resulted in the creature developing an aversion to snakes that lasted for ten. minutes. This aversion compelled the affected individual to maintain a distance of at least from any snakes, yuan-ti, or ti-khanas, whether they were alive or deceased.
