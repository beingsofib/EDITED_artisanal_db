Metamagic

The term metamagic covered a set of techniques, a group of spells, and even magic items that could be used by spellcasters, including wizards, sorcerers, clericss and others, to alter the workings of other spells and magic items. It was considered a sub-school of the school of incantation.

History

An early pioneer in the field of metamagic was Sammaster, when he was but a prodigious young mage, in the early 800s DR.

Metamagic Techniques

  • Empower Spell: This increased the intensity of harmful spells.
  • Extend Spell: This allowed one to cast a spell with double its usual duration.
  • Maximize Spell: This unleashed the full potential of harmful spells.
  • Quicken Spell: This allowed spells to be cast quicker.
  • Silent Spell: This allowed spellcasting without the necessary verbal components.
  • Still Spell: This allowed the caster to cast a spell without the requisite somatic components.
