Winding Water

The Winding Water was a river located along the Sword Coast, roughly 250 miles north of Baldur's Gate and the River Chionthar. It flowed from several tributaries, some as far away as the Marsh of Chelimber, and eventually flowed into the Sea of Swords south of the Troll Hills.

Description

The Winding Water formed the northern boundary of the Fields of the Dead.

The Time of Troubles and its consequences

During the Time of Troubles, at Boareskyr Bridge, the mortal Cyric had slain Bhaal in a battle that destroyed the mighty bridge and turned the river jet black. The bridge was later rebuilt, now bearing statues of Cyric and Bhaal at its opposite ends. However, the waters of the Winder remained poisonous from Boarskyr Bridge downstream to Trollclaw Ford.

The water along that stretch of the river had become an oily black, reeking both from its own foul stench and from the fish that had perished during the battle and in the years that followed. The migrating salmon that once leapt up the Winder to reach their breeding grounds in the Serpent Hills had vanished, poisoned by the corrupted flow.

The waters of the Winding were treacherous to the touch, seeping into the skin like venom and burning from within if swallowed. Though the black current lost its deadly bite when drawn from the river, its stench lingered — a foul memory of the godslaying that had poisoned the land.

By the time it reached Trollclaw Ford, the stench and black cloudiness had diminished significantly. Though still murky until it met the sea, the water had become drinkable and posed no further danger.

Notable Locations

Notable locations along the river were Boareskyr Bridge, Trollclaw Ford, and Serpent's Cowl.

The Coast Way crossing was a point where the Coast Way crossed the Winding Water.
