Sharn Wall

The Sharn Wall, also seen as Sharnwall, was a shimmering, silver-colored magical barrier in Anauroch created by the sharn to imprison the phaerimm, an ancient, malevolent, and powerful race that were imprisoned beneath Anauroch by the sharn after the collapse of Netheril.

Geography

This magical barrier separated an area of the Underdark beneath the Anauroch known as the Buried Realms, referred to as "Phaerlin" by the phaerimms, from the rest of the Realms. It overlapped most of Xun'Qoroth, an ancient beholder nation, save for the city of Ooltul.

At the edge of the Stonelands, a segment of the Sharn Wall was thinner and bulging, yet maintained its integrity. This anomaly was a result of an ongoing interaction between the barrier's powerful magics and the buried Netherese enclave of Synod's damaged mythallar, which had reconstituted itself into a state of raw magical energy when the Weave re-formed after Karsus's Folly.

Description

Though phaerimm were incapable of passing through the Sharn Wall, other creatures readily could.

History

In 329 DR, the sharns finally defeated the phaerimms, thus halting the spread of the Anauroch and imprisoning them in the Buried Realms.

The phaerimms would go on to conquer Xun'Qoroth, using the two-way portal between its capital of Xunqaq and the city of Ooltul to communicate with and pass items through to those few phaerimms who remained on the other side, namely the Triumvirate that ruled Ooltul. Over the next several centuries, it would be their main conduit to the outside world.

In 464 DR, the vampire Palron Kaeth sought to destroy the Sharn Wall and release the phaerimms using the blood of Khelben Arunsun and his father, but the two mages sacrificed themselves to stop him by using the Lupinaxe to shatter the Duskstaff of Sarael, causing a massive explosion in the sands of the Sword.

At some point prior to 684 DR, the Netherese mage Augathra was tracing the extent of the Sharn Wall, hoping to better understand the sharn, under a belief that they could hold the key to ending the visions that plagued her. Whilst doing so she stumbled upon the anomaly in the Stonelands, beneath a nondescript hill. Crossing over the magical barrier, she would find that this "hill" was in fact the fallen enclave of Synod and consequently the mythallar behind the anomaly. Seeing this as unique opportunity to tap into their combined power, Augathra decided to stay there and conduct experiments. Over time tales would spread of the fabled "Crypt of Augathra the Mad".

In 684 DR, using information obtained at Windsong Tower the mage Shar Cormrael retraced Augathra's steps back to this fabled crypt.

On Nightal 20, in the , a mountain range that seperated the Anauroch from the elven city-state of Evereska. A contingent of Evereskan tomb guardians under the leadership of Galaeron Nihmedu, a mage of the Weave, would find the humans attempting to dig a tunnel through the wall of an ancient elven burial chamber and try to apprehend the group. During this encounter, the conflicting energies of the magical bolts unleashed by the two casters produced a volatile, explosive reaction that would inadvertently puncture the Sharn Wall. This freed the phaerimms from their prison.

The two spellcasters, alongside the Vaasan warrior Vala Thorsdotter, tried to warn Evereska's ruling council. But due to refusing Melegaunt's aid, they were ultimately unable to repair it using their high magic, alongside their thralls. resulting in nigh all who had attacked Evereska being destroyed.

The Shrinshee would go on to use material of the Sharn Wall around the immediate breach to create a webwork of numerous forceblades, that would slice any who attempted to navigate the breach. This magical patchwork would later be renewed over time by several of the Seven Sisters.

In the , an agent of the City of Shade by the name of Hadrhune rediscovered the enclave of Synod during one of his many expeditions in the Anauroch to uncover lost Netherese ruins. Seeing the potential power to gain from its mythallar, he tried to negotiate with the hive of phaerimms inhabiting it. With their peoples' efforts in Evereska having been curtailed, the aberrations agreed to parlay, granting him access to the damaged mythallar in exchange for their leader Drullister being taught the ways of the Shadow Weave.

Whilst traversing the Anauroch these adventurers stumbled upon the ruins of Oreme, where they would rescue the sarrukh lich Terraseer from the machinations of the yuan-ti half-fiend Kharantes. In gratitude for their aid, Terraseer told them that an artifact in the area known as the Chalice of Amaunator had the power to stop the Shadow Weave's corruption of the Quess'Ar'Teranthvar. They would receive similar foretelling when interacting with the Augathra personality of the mythallar bound sharn, but the sharn's cryptic vision contained hints of repercussions for the Sharn Wall should they go through with it. After recovering the Chalice from Askilion's Tower, the adventurers would manage to pour its liquid sun upon the Quess'Ar'Teranthvar, dissolving the scrolls into a mound of semimolten metal that then exploded in a burst of positive energy, scattering them across Toril in their original state. However, what none of them could anticipate was that this burst of energy would also cause a shattering in the last remnants of the Sharn Wall. Though others would find this event to not in fact be the consummate destruction of the Sharn Wall.

In the , in the immediate wake of the City of Shade crashing into Myth Drannor, the Chosen of Mystra undertook a mission to repair the breach in the Sharn Wall. there were in fact many still dwelling within the Buried Realms. The Chosen each sacrificed some of Mystra's essence within them, lessening their own personal power, in order to stretch the Sharn Wall's magical barier over the breach and seamlessly plug it.
