Mamluk (disambiguation)

Mamluk was the Midani term for the specialised slave-soldiers of Zakhara. Uses included:

  • Mamluk, the caste of slave-soldiers belonging to Zakhara's Grand Caliph,
  • various mamluk organizations, including the Dauntless, Defenders, Devoted, Devout, Diligent, Dutiful, Exalted, Faithful, Honored, Parched, Respected, Studious, Valiant, Wanderers, Wondrous, and Youthful Societies,
  • the Mamluk Council, the representatives of the mamluks in Qudra's government,
  • the Gate of the Mamluks, an entrance to the Palace of the Enlightened Throne, and
  • Mamluk swords of obedience, magical Zakharan swords that charmed those they hit.

Mameluk was the Alzhedo term for slave soldiers in Calimshan. Uses included:

  • Mameluk, the general term for a warrior slave in Calimshan,
  • the Mameluks, the ruling body of the land derived from former slaves in Calimshan's Seventh Age, and
  • Mamlukkar, "the Slaves' Village", a town in Shoonach devoted to housing slaves.
