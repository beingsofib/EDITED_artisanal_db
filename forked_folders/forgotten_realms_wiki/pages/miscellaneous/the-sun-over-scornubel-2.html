The Sun Over Scornubel was an inn of Scornubel in the mid-14 century DR.

Interior

Outside from at least one room was a passage that ended on a small stair leading down onto a landing overlooking the inn's forehall. On that landing lay a lounge seat for patrons, with two smaller seats flanking the passage itself, for staff.

Atmosphere

Amongst other small luxuries, the rooms came with their own lock-props. Carved knobs next to the doors could fold down into makeshift tables. The rooms had windows.

Services

The inn had servants in uniforms, and guards. The uniforms were maroon-and-gold. Evenfeasts could be purchased, and would be delivered to the room.
