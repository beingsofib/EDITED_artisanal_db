The Council of Sorcerers, also called the council of mages, was a faction of magic-users who nominally served the Ffolk High Kings and Queens of the Moonshae Isles and who represented the single most powerful concentration of magic power in the whole of the Isles as of the mid–14 century DR.

Activities

The Council served as advisors to the High Kings and Queens at Caer Callidyrr. Part of their role was to warn of any dark magic that might threaten the lands of the Ffolk.

During the reign of High King Reginald Carrathal in the early-to-mid 14 century DR, the Council operated with a great deal of independence to pursue their own ambitions and exert control over the lands of Callidyrr. While they ostensibly served the High King, and nominally represented a major source of strength for his military, in truth they deferred to him only as much as needed to make him believe that he was in control. Given that the Council's presence concentrated an enormous amount of magical power under the High King, they provided him with a valuable threat to keep the other Ffolk rulers obedient, however they had no interest in actually helping him subdue any rivals or unruly underlings.

With the rise of the Kendrick dynasty, the council continued to serve the High Kings and Queens.

Tactics

The Council was noted for both the patience and the fickleness of its members. They monitored current events and would intervene only occasionally to nudge the forces of society in the direction that was most suitable for them. They were widely feared and seen with suspicion by the peoples of the Moonshaes.

Base of Operations

The Council operated out of the Palace of the High King in Caer Callidyrr. Their primary base was an eight-story circular tower atop a hill on the western side of the castle that seemed out of place amidst the rest of the grand fortress. It was only accessible via a bridge to a nearby corner tower. Many of these levels were dedicated to sleeping quarters, laboratories, and libraries, as well as to the council's meeting room at the top.

The Council would also sometimes meet in a secret cavern in the dungeon located approximately below the castle. This high chamber featured a circular table surrounded by a dozen chairs and several pools of runoff dripping from the overhanging stalactites.

History

The council was created by the order of High King Alec Carrathal IV in the early 14 century DR (approximately thirty years before the Darkwalker War). The original council consisted of nine mages, the leader and most powerful of whom was Curmavyss, who held his leading position for about three decades. During his tenure, the council grew to thirteen members and operated with a great deal of autonomy. By the mid–14 century DR, they served the High King only in theory, and in actuality, each councilmember wielded direct control over their own small piece of Callidyrr.

In the , a Thayan mage named Cyndre arrived in the Moonshaes. Recognizing that he was dangerous, Curmavyss sought to keep him close, and hesitantly invited him and his ally Alexei to join the Council. His concerns were well-placed, and within three years, Cyndre had arranged for Curmavyss and five of his allies on the council to be killed. Working to subvert or replace the remaining members, the Council of Sorcerers was remade in Cyndre's image as the Council of Seven.

By the , the twisted Council of Seven had allied with the likes of duergar and Hobarth, a priest of Bhaal; arranged to assassinate the kings of Snowdown, Moray, and Corwell; and worked to eradicate the druids of Alaron. Cyndre's hold over the Council was only broken when he was bound and sealed underground by the druid Robyn Kendrick at the Battle of the High Kings on Eleint 5 of that year.

The council would continue to operate in service to High King Tristan Kendrick. As of the , they worked directly for High Queen Alicia Kendrick.

Members

The council was composed of powerful mages and sorcerers who collectively wielded more arcane power than anywhere else in the Moonshaes.

In addition to the fluctuating number of mages who sat on the council, each councilmember had several students and apprentices, many of whom were considerably powerful in their own right. At its height in the mid–14 century DR, the total number of magic-users affiliated with the Council exceeded 50.

Notable Members

  • Curmavyss, the original leader of the council.
  • Cyndre, who usurped control of the council around the time of the Darkwalker War.
  • Alexei, an ally to Cyndre who ultimately turned against him.
