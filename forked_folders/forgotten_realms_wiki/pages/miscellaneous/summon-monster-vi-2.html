Summon monster VI, also known as monster summoning VI, was a conjuration spell that called a creature to fight for the caster. The version that was more popular by the Year of Wild Magic summoned outsiders; prior, the most popular version summoned natural monsters instead.

Effects

Summon monster VI functioned similarly to the spell able to be cast by less experienced casters: summon monster I. However, this more powerful spell allowed the caster to summon monsters such as bralani eladrin, jann, chaos beasts, chain devils, or xill. It could also summon elementals the size of horses, celestial polar bears or dire lions, or fiendish monstrous centipedes the size of huts, rhinoceroses, giant spiders the size of elephants, and giant constrictor snakes. In the water, it could summon celestial orcas or fiendish elasmosauruses. It yielded one of those creatures; alternatively, it could be used to summon a greater number of monsters from a lower level spell.

Descriptor note

When this was used to summon creatures of a certain type, the spell itself would be considered a spell of the creature's type.

History

The spell was attributed to Netherese arcanist Lucke in and was originally called Lucke's 6th monster summons.

As of the mid-to-late 14 century DR, any aarakocra cleric supported by at least four other aarakocra was able to conduct a ritual involving an aerial dance to cast this spell and summon a large air elemental.
