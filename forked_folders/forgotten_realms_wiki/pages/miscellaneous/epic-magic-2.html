Epic magic

Epic magic, also known as true dweomers or high-level magic, was magic of incredible power. It was usually associated with ancient magical kingdoms like Netheril, but became severely restricted with Mystra's Ban after Karsus's Folly in the . Nevertheless, items and places of epic magic could still be found by the 14 century DR.

Mystra placed a number of limits and impediments upon the use of such magic. These restrictions are as follows:

  • One's first attempt at casting epic spells would always be a failure. Moreover, they would always fail for all casters except those casters who had achieved the pinnacle of prior arcane power, and were among the most intelligent and wisest of their rank.
  • A second spellcaster, who must be almost as experienced and intelligent, could be present to act as an 'anchor', by using another spell or spells to link their will, mind, and life energy to the main caster. Without the help of an anchor, unintended wild magic results would ensue.
  • Casting such high-level magic was mentally and physically taxing; the casters involved would lose some small measure of their health and forget a few of their memorized spells. There was also a significantly high chance that they would lose their grasp of their greatest abilities, forcing them to spend some time reacquiring their previous skills and rank before making another attempt.
  • Mystra and Azuth were aware of all casting attempts, and would afflict all casters involved with feeblemindedness (as in the feeblemind spell), as well as remove the written spell from existence, if they considered the casting to be for purely selfish and/or malevolent ends, that is, running counter to the free and individualistic development of magic.

These limitations maintained an illusion of non-function; after the events of Karsus's Folly, Mystra altered the enchantments used in high-level magic such that they could not "take hold of the Weave", as well as made some of the symbols and glyphs mentally damaging to any creature trying to read, memorize, or experiment with them. This led to a widespread, though mistaken, notion that mortals could no longer cast epic magic at all, as was Mystra's intention. Despite the secrecy, Mystra commanded that some knowledge of these limitations be disseminated through visions to any mages who prayed to her, Azuth, Oghma, or Savras specifically and directly about such things.

Mystra's Magisters were exempt from these limitations, as their attempts would always succeed. However, Mystra and Azuth were free to immediately reverse the spell's effects, and the Magister would lose their office and seemingly die for their transgression. In truth, they would be thrust into Mystra's Choice.

Known Epic Spells

High-Level Spells of Netheril

  • Ioulaum's longevity: extended the caster's lifespan, but at a terrible cost of a life for every year gained.
  • Karsus's avatar: a very powerful spell which altered the very disposition of reality.
  • Lefeber's weave mythal: When cast, this spell allowed the magic of the caster or a targeted creature to be immune to the influence of a mythal.
  • Mavin's create volcano: used to create a volcano.
  • Mavin's earthfast: used to make large areas of underground tunnels impervious to cave-ins and similar disasters.
  • Mavin's worldweave: used to permanently modify the climate in a region.
  • Proctiv's breach/seal crystal sphere: used to permanently shut off a whole crystal sphere against access by spelljamming ships.
  • Proctiv's move mountain: a legendary Netherese spell that sheared off a mountaintop, allowing the creation of the famous Netherese enclaves.
  • Tolodine's killing wind: this spell was designed to kill huge numbers of creatures.
  • Tarchamus' Volcano: a devastating spell which recreated the effects of a volcano by opening a portal to Phlegethos underneath the caster.
  • Valdick's spheresail: a spell for sailing the crystal spheres.''''

Spells of Cormanthyr

  • Create mythal: one of the spells used in the creation of wizardly mythals.

Epic Spells

The following spells known in the Realms were created after Karsus's Folly. Some were known by the same names as the Netherese high-level spells, or were recreations.

  • Aumvor's fragmented phylactery: this spell allowed a caster to expand the number of their phylacteries, thus forcing a lich's enemies to destroy all phylacteries, so permanently destroying the lich became much harder.
  • Aumvor's soulshatter: this spell led to the separation of body and soul of a target.
  • Bonfire of insanity: used to resurrect large amounts of individuals as ghouls.
  • Cataract of fire: a spell that was very similar to flame strike.
  • Damnation: when the spellcaster touched a target creature, it was instantaneously transported to one of the layers of the Nine Hells or the Abyss.
  • Diluvial torrent: used to create quite a splash of water.
  • Dragon knight: used to summon a red dragon.
  • Epic gate: used to summon a balor.
  • Epic mage armor: the most powerful known variant of mage armor.
  • Epic warding: a powerful magical ward spell.
  • Greater ruin: a transmutation spell that was able to inflict immense damage on living and nonliving matter.
  • Hellball: a ball of energy that did enormous acid, fire, electrical, and sonic damage to everything in its blast radius upon detonation.
  • Horrendous dessication: caused everything to dry up within a large area.
  • Mass fowl: used to transform large amounts of opponents into chickens.
  • Mummy dust: used to summon one or two mummies, depending on the variation.
  • Mythanthor's create mythal: another way to create a mythal.
  • Necromantic singularity: used to kill large amounts of creatures with negative energy in its purest form.
  • Origin of species: achaierai: copied from the repertoire of a fiendish caster from Acheron, this spell literally created a new creature: an achaierai.
  • Vampiric feast: a spell that allowed the caster to drink the life force of their enemies.

Other Epic Spells

These epic spells are not known to exist in the Realms, but are listed here for completeness:

  • Animus blast: the caster could engulf their enemies in a coldball. If an individual was killed inside the ball, they would resurrect as a skeleton loyal to the caster.
  • Animus blizzard: used to engulf enemies in an unusually powerful burst of cold. Up to five victims that perished as a result of this blast were then instantly animated as wights.
  • Contingent resurrection: contingent resurrection returns the subject to life if they are slain.
  • Create living vault: this was used to create a construct known as a living vault to protect and hide treasures.
  • Crown of vermin: when someone wore a crown of vermin, one thousand venomous, biting and stinging spiders, scorpions, beetles, and centipedes apparently erupted from the very air around them.
  • Demise unseen: used to instantly slay a single target and at the same moment animate the body so that it appears that nothing has happened to the creature.
  • Dire winter: used to create a chilling situation.
  • Dragon strike: the spell summoned ten adult red dragons.
  • Eclipse: used to create a limited eclipse.
  • Eidolon: this spell allowed the caster to create a duplicate, which contained a fragment of their soul.
  • Enslave: used to make a permanent thrall of any living creature.
  • Epic counterspell: a powerful variant of counterspell, that allowed the caster to counter epic spells.
  • Epic repulsion: a ward spell that allowed to create a ward against a specific creature type.
  • Epic spell reflection: a powerful ward that protected the user against non-epic spells.
  • Eternal freedom: the subject goes boldly where others fear to tread, becoming permanently immune to many fear inducing spells and spell-like abilities.
  • Greater spell resistance: used to increase the spell resistance of a chosen creature.
  • Kinetic control: used to absorb, store, and redirect the energy contained in the swing of a club, the slash of a sword, or the piercing flight of an arrow.
  • Let go of me: a spell that allowed to greviously harm creatures grappling the caster.
  • Living lightning: used to create sentient lightning.
  • Lord of nightmares: the caster becomes replaced by a dream larva for some time.
  • Mass frog: used to transform large amounts of opponents into frogs.
  • Momento mori: a spell that allowed to kill creatures using only a thought.
  • Nailed to the sky: a spell that makes a target fly indefinitely until saved.
  • Peripety: ranged attacks targeted against the caster rebound on the original attacker.
  • Pestilence: when pestilence was successfully cast, a wave of illness radiated outward from the site of the ritual, instantly infecting every living thing in the area with the debilitating disease known as slimy doom.
  • Rain of fire: this spell summoned a swirling thunderstorm that rained fire rather than raindrops.
  • Raise island: used to raise an island from the sea.
  • Ruin: used to inflict grevious damage to living and nonliving matter.
  • Safe time: it could move the caster (or another target) out of harm’s way by shunting the target into a static time stream.
  • Soul dominion: used to take total control of the target’s body.
  • Soul scry: When cast, the caster was temporarily able to tap the consciousness of another sentient creature with whom they are familiar (by meeting, observing, or successfully scrying the subject), experiencing everything they do with all five senses.
  • Spell worm: used to infect the subject with a compulsive urge that uses up their spells for the day.
  • Summon behemoth: used to summon a behemoth - an outsider in animal form that had epic proportions, even larger and more powerful than dire animals.
  • Superb dispelling: a much more powerful version of greater dispelling.
  • Time duplicate: used to summon a future self.
  • Vengeful gaze of god: the target of this spell was subject to a fury like unto heavenly wrath.
  • Verdigris tsunami: used to create a tsunami of grass, shrubs, and trees that overgrows the area like a tidal wave.
