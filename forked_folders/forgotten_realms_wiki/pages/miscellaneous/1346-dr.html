1346 DR

  • A naval battle at Lisen Sands.
  • Baseless rumors spread that some 80,000 drow lurk in the Forest of Mir, triggering panic in Calimshan and Tethyr. In Calimport, folk venture into the Muzad under the city, aiming to wipe out any drow they find there. Others enter into slave-trading deals with actual drow agents and grow rich.
  • King Dynnegal of Moray is assassinated.
  • King Byron Kendrick of Corwell is assassinated.
  • High King Reginald Carrathal is killed by Cyndre.
  • Following centuries of silence from Tiamat, the Dragon Queen, the last few members of the Cult of Tiamat successfully summon the Dark Lady, an aspect of Tiamat, to Unther.
  • The sinister god Bhaal seeks to use a corrupted moonwell to send a manifestation known as "the Ravager" to the Moonshae Isles. It is during this time that the goddess Chauntea notices the crippled and faltering status of the Earthmother and begins to counter Bhaal's schemes for the Isles. Embracing the goddess Chauntea, the Great Druid Robyn Moonsinger uses her new powers to drive Bhaal's avatar back through the darkwell. King Tristan uses the Sword of Cymrych Hugh to seal the gate, severing Bhaal's link to the Moonshaes.
  • From nowhere Entropy manifested itself inside the House Karanok's mansion in Luthcheq killing a wizard they were torturing. The Karanoks saw this as a omen, began worshiping Entropy.
  • Early in the year, a great frost ruins crops in the Western Heartlands.
  • Tristan Kendrick becomes high king of the Moonshae Isles.
  • Selfaril rises to become High Blade of Mulmaster.
  • The Friendly Arm inn is built.
  • In Cormyr, the Sword Heralds' refuge, Dawninghunt, is found by adventurers. Within is discovered a chest of over a thousand large, valuable emeralds; four large and extensive spellbooks; and a number of minor magical items.
  • Of the seven students of the High Mages of Selu'Maraar, three of them—Keryth Adofaer, Talor Vurtalis, and Jhanra Merlistar—discover a new means of learning High Magic. Taranath provides them with three selu'kiira. Placed upon the students' foreheads, the Selu'Kiira send them into comas and pour the knowledge directly into their minds. The High Mages and students stay in seclusion at their estate outside Sylkiir.
  • The two volume Fallen Legacies: Arcaeological and Historical Analysis of the Mere of Dead Men, written by Khelben Arunsun, is published.
  • The elf and Harper agent Ashemmi disguises herself as a human wizard and is recruited into the Zhentarim at Darkhold.
