Lost Kingdoms

The Lost Kingdoms, also known as the Three Realms or the Buried Lands to the Bedine people, are the three survivor-states that emerged from the enclaves that were spared from destruction during the fall of ancient Netheril. They were formed in the region of north Faerûn that would later reform as the Anauroch desert.

Description

The Lost Kingdoms each grew out from one of the three floating cities that safely touched down upon Toril after Netheril's fall and occupied the southern and eastern portions of the former Netheril (region) as it became the Anauroch.

The realm of Anauria comprised the united gathering of men, elves, and half-elves, and was considered to be the wealthiest and most prosperous of the three. Anauria comprised the region just west and southwest of the Desertsmouth Mountains, from the southern point of At'ar's Looking Glass down to the Sword. It's capital was the luxurious Amazandar, the city Anauril had superb smithies, and Helvara agricultural markets. It was defended by two fortresses, Tower Hlithal to stop Araugul the west, and Tower Ramanath to block monsters from the Hunters' Hills.

Asram, with its capital Phelajarama was the kingdom that most-closely held on to Netheril's arcane traditions. The kingdom was located at least in part within the region of the Sword south of the Wall of Fallen Djinn mountains.

Possessing significantly less wealth than the other two realms, the people of Hlondath made their way in life as animal herders and craftspeople. It was located in what would be the northeastern region of the Sword, just west of the Border Forest, which it used as a source of lumber. It's most notable cities were the capital Mhaelos and Rulvadar, a fortified bastion against the hordes in the Moonsea North.

A few independent cities existed in the realm as well, including the lawless center of commerce to the east, Port Miir, and Oum a border town between Asram and Hlondath full of craftsmen and sages.

History

In the , the phaerimm of the Buried Realms in the Underdark began casting their magic to create the great desert of Anauroch. This would ultimately lead to the abandonment of Low Netheril, and the last remaining survivor-state of the magocracy nearly some 700 years later.

Karsus's Folly struck Netheril in , an event in which the newly-formed goddess Mystra demonstrated her grace and mercy. She spared the enclaves of Anauria, Asram, and Hlondath from crashing to Toril, a feat that was possible due to the great altitudes at which they soared above the ground.

While they survived the fall of Netheril, the three kingdoms each succumbed to ruin by a different cause, over the course of less than three hundred years.

The realm Asram fell in the , when it was beset by a plague brought down by the goddess Talona. Anauria came to an end in the , when it was overrun by the goblinoid army of Hlundadim. Hlondath was the last of the three kingdoms to collapse, having been abandoned in the after the encroachment of the Anauroch desert left the nation inhospitable.

Rumors & Legends

According to Bedine legend circa the 14 century DR, the desert of Anauroch was created by Three Ancient Tribes when they used their magic summoned the djinn of N'asr in order wage war against one another.

The creation myth continued to say that the gods intervened to stop the war and sent the three tribes to the far corners of Toril in an event referred to as the Scattering by the Bedine.
