Spirit fetch

Spirit fetch was a ritual that summoned a "fetch"—a small and insubstantial spiritual servant—to coerce another person or creature.

Effects

The caster of the ritual chose whether to summon a fey, primal, or shadow fetch to serve them, as well as specified the shape of the fetch (generally they took the form of a small and indistinct animal or humanoid). The caster then whispered a command of up to 10 words that they wished for the fetch to deliver to a creature or creatures, at which point it sped away to find its target(s). Upon finding the target(s), the fetch's mere presence served to communicate the caster's command (the fetch was otherwise unable to interact or speak). The target(s) were not compelled to obey the command, however the fetch would linger at their side as a constant reminder until they obeyed or until the ritual expired (which could be up to a month depending on the skill of the caster). A fetch could not be harmed or destroyed, although casting dispel magic could drive it away for 24 hours.

Because of the fetch's ability to seek out its target(s), the ritual was also useful simply for locating the target(s) rather than coercing them. However, some methods of warding against divination could render a creature unable to be found by a fetch, and could cause the fetch to seek them in the wrong direction.

Components

The ritual entailed 20 gp worth of material component and took ten minutes to enact. In addition, it required the caster to be able to identify their target(s), either by naming them, by visualizing their specific appearance, or by providing a physical sample of them (such as their blood).

Users

Spirit fetch was said to be a tool used by the Vistani.

The feywardens of Sarifal trained to be able to perform the ritual once each day without needing any material components.
