Fey crossing

A fey crossing was a site where the Prime Material plane overlapped with the Feywild, allowing for travel between the two planes. A fey crossing was not to be confused for a fey crossroads, which was a two-way portal that could connect a specific location in the Prime Material plane to a specific different location in the Feywild.

Description

Fey crossings were mysterious places marked by a mirroring of nature's beauty in both the Prime Material Plane and the Feywild. They could be as small as a doorway or as large as a city, and might occupy a clearing, a pool, a glade, a hill, a circle of mushrooms, the space beneath a fallen tree trunk, or any similar natural formation. Fey would sometimes mark more reliable crossings with standing stones, small obelisks, or deliberately arranged plants, while unmarked fey crossings might be detectable by a faint aura or outline where the planes met.

The magic of the Feywild spilled into the Prime Material plane at a fey crossing, which could purify and enrich the beauty and fertility of the land in its vicinity, making the area an appealing place to establish agrarian villages.

As fey crossings were tied to nature, they might be permanently destroyed if the landscape on one side was greatly changed or built over.

Location

A fey crossing could naturally occur in any place where the Prime Material plane and the Feywild touched One of most reliable ways to discover a fey crossing was to seek out to local legends and tales about mischievous fey or disappearing travelers, and to follow these stories to their source.

It was possible to open temporary crossings in locations where the barrier between the two planes was known to be thin, such as in places like Evereska, Silverymoon, and Spellgard. Doing so usually required using a ritual involving the casting of a handful of powerful spells. It was also known that baelnorns were capable of opening passages into the Feywild.

Activation

Travel between the planes was as simple as moving through the fey crossing, at which point the traveler would perceive themselves as seamlessly arriving from the Prime Material plane to the Feywild or vice versa. To an observer, the traveler would appear to vanish as they passed through the crossing.

In general, the only fey crossings that were always open were those located in the deepest or most remote places in nature. Sometimes a ritual known as fey passage was needed to open the way between the planes, and this ritual could generally also be used to force open any inactive crossing. The conditions for activating a fey crossing might be different on the Prime Material plane side versus the Feywild side of that crossing. Assuming any conditions were met, it was possible to accidentally pass through a fey crossing (often without immediately realizing it).

Attempting to enter a fey crossing during the chaos of combat could have unexpected effects, such as teleporting the traveler a few seconds into the future, causing their skin to turn thin and translucent, creating wacky or impossible weather, transforming them into an animal, or triggering various spell effects (sometimes harmful, sometimes helpful, and sometimes benign).

In some cases, fey crossings could be used to travel quickly between two locations on the Prime Material plane by taking a shortcut through the mercurial Feywild. Using such a route exposed travelers to the chaotic perils of spending time in the Feywild.

Notable Fey Crossings

  • Brokenstone Vale was said to contain fey crossings leading to hinterlands within the Prime Material plane. These crossings were overseen by lycanthropic druid.
  • An oasis in the middle of the Calim Desert (somewhere between Calimport and Memnon). On the Feywild side, the oasis was large and lush, and bore a charm that made it hard to resist staying for the night.
  • Citadel Umbra on Gwynneth in the Moonshae Isles was permeated with crossings to the Feywild as well as to the Shadowfell.
  • The Dernall Forest of Alaron in the Moonshae Isles contained many fey crossings leading to Feywild domains such as Spiderwood and Nachtur.
  • The Forgotten Forest in the Western Heartlands was known to contain fey crossings that made travel dangerous and unpredictable. Some of these were guarded by Pandi, an ethereal fey that appeared as a dog.
  • The High Forest of the North was known for its many fey crossings.
  • Within Kryptgarden Forest was a fey crossing that could only be activated by writing one's intended destination in the Feywild on an out-of-place road sign.
  • New Sharandar in Neverwinter Wood.
  • Within the woods of Prismeer were seven fairy rings which could function as fey crossings to many locations across the Prime Material plane.
  • Spiderhaunt Wood in Shadowdale was said to contain many fey crossings such that it was easy to slip into the Feywild while travelling there.
  • The Witchlight Carnival was said to contain a fey crossing that led to Prismeer. It was disguised as a mirror that required a passphrase to activate.

History

It was said that the first elves in Faerûn made their homes in the presence of fey crossings. Ancient eladrin were said to have nurtured and expanded many natural fey crossings to make them more useful, however these were largely abandoned by the 15 century DR.

Active fey crossings could be found throughout Faerûn following the Spellplague. They were sought out by those who hoped to forge a warlock's pact with archfey patrons.

Inhabitants

In the Prime Material plane, farmers and herders might establish small villages near fey crossings, drawn by lush land and clean water that was perfectly suited for raising healthy crops and animals or for hunting plentiful game. Such villagers remained wary of the fey creatures that often occupied the same areas, and cautioned each other against wandering too far outside their homes. Some villages were said to sprinkle shavings of iron or cold iron around their perimeters in an effort to ward off the fey.

Because fey crossings tended to arise in the remote wilderness, nomadic barbarians who roamed such areas also tended to be familiar with the perils of stumbling into a crossing. Some nomads of the Hordelands could sense the presence of fey crossings thanks to their practicing of primal magic.

In the Feywild, hamlets were sometimes founded near fey crossings, often to provide places for an exchange of goods and news between the planes. These hamlets were small collections of crooked cottages in which dwelt both fey and mortal residents who took it upon themselves to safeguard the crossing against dangerous travelers. The mortals here lived similarly pastoral lives as their counterparts on the Prime Material plane, but with a much deeper understanding of the fey.

Rumors & Legends

Tales told among the satyrs claimed that the first fey crossings were rifts in the fabric of the planes caused by the Dawn War.

Some legends claimed that the ancient eladrin sent many fey beasts through fey crossings to terrorize mortals, and that this was the true origins of the likes of displacer beasts and owlbears on the Prime Material Plane.

It was said that the gnomes of the Feywild knew secret ways of activating any fey crossing at will, and that they could use these secrets to teleport between fey crossings within the Feywild.
