Sensitivity to psychic impressions was a psionic power of the clairsentience discipline

Effects

With this power, a manifester could perceive the "psychic residue" lingering in an area from powerful emotions that were experienced there, granting them momentary visions of the person who experienced that emotion. Past everyday occurrences could not be detected and seen by a manifester, as they didn't leave behind any psychic residue. Only significant and highly dramatic events, such as deaths, battles, betrayals, childbirth, marriages, murders, other moments of great pain, or just about any other event where one emotion dominates. The vision of the past will be vague and shadowy to the manifester, as if viewing a dream, not offering any special knowledge of those involved. But the dominant emotion involved will be recognized very clearly.

Beginning with the most recent event and working backward, the manifester could sense distinct events from several centuries back, with this range extending at a minimum 100 years. One distinct event could be sensed and viewed for every 10 minutes that the manifester spent maintaining concentration.

For older versions of sensitivity to psychic impressions, a manifestation completely backfiring would result in a ghost appearing. The angry spirit would attempt to magic jar the psionicist.

Displays

This power manifested standard auditory and material displays.

Users

This power was primarily knowable by psions who specialized as seers though it was also found to be exhibited by some wild talents.

This power was exhibited as an innate psionic ability by greater shedu.

Known/Notable Users

  • Anthraxus, a powerful altraloth.
  • Charon, the boatman of the River Styx.
  • Talib the Lich-Killer, a wily old Zakharan wild talent and necromancer of the deathslayer variety.
  • Tithonnas, a legendary great wyrm topaz dragon.
