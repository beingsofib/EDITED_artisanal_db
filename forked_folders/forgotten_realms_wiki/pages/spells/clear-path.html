Clear path was an alteration spell that cleared a corridor in front of the caster as he or she traveled through rough terrain. The reverse of this spell, called clutter path, obfuscated the terrain behind the caster, covering any tracks.

Effects

This spell operated in natural environments that were considered rough terrain, such as jungles, forests, rocky terrain, or snow. It was not able to clear natural hazards such as lava or quicksand and had no effect on bodies of water. Structures and handmade barriers or magically created terrain were also immune to this spell. As the caster moved forward, a path wide was cleared of weeds, vines, stones, underbrush, snow and ice, or other such impediments to travel for in front of the caster. This created a continuous path as long as the caster continued moving forward. Clear path lasted for a number of hours and more experienced casters could extend it for more hours. Note that tracking the passage of the caster was child's play as the created path was quite obvious.

When cast in reverse, clutter path obscured the by area behind the caster as he or she traveled forward. Debris appropriate to the terrain was scattered about, covering the trail and making tracking much more difficult.

Components

In addition to verbal and somatic components, clear path required a knife blade and a piece of straw from a broom; clutter path required a handful of weeds and a handful of pebbles.

History

It was recorded in Yornar's Trail Companion, a holy text of the church of Mielikki.
