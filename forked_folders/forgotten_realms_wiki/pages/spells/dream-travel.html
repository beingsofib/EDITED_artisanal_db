Dream travel was a psionic devotion of the psychoportation discipline that allowed the psionicist to physically transport their body to distant locations by passing through the Region of Dreams.

Effects

This psionic power required psionicist to be asleep to utilize it. Once asleep, they would be mentally transported to the Region of Dreams and they had to begin fashioning within their mind a journey from their present location to the destination they desired to be. though later developments found that it was possible to dream travel to other planes of existence, so long as there existed creatures within those places that dreamed.

It was possible for psionicists to take multiple passengers within them, depending upon the extent of their psionic powers, though it was slightly more difficult to take nonsapient creatures. This required the passengers to also be asleep and physically touching both the psionicist and each other.

Dream travel allowed for adventuring a fair distance throughout the curtain that was the Region of Dreams, participating in a mengaerie of dreams along the way to their destination.

The user and any passengers physically remained in the location they were asleep at during the duration of this journey. Later developments of the power instead physically transported the body into the Regions of Dreams.

Older iterations of dream travel limited the distance to within of where the psion slept Later developments of dream travel expanded the possible range between locations to , with each requiring one minute of travel. Transference between planes of existence through use of dream travel always took around one to four hours of uninterrupted travel.

Displays

Dream travel exhibited only the standard visual display.

History

While originally available to all manner of psionicists, around the 1370's DR it was found to be exclusive to nomads, psions who specialized in psychoportation.

Users

This power was innate to the opinicus, a strange breed of psionic creatures endemic to the land of Zakhara. Beyond Toril, this power was innate to the baku of the Outlands. and the cloud rays of Athas, despite the latter having an animalistic intellect. They sometimes subconsciously activated dream travel while they slept or while in flight as they digested a meal.

Known/Notable Users

  • Lertis the Seer, a Banite high priest and psionic wild talent.
  • Tanith, an innkeeper and psionicist.
