Schism, formerly known as split personality, was a major psionic science that allowed a manifester to fragment their own consciousness, partitioning their mind into two independently functioning entities, or in some rare cases three. Over time it had been variously classified under the metapsionic, psychometabolism, and telepathy disciplines.

Effects

The fragmented personality created by schism was wholly autonomous within the manifester's body, though had no control over it. Back when the power was known as split personality, this fragmentation of consciousness persisted until the psion made a conscious choice to reverse it, whilst later iterations of this power were only temporary. While schism was active the two minds were capable of communicating telepathically and could manifest psionic powers separately, even at the same time. When manifesting psionic powers, the partitioned mind drew from the same pool of psionic energy within the psion's body. Beyond the benefit of being able to manifest powers twice as fast, schism granted psionicists the reprieve to undertake other tasks, such as physical combat, whilst their partitioned mind handled the wielding of psionics.

Enchantment-based affects directed against a psionicist under the effects of schism could potentially disrupt the power, ending it prematurely, but generally mind-affecting abilities could only affect one of a manifester's two minds. Back when it was known as split personality, psions had to establish contact with both halves of a mind separately. And if either half was somehow controlled, subdued, or destroyed the other consciousness would continue to act and control the body.

Later iterations of this power had the caveat that certain effects, could not affect the second mind created by schism.

Additionally, for split personality there was a risk to be had when rejoining the two halves of a consciousness if the psionicist was either suffering from an unwanted contact, wasn't in control of both portions of the mind, or had any psychic damage that had yet to be ameliorated. If any one of these three things were extant during the reunion of the two halves, there was a chance that it could go awry. When this happened the dominant personality would go unconscious for a few minutes, whilst the partition would take over during that time. Upon regaining consciousness, the psion would be free of all undesired influences.

Prerequisite Powers

Back when it was commonly referred to as split personality, a psionicist wanting to learn the power first had to possess psychic surgery.

Displays

When used this power typically manifested auditory and visual displays.

Significance

There existed a similar telepathy power, known personality parasite, which allowed a psion to briefly partition an opponent's mind. However, attempts to use that power were rendered null and void on targets that were under the effects of schism.

Elocaters under the effects of schism were incapable of activating their innate psionic ability of accelerating their own body.

Users

While once available to all manner of psions, in the late 14 century DR psionicists found that only those who specialized as telepaths had the capacity to learn schism.

This power was exhibited as an innate psionic ability by elder brains and adult intellect devourers. The power was even available to skeletal intellect devourers.

Known/Notable Users

  • The Mind Shard of Pandorym, the imprisoned form of the eponymous elder evil.
  • Nar'Lheeth, an illithid of the Tamer Creed in the city of Nar'Vheen.
