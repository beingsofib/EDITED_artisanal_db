Clairsentience

The clairsentience discipline of psionics revolved around the expansion of senses, identification of other beings, and psionic information gathering. Such powers could grant other senses like darkvision or true sight, the ability to sense poisons visual or auditory information taking place miles away, clairsentience had a subdiscipline called Scrying.

Description

Clairsentience powers often exuded from the manifester in a cone-shaped area of effect that moved with the caster and extended out towards whatever direction they were facing. Often committing one's time to further scrutinizing of an examined area could lead to deriving further information from a manifested power.

Creatures that were immune to divination magic were typically likewise immune to clairsentience powers.

Subdisciplines

Scrying: Powers that created an invisible sensor that sent information back to the manifester. These sensors had the same sensory acuity as the manifester and functioned as separate, independent sensory organs that could be relied upon if the manifester was suffering from some form of sensory impairment, like deafness or blindness. Such powers could be dispelled as if they were active powers and potentially detected by very intelligence creatures. Much like spells of the scrying subschool, scrying powers could be blocked by lead sheeting, as well as psionic protections like null psionics field or psionic mind blank. Whenever this occurred the manifester would sense that their power was being blocked.

Notable Users

  • Seers, psions who chose to specialize in mastery of clairsentience over all other disciplines. Their specialization in the discipline lead them to accessing clairsentience powers that were largely unobtainable by other psionics users.
  • Smargad, the emerald dragon thane of Sardior, whose area of focus was clairsentience.
