Metamorphosis, It was similar to the magical effect of polymorphing, though with wider applications.

Effects

All versions of metamorphosis shared the effect of not granting manifesters any special qualities possessed by the form they had assumed (such as tremorsense or regeneration), nor any unique abilities, including supernatural, psionic, and spell-like abilities. For example, if a manifester took the form of a red dragon they would not exhibit their fiery breath weapon.

A manifester would be granted the natural physical qualities of any form they assumed, such as proportional strength, mundane methods of locomotion (such as burrowing, flight, swimming), natural armor, and natural methods of attack (if any), including poison, whilst retaining their own mind. If an assumed creature form was capable of speech, the manifester could communicate as usual. And if a manifester took the form of an object, plant, or rock they would lose all mobility, but retain their normal senses and capacity to speak.

Any garments or equipment worn by the manifester would either be altered to conform to their new body shape, or meld into the new form and reappear exactly as they were once the manifested reverted their form. If an assumed form was wearing or wielding anything that the manifester's base form could feasibly wear or carry in an equivalent body part, then it would still be held at the time of reversion.

Prior to the Time of Troubles, shape alteration was quite similar to the spell polymorph self. During that time, any additional animal, mineral, or vegetable material that a manifester had on their person would be counted towards their overall weight for a transformation. Shape alteration also differed from later versions of the power in that the amount of psychic energy required for a manifestation was not static, but varied depending upon the overall weight of the form desired.

Following the Time of Troubles and fundamental changes in how psionics worked on Toril, metamorphosis was found to cause great physical stress upon a manifester. The shock to a manifester's system could be so bad that they passed out, but still expended psychic energy from the attempt. On some rare, lucky occasions a manifestation allowed for assuming the form of something three times the size of the manifester's body mass. And if a manifestation backfired, there was a chance the psionics user could become trapped in their new form.

By the late 14 century DR, the scope of metamorphosis had become far more narrowed. A manifester could not transform into a construct, elemental, outsider, undead, or template creature. Nor could a manifester assume a form that was smaller than , gaseous, ectoplasmic, or incorporeal. The capacity to transform into objects remained, though complex objects and mechanical mechanisms required some expertise associated with it. Additionally, metamorphosis did not allow for transforming into magic items, psionic items, psionically animated mechanisms, or objects formed out of ectoplasm. If destroyed or slain, the manifester's body would revert to its original form.

In whatever form a manifester took, they would any special attacks or qualities of their base form, minus those that required a particular limb. Additionally, a manifester had the freedom to designate physical qualities of their new creature form, so long as they were within the normal ranges of what was possible for that species. These included such things as hair color and texture, height, sex, skin color, and weight.

Displays

This power exhibited material and olfactory displays when manifested.

Users

This power was knowable by psionicists, particularly those who specialized as egoists, wild talents, and ardents and divine minds that followed the Natural World mantle.

Metamorphosis was exhibited as an innate psionic ability by amethyst dragons, baku, beljuril dragons, greater shedu, greater ssvaklors, and topaz dragons. Additionally, shades of those who possessed psionics in life often developed shape alteration.

In psionics terms, metamorphosis could be known by couatl.
