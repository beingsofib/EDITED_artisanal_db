Epic mage armor was a rare epic conjuration spell that was a much more powerful version of the common mage armor spell or less common greater mage armor and improved mage armor.

Effects

Upon casting the spell, whomever the caster touched was enveloped in an invisible force field that protected the target in much the same way as the most excellent armors, yet without any of its penalties, such as restricted movement or weight. Because it was a force field, incorporeal creatures or objects could not pass through it. The force field was very powerful, making the spell's target immensely hard to hit.

The spell could not be countered, interrupted, or dispelled using normal spells. To prevent the spell from being cast, one had to, for example, use an epic version of counterspell.

Development

Before it could be cast, epic mage armor had to be developed. Both divine and arcane casters could develop this spell. Developing an epic spell was different from learning normal spells. Once an epic spell was developed, the caster simply knew the spell. A developed epic spell became a part of the caster and could even be prepared without a spellbook (if a wizard was the caster).

To develop the spell, 414,000 gp and 9 days were needed along with precise arcane procedures. Only spellcasters well-versed in spellcraft could develop epic mage armor.

Components

The spell only required verbal and somatic components.
