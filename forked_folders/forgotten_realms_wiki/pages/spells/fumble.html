Fumble, originally called Berthot's blunder, was a common spell used by wizards.

Effects

When cast, creatures became clumsy within the spell's area of effect. Items and weapons were dropped and a walking or running creature tripped and fell. Fallen creatures struggled to stand and picking up a fallen item or weapon became difficult. A caster could target a single creature as well.

Components

In addition to verbal and somatic components, the spell required a material component: a small amount of solidified fat from milk.
