Mass fowl was a rare epic transmutation spell that allowed the caster to transform their opponents into poultry.

Effects

As of the Year of Wild Magic, 1372 DR, casting mass fowl caused every chosen target of medium or small size within 20 feet (6.1 meters) of the caster to be permanently transformed into a chicken. The casting time of the spell was short compared to normal spells.

Sufficiently powerful creatures were unaffected by this spell. Accounts varied on if the spell was subject to spell resistance. Individuals with high fortitude were sometimes able to resist the spell. It couldn't however be countered, interrupted, or dispelled using normal spells or physical attacks.

All items equipped by affected targets transformed into chicken were instantly destroyed upon transformation.

Development

Before it could be cast, mass fowl had to be developed. Both divine and arcane casters could develop this spell (except for warlocks). Developing an epic spell was different from learning normal spells. Once an epic spell was developed, the caster knew the spell. A developed epic spell became a part of the caster and could even be prepared without a spellbook (if a wizard was the caster). Only those well-versed in spellcraft could develop mass fowl.

Components

To cast mass fowl, the caster had to use verbal and somatic components.
