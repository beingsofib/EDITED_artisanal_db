Chill was a useful cantrip that apprentice wizards could learn. It reduced the temperature of a quantity of liquid or solid matter.

Effects

This cantrip had a range of . liquids and solids that amounted to no more than in volume. The drop in temperature was about 40 ℉ ( ℃) but could not go below . The cantrip and temperature change were nearly instantaneous but thereafter the chilled material acted normally in the conditions surrounding it, slowly warming back up to the ambient temperature.

Components

This cantrip required verbal and somatic components to cast. The caster gave a low whistle while making a downward-pointing gesture with the thumb.
