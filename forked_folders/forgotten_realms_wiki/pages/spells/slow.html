Slow (originally Quantoul's slowmorph and mindwitnesses, had innate magical abilities that mimicked this spell. Ocular adepts, humanoid servants of the Great Mother, the beholder goddess, also had the power of slow.

Effects

One or more creatures within close range both from the caster and each other were magically made slower in their actions and reflexes. Targets with high strength of will could resist the effects of the spell, which otherwise lasted for about a third of a minute or longer.

Several spells countered or negated the effects of this spell, including haste, freedom, freedom of movement,

Components

The spell required verbal and somatic components and a drop of molasses.

History

The spell was invented by prolific Netherese arcanist Quantoul in (1833 NY). It was also recorded in the Book of Bats.
