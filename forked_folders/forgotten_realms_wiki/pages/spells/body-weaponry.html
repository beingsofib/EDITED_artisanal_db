Body weaponry was a psionic devotion of the psychometabolism discipline that allowed a manifester to turn one of their arms into an approximation of various common forms of weapons.

Effects

By altering the molecules in their body, a manifester transformed their arms into the form of a weapon, changing its molecular makeup into either metal, wood, bone, stone, or a combination of metal and wood. While transformed the arm would behave in every respect like the weapon it was approximating. The manifester could not transform their arm into any weapon they weren't proficient in fighting with, nor could they transform it into a ranged weapon, such as a bow or crossbow.

Prior to the Time of Troubles, this power not only allowed a manifester to mimic a weapon, but armor as well. The type of weapon depended upon the manifester's degree of mastery with the devotion and their psionic powers as a whole, whilst the degree of armor depended upon these factors as well as whether the manifester was a cleric, fighter, or thief. The devotion operated as follows:

Tenth Degree +2 long sword
Eleventh Degree +3 long sword
Twelfth Degree +4 long sword

The various benefits of this old form of body weaponry could not be used in conjunction with an actual weapon or suit of armor.

Significance

Being that it was a power that manipulated the body's molecules, body weaponry could not be manifested while imprisoned by a phase trap.

Users

This power was knowable by psionicists

In psionics terms, body weaponry was known by charonadaemons, planetars, and solars.

Tessadyle robes, unique psionically active clothing developed by illithids, conferred the ability to manifest body weaponry once per day.

Known/Notable Users

  • Those possessed by Bane, acting as ersatz avatars, were said to have a power similar to body weaponry, often warping their hands into fierce talons.
