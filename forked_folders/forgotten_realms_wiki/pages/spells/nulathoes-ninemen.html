Nulathoe's ninemen (pronounced nin-em-en), was a rare necromancy spell that preserved a dead body from decay.

Effects

This spell halted the decay and rot of corpses for burial preservation or for a possible candidate of raise dead or resurrection spells. Any corpse treated with this spell could not become undead nor be affected by animate dead. This spell did not heal wounds, stop the loss of blood, or reverse any damage to the corpse, but it did keep joints strong and supple and muscles from contracting rigor mortis. This spell had no effect on the undead.

Components

In addition to a verbal component, the caster had to touch the corpse to complete the spell. Materials required were a small amount of blood ground to a powder. The blood was sprinkled over the head, neck, torso and joints, then the moonstone dust was sprinkled over the whole body.

History

Nulathoe's ninemen was one of the spells found in Nchaser's Eiyromancia, which the wizard Nchaser gave to the High Captain Taerl of Luskan. The book was later stolen, but not before the wizard Arbane and others were able to examine it.
