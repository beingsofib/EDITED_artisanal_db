Mirror image allowed the caster to bring several illusions of him or herself into being in the caster's immediate surroundings. The images behaved and looked exactly like the caster, and the caster was able to merge with, or split from, any of the images at will. If an image were struck, such as with a weapon, it disappeared for good.

Components

The spell required only verbal and somatic components.

History

The spell was attributed to Netherese arcanist Smolyn in and was originally called Smolyn's replica.

The Cormyrean state sword Ilbratha allowed its wielder to cast mirror image on him or herself (and the sword) once per day.

As of the late 15 century DR, there were some among the feywardens of Sarifal who, by embracing the trickery of the Feywild, could gain the ability to replicate this spell.
