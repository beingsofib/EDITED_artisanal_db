Body equilibrium was a psionic devotion of the psychometabolism discipline that allowed the psionicist to mitigate the effects of their own weight.

Effects

Through a successful use, the manifester would effectively reduce their weight to correspond to a surface, allowing them to walk on liquids, or eliminate damage from falling into a solid surface. However, it only slowed their fall through the air to per second, and in addition allowed a psionicist to make themselves light enough to be blown away by a gust, an effect unlike that of the spell. Early reports spoke of mishaps that caused the psionicist to become dramatically heavier instead, remaining so until they managed to reverse the effect.

It would not be of much help in walking unscathed over the Lethe river of Nessus, or the rivers of fire of Phlegethos, or the lake of Cocytus, or the river Styx of Hades.

Descriptions post-dating the Year of Wild Magic, 1372 DR, instead claimed it only mitigated damage from falls partly, and that walking on any surface was only possible at a restricted, yet brisk, pace.

Notable Users

The ability was extremely common, existing even as a wild talent. Elminster in particular displayed this power as one of the many he'd been born with. Some psionicists known to wield this power included Teliene Coloara, and many yuan-ti such as Sysalok or the ancient masters.

It was also known to be employed by aratha, lythlyx, brain spiders, illithids, alhoon, ulitharids, neothelids, elder brains, intellect devourers, baku (such as Peter Thrum), topaz dragons and amethyst dragons (such as Eldenser) planetars, solars, Charon, Anthraxus, Lolth, and Bane.
