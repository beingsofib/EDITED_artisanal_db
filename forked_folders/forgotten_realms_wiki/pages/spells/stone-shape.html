Stone shape was a transmutation spell or alteration spell that reshaped stone objects.

Effects

Fine detail was not possible, but natural or worked stone could be manipulated by means of this spell to form most any shape the caster desired—an arch, a statue, a door, or a wall with a hole in it, for example. If small moving parts were required, there was a chance the mechanism just wouldn't function.

Components

In addition to verbal and somatic components, the spellcaster needed a material component in the form of a lump of clay. This clay had to be crudely molded into the desired shape and then brought into contact with the stone to be reshaped. Some druids also required mistletoe.

History

The spell was attributed to Netherese arcanist Proctiv in and was originally called Proctiv's shape stone.

Urdunnir dwarves were blessed by Dumathoin with the ability to cast stone shape whenever they wished.
