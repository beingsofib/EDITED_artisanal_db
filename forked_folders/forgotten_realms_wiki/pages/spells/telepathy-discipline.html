Telepathy (discipline)

The telepathy discipline of psionics revolved around making contact with and affecting the minds of other sentient creatures, influencing or controlling their behavior. Such powers could allow a manifester to read minds, erase memories, instigate doubt towards one's senses, Its equivalent among the schools of magic was enchantment. telepathy had two subdisciplines called Charm and Compulsion.

Description

Using any telepathic power required making "contact" with a target's mind, which some referred to as being its own specific power. Some even claimed that this contact power was necessary for using the psionic defense modes. Many telepathic powers also had the advantage of not needing to be within line of sight of a target.

Subdisciplines

Charm: Powers that altered the way a target regarded the manifester, typically making them view the user as a good friend.

Compulsion: Powers that force an affected creature to act in some manner beyond their natural will or alter the way in which their minds work. Powers under this subdiscipline ranged from granting ongoing control over a subject, instigating actions or changes to a subject's mental state, or powers that allowed a manifester to determine the actions that subject's are compelled to do.

Reputation

Telepathy and its powers were often stereotyped, in the eyes of the average commoner and others less familiar with psionics, as the heart of what psionics were all about.

Notable Users

  • Telepaths, psions who chose to specialize in mastery of telepathy over all other disciplines.
  • Among the various forms of psionics users, wilders were notable for struggling to use telepathy powers. This was due to their temperamental personalities. This difficulty would further worsen as a wilder's mastery of psionic powers grew.
  • For Athasian thri-kreen, telepathy was the most common primary discipline among chakak (psionicists) and tekchakak (pack psionicists), as it aided in group coordination and communication within the clutch. However, the discipline's powers were of limited use to Athasian thri-kreen when it came to combat, due to the considerable differences between the kreen and non-kreen minds.
  • Hrodel, the crystal dragon thane of Sardior, whose area of focus was telepathy.
