Protection from liquids

Spell
nameProtection from Liquids
inventorSpielbuk Spayle
school2eAbjuration
level2eWizard 3

Protection from liquids was an abjuration spell designed to allow wizards to create a liquid-proof barrier around small object, particularly spellbooks, as its creator Spielbuk Spayle (a pseudonym) had lost his first ever spellbook to water damage inflicted by a dunk into a stream.

Effects

This spell could only be cast upon objects that were smaller than the spellcaster. It could not be cast upon a living creature. The magical barrier it created would protect from all manner of liquids. This included corrosive liquids like acid, hazardous blood like that of a greater medusa, and breath weapons of a liquid nature, like that of a black dragon.

Components

In addition to verbal and somatic components, casting this spell required a material component in the form of a handful of dust, which was to be sprinkled over the desired target.
