Mummy dust (spell)

nameMummy Dust
school3eNecromancy

Mummy dust was a rare epic necromancy spell that allowed the caster to summon mummies to their aid.

Effects

There were two known versions of this spell as of the Year of Wild Magic, 1372 DR. One allowed the caster to summon a single large chaotic evil mummy. while the other allowed for the summoning of two large mummies. Regardless of the variant, the mummies became servants to the caster, until they were destroyed or the caster lost control of them by attempting to control more undead in total than their power allowed.

The mummies differed based on the spell variant.

The single mummy variant summoned a mummy that was ambidextrous and proficient in two-weapon fighting, able to perform quick strikes against new opponents after downing one, able to see in the dark, and able to perform a whirlwind attack. This mummy had the usual undead immunities to death magic, disease, mind-affecting spells, paralysis and poison. This mummy was proficient with katanas and short swords, and was wielding those weapons in combat. When attacking, this mummy was able to poison individuals, making them less flexible. When weaponless, it could fight with its hands and inflict mummy rot by doing so. The mummy could create an aura of fear around it. Fire was especially effective when combating this mummy. Weak blows were unable to hurt it.

The two large mummies were made from dust in the area around the mummy dust that was required to summon them. The two mummies had most of the same immunities and also the vulnerability to fire. The mummies fought using their fists. They were highly alert, were able to fight while blinded, had great fortitude, and were able to perform powerful slams. At the sight of a mummy, viewers with weak wills would be paralyzed with fear for some time. Whether or not an individual resisted, that creature could not be affected again by that mummy’s looks for one day. The mummies could also inflict mummy rot on their opponents. Only the most powerful blows could significantly hurt the mummies; lesser blows were not as effective. Casting this variant harmed the caster's knowledge.

Mummy dust could not be countered, interrupted, or dispelled using normal spells. To prevent the spell from being cast, one had to, for example, use an epic version of counterspell.

Development

Before it could be cast, mummy dust had to be developed. Both divine and arcane casters could develop this spell. Developing an epic spell was different from learning normal spells. Once an epic spell was developed, the caster simply knew the spell. A developed epic spell became a part of the caster and could even be prepared without a spellbook (if a wizard was the caster).

To develop the spell, 315,000 gp and 7 days were needed along with precise arcane procedures. Only spellcasters well-versed in spellcraft could develop mummy dust.

Components

Components differed depending on the version being cast. The single-mummy variant only required verbal and somatic components. The two-mummy variant additionally required sprinkling some mummy dust and harmed the caster's knowledge.
