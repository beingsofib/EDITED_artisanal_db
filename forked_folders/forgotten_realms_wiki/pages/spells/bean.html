Bean

A bean was the edible seed of a variety of plants known as legumes, which were cultivated across the continents of Toril, including Faerûn, Maztica, and Zakhara. They could be prepared for consumption in a variety of different ways, including baking, stewing, mashed into a paste, or eaten raw as sprouts.

Availability

Areas of the Realms where non-distinct varieties beans could be found cultivated included the following:

  • Aurora's Whole Realms Catalogue sold a variety of different beans in its "Aurora's Larder" section, including broad, green, and long beans.
  • In the old Calim Empire, the fertile farmlands of the city-state of Cursrah grew beans, including broad and chickpeas.
  • In Cormyr, beans were among the staple crops of the small farming town of Eveningstar.
  • In the Dalelands, beans were among the crops of farmers in Shadowdale.
  • In east Faerûn, despite its lands arid lands typically being poorly suited for farming, beans were among the crops that some farmers in Threskel managed to cultivate.
  • In the waters of the Moonsea, there was one small unnamed island whose inhabitants subsisted on part from growing beans.
  • In the Shining South, one could find large vats of white beans for sale at some retailers in Vaelan and some private gardens in Halarahh grew their own pole-beans.
  • In the nation of Sembia, some of the outer farms of Selgaunt grew beans.
  • In the Vast, the villages of Fallentree and Stanham were known to grow beans among other crops. They could also be found for sale in the Ravens Bluff Market Place.
  • In the Vilhon Reach, beans could be found cultivated in the nation of Turmish.

Beyond Faerun

  • A variety of different beans grew on Kara-Tur's isolated Malatran Plateau.
  • In the Hordelands, the isolated kingdom of Delmyr counted beans among its crops.
  • On the continent of Maztica, beans were grown alongside other crops in marshland The city of Maxal has such fields of beans.
  • In Maztica's Pasocada Basin, beans were one of the staple food crops of the indigenous Azuposi, who typically stored them in ceramic pots.

Varieties

Some beans in the Realms were referred to broadly by the term "pole-bean", as they grew on leafy vines that twisted and climbed up surfaces as they grew. Such beans usually needed some kind of structure for support, known as a bean trellis or beanpole, a common form being that of a tripod of wooden spars.

True Beans

Bag of beans: Though ordinary in appearance, the dried beans found in this magic item exploded if dropped, but if properly planted into the ground could produce a wide array of magical effects.

Black bean: A variety of bean that was grown in many parts of Kara-Tur.

Broad bean

Cannelloni bean: A white bean endemic to the Malatran Plateau that was related to kidney beans.

Chickpea

Euphorbia: A flowering desert plant whose beans could be turned into an oil.

Green bean: Also known as snap beans or string beans, were a variety of beans distinguished by being harvested and consumed with their enclosing seed pods.

Kidney bean

Lima bean

Long beans: A variety of legume endemic to Maztica.

Marrado beans: Beans (presumably of the marrado plant) that resembled kidney beans, except blood-red in color. Endemic to eastern Amn, Tethyr, and Valashar. They had a peppery taste and were typically dried for export.

Red bean: A variety of bean that was grown in many parts of Kara-Tur.

Runner-vine: A pole-bean type of legume found in Cormyr.

Soybean: A legume that was a staple crop in many parts of the continent of Kara-Tur.

Umbellin: A spicy, brown bean endemic to the Elemental Plane of Fire. it was cultivated by the City of Brass in the nearby Obsidian Fields.

Fake Beans

A number of plants unrelated to legumes commonly had the term "bean" applied to their seeds. These included the following:

Castor bean: The seed of a dangerous plant. It was commonly used in wizard laboratories.

Cocoa: The small brown seed of a plant endemic to Maztica.

Kaeth: Otherwise known as coffee, a large, soft, reddish brown seed that was used to brew an energizing beverage of the same name.

Vanilla: A delicate cooking spice and seed of a plant endemic to Maztica.

Culture

  • Beans were such versatile ingredients that one could find some cookbooks dedicated to the ways of cooking with them, such as 1001 Ways to Cook Beans and Cobblers and Pies: Baked Beans Can Be Fun.
  • The creation myths of the Azuposi told that when they ascended from the realm Shipapu into the True World, the god Masauwu gave them seeds for beans, mayz, and squash, all of which he taught them how to cultivate.
  • In the land of T'u Lung, beans were believed to magically correlate with feathered creatures, particularly fowl, as well as the direction of South, the summer season, and elemental fire.

Usages

  • Beans were part of a variety of dishes across the Realms, including Lomatran baked beans, potato scones, roasted cockatrice, tortillas, and Zucchini Saiyama style.
  • Beans were also a common element of inn pottage across the Realms.
  • Dried beans were sometimes placed within sealed pouches, creating a toy known as a bean-bag.

Magic

  • Some spells in the Realms could use beans as material components. Gust of wind required the seed of any legume as a material component, whilst Darsson's cooling breeze required one that was dried. The divine version of control vapor required specifically a bean.
  • Creating a potion of clarity required brewing the concoction overtop scorched, pungent beans.
  • Wizards sometimes made use of legume seeds, such as beans, in a variety of capacities in their laboratories.

Notable Users

Businesses

  • The Crock and the Helm, an inn in Skullport.
  • The Inn of the Dripping Dagger, an inn and tavern in Waterdeep, offered its patrons mild or hotly spiced bowls of cooked beans for 3 cp.
  • Old Troll's Foot, a restaurant in Kirinwood, offered its patrons a spicy broth of artichokes, asparagus, and beans.

Individuals

  • Hephtos, a Vergadainan priest and street vendor in Ravens Bluff who generously handed out bean & grain based dishes in the city's Market Place. He was such a fan of the vegetables that he had authored both 1001 Ways to Cook Beans and Cobblers and Pies: Baked Beans Can Be Fun.
  • Storm Silverhand grew pole-beans in her home's kitchen garden.

Organizations

  • The Everlasting, the largest holy slayer organization in Zakhara, often kept their fortress Sarahin's kitchen granary stocked with beans.

Races & Sentient Creatures

  • Many cloud giants on Toril were avid gardeners, growing gigantic versions of produce, such as beans the size of turnips.
  • It was not uncommon to find fields of beans in halfling villages.

History

Sometime in the 14 century DR, as part of a stage performance at the Ale & Hearty in the city of Schamedar, the wizard Wiglaf used his magical abilities to conjure a great sky-reaching crop of vegetables that included beans.

In the , three Chosen of Silvanus got together on Sapra with the leaders of the Turmishan druidic order known as the Emerald Enclave and conducted a magical ritual that ended a famine that had been plaguing that nation. This caused all manner of food to instantaneously grow throughout the land, including beans.
