Vampiric feast

Vampiric feast was a rare epic necromancy spell that allowed the caster to drink in the life force of their opponents, as if a vampire.

Effects

As of the Year of Wild Magic, 1372 DR, casting vampiric feast made the caster drink in the entire life force of every chosen creature within 20 feet (6.1 meters), slaying them instantly and increasing the caster's health. The caster could only absorb enough life force to fully heal themself; the remaining life force dissipated into the fabric of the weave. The life-force of slain creatures also coalesced as a greater shadow, which helped the caster fight. The spell was even able to damage undead creatures.

Accounts varied on if the spell was subject to spell resistance. Fortitude played an important roll in partially resisting the effects of the spell. Individuals with high fortitude were sometimes able to successfully mitigate the effects of the spell, thus losing only about half of their health. This spell was not stopped by death ward or similar effects. Vampiric feast couldn't be countered, interrupted, or dispelled using normal spells or physical attacks.

Development

Before it could be cast, vampiric feast had to be developed. Both divine and arcane casters could develop this spell. Developing an epic spell was different from learning normal spells. Once an epic spell was developed, the caster knew the spell. A developed epic spell became a part of the caster and could even be prepared without a spellbook (if a wizard was the caster). Only those well-versed in spellcraft could develop vampiric feast.

Components

To cast vampiric feast, the caster had to use verbal and somatic components.
