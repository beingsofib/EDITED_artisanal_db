Spellscar

Spell
nameSpellscar

A spellscar was a mark or scar on an individual who was exposed to the Spellplague. It was a mild form of plaguechange that had the potential to grant unique magical powers to the afflicted individual. Such individuals were referred to as spellscarred, and some among them trained to master their powers.

Description

While spellscars could sometimes include physical abnormalities or injuries—such as blue scar tissue or strange runes on the skin—they were not necessarily accompanied by the types of bodily and mental deformities seen in other victims of the Spellplague. More commonly, they were invisible and intangible until activated, at which point they were characterized by a blue glow or blue flames. These flames might appear as jagged cracks on the skin, symbols or brands on the face, or even as halos or wings.

Spellscars were charged with powerful arcane energy. While there were some who strove to master their spellscars, activating them and using their power as they saw fit, to debilitating pain, although the blue flames of a spellscar did not burn or seriously injure the afflicted individual.

Acquisition

Acquiring a spellscar was the result of coming into contact with the Spellplague, Only some plaguechanged individuals acquired a spellscar: and more commonly in those who had come into contact with less virulent versions of the Spellplague's energies In some cases, a spellscar could manifest many years after an individual was exposed to the Spellplague, or even in people who had never come into direct contact with the Spellplague, although these were usually arcane spellcasters of some kind. Spellscars could also be inherited from a parent or ancestor, sometimes skipping one or more generations before reappearing.

The first appearance or emergence of a spellscar could be traumatizing. The event was sometimes accompanied by an eruption of plaguefire that disfigured the spellscarred individual and others nearby, or in some cases, caused the destruction of people and places in the immediate vicinity. Individuals who experienced such a catastrophic awakening of their spellscar were known as spellscarred harbingers, and were often feared and hated for the destruction that they had unintentionally caused.

While any creature could become spellscarred, giants, goblinoids, illithids, kobolds, lizardfolk, marine creatures, wyverns, It was possible to acquire more than one spellscar.

Abilities

Merely having a spellscar allowed afflicted individuals to sense the presence of the Spellplague nearby, but those spellscarred who wished to manifest more powerful, strange, and unique abilities had to train to do so. Many spellscarred individuals' lifespans were extended by one to two decades, and in rare cases, this longevity was inherited by their children.

While the powers bestowed by spellscars were generally beneficial to the afflicted individual, the use of a spellscar often came with a cost. Having a spellscar generally rendered the bearer more susceptible to the energies of the Spellplague, Some spellscarred individuals suffered from disturbing dreams or bizarre visions which sometimes conveyed obscure secrets about the nature of magic. Activating a spellscar within the confines of a mythal caused that mythal to treat the spellscarred individual as an intruder within its field.

Students of the Plague

Those spellscarred individuals who devoted themselves to studying and training with their spellscar generally gained the ability to wield fire- and force-based powers or energies that could wither both the body and mind. Some could grant themselves additional powers by focusing their spellscar's energies, such as by funneling the blue flames to their eyes to see invisibility

With sufficient training, so-called spellscarred savants could overcome many of the inherent limitations of their spellscars, and even tap into spellfire to heal themselves and energize or vaporize others as they saw fit. While potent, spellscars did not generally confer the types of monstrous power seen in creatures that had been plaguechanged in more extreme fashion.

History

Spellscars first appeared in the , as a consequence of the Spellplague. The initial wave of the Spellplague often caused more extreme mutations than just spellscars, but in the following years, the scars became more common among those exposed to pockets of the Spellplague, and spellscarred individuals could be encountered all across Toril and Abeir. in Samarach, near the Plaguewrought Land in the Vilhon Wilds, and in the Underdark, such as in Ch'Chitl, the Deep Wastes,

Spellscarred and plaguechanged individuals were often viewed as abominations, and as harbingers of fear and madness. Thus, they were often treated as outcasts, regardless of their own attitude toward their spellscar. while in the cities, the spellscarred were often quarantined in some fashion: Waterdeep preferred them to stay in Skullport or the slums of Mistshore, Baldur's Gate unofficially quarantined them to Scarred Alley outside the city walls, and Neverwinter held them first in a treatment center in the House of Knowledge before exiling them to Helm's Hold per the controversial "Protector's Law". Helm's Hold was the closest thing to a safe haven for spellscarred individuals, but even there they were not allowed in some taverns and faced a growing movement to expel them because of their "impurity". Elsewhere across Faerûn, the spellscarred were seen as lesser members of society in Amn and as deformed in the Moonsea. and in Turmish, the Emerald Enclave ruthlessly killed them, viewing their spellscars as blights against the natural order. Meanwhile, as of the , the wizards of Deep Imaskar were experimenting on spellscarred individuals, and had also led them to develop a way to remove spellscars entirely.

Still, there were those who were proud of their spellscars,

With the return of Mystra in the late 15 century DR, spellscars ceased to function as the Spellplague began to dissipate. Those who had visible scars or marks found that they now appeared as mundane but vivid tattoos, which in rare cases might glow when they cast arcane spells. In the years following the Second Sundering, many spellscars faded—and, in rare cases, even disappeared entirely—while others gained new magical properties.

Notable Spellscarred Individuals

  • Beaugrat, a barbarian whose spellscar allowed him to fire blasts of blue flame.
  • Vansi Bloodscar, an orc of the Many-Arrows tribe whose spellscar manifested as a ridge of fiery bones and spikes extending from her skin.
  • Sophraea Carver, whose spellscar granted her a limited sixth sense.
  • Catti-brie developed a spellscar in the shape of an hourglass before her death. Her reincarnation, Ruqiah, had two spellscars: one in the shape of Mielikki's holy symbol and the other in the shape of Mystra's holy symbol. These allowed her to shapechange and to call storms.
  • Myrin Darkdance, a wizard whose spellscar manifested as blue runes on her skin, and which allowed her to absorb the memories and magical powers of those around her, including others' spellscars.
  • Kalen Dren, whose spellscar made him numb to pain and other sensations.
  • Durral, a half-orc bouncer at the Knight 'n' Shadow tavern whose fists were spellscarred, which magnified the power behind his punches.
  • Duvan, a rogue with a spellscar that made him immune to the effects of the Spellplague and of other spellscars.
  • Elgreth, a treasure-hunter whose spellscar allowed him to detect magic.
  • Aoth Fezim, a Thayan mercenary whose spellscar appeared as blue flames within his blind eyes, and gave him extrasensory sight. It also stopped him from aging.
  • Fox-at-Twilight, whose spellscar burned the flesh of those she touched.
  • Grandmother Ash, an awakened tree with a spellscar that allowed her to create humanoid simulacra.
  • Gregor, an alchemist whose spellscar bleached the area around his left ear and gave him the ability to immediately know the alchemical properties of any ingredient.
  • Narla Gulmaern, a tiefling tavernkeeper whose spellscar disrupted arcane magic and rituals.
  • Penelope Harpell, a wizard whose spellscar manifested as a blob of discolored skin on the side of her torso. She was one of a few members of the Harpell family to have a spellscar in the late 15 century DR.
  • Marion Hexbane, an undead member of the Blackdagger Bandits whose spellscar increased her power.
  • Kara Hulmaster, whose spellscar was a permeant serpentine mark on one hand and manifested as a blue glow in her eyes,
  • Jahin, a water genasi with a hydra-shaped spellscar above her left ear.
  • Raidon Kane, a half-elf monk whose spellscar manifested as a blue tattoo on his chest and gave him advantages when fighting denizens of the Far Realm.
  • Madruen Morganoug, whose spellscar manifested as glowing skin and made him impervious to weapons.
  • Anusha Marhana, whose spellscar gave her a power similar to astral projection.
  • Moirah, whose spellscar manifested beneath her navel and gave her power over the passions of others.
  • Ruen Morleth, whose spellscar manifested as red eyes and allowed him to know when someone would die simply by touching them.
  • Sayeed, whose spellscar left him without the need for food, drink, or sleep.
  • Slanya, a monk whose spellscar manifested across her whole body and allowed her to enhance or suppress the spellscars of others.
  • Icelin Tearn, whose spellscar magnified the power of her spells but carried a risk of wild magic.
  • Eralynn Thunsonn, a dwarf who gained the ability to practice magic when her hands became spellscarred.
  • Seldra Tylmarande, a half-elf with a physical spellscar on her arm that manifested as blue flames wreathing her arms and face. It gave her the ability to control plaguechanged monsters.
  • Vraith, an elven wizard with a spellscar on her chest, which enabled her to use the souls of others to enact the Ritual of the Border.

Notable Spellscarred Organizations

  • The Heirs of Azure were an oft-violent organization that fought to protect their fellow spellscarred in Helm's Hold. It's most influential leaders were Meryth Heartwood and Halas.
  • The Order of Blue Flame was a pseudo-religious order that was ostensibly dedicated to helping people acquire and control their spellscars, but in truth they had sinister intentions to spread the Spellplague.
  • Scar Company was a ragtag military unit in Neverwinter formed of spellscarred troops. They were tasked with guarding against intrusions from the Chasm in the late 15 century DR.
