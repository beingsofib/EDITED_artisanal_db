Astral projection was a psionic power of the psychoportation discipline

Effects

Unlike probability travel, which physically transported a manifester into the Astral Plane, astral projection created an astral form, and all possessions on their person, leaving the physical body behind in a state of suspended animation. though in the late 14 century DR this distinction was found to have disappeared. When transporting others, the manifester and their passengers had to be linked together in a circle at the time of manifestation. Passengers had to remain with the manifester at all times, lest they risk being stranded on the Astral Plane. The power took a total of 30 minutes to manifest, but had an indefinite duration, terminating whenever the manifester desired. Negate psionics being manifested upon either the physical body or the astral form would terminate the power.

This astral form was solid on the Astral Plane and behaved in most situations as though it were a physical body, which was visible for the first that stretched out from the astral body before merging with the plane, fading out into an invisible and intangible state

When an astral body finally reached a destination outside of the Astral Plane with a color pool, a temporary physical body was formed. This resembled the manifester's original body down to the exact detail, including physical and mental abilities, though lacked any of their physical possessions except for magic items and items under a spell effect, and was still tethered to the physical body by the silver cord. Due to the principle of "planar dissonance", it was impossible for a manifester to enter and form a temporary body on the same plane of existence as their physical body, not even by entering through a color pool, nor could they direct magical or psionic powers into that plane. The version of astral projection used in the late 14 century DR seemed to lack these restrictions.

If either this second body or the astral form were to be destroyed, the silver cord and the manifester's consciousness would return to their physical body,

If the nearly indestructible silver cord was somehow severed, the manifester would be simultaneously killed both astrally and materially, with the astral form left to dissolve into the Astral Plane itself. This death was irrevocable, only possible to undo through powerful means such as the wish spell.

For older versions of astral projection, a manifestation backfiring had the consequence of attracting the attention of some powerful entity within the Astral Plane.

Displays

This power exhibited no display when manifested.

Significance

The Dimensional anchor spell and its psionic equivalent could prevent a manifester from using astral projection.

Users

This power was knowable to psionicists

Astral projection was exhibited as an innate psionic ability by baku, ancient or older brainstealer dragons, elder brains, dhours, some githzerai, psionic illithids, adult intellect devourers, opinicus, and shedu.

Powerful shamans of the Great Worm tribe, who worshiped Uthgar's Great Worm beast totem, were capable of assuming a "great worm" form that conferred a number of psionic abilities, including astral projection.

Known/Notable Users

  • Elrem the Wise, a powerful Uthgar shaman of the Great Worm tribe.
  • Lertis the Seer, a Banite high priest and psionic wild talent, who used astral projection to search that plane for Bane.
  • K'yorl Odran, a drow psionicist and the matron mother of House Oblodra.
  • Lugribossk, a proxy of the illithid god Ilsensine.
