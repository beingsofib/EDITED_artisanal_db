Raurivyl Ornshield was a former slave turned pirate and the captain of the infamous Scimitar of Fire, which was active on the Sea of Swords in the mid-to-late 14 century DR.

Description

He was an overweight Calishite human who kept his curly beard perfumed.

Abilities

Despite his weight, he was known to be a brawler.

Activities

Captain Raurivyl Ornshield was feared and respected as a pirate. With a hot-tempered hatred for the realm of Amn, Raurivyl focused his attacks on Amnian vessels, such as coasters and caravels. His ship's flame-red sails were infamous, scaring many merchants as soon as they set eyes on them, and his ship's swiftness and his crew's skill meant few other vessels could outdistance or outmaneuver the Scimitar of Flame and no ship he targeted got away unscathed.

At the least, Raurivyl would seize a defeated ship's cargo and capture important persons as hostages or hold them for ransom—if none was paid, the unfortunate captive was keelhauled. Those who survived were marooned on a shore miles from the nearest port, giving the Scimitar more time to get away. His attacks rarely killed a whole crew of scuttled a ship but he nonetheless took great joy in sinking Amnian ships.

Relationships

Captain Raurivyl Ornshield was served by his first mate, the half-orc Dreak. They made an effective pair, with Raurivyl having the brains and plans and Dreak the brawn.

Most of his crew were unaware of their captain's reasons for targeting Amn, let alone his past.

History

Although Calishite, Raurivyl Ornshield was born in Waterdeep around 1338 DR. While still a child, he was kidnapped and sold into slavery in the subterranean city of Skullport, and was transported to Amn. There, from the age of eight, he grew up a slave on a plantation lying east of Lake Esmel that was owned by the Colwyvv family. He befriended a fellow slave, the half-orc Dreak.

Then, with the advent of the First Dragonspear War in the , the number of guards at the plantation were reduced. Seizing the advantage, Raurivyl, Dreak, and other slaves made their escape. However, instead of running to safe havens in the north or south, Raurivyl and Dreak went west, taking a ship and fighting a vengeful war against Amn, its merchant houses, and the Council of Six. Helming the Scimitar of Fire, they became infamous as pirates, feared by both Amnian and Calishite ship captains for over a decade by 1370 DR.

Some time in the 1360s DR, a Waterdhavian sailor named Faerim came to be a captive aboard the Scimitar of Fire. Serving as a crewman, he would be a witness to Raurivyl's voyages to the west.

Seeking a desert island he might use as a safe haven and base, Raurivyl took the Scimitar of Fire westward on several voyages beyond the Gull Rocks. However, they found naught but a vast expanse of empty ocean. On three occasions they encountered the naval vessels of Evermeet, which rose out of the sea, hovered in the air, and tilted to direct their strange weaponry at the Scimitar. Via magical speaking horns, the elves ordered the pirates to leave. Sensibly, Captain Ornshield obeyed, but still the flying elven ships followed them until they were long gone.

Following months of such futile searching, Raurivyl returned to the Nelanther Isles. There, Faerim escaped, even taking some pirate treasure with him back to Waterdeep, along with tales of the elven ships. Raurivyl sheltered the Scimitar in a cavern harbor on the island of Carcathen and later located a safehouse he wasn't yet sure about in Ilyaport in Lantan.

As of 1370 DR, after laying low for a number of months at Carcathen, both Raurivyl and the crew had grown restless. Fortunately, the turmoil they found in Amn at the time attracted their attention. Yet Captain Raurivyl Ornshield attracted another's attention too: the Rundeen mercantile organization, who desired the Scimitar of Fire's services as pirates in the Shining Sea. In trade, they offered safe havens, ship repairs, and whatever else Raurivyl might wish for. However, although intrigued by a profitable alliance, he knew the Rundeen acted much the same as his Amnian enemies and so he was just as likely, if not more so, to start targeting Rundeen shipping instead.

Despite this, the Scimitar of Fire was reported to be Rundeen pirate vessel later that year.
