Ratin Sesescor was a cheese-loving rat that dwelt in Alexander Rainforest's home in the Lower City of Baldur's Gate in the late 15 century DR.

History

Before his death, Alexander Rainforest tried to befriend Ratin Sesescor, feeding him various cheeses, earning him the name "cheese-dropper, a person who drops cheese." Alexander discovered that the rat liked red, white, yellow, mellow, and sharp cheeses crumbled into small bits, and disliked smoked, blue, and waxed. When Alexander was murdered by the serial killer Dolor, Ratin eagerly ate the "red morsels" spilled from his friend's body, enjoying them more than cheese. Ratin did not speak Common, on account of being a simple rodent, but he witnessed the murder and could describe the killer as a dwarf dressed in all red, to whoever was capable of communicating with a beast through magic.
