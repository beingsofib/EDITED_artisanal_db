Vilander the fierce was a ferocious pirate captain from the Sea of Fallen Stars. After the mass-death of the population of the Whamite Isles, he survived in a lighthouse in Agenais with his prize, the Crown of Salt.

Description

By the , Vilander was visibly ancient. He still wore clothes befitting opf a pirate captain, including a coat and a perculiar hat. His body was reasonably light and easy-to-carry.

Personality

Vilander was racist, particularly to sea elves, whom he derided as "fish-elves". When angry, he loudly shouted abuse.

In undead-plagued Agenais, Vilander was security conscious and was ready to defend his lighthouse against any intruders. He was not above presenting as harmless to lure others into the range of his musket.

Abilities

Vilander was completely deaf. Although he wielded a musket, he was not particularly capable in a fight.

Possessions

Vilander's most significant posession was the Crown of Salt, an item of great significance from Myth Nantar. He kept it underneath his bed. If it was taken from him, he would try any tactic to retrieve it, but would eventually give up.

Vilander had a small fortune in treaures from the ocean stashed in his lighthouse. These included shells from sea turtles, urchin gems, precious pieces of coral, and pearls. The total price of these items was more than 6,000 gp.

Activities

On the stairs of his lighthouse in Agenais, Vilander had set up an unrelenting cavalcade of traps. These traps included falling beams, collapsing stairs, concealed spikes and tripwires. Inside, he lived a messy lifestyle and neglected to wash his dishes. He surveyed the town of Agenais from the windows of his lighthouse.

History

At some point, Vilander came into posession of the Crown of Salt, which was stolen from Myth Nantar by a pirate.

In early Marpenoth of the , as part of the Twelfth Serôs War, a powerful variant of kelpie used its song to charm the people of the Whamite Isles into drowning themselves. This decimated the population of Agenais, but Vilander's deafness made him immune to the kelpie's powers.

By mid-marpenoth of 1370 DR, he had barricaded himself inside of Agenais's lighthouse with his treasure.
