Xexillidaulgrymm was a shapeshifting fang dragon which terrorized Zazesspur in the mid-to-late 14 century DR. It was afflicted by a strange case of split personality.

Description

It was about long.

Personality

Xexillidaulgrymm spent much time in "borrowed" personalities, which were altered while he was inhabiting them: the people he became suffered from intense migraines, memory loss, mood swings, and an obsession with hunting "Grim" (actually Xexillidaulgrymm himself, when his own personality was not submerged) and with a certain amulet they all bore.

When in his own personality, Xexillidaulgrymm was sadistic, antagonizing, and proud of his murders; he was in turn obsessed with the amulet, and with defeating its "bearers", whose "use" of the amulet brought him equal agony to that which they felt.

Abilities

The dragon's ability to drain life energy with his bite had an effect that extended so far as to absorb a dying foe's likeness and memories, which submerged his own at times; the ability appeared to be linked to an amulet the dragon sought.

Possessions

Xexillidaulgrym was somehow linked to an enchanted amulet.

History

At some point, Xexillidaulgrymm had a hoard near Myth Drannor, which he'd then abandoned. The dragon was once attacked by a wizard who wielded a strange amulet, which he later made his own; its power was used in an attempt to steal the dragon's soul. To his recollection, the amulet had been wielded by numerous enemies throughout the centuries, all of them being vanquished, though the dragon's remembrances grew increasingly sketchy the more recent the slaying was.

In Flamerule of , Count Kelmar Dargren of Zazesspur went missing from his estate; his dead body was found in a nearby forest a tenday afterwards. However, throughout that time, the Count had been sighted in the city proper, behaving in an uncharacteristically irritable and volatile manner, leading assassins from the School of Stealth into battle with a spree killer known for their gruesome, wanton murders and extreme cruelty, with the face of each body removed cleanly and the rest of the carcass damaged beyond recognition, with signs that the killing had involved an inordinately long, excruciating torture beforehand. He knew the killer was Xexillidaulgrymm, whom he believed to have stolen his soul; bizarrely, the Count blacked out from migraines shortly before every time his assassins encountered the dragon he was hunting, or was otherwise missing from these hunts despite commanding them personally. Almost certainly, that was not the Count, but Xexillidaulgrymm himself, who had taken on his likeness and memories; in turn, this could only mean his death had been the wyrm's doing.

About five days before the end of his tenure impersonating Kelmar, one of his murders was a young girl, whose songs he still remembered. Distributing those to his men, he resumed his own personality briefly, and disguised itself as a beggar to ambush these men. Despite their magical aid, he slew them all easily, suffering only a shoulder wound. He also found himself noticed by a guard, whom he immediately attacked, seeking to quell any possibility the guard called for support.

Xexillidaulgrymm attacked the guard Thaedras after slaying all of the assassins; the feeding almost certainly overwhelmed the dragon's memories with those of its target. Captain Begg spoke to the guard later that day. His injuries from the feeding appeared to have vanished; he was also wearing an amulet, and had developed similar migraines and memory loss to those that Kelmar Dargren was displaying earlier. The dragon's disappearance, all in all, all but outright stated that the dragon had taken his memories and copied his shape, as he drained his lifeforce.

The dragon remained alive, after a fashion, into the year . At that time, its location was unknown.
