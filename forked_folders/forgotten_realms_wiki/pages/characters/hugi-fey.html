Hugi (fey)

Hugi was a middle-aged fey courtier in the Seelie Court of Queen Titania in the late 14 century DR.

Description

Hugi was quite limber for his age and noted for his wide eyes, full of wonder.

History

In the late 14 century DR, a group of adventurers from the city of Ravens Bluff journeyed to the plane of Ysgard in search of the legendary Well of Mimir, having been instructed by the city's Ministry of Art to do so in order to learn how a powerful githyanki artifact known only as the Great Machine could be destroyed. They arrived in the layer of Asgard by means of a ship, either the Fyrfload or Laughing Gull, having ridden as passengers from the gate-town of Glorium through the Whirling Gate. While coasting through the waters of Asgard, they eventually spotted along the shoreline the great beer hall Gloamingard.

Prior to their arrival, the wandering realm of the Seelie Court had arrived to the area and asked Gloamingard's owner for his hospitality. Surprised, but not wishing to offend Queen Titania, Reglin obliged. Following a grand feast and the rowdy courtiers gradually irritating the adventurers, Queen Titania gathered the fey around her with a clap and expressed a desire for entertainment, proposing a series of contests between the mortals and her courtiers. Her consort, Enilbright, further suggested that wagers be made to to make things more interesting. Agreeing with him, Titania offered to wager three magic items if the adventurers would wager three comparably permanent or major charged items in turn. She offered as prizes an ice sapphire, a Svartalfheim cloak, and a horn of Valhalla.

Unwilling to jeopardize the lives of his own men, who were largely befuddled by fey glamour and thus unlikely to perform well, Reglin turned to the adventurers and pulled them aside. He apologized to them for not having formally extended his hospitality earlier and beseeched them to aid him, offering each a silver armring worth 20 gold pieces, plus a gold armring for each contest they participated in.

In the third of four tests, the two groups competed to be the first to reach the far end of the hall's interior, with various tables and other furniture being pushed out of the way to accommodate them. Among the fey courtiers, Hugi was chosen as the champion of running.
