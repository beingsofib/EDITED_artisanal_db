Wyrmbait Nix was a dragon-hunter of Sembia in the mid-to-late 14 century DR.

Description

He had a scar from a red dragon's bite, located somewhere delicate.

Personality

He was always hungry, but loved to make bets, which he lost often.

Possessions

He had an ivory toothpick.

Activities

Wyrmbait Nix captured baby dragons for wizards' menageries. He spent winters in smaller towns, trolling the taverns for ladies he could show his scars to; he was especially fond of the Dragon Defeated tavern in Halfknot.

Relationships

He argued about dragons almost every night with two fellow tavern-goers at the Dragon Defeated: Silvenestri Silver and Badger Bates.

History

Wyrmbait Nix had been a dragon catcher for some time by 1362 DR, having scars from red dragons, blue dragons, and green dragons who'd bitten him.

In the mid-winter of the , the Dragon Defeated sign was repainted; the owner, Varney, had it redrawn to resemble Nix and two other regulars: Silvenestri Silver and Badger Bates. They soon became local celebrities, "dragon experts", taking part in a contest every mid--tenday where they judged tales of dragons.

During the last such contest held, in late winter or early spring of that year, the painter of the sign, Petra, challenged Silvenestri Silver on green dragons being cross-eyed, and produced a huge number of paintings of such green dragons, all of which she claimed to have painted from life. He corrected himself as speaking about white dragons, to which Petra responded by revealing her paintings of white dragons. Wyrmbait was surprised to hear she talked to dragons.

Badger Bates dismissed her paintings for not containing any pictures of Malaeragoth and when she claimed to have seen Malaeragoth more recently than he had, Silver required her to show proof. She painted Malaeragoth on a wall of the tavern, but refused to draw his eyes, instead simply describing them. Bates refused to pay up unless the eyes were drawn, though Nix believed her.

She finished the picture, took the betting money, and ran. When her picture of Malaeragoth came to life, however, Wyrmbait had the good sense and reflexes to jump out the window and run for the edge of town; he returned to find Badger Bates flattened. He related to the tavern owner that Petra was sorry about the damage.
