Thelgaar Ironhand was the king of Oman, on Oman's Isle in the center of the Moonshae Isles, as of the mid–14 century DR. By the end of his reign, he was known as the mightiest of the Northlander kings.

Description

He was a massive man. He had a great black beard that came to be streaked with gray in the last decade of his life.

Personality

Thelgaar had a magnetic character and carried himself as befitted a powerful ruler. He was a man of few words, often acknowledging his guests with little more than an imperious nod or hand gesture that would have seemed disrespectful from a man of lesser status. He did not deign to talk over the voices of others, instead waiting for them to be silent in his presence. His voice was loud and booming when he spoke.

He was not easily impressed with good food, yet he enjoyed lots of it.

Possessions

Thelgaar wielded an enchanted greatsword called Ironhand's Reckoning.

He kept a number of servants and slaves, many of them women from far off lands.

History

He was born in the village of Trondhelm on Oman's Isle,

The greatest threat to his unification of the bickering lords of Oman were the raiding giants of the Grampalt Highlands. He was known to personally lead his soldiers against these giants.

In the mid–1330s DR,

After nearly five decades in power, Thelgaar had cemented himself as the most powerful ruler of the most powerful Northman kingdom in the Moonshaes. By this time, he had come to believe that his people's future lay in peace and trade rather than war and raiding.

In Mirtul of the , several Northlander kings—including Grunnarch the Red of Norland and Raag Hammerstaad of Norheim—brought their hosts of longships to Thelgaar Ironhand's Iron Keep on Oman's Isle to prepare an attack on the southern Moonshae Isles. In the first council of northern Kings and Captains, Thelgaar announced his intention of making long lasting peace with the Ffolk and start a prosperous trading relationship. Most of the warriors that were present did not agree and decided to continue with the plan of raiding and pillaging the cities of Caer Corwell and Caer Callidyrr and the rest of the Ffolk lands. The same night, Thelgar was slain by Kazgoroth who took the identity of the king and carried on with the plan to attack Caer Corwell.

He was buried in a cairn within the Old Stone Wood.
