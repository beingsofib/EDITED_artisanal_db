Nagreous Twiddle was a gnome artificer and scientist from Sembia. In the , he voyaged on the Emerald Tayar to discover Myth Nantar with the aid of his experimental diving bell, the Colimpha.

Description

Nagreous stood tall and weighed . He had white hair, gray eyes, and skin that was a dark tan in color. He had a moustache, which he often twirled while speaking. He wore reading glasses.

Personality

Nagreous considered himself a genius, and others beneath him. He was a strong advocate for the scientific method, which he believed allowed everything in the universe to be explained. Conversely, he thought little of faith. He was the sort to talk at length to others about the values of his science. He didn't approve of wizards, as he didn't understand their magic.

Abilities

Nagreous was a prodigy and brilliant inventor. He was skilled with crafting firearms, shipbuilding, blacksmithing, and other forms of engineering. He could create clocks, basic steam engines, heliographs and more.

Possessions

Nagreous carried a great many useful and strange items. These included a horse pistol and smokepowder, a pocket watch with an alarm function, a prism, a large magnet in the shape of a horseshoe, a grappling hook, an experimental tank of air which allowed someone to breathe underwater. His greatest posession the Colimpha, a huge diving bell of his own design that could allow up to six individuals to safely descend deep underwater.

Activities

Much of Nagreous's inventions involved forging large pieces of metal. He worked with Sembia's navy, which tested prototypes for him.

Relationships

The noble Sturge Whitsunday and Nagreous had a respect for each other. Sturge considered Nagreous a national treasure of Sembia, although he sometimes became frustrated by Nagreous's pedantic lectures. Nagreous appreciated Sturge's intelligence, and worked hard to curry favor with the paladin.

The wizard Rondo Marsh had an unusual relationship with Nagreous. Rondo found Nagreous endlessly funny, and couldn't stop from laughing at his mannerisms and size. Nagreous thought little of Rondo's intelligence, and often tried to stop Rondo from laughing at him.

The half sea-elf Illsa Merrymore thought that Nagreous's inventions were idiotic and his lectures were boring. She mocked and belittled him at every possible occasion. Nagreous had set a goal to try and humble her somehow.

History

Nagreous was born in the gnomish town of Hardbuckler. His father had been an ironworker. The young Nagreous quickly showed signs of prodigy when he invented a system of ducts and a mechanical fan to ventilate and heat his father's forge. When he came of age, he relocated to Sembia and put his inventions to work in the shipyards. He spent 20 years inventing devices for ships in Sembia.

Nagreous's father died and the inventor's work shifted to involve huge acts of metal-casting. This led to him experimenting with diving bells. He let Sembia's navy test a prototype, and unveiled the finished Colimpha. He named the vessel after a relative, Colimpha Twiddle.

In mid-Mirtul of 1370, when Nagreuous was 82,

The crew sailed for eight days and passed the Pirate Isles on their way to the Whamite Isles, following a sea shanty that hinted at the location of Myth Nantar.

The crew positioned the ship over Mount Halaath, nearly a mile northwest of Myth Nantar. Nagreous and adventuring party climbed into the Colimpha, and descended to the seafloor. The shalarin Tu'olo'sara took care of them overnight, and the next morning they appeared before the Nantarn Council. They were tried as tresspassers in Myth Nantar, and charged by Tu'olo'sara with returning the Crown of Salt from the Whamite Isles in exchange for their freedom.

They were sent through a portal to the nation of Agenais in the Whamite Isles, which was littered with rotting undead from the Twelfth Serôs War. They returned to Myth Nantar as heroes and the Crown of Salt was returned to adorn Ri'ola'con's head. Nagreous and the rest of the party were each given special necklaces of adaptation made from coral. He returned to Selgaunt, where he was celebrated as a hero along with the party.
