Exeter the Excellent Mage Most Magnificent was a human wizard, who in his old age retired in the Nellie Thursday Home for Experienced Adventurers, until, bitter and jealous of his companion Wemicks accolades always being told, went back to adventuring and later to seek vengeance upon Wemick and the retirement home both.

Personality

As he become steadily older he became more crazed, and as advanced age took his rational mind he became ever more delusional, with delusions of grandeur. In the end he became mono-focused on Wemic and the retirement home as objects to not only destroy but break before him and his mighty powers.

Abilities

As a wizard he was able to cast the spells of burning hands, magic missile, unseen servant, continual light, detect invisibility, invisibility, shatter, dispel magic, hold person, melf's minute meteors, slow, fumble, polymorph other, stoneskin, telekinesis, cone of cold, teleport, and globe of invulnerability.

Possessions

He wore a pair of bracers of defense, a ring of feather falling, alongside a brooch of shielding. He wielded a staff of striking and a wand of fire. Also carrying a potion of flying.

Relationships

Once a member of the Company of the Swan, in his old crazed age he would refer to it as the Company of the Sitting Duck, and begin to resent all his old adventuring buddies. While being bitter against everyone he ever travelled with in his younger days, his burning hatred would be reserved from his fellow mage Wemick.

History

Exeter was a member of the Company of the Swan, alongside Matilda the White Lady, Henry the Shadow Master, Ratha Rann, Sagrais Bigelow, and his fellow wizard Wemick. With one illusionist and two wizards in the same adventuring company it was only natural that one would upstage the other, even if not intentionally. Harboring a small inferiority complex he had a friend-foe relationship with Wemick, but nothing too severe at that time. In time they all grew old and finally retired to the Nellie Thursday Home for Experienced Adventurers where Exeter and Wemic became roommates for several years. In the home however many of the stories told by the other members of the old adventuring company was about how Wemick did this or Wemick did that and never really about what Exeter did. As advanced age crept in he began suffering from a severe persecution complex and having delusions of grandeur.
He himself would duel Wemick in a fight to the death, believing that only then could he upstage Wemick and show that he had always been the greater mage.
