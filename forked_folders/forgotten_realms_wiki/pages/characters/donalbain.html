Donalbain was the founder of the bardic college at Broadleaf Lodge during the mid-to-late 14 century DR.

Activities

He was known to host debates, discussions, and lectures in the college. In cases when evil arose to threaten the Ffolk, he also hosted meetings of concerned bards in which they nominated spies to battle such threats.

He was happy to open his doors and share his resources with other bards, but he expected any visitors to respect and to share in kind.

Relationships

He employed three women from nearby Brannoch to help maintain his college: Branwyn, Dara, and Shannon. He was fond of all three, and would do anything necessary to protect them if the Lodge came under attack.

History

Donalbain acquired the rundown building of Broadleaf Lodge in the , and proceeded to repair the structure and establish his bardic college. The largest renovation was to transform the top floor of the Lodge's tower into a music hall, which required him to hire master craftsmen from Callidyrr to construct and plaster the room to create ideal acoustics. As magnificent as this music hall was, Donalbain's pride and joy was instead the lore hall on the level below it: this library contained a magnificent collection of knowledge, maps, and magic. However, as the library attracted more and more bards seeking to use it for their research, Donalbain was forced to apply magic mouths to each and every book and scroll that would raise objections if ever a bard "accidentally" tried to leave with it. In the , he created and stocked a hidden bolt hole under the floor of the great hall as a place to hide in cases of emergencies.

By the , his bardic college was bustling.
