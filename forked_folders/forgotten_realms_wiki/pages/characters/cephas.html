Cephas Windsouled

Cephas was a skilled genasi fighter and a descendant of the el Arhapan. He was an earthsoul genasi who later manifested a windsoul in 1479 DR. He was the long-lost heir to Pasha Marod el Arhapan, the leader of Calimport in the mid-15th century DR.

Description

As an earthsoul born in-between the Spellplague and Second Sundering, Cephas had deep, red/bronzed skin with gold szuldar and was heavily muscled as well as had a smooth scalp. Through his piecemeal armor, his muscles were shown. His enormous musculature was close to an adult goliath. Contrary, he was shorter than the goliath who possessed equal height of an adult minotaur above seven feet. From an outsider's view, he was a gladiatorial regalia wearing earthsouled fighter wielding an enormous flail like a child's sling. In his windsoul manifestation, he was slighter in his heavily muscled build with the lack of a broad of shoulders and hip, and possessed silver skin with blue szuldar. He had "hair" as short strings of crystal and physically resembled his father.

Once upon arriving at Almraiven, Cephas obtained loose-fitting clothes: loose cotton pants and an open-necked tunic. In Calimport, he wore a light linen robe, or gold-threaded silk trousers and an elaborately stitched brocade vest. He later wore the simplest clothes covered by his mended scale armor. Before his final talk with Azad the Free, he wore a cloak over his armor and could control his earthsoul-windsoul forms.

Possessions

Cephas wielded a great two-headed flail given to him by Azad the Free. The flail could be attached to the clips on his armored shoulders. He wore assorted pieces of magical scale armor. The strap-adjustable armor. The armor was later repaired in Calimport. He obtained a canteen after leaving Almraiven.

Double Flail

The two-headed flail had a black wooden rod with the width of Cephas's wrist and length of a runner's stride. The rod's ends were capped with blacksmelt bosses. The hanging chains, from the bosses, were sparkling silver, but they appeared too delicate to weigh the spiked steel spheres at the end.

Abilities

As an earthsoul genasi, Cephas innately could earthsock to move the earth with bodily motions when his feet were in contact with the land. Due to his restricted upbringing in Jazeerijah, he could not learn about this ability until leaving gladiatorial captivity and later arriving in Argentor. In 1479 DR, he learned more about being a genasi from Ariella Kulmina in Almraiven. His Second Soul manifestation to a windsoul innately gifted him windwalker, to harness the air and winds around him to lift himself from the earth. Less than tenday later, his contact with the Upper Calimport el Arhapan palace's foundation stone of earth-wind magical energies guided him to control his transition between earthsoul and windsoul. Months later in late 1479 DR, Cephas became a skilled genasi of both manifestations.

Combat

Trained and arena fighting as an earthsoul gladiator for about two decades, the armored Cephas fought by his heavy large flail with inexorable crushing blows, to hack-and-slash his opponents. He also had basic proficiency with other weapons. As a windsoul, he adopted the fluidness of sweeps and swings with his flail to fight.

Performance

Cephas joined the circus in late spring 1479 DR. He took over Tobin's strongman art and began his first performance in Argentor.

Languages

Until he joined the Circus of Wonders, Cephas was illiterate and only spoke Common and some Alzhedo. During his journey to Argentor and then to Calimport, he began learning writing from Corvus and later encountered Primordial. By the time he met the djinn Shahrokh in the Calim Desert, he was proficient in Auran (Old Alzhedo).

History

Childhood

Cephas' mother, Valandra el Shelsper, was an ancestral enslaved earthsoul genasi who manifested into a windsoul. With her windsoul form, she escaped her enslavement and later aided the Janessar. The Pasha Marod el Arhapan fell in love with her in her windsoul form, and she bore him an earthsoul genasi son after the Second Era of Skyfire. Their son's birth and her secret association with rebels had Valandra ran afoul of the Pasha. He immediately made her a slave the day after birth and tricked his champion gladiator, Azad the Free, into killing her in the arena in the . After his mother's death, a tenday after he was born, Cephas was raised by a minotaur nanny, Ninlilah adh Arhapan, the Musar of El Pajabbar. Azad, who became a household slave, read bedtime stories to Cephas. Azad later led a slave revolt and abducted young Cephas from the castle and took him into eastern Tethyr.

Island of the Free

Approximately the , Azad found an earthmote over a canyon near the Omlarandin Mountains and built a small settlement known as the Island of the Free on the mote. Azad's wife, Sheneerah, had Cephas be trained by many instructors, such as Grinta the Pike, for the young man to fight as a gladiator in the arena Azad built on the mote. Cephas used Azad's legendary double-headed flail in his fights, most of which he won. The arena was rigged with a canvas floor so that Cephas was never able to come into contact with the ground and thus was unable to use his earthsoul powers. When not in the arena or training, he was confined to a small chamber. He was forced to wear thick sandals and walk only on wooden walkways so that his feet never touched the earth. For less than two decades, Cephas was forced to endure these hardships and continually tried to escape the earthmote, but none of his attempts were successful. He was named "Cephas" by Azad in Old Alzhedo, the "son of stone".

Circus of Wonders

In the late spring of the , a Kenku assassin named Corvus Nightfeather found out Cephas' lineage through Saradush and Airspur, then concocted a month-long plan to rescue him from the Island of the Free. Corvus' allies the ranger Mattias Farseer and the twin halfling rangers Shan and Cynda participated in the raid to free Cephas. During this month, Cephas fought sixteen times in the earthmote arena with fifteen failed escapes until his fight with the goliath Tobin Tok Tor. Both flew away on Mattias's wyvern Trill. Once he was freed, he agreed to travel with Corvus and the Circus of Wonders he ran. Cephas was given the role of strongman so that the current strongman Tobin could realize his dream of playing a clown. Cephas began learning how to write from Corvus. After sone days, the circus traveled to the earthsoul genasi settlement of Argentor where they gave a performance. Corvus was working as agent for both the Weavepasha of Almraiven and Shahrokh, the djinn lord of Calimport. He informed both his masters that he had Cephas in his possession, whose genasi lineage was identified by his szuldar through Elder Lin, and Shahrokh sent a company of elite minotaur warriors, the El Pajabbar led by Ninlilah, to capture Cephas. They attacked the circus at Argentor, inflicting heavy casualties on the villagers and circus members although they were ultimately defeated and driven off.

Almraiven

In the aftermath of the minotaur attack, Cephas met the windsoul genasi swordmage Ariella Kulmina at Argentor. The circus divided with the revelation of Cephas being the fuse of the previous minotaurs' assault, therefore, the main troupe led by Whitey went to Cormyr, the others alongside Cephas followed Corvus to Almraiven. The kenku opened a gate to Almraiven that Cephas and other key members of Corvus' troop traveled through. The Weavepasha knew of Cephas' heritage and wanted to enspell him with death magic that would kill the Pasha when Cephas came into the Pasha's presence. In Almraiven, the Fluttering Leaf style Ariella "taught" Cephas to manifest as a windsoul. Cynda and Tobin were captured by renegade members of the Firestorm Cabal and transported to Calimport where they were readied for the Djen Arena. Guessing that Tobin and Cynda had been teleported to Calimport, Corvus and crew fled Almraiven, passed by the Shining Sea and traveled through the Plain of Stone Spiders toward the Calim Desert and Calimport within two days. The paranoid vizar of Almraiven, Munaa yr Oma el Jhotos, enlisted the aid of a bound demon known as the Spider that Waits to track Corvus' party. At nighttime, the demon caught up to the group near the Shining Sea and Mattias with Trill remained behind to fight the demon and sacrifice themselves so that Cephas and the group could escape.

Calimport

By sunrise following the demon's night attack, Cephas and friends traveled into the Calim Desert where they were intercepted by Shahrokh and his djinn allies. The group traveled to Calimport in a Shahrokh-conjured sand magical ship. In Calimport five days later, Cephas and Ariella were taken to the Pasha's lavish floating palace and given a large suite of rooms. Upon reuniting with his father, neither father nor son showed much affection for each other. Corvus was stripped of his magic items by Shahrokh and made a slave and Shan was taken to the gladiator stables. Corvus made contact with a resistance group who helped him to locate Tobin, Cynda and Shan. At night, Cephas learned the location of the magical foundation stone that kept the palace airborne. With his special sensitivity to the magical stone's wind and earth energies, the re-transitioned earthsoul Cephas removed it. The palace began to descend rapidly toward the packed arena below. In the ensuing panic, Corvus, Cynda, Shan and Tobin were able to escape the arena and rejoined Cephas and Ariella. Knowing Marod's true thoughts about his wife and son, Cephas performed patricide without his flail. Under Corvus's earlier instigation, Shahrokh left before the palace's fall to find the lost Ritual of Return tome, the Book of Calim.

Truth and Freedom

Months later in the , the circus troupe with Ariella and Lilah traveled back to the Island of the Free to liberate the slaves there. By then, Cephas easily transitioned between earthsoul and windsoul. Before his return, the circus stopped at Argentor for months to heal the mind-drugged Cynda with Elder Lin's healing. Cephas sadly failed to recruit Elder Lin and the other earthsouls to his cause but instead earned their disappointment. In his earthsoul state, he confronted, but did not kill, Azad the Free who explained how he was tricked into killing Cephas' mother. After obtaining the truth, Cephas joined the cause to actively resist slavery in Calimshan and fight the pashas.

Relationships

Marod el Arhapan

Pasha of Calimport since the end of the Second Era of Skyfire and the inherent gamemaster of the arena. Cephas's biological windsoul genasi father and mastermind of his mother's, Valandra el Shelsper, homicide in the . In 1479 DR, he was publicly ended by patricide after revealing his true thoughts about his wife and son.

Valandra el Shelsper

Cephas's mother and former freedom fighter agent of the Janessar faction of Calimport. She was a former enslaved earthsoul genasi hiding as a free manifested windsoul. Her unjust death later prompted Azad the Free to kidnap the toddler Cephas and had him experience a captive life to be a future ally of anti-slavery. Her true homicide induced her son to oppose Marod upon their long family reunion in Calimport as an adult.

Ninlilah the Nanny

One adh Arhapan and Musar of El Pajabbar, the twenty-five minotaur soldiers under the command of Marod el Arhapan and the suppose-to-be personal guards of Cephas. Cephas's minotaur nanny "Lilah" was his spiritual safe haven when he lived as a toddler in Calimport. Even when he reunited as an adult, he still vaguely remembered the security and healing brought by her physical presence. In 1479 DR, she became a one-horned minotaur because of her fight with Tobin in Argentor. With the death of his father, Lilah became Cephas's subordinate because he was the last el Arhapan scion. She appeared traveling with the other circus troupe members months after the fall of the Pasha of the Games.

Azad the Free

Former household slave and gladiator of the Pasha of the Games. Cephas's human bedtime storyteller as a toddler and later kidnapper. Azad took Cephas from Calimport during his rebellion out of care and guilt, leaving the young earthsoul genasi to survive on the Island of the Free as a gladiator. He prevented his wife from scarring Cephas. Secretly, Azad rigged all of the young genasi's matches to allow his survival. Upon his first visit to the Calim Desert, Azad's provided armor pieces were revealed to be magical items because the verbal revelation of the djinni skylord Shahrokh.

Grinta the Pike

A female orc warrior who, one of many but the sole living instructor, taught combat and many things to Cephas when he was learning to be a gladiator. She was freed by him in late-1479 DR after he returned to confront Azad.

Corvus Nightfeather

A three-side spy and the Circus of Wonders Ringmaster in 1479 DR. Cephas's kenku liberator from the gladiatorial arena, and after the Calimport pasha's death in the same year, he became Cephas's old friend comrade.

Mattias Farseer

Cephas's human life-savior in the Plain of Stone Spiders and circus troupe member. He helped the group escape from Almraiven. The ranger and his wyvern Trill died to allow the group to survive the Spider that Waits demon night attack and escape into the Calim Desert.

Tobin Tok Tor

Cephas's first friend in the Circus of Wonders. Tobin was a clanless goliath outcast picked up by Corvus and conducted the strongman performance until late spring 1479 DR because of the addition of Cephas. As a result, Cephas took over the strongman position and Tobin pursued his dream to be a clown. His kidnapping with Cynda from Almraiven to Calimport prompted Cephas and his group to rescue the pair, leading to the fall of the Calimport pasha.

Argentor Earthsouls

Cephas's friends and guides to understanding his abilities as an earthsoul genasi. They were mainly the children of the Argentor village leader Elder Lin: her eldest son Flek, young daughters Sonnett and Marashan. In the night raid by the El Pajabbar minotaurs during Cephas's first circus performance, Flek died protecting Cephas and Marashan.

Ariella Kulmina

An Akanûl windsoul genasi. Cephas's first in love and in intercourse. They became a couple since Cephas's first visit to Almraiven. The couple survived the fall of the Calimport pasha in 1479 DR. She later joined Cephas on his journey to freeing slaves through the Circus of Wonders months after.
