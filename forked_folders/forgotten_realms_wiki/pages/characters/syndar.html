Syndar was a young half-elven girl living in a logging camp of the Savage Frontier in the mid-to-late 14 century DR.

Description

Syndar was as beautiful as elves were.

Personality

She had her father's temper.

Abilities

She had been learning magic from her mother, and had displayed the ability to cast dancing lights.

Relationships

Syndar was friends with three local boys called Kirsk, Craster, and McDodd. They were also friends with Nollo Rellock, a caravaneer's son that came to the camp every year. Syndar's mother was an elf, and her father was a human.

History

In the , Nollo Rellock was a guest at a treehouse with four local kids at a logging camp somewhere in the Savage Frontier. The kids were familiar with his father's itinerant shows, and remembered particular exhibits from two years prior, though they hadn't forgotten he'd exaggerated on the authenticity of some of them. One of them said he'd done that thrice in three years, suggesting that was as long as they'd known one another, though he also described a fourth exhibit. During the conversation, Nollo let slip that he'd seen a dragon, and in his excitement, accidentally over-shared that his father had a baby dragon caged in the barn where he kept his wagons. The other kids' disbelief only egged him on.

Ultimately, Kirsk, one of the fellow kids, offered to pay if he showed them the dragon and if it really was a dragon. Nollo agreed to those provisions, and led them to the barn, where he pointed out the brass dragon, and offered to let them feed it for an extra copper piece. McDodd, the meanest, played keep-away with the fish rather than feeding it to the dragon—he got a gout of dragonfire burning his hair for that. In the commotion, a blue dragon broke into the barn and shattered a wagon, burying Nollo under its rubble. Fortunately for him, and for McDodd and Craster, who were too stunned to react to much, Kirsk took the wyrmling and fled out of the barn alongside Syndar. The blue was only interested in slaying the brass dragon.

Kirsk and Syndar ran into a sawmill to try and hide from the blue dragon, taking the brass dragon with them. There, Kirsk came up with a plan to distract the dragon using Syndar's magic, a wood-cutting axe, and a pile of lashed logs; they used Syndar's dancing lights to harass the dragon just long enough for Kirsk to swing down an axe on its tail. That cracked one of its scales, but it was enough for the dragon to respond by tail-slapping Kirsk out of the way. This upset a pile of logs, which rolled into it. This in turn distracted it long enough for another, larger brass dragon to turn up and the blue dragon fled. Before it left with its spawn, the other brass dragon gave them a large, shield-like scale.
