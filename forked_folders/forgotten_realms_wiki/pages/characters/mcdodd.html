McDodd was a young boy living in a logging camp on the Savage Frontier during the mid-to-late 14 century DR.

Description

He was pretty big, and older than Nollo Rellock, who was also a kid.

Personality

A braggart, McDodd often tried to be intimidating, though he wasn't very good at it.

Relationships

McDodd was the friend of locals Kirsk, Syndar, and Craster. They were friends with Nollo Rellock, a boy who came every year when his father's caravan passed by.

History

In the , Nollo Rellock was a guest at a treehouse with four local kids at a logging camp somewhere in the Savage Frontier. The kids were familiar with his father's itinerant shows, and remembered particular exhibits for two years prior, though they hadn't forgotten he'd exaggerated on the authenticity of some of them; one of them said he'd done that thrice in three years, strongly implying that was as long as they'd met, though he also described a fourth exhibit. During the conversation, Nollo let slip that he'd seen a dragon, and in his excitement, accidentally over-shared that his father had a baby dragon caged in the barn where he kept his wagons. The other kids' disbelief only egged him on.

Ultimately, Kirsk, one of the fellow kids, offered to pay if he showed them the dragon and it was in fact a dragon. Nollo agreed to those provisions, and led them to the barn, where he pointed out the brass dragon, and offered them to feed it for an extra copper. McDodd, the meanest, played keep-away with the fish rather than feeding it to the dragon; it got a gout of dragonfire burning his hair for that. In the conmotion, a blue dragon broke into the barn, and shattered a wagon, burying Nollo under its rubble. Fortunately for him, and for McDodd and Craster, who were too out of balance to react to much, Kirsk took the wyrmling and fled out of the barn alongside Syndar; it was only interested in slaying the brass dragon.

The chase between the dragons extended from that barn to a wooden mill. In the aftermath, Beccard Rellock found his son Nollo under some collapsed wooden planks; McDodd was close by, only getting up at around the same time, with help.
