King Dagdar was ruler of the Kingdom of Moray on the island of Moray in the Moonshae Isles during the early-to-mid–14 century DR.

Description

He had a large, bear-like build. His arms were as thick as the legs of other men and were covered in thick, black hair. His black hair was worn long around his shoulders and down his back while his face was concealed beneath a huge, black beard. He spoke in a booming voice.

Personality

Dagdar was crude and boastful. He was incredibly proud of his people and his kingdom, and loved to spin enthralling tales about the places in his realm. He dismissed most of the threats to his realms, including orcs and trolls, as mere nuisances.

He enjoyed hunting, and kept a pack of well-trained moorhounds for his hunting trips.

Possessions

When he embarked on a hunting expedition, he traveled with a number of hunters, houndmasters, and enough retainers to fill out six chariots.

Relationships

His son and heir was Dynnegall. It was speculated that he might have been descended from one of the fallen sons of the maiden of Highpeak.

He hated the Northmen of the northern Moonshaes. He also had little respect for the other Ffolk realms of Callidyrr, Corwell, and Snowdown, whom he saw as too soft. That said, he was grateful to King Bryon Kendrick of Corwell for helping to repel a Northmen invasion in the 1320s DR, although he was hesitant to admit this aloud.

History

Late in his reign, he hosted the sage Elminster in his realm and sought to show him a good time. He invited the sage to join him on a hunting expedition, which took them south of Dynnegall to hear the wailing of the maiden of Highpeak.

In the , he led a rare assault against the Northmen by attacking the village of Trondhelm in Oman. He died during this campaign and was later succeeded by his son as king.
