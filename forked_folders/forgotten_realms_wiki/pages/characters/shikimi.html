History

Shikimi was raised in a hidden village of elves located somewhere in Wa. On behalf of the village, her family's mission was to spy on the human world outside. To this end, Shikimi went to Kozakura, where she operated as a spy serving a human samurai family. In time, she felt a secret yet pure love for the head of the family.

But the machinations of followers of Asmodeus led to the family being destroyed by a rival samurai family. Shikimi could only save the lord's son, Tatsuwaka-maru, from being killed. The pair fled, along with Oni-guma, a warrior who also served the young lord, but were pursued by the Asmodeus-worshipers. They were aided by Kugetsu and Hakuroku-bou, a pair of mystics who'd come seeking to thwart the followers of Asmodeus.

They escaped into the mountains, but passed through a teleportation circle that unexpectedly spirited them all to the far side of Faerûn, to the Sword Coast.

Circa 1491 DR, the five companions arrived in the town of Phandalin, where they may have become caught up in stopping the oni Jadou-maru from recovering the Glove of Oni Claw at the Lionshield Coster.

Relationships

Shikimi was bound to protect the young lord Tatsuwaka-maru, the son of the man she loved.

Description

At age 145, she stood 152 centimeters (5 feet) tall and weighed 42 kilograms (93 pounds).

Personality

Shikimi was loyal to her friends above all, and did not care so much for ideals or strangers.

She blamed herself for a mistake that led to the death of her lord, and vowed to never repeat it. Thus, she was always cautious, sizing up a place upon entering and laying plans in case things went bad.

When Shikimi told a lie, she had a tell that could reveal her.

Possessions

Shikimi was armed with a short sword, two daggers, and a short bow, with twenty arrows in her quiver. She wore leather armor but also had ninja attire. She was also equipped with thieves' tools, a backpack, belt pouch, and a set of adventuring and burglary gear, including a bag of ball bearings, of string, of hempen rope, five candles, a crowbar, a bell, a hammer and ten pitons, a hooded lantern with two flasks of oil and a tinderbox, rations for five days, and a waterskin.
