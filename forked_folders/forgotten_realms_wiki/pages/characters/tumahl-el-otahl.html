Tumahl el Otahl, also known as the Rogue Son of El Otahl, was a legendary adventurer and pirate of the city of Calimport, Calimshan, late in its Eighth Age

History

He was born and lived in an area of Calimport that would be named for him: Otahl Sabban, also called Tumahl's Sabban, in what was later the Emerald Ward.

In his short life, Tumahl el Otahl was known to be a daring and always-laughing rogue who often fought, foiled, and frustrated the Nallojal navy and the Syl-pasha of Calimshan. His adventures would be told and retold to children in their nurseries and to adults in their taverns into the late 14 century DR.

As of 1370 DR, a large white marble statue of Tumahl stood in the center of the biggest bazaar in his namesake sabban, pointing a scimitar to the southwest, either toward the Pasha's Sabban or the Armada Ward docks as a challenge to the two figures with which Tumahl had most contended.

Tumahl's symbol was a slender dagger with no guard and a hole in the base and this, too, became the mark of Otahl Sabban.
