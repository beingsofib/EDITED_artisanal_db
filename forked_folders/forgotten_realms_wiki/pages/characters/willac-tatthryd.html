Willac Tatthryd was an adventuring human priest from Hillsfar whose death in the mid-to-late 14 century DR, led to a reincarnation as an ogre woman.

Description

At the age of twenty-three winters, Willac in her ogre's form was tall, reaching , weighting . She had black hair that the ogress wore in three long braids, dark brown skin, and eyes of the same shade of brown.

Personality

She was a friendly and affectionate individual despite her terrifying, brutish appearance of an ogre. By nature, she did not like violence and often was a voice of reason and kindness when adventuring in a group, even healing her enemies when the battle was over. Willac was quite fond of her armor, polishing the plate mail and shield almost daily.

Abilities

As both a fighter and a priest, Willac was trained in using footman's flail's, footman's maces, horseman's flails, and morning stars. She was also knowledgeable in the topics of healing and religion and was good at horseback-riding and animal handling. As a cleric, she had the following prayers ready on a daily basis: cure light wounds, command, light, detect magic, and hold person. However, in her ogreish body, Willac lost the ability to turn undead. Subsequently, her husband discovered that the true reason for losing that ability was a curse that the two sought adventurers' help in lifting.

Possessions

Her typical equipment included a silver holy symbol and a potion of mammal control. She wore a set of enchanted plate mail of etherealness and carried a small silver shield. As for weapons, Willac carried a footman's mace and a horseman's flail.

Relationships

Willac was truly in love with her human husband, and she appreciated his intelligence, seeking his advice and counsel on all topics.

History

Willac Tatthryd was born a human boy to a poor single farmer father in the city of Hillsfar, in the Moonsea region of Faerûn. Early in life, Willac found religion and realized disdain for a commoner's existence, becoming an adventuring priest when coming of age. At the age of twenty-one, Willac was mauled to death by a bear on the way to the kingdom of Cormyr. The dead priest's temple returned the adventurer to live via the reincarnation spell – the result was unexpected. Willac Tatthryd returned as an ogress. Learning to use the natural benefits of her new body, Willac learned the crude art of being a fighter, and subsequently, she got used to the bulky size, monstrous strength, and her female body, sex, and gender. However, her human peers found being around an ogre to be unnerving, and Willac returned to regular traveling and adventuring when she felt out of place at the temple.

Willac found many adventuring troupes to join, offering her muscles and healing magic. One notable adventure was when her band came across a gang of ogres. The entire group of adventurers was captured, including Willac, and was taken to the ogres' lair to be the main course for dinner. When the ogres' chieftain laid an eye on Willac, he immediately fell in love with the brutish beauty. The ogress managed to get the chieftain drunk by using her feminine woes, and the group fled captivity. Willac got away with the chieftain's plate mail of etherealness.

By the age of twenty-three, she married a fellow adventurer – the human mage Bregubrod Dryctcyni. The wizard courted the ogress for several months until proclaiming his undying love. Willac was not used to a man's affections and this much attention. But despite it all, she developed feelings for the little man, and the two tied the knot.
