Silvenestri Silver was a gnome treasure hunter of Sembia in the mid-to-late 14 century DR.

Personality

Silver took pride in his job hunting for treasure.

Abilities

He could employ illusions in some manner; the dwarf Badger Bates called him an illusionist and recalled an occasion where Silver claimed to cast an illusion. Silver himself claimed lesser gnomes couldn't have done what he did, strongly implying he was a mage of sorts.

Possessions

He had an embroidered hankie.

Activities

Silvenestri Silver was a treasure-stealer who took dragons' hoards, sometimes as part of a team. He spent winters in smaller towns, away from the cities; he was especially fond of the Dragon Defeated tavern in Halfknot.

Relationships

Silver often antagonized his companions at work, who believed they'd been cheated of their share. He argued about dragons almost every night with two fellow tavern-goers at the Dragon Defeated: Wyrmbait Nix and Badger Bates.

History

Silvenestri Silver often told and retold a tale about a black dragon with a phobia for snakes, which strongly implied the entirety of his hoard-stealing business had consisted entirely of that one time.

In the mid-winter of the , the Dragon Defeated sign got repainted; the owner, Varney, had it redrawn to resemble Silver and two other regulars: Badger Bates and Wyrmbait Nix. They soon became local celebrities, "dragon experts", taking part in a contest every mid-tenday where they judged tales of dragons.

During the last such contest held, in late winter or early spring of that year, Silvenestri claimed green dragons were often cross-eyed and dull-witted in response to a tale he was judging. That provoked a response from the painter of the sign, Petra, who challenged him on that, and produced a huge number of paintings of such green dragons, all of which she claimed to have painted from life. He corrected himself as speaking about white dragons, to which Petra responded by revealing her paintings of white dragons.

Badger Bates dismissed her paintings for not containing any pictures of Malaeragoth. When she claimed to have seen Malaeragoth more recently than he did, Silver required her to show proof. She painted Malaeragoth on a wall of the tavern, but refused to draw his eyes, instead simply describing them. Bates refused to pay up unless the eyes were drawn.

Silver egged on Petra, siding with the dwarf just to make trouble. When her picture of Malaeragoth came to life, however, he had the good sense and reflexes to jump out the window and run for the edge of town; he returned to find Badger Bates flattened.

He immediately took the dwarf's wallet and scale.
