Cireka was a githyanki General of the Guard of the First City Tu'narath.

Personality

Cireka believed the Githyanki were the superior race, more perfect species with greater mental and physical strength. She stated that no method was too extreme to achieve her people's goal of expansion and order. The githyanki general was ready to sacrifice herself for the Lich Queen, Vlaakith, believing this act would enrich her queen and her entire people.

History

Cireka was a daughter of Na'kulith and a High Clanswoman of the Kith clan. She also claimed to be a daughter of Vlaakith. During her life he became General of the Guard of the First City Tu'narath, Lord of the Qual'kith, and Conqueror of the Lingering Lands.

At some point, she came into the possession of the Silver Sword of Gith.

Cireka planned to conquer Faerûn to impose a new "glorious order". She believed this would ultimately bring peace to the planet, but through the means of occupation, enslavement, and destruction of existing societies.

The Slaad Lord Ygorl wished to take the Silver Sword from her, leading to a war between the two. In 1270s DR, Khelben Arunsun found them fighting in Rawlinswood and realized that if either one defeated the other, the victor's army would proceed to destroy all of Faerûn. To prevent either from winning, Khelben trapped both of them inside an enchanted cell of a demon stone, where the two were condemned to fight each other for eternity. Later, the demon stone was hidden in the Gemspark Mine.

Around 1373 DR, after a century of imprisonment, Cireka and Ygorl were released as a result of Ygorl's secret manipulations and shaping the future of the three kids, Illius, Rhannek, and Zhai, who later became the First Barons of Vaasa. After a short continuation of their unfinished encounter, the two fled the mine to gather their forces.

The githyanki general used a portal at Cedarleaf, a village close to the mine, to begin bringing her army through. During their attack, Ygorl teleported his slaad army to Cedarleaf. The village and its defenders were saved by the same trio of adventurers, who had become spectators to Cireka's and Ygorl's awakening.

Cireka made her way to an ancient githyanki stronghold in Chult.

She was eventually slain by the red dragon, Caminus.
