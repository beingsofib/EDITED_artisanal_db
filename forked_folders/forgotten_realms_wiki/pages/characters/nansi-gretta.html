Nansi Gretta was a writer and editor, as well as the proprietress of The Bibliophile, a humble bookstore located in the Lower City of Baldur's Gate in the late 15 century DR.

Description

Nansi was a human woman with a tan skin tone, gray eyes, and short, wavy brown hair.

Possessions

Nansi's shop stood just east of Beehive Goods and Wares, a general store in the Lower City. Her collection of books and scrolls included a large number of fiction and non-fiction works, including such smutty chapbooks as Yoshimo is Willing and One Night in Nashkel, and numerous copies of Lord Enver Gortash's memoirs. Notably, she offered magic scrolls of the revivify spell to those who could afford such a purchase.

Relationships

Nansi Gretta was a member of Morgan's Book Brigade book club. It was implied by Morgan's letters addressed to the shopkeep that Nansi harbored some affection or possibly feelings for the fellow Book Brigader Kepli.

History

During the crisis that consumed the city of Baldur's Gate in the , Nansi Gretta made a "generous" donation of sixteen copies of Lord Gortash's memoirs to the needy refugees who sought shelter in the city and outside of the city walls.

By that year, Nansi had spent some time working on and completed editing a messy manuscript of an adventure story. The draft was stored in her bedroom, and the book was marked up with countless comments in red ink, including such notes as "I don't buy it," "doesn't ring true," "get rid of whatever this is," "seriously?" etc.
