Fahid yn Nur al Suhal was a moneylender of Teshburl in Calimshan in the mid-to-late 14 century DR.

Relationships

Fahid was married to Nurih yr Catahra al Suhal, who was seen as shrewish.

He had a brother who ran his moneylender business before him.

History

Fahid inherited his moneylending company from his deceased brother, but he had no head for business. In contrast, Nurih very much did and collected several of her late brother-in-law's outstanding debts before taking the business over and making them very wealthy.

Activities

By 1370 DR, Fahid was merely involved in name only—Nurih was effectively in charge of the whole moneylending business and had even joined the Rundeen.

Personality

He was considered cowardly.
