Antarn the Sage was a sage from Athkatla, Amn, during the 14 century DR. He was best known for The High History of Faerûnian Archmages Mighty.

It is written by some that Elminster failed the test, and was destroyed by Mystra and replaced by another who took the name and face of the last Aumar of Athalantar. Others say Elminster went mad, and is mad yet. Still others affirm that he passed the test, and turned away from the divinity that might have been his, and so became the wise but twisted thing he is today... Antarn the Sage, in ''The High History of Faerûnian Archmages Mighty

Description

A snappy dresser, the man was short and slender, with a beard he dyed chestnut, to retain its natural color.

Personality

A lifelong loner who'd devoted himself to study and scrivening, he responded to danger with fatalism. Only book burning would enrage him, but rather than being shaken, he'd plot doom inwardly. The man also liked highcoin ladies-of-the-evening, and board games as foreplay.

Abilities

He was a sage in matters historical, local, royal, and arcane, whose knowledge of actual spells was greater than he was willing to admit to.

Possessions

He was habitually protected by an ironguard ring and a ring of shooting stars. Almost all of his time he spent in a tower on the east side of the city.

Activities

Antarn copied books for numerous Athkatlan thieves, who also supplied him with rare examples. On the occasions he went out of his tower, he bought the provender with which he cooked for his two live-in assistants. He was most often seen and identified when the three of them went to Taerandamlur's feasthouse for early dinners; this was the way the sage normally took clients. He also reported to clients through these same dinners.

More rarely, he strolled out to see the sights, and to engage the services of high-coin ladies-of-the-evening.

Relationships

He was attended by Hyelan Zorandel and Drethtar Wildtree, two retired adventuring warriors who served as his assistants, go-betweens, trade agents, and errand-runners. They were also his only friends. Through them, he had deals with other book-thieves of the city.

History

Though his best-known work was The High History of Faerûnian Archmages Mighty, published in the , and touching strongly on the subject of Elminster Aumar. This work was notable for it being a known record of certain less-known events in Elminster's life, such as his time in the laying of the Mythal of Myth Drannor, and him having been offered divinity in the .

Antarn was also the author of Dursunder Street: A Local History, and Huntsilver Errant: A Life of Ondamarr Huntsilver.

By , he had become an aging recluse.
