History

Oni-guma's clan dwelt in the mountains of Kozakura and were reputed to be descended from demons or oni. Generations past, they fought a great battle against an army commanded by a human general, then met to negotiate a truce. But at a banquet, their sake was poisoned, leading to their defeat.

A survivor of this treachery, Oni-guma prowled the capital city, seeking to slay those who warriors who'd profited off the battle. But when he encountered the young nobleman's son Tatsuwaka-maru, they dueled and he was surprisingly defeated. Mystified, Oni-guma began to serve the Young Lord, supporting him both publicly and behind-the-scenes.

But the machinations of followers of Asmodeus led to Tatsuwaka-maru's family being destroyed by a rival samurai family. Only Tatsuwaka-maru survived, saved by his father's spymaster Shikimi, and they fled with Oni-guma, but they were pursued by the Asmodeus-worshipers. They were aided by Kugetsu and Hakuroku-bou, a pair of mystics who'd come seeking to thwart the followers of Asmodeus.

They escaped into the mountains, but passed through a teleportation circle that unexpectedly spirited them all to the far side of Faerûn, to the Sword Coast.

Circa 1491 DR, the five companions arrived in the town of Phandalin, where they may have become caught up in stopping the oni Jadou-maru from recovering the Glove of Oni Claw at the Lionshield Coster.

Relationships

Oni-guma served the young lord Tatsuwaka-maru, whilst trying to understand how he'd been defeated.

Description

At age 24, he stood 195 centimeters (6.4 feet) tall and weighed 121 kilograms (267 pounds).

Personality

Oni-guma believed in the power of might and that the strong would rule. He responded to any challenge with force. Practically minded, he did not value wealth or fine manners, as things would not keep one alive.

As the only survivor of his tribe, he was driven to make sure they lived on in legend.

He had a habit of picking things up and accidentally breaking them.

Oni-guma revered a god or spirit known as the Great Bear.

Possessions

Oni-guma wielded a glaive or a longsword, along with four javelins. He wore no armor, only simple traveler's clothes. He also had a belt pouch with 10 gp and a backpack with a set of exploration gear including a bedroll, mess kit, tinder box, ten torches, rations for a tenday, a waterskin, and of hempen rope. He also had a staff, tools for trapping animals, and a bear skin.
