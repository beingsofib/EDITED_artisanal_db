Sagrais Bigelow was a halfling thief and one of the retired adventurers inhabiting the Nellie Thursday Home for Experienced Adventurers sometime in the mid-to-late 14 century DR.

Description

Sagrais had become quite rotund in his old age, reaching . He stood tall and his hair, once brown, had become entirely white and fled to the back of his head leaving only a sharp widow's peak.

Personality

He would insist he was as capable a thief in his 150[ year as he was when he was 25, but the extra weight he gained after retiring made him much less dexterous than he thought. His hearing was also slowly slipping, and oftentimes people would need to talk quite loudly to make themselves understood by him.

Abilities

Sagrais was fluent in several languages: Common, Thieves' cant, Elvish, Dwarvish, Gnomish and Orcish. As a rogue, he was proficient in handling slings, daggers, knives, and darts. He was also knowledgeable in the arts of herbalism, rope use, and fire building, and was a capable pony rider.

Possessions

He still owned a longtooth +2 dagger and a pair of bracers of defense, and often wore his ring of protection +2. He also owned a very useful pouch of accessibility and an enchanted dinner plate that would summon a meat-and-potatoes-type dinner whenever it was warmed over an open fire up to four times a day. When forced to go adventuring, he would carry a set of five scroll with the spells invisibility, hold person, wall of ice, mending, and magic missile.

Relationships

His friend of many long years, Henry the Shadow Master, also resided at the Nellie Thursday Home for Experienced Adventurers and they would often sneak out to smoke their favorite pipeweed and have a drink without Henry's wife discovering anything.

History

In his younger days, Sagrais worked with a human illusionist named Henry, who performed spells and tricks as a distraction while Sagrais emptied the pockets of the audience.

At the age of one hundred and fifty years, Sagrais accompanied several other geriatric adventurers, including his good friend Henry, on a quest to find Wemic the Wise's old missing roommate. Calling themselves the Company of the Swan they would be dragged across several worlds, the jungles of Quorsit; and the demi-plane of Kansas, where Sagrais would face Hedgeheart a dwarven thief in a duel.
