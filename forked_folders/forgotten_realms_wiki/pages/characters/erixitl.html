Erixitl was a young Maztican priestess of Qotal and the wife of the former Golden Legion officer Halloran in the second part of the 14 century DR.

Description

Erixitl was described as a beautiful woman with brown skin and long black hair.

Equipment

Erix wore a powerful pluma amulet made by her father that protected her from most magical attacks. She also wore the Cloak of One Plume, which was a sign of her favor with Qotal.

History

Early Life

Erixitl came from Palul a small settlement near Nexal. Her father Lotil was a worshipper of Qotal and a maker of pluma items. Her brother Shatil, however, turned to worship of Zaltec and became a cleric in his service.

At the age of 10, Erixitl was taken captive and held as a slave in the city of Kultaka. After being attacked by the son of her owner, Erixitl was sold to Kachin, a cleric of the god Qotal in Ulatos. Kachin freed Erixitl and told her she was to become a priestess.

Erixitl was not fully aware at the time that she had been chosen by Qotal to serve a purpose in his plan to return to Maztica and thwart the machinations of his divine brother Zaltec. The Ancient Ones, drow servants of Zaltec, had been seeking her life since they discovered that her existence threatened their plans to dominate Maztica. Qotal had in fact arranged for Erixitl to be captured by slavers when she was a child, because otherwise Zaltec's servants in Nexal were going to kill her when they found her. Qotal had similarly arranged for Erixitl to go from Kultaka to Ulatos, again to prevent Zaltec's servants from taking and killing her.

When the Ancient Ones discovered that she was in Ulatos, they again sought to destroy her there.

Ulatos to Nexal

Erix was kidnapped by a priest of Zaltec and taken to be sacrificed at a pyramid on top of the Twin Visages. Erixitl escaped their captivity. As she was escaping from them, the Golden Legion from Amn had just arrived on its historic voyage that discovered Maztica in 1361 DR, and they had arrived very close to Twin Visages.

Qotal, using his couatll servant Chitikas intervened once again, and Chitikas used his magic to make the Zaltecan priest to think that Martine, the daughter of Helmite Bishou Domincus, who had arrived with the voyage, was in fact Erixitl. The priest took Martine and sacrificed her instead of Erixitl. This led to war between the Golden Legion and Ulatos, at the end of which the Legion conquered the city and created Helmsport.

Chitikas appeared to Erixitl and told her about how Qotal had chosen her. She had many questions and troubles with the divine plan that she found herself in.

Erixitl was nearly killed during the battle between the Amnians and Payit of Ulatos but the Legionnaire Halloran rescued her. Alvarro a cavalry officer in the Legion had tried to run her down, but Halloran attacked him and knocked him out

Halloran had deserted the Legion shortly before the battle and the two of them together escaped into the jungle. They were attacked by hell hounds sent by the Ancient Ones and an Invisible stalker sent by the Legion, but prevailed both times. One of the Ancient Ones came to find them and fought Halloran, but Halloran slew him. It was then that Halloran discovered that he was a drow; a creature known well in Faerûn but largely unknown in Maztica. The Ancient Ones had kept their true identities hidden from the people of Maztica.

Nexal

Erixitl and Halloran journeyed westwards to Nexal. Erixitl had visions of the destruction of Nexal and she wanted to do everything in her power to prevent this from happening. Erix and Halloran had a disagreement and Erix traveled back to her home village of Palul. She arrived just before the Golden Legion and her brother Shatil convinced her to serve as a translator between the Nexalans and the men of the Legion, suggesting that she could help Nexal to avoid the fate of Ulatos. For that purpose, they welcomed the Legion into Palul and presented them with a feast, while using Erixitl to discuss between the parties. In actuality and unknown to Erixitl, the Nexalans had planned to ambush the Legion in Palul while the Legion's guard was down. However, the Legion figured out the ambush and attacked first, leading to a massacre in Palul.

Alvarro the cavalry officer who had tried to kill Erxitl in the battle at Ulatos did not initially recognize her when she served as a translator, but later realized who she was. He sought her out during the battle and found her. He forced her to the ground to rape her while one of his men kept watch nearby. Halloran arrived and fought him. Alvarro escaped back to the Legion and Halloran escaped with Erixitl.

Hal was seriously injured in the fight and Erix nursed him back to health. Hal and Erix were married by her father and he gave them both powerful feathermagic bracers as a wedding gift. Afterwards, the couple journeyed back to Nexal in a desperate attempt to prevent a war between the Golden Legion and Nexal.

Following the massacre at Palul, Naltecona the ruler of Nexal, surrendered to the Legion, which entered the city triumphant. Later, the Legion took him as a hostage to ensure that the people of Nexal remained under control. Erixitl continued to have visions of the destruction of Nexal and saw that it was going to come about when Naltecona was killed on a particular night. She and Halloran, working with Poshtli, the nephew of Naltecona, attempted to rescue him from the Legion to prevent this vision from coming true. They failed in their attempt and Erixitl was captured by the Legion. She was questioned by the Legion's commander Cordell and kept in a cell in the Palace of Axalt that the Legion had taken over within Nexal.

Darien the Legion's mage, who was secretly one of the Ancient Ones knew that Erixitl needed to die in order for Zaltec's plans to come to fruition. She arranged for Alvarro to go into Erixitl's cell to kill her. Erixitl, however, tricked Alvarro into thinking that she would lie with him and offered him octal that she poisoned with a potion. He drank it and died, and she escaped from her cell.

She met Halloran, Poshtli and her brother Shatil, who had come to the palace for her.

Night of Wailing

A huge mob of over a thousand Nexalan warriors had gathered in the plaza around Axalt's palace and Cordell brought Naltecona to the roof of the palace to get him to speak to the crowd in order to calm them down. Just as Naltecona was speaking to them, however, Darien then made her move and assassinated Naltecona within sight of the amassed mob. Erixitl, Halloran, Poshtli and Shatil then came to the roof of the palace at the same time and Darien saw her still alive, to her great shock, realizing that this meant that their plans would come to ruin, since the prophesy that they had been given told them that everything would fail if Qotal's chosen was not killed before Naltecona's death.

Darien escaped and a huge battle began the Legion and its allies, and the mass of Nexalan warriors. Halloran seeing Darien's betrayal, then connected the dots and realized that Darien was herself a drow and a member of the Ancient Ones. Chitikas arrived and teleported the four of them around the rooftop to chase after Darien and some of the Ancients Ones, but Darien succeeded in getting away. With the revelation that the Ancient Ones were the ones behind these events and Naltecona's death, they then went to the Highcave in Mount Zatal to confront them. They found them in the cave and fought them. When The Ancestor, the leader of the Ancient Ones, was about to strike Erixitl down, Chitikas lunged at him and plunged them both together into the Darkfyre, a powerful magical focus that the Ancient Ones held in Mount Zatal. When the pair fell in, the Darkfyre caused Mount Zatal to erupt in a massive explosion that brought destruction upon the city below. The Darkfyre corrupted the fire and ashes to cause horrific magical transformations to take place among the drow and human zealots of Zaltec. Erixitl and Halloran were protected in the midst of the explosion by Qotal's pluma magic.

They then joined together with the mass of refugees fleeing the city and headed southwards into the House of Tezca

Rebirth of Qotal

Erixitl became the leader of the mass of refugees heading south and their way was prepared for them by Qotal, who made oases to appear in the desert. At this point, she had also become pregnant by Halloran.

Poshtli, whom Qotal had transformed into a giant eagle during the destruction, flew over them and directed them. The zealots of Zaltec who were transformed into monsters during the Night of Wailing, formed an army and followed after them. At one point Poshtli began flying in a direction away from the oases that the refugees were following, and Erixitl decided that she needed to follow him. Halloran, her father, Coton the patriarch of Qotal and a Nexalan youth went with her, while the refugees continued south. A mob of trolls also broke off from the army of monsters to follow them. They followed Poshtli to Tewahca, the 'city of the gods', which was a large abandoned city in the midst of the desert.

They arrived there and did a rite that would have allowed Qotal to return back to Maztica. However, just as Qotal's avatar was re-entering the Prime Material plane, Zaltec's avater then appeared. Zaltec fought Qotal before he could fully emerge and forced Qotal to retreat back out of the Prime once again. Erixitl and the others fought the trolls and escaped from the city.

They then went on a long overland journey to Twin Visages, knowing that this was an alternative site where Qotal could return. Zaltec's avatar and the monster army following him, also came soon after. Darien and the other Ancient Ones, who had been transformed into driders had also arrived. Darien tried to stop Erixitl by using magic that made her sick, but nevertheless they still succeeded in performing the Rite that allowed for Qotal's avatar to return. Erixitl at the same time, gave birth to her child. Qotal's avatar returned fully in the flesh and then fought Zaltec's avatar near Twin Visages. However, Coton, then betrayed his god and sacrificing himself, caused the avatars of both gods to be banished from the Prime.

Halloran, Erixitl and their baby then went to the city of Tukan to live there.
