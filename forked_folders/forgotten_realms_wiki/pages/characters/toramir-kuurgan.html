Captain Toramir Kuurgan was a former Tethyrian naval officer and later leader of the Sea Reavers pirate fleet of the Nelanther Isles in the mid-to-late 14 century DR.

History

Toramir Kuurgan was once a commander of Tethyr's royal navy. But amidst the turbulent Tethyrian Interregnum after 1347 DR, he felt betrayed by the nobility for their selfishness and therefore betrayed his fallen kingdom. Circa 1350 DR, Toramir reassembled his flotilla with four ships and 200 crewmembers and officers loyal to him, and permitted safe passage back to the Dragon's Neck peninsula for any who refused and remained allegiant to the navy—only four departed. He joined his newly established fleet with the survivors of the old Sea Reavers and operated them as pirates for the next two decades. In time, their fleet grew to five vessels.

The Sea Reavers were amongst those Nelantheran pirate groups who held sway over the leaders of Tulmene, a small shipbuilding town on the Dragon's Neck. For some twenty years, they made the place a secret safe harbor for their kind. Two of the Sea Reavers' ships were damaged in the fighting, and when they were forced to enter the Race and its intense winds and currents, they were sunk. Incensed by the loss of ships and crewmen and corrupted by two decades of crime, Toramir had no desire to rejoin in the reformation of Tethyr and instead swore vengeance against the new queen, even to see her dead.

The Sea Reavers encountered difficulties supplying and maintaining their remaining ships and so in the late 1360s DR Toramir made a deal to work for the Rundeen mercantile consortium.

But with Tethyr's restoration in the , and a rebuilt strong and capable navy, pirates like the Sea Reavers found its shipping too hard a target and so turned to prey upon that of Amn. In late Eleint, the Sea Reavers attacked and looted four ships of a seven-strong fleet belonging to the Vemmil family of Amn, which were returning from a voyage to Zakhara. The other three made it to Murann. Some time the following year, under the auspices of the Rundeen, Toramir made an alliance with the ogre mage Sythillis of the Sythillisian Empire to blockade Amnian ports like Murann to prevent treasure ships fleeing, in exchange for a cut of that treasure, as well as stop the Amnians retaking the city. They hoped to acquire ships and a new safe port on the Sword Coast. By 1372 DR, the Rundeen and their pet Nelanther pirates like Toramir were using Murann as a safe haven and defending it against naval counter-attack.

Personality

A formerly lawful officer, Toramir grew to hate his former kingdom and was driven to madness and paranoia by a twenty-year-long of crime.
