Jafar el Uabar was a nirdey, or a chief collector, for the Rundeen mercantile organization in Volothamp, Calimshan, in the late 14 century DR.

Description

He was very thin and had elongated facial features that made him appear more like a caricature of a man. He had black hair that he kept carefully oiled.

Relationships

Jafar was the nephew of Tulmon el Uabar, a wealthy pearl diver of Volothamp.

History

Around 1370 DR, Jafar and his uncle had become estranged after a disagreement over the family business, so Jafar committed himself to the Rundeen in order to maintain his comfortable lifestyle and wealth.

Activities

He acted as mediator between merchants and also practiced a little smuggling. In this capacity, he often worked with Pasha Syan al Derak of the Knights of the Shield.

Jafar despised the "Gem Caleph" Adnan el Huzal, leader of the Pearl Diver's Guild of Volothamp, and would do anything to have him ejected from his position. He saw this as a means of reconciling with his uncle and even started considering the use of assassins, supplied by the Rundeen, in order to accomplish the goal.
