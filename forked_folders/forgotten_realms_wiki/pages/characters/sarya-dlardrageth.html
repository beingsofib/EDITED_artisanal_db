Countess Sarya Dlardrageth was a half-fiend sun elf sorceress, leader of the daemonfey, and a princess of House Dlardrageth. She was over five thousand years old as of the late 14 century DR.

Description

Sarya had deep red skin and bright orange and gold hair. She preferred to wear her magically protective black robes embroidered with gold. She had a seductive beauty and classic elven features. Her gold eyes burned like bright coals, and she had large, bat-like wings emerging from her back.

Possessions

Sarya wore the Gilded Gown of Selussa, which was a black robe embroidered with gold that was enchanted to the strength of steel, and carried a zalanthar wood staff called Miillaethm's Staff. She also had Aduce's Whip, and wore the Emerald Earring of Vhoadan.

She recovered a levitating Vyshaanti battle platform from the ruins of Nar Kerymhoarth. It was 40 feet (12 meters) across and had armored sides.

Relationships

Sarya's father was a balor. She had a son called Xhalph who was born after her coupling with a glabrezu. She also had a nephew named Xhalth and another son, Ryvvik, who was of vrock heritage and a psionics user.

History

Sarya was magically imprisoned around in a location on top of which Ascalhorn and later Hellgate Keep would be built.

In the , she was set free when the prison was cracked after the Harpers used the Gatekeeper's Crystal to raze the city of Hellgate Keep.

In the , burning with wrath for being imprisoned thousands of years, Sarya, attempted to take vengeance upon the descendants of her enemies. She freed the remainder of her daemonfey kin from Nar Kerymhoarth (the Nameless Dungeon), and moved from her hideout beneath Lothen of the Silver Spires to Myth Glaurach. This was where she corrupted the mythal, perverted its wards, and used it to summon an army of demons bound to her service. She also recruited orcs and ogres and with them first attacked small wood elf settlements in the High Forest, then launched an attack on Evereska.

After lackluster results on both attempts, she decided to secretly move into the ruins of Myth Drannor

Sarya's plans were, however, eventually thwarted in the Cormanthor War by the elven army of Seiveril Miritar, mercenary armies from Sembia, various small human armies of the Dalelands, and in no small part by the efforts of the sun elf wizard Araevin Teshurr. Sarya herself was slain by Araevin Teshurr.
