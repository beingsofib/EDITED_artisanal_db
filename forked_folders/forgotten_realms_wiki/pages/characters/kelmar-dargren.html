Count Kelmar Dargren was a nobleman of Tethyr in the mid-to-late 14 century DR, who was impersonated by Xexillidaulgrymm for a short time after his death.

Description

He was known to wear black silk shirts. He had a thin line scar on his left cheek from when he wasn't as skilled in swordsmanship, but no other scars. His skin was pale due to lack of sunlight, and his hair was coal black.

Personality

During his final days he'd grown secretive, erratic, and prone to bouts of rage, which wasn't the case before, but had lasted long enough for at least one maid at his regular tavern to grow accustomed to it. He was known to be something of a wastrel, a heavy drinker and often a duelist, though he'd tempered his drinking since his youth. He enjoyed the idea of being feared.

Abilities

His skill with the blade had drawn the attention of the School of Stealth, strongly implying he was good at it.

Possessions

Kelmar owned a manor outside the city. He wielded a saber with a basket hilt, made of silver.

Activities

Kelmar was known to sponsor a number of merchants in Zazesspur.

Relationships

He had associated himself with the School of Stealth, and the contact allowed him to hire assassins. He had a late brother, Lukan Dargren, after whose passing he became Count Dargren. Kelmar disapproved of the nascent monarchy, and had responded poorly to its rise.

His mother had a twin.

History

Life

Kelmar was the youngest son of the Dargren family of Tethyr. His disapproval of the restored monarchy drove a wedge between him and the rest of the family, rendering him a shame. He became the Count after his brother's death, taking over the family's finances. His body, not long dead, was found around Flamerule of the .

Impersonation

On Flamerule of 1372 DR, Xexillidaulgrym, a fang dragon afflicted by unique circumstances, had killed several people and assumed their identities, almost certainly including Kelmar himself. The dragon believed itself to be both a dragon and the nobleman. As Kelmar, Xexillidaulgrym had a curious amulet he employed to "hunt" Grim, and a single-minded drive to slay the monster, whom he believed to have stolen his soul. In truth, Kelmar had not turned up at his estate for a tenday before his body was found, at around the time the murders started; for whatever reason, the late Count's corpse had been found in the same woods where his brother's had been, strongly suggesting a link between his death and Lukan's.

While impersonating him, the dragon Grim had led assassins against the man, disappearing from the chases every time the dragon appeared; though unaware even in his identity as Kelmar of the gap, Grim never quite connected the dots: "Kelmar" was suffering from increasingly persistent migraines, becoming erratic and flying into fits of rage when unable to slay the dragon. On the last evening he was seen, the Count visited his aunt to look for aid in slaying the dragon; she provided him with deadly magical charms for his assassins. After they used those, the dragon became injured and drained the life of another person, the guard Thaedras, to heal; absorbing his memories alongside the life force seemed to overwrite Kelmar's memories, completing his passing.

Rumors & Legends

He was said to have slain his brother for the title. He made it a point of never addressing the rumors.
