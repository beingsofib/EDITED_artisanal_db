Husuf yn Harun el Halid was a nirdey, or a chief collector, for the Rundeen mercantile organization in the Emerald Ward of Calimport, Calimshan, in the late 14 century DR.

Relationships

He was the son and heir of Harun el Halid, a wealthy moneylender of Calimport.

Description

He was known to be quite handsome.

Activities

Circa 1370 DR, he was renowned for hosting extravagant parties in his beautiful house and it was rumored that his profligate spending had nearly bankrupted his father. In truth, thanks to the Rundeen, Husuf had no economic problems and had his own fortune. His role and rank in the Rundeen remained a secret.

His main rival, in both riches and looks, was Nasim el Pesarkhal, sultan of the Emerald Ward and nephew of the syl-pasha, Ralan el Pesarkhal. As the syl-pasha wanted to rid Calimshan of the Rundeen, he quite enjoyed their competition.

History

He had worked for the Rundeen in one role or another for almost all his adult life and, in the , was promoted to the role of nirdey of the Emerald Ward.
