Sisyphus was a mortal man imprisoned in the Underworld, the divine realm of Hades in Pluton for a perennial period. His name lived on as one of many dire legends in the multiverse, a warning against messing with the ever temperamental Olympian pantheon.

Activities

The eternal punishment that Sisyphus suffered within the Underworld was having roll a massive boulder up to the brow of a hill, and then topple it over. However, in every instance of him attempting this task, he would be overwhelmed by the boulder's weight at the last second and it would bounces back down. Thus was the legend of Sisyphus, forever condemned to repeat this process.

Any attempts made to ease his pain, even something as simple as giving him a drink, would draw the immediate attention and wrath of the deity Hades, for his suffering was but one part of a grander scheme.

History

At the request of his fellow Olympians, Hades kept Sisyphus a prisoner within his realm. but the thoughtless act that condemned him to Pluton was betraying divine secrets of the gods and later tricking Hades into handcuffs.

In the , on the 65th layer of the Abyss, a balor general by the name of Raachaak had caught wind of the location of a fabled Torilian artifact in the Utter East, known as the bloodforge. He ordered a trio of subservient vrocks to that Prime Material world to capture the bloodforge for him, commenting that once he got his hands on the device, "I'll raise an army large enough to overrun stinking Baator in a single roll of the Sisyphus Stone!"
