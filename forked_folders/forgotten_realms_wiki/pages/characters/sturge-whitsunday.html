Sturge Whitsunday was a paladin and politician of Selgaunt, from the prominent Whitsunday family. He was tapped for a political mission to find Myth Nantar in .

Description

Standing tall and weighing , he had fair skin, blond hair, blue eyes.

Personality

He was always ready with a glad hand and a welcome greeting, but often came off as just too slick for comfort. He was somewhat sensitive about his own physical clumsiness.

Abilities

Sturge was an excellent rider, and could hold his own at heraldry and bureaucracy both Sembian and elven. Even for a paladin, he was quite charismatic.

He also spoke elven.

Possessions

He bore, of note, an enchanted chain mail, a short sword of quickness, and a philter of persuasiveness. Curiously he also carried a ceremonial gold-lace coif (skull cap), and a full compliment of grooming accessories, including razor, mirror, towel, basin, two different formal tunics, a formal sleeveless surcoat, a silken full cape, leather belt, felt cap. Presumably, he didn't wear all of them at once.

Activities

Sturge Whitsunday had joined the Diplomatic Corps of Selgaunt right out of academy.

Relationships

He was the oldest child of the Whitsunday family. His younger sister was Aleine Whitsunday; their father was dead, and he believed she'd never disobey him like she was doing if he lived.

Of their possible companions, he thought Nagreous Twiddle was a national treasure, though he was trying on the nerves, and would have to be allowed to speak if engineering matters came to the fore. He looked down on Rondo Marsh, whom he thought of as nothing but a commoner understandably hating him for his high birth; he deferred to captain Coprates Bile, though he was not opposed to showing her, in private, how a real lady ought to act. He found Ilsa Merrymore to be stunning, but while he hoped to win her over, foremost in his mind was worrying about how to keep her from speaking out of turn when dealing with foreign emissaries.

History

If he failed, things would look grim for him come election season. If he succeeded, he'd get an enchanted conch to call the forces of Myth Nantar to the aid of Selgaunt.

Rumors & Legends

Though Sturge believed he'd been tapped, naturally, for his diplomatic skills, this was true in a different way than he thought - he was selected in hopes that he'd die in the mission, eliminating the able diplomat as a potential rival for mayor Andeth Ilchammar.
