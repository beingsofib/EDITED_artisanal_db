Jorey Fontalas was the entertainer who worked in the Happy Cow tavern in the city of Daggerford circa 1372 DR.

Description

Jorey was dressed in elaborate and beautiful garments and possessed sophisticated mannerisms that were a good indication of his profession of a thespian or a bard.

Personality

Jorey was a happy and upbeat person who loved interacting with strangers and was enthusiastic about his craft.

History

In 1372 DR, Jorey Fontalas worked as the bard entertainer at the Happy Cow who gladly sang songs and recited stories for the inn's patrons. One of the songs he often performed was the tale of Peredus the Great, a local folk hero, defender of caravans, and the owner of an enchanted blade.
