Kai Tsao Shou Chin was the emperor of Shou Lung in 1357 DR and 1358 DR

Personality

Kai Tsao was an individual who was calm and thoughtful and slow to anger. He ruled with a firm hand over his country. At heart, he was an honest man who admired heroes and believed that all Shou of good will could join him in the rebirth of the empire.

Relationships

Kai Tsao had several concubines, the third of whom was Lady Feng, a wu jen that had been exiled to the embassy of the Ginger Palace in the Dragon Coast region of Faerûn. Together they had one son, Prince Kao Chou Tang.

Activities

Under the Gathering of the Pearls policy, the emperor had regained a lot: the navy and armies had been rebuilt, and peace was made with Wa and Kozakura. However, the Mandarinate still opposed many of the emperor's ideas of reform. The addition of science and alchemy to the traditional ministries was feared by many, as was the replacement of corrupt officials with newcomers. The nobles didn't agree with him stopping the creation of new fiefs and properties from stolen peasant lands.

History

Kai Tsao was born as the eight son of emperor Hsiao Chin, the Seventh Emperor of the Kuo Dynasty. However, early in his life, his mother fell into a disagreement with the emperor and she fled with him from the Great Court, starting a new life in faraway Kao Shan.

Here, he lived as a simple noble until he ascended the Jade Throne in 1339 DR, taking the name of Kai Tsao Shou, meaning "reformer", or "one who reforms", as well as the alias He Who Rebuilds the Dragon.

With his first official proclamation, he declared the Gathering of the Pearls, creating many new ministries devoted to recovering what had been lost by the weak and foolish dynasties before him. He also commissioned great public works and revived the navy.

In 1357 DR, his greatest concern was the romantic involvement of Princess Mei Ling with a young prince of T'u Lung.

In 1358 DR, Kai Chin's main concern was with a series of attacks in Chu' Yuan Province carried out by mysterious assailants. The Emperor sent General Goyat Nagumo to pacify the area, but he went missing. Kai Chin then selected a group of adventurers amongst that year's Komite participants and tasked them with a mission to go to the uncharted Kumandang Valley to find General Goyat and return him dead or alive.

In 1359 DR, Kai Chin was greatly worried about the invasion of the Grand Army of the Tuigan, especially after they managed to break the Dragonwall and defeat the War Minister Kwan Chan Sen at the Tsen-Ching, a tributary of the Sheng-Ti River. Under suggestion of Minister of State Ju-Hai Chou, the Emperor convoked Batu Min Ho, a general of Tuigan ancestry, and ordered to him to resolve the matter. To ensure the loyalty of Batu, Kai Chin promised to watch over his wife and children.

Batu managed to siege the Tuigan army at the city of Shou Kuan, but meanwhile, Minister of Security Ting Mei Wan, who had been secretly collaborating with the Tuigan, killed Batu's wife and children. Batu returned to the Emperor to explain that Yamun Khahan had launched the war against Shou Lung only because Kwan Chan Sen and Ju-Hai Chou had conspired to have him killed and that Yamun only wanted those two men in exchange for peace.

Kai Chin agreed with the peace offer of Yamun Khahan after Batu exposed Ting Mei Wan as a traitor. He thusly ordered the death of Ting Mei Wan and handed Chan Sen and Ju-Hai Chou over to Yamun Khahan, then offered Batu one of three now-vacant seats in the Mandarinate. Batu, angry about the death of his family, instead resigned from the Shou military.
