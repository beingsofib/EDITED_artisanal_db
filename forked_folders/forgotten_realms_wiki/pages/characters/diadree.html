Diadree was an old woman of Orunn in the mid-to-late 14 century DR.

Description

By , Diadree was gaunt and filthy, with a single braid of gray hair. She had a broken ankle that hadn't healed properly, and her blue eyes were milky.

Personality

Diadree was fiercely, proudly independant, and had a temper; despite being unstable, she behaved sufficiently grounded in reality.

Relationships

Diadree spoke at times to Amrennathed, an amethyst dragon.

History

Diadree lived in a house just on the outskirts of Orunn, a village in the Mountains of the Alaoreum, near a cliff overlooking Fox Ear lake. She had been old for at least as long as Bahrn, a local inhabitant, remembered, which meant she would have lived there since at the very least, most likely; at some point while he was a kid, she'd broke a broom on his head.

During the passing of the great wyrm Amrennathed in the , Diadree was close enough that, when the dragon relinquished its secrets to the mountain, some of them became nestled in her mind. She remained in the mountain ever since, despite a severe foot injury and a new development of periodic tremors, because she held on to the hope that Amrennathed might return and take back her memories at some point.

After the same passing caused earthquakes that destroyed the village and forced its abandonment, Diadree remained at the site. The passing travelers she spoke to in the meantime, she told that she wanted to stay with Amrennathed; mentioning this name eventually drew the attention of the wizard Arlon, of the Cult of the Dragon, five years later.

Arlon hired Bahrn, who'd since become a mercenary, to guide him to Orunn. There, the two of them eventually managed to subdue Diadree. Arlon used his magic on Diadree to guide himself to Amrennathed's lair, leaving her and Bahrn behind. She talked Bahrn into chasing after the wizard, revealing he was from the Cult of the Dragon; though skeptical, he complied, carrying her to the lair.

She was injured in the fight that ensued, as Arlon drew the attention of Amrennathed's guardians and turned on her, but Bahrn defended her; afterwards, she asked to be left there, so that maybe in the presence of the dragon's remains, the parts of her mind that had stayed with her may fade from her memory. However, her injuries looked beyond severe, strongly implying that she was at risk of death.

Rumors & Legends

The mage Arlon falsely claimed Diadree was a witch, and his cousin, when seeking her out.
