King Svenyird Olafsson was ruler of the Northlander kingdom of Gnarhelm in the Moonshae Isles during the early-to-mid 14 century DR.

Description

He had a ruddy complexion. His build was not too large by Northalnder standards.

Personality

He was wise and patient, and slow to disregard his allies. He valued courage and the willingness to go to war even as he counseled in favor of peace. He loved a gripping tale.

Relationships

He was the son of King Rolf Olafsson of Gnarhelm and cousin to King Dagus of Olafstaad. His son and heir was Brandon Olafsson.

History

Circa , when Svenyird was still young, his father hosted Elminster at Olafstaad. In the face of the boy's desire to see the world, the sage agreed to take him back to Waterdeep to study with his apprentices and to become literate.

Svenyird received his first scars in a battle with the Ffolk sometime before the signing of the Treaty of Oman in the . He would go on to gain fame for slaying a sea dragon, whose head he would later mount in his throne room.

When his father died in the , Svenyird assumed his throne.

In the , he apppointed his son as an ambassador to the court of High King Tristan Kendrick in order to ascertain why the Ffolk had apparently violated the Treaty of Oman.

He died of illness in the , and was succeeded by Brandon.
