Saibhon el Bhiras was a human fighter, former slaver, businessman, and the Grand Yrshelem of Slaves of the Rundeen mercantile organization in the mid-to-late 14 century DR.

History

Saibhon was born the son of the leader of the Ice Chain slaver company based in Calimshan. When he was only fourteen, he joined the company and his father taught the young Saibhon about the business and the operations of both slave-raiding parties on land and slave ships at sea.

Then, when Shadi'ar nomads killed his father with an arrow in the Land of the Lions, the grieving Saibhon seized his father's frost brand weapon and battled the senior slavers over control of the company. He gained the support of the younger slavers over the following months, which guaranteed that he could retain command of the Ice Chain. Saibhon proved very competent as a slaver, always judging correctly which slaves would survive until they arrived at the markets in Calimshan and appraising slaves for strength or beauty. He was also generous to his employees. In time, a number of the senior slavers decided to retire, so Saibhon bought each of them a festhall business in Calimshan using his own coin, whilst retaining a 30% share in these businesses.

His ambition and talent were recognized by the Rundeen in the , when the First Yrshelem of Slaving decided to appoint Saibhon as his replacement and retired. Saibhon accepted and gave control of the Ice Chain to his best friend, though again he retained a 30% share.

Again following a retirement, in the , Saibhon was promoted to Grand Yrshelem of Slaves.

Activities

In his role as Grand Yrshelem of Slaves, circa 1370 DR, he studied and experimented with the Rundeen's slavery business as he sought to increase their profitability.

Personality

A ruthless and sadistic man, Saibhon had a love for a darker kind of festhall, where he often beat his companion for the evening, leaving them bruised and bleeding.

Description

He was a middle-aged man by 1370 DR.

Relations

Saibhon el Bhiras was despised by the Grand Yrshelem of Caravans, Rafi al Shuree, who considered him repellent and needlessly ruthless, but she was forced to tolerate him. She would argue for him to be ejected if he made enough serious mistakes that his position was threatened.
