Cirrothamalan, "Cirro", was a mist dragon of Chult, already old by the mid-to-late 14 century DR.

Description

It had yellow, slitted eyes, and a ponderous head.

Personality

It was introspective and incurious, but rather cordial to other dragons.

Abilities

Like any other mist dragon, it was plenty deadly enough.

Activities

Most of Cirrothamalan's time was given over to sleep, rumination, napping, meditation, rest, reverie, and contemplation.

Relationships

It was acquainted with the faerie dragon Zyx. Cirro hated woodpeckers, they often inadvertently harmed it.

History

Cirrothamalan was a long-time acquaintance of Zyx, a local faerie dragon in Chult. On the , Zyx woke him up, and called on him to help repel an incursion of humans who were moving into their area of Chult to settle. Cirro was only moved to action when Zyx mentioned that his own favourite grotto would be used by the humans if they weren't spooked away.

The mist dragon employed magic to blanket the camp in fog, while using icy wind damage their tools, scatter their animals, and chill them to the bone; per Zyx's report, the men believed his magic had been a storm, failing to connect that to any magic. In response, Zyx suggested they impersonate the Uluu Thalongh.

The old dragon agreed to the scheme, and covered in mud and branches, approached a clearing where the humans were working. However, at the sight, the humans believed him to be a mudman rather than the Uluu Thalong, probably because of too much mud and not enough branches, and attacked him rather than fleeing. Zyx insisted the humans not be killed, but when he attempted to join the fray and disable them, he became caught instead.

Upon identifying him as a faerie dragon, the humans broke off and decided to go elsewhere, believing those were too ornery, persistent, and annoying to coexist with. About a month later, Cirrothamalan was woken up by Zyx again to let him know he was off to go keep an eye on the humans, who had given the area a wide berth. He felt he'd miss the small dragon.
