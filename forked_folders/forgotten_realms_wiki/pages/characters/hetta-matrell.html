Hetta Debrinne Matrell was the founding matriarch of House Matrell, a noble mercantile family of Arrabar.

Personality

She was a very resourceful, resolute, and sweet old woman. She had a voice that was soft, though commanded attention. She was also a, self-professed, stubborn individual.

She had a shrewd business sense and was skilled at reading people. She possessed an uncanny ability for figuring out exactly what someone was planning or thinking about, and therefore what their likely reaction would be in any given situation.

Possessions

Hetta wore on the fourth finger of her right hand a silver, ring inset with a ruby.

Whilst her spirit was inside the ring, Hetta's mind would remain conscious and be capable of telepathically communicating with whosoever held the ring, as well as telepathically read their minds. Her spirit could eventually be returned to its body, though there was a limit to how much time could pass before this became nonviable. Additionally, it depended upon what condition her body was in.

The spirit bound within the ring could also possess the body of any another sentient creature that handled it, switching the placement of their minds and souls. And it could pass on some of its energy to anyone wearing the ring, healing injuries.

Relationships

Over the course of Hetta's marriage with Obiron Matrell I she had four children. In order of birth, these were Obril, Hesta, Dregaul, and Xaphira.

When Obril sired Emriana with Ladara Lazelle, She also had a positive relationship with their son Vambran,

Ladara Lazelle was a very dutiful daughter-in-law to the old woman, meekly following her around everywhere, spending much of her time providing Hetta assistance.

Hetta had connections with a number of Arrabar's greater houses, her great business acumen being respected in many Arrabarran social circles. Among them she counted Ariskrit Darowdryn, fellow noble matriarch of House Darowdryn, as one of her oldest friends and confidants. The women were often seen socializing during events of the Arrabarran nobility and often visited each other at their families' estates.

Activities

Whilst she was in charge of the estate, Hetta never allowed the soldiers that House Matrell employed as guards to smoke on duty, feeling that it would be a distraction to their duties.

She kept long hours, even well into her eighties.

In her old age Hetta continued to maintain her position as head of the Matrell household, but allowed her family to believe she left the entirety of House Matrell's day-to-day operations under the handling of her son Dregaul. and lack of understanding of what she and Obiron were trying to build, she entrusted only one tenth of their holdings to him. The rest of the family's assets were built up in secrecy over the course of eleven years through shrewd investments made with her daughter Xaphira, who at the time was separated from the rest of the Matrells. She kept this charade up in hopes that Dregaul would one day come to a true understanding of business.

History

Hetta was born on Alturiak 9,

She had her first child with Obiron, Hesta Matrell, on Alturiak 7, 1315 DR. Her next child, Obril, would be born on Ches 25, 1318 DR. Dregaul would follow on Eleint 2, 1320 DR. And her last child, Xaphira, would be born on Ches 17, 1336 DR.

As Hetta and Obiron raised their children, the Matrells were largely ostracized by the other noble families of Arrabar, on account of being a young merchant house with no social weight behind their name. A factor that was further aggravated by her husband, Obiron Matrell, being a denounced bastard-born of House Mestel. However, through her wit and knack for reading people, the couple managed to be quite successful businessmen. Whilst Obiron acted as the spokesperson in their business dealings, Hetta was always his trusted advisor on what was the right course of action to take. Under their joint ministrations the family business grew quite rapidly, achieving a strong and diversified portfolio by the time that their grandson Vambran Matrell was born in 1350 DR. And thanks to her shrewd and fair dealings with others, Hetta managed to get the Matrells invited to a number of coming-of-age birthday parties over the years.

In the , Hetta's husband tragically passed away. feeling uncertain that any of her children understood all of the nuances of business enough to take over House Matrell from her should she pass. Over the next few years the family's business operations would be handled jointly by Hetta, Dregaul, and Evester Matrell. As the years passed and Dregaul showed no signs of true business acumen, Hetta felt tempted to take full control over House Matrell away from him. Yet she kept staying her hand, hoping that one day he would either see the light.

In the month of Flamerule, in the , the members of House Matrell were attending the Night of Ghosts festival, hosted by Eles Wianar at the Generon. That night Hetta's grandson Vambran, along with two of his young friends and fellow noble scions, Adyan Mercatio and Horial Rohden, were playing around in the palace garden with a bejeweled crossbow that had been gifted to him by his uncle Kovrim Matrell. Whilst shooting plantains off a tree, Vambran unleashed a stray bolt and before they knew it a bolt was lodged in the chest of Rodolpho Wianar, a cousin of the Lord of Arrabar. at the behest of Grand Trabbar Lavant and Lord Eles, to fake the nobleman's death and frame the young Vambran for murder, all part of a machiavellian scheme of conquest that would take the next twelve years to unfold.

Xaphira and Dregaul knew that this accidental murder meant death for Vambran and ruin for their House. Desperate to see her nephew unharmed, Xaphira took the blame. She masqueraded as a masked assassin and fled Arrabar, knowing she may never be able to return. She was presumed dead by all but Hetta, who stayed in contact with Xaphira over the next eleven years. Together the two made several shrewd investments that ended up bolstering House Matrell's holdings.

As the years went on, Hetta's hope of Dregaul seeing the light soured. However, she had no idea that Dregaul had been teaching his reprehensible business practices to her grandson, Evester Matrell. Her reasoning for not usurping control from him changed to a new twofold reasoning. On one hand she anticipated he would get so in over his head with a scheme that it would show everyone else in the Matrell family that, not only was he unfit to run the household, but that Hetta was still as sharp as ever. On the other, she needed to let it go on so she could determine who in the family she could trust, who would recognize their House was unstable and needed fixing.
