Fizben the Magnificent was the alias of an adventuring human bard and minstrel who claimed to be very famous in the late 15 century DR.

Description

Fizben was a friendly man with "passable good looks" and a good singing voice. The adventurer possessed impressive charisma of an experienced actor. In the late 1480s DR, Fizben appeared to be in his forties.

History

Little was known about Fizben the Magnificent's past, not even where he was born. However, it was quite obvious that the name was just an alias, and his body language and skills of a performer were evidence of his past prominence. The man kept his past a secret and this secret was the likely reason for his wayfaring lifestyle and the use of a fake name. By the late 15 century DR, Fizben was known for his clever witticisms and a number of original ballads.

According to Blackfeather, a Harper agent, Fizben the Magnificent frequented "singing clubs" in Eshpurta and Darromar—establishments that offered traveling minstrels overnight lodgings, watered down house wine, and scant meals, in exchange for performing five or more songs for the guests.
