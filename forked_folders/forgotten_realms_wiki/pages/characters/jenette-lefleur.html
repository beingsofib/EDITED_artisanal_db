Jenette LeFleur was a lovely young enchantress Sunite worshiper, who made a name for herself as a singer and an adventurer, as soon as she arrived to the city of Ravens Bluff in the mid-14 century DR. Jenette was completely enamored with the city and considered its citizens her friends.

Description

Jenette LeFleur was a strikingly gorgeous human woman with delicate yet perfect complexion, pale skin, long black hair, and bright emerald green eyes. She stood at and weighed . When on the road or strolling around the Bluff, she was often seen wearing a red gown of finely detailed decoration and her hair was woven with flowers.

Personality

She was renowned for her modesty, thoughtfulness, and compassion, especially compared to an average worshiper of Sune Firehair. She spoke in soft and pleasant tone, rarely raising her voice apart when singing on praising her goddess. When the young enchantress was complimented, she only stated that her beauty was a gift from Sune and she did her best to display the gifts for the world. As a woman of beauty, Jenette had a small army of admirers, whom she always turned down gently and with grace, doing her best not to hurt them. Many men said that being turned down by Jenette LeFleur felt better than being accepted by the finest noble ladies of Ravens Bluff. Her kindness often led Jenette to find suitable lovers for those whom she turned down, giving her a reputation of a master matchmaker. When Jenette performed, she connected with the audience, masterfully wrapping them around her finger via sheer charisma and skillful flirting.

Jenette often spent her free time shopping, meeting friends, and being a busy socialite. In the late afternoons, the enchantress was often seen at a tavern of a fest hall, surrounded by books and scrolls. In the evenings, she performed at the Bluff's inns, focusing on love songs and ballads of lost love, but not dismissing well-penned compositions or catchy melodies.

Only the closest friends knew of Jenette's story and her once being a boy who angered Sune.

Abilities

As a mage, she learned how to wield staves and slings in combat. She also was quite proficient in reading and writing in both Common and Elvish. Jenette was versed in religion, etiquette, herbalism, pottery, singing, acting, and working with a number of disguises.

Possessions

Amongst her possessions one could find a ring of water breathing, a ring of protection, bullets of impact, bracers of defense, a necklace of memory enhancement, Skie's locks and bolts, a portable canoe, enchanted +2 staff, and potions of extra healing, all tucked away in her bag of holding.

Jenette never left home without parchment and a quill to write down ideas for poem and songs when the inspiration stroke as well as for writing down names of bachelors and bachelorettes to try and match them with a lover via a well-planned "accidental" meeting.

History

Jenette was born Jean LeFleur, a son of a priestess of Sune and a half-elven bard in the mountains just east of Ravens Bluff. Jean's mother was a loyal servant of the Church of Sune from the city of Selgaunt, tasked with maintaining a secret mountain garden for the church. The priestess's only contact with the outside world was Kalaine, an enchantress who visited the secret garden monthly.

After six years in the mountains, the priestess was ordered back to Selgaunt, however, the church did not allow males on the temple grounds. To stay close to her young child, the priestess dressed Jean in a girl's clothes, lying to the church about her son's gender, calling him by the name Jenette. She claimed that Jenette was an orphaned girl who wanted to become an initiate priestess of Sune. This lie continues on for more than seven years until the night before the initiation ceremony. An avatar of Sune Firehair graced the ceremony and publicly exposed the priestess's deception. The goddess called the lie an affront to her and demanded punishment. Sune declared that the mother will never see her son again and would be restricted to the temple until she performed penance sufficient to appease the angry deity. The avatar tasked the child with performing good deeds in Sune's name in order to prove his innocence. But his body and fate were changed with the goddess's touch, transforming the boy into a young maid. Sune promised to return when Jenette earned her forgiveness.

Now a woman, Jenette was left alone in the world, unable to see her mother. She tracked down Kalaine and convinced the enchantress to take young Jenette as an apprentice without realizing that the young lass was Kalaine's good friend's son in a new body. The apprenticeship laster for three years after which, Jenette embarked on the journey to appease Sune.

Just three years after arriving to Ravens Bluff, Jenette had already cemented her reputation of a well-known adventurer. She began her journey in the Bluff in her late teens, disguised as a boy and joining the Ravens Bluff Nightwatch. Jenette's disguise could not hide her feminine beauty and soon after, she revealed her true gender to everyone. Since then, Jenette partook in a number of quests alongside a number of adventuring troupes and even though many claimed that she was naught but a pleasant ornament to the groups, the enchantress proved herself more than once.

Soon after, Jenette joined the red-haired beautiful bard named Felina Fireheart with whom she saved coin to one day open an establishment of their own. The two planned to name the business, dedicated to arts, music, poetry, and songs, Firehair and Friends and had their eyes set on several buildings, big enough to also include a small shrine to Sune, available for sale in Ravens Bluff.
