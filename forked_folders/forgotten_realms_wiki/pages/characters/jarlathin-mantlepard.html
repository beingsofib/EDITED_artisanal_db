Lord Jarlathin Mantlepard was a nobleman of House Mantlepard in Cormyr in the late 14 century DR, husband to Dethelle Mantlepard, and father to Ileireya Mantlepard and Graer Mantlepard.

Description

In the late 14 century DR, Jarlathin was a fat and big-boned older man.

Possessions

Lord Mantlepard owned a grand manse in Suzail, a modest townhouse in the town of Marsember, and a massive country estate just south of Monksblade.

Relationships

Lord Jarlathin Mantlepard was married to a much younger woman, Dethelle, who was a well-known actress and the mother of Ileireya Mantlepard. Dethelle was Jarlathin's third wife and a successful head of House Mantlepard. Jarlathin's heir was his firstborn son from the first marriage to Yimgraetha Mantlepard, Graer Mantlepard. Darndrella Mantlepard née Horncastle, was Jarlathin's second wife, who left him for another man, and the marriage was dissolved by royal decree.

History

Some twenty years after the death of his first wife, Lord Jarlathin Mantlepard was rumored to have been assassinated in Sembia in the 1370s DR—according to some theories, by his own wife and daughter. The Mantlepard women were said to have been smuggling Lord Mantlepard's body back to the family crypt hidden underneath the family mansion's garden. Ileireya, Jarlathin's daughter, was said to have participated in actual duels over her father's corpse, including a duel against Ezegult Lareer of Selgaunt, the so-called "Lord Skulls." Even more rumors claimed that when Purple Dragons stopped the coach galloping across Cormyr's borders, they found Lord Mantlepard's body bound, buried under some dirt, and stuffed full of stolen rubies. These preposterous rumors quickly spread across the gossip-plastered upper-crust circles of Suzail in Cormyr.

The truth of Jarlathin's death, however, was more mundane: he perished in Yhaunn of heartstop while attending a late-night revelry in one of the city's lavish clubs called The Spouting SeaLyon during the family's month-long visit to Sembia. The details of the revelry were not revealed; however, the nature of the club suggested Lord Mantlepard's last hours of life were filled with expensive wine and the affections of "delicate-cloak ladies." Soon after the Lord's demise, his wife and daughter transported the body to one of Yhaunn's temples, where his demise was confirmed. Then, Jarlathin's body was loaded into the family coach, and the grieving family returned to Cormyr.

The family's attempt to quietly smuggle Lord Jarlathin's body back to Cormyr was the reason for the slew of rumors. This was done by his wife and daughter in an attempt to postpone the news of his death hitting the streets, to be able to move some of the Lord's portable wealth elsewhere before his firstborn son and heir, Lord Graer Mantlepard, could put a claim on it.

The truth of the Purple Dragons' strange discovery was the fact that Lady Dethelle Mantlepard was a skilled thief, who'd robbed numerous jewelers of Yhaunn and stuffed the loot down her dead husband's throat to smuggle them into Cormyr. The dirt that covered Jarlathin's body was simply Dethelle's attempt to stave off decomposition during a long summer ride back home. In Selgaunt, a passing water-carter sprayed the earth-covered cargo with water in jest, uncovering part of Jarlathin's body, which caused some commotion that prompted the local law officials to chase the coach through the city. The coach found shelter in a gated manse belonging to Lord Ezegult, a local crazed necromancer, who insisted the ladies join him for supper. Of course, when the body was discovered by the necromancer's stable servant, an argument broke out as Lord Ezegult wanted to claim the body. Ileireya Mantlepard defended her father's corpse in a sword-duel. Ileireya won, secured the corpse, and ended up having the "Lord Skulls" as her admirer. The necromancer used his undead servants to distract the officers and help the Mantlepard coach to escape Selgaunt.

Another duel arose over Lord Mantlepard, this time when Ileireya fought off a brigand and ended his life in the process. The coach continued on and rushed past a Purple Dragon border post. The Ladies Mantlepard claimed that the body was bound hands and feet by the necromancer before they could stop him. This was suspicious enough to have them detained and interrogated by the post commander Lionar Abdrin Baelbrook. When the body was being inspected by a War Wizard, Lord Jarlathin Mantlepard spilled the gems hidden in his throat. The War Wizard named Jestrel Halroaltaen was tasked with the interrogation, who, as it turned out, was a childhood friend and admirer of Lady Ileireya Mantlepard. Jestrel took the suspected murderers into his custody and disappeared along with them.

Jarlathin Mantlepard's body was left in the Purple Dragons' custody. The gems were collected and disappeared. Soon after, the body disappeared as well. To complicate things further, the Ladies Mantlepard declared Lord Jarlathin Mantlepard to be alive and well before going into hiding, making his heir, Lord Graer's claims on House Mantlepard unlawful. Lord Graer's hirelings, as well as Suzail's Highknights, were searching for Jarlathin and both Ladies Mantlepard.
