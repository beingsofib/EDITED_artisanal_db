Alexander Rainforest was an inhabitant of the Lower City of Baldur's Gate in the late 15 century DR.

Description

Alexander was a male high elf with chizled features, a light bronze skin, and wavy brown hair.

Posessions

Alexander Rainforest owned a house in the Lower City, neighboring Beehive Goods and Wares general store.

Relationships

Ratin Sesescor was the name of a rat what lived in Alexander's home. The man tried to befriend the rat, feeding it various cheeses, earning him the name "cheese-dropper." Alexander discovered that the rat liked red, white, yellow, mellow, and sharp cheeses crumbled into small bits, and disliked smoked, blue, and waxed. When Alexander was killed, Ratin eagerly ate the "red morsels" spilled from his friend's body.

History

Alexander Rainforest was born and bred Baldurian. He worked as an ombudsman in Baldur's Gate's docks. His job led him to investigate the ownership of Candulhallow's Tombstones and the activities of murderous cultists – something that his friends and co-workers thought to be just mad conspiracy theories. Alexander spied on the business and even hired Zhentarim agents. His discoveries were dismissed as ravings, and his friends claimed that this obsession made him dull. These friends were more interested in consuming copious amounts of alcohol at The Singing Lute. The investigations made Alexander Rainforest a target for the serial killer Dolor, who sought to join the unholy Murder Tribunal of Bhaal. He was murdered in the , shortly before the armies of the Cult of the Absolute attacked the city of Baldur's Gate. His body was left in his Lower City home, missing a hand, taken by the killer. Rainforest's tombstone was commissioned to be carved by Candulhallow's Tombstones in the Lower City. The grave marker was stored in Candulhallow's workshop. It read: "Alexander Rainforest. Meticulous in life, mellifluous in death."
