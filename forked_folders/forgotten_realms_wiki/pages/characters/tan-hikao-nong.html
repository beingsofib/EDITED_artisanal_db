Tan Hikao Nong, also called the Balladeer, was a wandering minstrel and author from the southern empire of T'u Lung in Kara-Tur in the mid–14 century DR.

Description

Tan Hikao Nong was noted to be an attractive and imposingly tall man, which made him appealing to many women. Though he was an impressive 145 years old, his appearance remained as that of a vigorous and healthy 45-year-old.

Personality

On top of his musical talent, Tan Nong had a deep passion for history and a thirst for knowledge, qualities he shared with Mei Lung and that the two would bond over. He was also noted to have a heroic demeanor, for which he was described as a "young Ch'en Hsiang come to earth" by his fans. Beneath the surface, however, there lay a tremendous amount of pain from his early life, which still haunted him many years later.

Abilities

Tan Hikao Nong used magic to enhance his performances and influence his audiences. However, he did not solely depend on said magic, as his performances were already impressive on their own, thanks to his immense talent as a performer and his strong voice.

Activities

Tan Nong traveled throughout the lands of Shou Lung and T'u Lung, performing both epic ballads and light-hearted tunes as he went. These songs were so widely beloved that they, in a sense, united the two empires through their popular cultures.

He also adapted some of his numbers into written form, such as the Romance of the Three Lords. The popularity of the written version of Tan Nong's Romance was so great that it played a direct role in the resurgence of the printing press in Shou Lung, which was, in contrast to its previous periods of popularity since the Li Dynasty, predicted to permanently remain in use as of .

As of , Tan Nong was increasingly considered to be the best candidate for the position of Court Poet, which had been vacant for a year following the death of the previous appointee, in Shou Lung. However, his origins in the breakaway nation of T'u Lung made the topic of his promotion a politically contentious one.

Relationships

Tan Nong had been a close friend of Mei Lung ever since the two met in Shang Stzi some time prior to . The two of them bonded over their mutual love of history, and Mei Lung was an ardent fan of his songs and ballads. In fact, Mei Lung considered Tan Hikao Nong to be his favorite amongst the many names and faces from T'u Lung he had come to personally know.

In his earlier years, he had studied under two great masters: Fargh Choi, who taught him enchantment magic, mastery of the elements and their correlates, and self-defense; and To Nanchi, who built upon his prior arcane training (including seventeen potent spells and the art of harnessing the power of the sun and moon) and taught him how to craft the enchanted flute he still carried with him as of . Many years after he had trained with these masters, he still deeply admired both of them.

History

While the details of Tan Hikao Nong's early life are unknown, Mei Lung mentioned to Elminster in his account that it was such a sorrowful time for him that he would never bring it up to anyone.

Tan Nong began his training in magic under the auspices of his first master, Fargh Choi, some time before the 14 century DR. After having been prepared both physically and mentally through intensive work and memorization of rites and invocations, Tan Nong studied scrolls and bamboo slips containing various philosophies and magical treatises that had been collected by his master. From these, he learned the basics of magic, which included invocations of the gods of the four directions and the art of feng shui. Tan Nong also learned to be careful in his spellcasting; as his master taught, the cosmos was like a singular sheet of cloth, and a superior man could restore balance to the celestial cloth while unwise manipulation of it would unleash demons and malignant spirits. Most importantly, he was trained in tong, a nonviolent martial art meant for self-defense in hand-to-hand combat.

While his training with Fargh Choi had tempered his youthful desires with much-needed wisdom, Tan Hikao Nong only truly began to take life more seriously about forty years later and after many escapades and relationships, and he thus sought out another mentor. At this point, he had heard reports about a man in Hachoni who had been playing his flute for ten days without stopping even once, causing many to believe he had been cursed and might soon perish. Tan Nong thus made haste for Hachoni in hopes of saving the man, but the man in question, To Nanchi, stopped as soon as Tan Nong arrived and told him that he had been waiting for him. Thus, To Nanchi took Tan Nong under his wing, and the two of them would travel together over the next twelve years.

During his time as To Nanchi's pupil, Tan Nong learned of the effects of the lunar and solar nodes on mortals and the celestial fabric and how to channel their power into his songs and spells to increase their potency. He also learned how to conduct the central shamanic ritual of the Great Exorcism Festival, an annual festival to drive away the misfortunes of the previous year. What Tan Nong would consider the most important thing he learned under To Nanchi was a particular process of creating an enchanted flute, which involved finding and felling a specific stalk of bamboo within a grove never before touched by man, oiling and polishing the resulting staff, and performing an enchanting ceremony wherein appropriate offerings were given and the holes of the flute were burned in only by looking at it. After that, it would take years for Tan Nong to master his enchanted flute, and as of , he still had much to learn.

Years later, Tan Hikao Nong caught the attention of Mei Lung while performing in Karatin and later at Sung Tsi's wedding. Mei Lung, impressed by his musical and arcane talent, eventually sought the Balladeer out, and the two would eventually meet in Shang Stzi while Mei Lung posed as an ambassador and advisor to Ambassador Hing Ti Chin of Shou Lung. After their initial meeting, Tan Nong met up with Mei Lung again at his apartments in the Imperial City, and the two of them quickly became friends. The two of them would later spend a week together in Wafang, and in their subsequent correspondence, Tan Nong shared information on the history, culture, and inner workings of T'u Lung as well as his own training under Fargh Choi and To Nanchi, which Mei Lung would compile in into an account of the nation for Elminster.

In , Tan Hikao Nong, who was a top candidate for the post of Court Poet in Shou Lung following the death of the previous appointee a year prior, visited the Shou imperial court. Having learned of a secret relationship between Princess Mei Ling of Shou Lung and a T'u Lung prince, he wrote and performed a fictionalized song about their romance during his time in Kuo Te' Lung. While most of the Court never caught on, the imperial family connected the dots, recalling an incident several months prior in which Mei Ling was kidnapped by the imperial court's enemies and then rescued by a young merchant captain.
