The Mistmaster was a very powerful, long-lived, and enigmatic human figure of the High Forest. Both an illusionist and cleric of Leira, he long resided within his own castle, the Citadel of the Mists.

Abilities

The Mistmaster had developed a number of his spells for the purpose of triggering the effects of the wardmist that guarded his Citadel. These operated in much the same way that a magic mouth spell would be triggered, meaning by intrusions or intruders performing specific actions. He also had the power to cloak his entire Citadel in swirling mists at will.

Possessions

Among the Mistmaster's personal possessions was the Sarbossa ring

Activities

By 1370 DR, the Mistmaster was a senior member of the Moonstars. Almost nothing was known about his dealings with Khelben Arunsun; some speculated that it was under Khelben's orders that the Mistmaster destroyed Hellgate Keep with the Gatekeeper's Crystal. What was sure was that the Mistmaster oversaw the treant Turlang and reported to Khelben all news about activities in the High Forest and its surroundings.

In his free time, the Mistmaster enjoyed capturing wild pegasi from the foothills north of the High Forest, then raising and training them for riders. Because of this hobby he not only kept a stable of pegasi at the Citadel of the Mists, but one could regularly find the beasts flying or walking in the northern forests around it.

Relationships

His closest allies and household retainers were Iltmul, a monk or cleric of Helm and member of the Everwatch Knights, Cherissa Mintaeril, a famed Cormyrian human fighter that venerated the dwarven goddess Haela Brightaxe, and Azure an enigmatic Silvaeren illusionist. He intrusted each of them with a ward token, allowing them to move about his home without triggering any of its defenses.

The Mistmaster was also considered an ally of the city of Silverymoon's sorceress ruler, Alustriel Silverhand. His timely magical aid to the Knights in Silver allowed them to triumph in several battles that were deemed impossible.

History

Around the 13 century DR, the Mistmaster resided in Thesk, in the city of Milvarune, where he worked as Leiran priest. According to some, he may have even been the highest ranking member of the Milvarune clergy.

In the , loyal agents of the Mistmaster finished construction of the Citadel of the Mist, build atop the foundations of an ancient fortress of Siluvanede that had been abandoned at the end of the Seven Citadels' War and was then warded by Eaerlanni archmages to protectively seal away its damned armory. The Mistmaster would spend years studying and unraveling the mysteries of the wardmist, such as the invisible dimension doors that riddled the structures halls, but in the process would lapse in his duties as a priest.

In 1369 DR, the Mistmaster enlisted the aid of two Harpers that would be willing to sacrifice themselves if need be in order to put an end to Hellgate Keep and entrusted each with a shard of the Gatekeeper's Crystal. With this plan having failed and their cover having not been blown the two Harpers began spreading rumors of gates and various magical items, ones that could allow them access to the Abyss, hoping to draw them out of hiding and trick them into overextending their forces.

Their rumormongering would provoke Hellgate Keep to send out expeditionary forces against the High Forest and the cities of Silverymoon and Sundabar in the summer of that year. and 75 centaurs led by Motril the Younger. News of Turlang's aid did not escape the notice of Tanta Hagara. In the latter half of that year, following the month of Eleasias, she commanded her forces to march upon High Forest and the Citadel of the Mists. Consisting of over a 100 fiends and 500 Blue Bear tribesmen, she was set on burning it to the ground and claiming its rumored artifact.

In return for the treants' aid, the Mistmaster agreed to allow Turlang and his company to expand Turlang's Wood over Hellgate Keep once it was reduced to ruins and seal it away forever. Though the illusionist would ultimately underestimated the extent to which Turlang would extend his reach.

While Hellgate Keep's forces were occupied away from their base, distracted by the heat of battle, the Mistmaster set off his plan. Cryshana and Spellviper positioned themselves atop the Keep's eastern and western towers respectively to signal the Mistmaster. Once signaled, he teleported to the nearby cliffs south of the Keep whilst invisible and hovered there under heavy protection as he activated his own shard of the crystal. Blazing beams of purplish energy proceeded to fire out from the three crystals and illuminated the skies, forming a triangular field of energy.

The Mistmaster then willed the Gatekeeper Crystal to compress the ancient wards that surrounded the Keep, stripping them away from their anchor places and absorbing them into the energy of the pyramid that marked the Crystal's area of effect. All other magic present was transformed into wild magic. The triangular field of energy then contracted towards a point farthest from the crystal's shards and unleashed an implosion, followed by a brutal explosion of wild magic.

For over a around, the ground shook from the force of this explosion and every building within the Keep was leveled, both above and below ground.

Following the destruction of Hellgate Keep, the Mistmaster received recurrent questioning from some of the most powerful wizards in the Realms, including Khelben and Elminster, about the whereabouts of the Gatekeeper's Crystal.

In the , he was among the many powerful mages whom Khelben brought together in order to cleanse part of the High Moor and restore Faer'tel'miir, a lost city of Miyeritar, an ancient elven empire.

Rumors & Legends

  • In the late 15 century DR, there were rumors that the Zulkir Talaskos Murthrond was possibly related to the Mistmaster, due to his propensity for conjuring magical mists. However, as far as Elminster knew the two mages had never met, let alone ever collaborated on anything.
