Batu Hsuang Wu was the headstrong, intelligent and independent daughter of Hsuang Yu Po and wife to Batu Min Ho

Description

She had silken hair, a slender face with delicate features.

Personality

She was daughter of the Shou Lung noble Hsuang Yu Po. Always intelligent and headstrong, she became the wife of Batu Min Ho, and later would come to reside in the Imperial Palace during the great war with the Grand Army of the Tuigan.

Possessions

She had a ring of protection and an enchanted short sword.

Relationships

The daughter of nobleman Hsuang Yu Po, wife of Batu Min Ho, and mother to Ji and Yo.

History

Born Hsuang Yu Wu, she married the son of a lowlander in Chukei, a soldier in her father's personal army. In order to marry her, Batu joined the Shou Lung army in 1348 DR, becoming one of the most promising young generals in Shou Lung and finally marrying Wu. He served well in the provincial Army of Chukei for eight years. He served well in the provincial Army of Chukei for eight years, and meanwhile she bore him two children, the son Ji, and the daughter Yo.
During the war with the Grand Army of the Tuigan Wu investigated reports of a spy in the Imperial court and discovered that Ting Mei Wan, the Minister of State security, was the spy. However her investigation was discovered by allies of Ting Mei Wan and they managed to set her and her husband up, she along with the children was killed by the Imperial soldiers as traitors of Shou Lung. Discovering the death of his wife and children, Batu Min Ho managed to clear their names, but spit on everything Shou Lung, he instead joined up with the tuigan emperor Yamun Khahan.
