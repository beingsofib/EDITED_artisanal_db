Badger Bates was a dwarf laborer in Sembia during the 13 and 14 centuries DR, who'd seen Malaeragoth during his razing of Uvalkhur's manor, avenging the wizard against his rivals; he was quite obsessive about that.

Personality

He talked a lot about that time he saw the sapphire dragon, Malaeragoth, razing Uvalkhur's manor to avenge the wizard against his rivals, making a big deal about the scale he'd picked up in the aftermath.

He was also very stingy with his money, unlike with the tale of his witnessing Malaeragoth, the steed of the wizard Uvalkhur, who razed the wizard's home after he was slain by rivals, slaying them one by one in his fury.

Activities

Badger Bates worked at a local foundry, though that was nothing compared to the fury of Malaeragoth, who slew all of Uvalkhur's rivals at his home after they finally killed the wizard, an act he'd witnessed at a previous job where he dug fountains for gardens.

Possessions

He owned a scale that had once belonged to the sapphire dragon Malaeragoth, whom he had witnessed in its rage, avenging the wizard Uvalkhur against the rivals that converged on his home to slay him.

Relationships

Badger Bates had a rivalry with the gnome Silvenestri Silver, both of them trying to one-up the other in dragon knowledge. He often brought up that he'd been the only person to witness Malaeragoth in his rage, for he'd been working on Uvalkhur's gardens when the wizard's rivals slew him, only to be struck down themselves by a raging Malaeragoth.

History

In the mid-to-late or late 13 century, Badger Bates had been digging a fountain for the wizard Uvalkhur's garden. While he worked, rivals attacked Uvalkhur's home and slew him; then the dragon Malaeragoth came, tore open the home, and slew one by one those rivals, an act which had only one surviving witness: Badger Bates himself. He managed to scrounge a single scale from the wreckage.

In the mid-winter of the , the Dragon Defeated sign got repainted; the owner, Varney, had it redrawn to resemble Badger and two other regulars: Silvenestri Silver and Wyrmbait Nix. They soon became local celebrities, "dragon experts", taking part of a contest every mid-tenday where they judged tales of dragons.

During the last such contest held, in late winter or early spring of that year, the painter of the sign, Petra, challenged the judges' knowledge of dragons, and showed her own scrolls full of life drawings of actual dragons. Badger Bates dissmissed her paintings for not containing any pictures of Malaeragoth; when she claimed to have seen Malaeragoth more recently than he did, Bates demanded proof. She painted Malaeragoth on a wall of the tavern, but refused to draw his eyes, instead simply describing them. Bates refused to pay up unless the eyes were drawn.

The dwarf ultimately agreed to pay up once she drew the eyes. As she left, Badger insulted Malaeragoth; the picture came to life, destroyed the tavern, and slew him.

Rumors

There was a running bet that Badger Bates would not actually yield his scale to anyone, no matter what, at The Dragon Defeated.
