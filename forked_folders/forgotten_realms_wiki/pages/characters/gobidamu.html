Gobidamu was a monk and the retired emperor of Kozakura in 1357 DR.

History

Gobidamu was emperor from 1326 DR until 1333 DR when he retired in favor of his son, Gokammu. Afterward, he became a monk of the Kanchai school at the monastery of Mount Ichi under the name Shoku.

Personality

Gobidamu liked his current life meditating and writing poetry, and he would never leave the temple. Despite this, he was greatly involved in the temple's politics, granting the temple's support to Takenaka Sugawara, as asked by his son Gokammu.
