Rafi al Shuree, also known as Rahimat yi Manshaka, was a human thief, a former adventurer, a merchant, and the Grand Yrshelem of Caravans of the Rundeen mercantile organization in the mid-to-late 14 century DR.

History

Rafi was born the daughter of a merchant of Calimshan, but for her not-so-beautiful appearance and her poor dowry she could not attract a husband. So, rather than resign herself to a lonely life within her father's household, Rafi decided to escape into an adventurer's life, while a loyal slave of hers impersonated her in the family house.

She joined an adventuring band and, in her journeys through Cormyr, the Dalelands, and Sembia, Rafi learned of caravan routes and of a desire for Calishite goods in the north that would serve her well.

On her return to Calimshan, she decided to enter the caravan business for herself. she adopted a secret, masculine identity as Rahimat yi Manshaka, a caravaneer and trader from Manshaka. In two years, 'he' profited so well that the Rundeen offered a membership, and she accepted. In this capacity, Rafi forged countless connections between Calishite merchants and buyers in northern Faerûn.

After two more years, Rafi dared approached her own family in her Rahimat guise in order to propose a marriage—to Rafi. As for choosing such a homely woman, 'he' claimed he "wanted a wife that no man would try to steal." Nobody suspected anything, her family accepted, and by this ruse, Rafi took into her employment the loyal slave woman who'd impersonated her years.

Around 1352 DR, Rafi became a nirdey—a high rank among the Mitalibbar—in Manshaka, and she finally told her Rundeen masters the truth about her gender and her ruse. Fortunately, they were more impressed by her skill and audacity. Thus, in the , she was promoted to the Grand Yrshelem. Only the other four Grand Yrshelem knew the truth about her; all the others in the Rundeen deal only with her male alias.

Personality

Rafi was quiet and did not like to attract attention, while "Rahimat" was an outspoken man.

Rafi was disgusted by her fellow Grand Yrshelem, Saibhon el Bhiras, the cruel master of slaves, whom she considered repellent and needlessly ruthless. However, she liked murder even less, so she was forced to tolerate him. She would argue for him to be ejected if he made enough serious mistakes that his position was threatened.
