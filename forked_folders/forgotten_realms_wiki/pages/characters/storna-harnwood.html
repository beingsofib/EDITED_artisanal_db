Darklady Storna Harnwood was a priestess of Shar and leader of the Night Brigade gang in Wheloon Prison in the late 15 century DR. She lead the Night Brigade from the Temple of Shar she had built atop the ruins of the Riverside Rats' former homes.

Description

Storna had pale skin, gray eyes, and long black hair.

Personality

Storna was wholly devoted to Shar and the tenets of the faith. She was cold, dispassionate, and ruthless in her goals. While a powerful and wealthy gang leader, she was unlike the others in that she considered the material boons secondary to the glorification of Shar. According to her cook, she ate the same supper every night—a simple and plain dish of meat and potatoes.

Possessions

Storna wore a suit of glossy black half-plate and carried a round small shield and ornate mace. She accessorized with a special pendant and a circlet of Shar.

Though fabulously wealthy from her time as gang leader, she discarded the treasure she had accumulated and let shadows play with it, and her personal quarters were a solitary space with little beyond an austere collection of shabby old furniture scattered about.

History

Storna led the Night Brigade from strength to strength through ruthlessness and guile. After years of work, their power was solidified inside the prison when they attacked and nearly destroyed the far less militant Riverside Rats gang of wererats, taking their hideout and transforming it into a Temple of Shar. The few survivors escaped through the Wererat's Entrance to the exterior of the prison, where they recruited some opportunistic allies to lead the counterattack. After the slaying of her lieutenants Master Thief Karissa, Battlemaster Agarta, and her Grand Arcanist Darstil Vurusk, Garmor the Grim set a mission to finish off Storna and destroy the gang once and for all. With the subtle assistance of Selûne helping to dispel the darkness and despair that filled the temple, the avengers broke through Storna's devotees and living shadow defenders, finally meeting her in battle. Storna wasn't even upset at her lieutenants being killed and her gang being destroyed, as that was the very essence of the final truth that Shar embodied. She met her end stoically, knowing that she had fulfilled her duty to the goddess as best she could.
