Nil was an enigmatic spy who operated in Cormyr in the late 15 century DR.

Description

Nil had pale skin and shoulder-length auburn hair. She carried a lute, a shortsword, and wore a short, low-cut white dress and sandals.

Personality

Nil was an expert manipulator and master of subterfuge who refused to be relegated or bound to any particular group or cause, and was drawn to drama, performance, and grandiosity. In spite of this, she was broadly sympathetic to Cormyr and the Harpers, and was opposed to the forces of Thultanthar.

History

Nil was approached by the Harpers to be recruited as an agent, but she refused, preferring to chart her own whimsical path. At some point she ended up infiltrating Wheloon Prison during the Shadovar invasion during the mid-1480s DR, and appeared to agents of the Purple Dragons when they delved into the depths of the prison, mysteriously sitting in the bowl of a large brazier while playing her instrument. Unable to proceed on her own mission to steal documents from Shar Manor, she provided the key to a locked room in the Manor and gave directions to the agents. Naturally, she refused to explain why she wanted the documents.

After her adventure in Wheloon, she joined forces with the fearsome mercenary Dom when they were hired by Returned Netheril to steal a piece of the Abyssal Planus Mechanus Nether Scroll, cultivating a persona as an amoral hireling. She fed information about the elite mercenaries to the Harpers without revealing her own identity, spilling details about Dom, Thoven, Udere, Rand, Pelloth, Song, Dolthin, and herself as they prepared ambushes along Greenweald Lane. When they finally met Bensen Virandson in battle, Nil led a team of mercenaries and grandly presented herself as an arch-nemesis, narrating in the third-person, before suddenly surrendering and revealing her status as a double agent. Fortunately for her, she survived this plot twist and lived to be dramatic some other day.
