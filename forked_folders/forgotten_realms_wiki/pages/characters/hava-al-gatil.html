Caliph Hava al-Gatil was the ruler of Halwa in 1367 DR.

Activities

Hava's main interest appeared to be his memoirs. He spent the vast majority of his time cloistered with his favorite scribes, writing of his personal exploits from adventures past.

The caliph left the responsibility of running Halwa to his trusted Chief Vizier, Zarad.

History

Hava was a skilled askar in his youth. He enjoyed adventuring before the death of his father forced him to assume the role of caliph.

Relationships

Hava had three daughters—Sikayah, Rajiyah, and Mahabbiyah—who were triplets nearing maturity in 1367.
