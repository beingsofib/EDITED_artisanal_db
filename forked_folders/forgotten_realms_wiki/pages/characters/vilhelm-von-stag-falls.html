Vilhelm von Stag Falls was the Baron of Stag Falls in the mid-to-late 14 century DR. He was turned into an undead by the vampire Romanisk Vlaensk, whom he accidentally freed during excavations under his castle, and made to terrorize Stag Falls until his ultimate destruction.

Personality

He had an interest in history.

Possessions

He still carried an enchanted sword, and perhaps a ring of spell storing with several charm person spells.

Activities

Once he'd been turned undead, he occupied much of his daytime away from the sun, planning on how to conquer a long-gone neighboring kingdom.

Relationships

After being turned undead, he became a thrall to Romanisk Vlaensk.

Heraldry

The heraldic device of the Stag Falls family was divided in four quarters. The upper left was white with a brown book, the lower right was white with a brown mace, and the other two quarters were taken by the emblem of Stag Falls, which was green with a silver stag. Overall was a vertical stripe, wavy of blue and white.

History

Vilhelm von Stag Falls became the Baron of the Stag Falls village after his father suffered a hunting accident. Like his father, he swore fealty to the Mayor of Ravens Bluff. He ruled lightly,

In thrall to Romanisk, Vilhelm mind-controlled his guardsmen, charming most of his staff, though some charms didn't take and others he simply forgot, such as Cassandra, who happened to notice he was no longer human; he may have ordered meat be served to him raw since, Then, amongst other deeds, he had Clarrisa, a bowyer, made to work by threatening the life of Darrold, a friend of hers; he had Fatrina Haldine of the Haldine family brought to him, which his guards failed at, ransacking the Haldine farm in an attempt to find her, convinced the guards that Gunther, a local priest of Torm, was fomenting rebellion, to have him killed; he then captured Churles, the blacksmith, to outfit the army his master meant to raise. He ordered these guards also slay all of Jeagermeister's dogs, for they bayed too loudly in Romanisk's presence, which enraged his master. When people fled the newly maddened baron and his guards, he also ordered the general store in town emptied to deny the rebellious villagers supplies; these guards also brought people to Romanisk on his orders, bashing doors to get at the recalcitrant villagers.

Adventurers from Ravens Bluff defeated him in battle, most likely during dinner or ambushing him in his study,
