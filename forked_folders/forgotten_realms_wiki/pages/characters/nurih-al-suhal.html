Nurih yr Catahra al Suhal was a moneylender of Teshburl in Calimshan in the mid-to-late 14 century DR and a member of the Rundeen.

Relationships

Nurih was married to Fahid yn Nur al Suhal, a cowardly but wealthy moneylender.

She had a younger sister, Melek, who was the wife of Roffas Calindor of Tethyr, who was a member of the Knights of the Shield.

History

Fahid inherited his moneylending company from his deceased brother, but he had no head for business. In contrast, Nurih very much did and collected several of her late brother-in-law's outstanding debts before taking the business over.

In the , Nurih joined the Rundeen.

Activities

By 1370 DR, Nurih was effectively in charge of the whole moneylending business, with Fahid merely involved in name only. She found it very profitable as Teshburl received a range of foreign currencies from visiting sailors. She also made loans to sailors needing to pay off a gambling debt. In addition, she served the Rundeen by regularly exchanging large sums of foreign coin from business done outside Calimshan into local currencies, as doing so in Teshburl brought fewer questions and outside interest than in Calimport.

Personality

She was seen as shrewish.
