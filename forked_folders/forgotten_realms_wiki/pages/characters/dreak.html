Dreak was a half-orc who was former slave turned pirate and the first mate of the infamous Scimitar of Fire, which was active on the Sea of Swords in the mid-to-late 14 century DR.

Relationships

He served as first mate of Captain Raurivyl Ornshield. They made an effective pair, with Dreak having the brawn and Raurivyl the brains and plans.

History

Dreak began as a slave in Amn, working on a plantation lying east of Lake Esmel that was owned by the Colwyvv family. There, he befriended a fellow slave, a human named Raurivyl.

Then, with the advent of the First Dragonspear War in the , the number of guards at the plantation were reduced. Seizing the advantage, Raurivyl, Dreak, and other slaves made their escape.

Seeking a desert island he might use as a safe haven and base, Raurivyl and Dreak took the Scimitar of Fire westward on several voyages beyond the Gull Rocks. However, they found naught but a vast expanse of empty ocean. On three occasions they encountered the naval vessels of Evermeet, which rose out of the sea, hovered in the air, and tilted to direct their strange weaponry at the Scimitar. Via magical speaking horns, the elves ordered the pirates to leave. Sensibly, Captain Ornshield obeyed, but still the flying elven ships followed them until they were long gone.

Following months of such futile searching, Raurivyl and Dreak returned to the Nelanther Isles. They sheltered the Scimitar in a cavern harbor on the island of Carcathen and later located a safehouse he wasn't yet sure about in Ilyaport in Lantan.

As of 1370 DR, after laying low for a number of months at Carcathen, Dreak, Raurivyl, and the crew had grown restless. Fortunately, the turmoil they found in Amn at the time attracted their attention. Yet they attracted another's attention too: the Rundeen mercantile organization, who desired the Scimitar of Fire's services as pirates in the Shining Sea. In trade, they offered safe havens, ship repairs, and whatever else they might wish for. However, although intrigued by a profitable alliance, Raurivyl knew the Rundeen acted much the same as their Amnian enemies and so he was just as likely, if not more so, to start targeting Rundeen shipping instead.

Despite this, the Scimitar of Fire was reported to be Rundeen pirate vessel later that year.
