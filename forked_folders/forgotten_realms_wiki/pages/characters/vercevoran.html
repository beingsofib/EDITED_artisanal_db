Vercevoran was a very old emerald dragon in the mid-to-late 14 century DR, who was mind-controlled by the Zhentarim during the .

Description

When in human guise, Vercevoran had a cold, bass voice, the hairless face of an ascetic, and slanted eyes that glowed bright green.

In his natural form, he was immense, bat-winged, and serpentine, with eyes of a paler green than the rest of its body.

Personality

The fate of humanoids meant barely anything to him, though he made efforts to repay debts.

Abilities

With his own power, he was able to take human form, shift to the Elemental Plane of Air, and pore through extensive texts at uncanny velocity.

Whether personally or indirectly, he was surmised to have contributed to some psionic application of power that generated ectoplasmic ghost-like entities that harassed and attacked people at great distances. He was also presumed to be part of a circuit of creatures that kept together an enchantment that allowed the Zhentarim to monitor traffic all across the Moonsea.

He was known to have manifested powers consistent with ectoplasmic cocoon, as well as employed a deadly sonic breath weapon and another that created a different, mental effect. Another of his mental powers effectively paralyzed its targets.

History

In the or before, Vercevoran was captured by the Zhentarim, enthralled by a cerebrilith the wizards had bound into service, and moved to Hulburg under heavy guard, where he was made part of a circuit of creatures that monitored ships on the Moonsea. The adventurers Pavel Shemov and Will Turnstone, investigating the trail of Sammaster, came to Hulburg and learned of his fate, quickly deciding they should deny the Zhentarim this advantage.

When the two adventurers finally made their way into its hideout, Vercevoran was commanded by the cerebrilith to slay them. Vercevoran briefly pursued Will Turnstone, manifesting a number of psionic powers in the process, eventually capturing the halfling. Fortunately, Pavel managed to slay the cerebrilith at that moment,

Before departing for the Elemental Plane of Air, Vercevoran briefly aided Will and Pavel in their search by quickly narrowing down the information available in a ruined temple to Oghma, but refused to involve himself any further.
