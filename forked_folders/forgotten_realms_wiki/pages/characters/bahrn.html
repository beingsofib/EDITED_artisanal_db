Bahrn was a mercenary of Turmish in the late 14 century DR.

Description

He had been a fat kid, with crooked teeth.

Personality

He was described as somewhat naive.

Abilities

He had an uncanny ability to use a morningstar by throwing it, being able to catch and pin someone's robe against a wall; this suggested he had absolutely incredible strength.

Possessions

He carried a morningstar, and wore a suit of fancy armor decorated with eye patterns made out of lapis lazuli.

Activities

He was a mercenary.

Relationships

His father was a farmer called Norint.

History

While a child, Bahrn and his friends had harassed Diadree, a local old woman living alone near Orunn, for which he'd gotten a broomstick shattered over his head, at some point after . He had left his home town of Orunn after his father died, when he was thirteen, becoming a mercenary afterwards. He was aware that it had been abandoned after it suddenly became geologically unstable in , with the earth becoming suddenly prone to frequent earthquakes which had rendered the land near-uninhabitable, almost certainly implying the events were chronologically close, if not related.

In the , he was hired by the wizard Arlon in Ironfang Deep as an escort and guide to Orunn; the wizard claimed that Diadree was his cousin, and a wizard to boot. Despite knowing that was fake, Bahrn agreed anyway, hoping to be there to prevent any harm that might befall her from Arlon; she was not known to have any friends. Indeed, once they neared her home, Bahrn failed to protect Arlon, whose face was roughed up by the old woman before he even noticed she was there. Bahrn convinced Arlon to be patient, rather than burn down her home, after she ran inside to hole up.

When she came back out, Bahrn caught her, and calmed her down somewhat; Arlon cast a spell on her clothes, which guided him to a ledge and cave. Prompted by Diadree, who warned he was almost certainly a member of the Cult of the Dragon, Bahrn followed him, carrying Diadree to the cave. There, Arlon attacked Diadree, demanding to know where the dragon's body was; Bahrn protected Diadree, and hurled his morningstar at the man, pinning one of the arms of his robe to the wall with it somehow. That was enough for the cave's guardians to slay him.

He offered to carry Diadree out, but she refused; he said he'd at least fix her roof.
