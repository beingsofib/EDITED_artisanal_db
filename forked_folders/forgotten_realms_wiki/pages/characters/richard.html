Richard was the original proprietor of the tavern and an inn named Den of the Seven Vales that stood in the City of Coin, Athkatla, in the mid-to-late 14 century DR.

History

By the , was already dead and his wife Patricia she was left a widow in charge of the Seven Vales. Richard was responsible for the inn's name and under his management, it employed belly-dancing women. This mede the Vales popular among Athkatlan males. After Patricia took over, the inn was renamed to the Den of the Seven Vales and the dancers were let go.
