King Rolf Olafsson was ruler of the Northlander kingdom of Olafstaad during the early-to-mid 14 century DR, and subsequently the king of all of Gnarhelm in the Moonshae Isles.

Personality

He was more cosmopolitan than the average Northlander king, having an eye for art as well as a respect for book learning. He maintained a collection of art and valuables (acquired mostly through raiding) that he curated tastefully.

Relationships

His son and heir was Svenyird Olafsson, whom he sent to study under Elminster when the boy was young.

History

Circa , Rolf received word that the city of Gnarhelm had been left undefended by King Sigurd Helmudson and was under attack by pirates. He led his cavalry on an overnight ride to the city, fearing that he would be too late to do anything but avenge the town. Instead, he found that the city had been saved, and there met Elminster during the sage's tour of the Moonshae Isles. Rolf invited Elminster back to his home at Olafstaad, where he entertained the sage for nearly a tenday before arranging to take him to Waterdeep via longship.

When King Helmudson died in the , Rolf Olafsson assumed his throne. He ruled over Gnarhelm, Olafstaad, and Sunderstaad.

In the , King Olafsson was witness to the signing of the Treaty of Oman, ending the centuries-long open hostilities between the Northlanders and the Ffolk.

He died of natural causes the following year, and was succeeded by Svenyird.
