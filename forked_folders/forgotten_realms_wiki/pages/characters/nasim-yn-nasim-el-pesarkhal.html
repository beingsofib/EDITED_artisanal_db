Nasim yn Nasim el Pesarkhal, or Nasim the Younger, was the sultan of the Emerald Ward of Calimport, Calimshan, in the late 14 century DR.

Relationships

He was a son of Nasim el Pesarkhal, who was the younger brother of Ralan el Pesarkhal, the syl-pasha of Calimshan. The ruler of the realm considered Nasim the Younger his favorite nephew.

He was cousin to Faruk el Pesarkhal.

His main rival in the ward, in both riches and looks, was Husuf el Halid, the spendthrift son of a wealthy moneylender.

Description

A young human man, Nasim was known to be strikingly good-looking.

Personality

Nasim was extremely loyal to his uncle the syl-pasha and preferred business to crime and intrigue. He had but one flaw: a predilection for gambling.

Activities

In his administration of Emerald Ward, Nasim was considered equal in talent to the sultan of the neighboring Trades Ward, his cousin Faruk.

Nasim's gambling habit meant that several of Calimport's criminals jostled to be his creditors and thereby acquire leverage over him and influence in the wealthy Emerald Ward.
