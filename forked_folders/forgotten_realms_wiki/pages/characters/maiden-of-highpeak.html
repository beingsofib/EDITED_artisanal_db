The maiden of Highpeak, also called the maid of Highpeak, was a mysterious and legendary woman whose anguished cries could be heard echoing across southern Moray in the Moonshae Isles as of the mid–14 century DR.

History

Her cries were heard and documented by Elminster during his visit to the Moonshaes in the early-to-mid 14 century DR. He transcribed her tale as told by King Dagdar of Moray, and speculated that the king himself might have been a descendant of the maiden.

Rumors & Legends

According to an old Moonshae tale, the maiden and her many sons dwelt in peace and isolation within a grand castle of glass high in the Orcskull Mountains. The sons grew tired of living in the castle, and so they constructed a glass bridge to a nearby mountain, and set out to explore. However, as they left, the castle disappeared. The sons would go on to become mercenaries, and all but the eldest would end up dying in battle. After many years, this lone and much older survivor returned home and rediscovered the bridge during a bitter winter. As he crossed it, he slipped on the ice and fell to his death. The maiden was grief-stricken, and her cries could henceforth be heard echoing out of the mountains during hot summers or freezing winters. The tale concluded with the thought that, one day, a descendant of the maiden would rediscover the castle and relieve the mother's suffering.
