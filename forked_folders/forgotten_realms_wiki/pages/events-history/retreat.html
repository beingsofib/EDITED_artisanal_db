The Retreat was the name given to the mass migration of elves from Faerûn to the Green Isle of Evermeet in the . It was instigated by the Elven Court, after years of thought and mediation by the leaders of the elf nations. During the Retreat, over ninety percent of the elves in the forest of Cormanthor migrated to Evermeet or Evereska.

History

When the elves abandoned the forests, they left behind legions of green warders, who continued to guard the burial sites and ancient groves that the elves had forsaken.

As they had no loyalty to the Elven Court, the Llewyrr elves of the Moonshae Isles largely did not join the Retreat.

In the Year of the Worm, 1356 DR, Luvon Greencloak sent an exclusive letter to the rulers of the Dalelands to inform them about the decision of the Elven Court of retreating to "a further land from Man." He pleaded the few remaining elves in Faerûn to contact him as soon as possible if they wished to join the Retreat. Luvon also went on to thank the Dalesmen for their good relationships during the days since the planting of the Standing Stone.

In the , Cambrizym of Candlekeep wrote that the Retreat had come to an end, though not by official proclamation but rather through having reached its own tipping point, by which almost all who had meant to leave had already done so, leaving only a few lonely pilgrims to make the journey by themselves. Shortly thereafter, the Retreat began to reverse itself, with many from Evermeet returning to Faerûn's shores.
