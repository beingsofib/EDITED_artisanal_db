Phobia

Disease
namePhobia
typeMental disorder

A phobia was an overwhelming fear or anxiety towards something, a response that was typically arbitrary and uncontrollable.

Acquisition

Many phobias could be acquired when a stressful or terrifying encounter left lasting mental marks, causing the fear response to become its own monster.

Magical effects could simulate phobias, creating feelings of terror and avoidance that compelled certain courses of action. These included spells like antipathy and fear, and magical items like the rod of terror and wand of fear. Psionics users could achieve the same with the power phobia amplification.

Effects

What separated phobias from typical fears was their intensity and irrationality. The victim might well not believe in the validity of the fear, but would be affected regardless. When exposed to the source of the fear, they would feel a very strong aversion, anxiety, or revulsion towards the subject, and could flee or cower rather than approach, touch, or otherwise deal with it. They might even be averse to speaking of the concept.

Some common phobias included:

  • Agoraphobia, the fear of open spaces.
  • Astraphobia, the fear of storms.
  • Claustrophobia, the fear of confined spaces or being trapped.
  • Entomophobia, the fear of insects.
  • Hemophobia, the fear of blood.
  • Hypnophobia the fear of sleep.
  • Monophobia, the fear of being alone.
  • Necrophobia, the fear of corpses and dead things.
  • Nyctophobia, the fear of the night or darkness.
  • Onomatophobia, the fear of a specific name.
  • Pnigerophobia, the fear of being smothered.
  • Psychrophobia, the fear of cold.
  • Xenophobia, the fear of strangers or foreigners.

Less mundane (but perhaps more rational) phobias included:

  • Aberraphobia, the fear of aberrations.
  • Dracophobia, the fear of dragons.
  • Faephobia, the fear of the fey.
  • Manaphobia, the fear of magic.
  • Myxophobia, the fear of oozes.
  • Phantasmaphobia, the fear of spectral undead.
  • Teratophobia, the fear of monsters.

Notable Affected

  • Most beholders had an unreasonably deep well of xenophobia and paranoia between their two minds that fueled their malevolence.
  • Cherish Hollow, who was terrified of most things except for zombies.
  • Jhesrhi Coldcreek, who developed a phobia of being touched by people after terrible mistreatment by ogres.
  • Kheros, a warrior who developed a crippling fear of blood.
  • Krimmon Amethystall, who had a phobia of water while the head of Tethyr's navy.
  • Ogolai Thuunlakalaga had a deep fear of griffons from childhood trauma.
  • Shadowheart, who developed a terrible fear of wolves and dogs, caused by partial memories emerging from her magically induced amnesia.
  • Storn the prospector had a paranoid fear of white dragons—though not an entirely unreasonable one to have in Icewind Dale.
  • Tymoriel, an adventurer who had a well-earned fear of lycanthropes.
  • Wulfgang Warts, a war leader with a crippling fear of Phantom Pass and similar places.
