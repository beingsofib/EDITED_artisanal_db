Mage Purges

The Mage Purges was a bloody and destructive campaign of wizard-killing perpetuated by Emperor Amahl Shoon VII between 427 DR and his death in 450 DR.

History

Emperor Amahl inaugurated his reign in 427 DR by expressing his rampant paranoia and hatred of those who could use arcane magic, starting wide-ranging purges and killings of suspected arcanists, their families, and their allies. The worst of the purges occurred in the first year of his reign, with more than 38,000 people slain and 44,000 fled to other lands due to presumed connections to the arcane arts, and every mage academy and guildhouse razed. Perpetuated by Shoon soldiers and common mobs driven to a frenzy, the Purges lost intensity after the first year but remained in effect until the destruction of the Shoon Imperium in 450 DR.

It was speculated that these purges and the mass exodus led to upheavals in other lands as the mages resettled themselves, such as the independence movements of Laothkund and Delthuntle.
