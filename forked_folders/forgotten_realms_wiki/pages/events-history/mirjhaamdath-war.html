The Mir–Jhaamdath War was a lengthy war in the First Age of Calimshan between Mir and Coramshan against the realm of Jhaamdath, which ultimately led to the formation of the Calimshan Empire.

Description

The war broke out in after Mir's eastern expansion reached the Lake of Steam region, while at the same time Jhaamdath's western expansion approached the same point. After a massive battle near Ankhport that ravaged the Mirran army, Jhaamdath slowly drove the Mirrans back to their Fort Saradush by . This forced Mir's close ally Coramshan to take the war seriously, as now their own borders were threatened. After "the negotiations which deemed unification a necessary gambit to ensure the survival of either realm" (likely the killing of the Murabir of Mir and his three generals) Bakkal Ukhar IV of Coramshan took control of Mir as well and added Coramshan's efforts to the war, providing its many priests and small cadre of wizards. After building up their forces for a year, the combined forces of Mir-Coramshan relieved the siege of Saradush, and over the next two months repeatedly defeated the Jhaamdathans and forced them back to Saelmur. Finally in a truce was declared between the powers, and they mutually agreed to avoid settling new colonies on the Lake of Steam.

With his victories in hand, Bakkal-Murabir Ukhar IV returned and declared the formation of the Calimshan Empire, comprising both Mir and Coramshan. The peace between the empires held for the remainder of the First Age, though Calishites suspected that the 24 major plagues that struck Calimshan were secretly the work of Jhaamdath.
