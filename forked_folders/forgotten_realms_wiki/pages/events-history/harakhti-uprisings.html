The Harakhti Uprisings were a great slave rebellion of the Shoon Imperium in the early 3 century DR.

History

On the Feast of the Moon in 204 DR, the gladiator-slave Harakhti broke from a small fighting arena in Almraiven and assembled an army of thousands of slaves and poor freemen who sought to fight the Shoon Imperium. Leading them north, he amassed more than 25,000 people from the Almraiven Peninsula. Though they were mostly an untrained mob, they were filled with an unquenchable lust for freedom and were fortified by many skilled gladiatorial slaves. They broke through the Shoon garrisons to the north and escaped into the Land of the Lions, gathering more followers as they went, and soon had 120,000 people—the largest single army in the region.

The newly-crowned Qysar Amahl Shoon VI responded in person at the head of his own army in late Uktar of the , eager to prove that the Shoon were not afraid to get their hands dirty. In the only notable achievement of his short reign, Amahl used his military experience to deftly meet the slave army in battle north and west of Ankhapur. After their forces had mutually destroyed each other by inflicting a total of 180,000 casualties, he then engaged in a fierce duel with Harakhti. Though the Emperor won, Harakhti considered it a kind of victory to force the Emperor of Shoon to fight a slave, and Amahl was forced to admit that although he was a mad dog and was put down like one, Harakhti had been a brilliant fighter. In order to make his point clear, he had the recaptured slaves—over half of all the slaves that had revolted—staked alive along the roadways of the empire, to slowly die in a most public manner. After returning to Shoonach and two days of celebrations, he was then assassinated by his brother, Shoon IV.

Although the slave revolt was ended by the duel and battle, a great many people were freed and disappeared beyond the authority of Shoon by Harakhti's actions. Centuries later, Harakhti the Deliverer was recounted as a heroic figure by the nomadic folk of Arnaden, whose ancestors had been freed by him.
