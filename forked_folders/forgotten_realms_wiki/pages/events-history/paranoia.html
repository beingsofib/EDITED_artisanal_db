Paranoia

Disease
nameParanoia
typeMental disorder

Paranoia was a strong feeling of being persecuted or threatened by others. In some cases, it could extend beyond a specific perception of danger and come to encompass a vast and vague conspiracy turned against the person.

Acquisition

  • The gem of delusion could inflict a random mental illness upon a victim, including an overpowering and severe form of paranoia.
  • The jewels of ardor, imbued with the avarice of Graz'zt, cultivated a feeling of overwhelming greed, envy, and paranoia in those exposed to the crystals. Dwarves were especially strongly affected by this.
  • Contact with demonically-affected chardalyn could cause general paranoia. In particular, a chardalyn figurehead of Errtu, found in Lac Dinneshere, caused uncontrollable paranoia in those that touched it regarding an imagined cult devoted to Auril.
  • Dragon bone stew gave the drinker the greed and paranoia appropriate to a great dragon.
  • Arcane blight caused intense feelings of paranoia before transforming someone into a nothic.

Effects

While in a state of elevated paranoia, the victim was overcome by delusions of being targeted by (real or imagined) individuals, groups, or the gods themselves. They might even adopt delusional and megalomaniacal beliefs about themselves that explained this apparent malice and jealousy. The evidence for this could be based on misinterpretation or simply invented, and it was difficult or impossible to dissuade these suspicions.

In many cases, a paranoid individual believed that their reputation, wealth, or position was in danger, but this could easily extend to physical threat. They might regard everyone with distrust, with even innocuous conversation and laughter between friends and strangers coming under suspicion of hiding something malevolent. They could respond to this feeling of danger by ensuring their own safety materially, such as by hiring guards, checking food and drink for poison, reinforcing their home with new locks and traps, and other security measures. In the most extreme of cases, overwhelming paranoia could lead someone to abandon or betray their friends, family, and allies due to perceived threats, possibly even trying to kill them first, either directly or by agents.

As befitting their grand and terrible interests, unhinged wizards like necromancers and alienists had a habit of extending the scope of their paranoid delusions to include rival archmages, angry gods, and strange alien planar nemeses. This resulted in similar interpersonal issues as other paranoids, but with more portals and fireballs.

Notable Affected

  • Most beholders had an unreasonably deep well of xenophobia and paranoia between their two minds that fuelled their malevolence.
  • Dragons were quite mentally stable, but weaker evil dragons were especially prone to falling to paranoia as they imagined countless threats.
  • The god Cyric was notably paranoid (among his many other problems), and this often influenced the behavior of his priests and followers.
  • Brains in jars were particularly vulnerable to mental illness, including paranoia, dementia, and schizophrenia.
  • Avarice, the tiefling evoker, was intensely paranoid and trusted none but her servant gargoyles.
  • Darribeth Meltimer, a mage adventurer, suffered from paranoia and hallucinations induced by the perils of Undermountain and the malevolent quasit Teeha.
  • Dradeel, a man who developed traumatized paranoia about werewolves after the trauma of being shipwrecked on Werewolf Island and then being imprisoned and experimented on in Spellhold.
  • Durlag Trollkiller, builder of Durlag's Tower, fell into paranoia and retreated to his fortress, seeking to protect his treasures. He lurked alone in the trap-filled tower until he died.
  • Gromnir Il-Khan, a bhaalspawn warlord, became extremely paranoid while besieged in Saradush. He kept himself and his soldiers in his fortress for fear of Melissan and other Bhaalspawn (he was right).
  • Hiflanyl, an elf trapped in Nalavara's hoard demiplane, became extremely paranoid of imagined threats including his own companions, and would regularly hide from everyone with invisibility.
  • Kalain, an artist in Waterdeep, became an isolated, misanthropic wreck after Dagult Neverember broke her heart and ruined her life, believing that all strangers were his agents sent to kill her.
  • Salitre, an inmate who became obsessed with protecting himself with traps while in Wheloon Prison.
  • Shangalar the Black was an exceptionally paranoid wizard who worked to defend himself from the entire world.
  • Toramir Kuurgan, former naval commander of Tethyr, became a paranoid and ruthless outlaw after decades among pirates.
  • Xardorok Sunblight, the duergar warlord, fell to paranoia and obsession from his exposure to the fell energies of chardalyn.
