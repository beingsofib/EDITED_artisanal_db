The Orcgate Wars, which began in , were originally a magical experiment that quickly escalated into a war of devastating proportions across the Old Empires.

History

In , the theurgist adept Thayd, last apprentice of the Imaskari wizards, opened a fateful portal to a brutal world dominated by empires of fanatically religious orcs. After inciting the wizards of Mulhorand and Unther into rebellion, he was executed, leaving no one in Faerûn with knowledge of the existence of the portal. For five years, the portal remained dormant until the orcs began pouring through, eventually laying siege to both Mulhorand and Unther for six years.

More fanatical than those of the north, these invading gray orcs and their clerics had developed amazingly powerful and deadly spells; the most potent of magics being their ability to directly call down avatars of their deities. Avatars of the gods of Mulhorand and Unther also dwelt on Faerûn, but weakened after the oppression under the Imaskari empire, were ill-prepared to defend against the orc hordes.

The orc invasion began in in the lands that later would become Thay, forcing the Mulhorandi to recruit mercenaries to help them quench the orc hordes, among them the warriors of the tribes of Nar, Rashemi and Sossrim. The war took a decisive turning point in the Battle of the Gods, when most of the gods of the Mulhorandi and Untheric pantheons were killed. One of the gods who perished in the war was Re. However, his death was not in vain. With his dying breath, he created hundreds of powerful enchanted greataxes imbued with magics what made them extremely deadly to orcs. These weapons became known as Re's Redemption and helped to turn the tides of war.

Despite their initial victories, the orc armies and their deities were heavily weakened, eventually being routed by the remnants of the Mulhorandi and Untheric pantheons after a rally in .

Aftermath

So complete was the destruction of the orc horde that the gray orcs in the region were never able to fully recover from their losses.

After returning to their homelands, the surviving mercenaries founded a series of kingdoms that eventually gave rise to the nations of Damara, Narfell, Rashemaar and Raumathar.
