Megalomania

Disease
nameMegalomania
typeMental disorder

Megalomania was a delusional state of mind recognizable as a form of madness, wherein the person believed themselves to be far greater than they truly were. It had similarities with delusional insanity, which was even less connected to reality.

Effects

A megalomaniacal individual believed that they were truly exceptional in some way—for example, that they were the smartest, strongest, most famous, or most beautiful person in the world, or most capable and notable in some other way. They naturally thought themselves fit for leadership and chafed under the commands of lessers (everyone), and took extreme offense to any suggestion that their delusions were not entirely true. They could very easily get themselves into trouble thanks to their inability to accurately judge their own merits, and if given authority over others they had the potential to cause even larger problems.

Notable Afflicted

Creatures

  • Most beholders believed that they were the supreme and most powerful example of their kind, and that all others were inferior or degenerate. This was matched by extreme paranoia towards imagined threats and xenophobia regarding all other beings.
  • Dragons were quite vain and proud compared to other creatures, but overall remained quite stable and sane within those norms. Red dragons were the most likely to fall to full-blown delusional megalomania. Gold dragons were overall the most mentally stable of dragonkind, but megalomania was the most likely condition if they did suffer anything.

Individuals

  • Balagos was exceptionally megalomaniacal even for a red dragon. He believed that he was the destined ruler of not just all dragons, but the whole world as well.
  • Edwin Odesseiron, a Red Wizard of Thay, consistently overestimated his ability and underestimated that of others. While indeed a powerful mage, he was immediately deposed after briefly conquering Thay, and was handily defeated and humiliated by the "inferior" Elminster.
  • Lord Hyraken of Hilp was fantastically self-centered and believed himself the rightful king of Cormyr.
  • Halsaime of Druid's Deep went insane with power and grandeur after being corrupted by a Shadovar staff.
  • Vulkaran the Dark was deluded by his own accomplishments, and ultimately replaced his people's own gods.
  • Zarr was a warrior who was struck by magic and afflicted with megalomania. He believed that he was a leader of supreme ability and destined to go down in history as the greatest of generals.
