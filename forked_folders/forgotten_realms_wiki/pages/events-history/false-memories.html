False memories

Disease
nameFalse memories
typeMental disorder

False memories were the result of a person's recollections being altered, either by existing memories being modified or entirely new ones being implanted. False memories could be accompanied by a form of amnesia as the memories were imperfectly replaced.

Acquisition

  • Modify memory could be used to alter existing memories or create new ones, in addition to sharpening or erasing them.
  • Programmed amnnesia could cause amnesia to any level, and could add entirely new memories as desired to shape the target's personality. It was powerful enough to actively alter their personality, alignment, and values.
  • Tailor memory could be used to insert a new memory without altering others.
  • People with multiple personalities could develop elaborate false memories to create the imagined histories of their personality fragments.
  • Those abducted into the realm of Bluetspur were often victim to mental techniques that gave them amnesia or false memories.

Effects

Altered memories were often imparted by manipulative spellcasters and psionicists that sought to subtly influence a person. By changing the details of a memory or adding a new one, a person's actions could be suitably redirected. However, most effects were unable to significantly directly alter a person's beliefs and personality.

False memories still needed to hold up to the conscious and unconscious scrutiny of the mind, and poorly-conceived alterations could be outright rejected with enough dissonance. Sufficiently nonsensical events that were physically impossible, wildly implausible, or completely out of character could be dismissed as bad dreams, intrusive thoughts, or drunken notions, even without them knowing they had been mentally intruded on. Even with the most powerful of memory-altering effects, if the memories were too dissonant or incomplete the victim could consciously hold them in suspicion and seek to investigate or cure them.

Notable Affected

  • Alias was a construct clone, and retained no memories of her creation and early existence. In addition, she had elaborate implanted memories that gave her the false impression of having already lived a full life.
  • Julith Harkspur was given false memories by Variance Amatick in order to implicate Keph Thingoleir as a dangerous enemy.
  • Lyraene was given false memories by Variance as well in order to obfuscate the cause of her injuries. Instead of being tortured, she believed she was wounded in a fair fight.
