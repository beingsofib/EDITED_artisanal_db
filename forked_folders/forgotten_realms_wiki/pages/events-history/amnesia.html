Amnesia

Disease
nameAmnesia
typeMental disorder

Amnesia was a mental condition that involved the loss of memory, which could be partial or complete. In certain circumstances, false memories were used to fill in the loss of memory.

Religion

Sufferers of mental illness frequently turned to the worship of Shar in the hopes of her curing their maladies. While some wished to have their lost memories restored, many more sought the oblivion of Shar as a means of relief from traumatic and painful memories.

Acquisition

The causes of amnesia could be physical, such as a head injury,

For some extraordinary individuals, it could be part of their creation, resurrection, or reincarnation, or come from experiencing petrification, possession, cloning, and other shocks to the system.

The waters of the River Styx inflicted powerful memory loss upon those that touched it or drank from it, potentially erasing everything from them. This affected mortals, the souls of petitioners, and even planar beings. Only a few creatures like amnizu

Creatures & Plants

  • Amnizu devils were immune to the Styx's effects, and their touch inflicted the same loss of memory upon their victims.
  • Oblex slimes absorbed memories from victims, temporarily inflicting an amnesic state until they had a chance to recover.
  • Obliviax was a peculiar plant that predated on sentient creatures and stole their memories.
  • The rose of forgetfulness could completely obliterate the memory, personality, and learned skills of one who smelled it.
  • Herald snakes had a venom that temporarily inflicted amnesia.
  • Sapient undead like ghosts often suffered partial or complete loss of memory or personality upon their reanimation or reinstantiation, and it could worsen over time. In particular, liches could suffer extreme mental disorders including dementia and memory loss if their ritual of transformation went wrong, or if they were starved of their diet of souls.

Magic

  • Brainkill destroyed a small part of the target's brain, causing an excruciating headache and physically erasing certain memories.
  • Forget caused the loss of recent memories.
  • Forget past caused recent memories to be lost.
  • Memory rot physically attacked the target's brain with rotting spores, damaging it and its ability to retain and process information.
  • Memory wrack temporarily severed the victim from their own long and short term memory, preventing them from recalling information or holding a thought for more than a few seconds.
  • Modify memory could outright erase sections of someone's memory, in addition to altering existing memories or implanting new ones.
  • Those abducted into the realm of Bluetspur were often victim to mental techniques that gave them amnesia or false memories.
  • Alienists who delved too deep into maddening lore could encounter a concept so horrid that their mind intuitively defended itself, and hid away everything even tangentially related to the threat. This caused a kind of amnesia that blocked out their knowledge of arcana, the world in general, and their own life, leaving the sage completely unmoored with no context for their existence. Despite losing everything, they could eventually recover their lost memories, though this could conflict with whatever new personality and beliefs had emerged in the meantime.

Magical Items

  • A potion of forgetfulness could erase a full day of recent memory.
  • The Stone of Golorr connected to the user's mind in order to impart information, and if that connection ended it would attempt to wipe out all knowledge of its existence.
  • A blade of forgetfulness caused victims to lose their recent memories, and prevented them from forming new ones for a few minutes.

Effects

Amnesia could vary widely in effect, ranging from missing the memories of specific events, time periods, people, or concepts, all the way to the complete loss of personality, acquired skills, and the basic facts of reality.

In mild cases, recurring amnesia could prevent the recall of minor facts and inconsequential events, creating an "absent-minded" appearance. In more extreme cases, it could continually block memories that were more than a few hours old.

Partial amnesia could leave the victim in a confused state where their memories and knowledge were self-obviously incomplete and did not have a coherent wholeness. Certain facts could be perfectly clear while others remained frustratingly blank. Skills and knowledge could be hidden to the person until their use was prompted, or lost entirely. In complete amnesia, the victim was utterly adrift in the world without any grounding.

Treatment

In some cases, amnesia was temporary and would eventually wear off once enough time had passed from the initial shock.

Medicine

  • The desert's night flower, found in the realm of Set, could undo the amnesic effects of the Styx.
  • Noblestalk was a mushroom that could be used to treat various ailments, including memory loss.

Magic

Various restorative spells could treat most forms of memory loss, depending on the severity and source. This included restoration, heal, limited wish, and wish.

  • Psychic drain could cause amnesia in hosts if it drained enough psychic energy from them. Temporary and then permanent amnesia were quickly followed by permanent brain damage and then a coma.
  • Remember helped restore lost memories, including from amnesia and possession, and could even provide insight from past lives.

Magic Items

  • Forgetful robes aided the memory of the wearer, and were intended to assist those with reduced faculties.
  • The necklace of memory enhancement gave the wearer immunity to any form of memory loss while wearing it, and gave them a limited form of eidetic memory.

Notable Affected

  • Abdel Adrian suffered partial amnesia after the many tortures and experiments inflicted upon him by the wizard Jon Irenicus.
  • Alias was a construct clone, and retained no memories of her creation and early existence. In addition, she had elaborate implanted memories that gave her the impression of having lived a full life.
  • Baelen Bonecloak suffered brain damage from a lifetime of substance abuse, and slowly lost his ability to process memories.
  • Billi Sharpe was cursed by a hag with both kleptomania and targeted amnesia regarding her brother.
  • The Dark Urge suffered total memory loss and obliteration of personality from physical trauma, after Orin the Red stabbed him in the brain and implanted a mind flayer tadpole in the wound. It was possible for him to recover some memories after receiving magical healing.
  • The mercenary adventurer Daud had amnesia from his early years, possibly from a head injury.
  • The angel Demascus was slain many times in the course of his duties, and on each resurrection lost all memory of what had passed.
  • The stone giant family of Gravillok, Speleosa, Rhodos, Obsidia, and Xortawere were cursed by Halaster to have a memory lasting only eight hours, keeping them trapped near Maddgoth's Castle in Undermountain.
  • Ireena Kolyana of Barovia experienced amnesia as a child after a terrifying dire wolf attack, leading to her adoption by a new family without knowing her origins.
  • Lyressa was a reanimated undead woman who had no awareness of her past life, despite her otherwise complete mental faculties.
  • The famous Mordenkainen experienced complete memory loss following his battle with the vampire Strahd in Barovia. A combination of the psychological trauma of his failure and the horrors of the domain drove him to the state. Fortunately, his state was curable.
  • The hero Minsc suffered a number of issues affecting his memory and reasoning that stemmed from his life of adventure. Most notably he suffered a gnoll-induced brain injury, and at one point he fell into the River Styx.
  • The Nameless One experienced various levels of amnesia throughout his many deaths and resurrections, developing several new discrete personalities as he encountered the world each time. This culminated in his final incarnation, which suffered the complete loss of his personality, self-awareness, and the basic facts of reality. He was able to handle this somewhat by working with those that had previously met him, leaving messages and resources for his future self, and magically storing memories for later recovery.
  • Palonya, a priest of Jergal, suffered a loss of memory after being afflicted with necromantic energies.
  • Shadowheart had complete memory loss magically inflicted upon her by Viconia DeVir as a means of ensuring her loyalty and devotion, though her memories and core personality traits continued to re-emerge.
  • Tristan Kendrick suffered temporary amnesia caused by the physical trauma of a sahuagin attack.
  • The Wanderer was a mysterious warrior who lost his memory after the Battle of Kiroshima.
