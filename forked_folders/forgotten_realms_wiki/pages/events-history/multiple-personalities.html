Multiple personalities

Disease
nameMultiple Personalities
other namesSchizoid
Schizophrenia
Split Personality
typeMental disorder

Multiple personalities described a condition that involved the presence of more than one personality or mind within a person.

Acquisition

The mind could attempt to defend itself against horrors and traumas by dissociating and splintering into new personalities, which could be extremely varied from the original.

Possession could cause a similar state, as the possessing entity influenced the host or took over completely. Alternatively, mental illnesses could be confused with possession if they presented similar symptoms.

Resurrection magic could rarely instil the target with a second personality belonging to someone else who had died near them.

People transformed into wingless wonders often developed multiple personalities as they succumbed to madness over time.

If beholders imagined something akin to having multiple personalities during a dream, it could generate multiple smaller copies of itself and create a beholder hive.

Effects

Once split, the person became host to multiple distinct personalities. These could be wildly different and contradictory compared to each other and the original, with different names, demeanors, beliefs, alignments, unique skillsets (real or deluded), and fictional personal histories. They could even be of different age, gender, or sex, but not comprehend that their form was ill-fitting. They might not be aware of or believe in the existence of the others, and might not share the same memories, causing a kind of amnesia.

  • The core personality, which was the original persona from which the others were drawn.
  • Fragment personalities, which were simple and limited personas based around a single personality trait or other concept. In some cases dozens or even a hundred fragments could appear.
  • Alter ego personalities, which were complex personas that functioned as distinct individuals. Only a few tended to appear relative to the fragments. They could believe that they possessed abilities or powers that the core did not, such as psionics and arcane magic.

These personas could emerge in calm moments, but most dangerously could appear during moments of stress. This could mean a personality taking charge that did not have the skills or mindset necessary for a life-or-death situation.

  • Eira, a chosen of Rillifane Rallathil, was infused with the essence of a slain chosen of Talona. This gave her a separate selfish and cruel personality that emerged in times of stress, with which she argued out loud to keep from controlling her actions.
  • Gestal, servant of Demogorgon, had multiple personalities.
  • Thalynsar the Ulithautarch was a strange ulitharilich that contained the decayed memories of an elder brain, fragmented into multiple personalities.
  • White Maw was a magical ooze that absorbed the memories and personalities of those it ate, using them to build a chaotic combined sentience.
  • Xzar the necromancer had multiple extra personalities, including a childlike one.

Some creatures innately had multiple minds as part of their body:

  • Demogorgon had two heads with two minds, Aameul and Hethradiah, which often schemed and fought against each other.
  • Ettins had two heads that operated their body as a team.
