The Tenth Serôs War was the tenth most major conflict in Serôs, the undersea realms of the Sea of Fallen Stars, centered around the fracturing and eventual dissolution of the merfolk empire of Hmurrath. This warring period lasted for sixty-one years, from to .

Location

A good deal of the conflict unfolded on the Hmur Plateau, which for centuries had been controlled by Hmurrath, though some fighting also took place to the west on the Haunted Plains, beneath the Bay of Yhaunn.

History

Causes

The empire of Hmurrath had long been ruled by kings and queens, referred to in the Merfolk language as 'Praessar', but in the 8 century DR the rightful rulership over the empire fell to six living princes. With Hmurrath having long since fallen into corrupt despotism, it was not long before long the princes squabbled amongst one another and broke out into conflict, beginning the Tenth War in .

Battles

More than just one conflict between two opposing forces, this Serôs War was comprised of a series of seven separate civil wars within Hmurrath that ended up tearing the empire apart, pitting nearly all of the aquatic races against one other and causing devastating losses of life. Aftermath

The end of this war would be marked by Serôsian historians as the end of Serôs's Third epoch, the Time of Merfolk, and the beginning of the Fourth Epoch.

With no warring remnants left to act as successors to Hmurrath, the state of Eadraal absorbed all of the empire's former territory. Eadraal would become the largest nation in Serôs to survive into the Fourth Epoch, followed by the sea elven kingdom of Keryvyr.

Queen Selana brought a reformation to the mindsets and beliefs of the merfolk people, She also led them back into harmony with the other races of Serôs, asking them to forgive the shalarin for the devastation they caused to the Hmur Plateau during the war.

Combatants

Throughout the Tenth War, the Dukars remained uninvolved from its many civil wars. Up until , they intervened only to uphold the Laws of Battle as well as to protect bystanders and innocents. In 787 TS, for reasons unknown the Dukars withdrew completely from all involvement in Serôsian politics and by the end of the year none could be found anywhere.
