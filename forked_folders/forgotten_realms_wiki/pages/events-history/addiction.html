Addiction

Disease
nameAddiction
other namesSubstance Abuse
typeMental disorder

Addiction was a disease of dependency on a substance such as alcohol or drugs (also called substance abuse), or a behavior like gambling, leading to compulsive consumption even to the point of self-harm. Addiction to alcohol was called alcoholism or sometimes dipsomania.

Acquisition

Addiction to substances could occur while using them to withstand physical or mental trauma, such as painkilling medication for injuries, or alcohol during times of stress.

Even dragons could become addicted to substances that induced strange effects in them, and these ultimately weakened and killed even the mightiest wyrms.

Effects

An addicted person spent much time, money, and effort as they indulged, hid, and supported their habits. Binging and bad doses could have their own bad effects beyond regular use, and withdrawal and cravings would have further unpleasant effects that encouraged continuous use. Their behaviour could become more erratic and potentially violent and self-destructive over time, and if they were unable to financially support the habit they would often sell of their material possessions, eventually resorting to crimes like theft to maintain it.

In particular, dipsomania was characterized by a sudden and compulsive need to consume alcohol until physically incapable, often suddenly triggered by mere proximity.

Treatment

Addiction could be fought off through medical treatment, immense willpower, and the assistance of friends and family, but the allure of the substance or behaviour usually remained. In the event of a relapse, the addiction could return just as strongly.

History

In the mid-14 century DR, the perfidious Joribel used Luiren spring cheese to get Foxilon Cardluck and his fellow halflings addicted, in order to make them desperate and dependent on him.

In the late 14 century DR, the Arcane Brotherhood sought to smuggle and sell cheap drugs into the Silver Marches in order to destabilize and weaken the confederation.

Notable Affected

  • Brindani had a dangerous addiction to silkroot.
  • Dahl Peredur developed an addiction to alcohol after the stress and horrors of working as a Harper.
  • Foxilon Cardluck had a desperate addiction to Luiren spring cheese.
  • According to Jan Jansen, Golodon the Unmanned had an addiction to poppy seed muffins.
  • Hala Myrt worked for the drug-dealer Orlpar Husteem, and was addicted to dreammist herself.
  • Japheth Donard the warlock was addicted to traveler's dust.
  • Jitters Corbwell was a gnome addicted to silkroot.
  • Najeeya bint Siddiq was a skilled flame mage kept addicted to dreambliss by her rival wife Qumasha al-Arus.
  • Magadon Kest, a psionicist, became addicted to contact with the Source, the mythallar of Sakkors.
  • Mullivy of the Edificant Library had an addiction to alcohol.
  • Olav Ruskettle, a famed bard, lost everything due to his gambling addiction. He even lost his name to Olive Ruskettle.
  • Priam Agrivar, a paladin, fell into depression and drunkenness after numerous setbacks.
  • The cleric Tristian had too-strong a fondness for gambling, even betting his holy symbol on a game of coroniir.
  • Vladislav, a Rashemi alcohol trader, had himself an addiction to tekkil.
  • Those that bought the services of the Society of Sensation in Sigil could become addicted to experiencing new things, unable to function in normal existence despite the Society's attempts.
  • Orcslayer dwarves were prone to mental illness stemming from extreme traumatic grief and rage, including self-destructive alcoholism.
