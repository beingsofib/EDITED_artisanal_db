The Seven Burnings was a short war of rebellion followed by devastating reprisals in the Shoon Imperium colonies of the Shaar region in the 5 century DR.

Location

The conflict encompassed the port city of Sheirtalar, and the interior towns of Sheirlantar, Kormul, Lhesper, Sebben, Rethmar, and Channathgate along the River Shaar and Lake Lhespen that rose in rebellion against the Shoon.

History

The Lapaliiya region came under Shoon control in 285 DR, but became near-independent in the lax reign of Qysara Shaani (367–427 DR).

As Qysar Amahl Shoon VII restored order in the early 5 century, the subjugated peoples of the Shaar colonies rose up against the oppressors. In the they managed to kill the officials and soldiers that oversaw the settlements, ending Shoon rule in the Shaar. Qysar Amahl responded in the latter half of the by sending his ruthless Qayadin Hakam yn Sarak el Sallah to restore order with a fleet of seventeen troop ships, and in the course of only two months he restored the dominion of the Imperium. First landing at Sheirtalar, his soldiers slew every rebel and much of the rest of the population, razing the city in the process. He then moved up the Shaar River and repeated the slaughter at Kormul, leading to the surrender of the remaining five rebel towns. Seeking to enact a lesson in power, the Bloody Qayadin continued to assault and destroy the remaining five settlements despite the end of hostilities. During this campaign, his Fanged Shield of Shyk Korort became a symbol of terror and tyranny in the region as it was used against the locals.

With the rebellion (and everyone else) thoroughly quashed, Hakam settled there as the nazir, or prefect, governing the region thence. Shoon soldiers were largely withdrawn from the region afterwards. Hakam lasted until the , when news of the fall of the Imperium reached the colonies. He was immediately torn apart by the widows of those he had slain, using naught but their bare hands.

The campaign was commemorated in the Sadimmin and Nallojal of Calimshan, with the Torch of the Seven Burnings campaign decoration for veteran soldiers and sailors.

In later ages, it was speculated that the undead of the Lhespenbog were derived from the rebels who were cornered and slaughtered there during the Burnings.
