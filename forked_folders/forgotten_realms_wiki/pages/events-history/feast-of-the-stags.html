The Feast of the Stags, also known as the Feast of Stags, was a two-day holy rite observed by followers of Malar from Highharvestide through Marpenoth 1 each year. It marked the highest point in the relationship between most communities and the church of Malar, and was one of the only activities undertaken by Malarites that was appreciated by those outside the faith.

Activities

For a tenday leading up to Highharvestide, the faithful of Malar embarked on a frenzy of hunting and killing of wild beasts in order to collect a vast amount of meat. On Highharvestide itself, they paraded through settled areas bearing the heads of their kills, and invited everyone to join them in feasting on the meats (as well as other side dishes). They explicitly invited those who were not followers of Malar, and honored "the Peace of the Table", meaning that even druids and other rivals of the faith were welcome to join in the gluttony.

During the feasts, Malarite clerics would make public declarations to hunt throughout the winter months in order to provide food for specific individuals in need, generally including the elderly, the infirm, orphans, and widows.

History

The rite was said to have been introduced in ancient times by Garmos Saernclaws.

Following the apparent death of Malar in the Spellplague, different interpretations of the Feast of Stags came to be practiced among factions of the Black Blood tribe on Moray in the Moonshae Isles. As of the 1470s DR, the lycanthropes led by Lady Amaranth at Caer Moray preached peace with their neighbors, and would bring food and smoked meats to any starving human communities as their observance of the Feast of Stags. Meanwhile, the lycanthropes led by Argon Bael saw this as a heresy, arguing that to observe the merciful Feast of Stags without observing other rites (such as blood sacrifices) was a perversion of their faith.
