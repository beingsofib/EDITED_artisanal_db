The Battle of Ejen Horo was a battle that occurred in the Hordelands in 1335 DR, in the valley of Ejen Horo, between a group of Tuigan warriors of the Hoekun clan and a company of ogres.

Location

This battle was fought on the west side of a fairly substantial, though shallow, creek that ran through a gully in Ejen Horo. This was close to the forested foothills beneath the Spiderhaunt Peaks.

History

This skirmish broke out over the killing and devouring the bodies of Yamun Khan's personal herd of Steppe horses by the coombe giant Billidûm and his band of Semphari ogres,

The coombe giant's initial response was to flee to the safety of the nearby wooded foothils, the ogres following along in a ragged line. While tempted to give in to his blood lust, Billidûm recognized that their chances against the Hoekun in the open plains of Ejen Horo. He kept goading his men on, urging them to run faster from the quickly gaining horsemen, but their retreat was ultimately obstructed by a creek. As they got to the creek, from the near side came a line of Billidûm slit to a halt, thinking of what to do, whilst his ogres made exclamations and some began to chant their battle oaths.

Combatants

The sides were unevenly matched in terms of number of units. Billidûm's forces included twelve heavily armored ogres. Their weaponry included the likes of axes, maces, crude spears, swords, and warpicks. If necessary, they resorted to throwing rocks at the enemy. Billidûm himself wielded a massive tree trunk as an impromptu club.

Yamun Khan's forces included twelve light cavalrymen and six of his seven Valiant Men, all heavy cavalrymen astride on barded Steppe horses, something that was unusual for them to do with their steeds. The Khan's seventh man, General Chanar, was away on a mission at the time.

The twelve light cavalrymen were adorned in kalats, fur caps with long ear flaps, and hip bound quivers. The heavy cavalry were outfitted in black khalats and scale mail. The light cavalry wielded composite bows and lances, whilst the six heavy cavalrymen used composite bows, kama-yari, and curved swords.
