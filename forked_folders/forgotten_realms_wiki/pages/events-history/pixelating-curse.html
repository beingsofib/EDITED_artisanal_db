Pixelating curse

The pixelating curse was a magical disease and curse that threatened Faerûn in the late 15 century DR. It came from Acheron on the floating Cube 1717, spread from the mad Archquadrone and controlled by the skull lord Vargo. The pixelating curse spread rapidly and changed creatures and objects alike into distorted and conformist shapes.

Acquisition

The pixelating curse was spread to those in close proximity to infected creatures, objects, and structures. Visitors were at risk of becoming infected on Cube 1717 after spending only 10 minutes there. There was an increased risk of becoming infected in the control at the center room of Cube 1717, where the body of the Archquardrone remained.

Cube 1717 was also fitted with an incredibly destructive weapon called a cube cannon. When caught in the range of a blast from the cube cannon, creatures and objects would become instantly infected.

Effects

Once pixellated, a creature's physical form changed in make-up - becoming a mass of 'pixels'. At first the pixels were imperceptible, but then slowly grew from the size of a specks to the size of small apples. It was possible in some rare cases for infections to affect creatures internally. These cases were no less potent, and could only be detected by examining a creature's internal organs or blood - until the final stages took hold and the rest of the creature was affected. These changes also affected any gear or items carried by infected beings.

The pixelating curse also affected the mind of those infected. As the disease progressed, they saw pixelated creatures and objects as pixelated, and lost the ability to discern features of non-pixelated ones. Their thoughts became more lawful, conformist, and rigorous. Their mind slipped into a group conciousness shared between all other pixelated creatures, communicating telepathically with them within of each other. The final stage of the disease caused their mind to be completely lost, as they believed the Archquadrone and whoever controlled it to be divine figures worthy of worship and subservience. Completely infected creatures became lawful neutral and preocupied with logic, all forms of measurements and a desire to conform to all social norms.

It was impossible to discern any detailed features of creatures or objects that were fully infected, they appeared only as a mass of colourful block shapes. When a pixelated creature died, its body remained pixelated. A mass of pixelated corpses was difficult to discern as such, and rather appeared as a strange blocky terrain resembling earthen terraces.

Even spells cast by pixelated creatures were themselves visually pixelated.

Treatment

The curse could be detected with the detect magic, which gave off an aura of transmutation. Stronger spells, such as cure disease, dispel magic, lesser and greater restoration could end its effects. The water of the River Styx also removed the curse from whatever it covered, however this of course risked serious memory loss. These magical remedies were not preventative, and individuals cured by them were at risk of being reinfected. Significantly powerful bursts of the pixelating curse, such as the exhuast of Cube 1717 could actually overpower and even pixelate Styx water. Amulets of the Styx prevented their wearer from contracting the pixelating curse. Significant divine power, such as that of Tiamat, could create auras that protected from the curse. Tiamat also provided magical boiling oil that could cure the pixelating curse. The oinoloth Malika Tahoun wore a special outfit that covered its entire body and prevented it from contracting the curse.

Truesight allowed beings to see pixelated objects and creatures in their non-pixelated forms. The specially-designed pixel glasses had a similar effect, but put the wearer at a greater risk of contracting the curse.

The pixelating curse could only be truly eradicated by destroying the Heart Engine, a component of the Archquadrone at the centre of Cube 1717.

History

The pixelating curse was created in Cube 1717 of Acheron when a quadrone from Mechanus was captured and experimented on by the derro Avnas, inspired by strange dreams from a Book of Patterns. Earlier experiments had covered a human in strange triangular spikes. The experiments drove the quadrone mad and corrupted it, growing in size and becoming the Archquadrone. It escaped and merged itself with the engines of the cube, taking control of the entire structure.

The Archquadrone spread the pixelating curse through Cube 1717 and soon had thousands of infected Acheronians under its control. With the power of the pixelating curse, the Archquadrone deposed and killed Earl Andromalius, the devil who formerly ruled Cube 1717.

The diabolical rulers of Acheron recognised that the pixelating curse put their entire plane at risk, and struck a deal with yugoloths to remove the threat. The devils and yugoloths forced the entire cube into the River Styx. This killed nearly all of those infected, but the pixelated forces used a desperate magical tactic to open a planar rift and escape into the sea of swords on the Prime Material plane. This was how the pixelating curse came to Toril in the late 15 century DR.

While on Toril, the skull lord Vargo, once a prisoner of Cube 1717, found the dying Archquadrone and turned it into the Six-Face Throne. Vargo took control of the Six-Face Throne, and along with it Cube 1717 and all those affected by the pixelating curse. A trireme of drow captained by Simak sailed from the Underdark to the island. All the drow but Simak were defeated or killed, and then infected with the pixelation curse.

Adventurers from the Sword Coast came to the Changing Island to rescue Sladek, and they became exposed to the pixelating curse. Vargo planned to attach their heads to its body, and take control of the entire Cube to spread the pixelating curse accross Faerûn and conquer the Sword Coast. The pixelated creatures also wanted to spread the pixelating curse to all of Toril, but they saw it as a peaceful gift and not an act of war.

Notable Carriers

  • Sladek the blue, a genasi wizard from the Sword Coast who became infected when he travelled to the Changing Island.
  • Avnas, the derro architect and engineer of Cube 1717, as well as her grey render Moxon.
  • Astilbe, a shadar-kai shadow dancer, who became infected on Cube 1717.
  • The disembodied head of Earl Andromalius.
  • A number of clockworks created by Avnas, including bronze scouts, stone defenders, iron cobras and oaken bolters.
  • A caravel belonging to the shadar-kai.

Other creatures on the Changing Island that were infected by the pixelating curse included a force of merregons, an army of skeletons and zombies, and its spawn, a hellfire engine and a steel predator.
