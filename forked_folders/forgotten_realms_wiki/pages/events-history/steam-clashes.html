The Steam Clashes, Steam War, or Ajhuutal Mutiny was a short war between between the great genie Calim and his rebellious vassal Ajhuu, pitting the Calim Empire against the Maridlands. It ended in the Shattering, a vast magical earthquake that destroyed and sank much of the region.

Description

In , Calim was forced to halt his empire's expansion and make peace with the elves of Tethir and Darthiir Wood, and the dwarves of Shanatar, fixing his borders at the River Agis and Alimir Mountains (the implication being that they had produced an incredible measure to threaten him with). In order to secure the peace further, he performed them the "service" of exterminating the giants and dragons that lived in the Marching Mountains. This military campaign took his full attention, meaning that he did not realize the dissatisfaction his reduced imperial ambitions was causing in his court.

Perhaps following the example set by the recent rebellion of other marids and their formation of the Marid States in the Sea of Fallen Stars, the marid lord Ajhuu claimed the easternmost portion of Calim's empire while he was distracted, and founded his capital of Ajhuutal. It took until for Calim to return from his campaigns and redirect his focus on his former vassal, prompting a series of devastating battles that failed to prove a victor. Ajhuu's lieutenants Haj and Luar were either slain or bound in the course of these battles, and it was believed that the site of it became Hajluar's Falls and the Maridflow river.

Calim's subjects noticed that Ajhuu's realm was far more free and egalitarian than their own, and this began to produce more internal dissension. Unwilling to tolerate any further appearance of weakness in his empire, in Calim gathered his dao allies and his magical artifacts from the Great Arrival, and used them to destroy Ajhuutal. In an event called the Shattering, the spell caused an immense earthquake that destroyed Ajhuu, his capital, and the land it stood on, sending the remains into the Shining Sea. Calim's zealousness in pursuing an overpowering victory meant that the earthquake then spread along the coast, sinking vast regions of his own empire and permanently altering the coastline. Though the price had been high, Calim ultimately ended the Ajhuutal Mutiny and secured peace for his empire, at least until the arrival of Memnon in .

Ajhuutal remained a figure of fascination for the future inhabitants of Calimshan, though it was confused with an unrelated ruined site that was dubbed Lost Ajhuutal.
