The Shoonach Conflagration, or the Great Fires of Shoonach, was the intentional arson and destruction of Shoonach's Imperial City, capital of the Shoon Imperium, at the hand of Prince Strohm of Tethyr. Along with the death of Emperor Amahl Shoon VII, this marked the end of the already teetering Shoon Imperium.

History

After forming a sizable rebel army and taking the throne of Tethyr from the abdicating King Priam Tornamn in 449 DR, King Silvyr Ithal proceeded to march on Shoonach, reaching the city in late Uktar. After a tenday of skirmishing and fighting, the two sides declared a truce and a challenge of single combat at the Taraqin Arena between Silvyr and Amahl VII on the Feast of the Moon. To the dismay of his followers Silvyr was slain by poison and the Tethyrian army was ambushed and slaughtered as well. For the next month Amahl and his forces celebrated their victory, believing that they had utterly vanquished the rebellious forces of Tethyr. Unfortunately for them, the clever Prince Strohm had hidden and protected much of the Tethyrian army, and proceeded to infiltrate part of them into the city under cover of the celebrations. On Hammer 30 of 450 DR King Strohm launched his own attack on the city, revealing his army encamped outside the city in concert with the forces within, having used the Imperial City's sewers, the tombs of Debukkher, and the slave population as cover (they were also joined by many slaves eager to see the overthrow of Shoon). With the assistance of his human and elven companions, Strohm assaulted the Imperial Mount and as the night turned to Midwinter, Emperor Amahl Shoon VII was slain. Thus Midwinter was celebrated as the Day of Freedom for many peoples of Faerûn as the breaking of Shoon subjugation.

After a day of fierce fighting with the remnants of the Shoon defenses and Amahl's heirs, Strohm's forces set fires around the Imperial City and let it burn. In the conflagration that followed the Imperial City was razed, killing the entire remaining royal family and the vast majority of the generals and officials who had been responsible for the running of the empire. Four days into the disaster, the great blue dragon Iryklathagra (nemesis of Shoon VII) arrived and negotiated with Strohm, coming to the agreement that she could loot the choicest magical treasures from the Palace Mount and in return never again hunt in Tethyr. Strohm left the ruined city and returned to Tethyr, what remained of the city's vaults and treasuries were looted by opportunists, and the Shoon Imperium collapsed in the years that followed.
